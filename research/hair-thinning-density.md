## 1. Executive Summary

Hair thinning and density loss are conventionally siloed as "trichology" treated with topical minoxidil (OTC) and prescription finasteride / dutasteride / oral spironolactone / oral minoxidil / clinic PRP injection series / mesotherapy / surgical hair transplant. The integrative reframing is different: hair thinning is the **scalp presentation of a four-node systemic loop** — (i) DHT-driven follicular miniaturisation via 5α-reductase type II in genetically susceptible follicles (androgenetic alopecia), (ii) micronutrient-substrate deficits — iron / ferritin, vitamin D, zinc, biotin (only if deficient), B12, omega-3, protein — that universally undermine anagen-phase quality, (iii) scalp microbiome dysbiosis (*Malassezia restricta/globosa* + *Cutibacterium* shift) driving low-grade folliculitis + barrier loss, and (iv) HPA-axis dysregulation, thyroid dysfunction (subclinical Hashimoto common in female pattern), and PCOS-overlap hyperandrogenism in women. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously modulates 5α-reductase / DHT axis, secures micronutrient floors, restores scalp microbiome + barrier and normalises HPA + thyroid addresses the upstream drivers — not the visible thin patch.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 DHT / 5α-reductase / androgen receptor axis (androgenetic alopecia).** Testosterone → DHT by 5α-reductase type II in dermal papilla cells of genetically susceptible follicles (frontotemporal + crown in men following Hamilton-Norwood; diffuse central thinning following Ludwig in women). DHT binds androgen receptor → miniaturisation: anagen phase shortens, catagen + telogen lengthen, terminal hair → vellus hair → eventually no hair (in advanced AGA). Men show ~95% of pattern hair loss is androgenetic; in women ~70% of pattern thinning has an androgenetic component. Targeted by oral saw palmetto (*Serenoa repens*, EMA HMPC monograph for BPH; off-label evidence in AGA), pumpkin seed oil (Korean RCT *Evid Based Complement Alternat Med* 2014 — 6-mo treatment showed +40% mean hair-count increase), oral *Polygonum multiflorum* (He Shou Wu — **hepatotoxicity caveat**), oral spearmint tea (anti-androgen, female AGA), oral inositol + zinc (anti-androgen), topical rosemary oil 0.5–1% (RCT-matched 2% minoxidil at 6 mo — Iranian RCT *Skinmed* 2015), topical pumpkin seed oil, topical saw palmetto extract, topical onion juice (Iraqi RCT — promotes regrowth in alopecia areata + AGA), topical green tea / EGCG. **Synthesis:** Damping DHT signalling is the upstream lever for both pattern types.

**2.2 Micronutrient floor: iron + vitamin D + zinc + omega-3 + protein.** Hair follicle is one of the body's most metabolically active tissues; substrate deficits universally compromise anagen quality. **Ferritin** is the most replicated nutritional driver — cohorts (UK / Indian / Iranian) consistently show ferritin <30 ng/mL correlates with telogen effluvium and AGA severity in women; ferritin 70+ ng/mL appears optimal for hair growth (Rushton hypothesis; mixed RCT evidence but mechanistically strong). **Vitamin D** receptor is expressed in hair follicle; low 25-OH-D correlates with AGA + alopecia areata + telogen effluvium. **Zinc** is cofactor for >300 enzymes including those of hair-shaft keratin synthesis. **Biotin** is hyped — only useful if biotin is actually deficient (rare; check before supplementing). **B12 + folate + methylation** crucial for follicle division. **Protein 1.2–1.6 g/kg** mandatory for keratin substrate. **Omega-3** improves scalp inflammation + sebaceous-gland health. Targeted by ferritin + Vit D + Zn + omega-3 + B12 + methyl folate + collagen / protein supplementation under DTC labs. **Synthesis:** Without micronutrient floor, every other lever stalls.

**2.3 Scalp microbiome + sebum + folliculitis + barrier.** Scalp surface harbours *Cutibacterium acnes*, *Staphylococcus epidermidis*, *Malassezia restricta* and *M. globosa*. Dandruff / seborrhoeic dermatitis correlates with *Malassezia* overgrowth + scalp inflammation; chronic low-grade folliculitis (Folliculitis decalvans, Lichen planopilaris, Central centrifugal cicatricial alopecia [CCCA]) can produce scarring hair loss in genetically susceptible (most CCCA cohorts are Afro-textured hair with traction + chemical-relaxer + heat-tool history). Targeted by anti-yeast scalp washes (zinc pyrithione 1%, ketoconazole-free salicylic + tea tree wash, neem decoction), gentle low-pH sulphate-free cleansers, scalp oils (Bhringraj / Eclipta alba, Brahmi, neem, rosemary), reduction of traction / chemical / heat damage, scalp massage (Japanese RCT *Eplasty* 2016 — 4-min daily standardised scalp massage 24 wk increased hair density). **Synthesis:** Healthy scalp microbiome + barrier is the soil; without it the follicle can't thrive.

**2.4 HPA-axis + thyroid + PCOS (female pattern).** Chronic stress accelerates catagen → telogen effluvium 2–3 mo after the stressor (post-COVID, post-childbirth, post-illness, post-major-loss). Subclinical hypothyroidism (TSH 2.5–4.5 with positive anti-TPO) is overrepresented in female AGA. PCOS / hyperandrogenism (elevated free testosterone, low SHBG) drives female AGA + hirsutism. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol −23–28% across RCTs), magnesium glycinate, omega-3, Bacopa, Yoga Nidra, sleep restoration, vagal tone practices, levothyroxine **only if user is already on it** (this protocol does not prescribe it; if Hashimoto-positive, the user makes their own call about prescription with prescriber), iodine + selenium + zinc thyroid-cofactor floor, inositol + spearmint + Vitex (PCOS pattern, women non-pregnant non-OCP-dependent). **Synthesis:** Without HPA + thyroid + PCOS correction (where applicable), pattern thinning rebounds under stress.

### 11.0 Self-administered clinical scoring instrument — Hamilton-Norwood / Ludwig + photo-trichoscopy

**Ludwig scale** (women, I–III) — self-staged via standardised central crown parting photo.

**SALT (Severity of Alopecia Tool)** — for alopecia areata; quadrant-based percentage scalp involvement.

**Trichoscopy via smartphone macro-lens** — adapter clips on phone ($20–60: Apexel 60×, iQonic) — visualise hair shafts + diameter variability + yellow dots / black dots / exclamation hairs (alopecia areata).

**Hair-pull-test** — gently pull ~60 hairs at 3 scalp regions weekly; >6 hairs extracted = active shedding.

**Daily shed count** (in shower drain + brush + pillow) — normal <100/day; >100 chronic = telogen effluvium concern.

**Photo-series protocol** (monthly): standardise lighting (5000 K LED or north window at 08:00); distance (30 cm close-ups, 1 m whole-face); 6 fixed views (front parting, mid-parting, vertex, both temporal angles, back); mid-grey background; bare clean dry hair (no styling product); same time of day; same parting. Save JPEG + RAW to encrypted cloud. Apps: **ScalpScope**, **Trichology Tracker**, **Hair Diary**.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Hair thinning is not "just genetics". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) in genetically susceptible follicles (most pattern hair loss), a hormone called DHT is shrinking your hair follicles every cycle — until terminal hairs become vellus (peach-fuzz) and eventually nothing; (2) substrate deficits — especially **ferritin (iron storage), vitamin D, zinc, omega-3, protein** — are starving the most metabolically active tissue in your body; (3) your scalp's microbiome and barrier may be inflamed (dandruff, *Malassezia* overgrowth, traction or chemical damage); and (4) stress, postpartum hormonal swings, or a quietly under-functioning thyroid can synchronise huge numbers of follicles into telogen (resting) phase — and 2–3 months later you shed in handfuls.

This protocol does **not** rely on indefinite prescription finasteride or dutasteride (with their post-finasteride syndrome reports), oral spironolactone, clinic PRP injection series, mesotherapy or surgical transplant. No clinic PRP. No transplant surgery. No hospital procedure.**

Expect a slow, steady rebuild. The first thing you will notice (Weeks 2–4) is that shedding rate drops. Around Week 12 short new vellus hairs appear at the hairline / parting; by Month 6 you see visible density gain. Real long-term change is a 6-to-12-month story. Advanced AGA (Norwood VI–VII or Ludwig III maxed out) will stabilise but won't dramatically re-grow without surgery — that's your call; the protocol does not require it.
