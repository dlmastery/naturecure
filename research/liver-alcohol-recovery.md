## 1. Executive Summary

Liver dysfunction is conventionally siloed as "stop drinking → statins for fatty liver → liver transplant if cirrhotic", with disulfiram/naltrexone/acamprosate for alcohol-use disorder. The integrative reframing: liver dysfunction and alcohol recovery are the hepatic and central-nervous expression of a **glutathione-depleted, mitochondrial-impaired, kupffer-cell-inflamed, steatotic, fibrosis-prone liver under chronic oxidative + AGE + endotoxin load**, driven by five convergent root mechanisms — (i) glutathione + NAC + S-adenosylmethionine (SAMe) + methylation depletion under chronic acetaldehyde + CYP2E1 + Tylenol + medication burden, (ii) mitochondrial dysfunction + fatty acid oxidation collapse → hepatic steatosis (NAFLD/MASLD/AFLD), (iii) Kupffer cell inflammation (LPS endotoxemia from leaky gut → TLR4 → NF-κB → IL-6/TNF/IL-1β) → steatohepatitis (NASH/ASH), (iv) hepatic stellate cell activation → TGF-β → collagen deposition → fibrosis → cirrhosis, and (v) alcohol-specific neuropharmacology — GABA-A potentiation + NMDA antagonism acute; chronic adaptation with GABA downregulation + NMDA upregulation → withdrawal hyperexcitability, thiamine depletion → Wernicke-Korsakoff risk, B-vitamin depletion. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously replenishes glutathione (NAC, glycine, ALA), restores mitochondrial fatty-acid oxidation (choline, betaine, TUDCA, carnitine, B-complex high-dose), dampens Kupffer cell inflammation (silymarin, curcumin, artichoke, omega-3, polyphenols), reverses early fibrosis (silibinin, vitamin E mixed tocopherols, omega-3, Bhumyamalaki, schisandra), and supports neurological recovery from alcohol (high-dose B-complex with **thiamine first** to prevent Wernicke, kudzu/puerarin to reduce craving, L-theanine + magnesium + glycine for GABA/glutamate balance, NAC for craving) addresses the upstream drivers — not just the LFT number.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

**2.1 Glutathione depletion + SAMe / methylation collapse.** Chronic alcohol + acetaldehyde + CYP2E1 induction + Tylenol (acetaminophen → NAPQI) + drug load deplete glutathione (GSH) — the master hepatic antioxidant. GSH requires cysteine (NAC supplies), glycine, glutamate. SAMe deficiency disrupts methylation — phosphatidylcholine synthesis (membrane integrity), DNA methylation, neurotransmitter synthesis. Targeted by NAC 600–1200 mg BID (cysteine precursor), glycine 3–5 g (GSH + GABA-like sleep), SAMe 400–800 mg/day (where tolerated), choline + betaine (TMG) 500 mg + 1 g, alpha-lipoic acid 300–600 mg (regenerates GSH + Vit C + E + CoQ10). **Synthesis:** GSH restoration is the upstream redox + detox lever.

**2.2 Mitochondrial dysfunction → hepatic steatosis (NAFLD/MASLD/AFLD).** Fatty acid β-oxidation depends on carnitine shuttle + CoQ10 + B-vitamin cofactors. Excess fructose, alcohol, refined carbs → de novo lipogenesis (DNL) via SREBP-1c + ChREBP → TG accumulation in hepatocytes → steatosis. Insulin resistance + adipose lipolysis → FFA flux to liver. Mitochondrial damage + ER stress → apoptosis + inflammation. Targeted by L-carnitine (1–2 g), CoQ10 (200 mg), choline + betaine, omega-3 EPA + DHA, berberine (AMPK activation), low-fructose + low-DNL diet, sprouted millets, intermittent fasting (if HPA-recovered), Zone 2 cardio, strength training. **Synthesis:** Restore β-oxidation, reduce DNL, lose visceral fat — steatosis reverses.

**2.3 Kupffer cell inflammation + endotoxemia (NASH/ASH).** Leaky gut → LPS in portal vein → Kupffer cell TLR4 → NF-κB → IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β → hepatocyte inflammation + apoptosis = steatohepatitis. Acetaldehyde directly damages hepatocytes + adducts proteins. Targeted by silymarin/silibinin (250–600 mg, EMA HMPC), curcumin (1–2 g), artichoke leaf extract (EMA HMPC), omega-3, Phyllanthus niruri / Bhumyamalaki (EMA HMPC), Liv.52 (Himalaya — multi-RCT), TUDCA 500–1000 mg (mitochondria + bile + ER stress), polyphenols, gluten + seed-oil + alcohol elimination, fermented foods, probiotic. **Synthesis:** Seal the gut + quiet the Kupffer cell = halt NASH/ASH progression.

**2.4 Hepatic stellate cell activation → TGF-β → fibrosis.** Persistent inflammation activates hepatic stellate cells → myofibroblast transformation → collagen I/III deposition → fibrosis → cirrhosis. Vitamin E mixed tocopherols (PIVENS RCT mixed but signal), silibinin, omega-3, hexitols cautious, weight loss 7–10% (NAFLD gold-standard intervention). Targeted by Vit E mixed tocopherols 400 IU, silymarin standardised, omega-3 2 g, Bhumyamalaki, kutki, schisandra, weight loss + Zone 2. **Synthesis:** Halt + slow-reverse early fibrosis (advanced cirrhosis genuinely beyond protocol scope — user's call for hepatology).

**2.5 Alcohol-specific neuropharmacology + Wernicke prevention.** Acute alcohol: GABA-A potentiation + NMDA antagonism. Chronic: GABA receptor downregulation + NMDA upregulation → withdrawal hyperexcitability, anxiety, seizure risk (genuine emergency in severe). Thiamine (B1) depletion → Wernicke encephalopathy (ataxia, confusion, ophthalmoplegia) → Korsakoff (amnestic). B6/B9/B12 depletion. Mg depletion → arrhythmia + tremor + low GABA. Targeted by **high-dose thiamine 100 mg BID — load-bearing prevention of Wernicke, must be initiated BEFORE any glucose/carbohydrate load (giving glucose first depletes residual thiamine and precipitates Wernicke)**; B-complex 50 mg high-potency BID; magnesium glycinate 400 mg; glycine 3–5 g; L-theanine 200 mg BID; kudzu (*Pueraria lobata*) — puerarin reduces alcohol craving and intake (UCLA + Korean + Chinese RCTs, but use as Tier-2 evidence object) 1000 mg BID; NAC 600 mg BID (craving + redox); milk thistle silibinin (hepatic). **Synthesis:** Thiamine FIRST + GABA/NMDA-rebalancing nutraceuticals + craving reduction = stable alcohol reduction.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

**5.1 Home weight + waist log.** Withings Body+ scale, simple tape. **7–10% weight loss is the highest-evidence NAFLD intervention** (multiple non-US RCTs).

**5.2 Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten, JNH, Clearlight).** 50–60 °C, 20–30 min, 3–4×/wk. Heat-shock proteins, mitochondrial biogenesis, sweat-mediated phthalate/BPA/heavy-metal elimination. Finnish KIHD cohort signal. Contraindication: severe hepatic decompensation, ascites, pregnancy, dehydration. Hydration mandatory.

**5.3 Home cold immersion (Plunge, Edge Tub, bathtub + ice).** 10–15 °C, 2–4 min, 3×/wk. Vagal + brown-fat + mitochondrial. Contraindication: severe CV, uncontrolled HTN, pregnancy.

**5.4 Castor oil pack over right upper quadrant.** Classical naturopathic + Ayurvedic; cotton flannel + warm castor oil + heating pad 30 min, 3–4×/wk PM. Lymphatic + hepatic-biliary stimulation. Grade C.

**5.5 Abdominal massage (Ayurvedic Abhyanga).** Self-massage with warm sesame or coconut oil 10 min AM, especially RUQ + lower abdomen. Vagal + lymphatic. Classical *Charaka* daily regimen.

**5.6 Coffee enema** — Gerson-protocol-derived adjunct, contested evidence; some users use; not mandated; **caution: hygiene, electrolyte, dependency risk**. Grade C-H.

**5.7 Home PEMF mat (Bemer, HealthyLine).** 1–10 Hz, 20 min/day. Microcirculation. Contraindication: pacemaker.

**5.8 Continuous glucose monitor (CGM).** Levels, Nutrisense, FreeStyle Libre 14d. Identifies post-prandial spikes that drive NAFLD.
