## 1. Executive Summary

Andropause / age-related testosterone decline is conventionally siloed as a "testosterone-deficiency syndrome" treated with testosterone replacement therapy (intramuscular cypionate/enanthate, transdermal gels, subcutaneous pellets, nasal/oral undecanoate), often with hCG, aromatase inhibitors, or PDE5-inhibitors layered on. The integrative reframing is different: men's vitality is the **multi-system signature of a four-node network** — (i) **HPG axis integrity** (hypothalamic GnRH pulsatility → pituitary LH/FSH → Leydig-cell testosterone + Sertoli-cell sperm); (ii) **HPA-axis / cortisol-DHEA imbalance** that suppresses testicular steroidogenesis and shifts pregnenolone away from sex steroids ("pregnenolone steal"); (iii) **metabolic-hepatic burden + adipose aromatase** (visceral adiposity, NAFLD and insulin resistance amplify aromatase activity, converting testosterone to oestradiol, raising SHBG, lowering free T); and (iv) **EDC body burden + sleep + micronutrient floor** (BPA, phthalates, atrazine, glyphosate, heavy metals directly impair Leydig function; sleep apnea suppresses morning T by 30–50%; widespread zinc, vitamin D, magnesium, B-vitamin deficiencies in modern cohorts). **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously restores HPG signalling (Ashwagandha KSM-66, fenugreek Testofen, tongkat ali Eurycoma longifolia, shilajit, maca, Kapikacchu), corrects HPA-DHEA balance (Ashwagandha, Holy Basil, Yoga Nidra), reverses metabolic-hepatic-aromatase burden (Mediterranean overlay, sprouted millets, Berberine, NAC, milk thistle, strength training), and clears EDC + sleep + micronutrient floor (sauna, organic diet, zinc, vitamin D, magnesium, B-complex, sleep apnea screen) restores morning energy, libido, body composition, mood and cognition — without exogenous testosterone.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 HPG axis (hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal) integrity.** Hypothalamic GnRH pulsatility (every 60–120 min) drives anterior pituitary LH → Leydig-cell steroidogenesis (cholesterol → pregnenolone → 17-OH-pregnenolone → DHEA → androstenedione → testosterone) and FSH → Sertoli-cell spermatogenesis. SHBG binds 50–60% of testosterone; albumin binds 35–45%; only ~2% is truly free and bio-bioactive. Aromatase (CYP19A1, expressed in adipose, liver, testes, brain) converts T → oestradiol; in obesity, aromatase activity rises, driving SHBG up and free T down. Key actors: GnRH, kisspeptin, LH receptor on Leydig cells, StAR, CYP17A1, 17β-HSD, AR, aromatase, SHBG, INSL3. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66, Tongkat ali (Eurycoma longifolia — TGA-listed), Fenugreek (Testofen), Shilajit (heavy-metal-tested), Kapikacchu, Maca, Tribulus (modest), Zinc, Vitamin D, Boron**. **Synthesis:** Restoring HPG signalling is the upstream lever.

**2.2 HPA-axis + cortisol-DHEA "pregnenolone steal".** Chronic stress + insufficient sleep + caffeine excess + alcohol elevate cortisol, which competes for pregnenolone substrate ("pregnenolone steal") and directly suppresses GnRH and Leydig steroidogenesis. DHEA-S falls with age (~80% by 75 vs peak at 25). The result: low morning T despite intact testes. Key actors: CRH, ACTH, cortisol, 11β-HSD1 and 2, DHEA, pregnenolone, glucocorticoid receptor in Leydig cells. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol −23–28% across RCTs), Holy Basil, Rhodiola, Bacopa, Yoga Nidra, MBSR, pranayama, sleep work**. **Synthesis:** Without HPA correction, T support regresses under life stress.

**2.3 Metabolic-hepatic burden + adipose aromatase.** Visceral adiposity, NAFLD/MASLD and insulin resistance establish a feed-forward loop: high adipose → high aromatase → high oestradiol → high SHBG → low free T → low muscle anabolism → more visceral adipose. Hepatic SHBG synthesis rises with insulin resistance and NAFLD. Visceral adiposity also raises leptin (a Leydig suppressor at high concentrations) and inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α) that suppress testes. Targeted by **Berberine, NAC, Milk thistle, Picrorhiza, sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets, Mediterranean overlay, Zone 2 cardio + heavy strength training, intermittent fasting if HPA stable**. **Synthesis:** Without metabolic-hepatic repair, testosterone gains plateau.

**2.4 EDC body burden + sleep + micronutrient floor.** Endocrine disruptors (BPA, phthalates, atrazine, glyphosate, organochlorine pesticides, heavy metals — lead, cadmium) directly impair Leydig function and aromatase regulation. Sleep deprivation drops morning testosterone 10–15% per week of restriction; obstructive sleep apnea suppresses T by 30–50%. Sub-clinical zinc, vitamin D, magnesium, B-vitamin deficiencies are widespread. Targeted by **glass storage, organic produce, clean cosmetics, PFAS-free cookware, water filter, infrared sauna for sweat-driven EDC, full cofactor floor (§6.6), Epworth + WatchPAT for OSA screen**. **Synthesis:** Without burden reduction + sleep + nutrient floor, the other three layers regress.
