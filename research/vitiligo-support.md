## 1. Executive Summary

Vitiligo is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" diagnosis — a cosmetic depigmentation treated with topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, clinic-based NB-UVB cabins and the new topical JAK inhibitors. The integrative reframing is different: vitiligo is the **skin presentation of a systemic NRF2/GPX4 collapse on a permeable gut barrier**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) iron-dependent lipid peroxidation (ferroptosis) of melanocytes, (ii) gliadin-zonulin-mediated intestinal permeability priming a chronic CD8⁺ T-cell attack on melanocyte antigens, (iii) microbiome dysbiosis with loss of *Akkermansia*/*Faecalibacterium*/SCFA producers, and (iv) HPA-axis hyperactivity that accelerates depigmentation under stress. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously upregulates NRF2/GPX4, seals the gut, restores SCFA-producing flora and downregulates cortisol addresses the upstream drivers rather than the cosmetic endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Triad (4-node network)

**2.1 Ferroptosis of melanocytes (NRF2/GPX4 collapse).** Melanogenesis generates H₂O₂, quinones and superoxide; IFN-γ and IL-1β from infiltrating CD8⁺ T-cells amplify mitochondrial ROS, deplete glutathione and downregulate **GPX4**. Loss of GPX4 with master TF **NRF2** and antiporter **System Xc⁻ (SLC7A11)** drives iron-dependent ferroptosis. Presentation: expanding, sharply demarcated achromic macules. Targeted by NRF2 activators (polydatin, quercetin, sulforaphane, *Polypodium leucotomos*, *Nigella sativa* thymoquinone) + glutathione-cycle support (NAC, ALA, selenium) + home red light. **Synthesis:** Reinforcing GPX4/NRF2 is the upstream brake on melanocyte loss.

**2.2 Gliadin → zonulin → leaky gut → autoimmune amplification.** Gliadin binds CXCR3 on intestinal epithelium, releasing **zonulin**, which triggers MyD88 → EGFR/PAR2 → tight-junction disassembly. LPS, undigested macromolecules and tTG-modified peptides enter lamina propria, sustaining CD8⁺ priming. Targeted by strict gluten elimination, zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, DGL liquorice, slippery elm and fermented foods. **Synthesis:** No layer holds if gluten + seed-oil intake keeps the gate open.

**2.3 Microbiome dysbiosis and SCFA depletion.** Indian, Chinese and European metagenomics show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Blautia*, *Roseburia* and expanded *Proteobacteria*. Loss of butyrate/propionate/acetate reduces GPR41/43 Treg signalling. Targeted by *Momordica charantia* polysaccharides, sprouted millets, fermented foods and live-strain probiotics. **Synthesis:** SCFA recovery is the immune-tolerance lever.

**2.4 HPA-axis hyperactivity.** Stress is a documented vitiligo trigger (Koebnerisation). Cortisol elevates ROS, suppresses Tregs, disrupts the gut barrier. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa*, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, home cold immersion. **Synthesis:** Without cortisol normalisation, the other three nodes regress under life stress.
