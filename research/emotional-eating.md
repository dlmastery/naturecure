## 1. Executive Summary

Emotional eating is conventionally siloed as a "willpower problem" or behavioural addiction, treated with cognitive-behavioural therapy referral, SSRI augmentation, GLP-1 agonist injections (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro), naltrexone-bupropion (Contrave), lisdexamfetamine for BED, or bariatric surgery. The integrative reframing is different: emotional eating is the **behavioural expression of a four-node neuroendocrine-metabolic-microbiome network** — (i) **HPA-axis hyperactivity** (chronic cortisol → visceral fat → leptin resistance → cravings), (ii) **blood-sugar volatility** (reactive hypoglycaemia → noradrenaline surge → carb seeking), (iii) **dopamine reward dysregulation** (palatable food → mesolimbic spike → tolerance → escalating intake), and (iv) **gut-brain-microbiome signalling** (dysbiosis → altered satiety hormones GLP-1/PYY, ghrelin, leptin → altered vagal afferent signalling → cravings). **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously regulates cortisol, anchors blood-sugar, retrains dopamine via novelty and movement, and rebuilds gut-brain satiety signalling addresses the upstream drivers — without chasing weight loss via GLP-1 injections or SSRI augmentation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 HPA-axis hyperactivity (stress → cortisol → cravings).** Chronic psychosocial stress, sleep deprivation, and inflammatory load elevate cortisol. Cortisol drives visceral adipose deposition, insulin resistance, and CNS reward sensitisation to high-calorie palatable foods (the "comfort food" circuit). Leptin signalling becomes blunted. NPY (neuropeptide Y) drives carbohydrate seeking. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66** (Indian RCTs Choudhary 2017 — cortisol −23–28%; food cravings ↓), **Rhodiola, Bacopa, Magnolia + Phellodendron (Relora)**, **pranayama (Anulom Vilom, Bhramari), 4-7-8 breath before meals**, **Yoga Nidra**, **cold water immersion**, **HRV biofeedback**, **vagal nerve stimulation (Nurosym, Sensate, Apollo Neuro)**, **sleep restoration**. **Synthesis:** Without HPA quieting, cravings recur at every life-stress spike.

**2.2 Blood-sugar volatility (reactive hypoglycaemia → adrenaline → carb-seeking).** Post-prandial glucose spikes (refined carb + sugar) trigger insulin surge, then **reactive hypoglycaemia 2–3 h later**, with adrenaline + glucagon + cortisol release. This cascade is felt as: shakiness, anxiety, irritability, "I need something sweet RIGHT NOW", brain fog. Chronic repetition cements the behavioural craving loop. Targeted by **fiber-anchoring meals** (15+ g fiber per meal), **protein-first / fat-first eating order**, **vegetables before carbs**, **vinegar pre-meal (15 ml ACV)**, **gymnema sylvestre 200–400 mg pre-meal** (Indian RCTs — blunts sweet taste perception 60–90 min), **berberine 500 mg TID with meals** (AMPK + glucose control; Chinese metabolic syndrome RCTs), **cinnamon 1–3 g**, **chromium picolinate 200 µg**, **post-meal 10-min walk**, **eliminating refined sugar + HFCS + ultraprocessed**, **continuous glucose monitor (CGM) for 2-week self-discovery (Levels, Nutrisense, Veri, Lingo) — non-prescription consumer-grade**. **Synthesis:** Stable blood sugar removes the metabolic driver of "I need something sweet NOW".

**2.3 Dopamine reward dysregulation.** Hyperpalatable foods (engineered to maximise the sugar-fat-salt bliss-point) drive supra-physiological mesolimbic dopamine release exceeding evolutionary food rewards. Repeated exposure → D2 receptor downregulation → tolerance → increasing intake required for the same hit. Mirrors substance-use disorder neurobiology (Stice, Volkow cohorts treated as evidence objects, not US-policy). Targeted by **L-tyrosine 500–1000 mg AM** (dopamine precursor), **L-theanine + caffeine AM** (smoothed reward + alertness), **N-acetylcysteine (NAC) 600 mg BID** (modulates nucleus accumbens glutamate, anti-craving signal in addictions literature), **morning sunlight + outdoor exercise** (dopamine + circadian), **novelty engagement** (new hobby, new music, new skill — natural dopamine), **cold immersion** (acute dopamine surge), **gradual reduction of ultraprocessed exposure** (palate reset 2–4 weeks), **Buddhist mindful-eating practices** (chew 20–30×; engage all senses; gratitude pause). **Synthesis:** Re-sensitising dopamine to whole-food rewards is the addiction-pattern lever.

**2.4 Gut-brain-microbiome signalling (satiety hormones + vagal afferents).** Microbiome composition shapes satiety: **Akkermansia muciniphila** induces GLP-1; **Lactobacillus gasseri** modulates leptin sensitivity; SCFA producers (Faecalibacterium, Roseburia, Eubacterium) activate FFAR2/3 → PYY + GLP-1 release. Vagal afferents from gut to NTS signal fullness. Dysbiosis + ultraprocessed-food-driven Proteobacteria expansion + LPS leak distort these signals — you don't *feel* full. Targeted by **fiber-anchoring 35–45 g/day** (most users get 12 g — this gap is enormous), **fermented foods 100–150 g daily** (kanji, sauerkraut, A2 kefir, kimchi, coconut kefir, fresh A2 dahi), **prebiotic foods** (chicory root, jicama, garlic, onion, leek, green banana resistant starch), **multi-strain probiotic 20–50B CFU** (Visbiome, Bio-K+, Florastor, Megaspore), **polyphenol diversity** (30+ plants/week — Tim Spector Zoe-style microbiome diversity target), **bone broth + L-glutamine + zinc-carnosine for barrier**, **eliminating gluten + seed oils + emulsifiers + ultra-processed foods**. **Synthesis:** Microbiome-driven satiety recovery removes the "I'm still hungry after a full meal" signal.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Emotional eating is not a willpower failure — it is four things happening in your body at the same time: (1) chronic stress raises cortisol, which drives you toward "comfort food" — your brain literally wires sugar-fat as a stress remedy; (2) your blood sugar goes up and down all day, and the crashes (the 3 PM dip) feel like "I need something sweet right now" because adrenaline is firing; (3) ultra-processed foods (engineered to maximise sugar-fat-salt) hijack the same dopamine reward circuit as addictive drugs — and your tolerance keeps rising; and (4) your gut microbiome and gut-brain signaling have shifted so you don't actually *feel* full after a full meal.

This protocol does **not** chase emotional eating with GLP-1 injections (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro), naltrexone-bupropion (Contrave), SSRIs, or lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse). Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the inside out — with **a pantry sweep of ultra-processed foods** (the single biggest first step), foods that anchor blood sugar (fiber 40 g, protein at every meal, fermented foods), OTC botanicals (Ashwagandha, Gymnema 15 min before meals to blunt the sweet circuit, Berberine, NAC, Saffron, L-Tyrosine, magnesium), pre-meal 4-7-8 breathing × 4 rounds (the single biggest behavioural lever), mindful eating (chew 20×, fork down, fullness check halfway), morning sunlight + outdoor exercise, sauna + cold water, kitchen closes at 19:30 (no late-night screen + snack pairing), and disciplined sleep (7.5–8.5 h is non-negotiable). **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.**

Expect a **fast** environmental + behavioural lift — pantry sweep + 4-7-8 + sleep + Gymnema deliver 30–50% reduction in **2–4 weeks**. The full supplement + diet stack takes **8–12 weeks** to deeply re-anchor metabolism + microbiome. Real, durable freedom from emotional eating is a 4-to-12-month story — but the changes are real and they hold.
