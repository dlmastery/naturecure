## 1. Executive Summary

Insulin resistance (IR) is conventionally siloed as a "pre-diabetes risk factor" leading to a step-wise pharmaceutical cascade — metformin, GLP-1 agonists, SGLT-2 inhibitors, eventually insulin — applied at thresholds that lag pathology by 5–15 years. The integrative reframing is different: IR is the **whole-body metabolic-inflammatory state of an evolutionarily-mismatched cell membrane, mitochondria, hepatocyte and microbiome**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) skeletal-muscle AMPK suppression with GLUT4 translocation failure and ectopic lipid (intramyocellular diacylglycerol → PKC-θ → IRS-1 Ser307 phosphorylation), (ii) hepatic de novo lipogenesis fed by fructose + refined carbohydrate plus impaired hepatic insulin clearance, (iii) adipose tissue inflammation with leptin/adiponectin/resistin/TNF-α dysregulation, and (iv) gut-microbiome dysbiosis with LPS endotoxemia driving systemic low-grade inflammation. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously reactivates AMPK, drains hepatic fat, reduces visceral adiposity inflammation and restores microbiome-derived SCFA addresses the upstream drivers — not the downstream glucose number.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Skeletal-muscle AMPK suppression + intramyocellular lipid + GLUT4 failure.** Sedentary lifestyle, chronic carbohydrate excess and mitochondrial dysfunction lower the AMP:ATP ratio chronically, deactivating AMPK and its downstream targets (ACC, PGC-1α, mitochondrial biogenesis). Intramyocellular diacylglycerol accumulates, activates PKC-θ, phosphorylates IRS-1 at Ser307 inhibitorily, blocks PI3K-Akt-AS160 signalling and traps GLUT4 in intracellular vesicles. Targeted by **Berberine HCl/dihydroberberine, *Gymnema sylvestre*, *Momordica charantia*, *Coccinia indica*, exercise (Zone 2 + resistance), creatine 5 g, time-restricted eating**. **Synthesis:** Restoring AMPK + emptying intramyocellular triglyceride is the upstream lever on muscle insulin signalling.

**2.2 Hepatic de novo lipogenesis + NAFLD / MASLD.** Dietary fructose and refined carbohydrate enter the liver, are converted to acetyl-CoA, then via ACC → malonyl-CoA → palmitate → triglyceride. Hepatic steatosis impairs insulin clearance, hyperinsulinemia worsens lipogenesis (the loop), and inflammatory hepatocytes release CRP, fetuin-A and DAMPs. Loss of hepatic insulin clearance (~50% of systemic insulin disposal is hepatic in health) inflates peripheral insulin and worsens IR. Targeted by **Berberine, Silybum marianum (Milk Thistle / silymarin), *Picrorhiza kurroa* (Kutki), choline 425–550 mg, betaine TMG 1–2 g, omega-3 EPA-rich, fructose elimination, alcohol elimination, sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets, time-restricted eating**. **Synthesis:** Without hepatic fat drainage, IR is uncorrectable from peripheral interventions alone.

**2.3 Adipose tissue inflammation + adipokine dysregulation.** Visceral adipose tissue (omental, mesenteric, retroperitoneal) expands beyond the storage capacity, hypoxic adipocytes recruit M1 macrophages, NLRP3 inflammasome activates, IL-6/TNF-α/resistin rise, adiponectin falls. Leptin resistance develops in the hypothalamus. The result is sustained low-grade systemic inflammation that maintains IR independently of glucose load. Targeted by **visceral fat reduction (resistance + Zone 2 + sleep + protein 1.2–1.6 g/kg), Curcumin Phytosome, Boswellia AKBA, omega-3 EPA-rich, polyphenol-dense diet (pomegranate, blueberry, green tea), Ashwagandha KSM-66 for cortisol-driven visceral fat**. **Synthesis:** Without visceral fat reduction, adipokine inflammation maintains IR even with perfect diet.

**2.4 Gut microbiome dysbiosis + LPS endotoxemia.** IR cohorts show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Lactobacillus*, *Roseburia* and expanded *Proteobacteria* (*Enterobacteriaceae*). Reduced SCFA (butyrate, propionate, acetate) impairs GLP-1/PYY signalling, weakens the mucin barrier, and allows LPS to translocate, binding TLR4 on Kupffer cells and adipocyte macrophages — sustaining IR. Targeted by **PHGG (partially hydrolyzed guar gum), acacia fibre, sprouted millets, fermented foods (kanji, sauerkraut, kefir), multi-strain probiotics + spore-based (MegaSporeBiotic, Just Thrive), Akkermansia-favouring polyphenols (pomegranate ellagitannins, cranberry, *Momordica charantia* polysaccharides MCPS-3)**. **Synthesis:** Restoring SCFA-producing microbiome is the immune-tolerance and incretin lever; no IR plan holds without it.

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

**Meal ordering protocol (Japanese / Australian cohorts):** fibre + protein FIRST → fat → carbohydrate LAST — reduces post-prandial glucose 30–40% with identical macros.

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers to eliminate

- **Sedentary work (>8 h sitting)** — strongest single modifiable risk factor; install standing desk, walk-pad treadmill (Walking Pad A1Pro), 2-minute walk every 30 min.

- **Shift work / night work** — disrupts circadian glucose-tolerance rhythm; if unavoidable, use blue-blockers + bright AM light + strict meal timing.
- **Chronic stress / unresolved trauma** — cortisol → visceral fat → IR loop. Address with yoga nidra, vagal practices, therapy if needed.
- **Endocrine disruptors** — BPA (canned food linings, plastic water bottles, thermal receipts), phthalates (vinyl, plastics, fragrance), PFAS ("forever chemicals" — non-stick cookware, water-repellent textiles, fast-food wrappers). Swap to glass / steel / ceramic; avoid microwaving plastic; filter water (Berkey, Aquasana RO).
- **Light at night (blue + bright)** — disrupts melatonin → IR. Blue-blockers after sunset; blackout bedroom.
- **Indoor air pollution** — VOC, particulate; HEPA + activated carbon air filter.
- **Smoking, vaping, alcohol** — all worsen IR through hepatic, oxidative and microbiome pathways.
- **Pesticide exposure** (farm work, lawn care) — organochlorine pesticides documented diabetogenic. Buy EWG Dirty Dozen organic.
- **Heavy metals** (arsenic in rice, mercury in large fish) — switch to lower-arsenic rice cultivars (basmati) or all-millet; small fish only (sardines, anchovies, mackerel).
- **Mainstream cooking oil chemistry** — swap industrial seed oils for ghee / coconut / olive (cold-pressed) / avocado.

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.0 Self-administered clinical scoring instrument — HOMA-IR + HbA1c + Fasting Insulin + CGM-derived TIR/CV

**HOMA-IR** (Homeostatic Model Assessment of Insulin Resistance) — fully self-administrable via DTC fasting glucose + fasting insulin lab; formula = (Fasting Insulin µIU/mL × Fasting Glucose mg/dL) / 405. Target <1.5 = excellent insulin sensitivity; >2.5 = IR; >3.5 = severe IR.

**HbA1c** — DTC fingerstick A1c kit (HbA1cNow at home, $30) or DTC venous lab. Target <5.4% = optimal; 5.5–5.6% = optimal range upper; 5.7–6.4% = prediabetes; ≥6.5% = T2D classified.

**Fasting insulin** — DTC venous. Target <8 µIU/mL = excellent; 8–10 = optimal upper; >10 = early IR; >15 = clear IR.

**OGTT** (oral glucose tolerance test) — DTC 2-h 75g, ideally with insulin curve (0/30/60/90/120 min). Most sensitive early-IR detector; 1-h glucose >155 mg/dL or 1-h insulin >100 µIU/mL = early IR even if fasting normal.

**CGM-derived metrics** (consumer-direct):
- **TIR (Time in Range 70–140 mg/dL)** — target >85%; >90% = excellent
- **CV (coefficient of variation = SD/mean)** — target <33%
- **MAGE** (mean amplitude of glycaemic excursions) — track meal effects
- **Post-meal peak** — target <140 mg/dL at 1 h; <120 mg/dL at 2 h
- **Mean glucose** — target <105 mg/dL
- **Time below 70** — should be near 0% off Rx

**Body composition self-tracking** — waist circumference (target M <94 cm, F <80 cm); waist-to-height ratio (<0.5); InBody/Tanita bioimpedance at home (Tanita BC-601, $200) for body fat %, visceral fat score, muscle mass — track monthly.

**Cadence:** baseline (Week 0), Wk 4, Wk 8, Wk 12, Mo 6, Mo 9, Mo 12.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) your muscles have stopped responding to insulin's "open the glucose door" signal because they're full of stored fat and you haven't asked them to work hard; (2) your liver has filled up with fat (fatty liver / MASLD), and a fatty liver makes more sugar than a healthy one — even when you're fasting; (3) the fat around your organs (visceral fat) is acting like an inflammation factory, sending out signals that worsen insulin resistance everywhere; and (4) the friendly bacteria in your gut — the ones that calm inflammation and signal "I'm full" to your brain — are starving on a refined-grain, low-fibre diet.
