## 1. Executive Summary

PMS is conventionally siloed as a "psychiatric" or "gynaecology" diagnosis treated with luteal-phase or continuous SSRIs, oral contraceptive pill (often drospirenone-containing), hormonal IUD, GnRH agonists, and in extreme PMDD cases bilateral oophorectomy. The integrative reframing is different: PMS is the **luteal-phase systemic signal of progesterone-estrogen ratio imbalance + GABA-system instability + serotonin / dopamine cyclical dip + estrogen clearance deficit + cofactor (magnesium, B6, omega-3) depletion + chronic stress / HPA-axis amplification**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) **luteal hormone imbalance** (relative estrogen excess + progesterone insufficiency; falling estrogen-progesterone at end-luteal triggers withdrawal symptoms), (ii) **neurotransmitter cyclical dip** — serotonin / dopamine / GABA fluctuate with cycle; falling allopregnanolone (progesterone metabolite) destabilises GABA-A signalling, (iii) **estrogen clearance deficit** — sluggish phase-I (CYP1A1/CYP1B1 producing 2- vs 16-hydroxy-estrogen) + phase-II (methylation, glucuronidation, sulphation) clearance through liver, with gut microbiome (β-glucuronidase activity, estrobolome) recirculating estrogen; constipation amplifies, and (iv) **cofactor + HPA + sleep substrate** — magnesium, B6 (P5P), omega-3, vitamin D, iron deficiencies all amplify; chronic stress + sleep deprivation hijacks the GABA / cortisol / serotonin loop. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously supports progesterone synthesis + reduces estrogen excess, stabilises neurotransmitters across the cycle, accelerates phase-I/II estrogen clearance, and provides cofactor + HPA + sleep support addresses the upstream drivers — not the SSRI / OCP / GnRH endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Luteal hormone imbalance — progesterone insufficiency + relative estrogen excess.** Following ovulation, the corpus luteum produces progesterone (peaking day 21 of a 28-day cycle); insufficient ovulation or short luteal phase → low progesterone → unopposed estrogen effects. Estrogen drives water retention, breast tissue proliferation, mood lability via serotonin / dopamine signalling. The end-luteal drop in both hormones triggers PMS / PMDD symptoms. Targeted by **Vitex agnus-castus (chasteberry)** — Hamburg / German studies show dopamine-D2 agonist action at pituitary → reduces prolactin → supports corpus luteum + progesterone synthesis; EMA HMPC monograph established for PMS; **NOT in pregnancy or IVF**. Other interventions: ovulation-tracking (Clue / Garmin / Oura / temperature + LH strips), seed-cycling traditional approach (flax + pumpkin follicular, sesame + sunflower luteal — no high-quality RCT but harmless food-based), reducing endocrine disruptors (BPA, phthalates, parabens), supporting healthy ovulation via stable insulin + adequate body fat + protein intake. **Synthesis:** Supporting healthy ovulation + corpus-luteum function is the upstream lever.

**2.2 Neurotransmitter cyclical dip — serotonin / dopamine / GABA / allopregnanolone.** Allopregnanolone (a progesterone metabolite) is a potent positive allosteric modulator of GABA-A receptors; falling allopregnanolone in end-luteal phase reduces GABA tone → anxiety, irritability, insomnia. Serotonin precursor (tryptophan) and metabolism varies with cycle; estrogen modulates 5-HT1A receptor density. Dopamine fluctuates with cycle. Targeted by **magnesium glycinate 300–400 mg** (GABA-A potentiation + NMDA modulation), **vitamin B6 (P5P) 50–100 mg** (neurotransmitter synthesis cofactor), **saffron 30 mg** (RCT-grade serotonin modulator, EMA HMPC monograph, **caution in pregnancy at high dose**), **L-theanine 200 mg** (GABAergic), **Bacopa monnieri** (modulates 5-HT + cholinergic), **ashwagandha KSM-66** (cortisol-mediated GABA support), **lemon balm**, **chamomile**, **L-tryptophan or 5-HTP** (NOT if on SSRI). **Synthesis:** Neurotransmitter stabilisation across the cycle softens the symptoms while §2.1 corrects the underlying driver.

**2.3 Estrogen clearance — liver phase-I/II + gut estrobolome.** Estrogen is metabolised in liver phase-I (CYP1A1 → 2-OH-E, "safer"; CYP1B1 → 4-OH-E, "more reactive"; CYP3A4 → 16-OH-E, "estrogenic") and phase-II (methylation via COMT, glucuronidation, sulphation). Methylation requires methyl-folate, methyl-B12, betaine, magnesium. Glucuronidation conjugates go to gut → if **β-glucuronidase** (made by certain gut bacteria — estrobolome) is high, conjugated estrogens get deconjugated and recirculate, raising body estrogen burden. Constipation amplifies. Targeted by **calcium-D-glucarate 500–1500 mg** (inhibits β-glucuronidase), **DIM (diindolylmethane) 100–200 mg** or **I3C (indole-3-carbinol)** from cruciferous (broccoli sprouts, brussels), **sulforaphane** (broccoli sprout extract / BroccoMax), **milk thistle** (silymarin liver support), **methylfolate + methyl-B12 + betaine + magnesium** (COMT cofactor), **fibre 30+ g daily**, **fermented foods**, gluten + alcohol elimination, weekly bowel-movement target (Bristol 3-4, ≥daily). **Synthesis:** Phase-I/II + estrobolome health clears the unopposed estrogen burden faster.

**2.4 Cofactor + HPA + sleep substrate.** Magnesium, B6 (P5P), omega-3 EPA, vitamin D, iron, calcium are all amplifying deficiencies in PMS cohorts. Chronic stress + sleep deprivation hijacks the system — cortisol depletes progesterone (pregnenolone steal), depletes serotonin, worsens insulin sensitivity, worsens sleep, amplifies symptoms. Targeted by full cofactor floor (§6.6), Ashwagandha KSM-66, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, HRV biofeedback, sleep hygiene 7–9 h, screen detox, exercise periodised to cycle. **Synthesis:** Without HPA + sleep + cofactor floor, the other three nodes plateau.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

PMS is not "all in your head" and it is not a moral failing. Inside your body during the luteal phase (days 15–28 of your cycle), four things are usually happening together: (1) your hormones — progesterone and estrogen — are out of balance, often with relatively low progesterone and unopposed estrogen; (2) your brain chemicals — serotonin, dopamine, GABA — dip cyclically as a progesterone metabolite called allopregnanolone falls; (3) your liver and gut may not be clearing estrogen efficiently, so the body recirculates extra estrogen and amplifies symptoms; and (4) your magnesium, vitamin B6, omega-3 EPA, vitamin D, iron levels are likely depleted, while modern life — alcohol, plastics, screens at night, chronic stress — adds amplification.

This protocol does **not** rely on luteal-phase SSRIs, oral contraceptive pill as default, hormonal IUD or GnRH agonists. Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the inside out — with **chasteberry (Vitex agnus-castus)** taken every morning to support progesterone (EMA-monographed, 6-cycle trial), **magnesium glycinate + vitamin B6** for neurotransmitter and GABA stability, **calcium-D-glucarate + DIM + sulforaphane + milk thistle** to accelerate estrogen clearance, **saffron, ashwagandha, L-theanine** for mood, **omega-3** for cyclical inflammation, classical Ayurvedic and TCM formulas (Ashokarishta, Shatavari, *Xiao Yao San*), **a full DTC blood panel** to find and fix what's actually low, cycle-aware exercise (heavier follicular, restorative luteal), home red-light therapy, infrared sauna, **Yoga Nidra nightly in luteal phase** (the single most effective non-pharmacological PMS practice), **eliminate alcohol especially luteal phase**, eliminate plastic / BPA / phthalates, switch to organic period products, and **prioritise sleep 8 hours nightly**. **No SSRI default. No OCP default. No GnRH.**

**Critically: chasteberry takes ≥3 cycles to show effect and 6 cycles to optimal benefit. Stop chasteberry + saffron extract immediately if you become pregnant. If you are trying to conceive or on IVF, coordinate carefully.**

Expect a slow, steady re-balance — not an overnight switch. The first thing you will notice (Cycles 2–4) is that physical symptoms (bloating, breast tenderness, cramping) reduce. Around Cycle 4–6 mood-dominant symptoms (irritability, anxiety, weepiness) substantively improve. Real long-term stability is a 6-to-12-cycle story.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a gynaecologist informed, that is your personal decision. The protocol does not require it.

## 14. Independent Healing Paradigm
