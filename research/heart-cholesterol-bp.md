## 1. Executive Summary

Heart disease is conventionally siloed as "high cholesterol → statin; high blood pressure → ACE-i / β-blocker / CCB; high risk → aspirin; advanced → stent/CABG". The integrative reframing: cardiovascular dysfunction is the vascular and metabolic expression of a **systemic endothelial-nitric-oxide collapse on a vascular-stiffening, RAAS-overactive, salt-sensitive, oxidised-LDL-laden, insulin-resistant, sleep-apnea-stressed substrate**, driven by five convergent root mechanisms — (i) endothelial dysfunction + nitric oxide deficit (eNOS uncoupling, ADMA elevation, low dietary nitrate, low L-arginine + L-citrulline), (ii) oxidised LDL + ApoB particle burden + Lp(a) genetic load triggering subendothelial atherogenesis, (iii) RAAS hyperactivity + salt-sensitive hypertension + sympathetic dominance + low magnesium, (iv) vascular stiffness from chronic inflammation + AGE accumulation + matrix metalloproteinase remodelling, and (v) sleep apnea + insufficient sleep raising nocturnal sympathetic tone, cortisol, BP and arrhythmia risk. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously restores endothelial NO (beetroot, L-citrulline, hibiscus, garlic aged extract, taurine, vitamin K2-MK7 routing Ca to bone not arteries), reduces ApoB/oxLDL (bergamot polyphenols, red yeast rice cautiously, plant sterols, omega-3 EPA-heavy, pantethine), normalises RAAS + salt-handling + sympathetic tone (magnesium glycinate, potassium-K rich foods, hibiscus tea, Arjuna, ashwagandha, vagal practices, Zone 2 cardio), reduces vascular stiffness (omega-3, polyphenols, vitamin C, Mediterranean-style + millet diet, Zone 2), and addresses sleep apnea + sleep — addresses the upstream drivers, not the cholesterol number in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

**2.1 Endothelial dysfunction + nitric oxide deficit.** Endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS) requires BH4 (tetrahydrobiopterin), L-arginine substrate, oxygen, NADPH. Oxidative stress oxidises BH4 → BH2 → eNOS "uncouples" → produces superoxide instead of NO. ADMA (asymmetric dimethylarginine) competes with L-arginine. Result: ↓ NO → ↓ vasodilation → ↑ BP, ↑ vascular tone, ↓ flow-mediated dilation. Targeted by dietary nitrate (beetroot 250–500 ml juice or 5 g powder — provides ~5–8 mmol NO3⁻), L-citrulline 6 g (bypasses arginase, raises plasma arginine + NO better than L-arginine), garlic aged extract (Kyolic — eNOS upregulation, polysulphides), hibiscus tea (anthocyanins, ACE-inhibitory-like activity), taurine 2–3 g (eNOS + Na/K-ATPase + sympathetic dampening), vitamin C, polyphenols, exercise (FMD improvement), vitamin D, omega-3. **Synthesis:** Restoring NO is the upstream vascular tone lever.

**2.2 Oxidised LDL + ApoB burden + Lp(a).** ApoB-containing particles (LDL, IDL, VLDL remnants, Lp(a)) enter sub-endothelial intima at sites of endothelial dysfunction, get oxidised by myeloperoxidase + Lp-PLA2, are taken up by macrophages → foam cells → fatty streak → plaque. Lp(a) is a genetically determined ApoB+kringle particle — independent risk factor unresponsive to statins. Targeted by bergamot polyphenolic fraction (BPF, 500–1000 mg, EU RCTs show LDL ↓ 25–30%, TG ↓ 30%), pantethine (Pantesin 600–900 mg/d, ↓ ApoB + raises HDL, well-tolerated alternative when red yeast rice not suitable), red yeast rice cautiously (monacolin K = lovastatin — EMA HMPC monograph + caution, contains the same myopathy risk as statins, requires CoQ10 co-supplementation, max 3 mg/d EU limit since 2022), plant sterols 2 g/d, omega-3 EPA-heavy (≥2 g EPA, EU REDUCE-IT-style ester, lowers TG 20–30%), niacin low-dose (slow-release 500–1000 mg PM, ↓ Lp(a) 20–30%, ↑ HDL — flush manageable with aspirin avoidance via slow titration), citrus pectin / soluble fibre, oat β-glucan 3 g/d, low-glycaemic diet, weight optimisation. **Synthesis:** Lower the particle number (ApoB), not just LDL-C; address Lp(a) where genetic.

**2.3 RAAS hyperactivity + salt-sensitive hypertension + sympathetic dominance.** Sodium-sensitive individuals (more common in South Asian, African, East Asian populations than commonly stated) respond strongly to NaCl with BP elevation via volume + RAAS. Aldosterone retains Na/loses K. Sympathetic overdrive (chronic stress, caffeine excess, poor sleep, untreated apnea) elevates BP through α-adrenergic vasoconstriction. Hypomagnesaemia (90% of Western diets) → vasoconstriction + arrhythmia. Targeted by Mg glycinate 400 mg, dietary K 4000+ mg (banana, coconut water, spinach, sweet potato — **caution if CKD or on K-sparing**), DASH-style sodium reduction (<2300 mg ideally <1500 mg salt-sensitive), hibiscus tea (3 cups/d — Egyptian RCT BP ↓ 7–13 mmHg systolic), Arjuna (*Terminalia arjuna* 500 mg BID — Indian RCT BP + cardiac index), Mukta Vati (Divya — classical Ayurvedic — Indian RCT BP), Pushkarmool, ashwagandha (cortisol), HRV biofeedback, slow breathing (6 breaths/min — proven SBP/DBP reducer EU RCTs), vagal practices. **Synthesis:** Rebalance Na/K + lower sympathetic tone + restore Mg.

**2.4 Vascular stiffness from inflammation + AGEs + MMP remodelling.** Chronic low-grade inflammation (hs-CRP >2), AGEs from cooked sugars/glycation/fructose, MMP-2/MMP-9 remodelling collagen-elastin — stiffen aorta + arteries → ↑ pulse pressure, isolated systolic HTN. Targeted by omega-3 (resolution), polyphenols, vit C, Mediterranean + millet diet (low AGE), Zone 2 cardio (FMD + arterial compliance), strength training (resistance vessel remodel), curcumin, sulforaphane, low fructose. **Synthesis:** Resolution biology + glycation reduction = arterial compliance restoration.

**2.5 Sleep apnea + sleep insufficiency + nocturnal sympathetic tone.** Untreated obstructive sleep apnea is a massive but under-recognised hypertension driver — apnoeic events ↑ sympathetic burst, ↑ cortisol, ↑ aldosterone, ↑ AF/AFlutter risk. Even without apnea, sleep <6 h doubles cardiovascular risk over 10 yr. Targeted by Epworth + home oximetry screen, nasal breathing restoration, weight optimisation if overweight, side-sleeping, oral myofunctional therapy, magnesium + glycine + low-dose melatonin (PM), blue-light cutoff, AM sunlight. Some users genuinely benefit from CPAP — that is their personal choice. **Synthesis:** Without sleep + apnea correction, the protocol fights against nightly sympathetic floods.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

**5.1 Home BP monitoring (validated upper-arm cuff).** Omron Platinum, Withings BPM Core, OMRON HEM-7600T-E. AM + PM, sitting, arm at heart, after 5 min rest, no caffeine within 30 min, no smoking. Average 7–14 days. **This is the load-bearing measurement instrument — not clinic BP.**

**5.2 HRV biofeedback + slow breathing.** HeartMath Inner Balance, Elite HRV, Lief, Oura, Whoop, Polar H10. **Slow breathing at 6 breaths/min × 15 min/day** reduces SBP/DBP 4–8 mmHg per EU RCTs (Modesti, Goyal). Resonance frequency typically 0.1 Hz.

**5.3 Zone 2 cardio (45 min, 3×/wk).** Brisk walk, jog, cycle, swim, elliptical at 60–70% HRmax (or "talk pace"). Hands-down strongest single CV intervention.

**5.4 Strength training (45 min, 2×/wk).** Compound lifts — improves arterial compliance + insulin sensitivity + body composition.

**5.5 Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten, JNH, Clearlight).** 50–60 °C, 20–30 min, 3×/wk. **Finnish KIHD cohort: 4–7×/wk sauna ↓ CV mortality 50%, all-cause mortality 40%.** HSP70, FMD, vasodilation. Contraindication: severe CV disease, uncontrolled HTN, pregnancy, dehydration; combine with rehydration.

**5.6 Home cold immersion (Plunge, Edge Tub).** 10–15 °C, 2–4 min, 3×/wk.
