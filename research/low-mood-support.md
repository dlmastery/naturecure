## 1. Executive Summary

Low mood and depression-like presentations are conventionally siloed under "Major Depressive Disorder" and treated with SSRIs, SNRIs, atypicals (mirtazapine, bupropion), tricyclics, MAOIs, augmentation strategies (lithium, atypical antipsychotics), ketamine clinic infusions, TMS, and ECT. The integrative reframing is different: low mood is the **emergent expression of a depleted neurotransmitter network on a backdrop of mitochondrial dysfunction, neuroinflammation, gut-brain dysbiosis, and HPA dysregulation, layered with the cognitive-behavioural patterns of avoidance, rumination, and loss of meaning**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) monoamine + BDNF depletion (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, BDNF, neurogenesis), (ii) chronic neuroinflammation with elevated IL-6 + TNF-α + hs-CRP shifting tryptophan into the kynurenine pathway away from serotonin, (iii) mitochondrial bioenergetic dysfunction with reduced ATP, reduced PGC-1α, and reduced cellular energy underlying anhedonia + fatigue, and (iv) gut-brain axis dysbiosis with altered microbiome shifting vagal afferent + serotonin precursor + SCFA signalling toward depressogenesis. The fifth layer — **behavioural-cognitive-relational** (rumination, avoidance, loss of meaning, social withdrawal) — is intervened by structured behavioural activation, CBT/ACT self-work, social co-regulation, and meaningful re-engagement. **Synthesis:** An OTC, home-based protocol that replenishes monoamines + BDNF, lowers neuroinflammation, rebuilds mitochondria, restores gut-brain microbiota, and provides behavioural-cognitive practices addresses the upstream drivers — not the symptom.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node + behavioural layer)

**2.1 Monoamine + BDNF + neurogenesis depletion.** Depression is associated with reduced serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, BDNF, and reduced hippocampal neurogenesis. The monoamine hypothesis is incomplete but partially explanatory. Key actors: TPH2 (tryptophan hydroxylase), tyrosine hydroxylase, DOPA decarboxylase, MAO-A/B, SERT, DAT, BDNF, TrkB, mTOR. Targeted by saffron (*Crocus sativus*) — gold-standard botanical with strong RCT data approaching SSRI efficacy in mild-moderate depression; **St John's Wort** (*Hypericum perforatum*) — EMA HMPC and Cochrane-supported for mild-moderate depression but with **heavy CYP induction drug-interaction caveats**; Rhodiola; SAMe (S-adenosyl-methionine) for methylation; 5-HTP / L-tryptophan (only if not on serotonergic Rx); curcumin; omega-3 EPA; Lion's Mane (BDNF/NGF); aerobic exercise (BDNF); sunlight; social connection. **Synthesis:** Rebuilding the monoamine + BDNF substrate is the neurochemical lever.

**2.2 Neuroinflammation / inflammation-driven depression.** Elevated IL-6, TNF-α, hs-CRP correlate with depression severity; the **kynurenine pathway** is activated by inflammation, shunting tryptophan away from serotonin synthesis toward neurotoxic quinolinic acid. Key actors: IL-6, TNF-α, IFN-γ, IDO (indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase), kynurenine, quinolinic acid, NMDA. Targeted by omega-3 EPA (high-dose), curcumin, resveratrol, *Boswellia* AKBA, *Nigella sativa* thymoquinone, gluten elimination trial, gut healing, anti-inflammatory diet (Siri Dhanyalu millet + greens + ferment), micronutrient floor (Zn, Mg, D), reduced visceral adiposity. **Synthesis:** Lowering systemic inflammation is the upstream anti-depressant lever.

**2.3 Mitochondrial bioenergetic dysfunction.** Depression — particularly the anhedonia + fatigue dimensions — correlates with reduced mitochondrial ATP, reduced Complex I/IV activity, reduced PGC-1α, oxidative damage. Key actors: Complex I, IV; ATP synthase; CoQ10; NAD+; carnitine; α-lipoic acid; PGC-1α; Mg. Targeted by CoQ10/Ubiquinol, NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR), acetyl-L-carnitine, α-lipoic acid, magnesium glycinate, creatine, B-complex (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B12), red light photobiomodulation (660 + 850 nm) + transcranial PBM, Zone 2 cardio rebuild. **Synthesis:** Restoring mitochondria substrates the energy + reward systems.

**2.4 Gut-brain axis dysbiosis + neuroinflammation feedback.** Depressed cohorts show altered microbiome with reduced *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Faecalibacterium*; increased zonulin + LPS; altered tryptophan-serotonin-kynurenine balance; reduced SCFA. Key actors: vagus nerve afferents, enteric serotonin (90% of body serotonin), microbiota-derived SCFA, tryptophan-kynurenine balance, zonulin/occludin, LPS-TLR4. Targeted by multi-strain probiotics ("psychobiotic" strains — *L. helveticus*, *B. longum*, Cerebiome blend), prebiotics, fermented foods, zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, gluten-free trial, polyphenol-rich foods. **Synthesis:** Sealing the gut and shifting microbiota is the gut-brain lever.

**Behavioural-cognitive-relational layer (intervention only, not a separate "mechanism").** **Behavioural activation** (gradual re-engagement in pleasurable + meaningful activities to break the rumination-avoidance cycle), **CBT-informed self-work** (cognitive restructuring; Padesky / Greenberger *Mind Over Mood*; Australian Russ Harris ACT), **MBCT (Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy)** — UK Williams + Teasdale + Segal — proven to reduce relapse in recurrent depression; **values clarification + meaningful re-engagement**; **social co-regulation** (face-to-face time with trusted others — load-bearing); **nature exposure** (forest bathing, gardening); **creative-expressive practices** (journaling, music, art); **gratitude practice** (3 things daily — Seligman / Australian positive psychology). **Synthesis:** Behavioural and relational shifts are non-negotiable — supplements alone do not restore meaning.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Low mood and depression are not "weakness" or "needing to try harder" — they are real, measurable shifts in your brain chemistry, mitochondrial energy, immune system, gut microbiome, and hormonal balance, layered with patterns of avoidance, rumination, and loss of meaning. Four things are usually happening at once: (1) your mood neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine) and your brain growth factor (BDNF) are depleted, (2) your body has low-grade inflammation that shifts tryptophan away from serotonin, (3) your cellular energy plants (mitochondria) are running low, and (4) your gut microbiome — which talks directly to your brain — is out of balance.

This protocol does **not** rely on SSRIs as a first move. Instead it rebuilds each of those layers from the outside in — with **mood-supporting botanicals** (saffron — strong RCT evidence comparable to fluoxetine in mild-moderate depression; curcumin; St John's Wort *only if you are on no prescriptions* because it interacts with many drugs); **adaptogens** (Ashwagandha, Bacopa, Rhodiola, Tulsi); **mitochondrial cofactors** (CoQ10, magnesium, B-complex, Lion's Mane); **omega-3 EPA** at high dose (target omega-3 index ≥8%); **probiotic + fermented foods** for gut-brain repair; **AM bright light** 30 minutes (especially load-bearing for winter SAD); **morning sunlight + outdoor time 60 min daily**; **sauna + cold immersion**; **transcranial red light** (optional Vielight); **aerobic exercise** (approaches SSRI efficacy in meta-analyses); **MBCT or ACT 8-week online course** (proven to reduce relapse); **behavioural activation** (gradual re-engagement with pleasurable + meaningful activities); **social co-regulation** (face-to-face contact with trusted others — non-negotiable); **values clarification + meaningful re-engagement**; and **gratitude practice**. **No prescription.
