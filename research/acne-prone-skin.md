## 1. Executive Summary

Acne is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" problem treated with topical retinoids, benzoyl peroxide, topical/oral antibiotics, hormonal pills and isotretinoin. The integrative reframing is different: acne is the **follicular presentation of a four-node systemic loop** — (i) androgen-driven sebum hypersecretion with altered sebum lipidome, (ii) follicular hyperkeratinisation occluding the sebaceous duct, (iii) *Cutibacterium acnes* (formerly *Propionibacterium acnes*) phylotype shift and *Malassezia* overgrowth on a dysbiotic skin microbiome, and (iv) insulin/IGF-1/mTORC1 signalling amplified by high-glycaemic-load Western diets, dairy, gut dysbiosis and HPA-axis dysregulation. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously normalises insulin/IGF-1/mTORC1, repairs the gut, modulates skin microbiome and selectively reduces 5α-reductase / androgen tone addresses the upstream drivers, not the comedone.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Androgen → sebum hypersecretion + altered sebum lipidome.** Free testosterone is converted to dihydrotestosterone (DHT) by 5α-reductase in sebocytes; DHT binds the androgen receptor (AR), upregulates sterol-response-element-binding-protein-1 (SREBP-1) and stearoyl-CoA desaturase, driving triglyceride + squalene synthesis. Squalene is then peroxidised (squalene peroxide is comedogenic and pro-inflammatory). Clinical presentation: oily T-zone, jawline, chest, back; flares premenstrually, on high-androgen states (PCOS, anabolic supplements). Targeted by spearmint tea (anti-androgen), saw palmetto, reishi (5α-reductase), zinc picolinate (anti-androgen + barrier), green tea EGCG topical, *Vitex agnus-castus* (luteal-phase hormonal modulation). **Synthesis:** Damping androgen → sebum is the upstream brake on the entire cascade.

**2.2 Follicular hyperkeratinisation (comedogenesis).** IL-1α and altered linoleic-acid : oleic-acid ratio in sebum drive infundibular keratinocyte cohesion failure — corneocytes pile up, plugging the follicle. The micro-comedone is the precursor of every visible lesion. Targeted by topical bakuchiol (plant retinoid analog), topical niacinamide 4%, topical 2% salicylic acid (BHA), oral zinc, omega-3 (restores linoleic acid). **Synthesis:** Open the follicle without stripping the barrier and the inflammatory cascade loses its substrate.

**2.3 *C. acnes* phylotype shift + *Malassezia* overgrowth.** Healthy follicles are colonised by *C. acnes* phylotype II/III; acne-prone follicles shift toward phylotype IA1, which produces porphyrins, LPS-like toxins, biofilm and triggers TLR2/4 → NLRP3 inflammasome → IL-1β. Concurrent *Malassezia furfur/restricta* overgrowth produces the small uniform monomorphic papules of "fungal acne" (Malassezia folliculitis). Targeted by topical tea tree oil 5%, *Indigo naturalis* topical, neem topical, manuka honey mask, oral berberine + AMPK pathway, oral probiotic (*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG, *L. paracasei*); for fungal sub-type, ketoconazole-free zinc pyrithione 1% wash + reduction of skin-fed yeasts. **Synthesis:** Restoring skin microbial balance is the immune-de-escalation lever.

**2.4 Insulin / IGF-1 / mTORC1 + gut-HPA axis.** Western diets high in refined dairy whey, high-glycaemic carbohydrate and ultra-processed foods raise insulin and IGF-1, which inhibit FoxO1 (a transcriptional brake on sebocyte lipogenesis) and activate mTORC1 — directly upregulating SREBP-1 and follicular hyperkeratinisation. Gut dysbiosis (depleted *Akkermansia*, *Faecalibacterium*) and chronic stress (cortisol → CRH on sebaceous glands → sebum) amplify the loop. Cohort data link acne severity to HOMA-IR. Targeted by low-glycaemic Siri Dhanyalu millet diet, dairy elimination, intermittent fasting (if appropriate), berberine, inositol (myo + d-chiro 40:1 for PCOS overlap), ashwagandha, Yoga Nidra. **Synthesis:** Without metabolic-gut-HPA repair, the follicular flare returns the moment topicals are stopped.

### 11.0 Self-administered clinical scoring instrument — GAGS self-photographed

**GAGS (Global Acne Grading System)** — six anatomic regions × lesion-type severity (0–4) × regional factor. Total 0–44 (mild ≤18, moderate 19–30, severe 31–38, very severe ≥39). Fully self-administrable.

**Procedure** (every 4 weeks): standardise lighting (5000 K LED or north window at 08:00); distance (30 cm close-ups, 1 m whole-face); 5 fixed views (front, left 45°, right 45°, hairline + forehead, jawline + neck); mid-grey background. Save JPEG + RAW to encrypted cloud (Proton Drive, iCloud E2E). Apps: **Skin AI (UK)**, **Aysa**, **DermAssist (Google)**, **MIRROR** for lesion-count overlay. Manual lesion count: inflammatory (papules + pustules + nodules) + non-inflammatory (open + closed comedones).
