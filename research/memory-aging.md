## 1. Executive Summary

Memory decline with aging is conventionally siloed as either "normal aging" (watch and wait) or, once a diagnostic label crosses the MCI/dementia threshold, as cholinesterase-inhibitor management (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine, memantine) and the new anti-amyloid biologics (lecanemab, donanemab) — none of which arrest the underlying drivers and all of which carry meaningful adverse events. The integrative reframing is different: age-associated memory impairment is the cortical/hippocampal expression of **four convergent root drivers** — (i) **mitochondrial-energetic failure** with NAD⁺ depletion and impaired ATP/PCr turnover, (ii) **chronic neuroinflammation** sustained by microglial priming, vagal-gut-LPS leak, and hyperhomocysteinaemia, (iii) **BDNF / synaptic-plasticity collapse** with reduced hippocampal neurogenesis, and (iv) **cerebrovascular insufficiency** — small-vessel disease, endothelial dysfunction, hypertension, hypoperfusion. A fifth, often-load-bearing driver in late-onset cognitive aging is **insulin resistance of the brain ("Type-3 diabetes")**. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously restores mitochondrial flux, quiets microglia, drives BDNF + neurogenesis, improves cerebral perfusion, and reverses metabolic-brain insulin resistance addresses the upstream drivers — independent of whatever drug class the user may or may not be on.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Mitochondrial energy collapse + NAD⁺ decline.** Brain consumes ~20% of resting cardiac output and ~25% of resting glucose. With age, NAD⁺ declines (CD38↑, NAMPT↓), Complex I + IV throughput drops, mitochondrial DNA damage accumulates, and PGC-1α-driven biogenesis slows. Hippocampal CA1 pyramidal neurons are particularly vulnerable. Targeted by Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR), Creatine 3–5 g, CoQ10/Ubiquinol, PQQ, Nicotinamide Riboside (NR) / NMN, B-vitamin floor, and transcranial photobiomodulation at 810 nm (Vielight Neuro Gamma 40 Hz; Australian/Japanese RCTs in MCI). **Synthesis:** ATP throughput is the upstream brake on the perceived "slowing".

**2.2 Chronic neuroinflammation + microglial priming.** Aged microglia drift to M1, sustaining IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β, complement C1q. Hyperhomocysteinaemia (Hcy >10 µmol/L) accelerates white-matter lesions and cognitive decline (Oxford VITACOG trial). Gut-LPS leakage adds peripheral inflammation. Periodontal *P. gingivalis* infiltration of brain has been mapped (Australian cohort). Targeted by Curcumin (Meriva), Boswellia AKBA, Omega-3 high-DHA, Methyl-B12 + Methyl-folate + B6 (homocysteine-lowering — Oxford VITACOG protocol), oral hygiene (interdental brush, periodontal hygiene), gluten-/seed-oil-free base. **Synthesis:** Quieting microglia + lowering Hcy is a prerequisite for any neurogenic stack to work.

**2.3 BDNF / synaptic-plasticity collapse + hippocampal neurogenesis decline.** Adult human hippocampal neurogenesis persists into the ninth decade (Madrid 2019 cohort, Llorens-Martín lab), but its rate falls with age and falls further in MCI/AD. BDNF and its signalling through TrkB drives neurogenesis, LTP, and dendritic spine maintenance. Targeted by Lion's Mane (*Hericium erinaceus*) hericenones/erinacines (Japanese RCT Mori 2009 in MCI), Bacopa monnieri CDRI-08 (Australian Stough RCTs), saffron (Iranian RCTs in mild-to-moderate AD vs donepezil), Centella asiatica (Mandukaparni — Indian and Australian RCTs), aerobic + resistance exercise (FINGER-style multi-domain trial, Finland), sauna heat-shock (Finnish epidemiology), and caloric restriction-mimetic intermittent fasting where appropriate. **Synthesis:** BDNF is the plasticity currency — memory rebuilds when it rises.

**2.4 Cerebrovascular insufficiency + small-vessel disease + endothelial dysfunction.** White-matter hyperintensities on MRI track cumulative vascular injury — hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidaemia, sedentary living. Cerebral autoregulation falls; endothelium-dependent vasodilation drops; NO bioavailability collapses (eNOS uncoupling, ADMA elevation). Targeted by Ginkgo biloba EGb 761 (German/Chinese RCTs in MCI; EMA HMPC monograph 2015), Vinpocetine (Hungarian/Eastern-European data), dietary nitrate (beetroot 300 mg, leafy greens), Cocoa flavanols (Cocoavia 500 mg flavanols — Italian/UK RCTs Brickman/Mastroiacovo on hippocampal blood flow), Pomegranate, Pycnogenol, *Gou Teng* (Uncaria) in TCM, Zone 2 cardio + strength training, hypertension control (DASH-style + Mg + K). **Synthesis:** Without cerebral perfusion, the other three nodes can't deliver their work to memory networks.

**2.5 (Auxiliary node) Insulin resistance of the brain (Type-3 diabetes).** Hippocampal insulin signaling failure precedes overt amyloid pathology; insulin-degrading enzyme competes with amyloid clearance. T2D doubles dementia risk. Targeted by Berberine, low-glycaemic Mediterranean-millet diet, intermittent fasting (when appropriate), exercise, Mg-glycinate, vitamin D, and weight management. (Coordinated with §3 + §6 + §7.6.)

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers to eliminate

- **Hearing loss left untreated** — single largest modifiable dementia risk factor. Audiogram at 50, 55, 60, 65. OTC hearing aids if loss confirmed.
- **Untreated sleep apnea** — strongly tied to memory decline. Home sleep study (WatchPAT, Lofta) if snoring, witnessed apnoea, AM headaches.
- **Sleep-fragmenting LED blue light after sunset** — f.lux / Iris / Night Shift; blue-blockers after sundown; warm bedroom bulbs ≤2700K.
- **Social isolation** — strong dementia risk factor. Schedule 2–3 social engagements weekly.
- **VOCs / formaldehyde / mould** — ERMI / HERTSMI-2 if water history; HEPA + activated carbon.
- **Endocrine disruptors** (BPA, phthalates) — glass storage, no plastic microwaving.
- **Anticholinergic burden from over-the-counter sleep aids** (diphenhydramine, doxylamine — Benadryl, ZzzQuil, Unisom) — strongly tied to dementia risk; **discontinue chronic use immediately**. Replace with Mg glycinate + Mg L-threonate + L-theanine + Bhramari + Yoga Nidra.
- **Heavy metals** (lead in old paint/pipes, mercury from amalgam + tuna) — urine As/Pb/Hg DTC (Doctor's Data) if suspicious; chelation strictly via qualified integrative practitioner (outside this OTC protocol).
- **Periodontal disease** — interdental brush daily; *P. gingivalis* mapped to brain in Australian cohort; treat actively at dentist.
- **Light pollution at night** — blackout bedroom.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Memory changes with age are not destiny — and they are not "Alzheimer's coming for you." Four things drive cognitive slowing as we age, and most are addressable: (1) the tiny power plants in your brain cells (mitochondria) start running on lower batteries; (2) the immune cells of your brain (microglia) drift into a slow simmer of inflammation, often fueled by gut leakage, gum disease, untreated hearing loss, or chronic stress; (3) your brain makes less of the chemical that grows new connections (BDNF), so learning and memory feel sticky; and (4) blood flow to your thinking parts drops — your prefrontal cortex and hippocampus are literally under-perfused.
