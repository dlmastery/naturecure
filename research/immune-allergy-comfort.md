## 1. Executive Summary

Allergic discomfort is conventionally siloed as "histamine over-release" treated with H1-blockers, intranasal steroids, leukotriene receptor antagonists and (escalation) sub-cutaneous immunotherapy. The integrative reframing: chronic allergic load is the surface expression of a **Th2-skewed immune system on a leaky gut and a vitamin-D-deficient Treg floor**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) IgE-mediated mast-cell degranulation amplified by chronic Th2/IL-4/IL-13 tone, (ii) intestinal-permeability priming with cross-reactive food antigens (oral allergy syndrome), (iii) microbiome dysbiosis with loss of Treg-inducing *Faecalibacterium*/*Akkermansia*/*Bifidobacterium*, and (iv) vitamin-D and quercetin/flavonoid deficiency reducing mast-cell-membrane stability and FoxP3⁺ Treg differentiation. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously stabilises mast cells, seals the gut, restores Treg-inducing flora and replenishes the vitamin-D/flavonoid floor addresses the upstream drivers — not just the histamine endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 IgE-mediated mast-cell degranulation amplified by Th2 tone.** Allergen cross-links FcεRI-bound IgE on mast cells and basophils, triggering Ca²⁺-dependent degranulation — histamine, tryptase, prostaglandin D2, leukotriene C4/D4/E4, PAF — plus de novo Th2 cytokines (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13). Histamine binds H1 on endothelium → vasodilation, plasma leak, itch; H2 on gastric/cardiac tissue; H3/H4 on immune cells. Chronic Th2 polarisation locks the response into a loop. Targeted by quercetin (mast-cell stabiliser), bromelain (synergistic), nettle root (lipoxygenase + H1), vitamin C (histamine N-methyltransferase cofactor), Tinospora cordifolia (Th2 → Th1 modulator), thymoquinone, Sitopaladi Churna. **Synthesis:** Stabilising the mast cell is the immediate symptomatic lever; rebalancing Th1/Th2/Th17/Treg is the upstream cure.

**2.2 Gut-permeability with cross-reactive food antigens (OAS).** Gliadin- and gut-dysbiosis-driven zonulin release opens tight junctions; partially digested food antigens (Bet v 1-homologues in apple, hazelnut, celery, peach for birch-pollen-sensitised users; profilins for grass-pollen users) cross the barrier and cross-react with respiratory allergens. Targeted by gluten-light/elimination diet, L-glutamine, zinc-carnosine, DGL, slippery elm, marshmallow root (mucilaginous demulcent), Akkermansia-supportive polyphenols (pomegranate, cranberry). **Synthesis:** Without gut sealing, mast-cell stabilisers chase a never-emptying tide.

**2.3 Microbiome dysbiosis & Treg insufficiency.** Allergic cohorts show depleted *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Bifidobacterium longum*, *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* and reduced butyrate. SCFAs (butyrate, propionate) signal GPR43/109A on colonic Tregs → FoxP3⁺ Treg expansion that suppresses Th2. Targeted by sprouted millets (fibre substrate), fermented foods (kanji, sauerkraut, kefir, dahi), multi-strain probiotic with documented allergy strains (*L. rhamnosus* GG, *B. longum* BB536, *L. paracasei* LP-33). **Synthesis:** SCFA recovery is the Treg lever; without it the Th2 loop self-reinforces.

**2.4 Vitamin-D / flavonoid floor deficiency.** 25-OH-D <30 ng/mL correlates with allergic-rhinitis severity, asthma exacerbations and reduced Treg counts in multiple non-US cohorts. Dietary flavonoids (quercetin, luteolin, kaempferol, apigenin) stabilise mast-cell membranes; modern diets rarely deliver >50 mg/day. Targeted by Vitamin D3 + K2 titrated to 50–70 ng/mL, quercetin 500–1000 mg/day, dietary onions/capers/apples/parsley, Ashwagandha for HPA balance (cortisol depresses Tregs at chronic excess). **Synthesis:** Without the D/Treg floor, every other intervention regresses each pollen season.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

**ImmuneOral domain — every device is consumer-purchasable and self-administrable. NO clinic immunotherapy injection, NO oral mucosal challenge in clinic.**

**5.1 Steam inhalation with eucalyptus / peppermint / tulsi.** Boil 1 L water; off heat; add 3 drops eucalyptus + 2 drops peppermint or 1 tsp tulsi leaves; towel over head; inhale 5–10 min, 2×/day during flare. Mucolytic + decongestant + mild antimicrobial. Contraindication: asthma + menthol sensitivity (skip peppermint); under 6 years (burn risk).

**5.2 Saline nasal irrigation (Neti pot / squeeze bottle).** Lukewarm sterile saline (1/4 tsp non-iodised salt + pinch baking soda in 240 ml previously-boiled water). Daily AM during season; BID during flare. NeilMed, Himalayan Institute Neti pot. **Critical: distilled or previously-boiled water only — never raw tap (rare amoebic risk).**

**5.4 Home infrared sauna** (Sunlighten, JNH, Clearlight) — 50–60 °C, 20 min, 3×/week. Heat-shock protein 70 induction; sweat-mediated phthalate/BPA elimination. Contraindication: significant CV disease, pregnancy.

**5.5 Home cold immersion** — 10–15 °C, 2–4 min, 3×/week. Vagal tone + anti-inflammatory. Contraindication: cold urticaria; significant cardiac disease.

**5.6 HEPA air purification + dehumidifier.** Bedroom HEPA 13/14 (Coway Mighty, IQAir HealthPro, Dyson HP). Dehumidifier to maintain 40–50% RH (reduces dust-mite/mould burden). Mattress + pillow allergen-encasement covers (Allerease, Mission Allergy).

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers to eliminate

- **Indoor allergens**: dust mites (allergen-encase mattress + pillow; wash bedding 60 °C weekly), mould (dehumidify to 40–50% RH; ERMI test if suspect), pet dander (HEPA bedroom; pet out of bedroom).
- **Outdoor allergens**: pollen calendar awareness (birch, grass, ragweed, Parthenium in India); shower + change clothes on returning home; sunglasses; mask outdoors during peak.
- **Indoor air**: VOCs/formaldehyde (new furniture off-gas 2 weeks + activated carbon); fragranced candles, plug-ins, incense (mast-cell triggers).
- **Personal care swap**: fragrance-free, dye-free shampoo/soap/laundry detergent (Seventh Generation, Attitude, Ecover). Phthalate/paraben-free.
- **Water**: shower filter (chlorine/chloramine → mast-cell trigger in sensitive users).
- **Occupational**: chemical fumes, latex (latex-fruit cross-reactivity), flour dust (baker's asthma).
- **EMF**: not primary; tightly controlled blue-light exposure protects sleep → Treg.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Allergies are not "just a histamine reaction". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) immune cells called mast cells are easily triggered by pollen, dust or food fragments and release histamine and other inflammatory chemicals — like an over-sensitive smoke alarm; (2) your gut wall has small gaps that let food fragments through, which cross-react with airborne pollen; (3) the friendly bacteria that calm the immune system are depleted; and (4) low vitamin D and low dietary flavonoids leave the immune "brake" (Treg cells) under-staffed.

This protocol does not just block histamine after it has already been released. It stabilises the mast cell from the outside (quercetin, bromelain, nettle, vitamin C), heals the gut wall (gluten reduction, fermented foods, marshmallow/slippery elm), restores the calming bacteria (multi-strain probiotic, sprouted millets, fermented vegetables), and rebuilds vitamin D + flavonoids.
