> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home nutrition and recovery plan for the whole journey — preconception through pregnancy (trimester by trimester) into the immediate postpartum (the first 6 weeks), the "fourth trimester" (6 weeks to 12 months), and lactation. We lead with the highest-evidence levers: folate and methyl-B12 before conception, iodine and DHA across pregnancy, calcium for pre-eclampsia prevention in low-intake settings, the Mediterranean pattern that lowers gestational diabetes risk, daily moderate exercise, pelvic-floor training throughout, and weekly self-administered EPDS screening for postpartum depression during the most vulnerable first 12 weeks. We are honest where the science is mixed — vitamin C and E do NOT prevent pre-eclampsia (Roberts 2010 NEJM null) and home fetal-doppler carries real anxiety and false-reassurance risk. *(WHO Antenatal Care 2016; Cochrane De-Regil folate 2015 A-grade; PRIDE Mediterranean trial Assaf-Balut 2017 A-grade; Davenport 2018 exercise A-grade; Hofmeyr 2018 Cochrane calcium A-grade in low-calcium populations; Cox 1987 EPDS validation)*
>

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What this dossier covers

> **For you, in plain words.** A single integrated nutrition plan that spans preconception, three trimesters, immediate postpartum, the fourth trimester, and lactation. Each phase has its own substrate needs, mental-health risk pattern, and red-flag list. *(WHO Antenatal Care 2016; NICE perinatal mental health 2014)*

### 1.2 The four phases at a glance

> **Quick start.** **Preconception (3+ months):** methylfolate 800 µg + methyl-B12 + iron if ferritin <30 + choline 450 mg + DHA 300 mg + vitamin D 4000 IU + alcohol zero, smoking zero. **Pregnancy:** Mediterranean pattern + 30-min daily moderate exercise + pelvic-floor training + iodine 250 µg + calcium 1000 mg food-first + home BP twice weekly from week 20 + low-dose aspirin 75-150 mg from week 12 ONLY IF high pre-eclampsia risk under §10.7.3. **Postpartum 0-6 weeks:** weekly EPDS, perineal recovery, lactation establishment, iron repletion if blood loss high, continued prenatal multi. **4th trimester (6 weeks-12 mo):** continued EPDS until week 12 then PHQ-9 monthly, pelvic-floor PT if any incontinence, postpartum thyroid screen at 6 weeks + 6 months. *(Cochrane De-Regil 2015; PRIDE Assaf-Balut 2017; Davenport 2018; Boyle 2012 Cochrane pelvic floor; Hofmeyr 2018 calcium; NICE aspirin pre-eclampsia)*

### 1.3 Why integrated, not siloed

The perinatal year is a **multi-system recalibration of seven phase-specific demands** — (i) preconception substrate (folate + methyl-B12 + iron + D + TSH <2.5 per Negro 2007); (ii) T1 (organogenesis + NVP + folate + choline); (iii) T2 (iron + iodine for fetal IQ + calcium + anatomy scan); (iv) T3 (DHA + calcium + pre-eclampsia surveillance + GDM screen + fetal-movement); (v) labour prep (pelvic-floor + perineal massage + dates wk 36); (vi) postpartum 0-6 wk (lochia + lactation + EPDS + mastitis prevention); (vii) 4th trimester + lactation 6 wk-12 mo (pelvic-floor remediation + postpartum thyroiditis + PPD + lactation). **Synthesis.** Meeting the substrate floor each phase + screening proactively for GDM, pre-eclampsia, postpartum thyroiditis, PPD + red-flag routing gives families the best chance of an uncomplicated journey.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (7-node phase model)

### 2.1 Seven phases — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Each of the seven phases has its own dominant nutrient, hormone and risk pattern. Naming them lets every meal, supplement and home test aim at the right one. *(WHO ANC 2016; Stephens 2015 protein reanalysis)*

### 2.2 Preconception (3+ months)

> **For you, in plain words.** Eggs take 90 days to mature, sperm take 74 days. The 3+ month window before conception is the highest-leverage time — folate, iodine, B12, iron, D3, choline, omega-3 DHA, alcohol zero, smoking zero, weight optimisation, TSH <2.5. *(Cochrane De-Regil folate 2015 A-grade NTD; Negro 2007 thyroid + fertility)*

**Quick start.** Methylfolate 800 µg + methyl-B12 1000 µg + iron 18 mg (only if ferritin <30 ng/mL on DTC) + vitamin D3 4000 IU + choline 450 mg + DHA 300 mg + iodine 150-250 µg + alcohol zero + smoking zero + BMI 19-30 target.

#### Open the science

Cochrane De-Regil 2015 (n=7 RCTs, n=18,891) — periconceptional folate reduces NTD ~70%; WHO recommends 400 µg/d from 3 months preconception (800 µg in many syntheses; 4 mg/d under specialist if prior NTD). Methylfolate (5-MTHF) preferred over folic acid in MTHFR-variant carriers (~30-40% of population). Methyl-B12 1000 µg sublingual for vegetarians/vegans (Refsum 2001). Iron 18 mg only if ferritin <30 (Bothwell 2000). Vitamin D 4000 IU if 25-OH-D <50 nmol/L (Roth 2017 Cochrane). Choline 450 mg (Caudill 2018 — child cognitive at 7y). Iodine 250 µg pregnancy + 290 µg lactation (WHO 2007; Bath 2018 — child IQ). DHA ≥200-300 mg (Carlson 2013, Khalil 2024). Subclinical hypothyroid TSH >2.5 raises miscarriage (Negro 2007 RCT) — DTC preconception thyroid screen.

### 2.3 First trimester (weeks 1-13)

> **For you, in plain words.** Organogenesis happens in the first trimester — every nutrient matters. Hyperemesis (severe morning sickness) needs ginger, B6, acupressure bands; if dehydration or weight loss >5% — clinic. *(Cochrane Matthews 2015 ginger for NVP; ACOG global synthesis acknowledged; SOGC guidance)*

**Quick start.** Continue full preconception stack. Add ginger 250 mg QID for nausea (or 1 tsp fresh grated in hot water) + vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) 25 mg TID + Sea-Band acupressure wristbands + small frequent dry meals + crackers before rising. STOP curcumin >500 mg/day, Vitex, ashokarishta, kapikacchu high-dose, shilajit (resume after week 14 if needed).

#### Open the science

T1 organogenesis means every teratogen exposure (alcohol, isotretinoin, valproate, warfarin, ACE inhibitors, lithium) and nutrient deficit has maximum impact. NVP 70-85% of pregnancies; hyperemesis gravidarum 0.3-3%. Cochrane Matthews 2015 (n=41 trials) — ginger improves NVP. B6 pyridoxine 10-25 mg TID — A-grade per Cochrane. P6 acupressure wristbands (Sea-Band) modest signal. HG with weight loss >5% + ketonuria + dehydration = clinic-route (IV fluids, doxylamine-pyridoxine Diclectin). Caffeine ≤200 mg/d (Cnattingius 2000). Avoid all alcohol — T1 especially.

### 2.4 Second trimester (weeks 14-27)

> **For you, in plain words.** Blood volume rises 45% — iron demand peaks. Iodine for fetal thyroid + brain. Calcium accumulation. Anatomy scan typically 18-22 weeks. Start home BP monitoring twice weekly from week 20. *(Bothwell 2000 iron; Bath 2018 iodine + IQ; Hofmeyr 2018 Cochrane calcium for pre-eclampsia)*

**Quick start.** Iron 27 mg/day (or 60-120 mg if ferritin <30 — under DTC monitoring) + iodine 250 µg + calcium 1000 mg (food-first: 200 mL yogurt + 30 g sesame + 100 g leafy greens + small fish bones via sardines = ~700 mg; supplement gap if <800 mg dietary) + DHA 300 mg minimum + home BP cuff (Omron M3, Withings BPM Core) twice weekly + 30-min daily moderate exercise.

#### Open the science

Maternal blood volume expands ~45%; iron demand rises (Bothwell 2000 — 1000 mg total elemental iron needed). Anaemia Hb <110 g/L raises adverse outcomes; ferritin <30 = depleted. Iodine deficiency leading preventable cause of intellectual disability (WHO 2007; Bath 2018 — child IQ). Calcium 1000-1200 mg pregnancy (food-first); Hofmeyr 2018 Cochrane (n=27 trials) — halves pre-eclampsia in low-calcium-intake populations (<900 mg/d). DHA accretion begins T2 (Carlson 2013, Innis 2008). Home BP from wk 20 — sustained ≥140/90 = gestational hypertension; >160/110 = severe + urgent.

### 2.5 Third trimester (weeks 28-40)

> **For you, in plain words.** DHA accretion peaks for fetal brain. Pre-eclampsia surveillance intensifies. GDM screening 24-28 weeks. Fetal-movement counting from 28 weeks. Pelvic-floor and perineal preparation. Dates from week 36 may shorten labour. *(Carlson 2013 DHA; Hofmeyr 2018 calcium; Al-Kuran 2011 dates; PRIDE Assaf-Balut 2017 GDM-reduction A-grade)*

**Quick start.** DHA 600 mg + calcium 1000 mg + iron continue + home BP twice weekly + home urine dipstick (protein/ketones) weekly + count fetal movements daily (target ≥10 movements over 2 hours when baby is active) + pelvic-floor 3×/day (Boyle 2012 Cochrane) + perineal massage from week 34 (Beckmann 2013 Cochrane) + 6-7 dates daily from week 36 (Al-Kuran small trial signal).

#### Open the science

T3 DHA accretion maximal (Innis 2008). PRIDE Assaf-Balut 2017 (Spanish RCT n=874) — Mediterranean diet + EVOO + pistachios reduced GDM by 25%. Davenport 2018 (Br J Sports Med systematic) — 30+ min/d moderate exercise reduced GDM + GHTN. Boyle 2012 Cochrane (n=22 trials) — antenatal pelvic-floor reduced postnatal incontinence ~30%. Beckmann 2013 Cochrane — perineal massage from wk 34 reduced trauma. Al-Kuran 2011 (n=69) — 6 dates daily from wk 36 → shorter labour. Fetal-movement counting from 28 wk — reduced movement = urgent obstetric same-day. GDM screen 24-28 wk via OGTT or HbA1c. Pre-eclampsia high-risk (prior pre-eclampsia, chronic HTN, diabetes, autoimmune, twin, BMI ≥35, first pregnancy ≥40, family history) — NICE low-dose aspirin 75-150 mg wk 12-36 clinic-route §10.7.3.

### 2.6 Labour preparation + immediate postpartum (0-6 weeks)

> **For you, in plain words.** Labour: breath training, hydration, support partner present, hospital bag ready by week 36. Immediate postpartum: lochia (bleeding) up to 6 weeks, perineal recovery, lactation establishment, EPDS weekly screen, baby-blues vs PPD differentiation. *(Hodnett 2013 Cochrane continuous support; Cox 1987 EPDS validation)*

**Quick start.** Hospital bag week 36 with snacks (dates, energy bars, electrolyte drinks), nursing pads, peri-bottle, large pads, comfortable nursing bras. Continuous labour support (doula, partner, midwife) — Hodnett 2013 Cochrane A-grade outcome. Postpartum: weekly self-administered EPDS from week 1 to week 12 (any score ≥10 prompts GP review; ≥13 strongly suggests PPD; any positive response to suicide question prompts urgent psychiatric review). Iron continued if blood loss high (typical vaginal birth 200-500 mL; check Hb at 6 weeks if symptoms of anaemia). Perineal recovery: ice packs first 24h, sitz baths from day 2, witch hazel pads, peri-bottle for hygiene, ibuprofen OTC short-course (lactation-compatible per LactMed). Lactation: skin-to-skin first hour, frequent latching (8-12 times/24h), IBCLC referral if any painful latch, low supply concern, or infant weight loss >7%.

#### Open the science

Hodnett 2013 Cochrane (n=22 trials, n=15,288) — continuous labour support reduced caesarean + instrumental birth + improved Apgar. Cox 1987 (EPDS validation n=84) — gold-standard self-administered PPD screen globally; 10 items, cut-off 10 = further assessment, 13 = PPD likely. Baby blues (50-80%, peaks day 3-5, resolves by 2 wk) distinct from PPD (persists >2 wk, may include intrusive thoughts). Mastitis prevention: frequent feeding + complete drainage + non-constrictive bras + lanolin + air-drying + correct latch. Early treatment: continued feeding affected side + warm-before/cold-after + ibuprofen + rest. Mastitis + fever + abscess = urgent.

### 2.7 Fourth trimester (6 weeks - 12 months) + lactation

> **For you, in plain words.** The "fourth trimester" is when pelvic-floor remediation, postpartum thyroid screen, return-to-cycle decisions, and the marathon of nightly feeds happen. PPD risk extends to 12 months; postpartum psychosis is rare but urgent. *(Berle 2013 lactation-safe SSRIs; O'Mahen 2014 + Sockol 2015 CBT-PPD meta A-grade)*

**Quick start.** Continue prenatal multi while lactating + DHA 300 mg + vitamin D3 4000 IU + iodine 290 µg + iron if ferritin still low + calcium 1000 mg + 2-3 L water/day + monthly EPDS from week 12 to month 12 + GP postpartum thyroid panel at 6 weeks and 6 months + pelvic-floor PT if any incontinence/heaviness/dyspareunia + lactation IBCLC if any feeding concerns.

#### Open the science

Postpartum thyroiditis ~5-10% in first year; classically biphasic; often missed — DTC TSH + fT4 + anti-TPO at 6 wk + 6 mo. PPD lifetime ~10-15% per Cox-validated EPDS cohorts. Behavioural first: CBT (O'Mahen 2014 + Sockol 2015 meta A-grade), MBSR (Perez-Blasco 2013 — mood + breastfeeding adherence). If insufficient: sertraline lactation-compatible first-line (Berle 2013 + Hale 2019 — minimal infant exposure). LactMed (Health Canada) is the lactation drug-safety gold-standard. Pelvic-floor dysfunction ~30% at 3 mo; PT effective (Bo 2007 + Wallace 2019 Cochrane). Return to cycle: 6-9 mo if fully breastfeeding, 6-8 wk if not.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Ten over-the-counter molecules across the perinatal year — nine pregnancy-safe and one preconception-only (Vitex). Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest null result for vitamin C + E in pre-eclampsia prevention. *(EMA monographs; Cochrane De-Regil 2015; Hofmeyr 2018; Roberts 2010 NEJM)*

### 3.2 The honest pre-eclampsia caveat (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Despite mechanistic plausibility, vitamin C 1000 mg + vitamin E 400 IU did NOT prevent pre-eclampsia in the large Roberts 2010 NEJM trial (n=10,154). Calcium DOES help — but only in low-calcium-intake populations. Low-dose aspirin 75-150 mg from week 12-36 in high-risk women is A-grade per NICE (clinic-route §10.7.3). *(Roberts 2010 NEJM; Hofmeyr 2018 Cochrane calcium; LeFevre 2014 aspirin review; NICE)*

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six traditions developed sophisticated pregnancy + postpartum care centuries ago. Ayurveda's *Garbhini Paricharya* (pregnancy regimen) and *Sutika Paricharya* (postpartum care), TCM's *Yue Zi* (sitting-the-month), Unani's pregnancy/postpartum protocols, Siddha women's tradition, Tibetan postnatal care, and Homeopathic remedies. We honour them — and surface honestly where evidence is sparse. *(Charaka Sharira Sthana; Kashyapa Samhita; Fu Qing Zhu Nü Ke; Avicenna Book III)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Home BP cuff

> **For you, in plain words.** A validated home BP cuff (Omron M3, Withings BPM Core) twice weekly from week 20 catches pre-eclampsia early. Goal <140/90 sustained.

**Quick start.** Buy Omron M3 or Withings BPM Core. Measure seated, rested 5 min, twice in succession (use the second reading), twice weekly from week 20, daily if any reading ≥140/90.

#### Open the science

Home BP monitoring in pregnancy is validated (Tucker 2017 BUMP trial UK + many cohort studies). Sustained ≥140/90 = gestational hypertension. ≥160/110 = severe — emergency. Pre-eclampsia = hypertension + proteinuria after week 20 — proteinuria detectable on home urine dipstick (Siemens Multistix, Roche Combur).

### 5.2 Home glucose / CGM

> **For you, in plain words.** Home glucose meter (Contour Next, Accu-Chek) or CGM (Stelo, Libre 3) helps track GDM and dietary response. *(Hewapathirana 2024 CGM pregnancy review)*

GDM screening at 24-28 weeks via OGTT or HbA1c. CGM increasingly accessible (Stelo OTC) — useful for adjusting carb intake response.

### 5.3 Home urine dipsticks

> **For you, in plain words.** Multistix or Combur strips for weekly protein + ketones check in third trimester. Cheap, fast, catches pre-eclampsia early.

### 5.4 Home fetal-doppler — honest caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Consumer fetal-dopplers carry real risk of false reassurance + anxiety. Many obstetricians counsel against home use. **Reduced fetal movement is the actionable signal — not absent doppler tones.** *(RCOG fetal movement guidance — global synthesis)*

If you use one, do NOT use it instead of monitoring fetal movement after 28 weeks. Reduced movement = urgent obstetric review same-day, NOT "I heard a heartbeat at home so it's fine."

### 5.5 Home pelvic-floor trainers

> **For you, in plain words.** Elvie Trainer + Kegel exercises are evidence-based for prevention and treatment of postpartum incontinence. *(Boyle 2012 Cochrane antenatal; Wallace 2019 Cochrane postnatal)*

Elvie Trainer biofeedback device with phone app, or unguided Kegels 3×/day (3 sets of 10 contractions, 10-sec holds). Boyle 2012 — antenatal pelvic-floor training prevents postnatal incontinence.

### 5.6 EPDS self-administered weekly

> **For you, in plain words.** Print the EPDS from any reputable source (NHS, WHO, NICE perinatal mental health). Self-score weekly weeks 1-12 postpartum, monthly weeks 13-52. Score ≥10 — GP review; ≥13 — strongly suggests PPD; any positive on suicide question — urgent psychiatric. *(Cox 1987)*

### 5.7 Lactation tracking apps + LactMed

> **For you, in plain words.** Huckleberry, BabyConnect, Glow Baby track feeds + sleep + nappies. LactMed database (Health Canada-accessible) is the gold-standard for any drug + breastfeeding safety check.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods (daily)

> **For you, in plain words.** Mediterranean + Indian-tempered pattern with extra emphasis on folate-rich greens, iodine sources, calcium from yogurt + small fish + sesame, omega-3 DHA from fatty fish, choline from eggs, iron from lentils + small red meat. *(PRIDE Assaf-Balut 2017; Bath 2018 iodine; Caudill 2018 choline)*

#### Open the science

- 150-200 g folate-rich greens (spinach, kale, methi, amaranth); 2 whole eggs (choline ~250 mg); 120-160 g low-mercury oily fish 3×/week (sardines, mackerel, wild salmon, herring); 200 mL plain yogurt (calcium ~250 mg); 30 g sesame (calcium ~280 mg); 150 g lentils/beans; 2 cups berries weekly; 30 g nuts (walnut + 2 Brazil nuts for selenium); 1-2 tbsp ghee + olive; tempering of mustard/cumin/ginger/turmeric; 2-3 cups bone broth/week; iodised salt OR seaweed 1×/week; 2-3 L water.

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Alcohol, smoking, high-mercury fish, raw fish/cheese/meat/eggs, liver excess, high-dose vitamin A, willow bark, blue cohosh, pennyroyal, mugwort, St John's Wort, high-dose caffeine.

#### Open the science

Alcohol (FASD); smoking + vaping (IUGR + preterm + SIDS); high-mercury fish (king mackerel, swordfish, shark, tilefish, bigeye tuna); raw fish/cheese/deli meat/unpasteurised milk (listeria + toxoplasmosis + salmonella); liver >50 g/week (retinol teratogen); vit A >10,000 IU (teratogen); willow bark + aspirin (unless prescribed); blue cohosh + pennyroyal + mugwort + rue + tansy (uterine/fetotoxic); St John's Wort (untested); caffeine >200 mg/day (Cnattingius 2000); industrial seed oils + trans fats + ultraprocessed + refined sugar.

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

> **For you, in plain words.** Cook to kill pathogens, sprout to release nutrients, ferment for probiotic + B12, slow-cook bone broth for collagen.

- All meats + fish cooked thoroughly (no rare).
- Eggs cooked until both yolk + white firm (or use pasteurised liquid).
- Wash all produce well; peel where possible.
- Sprout mung + chickpea + methi 8-10 h soak + 8-12 h germinate.
- Ferment yogurt, kanji, dosa, idli batter.
- Bone broth 24-48 h slow simmer with apple-cider vinegar.

#### 10.7.3 Clinic-route options (if home protocol plateaus or for medical indication)

### 13.5 Patient-facing primer (load-bearing)

Pregnancy and the postpartum year are not a medical condition — they are a normal physiological transition that benefits from a sound nutritional plan, careful screening for the few conditions that genuinely need clinical intervention (pre-eclampsia, GDM, PPD, postpartum thyroiditis), and clear escalation routing for true emergencies. This dossier gives you the OTC substrate floor (methylfolate, methyl-B12, iron only if depleted, DHA, choline, vitamin D, iodine, calcium food-first), home-monitoring tools (home BP, urine dipsticks, fetal-movement counting from wk 28, EPDS weekly), and A-grade behavioural cornerstones (Mediterranean diet, 30-min daily exercise, pelvic-floor training, perineal massage, continuous labour support, CBT/MBSR perinatal). Red flags (severe pre-eclampsia, postpartum psychosis, suicidal ideation, mastitis with abscess, haemorrhage, DVT/PE) need urgent care. You are sovereign — keep an obstetrician/midwife/GP in the loop if you choose; the protocol does not require it.

## 14. Closing paradigm
