## 1. Executive Summary

> **TL;DR.** Ringing in the ears is rarely "just nerve damage" — it has four causes you can work on at home. This section sets up the four-part picture the rest of the dossier acts on. *(Eggermont & Roberts 2004 review; Levine 1999 somatic-tinnitus study)*

Tinnitus is usually shrugged off as "incurable nerve damage" or, in severe cases, handled with off-label nerve drugs (gabapentin, tricyclics, benzodiazepines) — none of which fix the cause and all of which carry side-effects and dependence. A more honest reframe: long-term ringing is the perceived output of **four overlapping drivers** — (i) **inner-ear hair-cell damage** from noise, ear-toxic drugs and aging, (ii) **brain "volume turn-up"** in the hearing pathway when ear input drops (the phantom-limb effect of the ear), (iii) **poor blood flow** through the single tiny artery feeding the inner ear, and (iv) **neck, jaw, vagus-nerve and sleep imbalances** that amplify how loud the ringing *feels*. **Synthesis:** A natural OTC home-based protocol that protects hair cells, calms brain over-amplification, restores inner-ear blood flow and resets the neck-jaw-vagus loop hits all four drivers in parallel, independent of any drug class the user may already be on.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

> **TL;DR.** Four causes drive chronic ringing — inner-ear damage, brain "volume up," poor blood flow to the ear, and neck-jaw-vagus inputs. Naming them lets every food and supplement aim at the right one. *(Kujawa & Liberman 2009; Eggermont & Roberts 2004; Levine 1999)*

**2.1 Inner-ear damage from oxidative stress + over-fired nerves.**
> **TL;DR.** Loud noise, certain drugs and aging burn through the inner ear's natural antioxidants and over-fire the hearing nerve. Protecting hair cells is the upstream brake. *(Kujawa & Liberman 2009, Journal of Neuroscience)*

Loud noise, ear-toxic drugs (loop diuretics, certain antibiotics, cisplatin, chronic painkillers/aspirin) and aging deplete the inner ear's glutathione (the cell's main antioxidant), raise toxic oxygen species in outer hair cells, and over-fire the hearing nerve at its synapse — the "synaptopathy" model. Targeted by **N-acetyl-cysteine**, **magnesium** (Israeli army study, Attias 1994), **zinc** (Turkish study, Arda 2003, only in zinc-deficient people), **CoQ10**, **R-alpha-lipoic acid**, **methyl-B12** (Israeli study, Shemesh 1993), and by simply avoiding ototoxic exposure. **Synthesis:** Protecting hair cells is the upstream brake.

**2.2 Brain "volume turn-up" in the hearing pathway.**
> **TL;DR.** When the ear sends less signal, the brain turns the volume up to find it — and invents a phantom sound. Habituation, not drugs, is the durable fix. *(Eggermont & Roberts 2004; Cima 2012 mindfulness trial, UK Bristol)*

When the ear delivers a weaker signal, the brain's hearing relay-stations turn the gain up — and in doing so create a phantom signal. A specific cell type in the brainstem (fusiform cells) blends ear input with neck and face-nerve input, which is why neck/jaw movements often change the ringing. Targeted by **sound therapy** (broadband, notch — Pantev & Okamoto 2010 + the Hannover Tinnitracks app; fractal tones — ReSound, Oticon), **the Jastreboff retraining therapy**, **mindfulness for tinnitus** (UK Bristol Cima/McKenna), **Lion's Mane mushroom**, and **Bhramari humming breath**. **Synthesis:** Habituation, not drugs, is the durable lever.

**2.3 Poor blood flow to the inner ear.**
> **TL;DR.** The inner ear is fed by one tiny artery with no backups. High blood pressure, smoking and stiff blood vessels starve it. *(EMA Ginkgo herbal monograph 2015; Italian Belcaro 2014 Pycnogenol trial)*

The inner ear is one of the body's most energy-hungry tissues per gram, and its blood supply runs through a single end-artery (the labyrinthine artery, off the AICA in the brainstem) with no backups. High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking and a stiff inner lining of blood vessels (less nitric-oxide signalling) starve it. Targeted by **standardised Ginkgo (Tebonin brand)** (German trials Drewelow 2013, Procháska 2009 n=180; EMA herbal monograph 2015), **Pycnogenol pine-bark extract** (Italian Pescara trial, Belcaro 2014/2017), **Vinpocetine** (Hungarian Cavinton data), **L-arginine + L-citrulline**, **beetroot nitrate**, **cocoa flavanols**, and easy cardio (the "Zone 2" pace where you can still talk). **Synthesis:** Without inner-ear blood flow, nothing else delivers.

**2.4 Neck-jaw-vagus-sleep loop.**
> **TL;DR.** In about 4 out of 10 people, the ringing changes when they move their neck or jaw. Fixing those inputs often gives the fastest relief. *(Levine 1999, American Journal of Otology; Sanchez & Rocha 2011 review)*

Up to 4 in 10 tinnitus cases are "somatically modulable" — the pitch or loudness changes with jaw clenching, temple pressure, neck rotation or touch on the upper neck (C1-C3). Low vagal-nerve tone (poor heart-rate variability) and a stressed-out cortisol system amplify how loud it feels; broken sleep keeps the loop going. Targeted by **neck mobility + sub-occipital release**, **jaw release + a night-guard for grinding**, **at-home cervical traction** (Saunders over-the-door unit), **heart-rate-variability training** (HeartMath, Sensate), **side-sleeping with the unaffected ear up**, **Yoga Nidra**, **Bhramari + alternate-nostril breathing**. **Synthesis:** The neck-jaw-vagus lever often gives the largest drop in the Tinnitus Functional Index and is the lever most sufferers have never been offered.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

> **TL;DR.** Ear-ringing has four causes — inner-ear damage, brain "volume up", poor inner-ear blood flow, and a neck-jaw-vagus loop. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home; annoyance lifts in weeks, loudness in months. *(Cima 2012; Levine 1999; EMA Ginkgo)*

Ear-ringing is not "damage you have to live with" — and it is not all in your head. Four things drive it: (1) tiny hair cells in your inner ear get over-fired by noise, some medicines, or aging; (2) when those cells weaken, your brain turns the volume up to "find" the missing signal — and creates a phantom one; (3) the inner ear is fed by one tiny artery that gets squeezed by high blood pressure, smoking, or stress; (4) your jaw, neck, the calming vagus nerve and your sleep all feed the same brainstem switchboard that decides how loud your tinnitus *feels*.

This protocol does **not** chase tinnitus with off-label nerve drugs (gabapentin, tricyclics, benzodiazepines) or with Lipoflavonoid. It rebuilds each of the four layers — quiet background sound (for the brain "volume up" lever), supplements that protect the hair cells and improve inner-ear blood flow (Tebonin Ginkgo, Pycnogenol pine bark, NAC, magnesium, B12 if low, zinc if low), home red-light at the bone behind your ear, neck mobility + jaw release if those are part of your picture, OTC hearing aids if a hearing test shows loss, humming-bee breath twice a day, a low-salt MIND-millet way of eating, and disciplined sleep with a low-volume bedside sound machine. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.**

Expect a slow, layered shift. **Annoyance** lifts first (weeks 2-4), not loudness. By weeks 8-12 most users' self-score drops by 13 points or more — a meaningful change. Real habituation is a 4-to-9-month story. Neck-driven tinnitus moves fastest; decades-old brain-amplification tinnitus moves slowest — but it does move.

**One exception.** If your tinnitus *pulses with your heartbeat*, that is a blood-vessel sign — arrange imaging on your own initiative.
