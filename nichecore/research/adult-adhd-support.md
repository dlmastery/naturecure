> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for adult ADHD — surging in diagnosis, real, and chronically under-supported. It is an **adjunctive** plan: it does not replace formal psychiatric assessment if you suspect ADHD, and it does not replace stimulant medication if your prescriber has put you on one. Lead with the boring high-evidence levers: cognitive-behavioural therapy adapted for adult ADHD, exercise, sleep, iron + omega-3 measurement, executive-function scaffolding. *(WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034; Safren 2010 JAMA CBT-adult-ADHD A-grade; Bloch 2011 omega-3 ADHD meta)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO TCIM, India's AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS), EMA HMPC monographs, Australia TGA, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, plus classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator or US disease association is cited as authority.**
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every supplement, food, breath practice and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No prescription, no clinic visit, no hospital admission is in the home regime.** Stimulants (lisdexamfetamine/Vyvanse, mixed amphetamine salts/Adderall, methylphenidate/Concerta/Ritalin/Dexedrine), non-stimulants (atomoxetine/Strattera, guanfacine/Intuniv, clonidine), and adjunct off-label agents (bupropion, modafinil) are placed in §10.7.3 as referral context, never inside the daily protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users **already** on those medicines can see how this protocol interacts.
>
> **Red flag — suicidal ideation, untreated bipolar, or active substance use.** If you have suicidal thoughts, especially combined with untreated ADHD, that is a psychiatric urgency — contact crisis services on your own initiative. If you have a history of bipolar mania, a stimulant trial without a mood stabiliser in place can precipitate mania — formal psychiatric evaluation first. If you are self-medicating with cocaine, amphetamines of abuse, or unprescribed stimulants, substance-use treatment first; this protocol is not a stimulant replacement.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What adult ADHD actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Adult ADHD is not "trouble paying attention" — it is a 24-hour disorder of executive function, dopamine signalling, sleep, and emotional regulation, with high comorbidity with anxiety, depression, sleep dysregulation, iron deficiency in women, and substance use. *(Faraone 2021 World Federation; Polanczyk 2014 epidemiology)*

### 1.2 The four root drivers

> **Quick start.** Run an ASRS-v1.1 self-screen tonight; book a DTC ferritin + iron-panel + omega-3-index + 25-OH vit D this week (aim ferritin >50, omega-3 index >8%, vit D 40-60); start a fixed sleep window 23:00-07:00 with phone OUT of the bedroom; build an externalised task scaffold (Todoist or Notion ADHD-template); start one 30-min daily aerobic block; cut alcohol and screens before bed. *(Safren 2010 CBT-adult-ADHD A-grade; Cortese 2012 iron ADHD)*

### 1.3 Why "adjunctive," not "alternative"

Adult ADHD is best framed as four interlocking root drivers — (i) **catecholamine signalling dysfunction** — dopamine and noradrenaline in the prefrontal cortex are the targets of stimulants and atomoxetine, but they also respond to nutrition, exercise and certain plants; (ii) **executive dysfunction + working-memory deficit** — the part that runs planning, sequencing, time-estimation, task initiation; (iii) **sleep + circadian dysregulation** — delayed sleep phase is so common in ADHD it's almost diagnostic; sleep deprivation looks identical to ADHD symptoms; and (iv) **emotional dysregulation + rejection-sensitive dysphoria (RSD)** — the under-recognised emotional component that drives much adult ADHD suffering. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs CBT-adapted-for-ADHD, structured exercise, sleep optimisation, executive-function scaffolding, iron + omega-3 + vitamin D repletion, and a small graded supplement layer — sits **alongside** whichever conventional treatment your prescriber may have set, never against it.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers — catecholamine signalling, executive dysfunction, sleep dysregulation, and emotional dysregulation — explain why adult ADHD is more than a "focus problem." Naming each lets every food, exercise and supplement aim at the right one. *(Faraone 2021; Cortese 2017 Lancet Psychiatry)*

### 2.2 Catecholamine signalling

> **For you, in plain words.** ADHD is, in large part, a disorder of how the brain handles dopamine and noradrenaline in its planning regions. That's why stimulants help. Some foods and supplements support the same chemistry, less powerfully. *(Volkow 2009 dopamine ADHD; Faraone 2018)*

**The deeper layer.** Prefrontal cortex (PFC) dopamine D1 and noradrenaline α2A signalling underlies the working memory + impulse-control deficits in ADHD. Tyrosine and phenylalanine are precursors of dopamine; iron and vitamin C are cofactors. Magnesium, zinc, and B6 are cofactors in monoamine synthesis. Targeted by **iron supplementation if ferritin <50 ng/mL** (Cortese 2012 systematic review — A-grade in deficient subset, including RLS-ADHD overlap), **omega-3 EPA-rich 1-2 g/day** (Bloch 2011 meta — small but real effect, larger in younger), **zinc** if low (Bilici 2004 paediatric RCT — B-grade), **L-tyrosine 500-1500 mg AM** (variable evidence), **magnesium** if deficient, **saffron 30 mg** (Crocus sativus — Baziar 2019 RCT showed equivalence to methylphenidate in paediatric ADHD; Iranian replication-pending), **Pycnogenol 1 mg/kg** (Trebatická 2006 B-grade for paediatric ADHD), **Bacopa monnieri 300 mg** (cognitive, not ADHD-specific), and avoidance of stimulant-only "energy" patterns of high caffeine + low sleep + low protein. **Synthesis.** Catecholamines are the upstream chemistry; stimulants modulate them powerfully, and the home protocol's nutrient layer supports them without competing.

### 2.3 Executive dysfunction + working memory

> **For you, in plain words.** Adult ADHD is mostly an executive-function problem — planning, sequencing, time-estimation, task initiation, and emotional regulation. CBT-for-adult-ADHD specifically trains these skills. *(Safren 2010 JAMA; Solanto 2010 RCT)*

**The deeper layer.** Prefrontal executive networks (dorsolateral PFC, anterior cingulate, fronto-parietal control network) are underactivated; default mode network shows delayed switch-off during task. Time blindness, prospective memory failure, and task-paralysis are characteristic. Targeted by **CBT-adult-ADHD** (Safren 2010 + Solanto 2010 RCTs vs medication — strong adjunct evidence), **structured executive-function coaching** (ADHD-credentialed coach), **externalised scaffolding** (visible task lists, Todoist + Notion, calendar reminders, Pomodoro Technique, body-doubling apps like Focusmate / Cofocus), **environmental engineering** (clutter reduction, visual-cue placement, ADHD-friendly home office), **Bacopa monnieri** (Stough 2008 — improved working memory in healthy adults at 12 weeks), **ginkgo biloba** (Salehi 2010 — small adjunct signal), **phosphatidylserine** (modest evidence), **lion's mane mushroom** (Mori 2009 — cognitive in mild impairment). **Synthesis.** Executive scaffolding + skill-training is the home tool that compounds with whatever else you do.

### 2.4 Sleep + circadian dysregulation

> **For you, in plain words.** Delayed sleep phase is so common in ADHD it's almost part of the diagnosis. Sleep deprivation looks identical to ADHD. Fix sleep first. *(Hvolby 2015 ADHD sleep review; Van der Heijden 2007 melatonin)*

**The deeper layer.** Up to 70-80% of adults with ADHD have a circadian delay (DSPS pattern) with delayed dim-light melatonin onset (DLMO). Comorbid restless legs syndrome (RLS) is 5-10× more common in ADHD — iron-driven. Sleep apnea (under-screened in ADHD adults — especially with weight gain on stimulants). Targeted by **fixed sleep window** (23:00-07:00 protected), **AM bright-light exposure** within 30 min of waking (10,000-lux box if winter), **PM blue-light cutoff** ≥90 min before bed, **melatonin 0.3-0.5 mg micro-dose 4-6 h pre-target-sleep** for DLMO shift (NOT 5-10 mg for sleep induction), **magnesium glycinate evening**, **L-theanine 200 mg**, **CBT-i** if insomnia (Sleepio, Somryst apps), **restless-legs iron repletion** if ferritin <50, **OSA screening** (STOP-BANG, Epworth — home pulse-ox overnight if positive), **phone OUT of bedroom**, **no caffeine after 14:00**. **Synthesis.** Without sleep, every other layer underperforms; in many adults, sleep treatment alone halves apparent ADHD severity.

### 2.5 Emotional dysregulation + RSD

> **For you, in plain words.** The under-recognised half of adult ADHD is emotional — rejection-sensitive dysphoria (the gut-punch over-reaction to criticism or feeling left out), low frustration tolerance, mood lability. This is real, and named. *(Surman 2013 emotional dysregulation in adult ADHD; Dodson on RSD)*

**The deeper layer.** Emotional dysregulation is present in 30-70% of adult ADHD even without comorbid mood disorder; RSD describes the immediate, intense emotional pain of perceived or actual rejection / criticism. Often missed in formal diagnosis. Targeted by **CBT-for-emotional-regulation skills** (DBT-informed skills training, ACT for ADHD), **mindfulness-based ADHD training** (Mitchell 2017 small RCT), **MBSR**, **HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath Inner Balance — B for emotional regulation overlap), **Ashwagandha 600 mg KSM-66** (Chandrasekhar 2012 — stress + sleep), **Bacopa**, **Saraswatarishta** (Ayurvedic cognitive-emotional tonic), **L-theanine 200 mg**, **adequate sleep** (sleep loss massively amplifies RSD). **Synthesis.** Emotional regulation is the half of ADHD treatment most prescribers miss; it's also the half most responsive to home practices.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Bright light + circadian engineering

> **For you, in plain words.** AM bright-light box and PM blue-light cutoff shift the delayed circadian phase that comes with ADHD. *(Rybak 2006 ADHD + bright light; Van der Heijden 2007 melatonin DLMO)*

**Quick start.** 10,000-lux light box (Lumie Vitamin L, Philips HF3500, Verilux HappyLight) 20-30 min within 30 min of waking; blackout in PM ≥90 min before bed; orange-tint glasses (Swannies, BlockBlueLight) after sunset.

#### Open the science

Circadian phase shift via AM bright light + PM blue-light reduction is documented in adult ADHD with delayed sleep phase. Combine with low-dose melatonin (0.3-0.5 mg micro-dose, 4-6 h pre-target-sleep) for DLMO shift — not the high-dose 5-10 mg for sleep induction. AM sunlight exposure within 30 min of waking is the free alternative.

### 5.2 HRV biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** Real-time training of heart-rate-variability via app + finger sensor — modest but real ADHD-emotional-regulation evidence. *(Tinello 2022 biofeedback ADHD meta)*

#### Open the science

- HeartMath Inner Balance — sensor + app, 10 min/day coherence training.
- Lief — wearable patch + HRV training.
- Polar / Oura / Whoop — HRV tracking (not feedback).
- Emotiv MN8 — EEG headband.

B-grade for emotional regulation overlap; mechanism is vagal-tone training and prefrontal-cortex regulation.

### 5.3 Neurofeedback — present honestly

> **For you, in plain words.** Consumer EEG headbands (Muse, Mendi) have variable evidence — some users report focus shift, the trials are small and inconsistent. *(Cortese 2016 neurofeedback ADHD meta)*

#### Open the science

Cortese 2016 systematic review of neurofeedback for ADHD — evidence is positive in unblinded raters, weaker in blinded raters. Suggests significant non-specific effect. Home neurofeedback devices: Muse (~$200), Mendi (~$300). Reasonable to try 8-12 weeks, stop if no shift. **Not** a substitute for CBT or stimulants if indicated.

### 5.4 Movement-based interventions

> **For you, in plain words.** Exercise is a real ADHD intervention — single-bout cognitive boost is A-grade, chronic exercise improves attention. Not a substitute for stimulants in severe cases. *(Pontifex 2013; Den Heijer 2017 meta)*

#### Open the science

Pontifex 2013 (n=20 children, single-bout 20-min cycling) showed improved Stroop and academic performance immediately after exercise. Den Heijer 2017 systematic review of chronic exercise + ADHD — improved executive function and attention. Aerobic exercise 30-60 min, 3-5×/week. Strength training as cofactor. Yoga (Iyengar) supplementary. Time outdoors + green-space exposure reduces ADHD symptom expression (Taylor 2009).

### 5.5 Sleep-tracking & screening

> **For you, in plain words.** Track your sleep with a wearable, screen for sleep apnea with home pulse-ox if STOP-BANG positive, and treat any sleep disorder before assuming all symptoms are "ADHD."

#### Open the science

- Oura Ring, Whoop, Apple Watch sleep, Fitbit Sense — for sleep stage + HRV + temperature tracking.
- STOP-BANG questionnaire ≥3 → home pulse-ox overnight (Wellue O2Ring continuous overnight tracking) → if oxygen desaturations, formal sleep study route §10.7.3.
- Sleep diary 2 weeks before adjusting protocol.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods (daily)

> **For you, in plain words.** Eat for the dopamine + executive brain — protein at every meal, oily fish, leafy greens, berries, fermented foods, minimal refined sugar + processed food. *(Sonuga-Barke 2013 food + ADHD review; Pelsser 2011)*

#### Open the science

- **Protein at every meal** — 25-40 g protein at breakfast, lunch, dinner. Eggs, fish, lentil, chicken, tofu, Greek yoghurt. Protein stabilises blood glucose + provides tyrosine + phenylalanine for catecholamine synthesis.
- **120-160 g oily fish** 3×/week — long-chain omega-3.
- **150-200 g leafy greens** daily.
- **1-2 cups berries** — anthocyanins.
- **30 g nuts + seeds** (walnut, almond, pumpkin seed).
- **150 g fermented food** — gut-brain axis.
- **2-3 cups water-rich vegetables** daily.
- **20 g dark chocolate (≥85%)** afternoon — flavanols, modest stimulant.

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Sugar binges, energy drinks, ultra-processed food, late-day caffeine, alcohol — these directly worsen ADHD symptoms and sleep.

#### Open the science

- **Refined sugar + sweetened drinks** — glycaemic instability mimics ADHD.
- **Ultra-processed foods + food colourings** — Stevenson 2010 review.
- **Energy drinks** — high caffeine + sugar = false stimulant pattern.
- **Late-day caffeine (after 14:00)** — protects sleep.

- **Sleep medicine evaluation** — for OSA, RLS, DSPS workup if home screening positive.

## 13. 12-month implementation timeline & honest evidence-tier table

### 13.1 Month 0-1 — foundations

ASRS-v1.1 baseline, DTC labs (ferritin, omega-3 index, vit D, B12, thyroid), sleep window fixed, phone OUT of bedroom, bright-light box if winter, Combo 1 + Combo 3 begin, CBT-ADHD app start (Inflow or Mello), executive-function scaffolding (Todoist + Pomodoro + Focusmate).

### 13.2 Month 2-3 — full stack

Add Combo 2, Saraswatarishta evening, iron repletion if ferritin <50, saffron OR Pycnogenol layer trial 8 weeks, exercise dialled to 5×/week, formal ADHD evaluation if not yet done.

### 13.3 Month 4-6 — refinement

Reassess ASRS, PHQ-9, GAD-7; if stimulant Rx in play, evaluate effect with full home stack; if not controlled, audit sleep + comorbidities; add TCM cycling overlay (Gui Pi Tang OR Tian Wang Bu Xin Dan).

### 13.4 Month 7-12 — maintenance

Quarterly ASRS, sleep-tracking continued, annual labs, ADHD coach engagement if executive scaffolding plateaus, formal CBT-ADHD course if not yet done.

### 13.5 Quarterly self-audit

ASRS, sleep regularity, exercise adherence, caffeine + alcohol intake, task-completion %, comorbidity scales (PHQ-9, GAD-7).

### 13.6 Annual deep audit

DTC labs: ferritin, omega-3 index, 25-OH vit D, B12, full thyroid panel, fasting glucose + HbA1c, lipid panel, hs-CRP; ECG if on stimulant.

## 14. Closing paradigm

The paradigm here: adult ADHD is a four-driver neurodevelopmental condition and the home protocol stacks evidence-graded layers against each driver — sleep optimisation, CBT-for-adult-ADHD, structured exercise, and executive-function scaffolding as the unsung A-grade home cornerstones, a clean supplement layer (omega-3 EPA-rich, iron if deficient, magnesium, vitamin D, Bacopa, Ashwagandha, saffron or Pycnogenol as optional) sitting on top, classical traditions (Ayurveda Brahmi + Saraswatarishta + Medhya Rasayanas, TCM Gui Pi Tang + Tian Wang Bu Xin Dan + Korean Red Ginseng) honoured where they earn their place, and full transparency about what we don't recommend (St John's wort, self-medicating with stimulants of abuse, microdosing without diagnosis or trial evidence, "brain-training" games as primary). The clinic-route options (formal diagnostic workup, stimulants, atomoxetine, formal CBT-ADHD therapy, ADHD coaching) are documented honestly in §10.7.3 so the user already on them sees no conflict, and so the user whose home protocol plateaus or who suspects undiagnosed ADHD knows the gold-standard names. Sovereignty over your brain means: measure it (ASRS, ferritin, sleep), structure it (CBT, Pomodoro, body-doubling), feed it (protein at every meal, omega-3, micronutrient floor), and stack only what the evidence justifies.
