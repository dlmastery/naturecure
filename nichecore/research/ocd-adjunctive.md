## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What OCD actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** OCD is a real, measurable circuit problem — a cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) loop stuck in "did I check?" mode plus a serotonin/glutamate imbalance. It is not weakness, not bad parenting, not a moral failing. It responds to specific behavioural therapy and to specific molecules — and the protocol below targets both. *(Pauls 2014 Nature Reviews; Pittenger 2011 glutamate-OCD)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: score Y-BOCS-SR baseline; start an ERP-coach app (NOCD, GG OCD, *Rituals*); write your top three rituals. This week: order NAC 600 mg + Mg glycinate + inositol powder; coherent breathing 10 min BID; read first 3 chapters of Hyman & Pedrick *The OCD Workbook* or Grayson *Freedom from OCD*. *(Hyman & Pedrick; Grayson; NOCD app)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

Mainstream coverage shrugs OCD off as "an anxiety disorder you take an SSRI for." Honest reframe: OCD is the output of (i) **hyperactive CSTC loop** (OFC + ACC + caudate stuck firing), (ii) **glutamate/GABA imbalance** in striatum + ACC, (iii) **depleted serotonergic system** at 5-HT2C, (iv) **HPA-axis dysregulation** amplifying ritual urges, (v) **learned reinforcement loop** where rituals relieve obsession-anxiety and strengthen the circuit. **Synthesis.** Pairs ERP (A-grade frontline) with NAC + inositol + HPA support + cofactor floor — without replacing the SSRI option (§10.7.3) for those who need it.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive OCD — stuck CSTC loop, glutamate excess, serotonin shortfall, runaway cortisol, behavioural reinforcement. Naming them lets every herb, breath and exercise aim at the right driver. *(Pauls 2014; Pittenger 2011; Foa 2005)*

### 2.2 Stuck CSTC loop

> **For you, in plain words.** The cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical loop is the brain's "did I do that?" feedback circuit. In OCD it fires too long and too loud, so the "everything is fine" signal never arrives. ERP physically retrains it; high-dose SSRIs in §10.7.3 calm it pharmacologically. *(Pauls 2014 Nature Reviews; Saxena 1998 PET-OCD)*

#### Open the science

fMRI shows hyperactivity in the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC), anterior cingulate cortex (ACC), caudate, and thalamus in OCD — Saxena 1998 PET; Pauls 2014 Nature Reviews; Menzies 2008 meta. Successful ERP and SSRI treatment both normalise this circuit on follow-up imaging (Schwartz 1996; Baxter 1992). Targeted by **ERP** (A-grade lever), **mindfulness-augmented ERP** (Hertenstein 2012), **CBT with cognitive restructuring** (Wilhelm 2009), **I-CBT** for primary obsessions (O'Connor 2005), and **MBCT** for relapse prevention. **Synthesis.** Highest-evidence lever in this dossier — higher than any supplement.

### 2.3 Glutamate excess in striatum

> **For you, in plain words.** OCD brains show too much glutamate, the brain's main "go" signal, in the striatum and anterior cingulate. NAC, glycine, and memantine all calm glutamate — NAC has the cleanest OCD trial signal among OTC tools. *(Pittenger 2011 glutamate-OCD review; Sayyah 2012 NAC-OCD RCT)*

#### Open the science

Pittenger 2011 review and Rosenberg 2000 MRS data confirm elevated glutamate in the caudate and ACC of OCD. Targeted by **NAC** 2-3 g/day (Sayyah 2012 Iranian RCT n=48, augmenting fluvoxamine, striking Y-BOCS reduction; Afshar 2012 replication; Berk 2008 broader mood signal), **glycine** 60 g/day (Greenberg 2009 small refractory signal — challenging dose), **memantine augmentation** (Stewart 2010 — Rx in §10.7.3), **sarcosine** (Wu 2011 small signal). **Synthesis.** Cleanest mechanistic OTC lever in OCD — NAC is the only OTC with multiple positive OCD-specific RCTs.

### 2.4 Serotonin shortfall

> **For you, in plain words.** OCD shows a specific 5-HT2C receptor abnormality and broadly depleted serotonergic tone — which is why high-dose, long-duration SSRIs work where regular doses fall flat. Inositol (a serotonin signalling cofactor) and gentle dietary tryptophan loading help; 5-HTP is **excluded** because it cannot be safely stacked with the SSRIs many OCD users are already on. *(Soomro 2008 Cochrane SSRIs-OCD; Fux 1996 inositol-OCD RCT; El Mansari 2006 serotonin-OCD)*

#### Open the science

Soomro 2008 Cochrane confirms SSRI A-grade for OCD at HIGH doses (fluoxetine 60-80 mg, sertraline 200 mg, fluvoxamine 300 mg, paroxetine 60 mg, escitalopram 20-40 mg) — 10-12 weeks to response, 60-80% partial response. Clinic-route per §10.7.3, **never** in home regime. Home-OTC lever is **inositol** 12-18 g/day (Fux 1996 Israeli crossover RCT n=13, SSRI-magnitude reduction; Carey 2004 augmentation null in refractory — honest framing: first-line for milder cases, less robust as add-on). 5-HTP **excluded** — serotonin-syndrome risk with SSRIs. **Synthesis.** Without serotonergic repair the protocol leaks; high-dose SSRIs remain gold-standard pharmacotherapy in §10.7.3.

### 2.5 Runaway HPA cortisol axis

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress amplifies ritual urges — the more cortisol pumping, the harder the urge to check, wash, count. Ashwagandha, holy basil, phosphatidylserine, and sleep restoration drop the cortisol baseline so ERP exposures don't get steamrolled. *(Sousa 2006 HPA-OCD; Chandrasekhar 2012 ashwagandha-cortisol)*

#### Open the science

OCD shows flatter cortisol diurnal slope and elevated CAR in stress-comorbid subgroups (Sousa 2006; Kluge 2007). Targeted by **Ashwagandha** KSM-66 300-600 mg/day (Chandrasekhar 2012 ~28% cortisol reduction; Lopresti 2019 meta), **Holy basil** (Saxena 2012), **Phosphatidylserine** (Hellhammer 2004), **circadian sleep hygiene + CBT-i**, **morning sunlight + caffeine cut-off 14:00**. Ashwa excluded in pregnancy + hyperthyroidism. **Synthesis.** Without HPA reset, ERP exposures feel impossibly hard.

### 2.6 Behavioural reinforcement loop

> **For you, in plain words.** Every time a ritual brings temporary relief from an obsession, the loop gets stronger. ERP breaks that loop on purpose, by sitting with the obsession until the anxiety drops without doing the ritual. This is the single A-grade lever in OCD treatment. *(Foa 2005 ERP RCT; Olatunji 2013 ERP meta; Reid 2021 ERP meta)*

#### Open the science

Operant conditioning explains OCD escalation: obsession → anxiety → ritual relieves anxiety → ritual negatively reinforced → next-obsession threshold drops. ERP (Foa 2005 RCT n=149; Olatunji 2013 meta; Reid 2021 meta) deliberately exposes to the trigger without the ritual, allowing extinction learning. Variants: **mindfulness-augmented ERP** (Hertenstein 2012), **ACT** (Twohig 2010), **I-CBT** (O'Connor 2005), **MCT** (Wells), **habit-reversal training** (Azrin & Nunn) for BFRBs. **Synthesis.** THE lever — ERP follow-up durability exceeds SSRI post-discontinuation durability (Foa 2005).

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve OTC molecules with OCD-specific or close-adjacent trial signal. Cornerstone: **NAC 2-3 g/day** — only OTC with clean OCD-specific RCT signal (Sayyah 2012; Afshar 2012; Grant 2009). Inositol 12-18 g second (Fux 1996). Honest cautions: 5-HTP (NEVER with SSRIs), kava (liver-tox excluded), St John's Wort (CYP3A4 + serotonin syndrome — excluded). *(Sayyah 2012; Fux 1996; EMA SJW SmPC)*

### 3.2 N-acetylcysteine — the OCD cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** NAC is a cheap glutamate-modulator with the strongest OCD-specific OTC trial signal. Sayyah 2012 augmentation RCT n=48 showed striking Y-BOCS reduction; Grant 2009 RCT showed clear trichotillomania effect. Dose: 600 mg BID → 1500 mg BID. *(Sayyah 2012; Afshar 2012; Grant 2009)*

#### Open the science

Glutathione precursor + glutamate-modulator via cystine-glutamate antiporter (Berk 2013). Trials: **Sayyah 2012** n=48 (NAC 2.4 g augmenting fluvoxamine, large Y-BOCS drop); **Afshar 2012** replication; **Costa 2017** n=40 mixed (honest signal); **Grant 2009** tricho n=50 (large effect); **Grant 2016** excoriation n=66; **Berk 2008** broader mood. Titrate 600 → 1200-1500 mg BID. Side effects: GI (with food), sulfur-smell. **Caution**: nitroglycerin; asthma. **A** for tricho/excoriation; **B** OCD-augmentation.

### 3.3 Inositol — high-dose first-line OTC option

> **For you, in plain words.** Myo-inositol 12-18 g/day (grams, not mg) is a serotonergic second-messenger cofactor with small but striking RCT signal. Fux 1996 Israeli crossover RCT n=13 showed SSRI-magnitude reduction. Honest framing: works for mild-moderate; mixed as refractory augmenter. *(Fux 1996; Carey 2004)*

#### Open the science

Phosphatidylinositol precursor — 5-HT2C second-messenger. Fux 1996 crossover RCT n=13 (18 g/day × 6 weeks, 7-point Y-BOCS reduction); Carey 2004 augmentation n=10 null. Honest: first-line mild-moderate, less robust as SSRI add-on. Powder cheaper than caps. Titrate 4 g BID → 6 g BID → 9 g BID; dissolve in water/juice with meals. **Grade B** mild-moderate OCD monotherapy.

## 5. Biophysical & Sensory Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Highest-evidence layer for OCD is ERP — **structured exposure to obsessions while preventing the ritual**. Devices distant second. *(Foa 2005; Olatunji 2013; Reid 2021)*

**Quick start tonight:** (1) Score baseline Y-BOCS-SR + OCI-R (free PDFs). (2) Download an ERP-coach app: NOCD (most clinically grounded; subscription), GG OCD (free), *Rituals* (low-cost). (3) Write top three rituals on paper; pick easiest for tomorrow's exposure.

### 5.2 Exposure & Response Prevention (ERP) — the A-grade cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** ERP is the gold-standard OCD treatment. Principle: trigger your obsession on purpose, refuse the ritual, sit with discomfort until it drops. Foa 2005 RCT n=149 — ERP outperformed clomipramine. Olatunji 2013 + Reid 2021 meta confirm largest effect size in OCD. **More durable than meds at follow-up.** *(Foa 2005; Olatunji 2013; Reid 2021)*

**Self-guided routes.** Workbooks (library-free): Hyman & Pedrick *The OCD Workbook*; Grayson *Freedom from OCD*; Lee Baer *Getting Control*; Reid Wilson *Stopping the Noise in Your Head*; Winston & Seif *Overcoming Unwanted Intrusive Thoughts* (pure-O / harm-OCD). Apps: **NOCD** (clinically-grounded + optional therapist); **GG OCD** (free); **Rituals**; Bearable mood+ritual tracking. **Honest framing**: credentialed ERP therapist gets better outcomes for pure-O, harm-OCD, postpartum, scrupulosity, paedophilia-themed, Y-BOCS ≥24, suicidal thoughts. The user decides; the protocol does not require it. **Grade A.**

### 5.3 Inference-based CBT (I-CBT) for primary obsessions

> **For you, in plain words.** For pure-O, harm-OCD, scrupulosity where exposure feels impossible (you can't expose to "what if I'm a paedophile"), I-CBT teaches you to recognise the *reasoning trap* itself. O'Connor 2005 RCT showed equivalence to ERP. Self-guided: Aardema *The Sensitive Mind* + ICBT.com modules. *(O'Connor 2005)*

### 5.4 ACT for OCD

> **For you, in plain words.** ACT teaches defusion + values-based action — useful alongside ERP for harm-OCD and pure-O. Twohig 2010 RCT equivalence to PMR. Self-guided: Harris *Happiness Trap* + ACT Companion app. *(Twohig 2010)*

### 5.5 Mindfulness-augmented ERP and MBCT

> **For you, in plain words.** MBCT/MBSR augment ERP — help sit with discomfort during exposures. Hertenstein 2012 small RCT positive. *(Hertenstein 2012)*

### 5.6 Habit-reversal training (HRT) — for BFRBs

> **For you, in plain words.** For trichotillomania + excoriation, HRT is the A-grade lever. Azrin & Nunn's protocol: awareness training + competing response (e.g., clench fists 60 sec when urge fires) + social support. Combine with NAC 1200-2400 mg/day. *(Bate 2011 HRT meta; Grant 2009)*
