Supplements come second. **IBD is genuinely autoimmune-driven inflammation that can complicate fast — this dossier is NOT a substitute for the biologic conversation you may need.** *(WHO NCD strategy; Lang 2015 Israeli curcumin UC RCT; Gionchetti 2000 VSL#3 pouchitis; Suskind 2018 SCD PRODUCE; Levine 2019 CDED)*

>
> **RED FLAG — STOP READING AND CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES NOW IF:**
> - **Severe abdominal pain + distension + fever** (suspected toxic megacolon in UC, perforation in Crohn's).
> - **Bowel obstruction signs** (vomiting, no flatus, severe distension) — Crohn's stricture emergency.
> - **GI bleeding** — passing more than a cup of blood per bowel movement, or signs of haemorrhagic shock (dizziness, fast pulse, pallor).
> - **Severe dehydration** from intractable diarrhoea (>10 stools/day, dry mouth, dizzy, dark urine).
> - **Uncontrolled fever** during a flare — rule out abscess.
> - **Pregnancy + IBD flare** — specialist gastro + obstetric.
> - **New severe right-upper-quadrant pain + jaundice + dark urine** — primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) is a known IBD complication.
> - **Symptoms of bowel cancer** — IBD raises colorectal cancer risk; surveillance colonoscopy schedule starts ~8 years after IBD diagnosis.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What IBD actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis are conditions where the immune system attacks the lining of your gut, causing chronic inflammation, ulcers, diarrhoea, blood, weight loss, and exhaustion. They are NOT the same as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) — IBD is real tissue damage; IBS is a functional disorder. *(WHO IBD scoping; Ng 2017 Lancet global incidence)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Order a home fecal calprotectin test this week (IBDoc Bühlmann or NimbleDx). Switch to a Mediterranean or Crohn's Disease Exclusion Diet (CDED) — no ultra-processed foods, no emulsifiers (carrageenan, polysorbate-80, carboxymethylcellulose). Take curcumin (Theracurmin or Meriva) 500-1,000 mg twice daily. Take vitamin D3 4,000 IU/day. Get DTC labs (ferritin, B12, vit D, hs-CRP). *(Lang 2015 curcumin UC RCT; Levine 2019 CDED; Jørgensen 2010 vit D IBD)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

The mainstream model is "you have IBD, here's a biologic for life". The biologics genuinely save lives — the trial evidence (ACCENT, CHARM, GEMINI, UNITI, ADVANCE) is solid. A more honest reframe is that **IBD has five overlapping drivers** even when biologics are running: (i) **dysbiotic gut microbiome** with depleted SCFA-producing Firmicutes and expanded inflammatory Proteobacteria; (ii) **defective mucosal barrier** — tight junctions failing, mucin layer thin, paneth-cell defensin defects in Crohn's; (iii) **dysregulated immune response** — TNF-α, IL-12/23, IL-17, JAK-STAT, S1P pathway over-activation; (iv) **diet-microbiome crosstalk** — emulsifiers, refined carbs, low fibre, high red meat all worsen the picture; (v) **environmental triggers** — smoking (paradoxically protective in UC but harmful in Crohn's), NSAIDs, antibiotics in childhood, low vitamin D, urban-Western lifestyle. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **structured dietary therapy (SCD/CDED/Mediterranean) + curcumin + targeted probiotic strain + vitamin D + zinc + omega-3 + iron-if-deficient + stress reduction** runs alongside whatever conventional care the user chooses — and may, over time, lower flare frequency and allow conversations about taper. **It does NOT replace biologics for severe disease.**

### 1.4 What this dossier is NOT

> **For you, in plain words.** This is not a substitute for biologic therapy in severe IBD. If you have a high HBI score, deep ulceration on endoscopy, severe complications, or paediatric Crohn's, biologics like infliximab/adalimumab/vedolizumab/ustekinumab are A-grade trial evidence. The protocol respects that. *(ACCENT Hanauer 2002; GEMINI Feagan 2013; UNITI Sandborn 2016)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five drivers — microbiome dysbiosis, leaky mucosal barrier, dysregulated immune response, diet-microbiome crosstalk, environmental triggers. Naming them lets every food, supplement, and practice aim at the right one. *(Manichanh 2012 Nat Rev microbiome; Khor 2011 Nature genetics-environment review)*

### 2.2 Dysbiotic microbiome

> **For you, in plain words.** IBD guts have fewer of the "good" bacteria that make short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) like butyrate — the food gut-lining cells live on — and more of the inflammatory species. Feeding the good bugs the right fibres is foundational. *(Sokol 2008 Faecalibacterium prausnitzii deficit; Frank 2007)*

#### Open the science

IBD microbiomes show consistently lower Firmicutes (especially Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, an anti-inflammatory SCFA-producer), lower Bacteroidetes diversity, and expansion of Proteobacteria (adherent-invasive E. coli, especially in Crohn's). SCFA depletion (butyrate, propionate, acetate) starves the colonocyte — butyrate is its primary fuel — and impairs Treg differentiation. Targeted by **fermentable fibres** (psyllium, partially hydrolysed guar gum, oat β-glucan — once tolerance allows), **resistant starch** (cooled rice/potato, green banana flour — tolerance-dependent), **strain-specific probiotics** (VSL#3/Visbiome A-grade for UC maintenance and pouchitis; E. coli Nissle 1917/Mutaflor non-inferior to mesalamine UC maintenance), and avoiding **emulsifiers** (Chassaing 2015 Nature showed carrageenan, polysorbate-80, CMC induce colitis in mice). **Synthesis.** Restore the microbiome and the substrate disease shifts.

### 2.3 Leaky mucosal barrier

> **For you, in plain words.** The gut lining is one cell thick. In IBD, the seals between cells (tight junctions) fail, the mucus layer thins, and gut content leaks into the wall — feeding the immune response. *(Hollander 1988; Buhner 2006)*

#### Open the science

Zonulin-mediated tight-junction disassembly, defective mucin (MUC2) production, paneth-cell defensin defects (especially in NOD2-mutated Crohn's), and IL-13-driven claudin-2 up-regulation all increase paracellular permeability. Bacterial LPS and antigens translocate, perpetuating Th1/Th17 responses. Targeted by **zinc carnosine** (Mahmood 2007 Japan small trial), **L-glutamine** (Akobeng 2000 Crohn's small trial — modest signal), **vitamin D3** (Jørgensen 2010 Crohn's RCT), **mucilaginous herbs** (slippery elm, marshmallow root, plantago/psyllium — Fernández-Bañares 1999 UC RCT), **bone broth glycine + proline**, and **avoiding NSAIDs** (cyclo-oxygenase inhibition damages barrier directly). **Synthesis.** Heal the wall to stop the leak.

### 2.4 Dysregulated immune response

> **For you, in plain words.** In IBD the immune army won't stand down — it keeps attacking the gut wall. Curcumin, omega-3, vitamin D, and stress reduction can dial it down. Biologics dial it down hard and fast in severe disease. *(Khor 2011 Nature; Lang 2015 curcumin UC RCT)*

#### Open the science

IBD immune dysregulation involves Th1 cytokines (TNF-α, IFN-γ) dominant in Crohn's; Th2/Th17 mix in UC; expanded innate lymphoid cells type 3 (ILC3); defective Treg function. The biologic landscape directly targets these pathways — anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab) is A-grade for both diseases; anti-α4β7 vedolizumab is gut-selective; IL-12/23 (ustekinumab) and IL-23 alone (risankizumab) hit a different node; JAK inhibitors target downstream cytokine signalling; S1P modulators (ozanimod) trap lymphocytes in nodes. The home-protocol natural adjuncts also work here — **curcumin** is a multi-target anti-inflammatory (NF-κB, COX-2, JAK-STAT modulation); Lang 2015 Israeli RCT (n=50 UC induction) showed A-grade benefit with curcumin 3 g/d add-on to mesalamine. **Omega-3 EPA** is more mechanistically appealing than clinically proven — Cochrane Turner 2014 found null for Crohn's maintenance despite earlier Belluzzi 1996 NEJM signal; we surface that honestly. **Vitamin D** is both a hormone and immune modulator. **Synthesis.** The home stack supports the immune dial; severe disease needs the biologic conversation.

### 2.5 Diet-microbiome crosstalk

> **For you, in plain words.** What you eat reaches your microbes within hours and changes their behaviour for days. Specific carbohydrate diet (SCD), Crohn's Disease Exclusion Diet (CDED), and Mediterranean diet have actual IBD trial data; exclusive enteral nutrition (EEN) has the strongest paediatric Crohn's induction data of any therapy. *(Suskind 2018 PRODUCE SCD; Levine 2019 CDED; Wall 2014 EEN review)*

#### Open the science

Diet shapes the microbiome on a 24-48h timescale (David 2014 Nature). Emulsifiers (carrageenan, polysorbate-80, CMC) deplete mucus and induce colitis in mice (Chassaing 2015). High red meat and low fibre associate with IBD incidence in prospective cohorts (de Silva 2010 EPIC; Hou 2011 meta). Specific dietary protocols with IBD trial signal: **SCD** (Suskind 2018 PRODUCE n=18 paediatric Crohn's — clinical remission signal); **CDED** (Levine 2019 paediatric Crohn's n=78, induction equivalent to EEN at 6 weeks); **Mediterranean** (Strisciuglio 2022 UC paediatric small signal; Khalili 2020 prospective cohort Italian reduced UC incidence); **Low-FODMAP** for IBS-overlap symptoms (Cox 2017 quiescent IBD); **EEN** (Wall 2014 review — A-grade paediatric Crohn's induction). The protocol uses these as therapy, not "just diet". **Synthesis.** Diet is medicine in IBD; structured trials of specific protocols, not "eat healthy".

### 2.6 Environmental triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Smoking is the strangest IBD fact in medicine — it's PROTECTIVE in UC but HARMFUL in Crohn's. NSAIDs, childhood antibiotics, low vitamin D, ultra-processed food, and chronic stress are universal aggravators. *(Mahid 2006 smoking-IBD; Long 2016 NSAIDs)*

#### Open the science

Modifiable triggers: NSAIDs (block COX-1 mucosal protection, frequent flare trigger — Long 2016 prospective), early-life antibiotic exposure (microbiome disruption), low vitamin D (Jørgensen 2010), high stress/HPA-axis dysregulation (Mawdsley 2006), ultra-processed food + emulsifiers (Chassaing 2015), Western urban lifestyle (Ng 2017 Lancet rising incidence in industrialising countries), and the smoking paradox (Mahid 2006 meta — smoking cessation worsens UC course but improves Crohn's). Targeted by **strict NSAID avoidance** (acetaminophen/paracetamol preferred), **vitamin D 4,000 IU/d**, **stress reduction (CBT, MBSR)**, **emulsifier avoidance** (read labels), and **lifestyle alignment**. **Synthesis.** Remove the triggers, support the substrate, and the natural-adjunct work has room to operate.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Eight over-the-counter molecules. Curcumin and Boswellia have actual IBD RCT data; probiotic strains are highly strain-specific (Visbiome and Mutaflor are the ones with trial evidence). *(Lang 2015; Gerhardt 2001 Boswellia; Gionchetti 2000 VSL#3; Kruis 2004 Nissle)*

### 3.2 The honest omega-3 caveat (Rule 4)

> **For you, in plain words.** Omega-3 EPA is mechanistically appealing for IBD — but Cochrane Turner 2014 found NULL for Crohn's maintenance, despite an earlier Belluzzi 1996 NEJM RCT that showed benefit at 2.7 g enteric-coated EPA. We keep omega-3 as a general anti-inflammatory cofactor at 1-2 g EPA+DHA/day, NOT as an IBD-monotherapy. *(Belluzzi 1996; Turner 2014 Cochrane)*

#### Open the science

Belluzzi 1996 NEJM (n=78 Crohn's, EPA 2.7 g/d enteric-coated for 12 months) showed reduced clinical relapse 28% vs 69% on placebo. Subsequent EPIC-1 and EPIC-2 trials (Feagan 2008 JAMA) failed to replicate. Cochrane Turner 2014 confirmed null for Crohn's maintenance. The Belluzzi formulation was specific (enteric-coated free fatty acid), enabling pH-targeted release; later formulations were not equivalent. The protocol uses omega-3 EPA+DHA 1-2 g/d as general anti-inflammatory cofactor — not IBD-specific therapy.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 What to eat — mandatory foods

> **For you, in plain words.** Lean toward Mediterranean + CDED (Crohn's Disease Exclusion Diet) + low-emulsifier. Daily: cooked vegetables, lean protein, well-cooked fruits, fermented foods if tolerated. *(Levine 2019 CDED; Khalili 2020 EPIC Mediterranean)*

- 200-300 g cooked vegetables (zucchini, carrot, spinach, butternut squash, sweet potato)
- 60-100 g lean protein (fish, chicken breast, eggs, tofu if tolerated)
- 1-2 cooked fruits/day (cooked apple, banana, blueberry)
- 30 g extra-virgin olive oil
- 200 mL bone broth (glycine, proline, gelatin)
- 1-2 tbsp ground flax/chia (omega-3, soluble fibre)
- 50 g sourdough or rice (well-tolerated grains)
- 100 mL well-tolerated kefir if no dairy issue

### 6.2 What to avoid (with molecular reason)

- **Emulsifiers** (carrageenan, polysorbate-80, carboxymethylcellulose) — Chassaing 2015 Nature: mucus depletion and colitis induction.
- **Ultra-processed foods** — composite emulsifier + preservative + refined-carb load.
- **High-FODMAP foods in flare** — IBS-overlap aggravation.
- **Raw cruciferous in flare** — irritation.
- **Alcohol** — barrier disruption + medication interaction.
- **NSAIDs** — barrier disruption (Long 2016).
- **Excess red meat** — IBD incidence association (de Silva 2010).
- **Lactose** if intolerant.
- **Gluten** — controversial; some IBD patients improve, others don't. Personal trial.
- **Sugar / HFCS / artificial sweeteners** (especially polyols + sucralose).

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

Slow-cook, well-cook, pureé if needed. Bone broth daily. Fermented (in remission) — kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi. **Avoid raw vegetables in flare; cook everything thoroughly. Pressure-cook beans.**

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers

- **NSAIDs** — every variant. Substitute acetaminophen/paracetamol.
- **Antibiotics** — only when truly needed; protect microbiome.
- **Smoking** — Crohn's: quit (worsens disease and surgery rate); UC: do NOT start (smoking is paradoxically protective in UC — Mahid 2006 meta — but the cardiovascular harm exceeds the IBD benefit; nicotine patches have been studied separately).
- **Stress** — chronic-stress flare driver (Mawdsley 2006).
- **Sleep deprivation** — single-night sleep loss is documented flare trigger.
- **Emulsifiers** — read every label.
- **Plastics + endocrine disruptors** — glass food storage preferred.
- **Iron + tannins** — interactions if iron-deficient.
- **Occupational** — solvent exposure, heavy metals where applicable.
- **Indoor mould + VOCs** — chronic immune-burden.

### 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

Inflammatory bowel disease — Crohn's or ulcerative colitis — is when your immune system attacks the lining of your gut and won't stop. It is not the same as IBS. There is real tissue damage, real inflammation, real risk of complications. The mainstream model is biologics for life — for severe disease, that conversation is real and the trial evidence (ACCENT, CHARM, GEMINI, UNITI, ADVANCE) is solid. But there are five drivers we can also work on at home: a microbiome that has lost its anti-inflammatory bacteria; a gut lining that's leaking; an immune system stuck on attack; what you eat (which talks to your microbes within hours); and triggers like NSAIDs, smoking-if-Crohn's, ultra-processed food with emulsifiers, and chronic stress. The protocol pairs structured dietary therapy (Mediterranean-CDED hybrid, low-emulsifier, no NSAIDs) with curcumin (which actually has UC induction trial data — Lang 2015), Boswellia (Crohn's RCT — Gerhardt 2001), Visbiome / VSL#3 (A-grade for pouchitis and UC maintenance), vitamin D3 + K2, zinc carnosine, omega-3, and the highest-evidence behavioural work (CBT for IBD distress, MBSR, sleep extension). This is months-long. It is **not** a substitute for biologics in severe disease. **This is an independent, over-the-counter, home-based protocol. No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.** If you keep a gastroenterologist in the loop, that is your call. Surveillance colonoscopy on the IBD-CRC schedule is genuinely lifesaving — that conversation belongs in §10.7.3 and is your call too. The protocol respects the biologic conversation; it does not pretend natural adjuncts replace it.

## 14. Independent Healing Paradigm

**Sovereignty ethos**: OTC + home-based by design. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.** The user owns their body.

**Honest acknowledgment of evidence limits**: omega-3 is null for Crohn's maintenance (Cochrane Turner 2014); FMT is mixed for UC and null for Crohn's; long-term prednisone is harmful; some Ayurveda and homeopathy entries lack robust modern evidence and are graded T/H accordingly.
