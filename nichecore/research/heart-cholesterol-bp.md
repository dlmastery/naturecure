## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What cardio disease actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** "Heart disease" is not one thing — it is a small family of slow, decade-long problems (stiff arteries, sticky cholesterol, an over-revved nervous system, a stretched heart muscle) that share the same fixable roots. *(INTERHEART Yusuf 2004 — 9 modifiable risks explain ~90% of first heart attacks worldwide)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Buy a validated upper-arm home BP cuff this week (Omron 5 Series or above, Withings BPM Core, or Microlife WatchBP). Walk 30 minutes daily. Take 200 mg magnesium glycinate twice a day. Cut sugary drinks and ultra-processed food. Sleep 7-8 hours. Get one DTC lipid panel with apoB and a once-in-a-lifetime Lp(a). These five moves cover the highest-evidence layers. *(Cochrane DASH 2020; PREDIMED 2018; Aburto 2013 K and BP meta; Kostis 2002 home BP)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

The mainstream model is "your number was high so here's a pill". A more honest reframe: most adult cardiovascular disease is the endpoint of **five overlapping drivers** — (i) **endothelial dysfunction** (the blood-vessel lining stops making enough nitric oxide); (ii) **atherogenic lipid load** — apoB-carrying particles (LDL, Lp(a), remnants) that infiltrate the vessel wall when the lining is leaky; (iii) **autonomic imbalance** — sympathetic over-drive, low HRV, poor baroreflex; (iv) **insulin resistance + visceral adiposity** that turbocharges (i)-(iii); and (v) **stiff aging arteries** that raise systolic BP independent of everything else. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **diet + exercise + sleep + weight + tobacco-zero + alcohol-low** (the highest-grade frontline) with a tight cofactor floor (Mg, K, omega-3, vitamin D), targeted botanicals (hawthorn, garlic, hibiscus, berberine, bergamot, Pycnogenol), self-measured BP and self-ordered apoB hits all five drivers in parallel — and tells the truth where the evidence is mixed.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five drivers — leaky vessel lining, sticky cholesterol particles, an over-revved nervous system, fat-around-the-organs insulin resistance, and stiff aging arteries. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(INTERHEART Yusuf 2004; Libby 2021 atherosclerosis review)*

### 2.2 Leaky vessel lining

> **For you, in plain words.** When the blood-vessel lining stops making enough of its calming chemical (nitric oxide), arteries clamp down, plaques start, and blood pressure climbs. Beetroot, garlic, exercise and Pycnogenol target this. *(Vanhoutte 2017 endothelial dysfunction review; Sobenin 2008 garlic Russia)*

#### Open the science

The endothelium is a single cell-layer-thick organ lining every blood vessel. In health it makes nitric oxide (NO) via endothelial nitric-oxide synthase (eNOS), which relaxes the underlying smooth muscle, suppresses platelet activation, and keeps the lining anti-thrombotic. Oxidative stress (superoxide), high BP shear, hyperglycaemia, tobacco smoke, ApoB-particle infiltration, and inflammatory cytokines uncouple eNOS — it then makes superoxide instead of NO, accelerating the entire cascade. Targeted by **dietary nitrate** (beetroot — Webb 2008 Lancet, Kapil 2014), **aged garlic** (Sobenin 2008 Russia), **L-arginine + L-citrulline**, **Pycnogenol** (Belcaro 2014 Italy endothelial), **cocoa flavanols** (Heiss 2015 Germany), **Zone 2 aerobic exercise** (Green 2017 brachial-FMD meta), and **stopping tobacco**. **Synthesis.** Restore NO and every downstream node gets better.

### 2.3 Sticky cholesterol particles (apoB load)

> **For you, in plain words.** What matters is not your "total cholesterol" — it is how many apoB-carrying particles (LDL, Lp(a), remnants) are floating around. Lower the number of particles, lower the lifetime risk. *(Sniderman 2019 apoB meta; 4S, JUPITER, FOURIER, ODYSSEY — see §10.7.3)*

#### Open the science

Atherogenesis is driven by apoB-containing lipoprotein particles (LDL, IDL, VLDL remnants, chylomicron remnants, and Lp(a)) crossing the endothelium and being retained in the sub-endothelial space, oxidised, taken up by macrophages, and forming foam cells. Particle **number** (apoB or LDL-P) predicts events better than LDL-C concentration alone, especially in metabolic syndrome and T2D where particles are small and dense. Lp(a) is largely genetically determined, atherogenic and pro-thrombotic, and currently has no proven OTC lever — measure once in a lifetime. Targeted nutraceutically by **berberine** (Kong 2004 China — PCSK9-independent LDLR upregulation), **bergamot polyphenols** (Mollace 2011 Italy n=237), **red yeast rice** (caveat: monacolin K = lovastatin; quality variable; banned in some EU jurisdictions), **soluble fibre / β-glucan** (Whitehead 2014 oats meta), **plant sterols** (EFSA 2009), **garlic** (Ried 2016 garlic-lipid meta), and **omega-3 EPA-only purified** (REDUCE-IT Bhatt 2019 — but COMET-EE and STRENGTH null with mixed EPA+DHA, see §3.2 honesty).

### 2.4 Over-revved nervous system

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress, poor sleep and untreated sleep apnea keep the body's accelerator pedal pressed all night. The heart pays. Slow-paced breathing, MBSR, HRV training and sleep restoration target this. *(Joseph 2005 6-bpm breathing BP; Hughes 2013 MBSR-BP meta; Marin 2005 OSA Spain)*

#### Open the science

Sympathetic over-drive (raised noradrenaline, low HRV, low baroreflex sensitivity) raises BP, accelerates atherogenesis, promotes arrhythmia, and tracks with metabolic syndrome. Mechanism: chronic HPA-axis activation → cortisol → insulin resistance and visceral fat; chronic sympathetic firing → renal renin → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + sodium retention. Untreated obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) is a giant amplifier — each apnoeic event spikes BP and sympathetic outflow. Targeted by **slow-paced breathing at 6 breaths/min** (Joseph 2005 Italy; meta-analyses show ~4-5 mmHg drop), **MBSR** (Hughes 2013 meta — modest BP effect), **HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath data), **CBT for hypertension** (small but real signal), **home sleep test for OSA** (Lofta WatchPAT), **Yoga Nidra**, **Bhramari pranayama**, and **regular sleep timing**.

### 2.5 Insulin resistance + visceral fat

> **For you, in plain words.** Fat around the organs (not under the skin) drives high blood pressure, high triglycerides, low HDL and inflamed arteries — all at once. Reverse insulin resistance and the heart numbers usually follow. *(Reaven 1988 metabolic syndrome; Look AHEAD trial 2013)*

#### Open the science

Visceral adipose tissue is metabolically active — it secretes inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6), free fatty acids that overwhelm the liver (driving MASH/NAFLD — see liver-alcohol-recovery dossier), and angiotensinogen that feeds the RAAS axis. The result: small dense LDL, low HDL, high triglycerides, raised apoB, raised BP, raised CRP. Targeted by **weight loss** (every 1 kg ≈ 1 mmHg systolic drop, Neter 2003 meta), **berberine** (Yin 2008 China — comparable to metformin in head-to-head), **cinnamon** (Allen 2013 meta — small), **chromium picolinate** (mixed), **Mg glycinate** (Veronese 2016 meta), **18:6 time-restricted eating** (when metabolically appropriate), **Zone 2 cardio + strength**, and **post-meal walking** (Reynolds 2016 NZ).

### 2.6 Stiff aging arteries

> **For you, in plain words.** Arteries get stiffer with age — collagen accumulates, elastin frays. The top number (systolic) climbs even when the bottom number (diastolic) stays normal. This is the "isolated systolic hypertension of aging" pattern. *(Mitchell 2010 PWV review; Vlachopoulos 2010 aortic stiffness meta)*

#### Open the science

Aortic stiffening — measured as pulse wave velocity (PWV) — is independently predictive of cardiovascular events. Mechanism: cross-linking of collagen by advanced glycation end-products (AGEs), elastin fragmentation, calcification. Targeted by **cocoa flavanols** (modest PWV improvement), **dietary nitrate**, **resistance training** (modest), **Mediterranean diet pattern** (PREDIMED), **glycaemic restraint** (cuts AGE formation), **vitamin K2 MK-7** (Rotterdam cohort — controversial), and **aged garlic** (Williams 2005 Australia — small PWV signal). Honest note: this is the hardest node to move; the best lever is preventing it in your 30s-50s.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Fourteen over-the-counter molecules with non-US monograph or named trial backing. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on red yeast rice, niacin, fish-oil mixed EPA+DHA, and CoQ10 for primary prevention. *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; Cochrane omega-3 Abdelhamid 2018)*

### 3.2 Fish oil — the honest REDUCE-IT-vs-STRENGTH split

> **For you, in plain words.** "Fish oil for the heart" is more complicated than the bottle says. Purified EPA-only at 4 g/day (Vascepa) cut events in REDUCE-IT (2019) — but the mixed EPA+DHA formulation in STRENGTH (2020) and COMET-EE (2018) was null. Most generic supermarket fish oil is mixed EPA+DHA at much lower dose. For primary prevention, the meta-analyses (Cochrane Abdelhamid 2018) found little benefit on hard endpoints. We keep omega-3 as one layer — for the omega-3-index target, for the inflammation node, for the triglyceride-lowering signal at 2-4 g/day — but not as a guarantee. *(REDUCE-IT Bhatt 2019; STRENGTH Nicholls 2020; Cochrane Abdelhamid 2018)*

**The deeper layer.** REDUCE-IT used icosapent ethyl (purified EPA only) 4 g/day on top of statin in high-CV-risk patients with triglycerides 135-499; saw 25% relative risk reduction. STRENGTH used a mixed EPA+DHA carboxylic acid 4 g/day in similar population; null. Mechanism debate ongoing — EPA-only effect may be specific, or mineral-oil placebo in REDUCE-IT was pro-atherogenic. For OTC: aim for omega-3 index 8-11% (DTC labs: Omega Quant), use a high-EPA preparation (NOW Ultra Omega-3, Nordic Naturals ProEPA, Pure Encapsulations EPA Ultimate) at 2-4 g/day, get measured. **Grade B** for triglyceride lowering and omega-3-index correction. **Grade C** for primary CV prevention with mixed EPA+DHA. **Grade B** for secondary prevention with EPA-only purified preparation.

### 3.3 Red yeast rice — the honest monacolin-K caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Red yeast rice naturally contains monacolin K — the same molecule as the prescription statin lovastatin. So it CAN lower LDL meaningfully, but it carries the same statin risks (muscle pain, liver enzymes, drug interactions) without standardised dosing. The EU has restricted maximum monacolin K to 3 mg/serving. We do NOT recommend self-titrating red yeast rice as a "natural alternative" to statins — if you have decided you want lovastatin-class action, doing it knowingly via §10.7.3 is more honest. *(EFSA 2018 monacolin K restriction; CHRP trial Lu 2008 China)*

**The deeper layer.** Xuezhikang (Chinese standardised red yeast rice, 5-13 mg monacolin K/day) was tested in CHRP (n=4,870, post-MI Chinese population) — 45% relative risk reduction in major coronary events over 4.5 years. EU now restricts OTC red yeast rice to ≤3 mg monacolin K/serving (EFSA 2018) due to inconsistent batch potency and hepatotoxicity case reports. Pair with CoQ10 100-200 mg if used. **Grade B** in standardised Chinese formulation; **Grade C** in EU-restricted OTC form; **Documented for transparency, not recommended** as a stealth-statin path.

### 3.5 What we DO NOT recommend (honest exclusion list)

> **For you, in plain words.** Niacin (vitamin B3) at high doses raises HDL and lowers LDL — but AIM-HIGH (2011) and HPS2-THRIVE (2014) found no event reduction and increased adverse effects. We do NOT include high-dose niacin. Also excluded: high-dose vitamin E for primary CV prevention (HOPE-TOO 2005 null and signal of harm at high dose), routine antioxidant cocktails (Bjelakovic 2014 Cochrane null/harm), Lipoflavonoid for tinnitus claims, and megadose vitamin D for CV (VITAL 2019 null on CV events). *(AIM-HIGH 2011; HPS2-THRIVE 2014; HOPE-TOO 2005; VITAL 2019; Bjelakovic 2014)*

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six healing traditions described heart-and-vessel disease centuries before cardiology existed. ### 4.2 Ayurveda — *Hridroga* (heart disease)

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda calls heart disease *hridroga* — usually a mixed *vata-kapha* picture in modern lifestyle. The single most-studied Ayurvedic cardio herb is Arjuna bark — Indian trials (Bharani 2002, Dwivedi 2007) showed real benefit in stable angina and mild left-ventricular dysfunction. Liver/kidney panels at month 3 if on long-term use. *(Charaka Sutra ch. 30; Bharani 2002 India; Dwivedi 2007 India; CCRAS Arjuna monograph)*

**The deeper layer.** Classical category: *hridya* (cardiotonic) rasayanas, *vata-kapha hridroga*. ## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** A validated upper-arm blood-pressure cuff is the single highest-evidence biophysical investment you can make — it changes behaviour. Add a wearable for HRV, a single-lead ECG (Apple Watch, KardiaMobile) for AFib screening if family history or palpitations, and a home sleep test once. Sauna, cold immersion, slow-paced breathing devices, and PEMF are tier-2. *(Kostis 2002 home BP behaviour; Stergiou 2018 home BP RCT; Apple Heart Study Perez 2019 AFib detection)*

**Quick start, this week.**
1. Buy an Omron 5 Series upper-arm cuff (or Withings BPM Core, or Microlife WatchBP) — $50-150. Measure morning + evening for 7 days, take the average of days 2-7. That is your real BP.
2. If you have palpitations or a family AFib history, buy a KardiaMobile 6L ($90) or use the Apple Watch ECG.
3. If you snore, are overweight, or feel exhausted on waking — order one Lofta WatchPAT home sleep test ($200-300).
4. Walk 30 min after dinner every night.
5. Practice 5 minutes of 6-breath-per-minute slow breathing at bedtime.

### 5.2 Home blood-pressure cuff — the most important device

> **For you, in plain words.** A validated upper-arm cuff used twice a day for 7 days gives a much better picture than a clinic snapshot — and a 7-day home average over 135/85 mmHg is the threshold most international guidelines (NICE UK, ESH Europe, AHA, Japan JSH) treat as hypertension. *(Stergiou 2018 home BP RCT; ESH 2023 home BP)*

**The deeper layer.** Choose ONLY validated upper-arm cuffs — STRIDE-BP / British Hypertension Society / dabl Educational Trust validation lists. Validated models include: Omron 5-7-10 Series, Withings BPM Core (also ECG), Microlife WatchBP (also AFib screen), A&D UA-651SL, Welch Allyn Home 1700. Wrist cuffs are less accurate. Measurement protocol: sitting 5 min rest, feet flat, arm at heart level, no caffeine/exercise/smoke 30 min before, two readings 1 min apart, AM + PM, 7 days. Discard day 1; average days 2-7.

### 5.3 Home single-lead ECG — for palpitations or AFib screening

> **For you, in plain words.** Apple Watch Series 4+, Withings ScanWatch, KardiaMobile single- or 6-lead, and Fitbit ECG can all detect AFib reasonably well in symptomatic adults. The Apple Heart Study (Perez 2019, n=419,297) found ~84% positive predictive value vs simultaneous patch monitor. (FDA De Novo + CE mark — regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor.) *(Apple Heart Study Perez 2019; mSToPS Steinhubl 2018)*

**Practical.** Use these for SYMPTOMATIC tracking (you feel a flutter — take a reading) and for screening if family history of AFib or stroke. They are NOT diagnostic-grade for the asymptomatic general population — false positives are common. If a device flags AFib, do not panic — capture the trace, repeat, and arrange formal diagnostic workup on your own initiative.

### 5.4 Home pulse-wave-velocity / arterial-age estimators

> **For you, in plain words.** The Withings Body Cardio scale estimates pulse-wave velocity from the soles of your feet; Healthcheck-style smartphone apps estimate arterial age from heart-rate variability. Reasonable trend trackers, not gold-standard measurements. *(Withings BPM Core PWV; Cardio:Wave research)*

### 5.5 Home cholesterol and apoB testing — DTC labs

> **For you, in plain words.** Order a DTC lipid panel including apoB and a once-in-a-lifetime Lp(a) from LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, Thriva (UK), Cerascreen (EU), True Test (India), Walk-In Lab, or InsideTracker — repeat at baseline / month 6 / month 12. *(Sniderman 2019 apoB; LetsGetChecked panel)*

**Targets (the user discusses with their own physician if they have one):** apoB <80 mg/dL (high-risk targets lower, per ESC 2019 dyslipidaemia guideline); LDL-C <100 mg/dL primary prevention; non-HDL-C <130; triglycerides <150; HDL-C >40 (M) / >50 (F); Lp(a) <50 mg/dL.

### 5.6 Wearables for HRV, sleep and resting heart rate

> **For you, in plain words.** Oura Ring, Whoop strap, Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit and Polar all track resting heart rate, HRV, and sleep stages — useful for the §2.4 driver and for catching overtraining or illness. *(Wang 2018 HRV CV review; Snyder 2019 wearables review)*

### 5.7 Sauna + cold immersion — the Laukkanen lever

> **For you, in plain words.** The Finnish Laukkanen cohort (n=2,315, 20-year follow-up) found regular sauna use (4-7×/week) linked to 50% lower fatal CV events. Pair with a 30-60 second cold splash for vagal tone. *(Laukkanen 2015 Finland JAMA Int Med; Laukkanen 2018 update)*

**Practical.** Home options: Sunlighten Solo (full-spectrum infrared, ~$2,000-4,500), HigherDOSE infrared blanket (~$600), Therasage (~$800-1,500), JNH Lifestyles ($1,200-3,000). 20 min at 50-70°C, 3-7×/week. Cold immersion: Plunge tub ($5,000+), Edge Tub ($1,500), or simply a bathtub with ice ($20-50). **DO NOT use** sauna with unstable angina, severe aortic stenosis, recent MI, decompensated heart failure, or after alcohol; **DO NOT use** cold immersion in uncontrolled hypertension, recent MI, severe CAD, or pregnancy without clearance.

### 5.8 Slow-paced breathing devices

> **For you, in plain words.** Devices like RESPeRATE (Israeli FDA De Novo-cleared — regulatory-path context only; the evidence anchor is the Schein 2001 Israeli RCT) and free apps like Breathwrk, Awesome Breathing and the Apple Watch Mindfulness coach pace you to 6 breaths per minute, dropping BP modestly. *(Schein 2001 Israel; Joseph 2005 6-bpm BP)*

**Practical.** 15 min/day at 6 breaths/minute (inhale 5 s, exhale 5 s). RESPeRATE ($300+), Resmate, or any free 6-bpm timer/app.

### 5.9 HRV biofeedback hardware

> **For you, in plain words.** HeartMath Inner Balance ($179), Lief ($299), Polar H10 + EliteHRV (free app + $80 chest strap), and Garmin/Whoop/Oura HRV trends give you real-time feedback on your stress-relaxation state. *(McCraty 2015 HeartMath review)*

### 5.10 Continuous glucose monitor — metabolic-cardio coupling

> **For you, in plain words.** A 14-day CGM (Stelo from Dexcom, Lingo from Abbott, or prescription Libre/Dexcom) shows how YOUR food and stress spike YOUR glucose — useful for the §2.5 driver. Once-per-quarter 14-day window is plenty. *(Hall 2018 Stanford CGM in non-diabetics)*

### 5.11 Soft-shell home hyperbaric oxygen (optional)

> **For you, in plain words.** Soft-shell home HBOT chambers (OxyHealth Vitaeris 320, Summit to Sea Grand Dive) at 1.3 ATA offer mild oxygen enrichment. Cardio evidence is mechanistic only; not in our core protocol. *(Mechanistic; Hadanny 2020 Israel)*

### 5.12 Red-light therapy panels (optional adjunct)

> **For you, in plain words.** 660 + 810-850 nm red/NIR panels (Mito Red Light, Joov, Hooga) have mostly skin and mitochondrial evidence; cardio-specific evidence is preliminary. Optional adjunct, not a cornerstone. *(Hamblin 2018 PBM review)*

### 5.13 Home PEMF mats (optional adjunct)

> **For you, in plain words.** Bemer mat, Pulse Centers, Healthy Wave — small Eastern-European signal for circulation; **DO NOT use** with a pacemaker, in pregnancy, or with active cancer. *(Bemer published protocols)*

### 5.14 Hearing-aids and oral health (the surprise cardio levers)

> **For you, in plain words.** Periodontal disease (gum disease) is independently associated with CV events — see the oral-gum-breath dossier. Brush, floss, water-pick, and treat gums seriously. Hearing aids are not directly cardio — but untreated hearing loss tracks with cognitive decline, which tracks with cardio outcomes. *(Tonetti 2013 Italy periodontal-CV; Lin 2013 hearing loss-cognition)*

### 5.15 What to skip (in cardio context)

> **For you, in plain words.** Skip Kapalbhati and Bhastrika pranayama if you have hypertension or palpitations; skip very-cold immersion if uncontrolled hypertension; skip megadose niacin; skip "detox" colon cleanses; skip extreme sodium restriction below 1500 mg/day in elderly (J-curve risk). *(AYUSH pranayama contraindications; O'Donnell 2014 NEJM J-curve)*

### 7.7 Exercise periodization — the highest-evidence cardio lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Aerobic exercise is the single best non-drug cardio intervention — every 3.5 mL/kg/min of VO2max (one MET) lower mortality risk ~10-25%. Aim for 150 min Zone 2 + 2 strength sessions + 1 HIIT/week. *(Kodama 2009 VO2max meta; Mandsager 2018 fitness-mortality; HUNT3 Norway)*

**Weekly.**
- Mon / Wed / Fri — Zone 2 cardio 45-60 min at 60-70% HRmax (you can still talk in full sentences but not sing). Brisk walk, swim, cycle, row, jog.
- Tue / Thu — strength training 45 min: compound lifts (squat, deadlift, hinge, press, pull, row), 3 sets × 6-12 reps.
- Sat — HIIT 20 min (e.g. 4×4 Norwegian protocol: 4 min at 85-95% HRmax, 3 min rest, repeat 4×) OR sport. **NOT recommended** in uncontrolled HTN or known CAD without prior clinic-route clearance.
- Sun — restorative yoga + 60-90 min low-stress nature walk.

**Monthly cadence.** Week 1 — full intensity. Week 2 — moderate. Week 3 — deload (≤70% volume) + extra sauna + Yoga Nidra. Week 4 — full intensity.

**Time-restricted eating** (16:8 or 14:10) — modest CV signal in some trials, but null in others (Manoogian 2022 review). Optional, not mandatory; **NOT for underweight, frail elderly, pregnant, breastfeeding, or anyone with a history of disordered eating**.

### 10.5 Exercise / Sleep / Caffeine timing rules

- **Caffeine cutoff 14:00** — protects sleep. *(Drake 2013)*
- **Last meal ≥3 h before bed** — protects reflux and sleep. *(Iwakiri 2022)*
- **Magnesium glycinate** PM (sleep support).
- **Ashwagandha** ≥2 h before bed (vivid dreams in sensitive users).
- **Rhodiola / Eleuthero** AM only.
- **Melatonin (if used)** 30-60 min before sleep, 0.3 mg physiological dose preferred.
- **Probiotic** AM empty stomach OR with first meal.
- **HIIT** not within 4 h of bed — sympathetic activation.
- **Cold immersion** not within 2 h of bed — alerting.
- **Sauna** afternoon ideal; not within 2 h of bed (heat-load delays sleep onset).
- **BP measurement** AM (before food, caffeine, exercise) + PM (before dinner).

- **Tobacco** — zero, including vaping and second-hand.

- **TL;DR.** **Statins** (atorvastatin, rosuvastatin, simvastatin, pravastatin, pitavastatin) — 4S (1994, n=4,444, 30% mortality reduction), JUPITER (Ridker 2008, n=17,802, rosuvastatin in low-LDL/high-CRP), HPS (2002), CARDS — large body of hard-endpoint evidence. **Grade A**. - **TL;DR.** **Ezetimibe** — IMPROVE-IT (2015, n=18,144) added 6% MACE reduction on top of statin. **Grade A**. Cheap. *(IMPROVE-IT 2015)*
- **TL;DR.** **Bempedoic acid** — CLEAR-Outcomes (2023, n=13,970) reduced MACE in statin-intolerant patients. **Grade A** for statin-intolerant. *(CLEAR-Outcomes Nissen 2023)*
