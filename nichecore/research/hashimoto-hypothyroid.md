## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What Hashimoto actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Hashimoto's is autoimmune thyroiditis — your immune system, for reasons it has never fully explained, decided your thyroid gland is foreign tissue and is slowly destroying it. The result is an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism). You can slow the destruction with the right nutrition + immune + stress levers — but you cannot regrow a thyroid that has scarred. *(Caturegli 2014 Hashimoto historical review; Antonelli 2020 autoimmune thyroiditis)*

### 1.2 The four levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: order a comprehensive thyroid DTC panel (TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 + TPO Ab + Tg Ab + reverse T3). This week: start selenium 200 µg/day + zinc 15 mg + vitamin D3 5000 IU (if low). This month: trial a 6-week gluten elimination + log basal-body temperature every AM. Levothyroxine and T3 stay in §10.7.3. *(Toulis 2010; Krysiak 2019)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Hashimoto's is usually framed as "your thyroid is broken, take levothyroxine for life." A more honest reframe: Hashimoto is the **end-organ readout of four overlapping drivers** — (i) **autoimmune molecular mimicry + gut permeability** that triggers and sustains the antibody attack; (ii) **selenium / zinc / iodine micronutrient imbalance** that the thyroid's own machinery requires; (iii) **HPA-axis dysregulation + chronic stress + sleep loss** that worsen autoimmune activity and T4-to-T3 conversion; and (iv) **established thyroid scarring** that is partially irreversible — this is the lever the protocol cannot fully move and where clinic-route thyroid hormone replacement honestly belongs. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs an autoimmune-friendly diet + selenium + zinc + careful iodine assessment + stress / sleep / gut repair can slow antibody titres, improve quality of life, and in subclinical cases sometimes prevent progression to overt hypothyroidism. In overt hypothyroidism with established scarring, the protocol amplifies — but does not replace — thyroid hormone replacement coordinated with the user's own physician.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers run Hashimoto — autoimmune-gut signal, micronutrient imbalance, HPA-axis stress, and thyroid scarring. Naming them lets every food, supplement, breath and lab test aim at the right one. *(Caturegli 2014; Antonelli 2020)*

### 2.2 Autoimmune molecular mimicry + gut permeability

> **For you, in plain words.** Hashimoto antibodies (anti-TPO, anti-Tg) often cross-react with gluten and other food proteins. Healing the gut and trialling gluten elimination can lower antibody levels. *(Lerner 2017 gluten + autoimmune thyroid; Krysiak 2019 small RCT)*

**The deeper layer.** Molecular mimicry between gliadin (wheat protein) and thyroid peroxidase has been demonstrated in vitro (Naiyer 2008); intestinal permeability ("leaky gut") is elevated in autoimmune thyroid (Sategna-Guidetti 1998 celiac-thyroid overlap; ~5-10% of Hashimoto patients have undiagnosed celiac). Krysiak 2019 (Polish small RCT, n=34) showed gluten-free diet reduced TPO + Tg antibodies in non-celiac TPO-positive women. The Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) Abbott 2019 small N=17 trial showed quality-of-life and medication-reduction signal in Hashimoto. Targeted by **gluten-free trial (8 weeks minimum)**, **dairy elimination trial if AIP-protocol**, **L-glutamine + zinc carnosine for gut healing**, **probiotic strain L. rhamnosus GG + B. lactis**, **fermented foods (slow-titrate — many Hashimoto users react to high-tyramine ferments)**, and **periodontal hygiene** (gum inflammation worsens autoimmunity). **Synthesis.** Gut-immune axis is the upstream lever Hashimoto medicine has historically ignored.

### 2.3 Micronutrient imbalance (selenium, zinc, iodine, iron)

> **For you, in plain words.** The thyroid is a micronutrient-greedy gland. Selenium and zinc are nearly always low in active Hashimoto. Iodine is a special case — HIGH-DOSE iodine can WORSEN Hashimoto. *(Toulis 2010 + Drutel 2013 selenium meta; Kasagi 2003 iodine warning)*

**The deeper layer.** Thyroid peroxidase (TPO) requires iodine. Deiodinase enzymes (D1, D2, D3) that convert T4 to active T3 require selenium. Iron is required for TPO function. Zinc is required for thyroid hormone receptor function. Toulis 2010 meta-analysis + Drutel 2013 systematic review (Greek and European trials) showed selenium 200 µg/day reduced TPO antibody titres by ~30% over 3-6 months in Hashimoto. Kasagi 2003 (Japanese study) showed iodine excess WORSENS Hashimoto in iodine-replete populations — this is the load-bearing safety warning. The Mediterranean and Asian populations differ enormously in iodine status: Japanese-Korean seaweed-heavy diets vs Western iodised-salt-only intake. Targeted by **selenium 200 µg/day (Brazil nuts 1-2/day OR selenomethionine supplement)**, **zinc 15-30 mg/day (with copper 1-2 mg)**, **iron repletion ONLY IF FERRITIN <30** (don't iron-load on suspicion), **iodine — TEST FIRST via DTC spot-urine; supplement only if deficient (vegan, no-seafood, no-iodised-salt populations) and even then go LOW (150 µg/day RDA, not kelp-megadoses)**, and **vitamin D3 5000 IU if 25(OH)D <40 ng/mL** (autoimmune-favoured range is higher than general population). **Synthesis.** Get the nutrients right, but be conservative with iodine — this is where well-meaning thyroid protocols often do harm.

### 2.4 HPA-axis dysregulation

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress + sleep loss + cortisol dysregulation worsen autoimmune activity AND slow the conversion of T4 to active T3. The HPA-thyroid axis is bidirectional. *(Tsigos 2002 HPA review; Helmreich 2005 cortisol-thyroid axis)*

**The deeper layer.** Elevated cortisol suppresses TSH at the pituitary AND increases reverse T3 (the inactive metabolite that competes with active T3 at the receptor). Cortisol dysregulation activates Th17/Treg imbalance (the autoimmune driver). Sleep <6h independently worsens autoimmune activity. Targeted by **MBSR / mindfulness (Pradhan 2007 small signal for autoimmune; Wells 2014 wider mind-body)**, **CBT for autoimmune-coping**, **ashwagandha (Sharma 2018 small Indian trial showed T3/T4 rise in subclinical hypo — but caution autoimmune flare in some users)**, **adaptogenic herbs (rhodiola, eleuthero — graded carefully in autoimmune)**, **sleep hygiene with consistent window**, **HRV biofeedback (HeartMath, Lief)**, **yoga + pranayama (Sarvangasana is contraindicated in active Hashimoto with thyroid nodules)**, and **DUTCH cortisol panel for HPA mapping**. **Synthesis.** This lever often gives the largest quality-of-life gains in users whose TSH is "normal" but who still feel exhausted — the conversion + reverse-T3 issue.

### 2.5 Established thyroid scarring — partly irreversible

> **For you, in plain words.** Once Hashimoto has fibrosed and destroyed thyroid tissue, that tissue does not grow back. The home protocol can slow further damage but cannot regenerate a destroyed gland. This is where thyroid hormone replacement honestly belongs — in §10.7.3, coordinated with the user's own physician. *(Caturegli 2014; Wiersinga 2019 ETA)*

**The deeper layer.** Hashimoto histology shows lymphocytic infiltration → germinal centres → atrophic thyroiditis with fibrosis. The atrophic phase is largely irreversible — the gland cannot regenerate. SHIP-Trend (Völzke 2017 German population study) tracked progression: 5-year subclinical → overt rate ~5-25%. The protocol's role in established scarring is amplification (better conversion, better quality of life, lower antibody titres preventing further damage) — not regeneration. Targeted by **honest recognition + thyroid hormone replacement coordination in §10.7.3**, **continued selenium + zinc to protect remaining tissue**, **monitoring TSH + Free T3 + reverse T3 every 8-12 weeks during titration**, **thyroid ultrasound annually if nodule history**, and **partnering with prescriber rather than fighting them**. **Synthesis.** The home protocol is upstream and adjunctive. The pharmaceutical thyroid hormone is honestly the only lever that replaces what is gone.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 Ten OTC molecules — table

### 3.2 The honest iodine caveat (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Most well-meaning Hashimoto guides recommend kelp, seaweed and iodine drops. The Japanese Kasagi 2003 study and multiple European cohorts have shown HIGH-DOSE IODINE WORSENS Hashimoto in iodine-replete populations. You MUST test your iodine status first (DTC spot-urine through Thriva, LetsGetChecked, Cerascreen) and supplement ONLY if deficient and even then at modest RDA-level doses (150-220 µg/day), NEVER kelp megadoses. *(Kasagi 2003 Japan; Zimmermann 2009 Europe)*

**The deeper layer.** The thyroid requires iodine for hormone synthesis — but in autoimmune thyroiditis, excess iodine increases TPO immunogenicity and accelerates lymphocytic infiltration. Kasagi 2003 (Tokyo, n=181) showed restricting iodine intake normalised thyroid function in 78% of newly-diagnosed Hashimoto patients with elevated urinary iodine. The Western blanket recommendation to "supplement iodine for thyroid health" conflates iodine-deficient hypothyroidism (a separate condition, prevalent in non-iodised-salt countries) with autoimmune Hashimoto. Vegan + no-seafood + no-iodised-salt populations are at deficiency risk; everyone else should test before supplementing. **The iodine dose ceiling in active Hashimoto is the RDA (150 µg/day) — not kelp.**

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 Home DTC thyroid panel

> **For you, in plain words.** Order a comprehensive thyroid panel from a direct-to-consumer lab without needing a GP visit. The full panel: TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 + reverse T3 + TPO Ab + Tg Ab. *(LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, Paloma Health, ThyroidUK)*

**Quick start.** Order at baseline, week 12, month 6, month 12. Paloma Health offers a Hashimoto-focused panel ~$99. ThyroidUK direct lab panels. Avoid biotin supplements 72 h before draw (biotin interferes with thyroid immunoassays).

#### Open the science

The full home panel must include: TSH (pituitary signal), Free T4 (storage form), Free T3 (active form), reverse T3 (inactive metabolite that competes), TPO Ab (Hashimoto antibody), Tg Ab (second Hashimoto antibody). Many users find Free T3 and reverse T3 reveal the conversion problem that TSH alone hides. The autoimmune sub-types are most reliably tracked by TPO and Tg antibody titres over time (every 3-6 months).

### 5.2 Home basal-body temperature

> **For you, in plain words.** First-morning oral or axillary temperature (Barnes test approximation) tracks metabolic rate. Below 36.5°C (97.4°F) suggests sluggish metabolism; track trend daily. *(Barnes 1976 historical method)*

**Quick start.** Cheap digital thermometer; measure orally first thing AM before getting out of bed; log daily; trend over 30 days. Pair with TSH/Free T4 — these tell different stories.

### 5.3 Home iodine spot-urine

> **For you, in plain words.** A direct-to-consumer urine iodine test tells you whether you are deficient (<100 µg/L), adequate (100-200), or excess (>300). Test BEFORE supplementing iodine in any form. *(Thriva UK iodine panel; Hakala Labs)*

**Quick start.** Hakala Labs iodine spot-urine $50; Thriva UK includes iodine in panels; Doctor's Data. If deficient: 150 µg RDA only. If excess: cut kelp/seaweed/iodine drops immediately.

### 5.4 Home cortisol-thyroid DUTCH

> **For you, in plain words.** A direct-to-consumer dried-urine 24-h cortisol test (DUTCH Complete or Precision Analytical) maps the HPA-thyroid coupling — useful when TSH is "normal" but fatigue persists. *(DUTCH validation studies)*

**Quick start.** DUTCH Complete ~$300-400 through a licensed practitioner OR DTC equivalent; map the cortisol curve + thyroid metabolite pattern; useful for §2.4 driver.

### 5.5 Home red-light + infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Photobiomodulation (PBM) of the thyroid area shows preliminary evidence for autoimmune thyroid; Höfling 2013 Brazilian RCT (n=43) — small but positive. Infrared sauna 3-4×/week supports detoxification + immune modulation. *(Höfling 2013 Brazil)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red Light MitoMID or Joov Solo: 5-10 min at thyroid region (anterior neck), 2-3×/week. Distance 12 inches. Infrared sauna 130-140°F × 20 min, 3-4×/week.

#### Open the science

Höfling 2013 (n=43 RCT) — 830 nm laser PBM 10 sessions reduced anti-TPO antibodies + reduced levothyroxine dose in chronic autoimmune thyroiditis. Small N, but the only autoimmune-thyroid-specific PBM RCT in the literature. Graded C until larger trials confirm. Don't expect cornerstone effects — but it is a safe adjunct.

### 10.5 Timing rules

- **Levothyroxine 06:00 empty stomach + 60 min food gap + ≥4 h gap from divalent minerals (Ca, Fe, Mg, Zn) and ≥4 h gap from coffee + ≥4 h gap from soy.** *(Centanni 2006)*

- Caffeine cut-off **14:00**.
- Selenium AM with food (with breakfast Brazil nuts as backup food source).
- Zinc AM (with copper 1-2 mg).
- D3 + K2 with fat-containing meal.
- Magnesium PM (sleep + bowel motility + ensures ≥4 h from morning levo).
- Ashwagandha — TRIAL CAREFULLY; if autoimmune flare, discontinue.
- Probiotic AM empty stomach OR with first food.
- **Heavy meal + supine within 3 h** → reflux risk.
- **Sauna + alcohol** → never combine.

#### Open the science

Wiersinga 2019 European Thyroid Association guideline reviewed the T4/T3 combination question — the conclusion: most patients do well on T4 monotherapy, a minority (~10-20%) benefit from a careful T4+T3 trial coordinated with prescriber. Bunevicius 1999 Lithuanian RCT initially suggested T4+T3 benefit but replication trials (Clyde 2003 USA, Sawka 2003 Canada) were mixed. Hennessey 2018 reviewed natural desiccated thyroid honestly — the trial evidence is thin but preference data (Hoang 2013) showed some users prefer NDT. The Drutel 2013 meta-analysis on selenium showed dose-response benefit up to 200 µg/day with no clear additional benefit at higher doses + toxicity risk above 300 µg/day (Rayman 2012 selenium ceiling). LDN literature in Hashimoto is limited to case series — graded H. The SHIP-Trend (Völzke 2017) German population study tracked Hashimoto progression rates — useful for setting expectations on the timeline.

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

> **For you, in plain words.** What is going on, what to expect, and what NOT to expect.

You have Hashimoto's thyroiditis — your immune system, for reasons it has never fully explained, decided your thyroid gland is foreign tissue and is slowly attacking it. The result is an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism). This protocol is different from the "your thyroid is broken, take levothyroxine for life" path. It works UPSTREAM on the four drivers: autoimmune-gut signal (gluten-elimination + AIP), micronutrient imbalance (selenium + zinc + careful iodine), HPA-axis stress (sleep + MBSR + ashwagandha with caution), and the established thyroid scarring (where thyroid hormone replacement honestly belongs in §10.7.3).

What to expect: in TPO+ euthyroid and subclinical sub-types, the antibody titres often drop 25-40% in 6 months with consistent execution. Quality of life often improves first, lab numbers follow. In overt hypothyroidism (TSH >10), the protocol amplifies but does NOT replace thyroid hormone replacement — levothyroxine and (in some users) T3 are honest options coordinated with your prescriber. The home protocol stack: selenium (Brazil nuts + supplement) + zinc + vitamin D3 + omega-3 + gluten-free trial + AIP trial if antibodies don't drop + Mediterranean food + careful iodine assessment + MBSR + consistent sleep + selective herbs (Kanchanara Guggulu, Ashwagandha with autoimmune-flare monitoring).

What NOT to expect: a regrown thyroid. Hashimoto scarring is partly irreversible — once tissue is destroyed, it does not come back. The protocol slows further damage and improves conversion + quality of life. If you are overt-hypothyroid, you likely need replacement medication — that is honest, not a failure. Iodine megadoses can WORSEN Hashimoto — test first.

The sovereign ethos: this is an OTC, home-based, independent path. **No prescription, no clinic and no hospital visit is required in the home regime.** If you choose to keep a doctor in the loop — and overt-hypothyroid users should, especially during pregnancy — that is your personal decision.
