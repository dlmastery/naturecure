## 1. Executive Summary

Skin ageing is conventionally siloed as a "cosmetic dermatology" niche treated with prescription tretinoin, clinic chemical peels, fractional CO₂ / erbium / picosecond lasers, neuromodulators (botulinum toxin), hyaluronic-acid fillers, thread lifts, HIFU and surgical face-lift. The integrative reframing is different: visible skin ageing is the **dermal-epidermal manifestation of a four-node systemic loop** — (i) UV / pollution / infrared / blue-light exposome triggering MMP-1/3/9, neutrophil elastase, collagen degradation and elastosis, (ii) age-dependent collagen + elastin synthesis decline (post-30y women lose ~1% dermal collagen/year; post-menopause that accelerates to 2–3%) compounded by estrogen withdrawal, (iii) advanced glycation end-products (AGEs) cross-linking collagen → yellow-brown chromophore + loss of elasticity (CML, pentosidine) driven by glucose, fructose, lipid oxidation, smoking, and (iv) cellular senescence (p16-INK4a SASP), mitochondrial dysfunction, hyaluronic-acid loss and microbiome shift. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously blocks the exposome, supplies collagen + elastin substrate, blocks AGE formation, and quenches senescence + oxidative stress addresses the upstream drivers — not just the visible wrinkle.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Exposome: UV + pollution + IR-A + HEV + smoking + circadian disruption.** UV (290–400 nm) generates ROS → AP-1 → MMP-1/3/9 → collagen degradation, AP-1 → TGF-β suppression → ↓ collagen synthesis. UVA penetrates to dermis; UVB drives sunburn + DNA damage. **Infrared-A (760–1400 nm)** is the underrecognised major driver — penetrates deeper than UV, generates dermal ROS, upregulates MMP-1/9. **HEV blue light (400–500 nm)** induces ROS and pigmentation. **Particulate matter (PM2.5)** induces aryl-hydrocarbon receptor → CYP1A1 → ROS → MMP. **Smoking + e-cigarette vapour** drive collagen + elastin degradation (smoker's "wrinkle gallery"). **Circadian disruption** reduces nocturnal collagen synthesis (per-2/clock-2 rhythm). Targeted by mineral SPF 50+ (iron oxide-tinted for HEV), wide-brim hat, UV sunglasses, particulate-filter face mask in high-PM zones, antioxidant cluster (Vitamin C, E, ferulic, niacinamide), smoking cessation, blue-light filters on screens, consistent sleep. **Synthesis:** Block the exposome or no other lever holds.

**2.2 Collagen + elastin substrate + hormonal decline.** Type I + III collagen form 80–90% of dermal weight. Synthesis requires glycine + proline + lysine + vitamin C + copper + zinc + manganese. Post-30y collagen synthesis declines ~1%/year; post-menopause estrogen withdrawal accelerates loss 2–3%/year for ~5 years (Brincat London cohort). Elastin synthesis essentially stops post-puberty — elastin in adult skin is what was made before age 16, slowly degraded. Hyaluronic acid declines progressively. Targeted by oral hydrolysed collagen peptides 10–15 g + vitamin C, marine collagen / bovine collagen Type I+III, bone broth daily, oral hyaluronic acid, copper + zinc + manganese cofactors, silica (horsetail / bamboo), Vitamin C + E + ferulic topical, phytoestrogens for menopausal subset (red clover, soy isoflavones — **avoid in estrogen-sensitive cancer history**), Ayurveda Chyawanprash, Kumkumadi Tailam. **Synthesis:** Without substrate, the cell cannot rebuild.

**2.3 Glycation + AGE cross-linking + oxidative-glycative axis.** Glucose, fructose, and reactive carbonyls react non-enzymatically with collagen lysine + arginine residues → Schiff base → Amadori product → carboxymethyl-lysine (CML), pentosidine, glucosepane (irreversible cross-links). Glycated collagen is **stiffer, yellow-brown, and resistant to MMP turnover** — the "ageing skin yellowness". AGE-RAGE signalling on dermal cells perpetuates inflammation. Drivers: high-GI diet, fructose, soda, alcohol, smoking, hyperglycaemia. Targeted by low-GI Siri Dhanyalu diet, intermittent fasting (if appropriate), benfotiamine (transketolase-driven AGE-RAGE damping), alpha-lipoic acid, carnosine (oral + topical — direct AGE de-glycator), L-carnosine, taurine, NAC, polyphenols (resveratrol, EGCG, polypodium), cinnamon (insulin sensitisation), berberine. **Synthesis:** AGE blockade is the underrecognised anti-ageing lever conventional medicine ignores.

**2.4 Senescence + mitochondrial dysfunction + barrier loss + microbiome shift.** Senescent cells (p16-INK4a positive, β-galactosidase positive) accumulate in aged skin → SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype) → IL-6, IL-8, MMPs, TGF-β suppression — propagating inflammation and degrading neighbouring cells. Dermal mitochondrial mtDNA mutations accumulate. The aged stratum corneum has lower ceramides + free fatty acids → ↑ TEWL → barrier dysfunction. Skin microbiome shifts (decreased *Cutibacterium*, increased *Corynebacterium*). Targeted by senolytic candidates (quercetin + fisetin pulse, dasatinib excluded — Rx-only), NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR — emerging evidence), CoQ10, PQQ, Urolithin A (Mitopure), spermidine (wheat germ-based), spermidine-rich foods (aged cheese — caveat dairy, mushrooms, soybean), gentle prebiotic / postbiotic topicals, ceramide moisturisers. **Synthesis:** Clearing senescent cells + restoring mitochondrial function targets ageing biology directly.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Skin ageing is not "just genetics". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) sun, pollution, blue light from screens, and infrared heat are tearing down collagen via enzymes called MMPs; (2) your fibroblasts are producing about 1% less collagen per year (faster after menopause), and elastin essentially stopped being made in your teens; (3) sugar and fructose are quietly cross-linking your collagen into a stiffer, yellower form (this is called glycation, the same process that browns toast); and (4) senescent "zombie" cells accumulate and pump out inflammatory signals, while your mitochondria and microbiome shift.

This protocol does **not** rely on indefinite prescription tretinoin, hydroquinone, clinic lasers, Botox, fillers or surgery — all of which carry their own costs and risks.
