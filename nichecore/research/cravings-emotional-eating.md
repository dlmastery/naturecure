## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What "cravings" actually are

> **For you, in plain words.** A craving is your body's request for sleep, stable blood sugar, calm, dopamine, or comfort — and it has learned that a cookie delivers all five in 90 seconds. The protocol gives the same five outputs through cleaner inputs. *(Kristeller 2014 mindful eating; Brewer 2018 craving-to-quit)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: lights out by 22:30, no screens after 21:00, a real-food breakfast within 60 min of waking, 10-minute post-meal walk, and order a home iron + B12 + vitamin D + HbA1c panel. Cravings drop by 30-40% in the first 14 days from these alone. *(Spiegel 2004 sleep ghrelin; ADA post-meal walk Diabetes Care)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

#### Open the science

Cravings and emotional eating are conventionally framed as "willpower failure" or treated with prescription combination drugs (lisdexamfetamine for binge-eating disorder; naltrexone-bupropion or topiramate-phentermine for obesity-with-cravings) or therapy alone. The integrative reframing is different: cravings are the **brain-gut-glycaemic-microbiome-circadian network signal** that something upstream is misaligned, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) **HPA-cortisol-emotional axis** wiring reward + emotion toward calorie-dense palatable foods, (ii) **dopamine-reward sensitisation** to hyperpalatable industrial food (sugar-fat-salt) hijacking mesolimbic D1/D2 signalling, (iii) **glycaemic volatility** with reactive hypoglycaemia 60-120 min post-meal triggering acute carb cravings, and (iv) **gut microbiome dysbiosis** with low SCFA producers + serotonin precursor depletion driving cravings via vagal-gut-brain signalling. **Synthesis.** A protocol that simultaneously dampens HPA, re-sensitises dopamine, stabilises glycaemia, rebuilds the microbiome, and trains affect-regulation skills yields durable craving reduction — cravings become a signal you respond to, not a force you resist.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Stress wires you to want sugar; industrial food retrains your reward circuits; blood sugar swings drive 3 pm and 9 pm crashes; the gut microbes that should feed satiety signals are starving. Each driver gets its own intervention layer. *(Adam 2007 stress-eating; Volkow 2013 food reward; Spiegel 2004 sleep)*

### 2.2 Stress-cortisol-emotion axis

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress raises cortisol, which makes your brain want fat + sugar + salt to "feel safer." Ashwagandha, slow-paced breathing, Yoga Nidra, and a regular bedtime are the upstream brake. *(EMA ashwagandha monograph 2024; Lopresti 2019 ashwagandha stress-eating)*

#### Open the science

Chronic stress → elevated cortisol → upregulates NPY and AgRP (orexigenic peptides) → preference for energy-dense palatable food + visceral fat storage. Acute stress activates the amygdala → reward-seeking eating ("comfort food" loop). Adam 2007 *Physiol Behav* showed cortisol responders ate 50% more sweet/fatty food after a stress task than non-responders. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM** (Lopresti 2019 *Medicine* RCT, n=60, Sensoril cortisol -27%), **Rhodiola** (AM only, stimulating), **Holy Basil / Tulsi**, **L-theanine 200 mg**, **magnesium glycinate 300-400 mg PM**, **slow-paced breathing 5-6 breaths/min**, **Yoga Nidra**, **vagal practices** (humming Bhramari, gargling, cold splash). References: [EMA ashwagandha monograph 2024](https://www.ema.europa.eu/) · [Lopresti 2019 Sensoril stress-eating](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [Adam 2007 Physiol Behav stress-eating](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### 2.3 Dopamine-reward sensitisation

> **For you, in plain words.** Industrial sugar-fat-salt food lights up your reward circuits more powerfully than any whole food. Repeated exposure dulls your dopamine receptors, so it takes more food to feel the same "good." Eliminating the stimulus retunes your brain in 30-60 days. *(Volkow 2013 food reward; Schulte 2015 hyperpalatable foods)*

#### Open the science

Hyperpalatable foods (defined by Schulte 2015 *PLOS ONE* as foods >25% calories from fat AND >25% from added sugar, OR >25% fat AND >0.30% sodium) activate the mesolimbic dopamine system (D1/D2 in nucleus accumbens) more powerfully than whole foods. Repeated exposure reduces D2 receptor density (Wang 2001 PET imaging in obesity, similar pattern to substance use disorder). Targeted by **30-60 day elimination of industrial hyperpalatable foods**, **Mucuna pruriens** (natural L-DOPA, AYUSH-Kapikacchu monograph), **Rhodiola rosea** (dopaminergic), ***Gymnema sylvestre*** ("sugar destroyer" — temporarily suppresses sweet taste, retrains perception), **tyrosine 500-1000 mg AM**, **B6 P-5-P** (dopamine cofactor), **dopaminergic exercise** (HIIT, cold immersion 2-3 min). References: [Volkow 2013 reward](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [Schulte 2015 hyperpalatable](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [AYUSH Kapikacchu](https://www.ccras.nic.in/)

### 2.4 Glycaemic volatility

> **For you, in plain words.** A high-sugar breakfast spikes glucose, then crashes it 90 minutes later. The crash feels exactly like a craving. A protein + fat + fibre breakfast and a 10-minute post-meal walk fix the 11 am and 3 pm slumps for most people. *(Diabetes Care post-meal walk; EMA berberine monograph)*

#### Open the science

A high-glycaemic breakfast (cereal, juice, pastry, sweetened coffee) drives a glucose spike, an insulin spike, then reactive hypoglycaemia 60-120 min later. The subjective experience is "hangry" — anxious, foggy, craving more carbs. Chronic insulin resistance compounds it. Continuous glucose monitors (CGMs — Stelo, FreeStyle Libre, Dexcom Lingo) are now consumer-purchasable and reveal each user's personal trigger foods within 7-14 days. Targeted by **protein 30 g + fat + fibre at breakfast**, **15 mL apple cider vinegar pre-meal** (Johnston 2004 *Diabetes Care* postprandial glucose), **cinnamon 1-3 g**, **berberine 500 mg TID with meals**, **post-meal 10-min walk** (DiPietro 2013 *Diabetes Care*, n=10 prediabetics, walks beat single 45-min bout for glycaemic control), **strength training** 2-3×/week. References: [DiPietro 2013 post-meal walk Diabetes Care](https://diabetesjournals.org/care) · [Johnston 2004 vinegar Diabetes Care](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [EMA berberine monograph](https://www.ema.europa.eu/)

### 2.5 Gut-microbiome cravings axis

> **For you, in plain words.** The microbes in your gut talk to your brain via the vagus nerve. When fibre-eating microbes starve and sugar-fed yeast bloom, the gut sends "more sugar" signals upstream. A 30 g fibre, fermented-food, low-sugar reset shifts the conversation. *(Cryan 2019 microbiota-gut-brain; Bested 2013 psychobiotics)*

#### Open the science

The microbiome shapes vagal afferent signalling to the brainstem and hypothalamus; SCFA-producing bacteria (*Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, *Akkermansia*) generate butyrate, propionate and acetate that modulate satiety hormones (PYY, GLP-1) and serotonin precursor (5-HTP) synthesis. Western-pattern diets deplete these strains. Gut-brain feedback shapes food preference: germ-free mice show altered macronutrient preference; fecal-transplant studies in humans show measurable mood and craving shifts. Targeted by **30 g/day fibre** (psyllium, glucomannan, PHGG, oats, legumes, ground flax), **fermented foods daily** (kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi, natto, kanji), **specific psychobiotic strains** (*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* JB-1, *L. helveticus* + *Bifidobacterium longum* Wallace 2010), **polyphenol diversity** (green tea, dark chocolate ≥85%, berries, olive oil EVOO), **eliminate sucralose / aspartame / acesulfame K** (Suez 2014 microbiome dysbiosis from non-nutritive sweeteners). References: [Cryan 2019 microbiota-gut-brain Physiol Rev](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [Wallace 2010 probiotic mood](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) · [Suez 2014 Nature non-nutritive sweeteners](https://www.nature.com/)

### 3.4 Honestly downgraded — chromium, HCA, 5-HTP

> **For you, in plain words.** Three popular craving supplements that the evidence does not support. **Chromium picolinate** — Cochrane Tian 2013 found no benefit for cravings or weight in healthy adults; very small PMS-craving signal (Anderson 1997). **Garcinia cambogia / HCA** — Cochrane Onakpoya 2011 found no clinically meaningful weight effect; hepatotoxicity case reports. **5-HTP** — small acute craving signal in early trials but serotonin syndrome risk if combined with SSRIs, MAOIs, tramadol, St John's Wort; **do not combine**. *(Cochrane Tian 2013; Cochrane Onakpoya 2011)*

#### Open the science

**Chromium picolinate**: Cochrane Tian 2013 review (n=9 trials) found no clinically meaningful weight or craving effect. Anderson 1997 RCT did show modest PMS-craving improvement at 200 µg/day; acceptable as a §3 layer for PMS-specific cravings only, never as a cornerstone. **Garcinia cambogia / HCA**: Cochrane Onakpoya 2011 meta-analysis found weight effect of -0.88 kg, of doubtful clinical relevance; case reports of severe hepatotoxicity (Crescioli 2018). Excluded from the active protocol; documented for transparency. **5-HTP** (*Griffonia simplicifolia* seed extract): two small trials (Cangiano 1992, Ceci 1989, n<30 each) showed appetite/satiety effect; **serotonin syndrome risk** with SSRI / SNRI / MAOI / tramadol / St John's Wort co-administration (Gillman 2010); excluded as a default recommendation; permitted only for users not on any serotonergic medication and with explicit acknowledgement. *(Cochrane Tian 2013; Cochrane Onakpoya 2011; Gillman 2010)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 What home tools actually work

> **For you, in plain words.** Four home biophysical interventions with real evidence — HRV biofeedback, cold-water immersion, slow-paced breathing, and morning sunlight. PBM and CGM are §10.7.1 home-usable advanced therapies. *(Lehrer 2007 HRV biofeedback; Shevchuk 2008 cold therapy)*

### 5.2 HRV biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** A small device (HeartMath Inner Balance, Lief, Polar H10 + EliteHRV app) coaches you to breathe at 5-6 breaths per minute, which trains the vagus nerve and dampens craving-emotion waves. 10 minutes twice a day for 8 weeks moves the needle for most users. *(Lehrer 2007 meta; Goessl 2017 meta)*

#### Open the science

Heart-rate variability (HRV) biofeedback at resonance frequency (~0.1 Hz, 5-6 breaths/min) increases vagal afferent traffic, dampens amygdala reactivity, and improves emotional regulation. Goessl 2017 meta-analysis (24 RCTs) showed moderate-to-large effect for anxiety and stress. Devices: HeartMath Inner Balance ($129), Lief Patch ($499 + subscription), Polar H10 + EliteHRV (free app, $90 strap). Dose: 10-15 min BID × 8 weeks. Pair with §7 slow-paced breathing. *(Lehrer 2007; Goessl 2017)*

### 5.3 Cold-water immersion

> **For you, in plain words.** A 60-90 second cold shower or 2-3 minute cold plunge tub at 10-12°C in the morning shifts dopamine + norepinephrine + endorphin and dampens the urge to "self-soothe" with food later. *(Šrámek 2000 cold-water immersion physiology)*

#### Open the science

Cold-water immersion (10-15°C) raises plasma norepinephrine 200-300% and dopamine ~250% (Šrámek 2000); the dopaminergic lift can substitute for the sugar-fat-salt reward seeking. **Caution**: cardiovascular disease, pregnancy, severe hypertension, Raynaud's. Start 30 sec → progress to 2-3 min. Brands: Plunge ($4990), BlueCube ($6500), or a $40 chest-freezer conversion. *(Šrámek 2000; Cain 2024 cold-shower meta)*

### 5.4 Slow-paced breathing

> **For you, in plain words.** Six breaths per minute (5 sec in, 5 sec out) for 5-10 minutes activates the vagus nerve and ends a craving wave faster than any willpower technique. Free, anywhere, no device. *(Brown 2013 Sudarshan Kriya; Lehrer 2007)*

#### Open the science

Resonance-frequency breathing (~0.1 Hz) maximises HRV and parasympathetic dominance. Bhramari (humming-bee), 4-7-8 breath, box breathing, and coherent breathing all work. Apps: Breathwrk, Othership, free Bhramari guided. Dose: 5-10 min BID + as-needed when cravings hit. *(Brown 2013; Lehrer 2007)*

### 5.5 Morning sunlight + circadian

> **For you, in plain words.** 5-10 minutes of direct outdoor light within 30 minutes of waking anchors your cortisol rhythm, normalises melatonin onset 14-16 hours later, and reduces late-night cravings. Free. *(Walker 2019; Wright 2013 camping-circadian)*

#### Open the science

The suprachiasmatic nucleus needs >1000 lux of morning light to anchor the circadian rhythm. Without it, cortisol curve flattens and evening dopamine-driven food seeking rises. Combine with a no-caffeine-after-14:00 rule. *(Wright 2013; Walker 2019)*
