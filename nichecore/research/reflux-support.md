> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for heartburn, acid reflux, and the cluster doctors call GERD. We lead with **head-of-bed elevation + left-side sleep + 3-hour pre-bed eating gap (Grade A behavioural lever)**, **weight loss when relevant (Grade A)**, **DGL chewable licorice + alginate raft (Grade B)**, and **the Mediterranean-style low-acid diet (Grade B)**. Supplements come second. We are honest about the role of PPIs — they work, but long-term use raises real risks (SIBO, B12 deficiency, fracture risk, microbiome shift) — and we walk through the evidence-based self-managed taper for users already on them. *(WHO TCIM 2025-2034; EMA HMPC monographs; Cochrane Khan 2007 alginates; Lam 2013 PPI long-term)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO Traditional & Complementary Medicine Strategy 2025-2034, India's Ministry of AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS, PCIMH), EMA herbal monographs (licorice, slippery elm, marshmallow root, chamomile, Iberogast STW-5), Australia TGA, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada Natural Health Products, Tibetan Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, and the classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator or US disease association is cited as authority.**
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every food protocol, supplement, behavioural change and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No prescription, no clinic, no hospital visit, no surgery is recommended in the home regime.** Clinic-route options (PPI escalation, H. pylori triple therapy, endoscopy for red flags, fundoplication surgery, magnetic sphincter augmentation, LINX device) are placed in a clearly labelled §10.7.3 referral addendum.
>
> **Red flags — when home is NOT enough.** Difficulty swallowing or food sticking, unintentional weight loss, vomiting blood (or coffee-ground vomit), black tarry stool, chest pain that radiates or worsens with exertion, new symptoms after age 50, or symptoms despite full home protocol for ≥8 weeks — arrange gastroenterology assessment on your own initiative. This protocol cannot screen for Barrett's oesophagus or oesophageal cancer.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What reflux actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Reflux is the lower oesophageal sphincter (the valve between your food pipe and stomach) failing to stay closed. Four drivers cause it. *(Vakil Montreal Definition 2006; Katzka 2020 GERD review)*

**Quick start.** Tonight: raise the head of your bed 6-8 inches (bricks or a wedge); eat dinner 3 hours before bed; switch to left-side sleeping; skip alcohol and chocolate this week.

#### Open the science

GERD (gastro-oesophageal reflux disease) affects ~20% of adults globally. The mechanism is multifactorial: **(i) lower-oesophageal sphincter (LES) incompetence** (transient LES relaxations, hiatus hernia, low resting tone); **(ii) delayed gastric emptying** (food sits in the stomach longer, fermenting and increasing pressure); **(iii) increased intra-abdominal pressure** (obesity, tight waistbands, late large meals, supine position after eating); **(iv) oesophageal mucosal sensitivity** (some experience non-erosive reflux disease — NERD — with normal acid exposure but heightened sensation). **Synthesis.** Behavioural + mechanical + dietary levers attack the first three drivers; mucosal soothers + visceral analgesia address the fourth. *(Vakil 2006; Katzka 2020 Lancet GERD review)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive reflux — a weak valve, slow stomach emptying, pressure pushing stomach contents up, and an oversensitive food pipe. Each has its own home lever. *(Katzka 2020; Vakil 2006)*

**Quick start.** Pick ONE driver to work on this week. Most users start with the mechanical levers (head-of-bed, left-side sleep, eating gap) + alginate raft after dinner. The rest layers on later.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes — weak LES, slow stomach emptying, raised abdominal pressure, oversensitive food pipe. Naming them lets every lever target the right one. *(Katzka 2020)*

### 2.2 Lower-oesophageal-sphincter incompetence

> **For you, in plain words.** The valve at the bottom of your food pipe is meant to stay closed except when swallowing. Triggers (alcohol, chocolate, mint, fatty meals, smoking, pregnancy, hiatus hernia) make it relax inappropriately. Identify and remove your triggers. *(Kaltenbach 2006 BMJ trigger review; Katzka 2020)*

#### Open the science

LES tone is influenced by hormones (progesterone lowers it — peri-menstrual flares; perimenopause), drugs (calcium channel blockers, nitrates, anticholinergics, theophylline), foods (alcohol, chocolate, mint, fat, citrus), tobacco, and structural issues (hiatus hernia — present in ~50% of GERD patients). Transient LES relaxations (TLESRs) are the dominant mechanism in non-erosive reflux. **Synthesis.** Trigger identification + removal is the foundational home lever.

### 2.3 Delayed gastric emptying

> **For you, in plain words.** Food that lingers in the stomach ferments and presses up against the valve. Smaller meals, slow eating, ginger, and bitters all help. *(EMA ginger monograph 2012; Hu 2011 gastric emptying)*

#### Open the science

Gastric emptying half-time should be ~90 min for a mixed meal. Delayed emptying (gastroparesis subclinical or clinical) is common in GERD, diabetes, post-viral, hypothyroidism, and on opiates / anticholinergics. Targeted by: **smaller meal volumes** (largest meal at midday), **slow eating** (≥20 min per meal), **ginger** (EMA monograph; Hu 2011 RCT), **bitters** (gentian, Iberogast), **post-meal walk** (10-15 min), and **avoiding lying down within 3 h post-meal**.

### 2.4 Raised intra-abdominal pressure

> **For you, in plain words.** Belly fat, tight waistbands, pregnancy, late large meals, and lying flat all push stomach contents up. Weight loss (when relevant), looser clothes, head-of-bed elevation, and left-side sleep all directly address this. *(Singh 2013 weight-loss-GERD; Khan 2012 left-lateral; Stanciu & Bennett 1977 head-of-bed)*

### 2.5 Mucosal sensitivity (non-erosive reflux)

> **For you, in plain words.** Some people have heartburn with completely normal acid levels — the oesophagus has become sensitised. Mucosal soothers (DGL licorice, slippery elm, marshmallow root, aloe inner-leaf juice) coat and calm the lining. Gut-directed hypnotherapy retrains pain signalling. *(Calabrese 2003 NERD; EMA licorice; Whorwell 1984)*

### 2.6 Sub-types & realistic prognosis

> **For you, in plain words.** Seven sub-types — non-erosive and post-meal occasional move fastest; erosive needs care; Barrett's history needs ongoing surveillance (clinic). *(Vakil 2006; Katzka 2020)*

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Nine over-the-counter molecules and herbs, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Lead with the alginate raft (Gaviscon — sodium alginate + bicarbonate floats on stomach contents) and DGL licorice. *(Cochrane Khan 2007 alginates; EMA licorice; Madisch 2004)*

**Quick start.** Alginate (Gaviscon Advance — sodium alginate + bicarb) 10 ml after each meal and at bedtime — Cochrane-supported physical "raft" that sits on top of stomach contents preventing reflux.

### 3.2 Alginate raft — Cochrane-backed cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** Sodium alginate (from brown seaweed) + bicarbonate forms a gel "raft" that floats on top of your stomach contents, preventing them from refluxing up. Cochrane meta-analysis supports use. Gaviscon Advance (UK formulation) is the cleanest. *(Cochrane Khan 2007; Leiman 2017 alginate-postprandial RCT)*

**Quick start.** Gaviscon Advance 10 ml after main meals and at bedtime. Available globally. **Grade B+**.

### 3.3 DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice)

> **For you, in plain words.** Licorice root chewed before meals coats the oesophagus and stomach. DGL (deglycyrrhizinated) removes the blood-pressure-raising compound, so it's safe long-term. *(EMA licorice monograph 2013; Morgan 1985 DGL ulcer trial)*

**Quick start.** 1-2 chewable tablets (380 mg DGL) chewed slowly 15-20 minutes before meals. **Grade B** (long traditional use + small modern RCTs).

## 5. Biophysical & Mind-Body Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence layer for reflux is mechanical: head-of-bed elevation 6-8 inches + left-side sleep + 3-hour pre-bed eating gap + weight loss when relevant. Supplements come after. *(Khan 2012 left-lateral; Stanciu 1977 head-of-bed; Singh 2013 weight loss)*

**Quick start, tonight:**
1. Raise the head of your bed 6-8 inches (bricks or a wedge — NOT extra pillows, which kink the neck).
2. Switch to left-side sleeping (right-side worsens reflux).
3. Finish dinner 3 hours before bed.
4. Take alginate (Gaviscon Advance) 10 ml after dinner.

### 5.2 Head-of-bed elevation

> **For you, in plain words.** Sleeping with the head of your bed raised 6-8 inches uses gravity to keep stomach contents down. Use bricks under the bedposts or a foam wedge that elevates from the hips up — not just stacked pillows. Stanciu 1977 RCT shows clear benefit. *(Stanciu & Bennett 1977 head-of-bed; Khan 2012)*

**Quick start.** MedSlant wedge ($60-100), MedCline wedge with side-sleeping insert ($300-500), or 6-8 inch bricks/blocks under bed-frame head legs.

### 5.3 Left-side sleeping

> **For you, in plain words.** Right-side sleeping puts the lower oesophageal sphincter below the stomach — reflux happens. Left-side sleeping puts it above the stomach — gravity assists closure. Khan 2012 confirmed. *(Khan 2012 left-lateral-decubitus RCT)*

**Quick start.** Use a body pillow to keep you on your left side. MedCline wedge includes a left-side-sleeping insert.

### 5.4 Weight loss (when relevant)

> **For you, in plain words.** Excess abdominal weight pushes stomach contents up. Even 5-10% body-weight loss in someone with BMI >25 reduces reflux substantially. *(Singh 2013 weight-loss-GERD; Jacobson 2006 NEJM)*

**Quick start.** Mediterranean-style diet + 30 min daily walk + strength 2×/week. Refer to NicheCore weight-management protocols if relevant.

### 5.5 3-hour pre-bed eating gap

> **For you, in plain words.** Eating within 3 hours of bedtime is one of the strongest reflux triggers. Aim for dinner finished by 19:00 if bedtime is 22:00. *(Fujiwara 2005 Japan pre-bed-meal-GERD; Piesman 2007)*

### 5.6 Gut-directed hypnotherapy for visceral hypersensitivity

> **For you, in plain words.** For non-erosive reflux (normal endoscopy + symptoms), the oesophagus is oversensitised — gut-directed hypnotherapy retrains the brain's pain interpretation. *(Whorwell 1984; Calabrese 2003 NERD)*

**Practical.** Nerva app (Mind Over Gut) extended to reflux protocols; Mahana IBS for functional gut + reflux overlap.

### 5.7 Slow paced breathing + diaphragmatic breathing

> **For you, in plain words.** Slow paced breathing at 5-6 bpm activates the vagus nerve and improves LES tone. Eherer 2012 RCT showed diaphragmatic breathing reduced reflux. *(Eherer 2012 diaphragmatic breathing GERD; Lehrer 2014)*

### 5.8 Chewing gum (sugar-free, after meals — paradoxical aid)

> **For you, in plain words.** Sugar-free chewing gum AFTER meals stimulates saliva (alkaline) which neutralises any reflux — Avidan 2001 small Stanford trial supports. **Caveat:** chewing gum can WORSEN bloating in IBS-overlap patients (swallowed air + polyols). *(Avidan 2001 chewing-gum-GERD)*

### 5.9 Smoking cessation

> **For you, in plain words.** Nicotine lowers LES tone — smoking cessation is non-negotiable for GERD. *(Kahrilas 1990 smoking-GERD)*

### 5.10 At-home pH monitoring (emerging)

> **For you, in plain words.** Wireless 48-hour pH capsules exist (Bravo) but are clinic-placed. Home options remain limited; subjective symptom logs remain primary. *(Pandolfino 2008 Bravo)*

### 5.11 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Peppermint oil (relaxes LES — worsens reflux even though it helps IBS); spearmint; extra pillows (kink neck, worsen reflux); right-side sleeping; eating within 3 h of bed; tight waistbands; smoking. *(EMA peppermint LES caution; Khan 2012)*

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Eat the Mediterranean low-acid-trigger base

> **For you, in plain words.** A Mediterranean base with reflux-aware modifications: oily fish, low-fat protein, leafy greens, cooked-and-cooled starches, olive oil, no late dinners, smaller portions, no late alcohol, no carbonation. *(Mone 2016 Mediterranean-GERD; Singh 2013)*

**Quick-start summary.** No alcohol routinely, no chocolate, no mint, no citrus during flare phase, no caffeine after midday, no late dinners.

### 6.2 Mandatory foods — daily grams

- **TL;DR.** Wild oily fish — 150 g, 3-4×/week — omega-3, B12. *(UK SACN 2020)*
- **TL;DR.** Pasture-raised eggs — 2/day — choline, B12. *(Vital Farms)*
- **TL;DR.** Leafy greens (spinach, kale, chard) — 250 g/day. *(Mone 2016)*
- **TL;DR.** Cooked-and-cooled starches (potato, rice, quinoa) — 200 g/day. *(Birt 2013)*
- **TL;DR.** Low-acid fruits — banana, melon, papaya — 150 g/day. *(Mone 2016)*
- **TL;DR.** Pumpkin + sunflower seeds — 30 g/day. *(USDA SR-28)*
- **TL;DR.** Extra-virgin olive oil — 20 ml/day (moderate; high fat slows emptying). *(PREDIMED)*

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

Reflux is the lower oesophageal sphincter (the valve between your food pipe and stomach) failing to stay closed, with four driver patterns: a weak valve, slow stomach emptying, raised abdominal pressure, and (in some) an oversensitised food pipe. The good news: the four highest-evidence interventions are not drugs at all — they are MECHANICAL. Tonight: raise your bed-head 6-8 inches (bricks or a wedge, NOT extra pillows); sleep on your LEFT side; finish dinner 3 hours before bed; take alginate (Gaviscon Advance) 10 ml after each meal and at bedtime.
