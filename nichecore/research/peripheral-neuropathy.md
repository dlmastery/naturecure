The single highest-evidence lever is **tight blood-sugar control for diabetic neuropathy** (DCCT/EDIC); the strongest OTC supplement is **alpha-lipoic acid (ALA) 600 mg/day** (Ziegler SYDNEY trials, NATHAN 2011 long-term oral). Home red-light/NIR photobiomodulation, capsaicin topical 0.075%, methylcobalamin, benfotiamine, and acetyl-L-carnitine each have specific trial signals. *(DCCT/EDIC 1993; Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006; NATHAN 2011; Mason 2004 Cochrane capsaicin; Sima 2005 ALCAR)*

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What peripheral neuropathy actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** PN is damage to nerves outside the brain + spinal cord — longest nerves first (toes-feet, then fingers). 17 sub-types and a dozen mechanisms; tight glycemic control is the biggest preventive lever for DPN. *(Tesfaye 2010 Toronto Diabetic Neuropathy consensus; Callaghan 2012)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: do a 10-gram monofilament check at home (Semmes-Weinstein test — cheap online); check both feet in a mirror; remove socks and look for redness, blisters, ulceration. This week: order alpha-lipoic acid 600 mg, methylcobalamin 1000 µg sublingual, benfotiamine 300 mg, an acetyl-L-carnitine 1000 mg supplement; if diabetic, order a CGM (FreeStyle Libre, Stelo) and target time-in-range 70-180 mg/dL; start a daily 30-min walk. Read the first three chapters of any tight glycemic-control workbook (e.g. Hallberg / Westman / Bernstein for T2D). *(Tesfaye 2010; Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006; DCCT)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story shrugs neuropathy off as "just diabetic nerve damage, take pregabalin and live with it" — which is at best a third of the picture. A more honest reframe: peripheral neuropathy is the output of (i) **metabolic-mitochondrial damage** (hyperglycaemia + AGE + polyol-pathway flux + oxidative stress → axonal degeneration in DPN), (ii) **microvascular ischemia** (nerve vasa nervorum failure → ischemic axonopathy), (iii) **cofactor depletion** (B12 especially in metformin users, thiamine in alcoholics, B6 toxicity from over-supplementation), (iv) **direct neurotoxicity** (chemotherapy, alcohol, heavy metals, statins, certain antibiotics, HIV), and (v) **autoimmune / inflammatory damage** (CIDP, paraneoplastic, autoimmune small-fiber). **Synthesis.** Pairs tight glycemic control + Mediterranean diet + exercise (the A-grade frontline for the most common type, DPN) with alpha-lipoic acid + benfotiamine + methylcobalamin + acetyl-L-carnitine (the metabolic-mitochondrial layer) + topical capsaicin/lidocaine + home photobiomodulation (the symptom layer) + cofactor floor — hitting all five drivers in parallel.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes — metabolic-mitochondrial damage, microvascular ischemia, cofactor depletion, direct neurotoxicity, autoimmune inflammation. Naming them lets every supplement, food, exercise and device aim at the right driver. *(Tesfaye 2010)*

### 2.2 Metabolic-mitochondrial damage

> **For you, in plain words.** In diabetic neuropathy, high blood sugar over years drives oxidative damage to the longest nerves first (toes, feet, fingers). Alpha-lipoic acid (a mitochondrial antioxidant) and benfotiamine (a fat-soluble thiamine that blocks the AGE pathway) are the two strongest OTC molecules here. Tight blood-sugar control is the **A-grade prevention** lever. *(DCCT 1993; Ziegler SYDNEY trials; Stracke 2008 benfotiamine)*

#### Open the science

Hyperglycaemia drives polyol-pathway flux (sorbitol accumulation), AGE formation, PKC activation, hexosamine flux, and mitochondrial oxidative stress (Brownlee 2001 unifying hypothesis) → axonal degeneration. DCCT (1993) showed intensive glycemic control reduced DPN incidence 60% over 6.5 years; EDIC follow-up showed durable benefit. Targeted by **tight glycemic control** (DCCT A-grade; CGM-guided 70-180 mg/dL TIR), **alpha-lipoic acid** 600 mg/day (Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006 oral RCT n=181; NATHAN 2011 4-year long-term oral 600 mg trial showed neurologic-deficit reduction), **benfotiamine** 300-600 mg/day (Stracke 2008 BENDIP RCT n=165; Haupt 2005), **acetyl-L-carnitine** 1000-2000 mg/day (Sima 2005 ALCAR pooled RCTs n=1,257; Onofrj 1995 vincristine-CIPN; de Grandis 2002), **N-acetylcysteine** 1200 mg/day for oxidative stress overlap. **Synthesis.** This is the cleanest mechanistic lever for OTC supplements in DPN — and ALA has the strongest single signal.

### 2.3 Microvascular ischemia

> **For you, in plain words.** Nerves get oxygen from tiny blood vessels (vasa nervorum). When these vessels narrow — from diabetes, smoking, statin-related lipid issues, or general atherosclerosis — the nerves slowly starve. Mediterranean diet, exercise, smoking cessation, and Padma 28 (the Tibetan microcirculation formula) all support the vessel layer. *(Tesfaye 2005 EURODIAB; Drosch 2007 Padma 28 claudication)*

#### Open the science

Vasa nervorum endothelial dysfunction is documented in DPN biopsies (Cameron 2001). EURODIAB cohort (Tesfaye 2005) showed BP + lipids + smoking are independent predictors. Targeted by **Mediterranean diet** (PREDIMED), **aerobic + resistance exercise** (Smith 2013 RCT n=17 DPN; Streckmann 2014 chemo-neuropathy meta), **smoking cessation** (UK NICE smoking-vascular), **statin therapy if indicated** (but watch statin-PN as a separate driver in §2.5), **Padma 28** (Drosch 2007 Swiss claudication RCT — supports microcirculation), **ginkgo biloba EGb 761** in DPN-cognitive overlap (modest signal). **Synthesis.** Slow-moving but durable layer — best paired with §2.2 metabolic lever.

### 2.4 Cofactor depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** B12 deficiency causes a neuropathy that is reversible if caught early. **If you take metformin, you are at high risk** — Out 2018 and Aroda 2016 reanalysis confirmed long-term metformin depletes B12 in 10-30% of users. Thiamine deficiency in alcoholics; B6 toxicity from over-supplementation. Measure first; correct deficiencies; don't blanket-supplement. *(Out 2018 DPP B12 reanalysis; Aroda 2016 metformin B12; BJ Haematology B12)*

#### Open the science

B12 deficiency (serum <300 pg/mL, MMA >270 nmol/L) causes subacute combined degeneration + peripheral neuropathy. Long-term metformin (≥4 years) drops B12 absorption (Out 2018 DPP/DPPOS analysis n=2,155; Aroda 2016 reanalysis). Methylcobalamin (the active form) is the supplement of choice — sublingual 1000-2000 µg/day. Thiamine deficiency in alcohol-use disorder drives a separate beriberi/Wernicke-Korsakoff neuropathy — benfotiamine 300 mg + thiamine 100 mg + niacin + folate. B6 (pyridoxine) toxicity from chronic high-dose >200 mg/day causes a sensory neuropathy that can mimic DPN (Schaumburg 1983) — keep B6 supplementation ≤25-50 mg P-5-P form. **Synthesis.** Often the highest-leverage missed driver — especially in metformin users.

### 2.5 Direct neurotoxicity

> **For you, in plain words.** Chemotherapy (platinum, taxanes, vincristine), alcohol, heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic), some statins, certain antibiotics (fluoroquinolones, metronidazole long-term), HIV antiretrovirals — all can directly damage peripheral nerves. Best lever: **stop the offending exposure** wherever possible. Alpha-lipoic acid, acetyl-L-carnitine, vitamin E, omega-3 EPA-rich, and curcumin have small CIPN signals. *(Streckmann 2014; Pace 2010 vit E; Ghoreishi 2012 omega-3; Panahi 2014 curcumin)*

#### Open the science

Platinum (cisplatin, oxaliplatin) damages dorsal root ganglion neurons; taxanes (paclitaxel, docetaxel) impair axonal transport; vincristine causes microtubule disruption (Argyriou 2012 CIPN review). Targeted by **chemo dose-attenuation** (oncologist decision, not protocol), **acetyl-L-carnitine** 1000-3000 mg/day (Sima 2005 pooled, Bianchi 2005 chemo-CIPN, Hershman 2018 mixed for paclitaxel — honest mixed signal), **vitamin E 400 IU/day** for cisplatin (Pace 2010 RCT — positive for cisplatin; Argyriou 2006 oxaliplatin null — honest mixed), **omega-3 EPA-rich** for paclitaxel (Ghoreishi 2012 RCT positive), **curcumin** small Panahi 2014 signal, **alcohol cessation** for alcoholic-neuropathy, **CoQ10 100-200 mg/day** for statin-PN (Caso 2007 small RCT). **Synthesis.** Often best prevented at the source.

### 2.6 Autoimmune / inflammatory

> **For you, in plain words.** Some neuropathies are caused by your immune system attacking nerves directly — CIDP (chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy), AIDP/Guillain-Barré (acute), paraneoplastic, autoimmune small-fiber. **These are NOT home-protocol targets** — they need a neurologist's diagnosis and treatment. The home protocol's role is to support background nerve health while a specialist manages the immune disease. *(Hughes 2006 CIDP; AAN 2008 GBS guideline as factual reference, not authority)*

#### Open the science

CIDP shows progressive demyelination + albuminocytologic dissociation on lumbar puncture; treated clinically with IVIG, plasma exchange, or steroids — all clinic-route. Paraneoplastic neuropathy (Anti-Hu, Anti-CV2) requires oncology workup. Sjögren's small-fiber neuropathy requires rheumatology. The home protocol DOES support: anti-inflammatory Mediterranean diet, omega-3, curcumin, vitamin D sufficiency, gut microbiome (anti-LPS, pro-SCFA). **Synthesis.** Recognise + refer; the home protocol is adjunctive only here.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve OTC molecules with neuropathy-specific or close-adjacent trial signal. Cornerstone: **alpha-lipoic acid 600 mg/day** — Ziegler SYDNEY 2 + NATHAN 2011 long-term oral both A-grade for DPN symptoms + neurologic-deficit reduction. **Methylcobalamin** sublingual + **benfotiamine** + **acetyl-L-carnitine** form the metabolic-mitochondrial frontline. **Caution on B6** — high-dose B6 can CAUSE neuropathy; keep ≤25-50 mg P-5-P. *(Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006; NATHAN 2011; Stracke 2008; Sima 2005; Schaumburg 1983 B6 toxicity)*

### 3.2 Alpha-lipoic acid — the DPN cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** ALA is a mitochondrial antioxidant with the strongest DPN-specific OTC trial signal. Ziegler SYDNEY 2 RCT n=181 — clear DPN symptom reduction at 600 mg/day. NATHAN 2011 4-year long-term oral 600 mg — neurologic-deficit reduction. R-isomer biologically active; 600 mg/day AM empty stomach (food reduces ~30%). *(Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006; NATHAN 2011)*

#### Open the science

ALA recycles glutathione + vitamin C + vitamin E; is both fat- and water-soluble (unique); chelates transition metals (Packer 1995). **Ziegler SYDNEY 2 2006** Iranian RCT n=181 (600 mg oral × 5 weeks vs placebo; TSS reduction); **NATHAN 2011** 4-year long-term RCT n=460 (600 mg oral; NIS-LL reduction — neurologic-deficit endpoint); **Mijnhout 2012 meta-analysis** (15 RCTs) confirms B+ for DPN symptoms; **Han 2012** for diabetic-foot neuropathy. **Caution**: theoretical interaction with thyroid hormone (4 h gap recommended); biotin lab assay interference. Side effects: GI upset (with food if needed); rare allergic reactions. **Grade B+** for DPN symptoms; **Grade A** for the 600 mg/day NATHAN endpoint.

### 3.3 Methylcobalamin — for B12-deficiency and adjunctive DPN

> **For you, in plain words.** Mandatory if long-term metformin, vegan/vegetarian, age 65+, PPIs/H2-blockers, or B12-deficiency signs. Sun 2005 small DPN signal even without deficiency. Sublingual 1000-2000 µg/day. Measure first (MMA + active-B12). *(Out 2018; Aroda 2016)*

#### Open the science

Active form B12 (avoids the methylation step). Out 2018 DPP/DPPOS reanalysis n=2,155 confirmed metformin long-term lowers B12 + raises MMA + correlates with peripheral neuropathy risk; Aroda 2016 replication. Methylcobalamin sublingual 1000-2000 µg/day; injectable 1000 µg q-monthly for documented deficiency (clinic-route). Always test MMA + active-B12 + holo-TC before supplementing chronically. **Grade B** for B12-deficiency neuropathy reversal; **Grade C** for DPN augmentation.

## 5. Biophysical & Sensory Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Highest-evidence lever for DPN: **tight glycemic control + Mediterranean + exercise** (DCCT/EDIC; Smith 2013). Devices second. Home PBM panels, capsaicin, TENS, monofilament + tuning-fork screening. *(DCCT; Smith 2013; Hsieh 2012; Mason 2004 Cochrane)*

**Quick start tonight:** (1) Buy 10-g monofilament + 128-Hz tuning fork ($20 online); test feet at great toe + ball + heel — cannot feel monofilament = protective-sensation loss → urgent podiatry. (2) Daily mirror foot inspection. (3) Apply capsaicin 0.075% BID for 6+ weeks (gloves; initial burning decreases).

### 5.2 Tight glycemic control for DPN — A-grade prevention

> **For you, in plain words.** The DCCT/EDIC trial showed intensive glycemic control reduced DPN incidence 60% over 6.5 years; durable benefit at 14-year follow-up. **A-grade**. For T2D, CGMs (FreeStyle Libre, Stelo, Dexcom) make tight control achievable at home — target time-in-range 70-180 mg/dL >70%. *(DCCT 1993; EDIC 14-year follow-up; Aleppo 2023 CGM TIR consensus)*

### 5.3 Home photobiomodulation (red-light / NIR)

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red-light + near-infrared panels and devices (Joovv, Mito Red Light, LightStim, In Light Wellness) deliver wavelengths (630-680 nm + 810-850 nm) shown to reduce DPN symptoms in small trials. Hsieh 2012 RCT and Schindl 1999 small trial both positive. 10-20 min over affected feet, daily. *(Hsieh 2012; Schindl 1999)*

| Device | TL;DR + Use + Cost | Grade | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Joovv Mini/Solo/Duo + Mito Red Light + LightStim | Red + NIR panels for feet — 10-20 min daily; $200-2,500. | B | *(Hsieh 2012)* |

### 5.4 Topical capsaicin 0.075% — Cochrane-supported

> **For you, in plain words.** Capsaicin (chili extract) topical 0.075% cream depletes substance P in pain nerves over 6-8 weeks. Mason 2004 Cochrane meta-analysis confirmed effectiveness for DPN. Apply BID; wear gloves; expect 1-2 weeks of initial burning that decreases. **Grade B+**. *(Mason 2004 Cochrane; Derry 2017 Cochrane)*

OTC brands: Zostrix HP 0.075%; Capzasin-HP; Salonpas Capsaicin. **Caution**: do not apply to broken skin, eyes, mucous membranes. Wash hands thoroughly. **Clinic-route option**: capsaicin 8% Qutenza patch (Rx-strength; NEUPSIG cited as factual regulatory-path context only — see §10.7.3).

### 5.5 Home TENS units

> **For you, in plain words.** Home transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) units deliver low-current electrical pulses to mask neuropathic pain. Cochrane mixed for DPN specifically (Jin 2010); useful for symptom-relief in some users. 20-30 min as needed. *(Jin 2010 Cochrane)*

Brands: iReliev, OMRON, AccuRelief — $30-150. **Caution**: not over pacemakers, not on broken skin, not during pregnancy on lower abdomen.

### 5.6 Home foot-inspection + monofilament + tuning fork

> **For you, in plain words.** Daily foot inspection (mirror) + 10-g monofilament test + 128-Hz tuning fork = the three home tools that prevent diabetic foot ulcers + amputation. Test monofilament at 10 sites; tuning fork at the great toe; loss of either is a red flag. *(Crawford 2007 ulcer-prevention review)*

Monofilament kits: $5-15 online. 128-Hz tuning fork: $10-25 online. Foot mirror: $10. **Daily use is mandatory if you have any DPN.**

### 5.7 Home CGM for diabetic neuropathy (A-grade preventive)

> **For you, in plain words.** Continuous glucose monitors (FreeStyle Libre 3, Stelo, Dexcom G7) make tight glycemic control achievable. Target TIR (time in range 70-180 mg/dL) >70%. Single largest preventive lever for DPN. **A-grade**. *(DCCT; Aleppo 2023 TIR consensus)*

### 5.8 Home BP cuff for autonomic-neuropathy

> **For you, in plain words.** Diabetic autonomic neuropathy causes orthostatic hypotension. Home BP cuff with orthostatic check (lying → standing 1 + 3 + 5 min) catches it. *(Vinik 2003 autonomic-DPN)*

Brands: OMRON, Withings BPM Connect — $40-130.

### 5.9 Home heated socks / warming for cold-allodynia

> **For you, in plain words.** Some neuropathy presents with cold-allodynia — pain triggered by mild cold. Heated socks help. **CAUTION** if you have protective-sensation loss (monofilament-negative) — burn risk because you may not feel overheating. *(case literature)*

### 5.10 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic high-dose B6 (causes neuropathy); chronic alcohol (causes neuropathy); chronic fluoroquinolone antibiotics for non-essential use (neuropathy + tendinopathy); chronic high-dose anti-cholinergic OTC pills (dementia signal); barefoot walking if protective-sensation lost (foot injury risk you won't feel). *(Schaumburg 1983; UK MHRA fluoroquinolone 2019; UK MHRA anticholinergic 2022)*
