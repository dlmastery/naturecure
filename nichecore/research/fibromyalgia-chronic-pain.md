## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What fibromyalgia actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Fibromyalgia is a real condition where the spinal cord and brain become over-sensitive to pain signals (central sensitisation), with non-restorative sleep, fatigue, brain fog, and tender points. It is not "in your head" — it is in your nervous system. *(Sluka 2016 Pain review; Häuser 2017 Nat Rev Dis Primers)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Get a sleep tracker tonight (Oura, Whoop, or a Withings ScanWatch). Start tai chi 2-3 sessions/week (Wang 2018 A-grade). Daily CBT-for-chronic-pain workbook or app (Curable, Bearable). Magnesium malate 400 mg/day. Vitamin D3 4,000 IU/day. Self-screen with FIQR. *(Wang 2018 BMJ; Eccleston Cochrane CBT-CP; Bagis 2013 Mg malate; Wepner 2014 vit D)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

The mainstream model is "you have fibromyalgia, here's duloxetine and pregabalin". A more honest reframe: most fibromyalgia is the endpoint of **five overlapping drivers** — (i) **central sensitisation** — the spinal cord and brain wind up pain amplification; (ii) **non-restorative sleep + sleep-architecture disruption** — alpha-EEG intrusion into deep sleep; (iii) **autonomic nervous-system dysfunction** — low HRV, sympathetic over-drive, depression-anxiety overlap; (iv) **mitochondrial dysfunction + oxidative stress** in pain-pathway tissue (CoQ10 depletion is a documented biomarker); (v) **upstream trauma + chronic-stress activation** — adverse childhood experiences, PTSD, and chronic life stress strongly predict fibromyalgia onset. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **tai chi/qi gong + CBT-CP + sleep extension + trauma-informed pacing + HRV biofeedback** (the A-grade frontline) with mitochondrial support (CoQ10, magnesium malate, D-ribose), vitamin D restoration, low-dose-naltrexone conversation (clinic-route), and pranayama hits all five drivers in parallel. Critically, **graded exercise pacing — overtraining worsens fibromyalgia**.

### 1.4 What this dossier is NOT

> **For you, in plain words.** This is not a substitute for working with a credentialed pain therapist or pain-specialised PT if home protocol plateaus. CBT-CP and ACT have A-grade trial evidence — self-guided versions exist, but a trained therapist gets better outcomes for many people. The user decides. *(Eccleston Cochrane CBT-CP; Wicksell 2013 ACT)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five drivers — central sensitisation, non-restorative sleep, autonomic dysfunction, mitochondrial dysfunction, and trauma/chronic-stress activation. Naming them lets every food, breath, supplement, and practice aim at the right one. *(Sluka 2016 Pain; Häuser 2017 Nat Rev DP)*

### 2.2 Central sensitisation

> **For you, in plain words.** The spinal cord and brain pain pathways become wound up — they amplify normal touch into pain. NMDA receptors over-activated. Low-dose-naltrexone, slow paced breathing, MBSR, tai chi, and pacing dial it down. *(Sluka 2016; Younger 2013 LDN)*

#### Open the science

Central sensitisation involves NMDA-receptor-mediated wind-up at the dorsal horn, descending modulation failure (impaired serotonergic/noradrenergic inhibition from rostral ventromedial medulla and periaqueductal grey), reduced opioid receptor signalling, and microglial activation. Younger 2013 showed pilot evidence that low-dose naltrexone (LDN, 4.5 mg/d off-label) attenuates microglial inflammation in fibromyalgia; Bruun-Plesner 2020 Danish RCT confirmed. Targeted by **low-dose naltrexone (clinic-route §10.7.3)**, **MBSR** (Lauche 2013 meta), **slow paced breathing 5-6 bpm** (Lehrer 2014 HRV meta), **tai chi** (Wang 2018 A-grade), and **pacing** (Goudsmit 2009 envelope-of-energy theory). **Synthesis.** Wind-up is reversible with the right inputs.

### 2.3 Non-restorative sleep

> **For you, in plain words.** Sleep in fibromyalgia is broken — alpha brain-waves intrude into deep sleep, so you never feel rested. Fixing sleep is THE highest-yield home lever. Sleep extension + CBT-i + magnesium + cool room + dark room. *(Moldofsky 1975 alpha-intrusion; Roizenblatt 2001 polysomnography fibro)*

#### Open the science

Moldofsky 1975 showed that depriving healthy people of stage-3/4 NREM sleep produced fibromyalgia-like symptoms. Subsequent polysomnography (Roizenblatt 2001, Brazil) confirmed alpha-EEG intrusion into NREM in fibromyalgia patients. Restless legs (RLS, ferritin <50), sleep apnea (STOP-BANG), and dysregulated melatonin are common comorbidities. Targeted by **CBT-i** (Sleepio, Somryst, Espie 2012 A-grade for insomnia generally; Martínez 2014 specifically for fibro), **magnesium glycinate evening 200-400 mg**, **melatonin low-dose** (Hussain 2011 fibro small RCT; 0.3-1 mg physiological dose preferred), **L-theanine 200 mg evening**, **ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg evening** (Salve 2019), **cool dark room 18-19°C**, **blue-light cut 90 min before bed**, **weighted blanket** (Ackerley 2015 small RCT — sleep + anxiety), and **STOP-BANG screen → home sleep study if positive**. **Synthesis.** Sleep is the foundational lever; without it the rest of the protocol struggles.

### 2.4 Autonomic dysfunction

> **For you, in plain words.** In fibromyalgia, the nervous system is stuck in sympathetic over-drive — heart rate variability is low, anxiety and depression overlap heavily, and you can't drop into rest-and-digest. Slow breathing, HRV biofeedback, MBSR, and yoga retrain the autonomic system. *(Martínez-Martínez 2014 HRV-fibro; Solano 2009 HRV meta)*

#### Open the science

Fibromyalgia patients consistently show reduced HRV, particularly the high-frequency (parasympathetic) component, indicating sympathetic dominance and impaired baroreflex. POTS overlap is common; orthostatic intolerance is a frequent comorbidity. Anxiety and depression are present in 50-80% — the trauma-PTSD axis is over-represented. Targeted by **HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath Inner Balance, Lief, Polar HRV — Lehrer 2014 meta), **slow paced breathing 5-6 bpm** (Lehrer 2014), **MBSR** (Lauche 2013 meta), **yoga** (Curtis 2011 fibro signal), **tai chi** (Wang 2018), **CBT for depression-anxiety overlap** (PHQ-9 + GAD-7 self-administered + Mind Over Mood workbook), and **trauma-informed therapy resources** (Internal Family Systems, Somatic Experiencing, ACT). **Synthesis.** The autonomic dial is daily work, not a one-shot intervention.

### 2.5 Mitochondrial dysfunction

> **For you, in plain words.** Pain-pathway cells in fibromyalgia run low on energy. CoQ10 and D-ribose can help. Mitochondrial-fuel theory has trial support. *(Cordero 2013 CoQ10 fibro RCT; Sawaddiruk 2019 meta; Teitelbaum 2006 D-ribose)*

#### Open the science

Skin biopsies in fibromyalgia patients show reduced CoQ10, increased mitochondrial ROS, and impaired electron-transport-chain efficiency (Cordero 2010). Cordero 2013 RCT showed CoQ10 300 mg/d improved fibromyalgia symptoms and reduced inflammation markers. Sawaddiruk 2019 meta-analysis confirmed B+ for CoQ10 in fibromyalgia specifically. D-ribose (Teitelbaum 2006 open-label) showed energy and pain improvement. Magnesium malate (Russell 1995 small + Bagis 2013) is specifically researched for fibromyalgia (the malate-cycle theory). Targeted by **CoQ10 ubiquinol 100-300 mg/d**, **D-ribose 5 g three times daily**, **magnesium malate 400-1,200 mg/d** (Russell 1995, Bagis 2013), **L-carnitine** (mitochondrial fatty-acid transport), and **moderate exercise** (mitochondrial biogenesis). **Synthesis.** Mitochondrial support has direct fibromyalgia trial backing.

### 2.6 Trauma and chronic-stress activation

> **For you, in plain words.** Many people with fibromyalgia have a history of childhood adversity, accidents, or chronic life stress. Trauma-informed work (not just generic "stress management") often shifts the picture. *(Hauser 2011 ACE-fibro; Cohen 2002)*

#### Open the science

Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs), PTSD, and chronic life stress strongly predict fibromyalgia onset (Hauser 2011 meta — ACE score correlates with fibromyalgia severity). The HPA axis is dysregulated — flattened cortisol curve, low morning cortisol, sometimes elevated evening cortisol. Targeted by **trauma-informed therapy** (Internal Family Systems IFS, Somatic Experiencing SE, EMDR, ACT — credentialed practitioner OR self-guided workbooks), **MBSR and ACT** (Wicksell 2013 fibro ACT RCT), **vagal-tone work**, **safe-and-sound protocol Porges** (clinic-only), **journaling for emotional processing**, and **community support** (peer support — Wegovy is not a community; the user needs human connection). **Synthesis.** Unaddressed trauma keeps the system on high-alert and undermines every other layer.

### 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

Fibromyalgia is a real condition — your spinal cord and brain have become over-sensitive to pain signals (this is called central sensitisation), and the deep, restorative part of your sleep has been disrupted by a brain-wave intrusion. It is not "in your head" in the dismissive way it gets said; it is in your nervous system, and the trial literature reflects that. The mainstream model is duloxetine + pregabalin — both A-grade for fibromyalgia, and there is no shame in needing them; that conversation belongs in §10.7.3 and is your call. But there are five drivers we can also work on at home: central sensitisation (slow paced breathing, MBSR, tai chi); non-restorative sleep (the highest-yield lever — sleep extension, CBT-i, magnesium, weighted blanket, room cool and dark); autonomic dysfunction (HRV biofeedback, slow breathing); mitochondrial dysfunction (CoQ10, magnesium malate, D-ribose — all with fibromyalgia-specific trial data); and trauma + chronic-stress activation (trauma-informed therapy, ACT, MBSR). Wang 2018 in the BMJ showed tai chi was equivalent to aerobic exercise for fibromyalgia — that is A-grade evidence for a gentle, free, learn-on-YouTube practice. **This is months-long work — not an overnight reversal.** Pacing matters more than pushing — overtraining worsens fibromyalgia. **This is an independent, over-the-counter, home-based protocol. No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.** If you choose duloxetine, pregabalin, or low-dose-naltrexone, that is your call; §10.1 shows you how the home stack interacts. If suicidal thoughts come, please call a crisis line — 988 in the US, 116 123 UK Samaritans, IASP global directory — fibromyalgia carries real suicide risk and you are not alone.

## 14. Independent Healing Paradigm

This protocol differs from the mainstream model because it does the work the 12-minute rheumatology visit doesn't have time for: tai chi (A-grade per Wang 2018 BMJ — and free), CBT-for-chronic-pain (A-grade per Eccleston Cochrane — and self-guided via Curable, Mind Over Mood, Pathways), structured sleep extension, magnesium malate at the dose Bagis 2013 used, CoQ10 ubiquinol at the dose Cordero 2013 used, vitamin D restoration, trauma-informed nervous-system work, and pacing — none of which require a prescription.

**Sovereignty ethos**: OTC + home-based by design. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.** The user owns their body.

**Honest acknowledgment of evidence limits**: 5-HTP is contraindicated with SSRI/SNRI/MAOI/tramadol — serotonin syndrome — and we surface that aggressively. Chronic opioids do NOT help fibromyalgia long-term and carry addiction risk. Homeopathy entries are graded T/H — documented tradition, modern evidence contested. Duloxetine, pregabalin, milnacipran, and low-dose-naltrexone have real trial evidence and belong in §10.7.3 as honest clinic-route options. The protocol does not pretend natural adjuncts replace them when the home work plateaus.

**Suicide-prevention is central** — fibromyalgia carries real suicide risk and the protocol surfaces crisis lines (988, 116 123 Samaritans, IASP global directory) at the top and bottom of the dossier.
