## 1. Executive Summary

IBS is conventionally treated as a diagnosis-of-exclusion symptom cluster — chased with antispasmodics, FODMAP elimination handed to a dietitian, rifaximin if SIBO is suspected, low-dose TCAs or SSRIs for the brain-gut axis. The integrative reframing is different: IBS-like patterns represent the **downstream signal of five convergent root mechanisms** — (i) **SIBO / IMO overgrowth** with displaced colonic-type flora and methanogen archaea in the small bowel fermenting carbohydrate to H₂, CH₄ and CO₂; (ii) **visceral hypersensitivity + brain-gut-axis dysregulation** with TRPV1 sensitisation, altered 5-HT signalling, and bidirectional vagal disturbance; (iii) **post-infectious anti-vinculin autoimmunity** (Pimentel framework) where prior food-poisoning generates anti-CdtB antibodies that cross-react with vinculin, damaging interstitial cells of Cajal and the MMC — explaining 60% of IBS-D; (iv) **mast cell + histamine axis dysregulation** with localised mucosal mast-cell activation amplifying visceral hypersensitivity disproportionate to actual luminal contents; (v) **bile acid malabsorption** in IBS-D (up to 30%) with excess bile reaching the colon driving osmotic + secretory diarrhoea. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously addresses SIBO/IMO, restores vagal-brain-gut signalling, calms mast cells, supports MMC, and rebuilds mucosal barrier addresses the upstream drivers — not the symptom cluster.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

**2.1 SIBO / IMO overgrowth — the central node (~50–80% of IBS).** Pimentel's group has documented that the majority of IBS cases carry small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (>10⁵ CFU/mL — typically diagnosed as Hydrogen ≥20 ppm rise within 90 min on lactulose breath test) OR intestinal methanogen overgrowth (CH₄ ≥10 ppm at any time). Methanogen-IMO drives IBS-C (methane physically slows transit ~59%); hydrogen-SIBO drives IBS-D; sulfur-reducing bacteria drive IBS-D with sulfur-egg-burps and urgency. Risk factors: prior food poisoning, PPI/antibiotic history, vagal hypotonia, low MMC. Targeted by **berberine 500 mg TID + oregano oil 200 mg BID + allicin 450 mg TID (if methanogen+) for 4-week antimicrobial phase, neem, *Phellodendron* (Huang Bai), lactoferrin, Triphala (gentle prokinetic), prokinetic herbs (ginger, Iberogast)**. **Synthesis:** Without addressing SIBO/IMO substrate and population, symptoms return within days of stopping any other intervention.

**2.2 Visceral hypersensitivity + brain-gut-axis dysregulation.** The enteric nervous system contains 200–600 million neurons, communicates bidirectionally with the brain via vagal afferents, and produces ~90% of body serotonin. In IBS, TRPV1 + TRPA1 + TRPM8 receptors are sensitised — normal luminal distension is perceived as pain at thresholds 30–50% lower than controls. Stress, anxiety, depression amplify via descending modulation. Targeted by **peppermint oil enteric-coated 0.2 ml TID (Grade A — Cochrane systematic review IBS 2019), Iberogast (STW-5) 20 drops TID, gut-directed hypnotherapy (Manchester / Monash protocols — Grade A; Nerva, Mahana, Zemedy apps), Yoga Nidra (Bihar School — Indian RCT IBS-SSS improvement), MBSR / MBCT, consumer VNS (Nurosym, Sensate, Pulsetto), 4-7-8 breath pre-each-meal, HRV biofeedback (HeartMath, Lief), CBT for IBS via app**. **Synthesis:** Even with SIBO resolved, persistent visceral hypersensitivity means the gut "remembers" the alarm pattern; rewiring the brain-gut axis is the long game.

**2.3 Post-infectious anti-vinculin autoimmunity (Pimentel framework — explains 60% of IBS-D).** Pimentel's Cedars-Sinai group documented that food poisoning by *Campylobacter*, *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *E. coli* O157, or norovirus generates anti-CdtB antibodies (Cytolethal Distending Toxin B). These cross-react with **vinculin** — a structural protein in the interstitial cells of Cajal (the pacemaker cells of the gut). The result is permanent damage to MMC + colonic propulsion, predisposing to lifelong SIBO/IMO recurrence and IBS. The "IBS-Smart" diagnostic test measures anti-CdtB + anti-vinculin antibodies. Targeted by **vagal training to compensate for damaged Cajal cell function (humming, gargling, 4-7-8 breath, Nurosym VNS), prokinetics (ginger, Iberogast, low-dose 5-HTP — careful with SSRI flag), MMC support, microbiome restoration**. **Synthesis:** Anti-vinculin pattern means the user is at lifelong elevated SIBO recurrence risk and benefits from chronic low-dose prokinetic support — not as a cure but as ongoing maintenance.

**2.4 Mast cell + histamine axis dysregulation (founder's gut-mast-cell emphasis).** Gut mucosal mast cells release histamine, tryptase, prostaglandins, leukotrienes locally — driving visceral hypersensitivity and sub-clinical inflammation. Triggers: histamine-rich fermented foods, histamine-liberating foods (citrus, tomato, chocolate, alcohol), stress, dysbiosis, mould exposure. DAO (diamine oxidase) enzyme insufficiency amplifies. ~30–40% of IBS sub-population has significant mast-cell component. Targeted by **low-histamine phase trial 4–6 weeks, DAO enzyme (Histamine Block, Daosin) pre-meal, quercetin 1 g BID (mast-cell stabilisation), vitamin C 1–2 g, Nigella sativa, holy basil tulsi, Boswellia AKBA, Mg glycinate, vagal practices**. **Synthesis:** In the mast-cell sub-type, addressing histamine is rate-limiting for relief.

**2.5 Bile acid malabsorption (BAM) — up to 30% of IBS-D.** Normally, 95% of bile acids are reabsorbed in the terminal ileum and recycled. In BAM (post-cholecystectomy, ileal disease, idiopathic), excess bile acid reaches the colon, where it acts as an osmotic + secretory laxative — driving urgent watery diarrhoea, especially post-prandially. BAM is dramatically under-diagnosed. Sometimes screened with 75-Se-HCAT (UK/EU) or 7α-C4 blood test. Targeted by **bile-acid binders OTC: psyllium 5–10 g BID (binds bile acids gently), apple pectin, modified citrus pectin, sunflower lecithin, soluble PHGG (Sunfiber), reduced dietary fat in BAM sub-type, taurine 1 g (helps bile conjugation), bitters cautiously**. **Synthesis:** A user with IBS-D refractory to FODMAP and antimicrobial work may have BAM as the missed driver.

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.0 Self-administered clinical scoring instrument — IBS-SSS + IBS-QOL + Bristol + daily VAS

**IBS-SSS** (Irritable Bowel Syndrome Severity Scoring System, UK-developed by Francis & Whorwell) — 5 items (abdominal pain severity + frequency × days/10, abdominal distension, bowel habit dissatisfaction, life interference). Range 0–500. <75 = remission; 75–175 = mild; 175–300 = moderate; >300 = severe. **Target <75 by month 6.** Primary endpoint of Cochrane IBS trials.

**IBS-QOL** (Patrick) — 34-item quality-of-life. Range 0–100, higher = better. Target >75 by month 6.

**Bristol Stool Chart** (UK-developed, Heaton scale) — daily diary; target type 3–4 consistently for IBS-M; type 4 for IBS-C; reduction in type 6–7 for IBS-D.

**Daily Bloating / Pain / Urgency-VAS** (visual analogue scale 0–10) — track AM and PM in app (Cara Care, Bowelle, Mahana, Nerva) for 14 days baseline + ongoing.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

IBS isn't "just stress" — it's a real five-layer condition in your gut that conventional medicine often treats as a diagnosis-of-exclusion. (1) Up to 80% of IBS has small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) or methanogen overgrowth (IMO) — bacteria living where they shouldn't, fermenting your food into gas. (2) Your gut has become hypersensitive — pain receptors fire at distensions that wouldn't bother a normal gut, because the brain-gut communication highway has lost its normal noise-filtering. (3) If you had food poisoning 6 months to 10 years ago, your immune system may have generated antibodies that damage the pacemaker cells of your gut (this is the anti-vinculin pattern, present in ~60% of IBS-D — it can be tested directly). (4) Your gut mast cells may be over-reactive, releasing histamine that makes things feel inflamed even without much going on. (5) If you have IBS-D, up to 30% have bile acid malabsorption — bile flooding the colon and driving urgent diarrhoea.

This protocol does **not** chase the symptom with rifaximin alone, linaclotide / lubiprostone / eluxadoline, antispasmodics, low-dose TCAs, or SSRIs as the primary path. Instead, it works on all five layers — using herbs that reduce SIBO bacteria (berberine, oregano oil, allicin if you're a methane-producer), gut-specific probiotics with Grade A evidence (L. plantarum 299v from Polish RCTs, B. infantis 35624 from UK Whorwell RCT), spasmolytic enteric-coated peppermint oil (EMA-recognised), Iberogast (German RCTs, EMA), retraining the brain-gut axis (Manchester / Monash University gut-directed hypnotherapy via Nerva or Mahana app — Grade A), and bile-binders / PHGG / Mg for the appropriate sub-type. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.**

Expect a slow, steady re-balancing — not an overnight fix. You may feel worse for 5–10 days as die-off happens (Herxheimer), then relief. By Week 4 most people see 30–40% less symptom severity. By Week 12, with reintroduction of trigger foods carefully mapped, sustainable normality.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a physician informed, that is your personal decision. The protocol does not require it.
