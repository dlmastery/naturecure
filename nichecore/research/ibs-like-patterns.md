> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for the cluster doctors call "irritable bowel" — cramping, bloating, urgent or sluggish bowels, gas, and the gut-anxiety loop. We lead with **the low-FODMAP elimination diet (Monash University, Grade A)**, **gut-directed hypnotherapy or CBT (Grade A)**, and **enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules (Cochrane-supported, Grade B+)** — these are the three highest-evidence layers. Supplements come second. We are honest where the science is mixed — including for most probiotic strains, where the evidence is strain-specific and most marketed blends have no trial data behind them. *(WHO TCIM 2025-2034; Monash University FODMAP programme; Cochrane Ford 2014 peppermint oil; Cochrane Ford 2018 antispasmodics)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO Traditional & Complementary Medicine Strategy 2025-2034, India's Ministry of AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS, PCIMH), EMA herbal monographs (peppermint oil, caraway, STW-5 Iberogast), Australia TGA, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada Natural Health Products, Tibetan Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, and the classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator or US disease association is cited as authority.** The narrow factual exception is the regulatory-path note about the Mahana IBS app (FDA De Novo grant 2020) in §10.7, used as historical context only — the evidence anchor for that app remains the Everitt 2019 *Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol* ACTIB trial.
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every food protocol, supplement, breath practice, app, and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No prescription, no clinic, no hospital visit, no surgery is recommended in the home regime.** Clinic-route options (rifaximin for SIBO, prokinetics, gastroenterology endoscopy/colonoscopy for red flags, faecal microbiota transplant) are placed in a clearly labelled §10.7.3 referral addendum, never in the home protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users already on a prescription can see how it interacts with this protocol.
>
> **Red flags — when home is NOT enough.** Blood in stool, unexplained weight loss, anaemia, fever, severe night pain that wakes you, family history of colon cancer or inflammatory bowel disease, or new symptoms starting after age 50 — arrange a gastroenterology assessment on your own initiative. This protocol cannot screen for a malignancy or active inflammatory bowel disease.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What IBS-like patterns actually are

> **For you, in plain words.** "Irritable bowel" is not a single disease — it is a name for five overlapping drivers you can work on at home. *(Drossman Rome IV 2016; Vanner 2016)*

**Quick start.** Tonight: write down the last three meals before your last flare; cut high-FODMAP foods for two weeks (onion, garlic, wheat, apple, milk); start one peppermint-oil capsule with each meal; download the Nerva gut-directed hypnotherapy app or the Mahana IBS CBT app.

#### Open the science

The Rome IV criteria define IBS as recurrent abdominal pain ≥1 day/week for 3 months, linked to defecation, with changes in stool frequency or form, and no red flags. But that label collapses at least five distinct biological drivers — diet/FODMAP fermentation, visceral hypersensitivity, brain-gut signalling, microbiome dysbiosis (often with SIBO overlap), and bile-acid/motility imbalance. A home protocol that hits these in parallel outperforms any single intervention. *(Drossman Rome IV 2016; Vanner 2016 Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol IBS mechanisms review)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive the IBS cluster — fermentable foods, an over-reactive gut wall, an over-firing gut-brain conversation, an imbalanced microbiome (sometimes with bacterial overgrowth), and bile-acid or motility timing problems. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Vanner 2016; Pimentel 2020 SIBO review)*

**Quick start.** Pick ONE driver to work on this week, not all five. Most users start with FODMAPs (highest-evidence dietary lever) + a gut-directed hypnotherapy app (highest-evidence behavioural lever). The rest layers on later.

#### Open the science

The five-driver framing matches the Rome IV multidimensional model: **(i) luminal triggers** (FODMAPs, gluten sensitivity without coeliac, food chemicals like salicylates and amines); **(ii) visceral hypersensitivity** (the gut wall fires pain signals at normal stretch — a learned amplification driven by early-life stress, infection, or chronic anxiety); **(iii) brain-gut axis dysregulation** (the vagus nerve and the central pain matrix amplify or dampen gut signals — gut-directed hypnotherapy and CBT target this); **(iv) microbiome dysbiosis** (low Bifidobacterium, low diversity, sometimes overlapping SIBO or methanogen overgrowth); **(v) bile-acid and motility timing** (about 1 in 3 with diarrhoea-predominant IBS have measurable bile-acid malabsorption; motility problems drive constipation-predominant). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **FODMAP elimination + gut-directed hypnotherapy + peppermint oil** as the cornerstone, with strain-specific probiotics, partially-hydrolysed guar fibre, and stress regulation, hits all five drivers in parallel.

### 1.3 What this protocol is NOT

> **For you, in plain words.** This is not a one-month "gut detox" or a colon cleanse. The expected timeline to meaningful symptom drop is 4-12 weeks for diet + behavioural layers, and 3-6 months to full remodelling. *(Halmos 2014 FODMAP RCT; Everitt ACTIB 2019)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive IBS-like patterns — fermentable foods, an over-reactive gut wall, an over-firing brain-gut conversation, microbiome imbalance, and bile-acid or motility problems. Each has its own home lever. *(Vanner 2016; Drossman 2016)*

**Quick start.** Read the next five sub-sections in order. Identify which 1-2 drivers fit you best. Then pick the matching levers in §3-§7.

### 2.2 Fermentable food triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Some carbohydrates (FODMAPs — fermentable oligo-, di-, mono-saccharides and polyols) reach the colon undigested. Bacteria ferment them and produce gas, which stretches a sensitive gut wall and creates pain and bloating. Cut them for 2-6 weeks, then reintroduce one at a time. *(Halmos 2014 Australia FODMAP RCT n=30; Monash University FODMAP database)*

#### Open the science

FODMAPs are short-chain carbohydrates poorly absorbed in the small intestine: fructans (wheat, onion, garlic), galactans (legumes), lactose (milk), excess fructose (apple, mango, high-fructose corn syrup), and polyols (sorbitol, mannitol — apple, pear, stone fruit, sugar-free gum). They reach the colon intact and undergo bacterial fermentation, producing hydrogen, methane, and short-chain fatty acids. In someone with normal gut sensitivity this is harmless; in someone with visceral hypersensitivity (§2.3), the resulting stretch triggers pain. The Monash low-FODMAP protocol is the most validated dietary intervention for IBS, with multiple RCTs (Halmos 2014 n=30; Staudacher 2017 n=104) showing ~70% response rate. **Synthesis.** Cut the trigger first, then rebuild tolerance. *(Halmos 2014 Gastroenterology; Staudacher 2017 Gastroenterology; Monash University FODMAP database)*

### 2.3 Over-reactive gut wall

> **For you, in plain words.** In IBS, normal amounts of gas, food, or stretch fire pain signals — the gut wall has been "tuned up" by early-life stress, a past gut infection, or chronic anxiety. Hypnotherapy and CBT retrain this. *(Whorwell 1984; Cima 2012; Lackner 2018 ACG RCT)*

#### Open the science

Visceral hypersensitivity is the lowered threshold at which the gut wall sends pain signals. Mechanisms: increased mucosal mast-cell density (Barbara 2004), upregulated TRPV1 nociceptors, dysregulated descending pain modulation in the periaqueductal grey. Drivers include early-life adverse events, post-infectious IBS (after acute gastroenteritis ~10% develop chronic IBS), and chronic stress via HPA-axis dysregulation. Targeted by **gut-directed hypnotherapy** (Whorwell Manchester protocol — n=250 70% response sustained at 5 years), **CBT-for-IBS** (Lackner 2018 ACG four-session RCT n=436), **peppermint oil capsules** (Cochrane Ford 2014 supports), and **mast-cell stabilisers** (quercetin, Perilla, DAO-rich foods). **Synthesis.** Retraining the gut-brain pain loop is one of the most durable levers — and it has the strongest evidence base of any IBS intervention.

### 2.4 Brain-gut signalling loop

> **For you, in plain words.** The vagus nerve carries 80% of its signals UP from gut to brain, not the other way. Anxiety amplifies gut symptoms; gut symptoms amplify anxiety. Breaking the loop with vagal practices, paced breathing and HRV training shifts both. *(Bonaz 2018 vagal-IBS review; Mayer 2015 Gut Feelings)*

#### Open the science

The brain-gut axis is bidirectional and mediated by the vagus nerve (CN X), the enteric nervous system (~500 million neurons in the gut wall), HPA-axis cortisol signalling, and microbiome-derived neurotransmitters (serotonin precursors, GABA, SCFAs). Low vagal tone (poor HRV) correlates with IBS severity (Pellissier 2014). Targeted by **slow paced breathing 5-6 bpm** (resonance frequency breathing — raises vagal tone), **Bhramari humming breath** (raises nasal nitric oxide, gentle vagal stimulation), **HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath, Lief, Polar H10), **gut-directed hypnotherapy** (vagal-mediated visceral analgesia), **app-based CBT and MBSR**, and **at-home transcutaneous auricular vagus-nerve stimulation** (Nurosym pilot data in IBS — Drewes 2019). **Synthesis.** The brain-gut axis is the lever that lets the other four layers actually work.

### 2.5 Microbiome dysbiosis + SIBO

> **For you, in plain words.** People with IBS often have lower microbial diversity, lower Bifidobacterium, and sometimes bacterial overgrowth in the small intestine (SIBO). At-home breath tests can tell you which pattern you have. Specific probiotic strains help — but most are marketing. *(Pimentel 2020 SIBO review; Ford 2018 ACG probiotic guideline)*

#### Open the science

Dysbiosis in IBS: reduced diversity, low Bifidobacterium, low Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, sometimes high Methanobrevibacter smithii (in constipation-predominant + abdominal distention). SIBO overlap: ~30-50% of IBS-D shows positive lactulose or glucose hydrogen breath test for bacterial overgrowth (Pimentel 2020). Targeted by **specific strain probiotics with RCT data** (Bifidobacterium infantis 35624 for global IBS symptoms — O'Mahony 2005 n=77; Lactobacillus plantarum 299v for IBS pain and bloating — Niedzielin 2001 n=40; Saccharomyces boulardii for diarrhoea — McFarland 2010 meta-analysis), **partially hydrolysed guar gum** (PHGG, Sunfiber — Polymeropoulos 2014 n=120 IBS), **inulin/FOS for slow tolerance build only** (causes flares in low-FODMAP responders), **at-home stool microbiome tests** (Viome, ZOE, Sun Genomics — interpretive, not diagnostic), and **at-home SIBO breath testing** (FoodMarble Aire device, Quintron home kits). **Synthesis.** Test before treating; rebuild diversity slowly.

### 2.6 Bile-acid + motility timing

> **For you, in plain words.** About 1 in 3 with diarrhoea-predominant IBS actually have bile-acid malabsorption — the gut is dumping too much bile into the colon and irritating it. Slow constipation-predominant IBS is often a methane-overgrowth or transit-timing problem. Different fibres, different timing, different protocol. *(Aziz 2015 bile-acid IBS meta-analysis; Pimentel 2020)*

#### Open the science

Bile-acid malabsorption (BAM) is found in ~30% of IBS-D patients via SeHCAT scan or 7α-hydroxy-4-cholesten-3-one blood test. Mechanism: ileal bile-acid transporter dysfunction → unabsorbed bile salts → colonic secretion + motility stimulation → urgent diarrhoea, often morning, often post-meal. Targeted by **bile-acid binders** (colesevelam — clinic-route, in §10.7.3; psyllium husk binds bile acids modestly at home), **soluble fibre rebalance** (psyllium 5-10 g/day), and **time-of-day eating** (largest meal mid-day rather than evening). Methane-driven IBS-C: targeted by **mastic gum** (antimicrobial), **berberine** (preliminary IBS-C data), **ginger + triphala** for motility, and clinically by **prokinetics** (prucalopride — §10.7.3). **Synthesis.** Get the right test (DTC lab or home breath test) before assuming "everyone with diarrhoea needs probiotics".

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Eleven over-the-counter molecules and herbs, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on most generic probiotic blends and on aloe juice. *(EMA HMPC peppermint 2020; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; Cochrane Ford 2014)*

**Quick start.** If you do nothing else, start enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules (Cochrane-supported) AND partially-hydrolysed guar gum (Sunfiber 5 g/day) — these are the two highest-evidence supplemental layers.

### 3.2 Peppermint oil — the Cochrane-backed cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** Enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules (so they don't reflux) are the highest-evidence supplemental layer for IBS. The 2014 Cochrane meta-analysis (Ford et al.) and the IMAGINE trial (Weerts 2020 Netherlands) both support 180-240 mg three times daily for 4-12 weeks. The classical EMA HMPC monograph (2020) backs the use. *(Cochrane Ford 2014; Weerts 2020 IMAGINE; EMA HMPC peppermint 2020)*

**Quick start.** 1 enteric-coated capsule (180-225 mg) with each main meal, 30-60 minutes before eating, for 4-12 weeks. If you have reflux, take with food. Stop if heartburn worsens.

#### Open the science

Peppermint oil's active components (menthol, menthone, 1,8-cineole) inhibit smooth-muscle calcium channels in the gut wall — direct antispasmodic action. Cochrane Ford 2014 systematic review of 9 RCTs (n=726) showed pooled RR of symptom improvement vs placebo of 2.23 (95% CI 1.78-2.81). The IMAGINE trial (Weerts 2020, Gastroenterology, n=190 from Maastricht) compared enteric-coated peppermint oil 182 mg TID vs small-intestinal-release vs placebo — showed clear symptom benefit. The EMA HMPC monograph (2020) recognises peppermint oil for symptomatic relief of mild spasms in the gastrointestinal tract, mild abdominal pain, flatulence and bloating. **Grade B+** (multiple RCTs, Cochrane-supported, EMA monograph). Caution: enteric coating essential — uncoated capsules release in stomach, worsen reflux, less colonic delivery. Brands: Heather's Tummy Tamers (enteric-coated); Mintec (Australia TGA-listed); IBgard (sustained-release multi-coated microspheres).

### 3.3 Probiotics — the honest strain-specific caveat (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Most marketed "IBS probiotics" have no trial data behind the actual strain. Only a handful of named strains have RCT support for IBS — Bifidobacterium infantis 35624 (Align), Lactobacillus plantarum 299v (Bio-Gaia, Jarrow), Saccharomyces boulardii (Florastor) for diarrhoea, and the multistrain VSL#3/Visbiome formulation. We list these because the strain matters, not the brand marketing. *(O'Mahony 2005 n=77; Niedzielin 2001 n=40; McFarland 2010 meta-analysis; Ford 2018 ACG guideline)*

**Quick start.** Pick ONE strain that matches your sub-type, take it for 4-8 weeks, and assess. Do not stack 5 random probiotics — the evidence is not for "more is better".

#### Open the science

Specific strains with positive RCTs:

- **Bifidobacterium infantis 35624** (sold as Align in US, Symprove in UK): O'Mahony 2005 RCT n=77 showed improvement in global IBS symptoms vs placebo.
- **Lactobacillus plantarum 299v**: Niedzielin 2001 n=40 — improvement in pain and bloating.
- **Saccharomyces boulardii CNCM I-745** (Florastor / Ultra-Levure): McFarland 2010 meta-analysis — benefit for diarrhoea, antibiotic-associated diarrhoea, post-infectious IBS.
- **VSL#3 / Visbiome** (eight-strain formulation): Kim 2003 n=25, Whorwell 2006 n=362.
- **Bifidobacterium lactis HN019**: Waller 2011 — bowel transit improvement in functional constipation.

Cochrane Ford 2018 systematic review concluded the evidence base is patchy and strain-dependent. Most commercial multi-strain blends have no trial data for the specific formulation. **Grade B for the named strains above; Grade C for multi-strain marketed blends without trial data.**

### 3.4 Partially-hydrolysed guar gum (PHGG) — the kind-on-the-gut fibre

> **For you, in plain words.** Most fibre is harsh on an IBS gut. Partially-hydrolysed guar gum (Sunfiber, Regular Girl) is the rare exception — it ferments slowly and gently, helps both diarrhoea and constipation, and several trials show benefit at 5-10 g/day. *(Polymeropoulos 2014 n=120 IBS; Russo 2015 Italy)*

**Quick start.** 5 g/day in water or food, build slowly over 2 weeks. No "low-FODMAP violation" — PHGG is tolerated even on a strict low-FODMAP diet (Monash-certified low-FODMAP).

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six healing traditions described IBS-like patterns centuries before gastroenterology existed. TCM in particular has a remarkably rich gut formulary — and Iberogast (a German blend) has the strongest modern monograph. *(Charaka Chikitsa ch. 15-19; Shang Han Lun; Tai Ping Hui Min He Ji Ju Fang; Avicenna's Canon; Siddha Maruthuvam; Tibetan Four Tantras)*

**Quick start.** Pick ONE tradition that resonates and ONE formula from it. Most users pair Iberogast (German) + Hingvastak (Ayurveda) + one TCM granule formula matched to their pattern.

### 4.2 Ayurveda — Grahani + Atisara + Anaha

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda calls IBS-like patterns Grahani roga (small-intestine disease), Atisara (diarrhoea pattern), or Anaha (bloat/distention pattern). The classical formulas are Hingvastak churna (a gas-and-bloat powder taken with the first bite of food), Avipattikar churna (for reflux-overlap), Kutajarista (for diarrhoea), Bilvadi gulika (for diarrhoea + cramping), and Triphala (gentle nightly reset). Charaka and Sushruta dedicated whole chapters to gut patterns. *(Charaka Chikitsa ch. 15 Grahani; Charaka Chikitsa ch. 19 Atisara; AYUSH-CCRAS monographs)*

#### Open the science

Classical category for IBS-D: Pittaja atisara (heat-pattern diarrhoea) or Vataja atisara (wind-pattern diarrhoea); for IBS-C: Anaha (distention) with vata-pradhana grahani; for bloating: Adhmana. Central interventions: Deepana-pachana (kindling digestive fire), Vata-Pitta-Kapha rebalancing per pattern. Classical sources: Charaka Samhita Chikitsa ch. 15 (Grahani) + ch. 19 (Atisara); Sushruta Uttara Tantra; Ashtanga Hridaya Chikitsa; Bhavaprakasha. Kerala lineage: Kottakkal, Vaidyaratnam, Arya Vaidya Sala; North Indian: Baidyanath, Dabur, Patanjali; AYUSH-CCRAS monographs.

## 5. Biophysical & Mind-Body Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence non-dietary layer for IBS is gut-directed hypnotherapy (apps like Nerva, Mahana IBS, or the Whorwell Manchester protocol). Devices come after. *(Peters 2016 Nerva trial; Everitt 2019 ACTIB; Whorwell 1984)*

**Quick start, tonight:**
1. Download the **Nerva** app (Mind Over Gut, Australia — 6-week gut-directed hypnotherapy programme) OR **Mahana IBS** (CBT-based, 90-day programme).
2. Buy enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules.
3. Start a 10-minute slow paced breathing practice (5-6 breaths per minute) before each main meal.

### 5.2 Gut-directed hypnotherapy — strongest cognitive lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Twenty minutes a day of guided audio that retrains how your brain interprets gut signals. The Whorwell Manchester protocol (Whorwell 1984, n=30; Gonsalkorale 2003 n=250) is the gold standard, with response rates around 70% sustained at 5 years. Nerva and Mahana IBS bring this to your phone. *(Whorwell 1984 Lancet; Gonsalkorale 2003 Gut; Peters 2016 Nerva)*

**Quick start.** Nerva app (6-week structured programme, AU/UK/US/CA, ~$80 one-time or $20/month); Mahana IBS app (90-day CBT-based programme); free alternatives include the IBS Audio Program 100 (Healthy Audio). 15-20 min/day during the structured phase.

#### Open the science

Whorwell's Manchester protocol is the original gut-directed hypnotherapy: 12 weekly sessions, 30-45 min each, focused on guided imagery placing one hand on the abdomen and using metaphors of a calm river, a softening, releasing knot, etc. Gonsalkorale 2003 long-term follow-up showed 71% maintained substantial improvement at 5 years. Peters 2016 (Monash University, Australia) trialled an app-delivered version (precursor to Nerva) showing comparable benefit to live therapy in mild-moderate IBS. **Grade A** for the Whorwell protocol; **Grade B+** for app-delivered versions. The mechanism involves modulation of visceral pain perception via descending pain pathways (periaqueductal grey), reduced rectal hypersensitivity on barostat testing, and HPA-axis dampening.

### 5.3 CBT for IBS — Mahana IBS app and beyond

> **For you, in plain words.** Gut-directed cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) is one of the strongest non-pharm interventions in any of medicine. Self-applicable via apps or workbooks. Mahana IBS holds an FDA De Novo grant 2020 as a prescription digital therapeutic, but is home-administrable. *(Lackner 2018 Gastroenterology n=436; Everitt 2019 ACTIB Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol n=558; Mahana 2020 — FDA De Novo, regulatory-path context only)*

**Quick start.** Mahana IBS app (90-day programme); Everitt ACTIB study used a 24-week web-based programme (now offered free via the UK NHS as Regul8). Self-help workbook: *Reclaim Your Life from IBS* by Melissa Hunt. **Grade A** for CBT for IBS distress, pain, and bloating.

### 5.4 Slow paced breathing + HRV biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** Six breaths per minute (5-second inhale, 5-second exhale) directly activates the vagus nerve and reduces visceral hypersensitivity. Free or low-cost — phone apps make this trivial. *(Lehrer 2014 HRV-biofeedback review; Sgoifo 2015 vagal-IBS)*

**Quick start.** Free apps: Breathwrk, Awesome Breathing, Othership. Paid HRV hardware: HeartMath Inner Balance ($179), Lief (heart-wearable, $499), Polar H10 chest strap + Elite HRV app ($90 + free). 10 minutes twice a day. Best timing: 06:30 and before each main meal.

### 5.5 At-home SIBO breath testing

> **For you, in plain words.** A small wearable device (FoodMarble Aire 2) reads your breath hydrogen and methane in real time — so you can identify which foods set off fermentation and whether you have small-intestinal bacterial overgrowth. Optional but powerful for stuck cases. *(Pimentel 2020 SIBO review; FoodMarble Aire 2 validation)*

### 5.6 At-home stool microbiome tests

> **For you, in plain words.** Mail-in kits sequence your gut microbiome and give a personalised food-and-supplement report. **The interpretive reports vary widely in quality** — useful for direction, not diagnosis. *(Microbiome test landscape review 2024; Costea 2017 methods comparison)*

### 5.7 Low-FODMAP elimination — the Monash protocol

> **For you, in plain words.** Three phases: (1) strict elimination of all high-FODMAP foods for 2-6 weeks; (2) systematic reintroduction one FODMAP family at a time over 6-8 weeks; (3) personalised maintenance with only your trigger foods restricted. The Monash University app is the gold-standard FODMAP database. *(Halmos 2014 Gastroenterology n=30; Staudacher 2017 Gastroenterology n=104; Monash University FODMAP database)*

**Quick start.** Download the **Monash University FODMAP Diet** app ($10 one-time, AU/UK/US/CA — the only authoritative database). Plan a strict 2-week elimination. After symptom relief, reintroduce ONE FODMAP family per week (mannitol → sorbitol → lactose → fructose → fructans → galactans) with the Monash challenge protocol. **Grade A** for symptom response (~70% in IBS); **Grade B** for nutritional sustainability long-term.

#### Open the science

The low-FODMAP diet was developed at Monash University Melbourne by Sue Shepherd and Peter Gibson in the early 2000s. Halmos 2014 was the landmark RCT (n=30 IBS, n=8 healthy controls) showing low-FODMAP cut symptoms in 70% of IBS subjects vs typical Australian diet. Staudacher 2017 confirmed in n=104. Long-term concerns: prolonged strict elimination depletes Bifidobacterium (Staudacher 2017 showed this), so the reintroduction phase is non-negotiable — the strict phase is a diagnostic tool, not a lifestyle. The Monash app updates the FODMAP database with new food testing every few months.

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

The cluster doctors call "irritable bowel" is not a single disease and not "all in your head." It is a real, biological pattern with five overlapping drivers — fermentable foods, an over-reactive gut wall, a brain-gut conversation that has learned to amplify normal signals, microbiome imbalance, and (in some) bile-acid or motility timing problems. The good news: each of these has a home lever. The single highest-evidence intervention is not a drug at all — it is gut-directed hypnotherapy delivered via apps like Nerva or Mahana IBS, which retrains your brain's pain interpretation in 15-20 minutes a day. Pair it with the Monash low-FODMAP elimination (a 2-6 week diagnostic, NOT a forever diet — reintroduction is essential), enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules (Cochrane-supported), partially-hydrolysed guar gum, and a single matched probiotic strain. Expect meaningful symptom drop in 4-12 weeks, not 4 days. This is an independent, OTC, home-based protocol. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital visit. No surgery.** You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a physician in the loop or to pursue a clinic-route option (rifaximin for SIBO, prokinetics, endoscopy for red flags), that is your personal call — the protocol does not require it. Red flags that DO require clinical assessment: blood in stool, unexplained weight loss, anaemia, fever, family history of colon cancer or IBD, or new symptoms after age 50.
