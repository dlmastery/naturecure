## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What migraine actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Migraine is not a "bad headache." It is a brain-state disorder — the brain's pain-processing, mood, light-and-sound-processing and gut systems all temporarily go offline together. The good news: it has the strongest natural-medicine evidence base of any common neurological condition. *(Goadsby 2017 Cephalalgia review)*

### 1.2 The four levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: start a migraine diary app (Migraine Buddy, N1-Headache, MigraineCoach). This week: begin riboflavin 400 mg AM + magnesium glycinate 600 mg PM + CoQ10 100 mg × 3 — the three A-grade preventive supplements. This month: enrol in app-based CBT-for-migraine, set a consistent sleep window, and trial a Cefaly e-TNS device (consumer-purchasable). Triptans and CGRP monoclonals stay in §10.7.3. *(Schoenen 2008; Wang 2003; Sandor 2005; Penzien 2015)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Migraine is usually framed as "take a triptan when it hits" — a downstream rescue model that leaves the actual brain-state untouched. A more honest reframe: migraine is the **end-organ readout of four overlapping drivers** — (i) **mitochondrial energy deficit** in cortical and trigeminovascular neurons (the riboflavin / CoQ10 / magnesium target); (ii) **trigeminovascular CGRP-driven inflammation** (the feverfew / butterbur target and the CGRP-monoclonal target in §10.7.3); (iii) **cortical-spreading-depression susceptibility** (the magnesium + omega-3 + sleep target); and (iv) **central sensitisation + chronification + medication-overuse** that emerges when the first three are untreated (the CBT + biofeedback + structured-trigger-mapping target). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs A-grade preventive supplements + behavioural therapy + home neuromodulation + trigger mapping cuts migraine days in many users by 30-50% — comparable to topiramate or amitriptyline preventive efficacy without the side effects.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers run migraine — mitochondrial energy deficit, trigeminovascular inflammation, cortical-spreading-depression susceptibility, and central sensitisation / chronification. Naming them lets every supplement, breath and device aim at the right one. *(Goadsby 2017; Edvinsson 2019 CGRP)*

### 2.2 Mitochondrial energy deficit

> **For you, in plain words.** Migraine brains run hotter on less fuel. Riboflavin, CoQ10 and magnesium are the three molecules that refill the tank — and all three have A-grade prevention trials behind them. *(Schoenen 1998 + 2008; Sandor 2005; Wang 2003)*

**The deeper layer.** Cortical and trigeminal neurons in migraineurs show reduced ATP production, lowered phosphocreatine reserve and impaired Complex I activity on phosphorus-31 MRS studies (Welch 1989). The Schoenen 1998 Belgian RCT (n=55, 3-month) showed riboflavin 400 mg/day cut attack frequency by 50% in 59% of patients — a remarkable effect size. The 2005 Sandor Swiss RCT (n=42) on CoQ10 100 mg × 3 showed similar prevention. The Peikert 1996 + Wang 2003 magnesium RCTs (especially in migraine-with-aura) showed magnesium oxide 600 mg/day cut attack frequency. Targeted by **riboflavin 400 mg**, **CoQ10 100 mg × 3**, **magnesium 400-600 mg (glycinate or oxide)**, **alpha-lipoic acid**, and **mitochondrial-supporting Mediterranean diet**. **Synthesis.** This is the highest-evidence non-pharm layer in all of migraine medicine.

### 2.3 Trigeminovascular CGRP inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** A protein called CGRP fires from the nerves around your skull's blood vessels during an attack — that is what the new monoclonal injections target. Feverfew and butterbur push the same lever from a different angle. *(Edvinsson 2019; Diener Mig-99 2005; Lipton 2004 butterbur)*

**The deeper layer.** Trigeminal-nerve endings release calcitonin-gene-related-peptide (CGRP) during attacks, causing vasodilation + neurogenic inflammation + mast-cell degranulation. The CGRP monoclonals (erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab, eptinezumab) and gepants (rimegepant, ubrogepant, atogepant) target this directly — they live in §10.7.3 because they are clinic-route prescriptions. Targeted at home by **feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) — Diener Mig-99 2005 (German RCT, n=170) showed 1.9 attacks/month reduction**, **butterbur (Petadolex PA-free) — Lipton 2004 (n=245) showed 48% responder rate**, **ginger (acute, Cady 2011)**, and **curcumin (anti-inflammatory)**. **Synthesis.** Two strong herbs (feverfew + PA-free butterbur) shift this lever measurably.

### 2.4 Cortical-spreading-depression susceptibility

> **For you, in plain words.** The aura is a slow wave of electrical quiet sweeping across your brain's surface. Magnesium, omega-3 and steady sleep raise the threshold for this wave starting. *(Leao 1944 CSD; Pietrobon & Striessnig 2003)*

**The deeper layer.** Cortical spreading depression (CSD) is a slow propagating wave of neuronal depolarisation followed by suppression — the neurophysiological substrate of aura. Susceptibility is raised by magnesium deficiency, glutamate excess, sleep deprivation, dehydration and hormonal swings. Magnesium specifically blocks the NMDA receptor at the Mg²⁺ block site — and the Wang 2003 RCT showed magnesium prevention is most pronounced in migraine-WITH-aura. Targeted by **magnesium 400-600 mg/day** (the with-aura cornerstone), **omega-3 EPA + DHA** (Ramsden 2013), **steady sleep window**, **hydration**, **vitamin B6 + folate + B12 for menstrual migraine** (the homocysteine pathway, Lea 2009), and **avoiding the trigger foods that lower CSD threshold** (alcohol especially red wine, aged cheese, MSG, nitrates in cured meats). **Synthesis.** This is the with-aura sub-type's primary lever.

### 2.5 Central sensitisation + chronification

> **For you, in plain words.** When migraines run untreated for years, the brain's pain system rewires to be more triggerable — and overusing triptans / NSAIDs creates "rebound" headaches. Cognitive therapy and biofeedback retrain that system. *(Penzien 2015 CBT meta-analysis; Nestoriuc 2008 biofeedback meta)*

**The deeper layer.** Chronic migraine (≥15 headache days/month) develops central sensitisation — allodynia (pain from non-painful touch), peri-orbital sensitivity, persistent low-grade headache between attacks. Medication-overuse headache (MOH) emerges when triptans, ergots or NSAIDs are used ≥10-15 days/month — a paradoxical pharmacologically-induced chronification. Targeted by **CBT for migraine — Penzien 2015 meta (A-grade prevention)**, **MBSR — Wells 2014 (Cephalalgia, n=19 pilot then larger)**, **HRV biofeedback — Nestoriuc 2008 meta**, **trigger mapping and behavioural pacing**, **medication-overuse drug holiday under coordinated taper (in §10.7.3)**, and **home neuromodulation (Cefaly, gammaCore, Nerivio)**. **Synthesis.** This is the lever for the chronic and medication-overuse sub-types — and it is where most of the A-grade behavioural-therapy evidence sits.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 Eleven OTC molecules — table

> **For you, in plain words.** Eleven over-the-counter molecules with a real randomised trial or non-US monograph behind them. The B-vitamin / mineral / CoQ10 trio is the A-grade cornerstone; feverfew and PA-free butterbur are the B+ herbal layer; the rest are honest C-tier adjuncts. *(Pringsheim 2012 Canadian Headache Society guideline — used as scientific synthesis only)*

### 3.2 The A-grade trio — riboflavin, magnesium, CoQ10

> **For you, in plain words.** Three molecules with A-grade prevention trials. The Schoenen Belgian riboflavin trial, the Peikert and Wang magnesium trials, and the Sandor Swiss CoQ10 trial together form the strongest natural-medicine evidence base in all of neurology. Start these three first. *(Schoenen 1998 + 2008; Peikert 1996; Wang 2003; Sandor 2005)*

**The deeper layer.** Schoenen 1998 (Belgian RCT, n=55, 3 months) — riboflavin 400 mg/day cut attack frequency 50% in 59% of patients vs 15% placebo. Schoenen's 2008 follow-up review confirmed efficacy. Peikert 1996 (German RCT, n=81, 12 weeks) — magnesium oxide 600 mg/day cut attack frequency 41.6%. Wang 2003 (US RCT, n=39 pediatric) — magnesium oxide 9 mg/kg/day cut attack frequency particularly with aura. Sandor 2005 (Swiss RCT, n=42, 3 months) — CoQ10 100 mg × 3 cut attack frequency by ~50% in 47% of patients vs 14% placebo. All three are at minimum B+ grade and Pringsheim 2012 Canadian Headache Society Guideline gave riboflavin and magnesium "strong" recommendations. **Stack them all three from day 1.**

### 3.3 The B+ herbal layer — feverfew, PA-free butterbur

> **For you, in plain words.** Feverfew (the German Mig-99 trial) and butterbur (the Lipton Petadolex trial) are the two herbs with real prevention RCTs. **Butterbur — only PA-free formulations like Petadolex**; raw butterbur contains hepatotoxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids and is unsafe. *(Diener Mig-99 2005; Lipton 2004)*

**The deeper layer.** Diener 2005 (German RCT, n=170) — Mig-99 standardised feverfew extract 6.25 mg × 3/day cut attack frequency by 1.9 attacks/month vs 1.3 placebo. Lipton 2004 (n=245, US-led but cited as the trial-evidence anchor, not US-regulatory authority) — butterbur Petadolex 75 mg BID cut attack frequency 48% (responder analysis) vs 26% placebo. The German Commission E monograph supports both. **Butterbur safety:** in 2012 the German BfArM and the European EMA withdrew uncontrolled butterbur preparations because hepatotoxicity reports came from PA-containing formulations. PA-free Petadolex (Weber & Weber, Germany) remains available but is no longer registered as a drug in Germany — sold as supplement only. Monitor LFTs every 3 months on butterbur.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 Cefaly e-TNS device

> **For you, in plain words.** A small consumer-purchasable forehead device that delivers external trigeminal-nerve stimulation; the Schoenen 2013 PREMICE trial showed it cut migraine days. Available without prescription. *(Schoenen 2013 Belgium)*

**Quick start.** Cefaly Dual (~$400) — 20 min/day on forehead for prevention; 60 min for acute. Daily use for prevention.

#### Open the science

Schoenen 2013 (PREMICE, Neurology, n=67 Belgian RCT) — Cefaly daily 20 min for 3 months: responder rate 38.1% Cefaly vs 12.1% sham. Cefaly FDA cleared 2014 (regulatory-path context — not the evidence anchor). The Schoenen trial is the evidence anchor.

### 5.2 gammaCore nVNS

> **For you, in plain words.** A handheld vagus-nerve stimulator pressed against the neck — consumer-purchasable; PRESTO + ACT-1 trials show acute and preventive signal. *(Tassorelli 2018 PRESTO; Silberstein 2016 ACT-1)*

**Quick start.** gammaCore Sapphire (~$600 + monthly stimulation cards). Two 2-min stimulations on each side of the neck at attack onset, repeat in 20 min if needed.

#### Open the science

PRESTO (Tassorelli 2018 Neurology, n=243) — gammaCore acute cluster + migraine: 30.4% pain-free at 30 min vs 19.7% sham. ACT-1 (Silberstein 2016 cluster, n=150). gammaCore FDA cleared 2017 (regulatory-path context — not the evidence anchor).

### 5.3 Nerivio REN

> **For you, in plain words.** An upper-arm wearable that delivers remote electrical neuromodulation; phone-paired; the MOMENT trial showed acute effect. *(Yarnitsky 2017 MOMENT)*

**Quick start.** Nerivio (~$99/month subscription) — 45-min stimulation on upper arm at attack onset, paired with smartphone app. Up to 18 stimulations/month.

#### Open the science

MOMENT (Yarnitsky 2017 Headache, n=139) and the 2019 confirmatory trial — Nerivio REN: 67% pain relief at 2 h vs 39% sham. Nerivio FDA cleared 2019 (regulatory-path context — not the evidence anchor).

### 5.4 Migraine diary apps

> **For you, in plain words.** A smartphone diary is the trigger-mapping cornerstone. Migraine Buddy, N1-Headache, MigraineCoach. Free or low-cost; daily 2-minute log. *(Hundert 2014 mobile health migraine)*

**Quick start.** Pick one app, log every attack + meals + sleep + weather + cycle phase + stress + alcohol within 5 minutes of attack onset. Review month-end with the protocol.

### 5.5 Home environmental tools

> **For you, in plain words.** Blue-light blockers for photophobia, ice/heat packs for acute, magnesium-bath for night relief — small evidence each but cumulatively useful. *(Sprouse-Blum 2013 ice)*

**Quick start.** TrueDark or BlockBlueLight glasses for photophobia. Ice pack on neck + base of skull at attack onset. Magnesium-chloride bath (250-500 g Dead Sea salts) 2×/week for sleep.

### 5.6 Home BP + pulse-ox screen

> **For you, in plain words.** A home BP cuff and pulse-oximeter — to screen for secondary-cause patterns (severe hypertension, hypoxia) and to track autonomic changes. *(Wells 2014 autonomic in migraine)*

**Quick start.** Omron BP cuff weekly + pulse-ox monthly. Track HRV via Oura / Whoop / HeartMath. Pattern of nocturnal SpO2 dips → consider home sleep test for OSA.

**Quick start.** Daily floor: 250 g leafy greens (Mg + folate); 30 g raw nuts (walnut, almond, pumpkin seed for Mg); 100 g berries (anthocyanins); 30 ml EVOO; oily fish 3-4×/week (EPA + DHA); 60-100 g legumes. Drink: 2-3 L water + minimum 2 L hydration daily.

**Quick start.** Cook fresh daily. Limit aged/fermented foods initially; reintroduce one at a time after 6 weeks of stability. Eat protein at every meal to stabilise blood sugar.

### 10.5 Timing rules

- Caffeine cut-off **14:00** — consistency over zero; sudden withdrawal triggers attacks. *(Goadsby 2017)*
- Magnesium glycinate PM (sleep + Mg accumulation); split AM small + PM larger.
- Riboflavin AM with food — pee will turn fluorescent yellow (normal).
- CoQ10 with fat every dose.
- Feverfew × 2-3 with meals; never on empty stomach.
- Butterbur PA-free × 2 with food; LFTs every 3 months.
- Melatonin 30-60 min pre-sleep; ≤3 mg.
- Steady moderate caffeine — never zero, never 8 cups.
- Steady hydration — 2-3 L/day; salt + water before exercise.
- **NEVER Kapalbhati / Bhastrika** — BP spikes trigger attacks.
- **NEVER inverted yoga in pulsatile sub-type or hypertensive overlap**.

### 10.6 Strictly avoid (migraine-specific)

- **Skipped meals + dehydration** — top dietary trigger.
- **Aged cheese, cured meat (nitrites), red wine** — classical trigger triad.
- **MSG, aspartame, sucralose (large)** — diary-confirm individually.
- **Sudden caffeine swings** — never quit cold; never binge.
- **Ergot-containing herbs** in pregnancy or with triptans.
- **Pregnancy:** Feverfew (uterine), butterbur (theoretical), high-dose vitamin A.
- **High-dose ephedra / Ma Huang** — CV + migraine risk.
- **Kava** + Petadolex — combined hepatotoxic risk.
- **St. John's Wort** with any antidepressant or triptan.
- **Cold-water immersion + cardiovascular disease** — self-screen.

#### Open the science

STRIVE (Goadsby et al., NEJM 2017, n=955) showed erenumab cut migraine days ~3.2-3.7 vs 1.8 placebo over 6 months. ARISE confirmed for low-dose erenumab. PROMISE-2 (Lipton 2020) showed eptinezumab IV quarterly for chronic. PREEMPT 1 (Aurora 2010) and PREEMPT 2 (Diener 2010) established Botox for chronic migraine — 31 injection sites quarterly. The Pringsheim 2012 Canadian Headache Society guideline (used as scientific synthesis, not as US-regulatory authority) gave riboflavin and magnesium "strong" recommendations and CoQ10 and feverfew "weak-positive." ## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

> **For you, in plain words.** What is going on, what to expect, and what NOT to expect.

You have migraine — your brain's pain-processing, mood, light-and-sound-processing and gut systems temporarily go offline together. It is not "just a bad headache"; it is a brain-state disorder. The good news: migraine has the strongest natural-medicine evidence base of any common neurological condition. The riboflavin-magnesium-CoQ10 trio alone has multiple A-grade randomised trials behind it.

This protocol is different from the "take a triptan when it hits" path — it works UPSTREAM, on the four drivers (mitochondrial energy, trigeminovascular inflammation, cortical-spreading-depression susceptibility, central sensitisation). The protocol stack: A-grade supplement trio + feverfew + butterbur PA-free + omega-3 + CBT/MBSR + HRV biofeedback + home neuromodulation device (Cefaly or gammaCore or Nerivio) + a diary app + trigger-elimination + consistent sleep + Mediterranean diet.

What to expect: first 4 weeks are pattern shift and learning your CGM-equivalent (the diary). By week 8 most users see meaningful day-count drops. By month 3-4, the majority of episodic-pattern users (with consistent execution) cut headache days 30-50%. Chronic migraineurs take 4-6 months and may benefit from adding clinic-route options in §10.7.3. Menstrual sub-types respond well to the menstrual mini-prevention overlay.

What NOT to expect: overnight cure. Migraine doesn't go away in a week. Triptans, CGRP monoclonals, gepants, Botox are real options if the home protocol plateaus — they live in §10.7.3 and the trial data is real. The protocol respects your sovereignty.

The sovereign ethos: this is an OTC, home-based, independent path. **No prescription, no clinic and no hospital visit is required.** If you choose to keep a doctor in the loop — and chronic + medication-overuse sub-types should — that is your personal decision.
