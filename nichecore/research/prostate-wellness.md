> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for men whose prostate is starting to enlarge with age — slower urinary stream, getting up at night to pee, dribbling after voiding, a sense of incomplete emptying, urgency, and the slow march of "older man's plumbing." It is **not** a prostate-cancer protocol (separate workup pathway), not an alpha-blocker prescription, not a 5-alpha-reductase-inhibitor protocol. It is the layered, honest, evidence-graded path: pelvic-floor training, timed voiding, fluid-timing, weight loss (BPH improves nearly linearly with waist loss), the named OTC supplements (saw palmetto with honest CAMUS-null caveat, pygeum, beta-sitosterol, stinging nettle root, pumpkin seed oil, lycopene, zinc), and an honest conversation about where the clinic-route alpha-blockers, 5-ARIs, tadalafil, TURP, Rezum, UroLift, HoLEP, and prostate artery embolisation sit. *(WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034; Tacklind 2012 saw palmetto Cochrane; Barry 2011 CAMUS NEJM)*
>
> **PSA monitoring framing (honest).** Home PSA test kits (LetsGetChecked, Everlywell) are useful for **monitoring trajectory in a man who has already discussed PSA with himself** — they are not a stand-alone "should I have a prostate problem" screening tool. PSA is elevated by BPH, prostatitis, recent ejaculation, recent cycling, urinary infection, and prostate cancer — and a single elevated reading should be repeated, not panicked over. Velocity (change over time) matters more than absolute number. If your PSA is >4 ng/mL, rising >0.75 ng/mL/year, or you have any red-flag symptoms below, this is a urology workup pathway that is outside the OTC scope of this protocol — but the home protocol can run in parallel safely.
>
> **Red flags — when this is not just BPH (rule-out, not self-manage).** (1) **Visible blood in urine (gross haematuria)** — rule out bladder cancer, kidney pathology; urgent imaging. (2) **Acute urinary retention** (cannot pee at all for 8+ hours, painfully distended bladder) — emergency room; catheterisation. (3) **Persistent night-waking + recurrent urinary infections** — urology workup; rule out obstructive uropathy. (4) **Rapidly rising PSA** (>0.75 ng/mL/year) or PSA >4 ng/mL on repeat — urology + MRI fusion biopsy pathway. (5) **Bone pain + weight loss + fatigue + urinary symptoms** — rule out metastatic prostate cancer urgently. (6) **Erectile dysfunction in a man <50 with cardiovascular risk** — ED is sometimes the first sign of coronary artery disease; cardiovascular workup. (7) **Testicular pain or hard mass** — urology urgent.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What BPH actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) is the slow, age-related growth of the prostate gland. It is not cancer. By age 60, more than half of men show some BPH on histology; by 80, almost all. The symptoms — slow stream, hesitancy, urgency, frequency, nocturia, dribbling — come from the enlarged gland squeezing the urethra and irritating the bladder neck. *(McVary 2006 review; Roehrborn 2008 BPH epidemiology)*

**Quick start.** Score yourself on the **IPSS (International Prostate Symptom Score)** — 8 questions, free, self-administered. Total 0–7 = mild, 8–19 = moderate, 20–35 = severe. Re-score every 8–12 weeks. This is the only diagnostic that matters for tracking response; everything else is downstream.

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: no fluids in the 2 hours before bed; reduce caffeine + alcohol after 18:00; bladder diary for the next 3 days. This week: start saw palmetto 320 mg Permixon or equivalent daily, pumpkin seed oil 500 mg BID, pelvic-floor (Kegel for men) 3 sets × 10 reps daily using Squeezy app, walking 30 min daily. This month: order a home PSA test (LetsGetChecked, Everlywell) to establish your baseline trajectory; full IPSS scoring at baseline. *(EMA HMPC Serenoa monograph; Cucurbita pepo monograph; AUA pelvic floor for men — Squeezy app)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) is conventionally siloed as "androgen-driven gland enlargement" treated with alpha-blockers (tamsulosin 0.4 mg, alfuzosin 10 mg, silodosin 8 mg — relaxes smooth muscle at the bladder neck, fastest symptom relief, ejaculatory dysfunction is common side-effect), 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors (finasteride 5 mg, dutasteride 0.5 mg — shrinks the gland over 6–12 months by blocking DHT, sexual side-effects including post-finasteride syndrome are real and contested), daily low-dose tadalafil 5 mg (helps both BPH and ED), combination therapy (CombAT trial showed dutasteride + tamsulosin > either alone), and surgical procedures (TURP, Rezum water-vapor, UroLift, HoLEP, prostate artery embolisation). The integrative reframing is different: BPH is the **multi-system signature of a four-node network** — (i) **prostatic stromal-epithelial 5α-reductase activity + intraprostatic DHT** (DHT — dihydrotestosterone — drives stromal smooth muscle and epithelial growth; saw palmetto, pygeum and stinging nettle root partially inhibit 5α-reductase); (ii) **prostatic + bladder-neck smooth muscle tone via α1-adrenergic receptors** (the dynamic component of obstruction — smooth muscle is more responsive to stress, posture, caffeine, sympathetic load than gland-size alone); (iii) **chronic low-grade prostatic inflammation + metabolic syndrome** (visceral adiposity, insulin resistance, NAFLD, lipid dysregulation, leptin elevation all feed prostatic inflammation; weight loss improves IPSS nearly linearly); (iv) **pelvic-floor dysfunction + lifestyle amplifiers** (weak or hypertonic pelvic-floor, sedentary cycling pressure, prolonged sitting, late-evening fluid load, urge-frequency self-amplification, sleep-disordered breathing). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously lowers intraprostatic DHT, calms α1-adrenergic smooth-muscle tone, drains the metabolic-inflammatory load, and retrains the pelvic floor produces a real IPSS-mapped reduction for the majority of men with mild-to-moderate BPH — without alpha-blockers, 5-ARIs, or surgery — and for the small subset where it does not, §10.7.3 documents what the clinic-route actually offers.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers explain almost every BPH symptom — DHT pushes gland growth, smooth-muscle tone tightens the outflow, metabolic + inflammatory load amplifies it, and pelvic-floor + lifestyle habits shape the daily experience. Each gets a targeted home layer. *(Roehrborn 2008 BPH review; Parsons 2006 metabolic-BPH; McVary 2006)*

### 2.2 Intraprostatic DHT signalling

> **For you, in plain words.** The prostate runs on a male hormone (DHT — dihydrotestosterone) that is made inside the gland from testosterone. Several plant compounds partially block this conversion — saw palmetto, pygeum, stinging nettle root, beta-sitosterol — though the evidence is mixed and the Cochrane 2012 review of saw palmetto monotherapy found null effect (we surface that honestly). *(Tacklind 2012 Cochrane; Barry 2011 CAMUS NEJM; EMA HMPC Serenoa)*

**Quick start.** Saw palmetto extract (Permixon or Prostaserene 320 mg) AM with breakfast for a 6-month trial — stop honestly if no IPSS shift; pygeum (Pygeum africanum) 100 mg BID; stinging nettle root (Urtica dioica) 240 mg BID; beta-sitosterol 60 mg BID.

#### Open the science

The prostate gland is androgen-dependent. Testosterone enters the prostatic stromal cell and is converted by 5α-reductase isoenzymes (predominantly type 2 in stroma) to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which has 5× higher affinity for the androgen receptor than testosterone. DHT drives stromal smooth-muscle hypertrophy + epithelial proliferation, producing the gland enlargement. Saw palmetto (Serenoa repens) contains fatty acids and sterols that exert mild 5α-reductase inhibition + α1-adrenergic blockade + anti-inflammatory effect in vitro and in some clinical trials. **The honest Cochrane story:** Wilt 1998 Cochrane was positive; Tacklind 2012 Cochrane updated with newer trials and found Serenoa was no more effective than placebo for moderate-to-severe BPH; the large CAMUS trial (Barry 2011, NEJM, n=369, 18 months, 320 mg/day Permixon) was NULL for IPSS, peak flow, and quality of life vs placebo. Permixon-specific extracts continue to show signal in European trials but the Cochrane meta-analytic conclusion is null. Key actors: 5α-reductase type 2, androgen receptor, DHT, stromal myocytes, prostatic epithelium. Targeted by **saw palmetto (mixed evidence, B-grade for Permixon-specific, X-grade if expecting full alpha-blocker-equivalent response), pygeum (B-grade, Wilt 2002 Cochrane positive for nocturia), stinging nettle root (B-grade combo with saw palmetto, Krzeski 1993 + Lopatkin 2005), beta-sitosterol (B-grade, Wilt 1999 meta-analysis), pumpkin seed oil (C-grade, Vahlensieck 2015 Italian trial signal)**. **Synthesis.** The DHT layer is one of four — not the cornerstone given honest Cochrane evidence.

### 2.3 α1-adrenergic smooth-muscle tone

> **For you, in plain words.** Beyond gland size, the dynamic squeezing of the urethra by smooth muscle at the bladder neck and prostatic stroma is what makes the stream slow — particularly under stress, after caffeine, after sitting too long, after a tight belt. Stress reduction, gentle stretching, slow breathing, magnesium, and proper hydration loosen this layer in days, not months. *(McConnell 2003 MTOPS study confirming the dynamic component)*

**Quick start.** Magnesium glycinate 300 mg PM + slow paced breathing 5–6 bpm 10 min daily + Bhramari 5 min + caffeine cap 200 mg/day + walking 30 min/day to discharge sympathetic load + posture work (sitting + lumbar support + no prolonged cycling).

#### Open the science

The prostatic stroma and bladder neck are richly innervated by α1A-adrenergic receptors. Sympathetic tone increases smooth-muscle constriction at this junction, producing the dynamic component of bladder-outlet obstruction. The MTOPS trial (McConnell 2003, n=3,047, NEJM) showed that alpha-blockers (doxazosin) and 5-ARIs (finasteride) work on different components and combination outperformed either alone — confirming the two-layer model. Stress, caffeine, sympathetic activation, prolonged sitting, tight pelvic posture all amplify the dynamic component. Key actors: α1A receptor, prostatic stromal smooth muscle, sympathetic nervous system, parasympathetic tone, pelvic floor reflex arc. Targeted by **slow paced breathing 5–6 bpm (vagal — Lehrer 2003), Bhramari, HRV biofeedback, magnesium glycinate (smooth-muscle relaxant), caffeine cap, walking + sympathetic discharge, postural work, pelvic-floor PT (where hypertonic pelvic floor amplifies the tone), CBT-i for sleep-disordered breathing**. **Synthesis.** This is the fastest-shifting layer — many men feel real improvement in 2–4 weeks once they reduce caffeine + start magnesium + walk + breathe.

### 2.4 Metabolic-inflammatory load

> **For you, in plain words.** Belly fat, insulin resistance, fatty liver, high triglycerides, and chronic low-grade inflammation all feed BPH — Parsons 2006 showed that metabolic syndrome is one of the strongest independent risk factors for BPH progression. Lose 8–10 kg of visceral fat and IPSS often drops 30%+ without any prostate-specific supplement. *(Parsons 2006 metabolic-BPH cohort)*

**Quick start.** Mediterranean-millet base diet (§6), berberine 500 mg TID with meals, milk thistle 300 mg BID, Zone 2 cardio 30 min × 4–5/week + 2 strength sessions/week, waist tape target ≤95 cm (40+ years).

#### Open the science

Visceral adiposity, NAFLD/MASLD and insulin resistance establish a feed-forward loop: high adipose → high aromatase → high oestradiol → high SHBG → low free testosterone → relative DHT-prostate exposure; AND high insulin → elevated IGF-1 → prostatic proliferation; AND high CRP, IL-6, TNF-α → prostatic inflammation. Lycopene (lyc) from cooked tomatoes is associated with lower prostate disease across multiple cohorts (Etminan 2004 meta) — though randomised data are mixed. Sleep apnea (intermittent hypoxia + sympathetic spikes) independently worsens BPH symptoms via nocturia. Key actors: insulin, IGF-1, aromatase, leptin, IL-6, TNF-α, CRP, NAFLD enzymes. Targeted by **Mediterranean-millet base diet, berberine, milk thistle, sprouted small millets, cooked tomato + lycopene-rich foods (or 15 mg lycopene supplement, Bhuvaneswari 2007 + Etminan 2004 honest read), Zone 2 cardio + strength, weight loss, sleep-apnea screen (Epworth)**. **Synthesis.** The metabolic layer is the most under-prioritised — men chase saw palmetto when waist-loss + Mediterranean shift would have moved IPSS more.

### 2.5 Pelvic-floor + lifestyle amplifiers

> **For you, in plain words.** Pelvic-floor training (Kegel for men, taught by the Squeezy app) genuinely helps urinary stream + post-void dribble + storage symptoms — Newman 2014 RCT. Timed voiding, fluid timing, bladder diary, no fluids 2h pre-bed, and no late-evening alcohol move nocturia. Stop prolonged cycling if it irritates you (saddle pressure). *(Newman 2014 pelvic-floor for men; Tibaek 2007)*

**Quick start.** Squeezy app pelvic-floor sets 3×/day × 10 reps + bladder diary 3 days (frequency + volume) + no fluids 2h pre-bed + caffeine cutoff 14:00 + alcohol after 18:00 minimal + bidet for hygiene (reduces irritation in CPPS overlap).

#### Open the science

Pelvic-floor training improves both storage and voiding symptoms in BPH — Newman 2014 RCT (n=120) showed significant IPSS improvement with 12-week pelvic-floor protocol. Bladder retraining via timed voiding reduces frequency-urgency. Fluid timing (no fluids 2h pre-bed) directly reduces nocturia. Sleep-disordered breathing (OSA) amplifies nocturia via atrial natriuretic peptide release at night. Prolonged cycling can exacerbate symptoms via pudendal nerve compression + perineal pressure. Targeted by **Squeezy app (UK NHS-developed, A-grade for pelvic floor adherence), bladder diary, timed voiding, fluid timing, OSA screening (Epworth + Apple Watch night SpO2 + WatchPAT home study), cycling-saddle modification or substitution, posture work**. **Synthesis.** This is the cheapest, fastest, most under-used layer.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 Priority order — what works at home

> **For you, in plain words.** Home tools that move the dial: pelvic-floor training via Squeezy app (cornerstone — Newman 2014), bladder diary + timed voiding (cornerstone — cheap), home heating-pad over lower abdomen for prostatitis pain, infrared sauna 2–3×/week, home red-light over lower abdomen, HRV biofeedback, optional home PEMF mat. *(Newman 2014; Beever 2009)*

### 5.2 Pelvic-floor + Squeezy app

> **For you, in plain words.** Newman 2014 RCT showed 12-week pelvic-floor protocol significantly improved IPSS. The Squeezy app (UK NHS-developed) is the most-used adherence tool — 3 sets of 10 reps daily, 12-week structured progression. *(Newman 2014)*

**Quick start.** Download Squeezy (UK NHS app, $7), or use the Kegel Trainer / KegelMaster. Sets: 3×/day × 10 reps × 5 second holds initially, progressing to 10 second holds by week 6.

### 5.3 Bladder diary + timed voiding

> **For you, in plain words.** A 3-day bladder diary (frequency + volume + urgency + nocturia) is the cheapest diagnostic and the foundation for timed voiding (urinate every 2–3 hours by clock, not by urge). Bladder retraining reduces urgency by stretching the urge-frequency cycle. *(Newman 2014; Burgio 2002)*

**Quick start.** Paper bladder diary or use the **Continence Foundation Australia** template; 3 days of logging; then schedule voids every 2–3 hours during the day.

### 5.4 Heating pad + lower-abdomen warmth

> **For you, in plain words.** Heat over the lower abdomen and perineum helps prostatitis-CPPS pain — gentle, cheap, home-safe. *(Anothaisintawee 2011)*

**Quick start.** Sunbeam Xpress-Heat or hot-water bottle — 40°C, 30 minutes, lower abdomen, as needed for CPPS pain.

### 5.5 Home red-light + infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red-light panel (660 nm + 850 nm) over the lower abdomen for 15 minutes daily may support circulation + inflammation. Infrared sauna 2–3×/week supports circulation + sweat-driven detoxification. *(Hamblin 2017; Beever 2009)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red Light MitoMIN, Hooga HG500, Joov Solo — 15 min, 6 inches; Sunlighten Solo / JNH IR sauna 40 min 3×/week.

### 5.6 HRV biofeedback + breath

> **For you, in plain words.** HRV biofeedback at 5–6 breaths/min (HeartMath Inner Balance, Lief) is B-grade for stress reduction — and stress amplifies the α1-adrenergic component of BPH. *(Lehrer 2003)*

### 5.7 Optional home PEMF

> **For you, in plain words.** Home pulsed-electromagnetic-field mats (Bemer, Healthy Wave) have small-trial signal for circulation + inflammation — optional adjunct if other layers plateau. *(general PEMF review)*

### 6.6 Environmental & occupational triggers

#### Open the science

The MTOPS trial (McConnell 2003 NEJM, n=3,047) demonstrated that doxazosin + finasteride combination outperformed either alone for BPH progression. The CombAT trial (Roehrborn 2010, n=4,844, 4 years, dutasteride + tamsulosin) confirmed long-term combination superiority. CAMUS (Barry 2011 NEJM, n=369, saw palmetto 320 mg vs placebo 18 months) was the most influential null trial for saw palmetto monotherapy. Tacklind 2012 Cochrane updated meta-analysis was overall null for Serenoa. Rezum, UroLift and HoLEP each have published pivotal trials (McVary 2016, Roehrborn 2013, Kuntz 2008) — FDA clearance dates appear only as factual regulatory-path context, never as evidence anchor. The home protocol's stance: the §10.7.3 options are honestly described so the sovereign user can make their own decision when IPSS hasn't moved enough with the home protocol over 6 months.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

BPH is not cancer. It is the slow age-related growth of the prostate that more than half of men over 60 have. The symptoms — slow stream, getting up at night, urgency, dribbling — come from the gland squeezing the urethra plus smooth-muscle tone tightening the outflow. The integrative path is not a single herb. It is layered: the **pelvic-floor layer** (Squeezy app, 3 sets daily) is A-grade and under-used; the **bladder retraining layer** (timed voiding, no fluids 2h pre-bed) is A-grade and cheap; the **metabolic-inflammatory layer** (Mediterranean-millet + cooked tomato + weight loss + exercise) often moves IPSS more than any herb; the **herbal layer** (saw palmetto, pygeum, nettle, beta-sitosterol, pumpkin seed oil) is documented honestly — saw palmetto monotherapy has a null Cochrane meta-analysis (CAMUS 2011 NEJM), Permixon-specific has some signal, pygeum and nettle have B-grade. Run the protocol for 3–6 months before judging anything. Track your **IPSS** at baseline, week 12, month 6, month 12. **Home PSA testing** monitors trajectory, not screening — a single elevated reading is not cancer. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.** If you choose to keep a physician informed, that is your personal call. If you have any red-flag symptom (visible blood, can't pee at all, rapidly rising PSA), that is a urology pathway outside this protocol.
