## 1. Executive Summary

Brain fog is conventionally siloed as a "vague subjective complaint" or off-loaded into ADHD-stimulant prescribing, Modafinil/armodafinil-class wakefulness drugs, or psychiatric SSRI augmentation. The integrative reframing is different: persistent brain fog is the cortical presentation of a **four-node bioenergetic failure** — (i) mitochondrial energy collapse with impaired ATP/PCr turnover in prefrontal and hippocampal neurons, (ii) chronic low-grade **neuroinflammation** driven by microglial priming, vagal-gut-brain LPS leak and IL-6/TNF-α tone, (iii) **BDNF suppression** with reduced hippocampal neurogenesis and synaptic plasticity, and (iv) **cerebrovascular hypoperfusion** with endothelial dysfunction, low nitric oxide, and disturbed cerebral autoregulation. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously restores mitochondrial flux, quiets microglia, drives BDNF, and improves cerebral perfusion addresses the upstream drivers rather than chasing wakefulness with stimulants.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Mitochondrial energy collapse.** Prefrontal and hippocampal neurons are among the most metabolically expensive cells in the body — a single neuron burns ~4.7 × 10⁹ ATP molecules per second at rest. Brain fog cohorts (post-viral, peri-menopausal, insulin-resistant, sleep-deprived) show on ³¹P-MRS reduced phosphocreatine-to-ATP ratios, lower NAD⁺, depleted CoQ10, and impaired Complex I + IV throughput. Key actors: PGC-1α, SIRT1/3, mitochondrial fission/fusion (Drp1, Mfn1/2), NAD⁺ salvage (NAMPT). Presentation: morning slowness, mid-afternoon collapse, "thick" thinking, word-finding lag, mental fatigue disproportionate to physical work. Targeted by Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR), CoQ10/Ubiquinol, PQQ, Creatine 3–5 g, NAD⁺ precursors (NR / NMN), Rhodiola, B-vitamin floor, and home transcranial PBM (810 nm). **Synthesis:** ATP throughput is the upstream brake on the perceived "fog".

**2.2 Neuroinflammation and microglial priming.** Chronic microglial activation (M1-skew) sustains IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β and quinolinic acid via the kynurenine pathway, displacing tryptophan from serotonin synthesis and producing excitotoxic NMDA tone. Vagal-gut-brain axis: LPS translocation from a permeable gut activates TLR4 on enteric afferents, transmitted to nucleus tractus solitarius and propagated via cytokines across the blood-brain barrier. Post-viral cohorts (long COVID, post-EBV, post-influenza) show persistent microglial PET signal years after acute infection. Targeted by curcumin (Meriva/BCM-95), Boswellia AKBA, Bacopa monnieri, Lion's Mane (*Hericium erinaceus*), Omega-3 (EPA-heavy), Magnesium L-threonate, Quercetin, and a gluten-/seed-oil-free dietary base. **Synthesis:** Quieting microglia is mandatory before any nootropic stack can be felt.

**2.3 BDNF suppression and impaired neurogenesis.** Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor — released by neurons in response to exercise, caloric restriction, social novelty, and sleep — drives synaptic plasticity, hippocampal neurogenesis (subgranular zone), and long-term potentiation. Brain fog cohorts run low. BDNF is suppressed by chronic cortisol, IL-6, insulin resistance, sedentary behaviour, and sleep restriction; it is upregulated by Zone 2 cardio, resistance training, Lion's Mane (hericenones, erinacines crossing BBB → NGF/BDNF upregulation), Bacopa (bacosides), exercise, sauna heat shock, and intermittent fasting (modest). Targeted by Lion's Mane 1000–2000 mg, Bacopa 300–600 mg, exercise periodization (§7.6), saffron, and *Centella asiatica* (Gotu Kola/Mandukaparni). **Synthesis:** BDNF is the plasticity currency — fog clears when it rises.

**2.4 Cerebrovascular hypoperfusion and endothelial dysfunction.** Cerebral autoregulation, regional cerebral blood flow (rCBF), and endothelium-dependent vasodilation all decline with insulin resistance, hypertension, sedentary living, and visceral adiposity. Low NO bioavailability (eNOS uncoupling, ADMA elevation) compounds. Peri-menopause adds an estrogen-mediated NO drop. Brain fog co-occurs with cold extremities, orthostatic intolerance, and low HRV. Targeted by *Ginkgo biloba* EGb 761, Vinpocetine, beetroot-NO loading (300 mg dietary nitrate), L-citrulline, Cocoa flavanols (Cocoavia, 500 mg flavanols), Gou Teng (*Uncaria rhynchophylla*) in TCM, Bhujangasana / inversions (with HTN caution), and resistance + Zone 2 training. **Synthesis:** Without cerebral perfusion, the other three nodes can't deliver their work to cortex.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer
