We lead with the highest-evidence levers (sleep, exercise, light, protein-first eating, and a single-tasking practice). Supplements come second. We are honest where the science is mixed — including for Ginkgo, which the largest Cochrane review found does NOT improve cognition in most people. *(WHO Traditional Medicine Strategy 2025-2034; Cochrane Birks & Grimley Evans 2009 Ginkgo for cognition; AYUSH CCRAS Medhya Rasayana monographs)*

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What brain fog actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** "Brain fog" is not a diagnosis — it is a pattern of slowed processing, weaker working memory, and dragged-out word-finding that has five common drivers you can work on at home. *(Theoharides 2021 long-COVID neuroinflammation; Maki 2018 perimenopausal cognition)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: protect your sleep window (lights out by 22:30, no screens after 21:00, 18-20°C bedroom). This week: cut caffeine after 14:00, walk 45 minutes a day at a pace you can still talk through, eat protein at breakfast (30 g), order a direct-to-consumer thyroid + B12 + ferritin + vitamin D + HbA1c panel. These four moves cover the highest-evidence levers. *(Walker 2017 sleep review; Erickson 2011 exercise + hippocampus RCT)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Brain fog is usually shrugged off as "stress" or chased with stimulant prescriptions or expensive nootropic stacks — neither of which fixes the cause. A more honest reframe: persistent foggy thinking is the perceived output of **five overlapping drivers** — (i) **fragmented or insufficient sleep** (the single highest-impact driver, almost always present); (ii) **neuroinflammation and microglial priming** (post-viral, post-burnout, after chronic stress, in metabolic syndrome); (iii) **vascular or metabolic insufficiency** (insulin resistance, anaemia, low thyroid, low B12, dehydration, low vitamin D, low ferritin); (iv) **HPA-axis depletion** from long-term stress and over-caffeination; and (v) **attentional fragmentation** from chronic multi-tasking, smartphone over-use, notification load and the loss of single-task practice. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **sleep + Zone-2 exercise + protein-first eating + single-task practice** (the highest-grade frontline) with neuroinflammation cooling, vascular support, HPA reset, and an honest supplement stack hits all five drivers in parallel.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive the fog — bad sleep, brain inflammation, poor blood flow and metabolism, exhausted stress axis, and a fragmented attention habit. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Walker 2017; Theoharides 2021; Erickson 2011; McEwen 2008 allostatic load; Madore 2020 media-multitasking & memory)*

### 2.2 Fragmented sleep

> **For you, in plain words.** Even one short night of bad sleep cuts your prefrontal cortex's processing speed measurably the next day. Chronic short sleep is the most under-treated cause of "brain fog". *(Walker 2017; Killgore 2010 sleep-deprivation cognition)*

**The deeper layer.** Sleep — particularly slow-wave sleep — drives glymphatic clearance of beta-amyloid and other metabolites from the brain (Iliff 2012; Xie 2013). Fragmented sleep collapses prefrontal-dependent working memory, slows reaction time, impairs hippocampal consolidation, and elevates next-day cortisol. Sleep apnea, restless legs, evening alcohol, late caffeine, late blue-light exposure, an over-warm bedroom, and chronic short bedtime are the typical culprits. Targeted by **strict circadian anchors** (AM sunlight, 14:00 caffeine cut-off, 21:00 screen-cut-off, 22:30 lights out), **CBT for insomnia** (Sleepio, Somryst — Cochrane A-grade), low-dose **melatonin** (0.3-1 mg), **magnesium glycinate**, **L-theanine**, **Ashwagandha KSM-66**, **glycine 3 g**, and **home sleep apnea screening** (Lofta WatchPAT) where snoring or daytime sleepiness suggest it. **Synthesis.** No supplement compensates for chronic short sleep. Fix sleep first.

### 2.3 Neuroinflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** When the brain's immune cells (microglia) get stuck "on" — after a viral infection, after burnout, in metabolic syndrome — they release inflammation that slows neuron firing and dulls memory. *(Theoharides 2021 long-COVID; Hampel 2018 microglial priming)*

**The deeper layer.** Persistent microglial activation, mast-cell release of histamine and tryptase in the CNS, elevated IL-6 / TNF-α / IL-1β, and breakdown of the blood-brain barrier all create a sustained low-grade neuroinflammation. Triggered by SARS-CoV-2 (long-COVID), EBV reactivation, mould-VOC exposure, post-burnout HPA collapse, chronic high-glycemic diet, gut dysbiosis (LPS endotoxemia), poor vagal tone. Targeted by **omega-3 EPA+DHA (2-3 g)**, **curcumin + piperine + ghee**, **luteolin** (Theoharides protocol), **palmitoylethanolamide (PEA)**, **quercetin**, **resveratrol**, **Bacopa monnieri** (the only Tier-2 monograph-backed brain-tonic with B-grade RCT data), **Lion's Mane**, **low-histamine eating during flares**, **vagal-tone training**. **Synthesis.** Cooling the microglia is what un-sticks the cognitive slowdown in post-viral and burnout patterns.

### 2.4 Vascular & metabolic insufficiency

> **For you, in plain words.** Your brain uses 20% of your oxygen and glucose. High blood pressure, insulin resistance, low thyroid, low B12 and low iron all starve it. *(Yaffe 2014 insulin resistance & cognition; Smith 2008 B12 & cognition meta-analysis)*

**The deeper layer.** Brain perfusion runs through the carotids and vertebrals; cerebrovascular reactivity drops with hypertension, dyslipidaemia, smoking, and endothelial-NO dysfunction. Insulin resistance reduces brain glucose uptake (the "type 3 diabetes" framing). Hypothyroidism slows neuronal metabolism. B12 deficiency demyelinates the long axons that carry working-memory circuits. Iron deficiency cuts oxygen carriage. Targeted by **Zone-2 cardio (the single highest-evidence non-pharm cognitive intervention — Erickson 2011 hippocampal volume RCT)**, **MIND-Mediterranean-millet eating** (Morris MIND-diet 2015; Petridou 2019 Greek cohort), **DTC thyroid + B12 + ferritin + vitamin D + HbA1c at baseline**, **methyl-B12 if low**, **iron only if ferritin <30**, **methylfolate**, **vitamin D3 + K2**, **L-arginine + L-citrulline**, **dietary nitrate (beetroot)**, **cocoa flavanols**. **Synthesis.** Without cerebral perfusion and the right cofactors, no nootropic delivers.

### 2.5 HPA-axis depletion (burnout fog)

> **For you, in plain words.** Years of "always on" living burn the stress axis out — morning cortisol becomes flat, energy crashes by 11 a.m., evening cortisol rises and ruins sleep. The fog is downstream. *(McEwen 2008 allostatic load; Maki 2018 perimenopausal HPA + cognition)*

**The deeper layer.** Chronic stress shifts the diurnal cortisol curve from a healthy AM peak + PM trough to a flattened or inverted curve. Hippocampal CA1 neurons take direct hits from sustained glucocorticoid exposure. Allopregnanolone (a calming neurosteroid) drops; GABA tone weakens; the prefrontal cortex loses top-down control over the amygdala. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (A-grade, Choudhary 2017 Australia)**, **Rhodiola rosea (B-grade, Olsson 2009 Sweden)**, **Holy Basil (Tulsi)**, **Bacopa monnieri**, **strict caffeine discipline**, **Yoga Nidra / NSDR**, **MBSR / MBCT**, **slow-paced breathing 5-6 bpm**. **Synthesis.** This is the lever burnout sufferers most need and most over-stimulate around. The first move is often to take stimulants AWAY, not add more.

### 2.6 Attention fragmentation

> **For you, in plain words.** The smartphone and notification economy has trained most adults to context-switch every 47 seconds. Working memory cannot consolidate at that pace; the fog is the byproduct. *(Madore 2020 media-multitasking & memory; Mark 2008 attention switching)*

**The deeper layer.** Heavy media-multitaskers show measurably worse working memory, task-switching cost, attentional filtering, and episodic memory consolidation than light multitaskers (Ophir 2009; Madore 2020 *Nature*). Constant context-switching keeps the salience network (anterior cingulate, anterior insula) inflamed and the default-mode network unable to consolidate. Targeted by **deep-work / single-task blocks (90 min)**, **notification deletion** (delete Slack, email, social-media notifications from the lock screen), **phone-in-a-drawer protocol**, **dedicated focus apps** (Forest, Flow State, Brain.fm — evidence honest at C-grade), **CBT for digital overload**, **20:20:20 screen-rest rule**, **deliberate boredom** (no input for 10-30 min, 2×/day). **Synthesis.** The cheapest, highest-impact lever in this dossier. No supplement competes with restoring single-task practice.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on Ginkgo, the slow 6-12 week Bacopa onset, and the weak human evidence for the racetam family. *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS Medhya Rasayana; Cochrane Birks 2009 Ginkgo)*

### 3.2 Ginkgo — the honest Cochrane caveat (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Standardised Ginkgo (Schwabe's Tebonin EGb 761) is the most-studied herb for cognition — but the largest Cochrane review (Birks & Grimley Evans 2009; updated 2018) found it does NOT prevent or treat dementia and shows no consistent benefit for primary cognitive complaints. The EMA HMPC monograph 2015 keeps it for "cerebral insufficiency" — the symptom cluster of mild forgetfulness and slowed thinking — where smaller trials show modest signal. We list it as ONE layer of mixed evidence, not the cornerstone. Try 8-12 weeks; stop if no shift. *(Cochrane Birks & Grimley Evans 2009; EMA Ginkgo HMPC monograph 2015)*

**The deeper layer.** Standardised EGb 761 (24% flavone glycosides, 6% terpene lactones) modulates platelet-activating-factor antagonism, cerebral microcirculation, and acts as a mild MAO-inhibitor. Positive RCT data exists in mild cognitive impairment with neuropsychiatric symptoms (Ihl 2011 GOTADAY; Napryeyenko 2007). The 2018 Cochrane update concluded no convincing evidence for primary cognitive complaints or dementia prevention. **Grade C** in primary brain-fog/focus context; **Grade B** for cerebral-insufficiency cluster where the EMA monograph holds. Honest re-frame: an adjunct, not a cornerstone.

### 3.3 Bacopa — the slow-onset honest frame

> **For you, in plain words.** Bacopa monnieri (called Brahmi in Ayurveda) is the only herb in this dossier with multiple randomised trials and an AYUSH-CCRAS monograph for cognition. Honest catch: it takes 6-12 weeks before any benefit appears — most users stop too early. Take 300-600 mg standardised extract daily for 12 weeks before deciding. *(Stough 2001 Australia; Stough 2008; Kongkeaw 2014 meta-analysis; AYUSH-CCRAS Medhya Rasayana)*

**The deeper layer.** Bacopa monnieri standardised to 50% bacosides (KeenMind, Himalaya Bacopa) modulates serotonin (5-HT2A), GABA, BDNF, and acts as a free-radical scavenger in the hippocampus. Stough 2001 (Australian Swinburne University, n=46) showed significant improvement in verbal learning and working memory at 12 weeks vs placebo. Kongkeaw 2014 meta-analysis (n=437 across 9 RCTs) confirmed delayed-recall improvement. AYUSH-CCRAS Medhya Rasayana monograph lists Brahmi as the lead cognitive rasayana. **Grade B**. Honest caveats: nausea/loose stool in ~15%; takes 6-12 weeks; not a fast-acting nootropic.

### 3.5 Honest skip-list — the popular-but-weak

- **Phosphatidylserine.** Bovine-source legacy; soybean PS modest evidence; honest C-grade; not in cornerstone.
- **Lipid-based "miracle" nootropic stacks** (Alpha Brain, Mind Lab Pro, etc.). Marketing-heavy, evidence honest at C. We prefer single-ingredient transparency.

## 5. Biophysical & Cognitive-Practice Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence layers for chronic brain fog are sleep restoration + Zone-2 cardio + protein-first eating + single-task practice. Devices come after — and several popular ones (consumer tDCS especially) carry honest mixed-evidence caveats. *(Walker 2017 sleep review; Erickson 2011 hippocampus RCT; Madore 2020 multitasking; Cochrane Elbers 2024 home tDCS)*

**Quick start, tonight:**
1. Set a 14:00 caffeine cut-off and a 21:00 screen cut-off. Lights out by 22:30.
2. Delete Slack, email, and social-media notifications from your phone's lock screen. Move the apps off the home screen.
3. Schedule one 90-minute single-task block tomorrow morning, phone in another room.

### 5.2 Sleep restoration — the cornerstone

> **For you, in plain words.** No supplement compensates for chronic short sleep. 7-9 hours, with consistent 22:30 lights-out and AM sunlight, is the single highest-evidence cognitive intervention. *(Walker 2017; Iliff 2012 glymphatic)*

**Practical, tonight.** AM sunlight within 30 min of waking (10 min outdoors). 14:00 caffeine cut-off. 21:00 screen cut-off (or true blue-light glasses + Night Shift / f.lux). Bedroom 18-20°C, blackout curtains. Bedside sound machine on low (myNoise, Marpac Dohm). No alcohol within 3 h of bed. Magnesium glycinate 400 mg + L-theanine 200 mg + Ashwagandha KSM-66 300 mg at 21:30 (NOT for pregnancy/breastfeeding/Rx sedative users). Track with Oura, Whoop, or paper PSQI weekly.

### 5.3 Zone-2 cardio (the highest non-pharm cognitive lever)

> **For you, in plain words.** 45 minutes of walking or cycling at a pace you can still talk through, 4-5 days a week, grew the hippocampus measurably over a year in Erickson's randomised trial — the strongest non-pharmacological cognitive intervention we have. *(Erickson 2011 PNAS; Hillman 2008 exercise + cognition review)*

**Practical.** Heart-rate zone 60-70% of max (about "I can talk in full sentences"). 45 min Mon/Wed/Fri (minimum). Walking, cycling, swimming, rowing, dancing. Strength training Tue/Thu 45 min (compound lifts — squat, deadlift, press, row). Saturday HIIT 20 min OR sport. Sunday restorative + long walk. **Grade A** for the cognitive intervention; **A** for the cardio-metabolic outcomes.

### 5.4 Protein-first eating

> **For you, in plain words.** 30 g of protein at breakfast prevents the 11 a.m. crash, blunts post-meal glucose, and stops the muscle-protein-synthesis fog of intermittent low-protein eating common in fog sufferers. *(Layman 2003 protein-cognition; Mamerow 2014 even-protein-distribution RCT)*

**Practical.** Breakfast 30 g protein: 3 eggs + 30 g cheese (~30 g) OR 200 g Greek yoghurt + 30 g whey + walnuts OR 150 g salmon + 2 eggs. Lunch 30-40 g protein: lentil dal 200 g + grilled fish 100 g, OR chicken-and-greens bowl. Dinner 25-30 g protein. Daily total 1.4-1.8 g/kg lean mass for cognitive support and lean-mass preservation.

### 5.5 Single-task / deep-work practice

> **For you, in plain words.** Schedule a 90-minute single-task block daily — phone in another room, notifications off, one tab, one task. The strongest behavioural lever for restoring working memory. *(Newport 2016 Deep Work synthesis; Madore 2020 *Nature* multitasking)*

**Practical.** 06:30-08:00 OR 09:00-10:30 (whichever your peak): one 90-min deep-work block. Phone in a drawer. Notifications off. One task. Pomodoro 50/10 alternative for those who can't sustain 90 min. Free apps: Forest (phone-locks), Flow State, Brain.fm (focus audio — C-grade evidence), Cold Turkey (site blocker), Freedom (cross-device blocker). After 4 weeks, two 90-min blocks. After 8 weeks, the practice generalises.

### 5.6 Notification deletion — the cheapest lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Delete Slack, email and social-media notifications from your lock screen. Move the apps off the home screen. The strongest single-day cognitive intervention you can do at zero cost. *(Mark 2008 interrupted-attention work; Kushlev 2016 notification-distress RCT)*

**Practical.** Settings → Notifications → off for: Slack, email, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, WhatsApp groups (keep individual chats), news apps. Move the apps off the home screen into a "Distractions" folder buried two pages deep. Check email/Slack at three scheduled windows (10:00, 14:00, 17:00).

### 5.7 CBT for insomnia (CBT-i) — the under-used lever

> **For you, in plain words.** When fog is sleep-driven, CBT-i is more effective than any sleep drug, with no rebound and no dependence. Self-applicable via Sleepio (UK), Somryst (US-DTC), or the free CBT-i Coach app. *(Trauer 2015 CBT-i meta-analysis; Espie 2012 Sleepio RCT)*

**Practical.** Sleepio (£199 6-week programme, NHS-funded in parts of the UK), Somryst (US DTC), or free CBT-i Coach (Veterans Affairs app — listed as a free tool, not as a US authority). 6-8 week course; 80%+ achieve durable sleep improvement. **Grade A**.

### 5.8 Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) and MBCT

> **For you, in plain words.** 8-week Kabat-Zinn MBSR or Segal MBCT improve cognitive control, working memory, and reduce mind-wandering — the highest-evidence cognitive practice we have. *(Mrazek 2013 MBSR + working memory RCT; Chiesa 2011 MBSR meta-analysis)*

**Practical.** 8-week course (Bangor UK, Brown, Oxford UK, Padmasambhava Buddhist Centre, or local MBSR teacher). Daily 30-min sit + 10-min body scan + informal practice. Free audio: Mark Williams 8-week MBCT; Jon Kabat-Zinn MBSR; Tara Brach. **Grade A** for stress reduction; **Grade B** for cognitive measures.

### 5.9 Transcranial photobiomodulation (red-light to the head)

> **For you, in plain words.** Pulsed 810 nm near-infrared light to the head and intranasal — Vielight Neuro Gamma + 810 intranasal — shows early signal for cognitive support in mild cognitive impairment. No definitive RCT yet — honest C-grade. *(Hamblin 2018 transcranial PBM review; Saltmarche 2017 Neuro Gamma case series)*

**Practical.** Vielight Neuro Gamma (810 nm pulsed at 40 Hz, 25 min, 5×/week) + Vielight 810 intranasal (25 min, 5×/week). Eye protection mandatory. **Do not use** in pregnancy, with head/face cancer, photosensitising drugs, brain surgery history, or severe seizure disorder. **Grade C** for cognition. ~$1,749 + $299.

### 5.10 Home tDCS — the honest mixed-evidence caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Consumer transcranial direct-current stimulation devices (Halo Sport, Foc.us, Caputron) deliver tiny electrical currents to the scalp. Lab tDCS has signal for some cognitive tasks; **consumer-grade devices have honestly mixed evidence** and the Cochrane 2024 home-tDCS review found insufficient evidence for routine use. We list these for transparency, not as a cornerstone. *(Cochrane Elbers 2024 home-tDCS; Horvath 2015 tDCS meta-analysis critique)*

**Practical.** Caputron Mini-CT ($295), Foc.us Go Flow ($199). 2 mA, 20 min, 3-5×/week. **Read the safety sheet**; do not use over damaged skin, with pacemaker, in pregnancy, with seizure history. **Grade C** consumer-grade; lab-grade tDCS for specific tasks **B**.

### 5.11 Consumer EEG / neurofeedback (Muse, Mendi, Sens.ai)

> **For you, in plain words.** EEG headbands give real-time feedback on your meditation depth. They make the practice stickier — but the cognitive outcome benefit beyond a regular meditation practice is honestly modest. *(Brandmeyer 2016 EEG-meditation review; Muse RCT data)*

**Practical.** Muse 2 ($249), Mendi ($299), Sens.ai ($1,499 premium). 10-15 min daily. Use as a behavioural commitment device, not as the cognitive intervention itself. **Grade C**.

### 5.12 Cognitive training apps — the honest transfer-effect caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** BrainHQ (Posit Science), CogniFit, Lumosity — daily cognitive training apps. They reliably make you better at the games. The honest catch — Cochrane reviews and the 2014 Stanford consensus letter found the "transfer" to real-world cognition is modest at best. *(Stanford 2014 cognitive-training consensus; Cochrane Gates 2019 computerised cognitive training)*

**Practical.** BrainHQ ($14/month) has the best evidence in the IMPACT trial; Lumosity and CogniFit are pleasant but transfer-effect honestly C. Spaced-repetition apps (Anki, Quizlet) are different — for actual knowledge retention they're Grade A and worth daily use.

### 5.13 Focus / cognitive-state apps

> **For you, in plain words.** Brain.fm uses functional music for focus; Flow State for time-locked focus blocks; Forest grows trees for phone-off time. They are behavioural commitment devices — honestly C-grade for the cognitive effect itself, useful for the behavioural anchor. *(Brain.fm Wang 2019; Forest behavioural data)*

### 5.14 HRV biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** HeartMath, Lief, Polar H10 + Elite HRV teach slow paced breathing (5-6 bpm) — the most reliable way to lift vagal tone and reduce the stress side of the fog. *(McCraty 2014 HeartMath review; Lehrer 2013 HRV biofeedback)*

**Practical.** HeartMath Inner Balance ($179), Lief patch ($499), or free Elite HRV app + Polar H10. 10-15 min twice a day at 5-6 breaths per minute (5-sec in, 5-sec out, or 4-sec in, 6-sec out). **Grade B**.

### 5.15 Sauna + cold splash

> **For you, in plain words.** 20 minutes in a home sauna at 50-60°C, 3×/week, then a 30-60 second cold splash — Finnish Laukkanen 2018 cohort showed cardiovascular and dementia-risk reductions. **Not for** uncontrolled cardiovascular disease, pregnancy, or alcohol use that day. *(Laukkanen 2018 Finland sauna-dementia cohort)*

**Practical.** Sunlighten Solo, HigherDOSE blanket, infrared barrel sauna. Plunge tub OR cold shower. 20 min hot → 1 min cold, 3-4 cycles, 3×/week. **Grade B** for dementia risk reduction; **B** for cognitive resilience.

### 5.16 Light therapy — bright-light box AM

> **For you, in plain words.** If you live in a winter climate, 10,000 lux for 20-30 min within 30 min of waking re-anchors your circadian rhythm and lifts seasonal cognitive slowdown. *(Cochrane Pail 2011 bright-light SAD; Eastman 1998 winter circadian)*

**Practical.** Carex Day-Light Classic Plus, Northern Light Technologies, Beurer TL90. 10,000 lux for 20-30 min at 06:30-07:30. **Grade A** for seasonal affective disorder; **B** for circadian-driven cognitive slowdown.

### 5.17 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Stacked unregulated nootropic powders, modafinil bought from grey-market sites, caffeine pills above 200 mg, "smart drink" sweetener-loaded RTDs, sleep deprivation framed as productivity, anti-cholinergic OTC sleep pills (Benadryl, ZzzQuil), chronic alcohol "to wind down". *(WHO; UK MHRA anti-cholinergic burden 2022)*

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.1 What to measure, in plain words

### 11.2 Self-administered cognitive scoring

> **For you, in plain words.** The main self-score is PROMIS Cognitive Function — 8-question form, scored 0-100. Add the Cognitive Failures Questionnaire (25 items) and a daily 5-min cognitive battery. All scoring is done at home. *(Cella 2010 PROMIS; Broadbent 1982 CFQ)*

Primary: **PROMIS Cognitive Function Short Form 8a** — 8-question, 5-min self-report; 0-100 T-score; population mean 50, SD 10. Cadence: baseline / week 4 / 8 / 12 / month 6 / 9 / 12.

Secondary: **Cognitive Failures Questionnaire (CFQ — Broadbent)** — 25-item, 0-100; lower = better. Cadence: baseline + week 12 + month 6 + 12.

Tertiary: **Daily 5-min cognitive battery** — Cambridge Brain Sciences (free academic version), CogniFit, BrainHQ Frontiers (free initial battery), Lumosity Insights. Track processing speed, working memory, attention.

Overlay (sleep, mood, anxiety): **PSQI** (target <5), **ISI** (insomnia), **GAD-7**, **PHQ-9** — baseline + month 3, 6, 12.

Objective adjuncts: home BP 7-day average (Omron / Withings); daily HRV (HeartMath / Oura / Whoop); home sleep test (Lofta WatchPAT) if snoring or Epworth >10. Track in Bearable, Cronometer, or paper.
