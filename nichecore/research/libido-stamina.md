>
> **Sudden-onset erectile dysfunction in men is a cardiovascular red flag (load-bearing).** ED is sometimes the first visible sign of coronary artery disease, with the penile-artery-vs-coronary-artery diameter difference making vascular dysfunction appear in the penis before the heart. **A man under 50 with sudden-onset ED — especially with any cardiovascular risk factor (BMI ≥27, BP ≥130/85, HbA1c ≥5.7, smoker, family history) — needs a full cardiovascular workup** alongside this protocol. ED is not "just sexual"; it is endothelial. This protocol's vascular layer (Mediterranean + omega-3 + magnesium + exercise + L-citrulline + Korean Red Ginseng + Pycnogenol) addresses endothelium honestly, but a man with chest pain or exertional shortness of breath belongs in a cardiology pathway first.
>

(2) **Peyronie's disease** (palpable plaque, penile curvature on erection, painful erection) — urology referral; the home protocol's L-arginine + vitamin E layer is documented but a clinic conversation is appropriate. (3) **Priapism** (painful erection lasting >4 hours) — emergency room urgently. (4) **Sudden loss of libido + headache + visual changes** — rule out pituitary tumour (prolactinoma); DTC prolactin first. (5) **Painful intercourse in women** beyond perimenopausal dryness — rule out endometriosis, vaginismus, vulvodynia, pelvic floor hypertonia, infection, GSM. (6) **New cyclical bleeding** in a postmenopausal woman after starting any phyto-oestrogen — gynaecology workup; rule out endometrial pathology. (7) **Severe relational distress or coercion** — that is its own conversation outside this protocol's scope; couples or individual therapy first.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What "low libido" actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Low libido and reduced stamina are rarely one thing — they are the visible apex of a four-node network of stress, hormones, vascular function, and relational dynamics. Most people fit into more than one of these nodes; treating only one (e.g. taking tadalafil for a stress-driven problem) explains why the prescription path so often disappoints. *(Brotto 2017 women's sexual desire review; Hatzichristou 2010 men's review)*

**Quick start.** Score yourself on **IIEF-15** (men) or **FSFI** (women) at baseline. Both are free, self-administered, and the standard outcome instrument in trials. Score the **DSDS** (Decreased Sexual Desire Screener) for either sex if desire is the dominant complaint. Re-score every 8–12 weeks. This is the only diagnostic that matters for tracking response.

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: lights out by 22:30, screens off 90 min before bed, alcohol cap ≤4 drinks/week, phone outside the bedroom. This week: strength training 3×/week (men) or 2×/week + yoga (women), Mediterranean-millet base diet, Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM, magnesium glycinate 300 mg PM, vitamin D3 4000 IU AM. This month: order a home testosterone panel (men: Hone Health, LetsGetChecked) or DUTCH dried-urine (women: Precision Analytical) at 07:00–09:00 fasted to establish your baseline; baseline IIEF-15 or FSFI; baseline DSDS. *(Wankhede 2015 Ashwagandha; Hong 2002 KRG; Gonzales 2009 maca)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Sexual dysfunction is conventionally treated by silo — ED with PDE5-inhibitors, women's HSDD with flibanserin/bremelanotide, low T with TRT, GSM with vaginal oestradiol — leaving the relational, behavioural and stress layers under-addressed. The integrative reframing: libido + stamina are a **four-node network** — (i) **HPA-axis stress + sleep loss + serotonin trough** (sleep restriction drops T 10–15%/week in men (Leproult 2011); SSRIs block desire and orgasm); (ii) **sex-hormone signalling** (men: Leydig T + free T; women: ovarian + adrenal androgen, oestradiol-progesterone, postmenopausal GSM); (iii) **endothelial / vascular function** (penis and clitoris share NO-cGMP biology; metabolic syndrome impairs endothelium); (iv) **relational + behavioural + pelvic-floor layer** (couples communication, performance anxiety, sensate focus, pelvic-floor work, GSM home layer). **Synthesis.** A home protocol covering all four nodes produces real IIEF / FSFI / DSDS shift for most — without prescriptions — and §10.7.3 documents clinic-route options honestly.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers explain almost every low-libido / low-stamina story — stress amplifies it, sex-hormone signalling weakens, vascular function dips, and the relational/behavioural layer fails. Each gets a targeted home layer. *(Brotto 2017; Hatzichristou 2010; Leproult 2011; Bhasin 2018)*

### 2.2 HPA stress + sleep + serotonin trough

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress drops libido in both sexes — cortisol competes with sex-hormone signalling, and the brain in stress de-prioritises arousal. Sleep loss alone drops morning testosterone in men 10–15%/week. Ashwagandha, MBSR, slow breathing, and a 22:30 bedtime fix this layer faster than any pill. *(Choudhary 2017 Ashwagandha cortisol RCT; Leproult 2011 sleep restriction-T trial)*

**Quick start.** Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM + Holy Basil (Tulsi) tea 2 cups + lights out 22:30 + Yoga Nidra 30 min at bedtime + slow paced breathing 5–6 bpm 10 min.

#### Open the science

Chronic stress + insufficient sleep + caffeine excess + alcohol elevate cortisol, which competes for pregnenolone substrate ("pregnenolone steal") and suppresses GnRH + testicular steroidogenesis in men, and adrenal DHEA + ovarian function in women. SSRIs widely impair desire, arousal and orgasm via 5-HT2A activation downstream of serotonin reuptake inhibition. Sleep restriction drops morning testosterone (Leproult 2011) and reduces women's responsive desire and arousal. Key actors: HPA axis (CRH-ACTH-cortisol), DHEA, pregnenolone, glucocorticoid receptor, 5-HT 1A/2A, monoamine oxidase A. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol −23–28% across Choudhary 2017, Lopresti 2019, Salve 2019 RCTs), Holy Basil (Tulsi), Rhodiola, Bacopa, Saffron, Yoga Nidra, MBSR, pranayama, slow paced breathing, strict sleep work, CBT for anxiety/depression if comorbid**. **Synthesis.** Without HPA correction, libido gains regress under life stress.

### 2.3 Sex-hormone signalling

> **For you, in plain words.** Men's testosterone (especially free T) drives libido; women's libido tracks free testosterone + oestradiol + DHEA + cycle phase. Men: Ashwagandha, tongkat ali, fenugreek, shilajit, zinc, vitamin D, sleep, and strength training restore signalling. Women: shatavari, saffron, maca, DHEA cautious, plus the §2.2 stress layer move it. *(Wankhede 2015; Tambi 2012 tongkat ali; Steels 2011 fenugreek; Gonzales 2009 maca; Agha-Hosseini 2008 saffron)*

**Quick start.** *Men:* Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM + tongkat ali (Physta/LJ100) 200 mg AM + zinc picolinate 15 mg PM + vitamin D3 4000 IU AM + maca 1500–3000 mg AM. *Women:* Shatavari 500 mg BID + saffron 28 mg AM + maca 1500–3000 mg AM + Ashwagandha 600 mg PM + vitamin D3 4000 IU AM.

#### Open the science

**Men:** Hypothalamic GnRH pulsatility → pituitary LH → Leydig-cell testosterone via the cholesterol-pregnenolone-DHEA-androstenedione-testosterone pathway. SHBG binds 50–60% of T; only ~2% is free + bioactive. Aromatase (CYP19A1) in adipose/liver/testes converts T → oestradiol; in obesity, aromatase activity rises, dropping free T. Wankhede 2015 Indian RCT (n=57) showed Ashwagandha + resistance training raised T by 96 ng/dL vs placebo + training over 8 weeks. Tambi 2012 (n=76 Malaysian men) — tongkat ali 200 mg/day raised free T over 4 weeks. Steels 2011 Australian fenugreek RCT showed modest T + libido improvement. Biswas 2010 Indian Shilajit RCT (n=60, 90 days) showed total T ↑ 23%. **Women:** Sexual desire tracks ovarian and adrenal androgen (free T + DHEA-S), oestradiol-progesterone balance, cycle phase (peri-ovulatory desire peak), and post-menopausal GSM-related dyspareunia. Gonzales 2009 Peruvian maca RCT showed libido improvement without altering hormone levels — non-hormonal mechanism. Agha-Hosseini 2008 Iranian saffron RCT improved sexual function in women on fluoxetine. Targeted by **Ashwagandha, tongkat ali (men), fenugreek (Testofen), shilajit, zinc, vitamin D, boron (men); shatavari, saffron, maca, ashwagandha, fenugreek-female-libido studies (Rao 2015), DHEA cautious (women — post-menopausal HSDD evidence)**. **Synthesis.** Sex-hormone signalling is the second lever after HPA.

### 2.4 Endothelial / vascular function

> **For you, in plain words.** The penis and clitoris are vascular organs — erection and clitoral engorgement depend on healthy endothelium making nitric oxide. Mediterranean diet, omega-3, L-citrulline, Korean Red Ginseng, Pycnogenol, exercise, and weight loss restore the endothelial layer. *(Cormio 2011 L-citrulline ED RCT; Hong 2002 KRG ED RCT; Stanislavov 2003 Pycnogenol+L-arg ED RCT)*

**Quick start.** Mediterranean-millet base + omega-3 EPA 1000 + DHA 500 mg AM + L-citrulline 3 g AM + Korean Red Ginseng 900 mg TID (or as needed before activity) + Pycnogenol 60 mg + L-arginine 1 g AM + Zone 2 cardio 30 min × 4–5/week + strength 3×/week.

#### Open the science

Erection (and clitoral engorgement) requires endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS) → NO → guanylate cyclase → cGMP → smooth-muscle relaxation in cavernosal arteries → engorgement. Metabolic syndrome, NAFLD, insulin resistance, EDCs and ageing impair eNOS function. The penile artery (1–2 mm) shows vascular dysfunction before the coronary artery (3–4 mm) — hence the cardiovascular sentinel framing. L-citrulline (arginine precursor that bypasses gut metabolism) raises plasma arginine more effectively than L-arginine alone; Cormio 2011 Italian RCT (n=24 mild ED) showed citrulline improved erection hardness. Korean Red Ginseng (Panax ginseng) — Hong 2002 J Urol RCT (n=45) — improved IIEF in men with mild-moderate ED. Pycnogenol + L-arginine (Prelox) — Stanislavov 2003 — improved IIEF. Targeted by **Mediterranean diet, omega-3, L-citrulline 3 g, Korean Red Ginseng (KRG) 900 mg TID, Pycnogenol 60–120 mg, L-arginine 1–3 g, beetroot juice (nitrate), epimedium (icariin — PDE5 modulator), Zone 2 cardio + strength + weight loss**. **Synthesis.** This is the layer that PDE5-inhibitors target downstream — fixing the upstream endothelium is the integrative move.

### 2.5 Relational + behavioural + pelvic-floor

> **For you, in plain words.** Sex is a behavioural skill and a relational practice — not just a hormone-vascular event. Sensate focus (Masters & Johnson), couples communication (Gottman, Esther Perel), mindful-sex training (OMGYes for women, Coral, Lover), pelvic-floor work (Squeezy men, Elvie/Perifit women), and addressing performance anxiety + GSM are A-grade non-pharm layers. *(Masters & Johnson 1970; Brotto 2017; Newman 2014 pelvic-floor; Gottman couples work)*

**Quick start.** Sensate focus exercises (free protocols online or via a sex therapist — book recommendation: "Come As You Are" by Emily Nagoski for women, "She Comes First" by Ian Kerner; OMGYes course for women; Lover or Coral app for both). Pelvic-floor: Squeezy app for men, Elvie Trainer / Perifit / kGoal for women. Couples communication: Gottman 7-Principles workbook + Esther Perel's "Mating in Captivity" or "The State of Affairs". *(Masters & Johnson 1970; Brotto 2017; Gottman 1999)*

#### Open the science

Sensate focus (Masters & Johnson 1970) is the foundational behavioural sex-therapy protocol — couples re-learn non-genital touch + gradually escalate, decoupling performance pressure from sensation. Helen Singer Kaplan extended it for desire-disorder work. Esther Perel's clinical work focuses on the desire-vs-intimacy paradox in long-term relationships. The Gottman method (research-based couples therapy) maps the four predictors of relationship failure (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) and the antidotes. Brotto 2017 reviewed mindfulness-based interventions for women's sexual dysfunction with B-grade evidence. Newman 2014 RCT showed pelvic-floor training improved IIEF in men. Pelvic-floor hypertonia in women contributes to dyspareunia + low desire; Elvie / Perifit / kGoal trainers gamify the practice. GSM (genitourinary syndrome of menopause) — vulvovaginal atrophy, dryness, dyspareunia — affects ~50% of postmenopausal women and is dramatically under-treated; the home protocol's vitamin E suppositories, sea buckthorn oil, hyaluronic acid, and ospemifene-equivalent layered work supports the OTC layer, but vaginal oestradiol (Rx) is in §10.7.3 as the clinic-route option. Targeted by **sensate focus, sex therapy (Helen Singer Kaplan model), Esther Perel work, OMGYes, Coral, Lover apps, Gottman method, pelvic-floor training (Squeezy men, Elvie Trainer + Perifit + kGoal + Kegelmaster women), CBT for performance anxiety, masturbation literacy (Nagoski), GSM home layer (vitamin E suppositories, sea-buckthorn oil, hyaluronic acid suppositories)**. **Synthesis.** This is the under-used layer that often moves the needle more than any herb — particularly for relational + women's-desire presentations.

### 3.4 Honest non-recommendation: Yohimbine

> **For you, in plain words.** Yohimbine (Pausinystalia yohimbe) is a B-grade alpha-2 adrenergic antagonist for organic ED in trials, but it has real cardiovascular cautions: BP spike, anxiety, tachycardia, hypertensive crisis with tyramine-rich foods + MAOIs. #### Open the science

TRAVERSE (Lincoff 2023 NEJM, n=5,246, 33 months) reversed the previous concern about TRT cardiovascular safety in men with documented low T and CV risk — non-inferior to placebo for major adverse cardiac events. However, TRT suppresses the HPG axis, drops sperm count (often irreversibly without hCG co-therapy), can raise haematocrit excessively, and is a lifelong commitment. Hong 2002 J Urol (n=45 Korean men) showed Korean Red Ginseng 900 mg TID improved IIEF — B-grade for organic ED. Cormio 2011 Italian RCT (n=24) showed L-citrulline 1.5 g/day improved erection hardness in mild ED. Stanislavov 2003 demonstrated Pycnogenol + L-arginine improved IIEF.
