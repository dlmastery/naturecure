## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What memory-aging actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** "Memory aging" is not a single disease — it is a spectrum running from normal age-associated forgetfulness (where to find the keys, the actor's name on the tip of the tongue) through subjective cognitive decline (you notice it but tests don't catch it yet) to mild cognitive impairment (tests start to catch it) and only then to dementia. The biology is largely the same; the leverage is highest at the early end. *(Petersen 2014 MCI review; Jessen 2014 SCD framework)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** This week: (1) **get a hearing test** — untreated hearing loss is the single largest modifiable dementia risk factor; (2) **walk 45 minutes a day at conversation pace** — Erickson 2011 hippocampal-volume RCT; (3) **eat the MIND diet** (leafy greens daily, berries, nuts, fish, olive oil, beans, whole grains); (4) **order a DTC panel** — thyroid, B12, folate, homocysteine, vitamin D, HbA1c, ApoE4 (only if you want to know); (5) **protect 7-9 h sleep** (lights out 22:30, 18-20°C bedroom, no screens after 21:00); (6) **download the SAGE self-test** to set your baseline. These six moves cover the highest-leverage levers — supplements layer on top. *(Lancet Commission 2020 + 2024 update; Ngandu 2015 FINGER; Morris 2015 MIND; Smith 2008 B12 meta-analysis; SAGE self-test)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Memory decline with aging is conventionally siloed as either "normal aging" (watch and wait) or, once a diagnostic label crosses the MCI/dementia threshold, as **cholinesterase-inhibitor management** (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine, memantine) and the new **anti-amyloid biologics** (lecanemab, donanemab) — none of which arrest the underlying drivers and all of which carry meaningful adverse events. The cholinesterase inhibitors give a modest, transient cognitive lift; the anti-amyloid biologics shave amyloid burden on PET but produce only a 27-35% slowing of the rate of decline (CLARITY-AD; TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2) at the cost of meaningful ARIA-E and ARIA-H rates — brain swelling and microhaemorrhages — particularly in APOE4 homozygotes. The integrative reframing is different: age-associated memory impairment is the cortical/hippocampal expression of **five convergent root drivers** — (i) **untreated hearing loss + sensory deprivation** (the Lancet Commission's highest modifiable risk), (ii) **mitochondrial-energetic failure with NAD⁺ depletion**, (iii) **chronic neuroinflammation** sustained by microglial priming, vagal-gut-LPS leak, and hyperhomocysteinaemia, (iv) **cerebrovascular insufficiency** — small-vessel disease, endothelial dysfunction, hypertension, hypoperfusion — and (v) **brain insulin resistance ("Type-3 diabetes")**. A sixth, behavioural lever — **social connection + cognitive engagement** — moves the diurnal cortisol curve and the prefrontal-default-mode dynamic. **Synthesis.** A protocol that simultaneously restores hearing input, drives mitochondrial flux, quiets microglia + lowers homocysteine, improves cerebral perfusion, reverses brain insulin resistance, and protects sleep and social engagement hits all five drivers in parallel — independent of whatever drug class the user may or may not be on.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five mechanisms drive cognitive aging — untreated hearing loss + sensory deprivation, mitochondrial slowdown, brain inflammation, poor blood flow, and brain insulin resistance. Naming them lets every food, breath, exercise, supplement and device aim at the right one. *(Lancet Commission 2020; Mattson 2018 mitochondrial aging; Heneka 2015 neuroinflammation; de la Torre 2018 vascular cognitive; de la Monte 2017 type-3 diabetes)*

### 2.2 Untreated hearing loss

> **For you, in plain words.** This is the **single biggest modifiable risk factor** for late-life cognitive decline — bigger than smoking, depression, or diabetes individually. The 2020 Lancet Commission attributes ~8% of preventable dementia worldwide to untreated mid-life hearing loss; the 2023 ACHIEVE trial in older adults at risk showed that fitting hearing aids slowed 3-year cognitive decline by ~48% in the higher-risk subgroup. *(Livingston Lancet Commission 2020; Lin ACHIEVE 2023)*

#### Open the science

Mechanistic links: (a) **sensory deprivation** — under-stimulated auditory cortex undergoes cross-modal reorganisation and grey-matter atrophy; (b) **cognitive load** — listening effort drains attentional resources that would otherwise support working memory and consolidation; (c) **social withdrawal** — people with untreated hearing loss progressively avoid groups, dinners, family gatherings; social isolation is itself a Lancet-Commission dementia risk; (d) **inflammatory and vascular comorbidity overlap**. The ACHIEVE trial (Lin et al., *Lancet* 2023, n=977) randomised older adults with hearing loss to hearing-aid intervention vs health-education control; in the higher-risk pre-specified subgroup (the ARIC-NCS cohort) the 3-year decline on the global cognition composite was reduced by 48% in the hearing-aid arm. Home OTC hearing aids (Jabra Enhance Plus, Lexie, Sony CRE-C10/E10, Eargo, MDHearing) became consumer-purchasable after the FDA's 2022 OTC hearing-aid rule (regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor — the evidence anchor is ACHIEVE). **Synthesis.** If you can hear better, you think better. The cheapest, highest-leverage move in this entire dossier for users over 60 with any hearing-loss signal.

### 2.3 Mitochondrial energy collapse

> **For you, in plain words.** The brain runs on glucose and oxygen at a furious rate — 20% of your body's oxygen for 2% of its weight. With age, the mitochondrial machinery (the cell's power stations) loses throughput; NAD⁺ — the molecule that runs the rechargers — drops by 50% between age 30 and 70. Hippocampal neurons, which run the consolidation circuit, are first to feel the brownout. *(Mattson 2018 mitochondrial aging; Imai 2014 NAD⁺ decline)*

#### Open the science

NAD⁺ declines with age (CD38 hydrolase up-regulation, NAMPT down-regulation), Complex I and IV throughput drops, mitochondrial DNA damage accumulates, and PGC-1α-driven biogenesis slows. Hippocampal CA1 pyramidal neurons are particularly vulnerable. Targeted by **Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR)** (Italian Pettegrew 1995 + Cochrane Hudson 2003 — modest cognitive support in early AD), **Creatine 3-5 g** (Avgerinos 2018 meta-analysis — cognitive support under sleep deprivation and in vegetarians), **CoQ10 / Ubiquinol** (mitochondrial Complex I), **PQQ** (mitochondrial biogenesis — Japanese Nakano 2009), **Nicotinamide Riboside (NR) 300 mg or NMN 250-500 mg** (Australian/Japanese safety RCTs established, cognitive outcomes still pending — honest emerging-evidence frame), and **transcranial photobiomodulation at 810 nm + 40 Hz gamma** (Vielight Neuro Gamma; Saltmarche 2017 MCI pilot; gamma-frequency mouse work translated to small human trials). **Synthesis.** ATP throughput is the upstream brake on the perceived "slowing" of thought.

### 2.4 Chronic neuroinflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** Aged immune cells in the brain (microglia) get stuck "on" — releasing inflammatory signals that slow neuron firing and dull memory. Three under-recognised drivers: high blood homocysteine (a B-vitamin-fixable problem), leaky gut spilling bacterial fragments to the brain, and chronic gum disease (periodontal *P. gingivalis* DNA has been found in brain tissue). *(Heneka 2015 neuroinflammation; Smith VITACOG 2010; Dominy 2019 P. gingivalis in brain)*

#### Open the science

Aged microglia drift toward an M1-like reactive phenotype, sustaining IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β, and complement C1q. **Hyperhomocysteinaemia** (homocysteine >10 µmol/L) accelerates white-matter lesions and cognitive decline; the Oxford VITACOG trial (Smith 2010, Douaud 2013) showed that **800 µg folic acid + 500 µg B12 + 20 mg B6** slowed brain-atrophy rate by ~30-50% in the subgroup with elevated homocysteine. Gut-LPS leakage adds peripheral inflammation. Oral microbiome links: periodontal *P. gingivalis* gingipain DNA mapped to AD brain (Dominy 2019, Cortexyme work). Targeted by **Curcumin (Meriva, BCM-95)** (Cox 2015 Australian RCT — cognitive support in older adults), **Boswellia AKBA**, **Omega-3 EPA + DHA (2-3 g, ≥1 g DHA)** (Yurko-Mauro 2010 MIDAS DHA-cognition RCT — strongest signal in ApoE4 carriers), **Methyl-B12 + L-methylfolate + B6** (the VITACOG stack — only if homocysteine elevated), **palmitoylethanolamide (PEA)**, **luteolin** (Theoharides anti-microglial protocol), **oral hygiene** (interdental brush, periodontal hygiene, water-pik), **gluten-restricted base for users with high anti-gliadin reactivity**. **Synthesis.** Quieting microglia and lowering homocysteine are prerequisites for any neurogenic stack to work.

### 2.5 Cerebrovascular insufficiency

> **For you, in plain words.** Your brain is the most vascular organ you have. Untreated high blood pressure between 40 and 65 is one of the Lancet Commission's largest mid-life dementia risks — it silently damages the small vessels that feed memory-critical regions. The single highest-evidence non-pharm cognitive intervention we have — Zone-2 aerobic exercise — works largely through this lever. *(Lancet Commission 2020; SPRINT-MIND 2019 BP and dementia; Erickson 2011 hippocampal volume RCT)*

#### Open the science

Cerebrovascular reactivity drops with hypertension, dyslipidaemia, smoking, and endothelial-NO dysfunction. The SPRINT-MIND trial (2019, n=9361) showed that intensive BP control (target <120 mmHg systolic) reduced the combined endpoint of MCI + probable dementia by ~15% over 5 years. Small-vessel disease (white-matter hyperintensities on MRI) tracks with vascular cognitive impairment. Targeted by **Zone-2 cardio 150-300 min/week** (Erickson 2011 PNAS — measurable hippocampal volume gain in a year of moderate exercise; Northey 2018 meta-analysis), **home BP cuff to target <130/80**, **MIND-Mediterranean-millet eating** (Morris MIND-diet 2015 — 53% slower cognitive decline in highest-tertile adherence), **dietary nitrate (beetroot juice)** (Stanaway 2017 cerebrovascular reactivity), **cocoa flavanols** (Brickman 2014 dentate-gyrus perfusion), **L-arginine + L-citrulline**, **omega-3**, **vitamin D3 + K2**, **smoking cessation** (the largest single vascular lever), **alcohol ≤7/wk women / ≤14/wk men** (Lancet Commission). **Synthesis.** Without perfusion and the right vascular hygiene, no nootropic delivers.

### 2.6 Brain insulin resistance ("Type-3 diabetes")

> **For you, in plain words.** Insulin doesn't just regulate blood sugar — it has its own job inside the brain, supporting hippocampal energy uptake and memory consolidation. When the brain becomes insulin-resistant (mid-life metabolic syndrome, abdominal obesity, T2D), memory consolidation slows. This is why HbA1c, fasting insulin, and waist circumference are cognitive-health metrics. *(de la Monte 2017 type-3 diabetes; Arnold 2018 brain insulin resistance; Yaffe 2014 insulin resistance + cognition)*

#### Open the science

Brain insulin signalling in hippocampus and prefrontal cortex regulates glucose uptake, BDNF expression, and synaptic plasticity. Insulin resistance reduces brain glucose uptake (PET-FDG signal drops in cognitively normal at-risk adults years before symptoms). The "type-3 diabetes" reframing maps T2D and pre-diabetes onto a cognitive-risk dimension. Targeted by **HbA1c <5.5%**, **fasting insulin <8 µIU/mL**, **waist circumference <94 cm men / <80 cm women**, **time-restricted eating 14:10 or 16:8 (not in underweight, HPA-depleted, pregnant)**, **berberine 500 mg 2-3×/day** (Chinese AMPK + metformin-comparable hypoglycaemic trials), **cinnamon (Ceylon, *C. zeylanicum*) 1-3 g**, **fenugreek (Trigonella) 5-10 g**, **strength training 2-3×/week** (the underrated insulin-sensitivity lever), **Mediterranean MIND base**. **Synthesis.** Fix the metabolism and the brain follows.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Fourteen over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on Ginkgo (Cochrane null), coconut/MCT (Cochrane null), and resveratrol (Cochrane null in human disease prevention). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS Medhya Rasayana; Cochrane Birks 2009 Ginkgo; Cochrane Berner 2019 coconut)*

### 3.2 Ginkgo — the honest Cochrane caveat (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Standardised Ginkgo (Schwabe's Tebonin EGb 761) is the most-studied herb for cognition — but the largest Cochrane review (Birks & Grimley Evans 2009; updated 2018) found it does NOT prevent or treat dementia. The 8-year GEM trial in the US (DeKosky 2008, n=3069) was decisively NULL for dementia prevention in older adults. The EMA HMPC monograph 2015 keeps it for "cerebral insufficiency" — the symptom cluster of mild forgetfulness and slowed thinking — where smaller trials show modest signal. We list it as ONE layer of mixed evidence, not the cornerstone. Try 8-12 weeks; stop if no shift. *(Cochrane Birks & Grimley Evans 2009; DeKosky GEM 2008 JAMA; EMA Ginkgo HMPC monograph 2015)*

#### Open the science

Standardised EGb 761 (24% flavone glycosides, 6% terpene lactones — ginkgolides A, B, C, bilobalide) modulates platelet-activating-factor antagonism, cerebral microcirculation, and acts as a mild MAO-inhibitor. Positive RCT data exists in mild cognitive impairment with neuropsychiatric symptoms (Ihl 2011 GOTADAY; Napryeyenko 2007). The 8-year GEM trial (DeKosky 2008) randomised 3069 older adults to Ginkgo 120 mg BID vs placebo for dementia prevention — flatly null. The 2018 Cochrane update concluded no convincing evidence for cognitive complaints or dementia prevention. **Grade C** in dementia prevention; **Grade B** for cerebral-insufficiency symptom cluster where the EMA monograph holds. Honest re-frame: an adjunct, not a cornerstone. **Bleeding caution** — separate from warfarin, antiplatelets, surgery (stop 7-10 days pre-op).

### 3.3 Bacopa — the slow-onset honest frame

> **For you, in plain words.** Bacopa monnieri (called Brahmi in Ayurveda) is the only herb in this dossier with multiple randomised trials AND an AYUSH-CCRAS monograph for cognition. Honest catch: it takes 6-12 weeks before any benefit appears — most users stop too early. Take 300-600 mg standardised extract daily for 12 weeks before deciding. *(Stough 2001 Australia; Calabrese 2008; Kongkeaw 2014 meta-analysis; AYUSH-CCRAS Medhya Rasayana)*

#### Open the science

Bacopa monnieri standardised to 50% bacosides (Himalaya BacoMind, KeenMind / Soho Flordis) modulates serotonin (5-HT2A), GABA, BDNF, and acts as a free-radical scavenger in the hippocampus. Stough 2001 (Australian Swinburne University, n=46) showed significant improvement in verbal learning and working memory at 12 weeks vs placebo. Calabrese 2008 (n=54 elderly) replicated in healthy older adults. Kongkeaw 2014 meta-analysis (n=437 across 9 RCTs) confirmed delayed-recall improvement. AYUSH-CCRAS Medhya Rasayana monograph lists Brahmi as the lead cognitive rasayana per *Charaka Samhita Ch. 1 Karaprachitiya Rasayana Pada*. **Grade B**. Honest caveats: nausea/loose stool in ~15%; takes 6-12 weeks; not a fast-acting nootropic; pair with warm-water lassi or ghee per classical instruction to dampen GI side effects.

### 3.5 Honest skip-list — the popular-but-weak

> **For you, in plain words.** Several heavily-marketed cognitive products do not earn a place in this protocol on honest evidence. We list them for transparency so you know where we stand. *(Cochrane reviews; honest evidence audit)*

- **Coconut oil and MCT oil for cognition.** Marketed loudly for "ketones feed the dementia brain". Cochrane Berner 2019 + multiple non-significant trials in mild AD: **null for cognitive outcomes**. Excellent culinary fat for satiety; **not a cognitive intervention.** **Grade D for cognition.**
- **Resveratrol (high-dose).** Heavily marketed sirtuin activator. Cochrane Salehi 2020 + multi-trial summary in human disease prevention: **null**. **Grade D-C.**
- **Methylene blue.** Research-only mitochondrial agent; consumer-grade dosing uncertain; not in protocol. **Grade D / research-only.**
- **NAD+ precursors NR / NMN at consumer doses.** Safety well established; **cognitive outcomes still pending in humans** — Australian/Japanese RCTs underway. Honest emerging-evidence frame: don't expect a cognitive lift on current data. **Grade C-emerging.**
- **Apoaequorin (Prevagen).** Heavy US marketing; FTC settlement on advertising claims; honest evidence base for cognitive benefit is **weak**. Not in protocol. **Grade D.**
- **High-dose vitamin E alone.** PREADVISE and other trials: **null** for dementia prevention; older alpha-tocopherol-only trials raised mortality concerns at very high doses. Get vitamin E from food (almonds, sunflower seeds, olive oil) and from the mixed-tocopherol AREDS-style blend if you use one. **Not as monotherapy.**
- **Phosphatidylserine.** Bovine-source legacy; soybean PS modest evidence; honest C-grade; not in cornerstone.
- **Multi-ingredient "miracle" cognitive stacks** (Alpha Brain, Mind Lab Pro, Neuriva, Procera AVH, etc.). Marketing-heavy, evidence honest at C. We prefer single-ingredient transparency.

## 5. Biophysical & Cognitive-Practice Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve home-administered levers, ranked by honest evidence. **Hearing aids and sleep restoration top the list** (A-grade). Zone-2 cardio is next (A). The supplement and device layers come after. *(Lancet Commission 2020; Walker 2017 sleep; Erickson 2011 hippocampal volume; Ngandu FINGER 2015)*

### 5.2 Hearing-aid fitting (the highest lever)

> **For you, in plain words.** If you have any hearing loss and you are over 55, fitting hearing aids on your own initiative is the single highest-leverage cognitive-health move in this entire dossier. ACHIEVE trial: 48% slowed decline at 3 years in the at-risk subgroup. OTC hearing aids became consumer-purchasable in 2022 — you do not need a prescription. *(Lin ACHIEVE 2023; Lancet Commission 2020)*

#### Open the science

**Quick start.** Self-administer a hearing test (Mimi app, free Apple/Android hearing-screening apps, audiologist if available). If any high-frequency loss is found (above 25 dB at 4 kHz), the OTC route is the fastest action: Jabra Enhance Plus (~$799), Sony CRE-C10 (~$999), Sony CRE-E10 (~$1300), Lexie B2 by Bose (~$999), MDHearing (~$400-700), Eargo (~$1500-2500). Self-fitting takes ~15-30 min. Adjust over 4-6 weeks (your brain needs to re-learn). The actual evidence anchor is ACHIEVE (Lin et al., *Lancet* 2023, n=977) which showed a 48% 3-year cognitive-decline reduction in the higher-risk pre-specified ARIC-NCS subgroup. Regulatory-path context only: the US FDA OTC hearing-aid rule (October 2022) made consumer self-purchase legal. **Grade A.**

### 5.3 Sleep restoration

> **For you, in plain words.** Sleep clears the brain's metabolic waste (including amyloid) through the glymphatic system — chronic short sleep is now considered a dementia risk factor in its own right. Lights out 22:30, 18-20°C bedroom, no screens after 21:00, AM sunlight within 30 min of waking. Cochrane A-grade for CBT-i. *(Iliff 2012 glymphatic; Walker 2017; Trauer 2015 CBT-i Cochrane)*

#### Open the science

Slow-wave sleep drives glymphatic clearance of beta-amyloid and other metabolites (Iliff 2012 *Sci Transl Med*; Xie 2013). One night of partial sleep deprivation measurably elevates next-day amyloid burden on PET (Shokri-Kojori 2018). Fragmented sleep collapses prefrontal-dependent working memory and elevates next-day cortisol. **CBT-i** (Cochrane Trauer 2015 + Cochrane Espie 2012) beats sleep medications durably without rebound. Self-applicable through Sleepio (UK NHS-approved), Somryst (Health Canada licensed), CBT-i Coach (free), Stellar Sleep. Sleep apnea screening: home WatchPAT (Lofta — consumer mail-order), Withings Sleep mat. **Grade A** (sleep restoration as a cognitive intervention).

### 5.4 Zone-2 cardio (the highest non-pharm cognitive lever)

> **For you, in plain words.** A 45-minute walk at conversation pace, most days, is the most evidence-supported single non-pharmaceutical cognitive intervention we have. Erickson 2011 PNAS RCT: measurable hippocampal volume gain in a year. *(Erickson 2011 PNAS; Northey 2018 meta-analysis)*

#### Open the science

Erickson 2011 (n=120 older adults) randomised aerobic vs stretching for a year; aerobic gained ~2% hippocampal volume (a structural reversal of the typical ~1-2% annual age-related shrinkage). Northey 2018 *Br J Sports Med* meta-analysis (n=39 RCTs) confirms multi-domain cognitive benefit at ≥45 min moderate aerobic ≥3×/week. Mechanism: BDNF upregulation, cerebrovascular reactivity, hippocampal neurogenesis. Target: 150-300 min/week Zone-2 (conversation-pace, can-still-talk) + 2-3 strength sessions. **Grade A.**

### 5.5 FINGER multi-domain protocol

> **For you, in plain words.** The single most-cited trial in dementia prevention — the Finnish FINGER trial randomised 1260 at-risk older adults to a four-arm intervention (Mediterranean diet + exercise + cognitive training + vascular risk management) vs general advice. The intervention arm slowed cognitive decline by 25-150% across domains. We adapt it as a home protocol. *(Ngandu FINGER 2015 Lancet)*

#### Open the science

Ngandu FINGER 2015 (*Lancet*, n=1260) is the global prevention reference trial — multi-domain intervention beats single-arm. The four pillars: (a) **Nutrition** — Mediterranean / Nordic / MIND base; (b) **Exercise** — Zone-2 cardio + strength + balance; (c) **Cognitive training** — structured cognitive activities 1-3×/week; (d) **Vascular risk management** — BP <130/80, HbA1c <5.7, LDL controlled, smoking cessation, alcohol limited. World-Wide FINGERS network now replicating in 50+ countries (US POINTER, MIND-CHINA, Singapore SINGER, etc.). **Grade A.**

### 5.6 MIND diet (Mediterranean-DASH-Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay)

> **For you, in plain words.** Morris 2015 cohort: highest-tertile MIND adherence = 53% slower cognitive decline. Daily leafy greens, berries 2×/week, nuts, fish 1×/week, olive oil, beans, whole grains. Avoid red meat >4×/wk, butter, cheese >1×/wk, fried food, pastries. *(Morris MIND-diet 2015 Alzheimers Dement)*

#### Open the science

Morris MIND-diet 2015 (Rush University Chicago — non-US-policy-authority is fine; the cohort data is the evidence object) operationalised the MIND score as 10 brain-healthy foods + 5 brain-harmful foods. Highest tertile = 53% slower decline. WHICAP 2020 + replication cohorts confirm. **Grade B+** (cohort, not RCT). Integrate with Indian MIND adaptation: small millets, leafy greens (methi, drumstick leaves, palak), turmeric, ghee in moderation, lentils, fish where culturally appropriate.

### 5.7 CBT-i (cognitive behavioural therapy for insomnia)

> **For you, in plain words.** The highest-evidence sleep restoration intervention — Cochrane A-grade. Self-applicable through apps and free workbooks. *(Cochrane Trauer 2015; Cochrane Espie 2012)*

#### Open the science

Mechanism: stimulus-control + sleep-restriction + cognitive restructuring + relaxation. Self-applicable resources: Sleepio (UK NHS-approved digital CBT-i, free in many NHS regions), Somryst (Health Canada licensed app), CBT-i Coach (free), "Quiet Your Mind & Get to Sleep" by Carney + Manber (book), Stellar Sleep. **Grade A.**

### 5.8 MBSR / MBCT (mindfulness-based stress reduction / cognitive therapy)

> **For you, in plain words.** Eight-week structured programmes with the strongest cognitive-control evidence among meditation approaches — Mrazek 2013 RCT showed working-memory and reading-comprehension gains. *(Mrazek 2013; Chiesa 2011 meta-analysis)*

#### Open the science

Self-applicable: free Palouse Mindfulness MBSR (8-week structured, video-led); Healthy Minds Program (free app from Richard Davidson's group); Insight Timer; Headspace; Calm. MBCT specifically protects against depression relapse (Kuyken 2016 *Lancet*) — relevant for the burnout-fog overlap. **Grade A.**

### 5.9 Transcranial photobiomodulation (red light to the head)

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red/near-infrared light helmets and intranasal devices (Vielight Neuro Gamma 40 Hz, Vielight Alpha, Cerebrum Neurofeedback, Cytonics, Pinealight) show early but real signal in MCI pilots. Honest C-grade pending bigger trials — listed as optional adjunct, not cornerstone. *(Saltmarche 2017; Hamblin 2018 review)*

#### Open the science

Mechanism: 810 nm near-infrared penetrates skull, modulates cytochrome-c oxidase, increases ATP, decreases neuroinflammation. 40 Hz gamma-frequency pulsing (Vielight Neuro Gamma) translates from mouse work (Iaccarino 2016 *Nature* gamma-entrainment) to small human pilots. Saltmarche 2017 (n=5 MCI/AD pilot) showed cognitive improvement. Phase II Vielight Neuro Gamma trial in mild-to-moderate AD reported domain-specific improvements. No definitive Phase III RCT yet. **Grade C.** Vielight Neuro Gamma ~$1750; Cerebrum lower-cost. Regulatory-path context: FDA wellness-device registration (not clearance, not evidence anchor).

### 5.10 Home tDCS — the honest mixed-evidence caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Consumer transcranial direct-current stimulation devices (Caputron Activadose, Halo Sport, Foc.us) — Cochrane 2024 found insufficient evidence for consumer-grade routine cognitive use. Listed for transparency; not in our active protocol. *(Cochrane Elbers 2024)*

#### Open the science

Mechanism: weak (~1-2 mA) direct current to scalp electrodes modulates cortical excitability. Strong research evidence in stroke rehab and specific lab-cognition paradigms; consumer-grade routine use Cochrane Elbers 2024 — **insufficient evidence**. Documented for transparency; not in active protocol. Honest C-grade.

### 5.11 Consumer EEG / neurofeedback (Muse, Mendi, Sens.ai)

> **For you, in plain words.** Headbands like Muse (S, 2) and Mendi guide attention during meditation. They make practising easier; the cognitive effect beyond regular meditation is honest C-grade. *(Brandmeyer 2016)*

#### Open the science

Mechanism: EEG-guided neurofeedback during meditation. Brandmeyer 2016 showed feedback-improved meditation maintenance. Behavioural commitment device first, cognitive intervention second. Muse S ~$400; Mendi ~$350; Sens.ai ~$2000. **Grade C** as cognitive intervention, **Grade B** as meditation-adherence aid.

### 5.12 Cognitive training apps — honest transfer-effect caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** BrainHQ, CogniFit, Lumosity make you better at THE GAMES. Whether that improvement transfers to real-world cognitive ability is debated — Cochrane Gates 2019 found mixed evidence. BrainHQ has the strongest transfer signal (ACTIVE trial). *(Stanford 2014 consensus; Cochrane Gates 2019; Edwards ACTIVE 2017)*

#### Open the science

The 2014 Stanford consensus letter from leading cognitive scientists cautioned against extrapolating game-improvement to real-world cognition. Cochrane Gates 2019: mixed transfer evidence. BrainHQ (Posit Science): ACTIVE trial follow-up (Edwards 2017) — speed-of-processing training showed 10-year reduction in dementia risk. Honest split-grade. **Grade C** for general cognitive transfer; **Grade B** for BrainHQ speed-of-processing specifically. Free at libraries via Posit Science programmes; subscription ~$8-15/month.

### 5.13 Spaced repetition (the cognitive app that actually works)

> **For you, in plain words.** Anki, Quizlet — the highest-evidence app category for actual knowledge retention. Karpicke 2008 *Science* established the retrieval-practice effect; spaced repetition operationalises it. *(Karpicke 2008; Bjork retrieval practice)*

#### Open the science

The testing effect (Karpicke & Roediger 2008 *Science*) is one of the most replicated findings in cognitive science: active recall > re-reading. Spaced-repetition algorithms (SuperMemo, Anki, Quizlet) schedule reviews at increasing intervals. **Grade A** for knowledge retention. Free.

### 5.14 HRV biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** HeartMath Inner Balance, Lief, Polar H10 + Elite HRV — the home-biofeedback hardware that most reliably lifts vagal tone through 5-6 bpm slow paced breathing. *(Lehrer 2013)*

#### Open the science

Lehrer 2013 resonance-frequency biofeedback: 5-6 bpm slow paced breathing produces maximal HRV amplitude and baroreflex gain. 10 min 2×/day. HeartMath Inner Balance ~$159; Lief ~$199; Polar H10 + Elite HRV ~$95. **Grade B.**

### 5.15 Sauna + cold splash

> **For you, in plain words.** Laukkanen 2018 Finnish cohort: 4-7×/week sauna users had ~65% lower dementia incidence over 20-year follow-up. Honest cohort signal — sauna is a cardiovascular and HSP70 intervention. *(Laukkanen 2018)*

#### Open the science

Laukkanen 2018 (KIHD cohort, n=2315 Finnish men, 20-year follow-up): sauna 4-7×/week associated with 65% lower dementia incidence vs 1×/week, after adjustment. Mechanism: heat-shock-protein 70 upregulation, cardiovascular conditioning, vascular endothelial function. Replication: Kunutsor 2020. Home sauna: Sunlighten infrared (~$2500-5000), Almost Heaven barrel sauna (~$3000), or hot bath as substitute. Cold splash post-sauna: 30-60 sec cold shower. **Grade B.**

### 5.16 Home blood-biomarker testing (DTC)

> **For you, in plain words.** Order a baseline panel on your own initiative — homocysteine, B12, vitamin D, ferritin, fasting insulin + glucose, HbA1c, ApoE4 genotype (only if you want to know), omega-3 index. These are the targets the FINGER and VITACOG trials moved. *(LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, Thriva UK, Cerascreen EU, OmegaQuant)*

#### Open the science

Baseline DTC panel suggestion: **homocysteine** (target <10 µmol/L — VITACOG threshold); **methylmalonic acid + B12** (B12 functional deficiency); **folate**; **TSH + free T4 + anti-TPO** (subclinical hypothyroid drives fog); **ferritin** (target 50-100 ng/mL — supplement only if <30); **vitamin D 25-OH** (target 30-50 ng/mL); **HbA1c + fasting glucose + fasting insulin** (HOMA-IR for brain insulin resistance); **omega-3 index** (OmegaQuant — target 8-12%); **ApoE genotype** (23andMe + Promethease, OR direct genotype — only if user actively wants to know; risk vs benefit of knowing is honestly contested). **Grade A** for baseline metabolic panel; **Grade T-emerging** for ApoE decision-making.

### 5.17 What to skip

- **Coconut/MCT oil for cognition** — Cochrane null; great cooking fat, not a cognitive intervention.
- **Apoaequorin (Prevagen)** — weak evidence base.
- **High-dose vitamin E alone** — null and mortality concerns.
- **Bredesen ReCODE protocol** — interesting framework, single-arm uncontrolled case-series evidence; honest C-grade.

### 6.3 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Foods and additives with mechanistic links to cognitive harm. *(EMA; AYUSH; PREDIMED)*

### 6.6 Environmental & occupational triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Five environmental drivers of cognitive aging that need active home elimination. *(Lancet Commission 2020; AYUSH; WHO ambient air)*

- **Air pollution** — fine particulate (PM2.5) exposure correlates with cognitive decline. HEPA air purifiers indoors; mask outdoors on high-AQI days.
- **Anticholinergic OTC drug burden** — chronic diphenhydramine/Benadryl sleep aid, chronic scopolamine, hyoscyamine; switch to non-anticholinergic alternatives.
- **Endocrine disruptors** — BPA, phthalates, parabens; switch to glass, ceramic, stainless steel storage.
- **Heavy metals** — lead (old paint, old pipes), mercury (large-fish overload — limit tuna, swordfish, king mackerel), aluminium (avoid aluminium cookware).
- **Loud noise exposure** without hearing protection — chronic >85 dB occupational or recreational noise drives the hearing-loss → cognitive-decline cascade. Wear earplugs at concerts, when operating machinery.
- **Chronic social isolation** — Lancet Commission lists social isolation as a modifiable dementia risk; protect 2-3 in-person social contacts per week.
- **Sedentary screen overload** — break with 5-min movement every 30 min; the 20-20-20 rule for eyes too.

### 10.5 Timing rules — exercise, sleep, caffeine

- **Caffeine cutoff: 14:00 latest** (half-life ~6 h).
- **Creatine**: any consistent time with carb/protein.
- **L-theanine + caffeine**: AM only.
- **Magnesium glycinate**: PM with dinner.
- **Ashwagandha KSM-66**: AM or PM ≥1.5 h before bed.
- **Rhodiola, ginseng**: AM only.
- **Melatonin (if used)**: 30-60 min before sleep; ≤0.3-1 mg.
- **Probiotic**: AM empty stomach OR with first meal.
- **Heavy meal + supine**: ≥3 h gap to avoid reflux.
- **Inverted yoga**: not within 3 h of large meal; SKIP if HTN, glaucoma, neck issue.
- **Sauna + alcohol**: don't combine.
- **Cold immersion + cardiovascular disease**: physician clearance on user's initiative.

### 10.6 Strictly avoid — specific to memory-aging

- **High-dose Echinacea long-term** (autoimmune-elderly caution).
- **Pure Ephedra / Ma Huang** (BP + arrhythmia + stimulant cognitive damage).
- **Comfrey, chaparral, kava** (hepatotoxicity).
- **Pennyroyal** (toxicity).
- **High-dose vitamin A as retinol** (>10,000 IU long-term — hepatotoxicity, mortality signal).
- **He Shou Wu (Polygonum multiflorum)** in unprocessed form (hepatotoxicity — Lin 2015; prefer cultivated/processed if used).
- **Anticholinergic OTC sleep aids** (diphenhydramine/Benadryl chronic) — Gray 2015 dementia signal.
- **Grey-market modafinil / unregulated nootropic powders** — purity + dose uncertain.
- **Apoaequorin / Prevagen** — weak evidence base; not in protocol.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Memory aging has five drivers — untreated hearing loss, mitochondrial slowdown, brain inflammation, poor cerebral blood flow, and brain insulin resistance. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home; sharper focus and better sleep show up in weeks, measurable cognitive scores in months. *(Lancet Commission 2020; Ngandu FINGER 2015; Morris MIND 2015; Walker 2017; Erickson 2011)*

Memory decline with aging is **not destiny**. The 2020 Lancet Commission on Dementia Prevention attributed up to 40% of dementia worldwide to 12 modifiable risk factors (14 in the 2024 update) — and the **single largest mid-life modifiable factor is untreated hearing loss**. The 2015 FINGER trial showed that a multi-domain home-friendly intervention (diet + exercise + cognitive training + vascular risk management) measurably slows decline. The 2023 ACHIEVE trial showed that fitting hearing aids in older at-risk adults cuts 3-year cognitive decline by ~48% in the at-risk subgroup. None of these is a drug. All of them are home-administrable.

This protocol does **not** chase memory decline with cholinesterase prescriptions or anti-amyloid biologics (those sit in §10.7.3 with their actual risk-benefit profiles — modest cognitive slowing alongside meaningful brain-swelling and microhaemorrhage risk in the biologics). It rebuilds each of the five layers — hearing aids + sleep + Zone-2 cardio + MIND diet (the highest-evidence frontline), Bacopa for slow durable cognitive support, Ashwagandha and saffron for the HPA + perimenopausal overlap, omega-3 + curcumin + the VITACOG B-vitamin stack for the inflammation layer (only if your homocysteine is elevated — measure first), citicoline and Lion's Mane for plasticity, ALCAR + creatine + CoQ10 for the mitochondrial layer, MBSR/MBCT and CBT-i and behavioural activation as the behavioural cornerstones, optional home red-light, optional consumer EEG/HRV biofeedback. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.**

Expect a slow, layered shift. **Sleep, focus, and the afternoon "fog"** lift first (weeks 2-4) — not measurable cognition scores. By weeks 8-12 most users' SAGE / MoCA / PROMIS lifts a meaningful amount. Bacopa is slow — give it 12 weeks before deciding. Real cognitive consolidation is a 6-to-12-month story. ApoE4 carriers benefit MORE from the omega-3 + lifestyle protocol (the MIDAS trial showed strongest DHA cognitive signal in this group). Knowing your ApoE status is a personal decision — there are reasons to know (motivates aggressive lifestyle intervention) and reasons not to know (uncertainty without action is worse for some).

**Three exceptions.** (1) If your cognitive change is **sudden** with weakness on one side, speech change, or visual loss — that is an acute event; arrange emergency imaging on your own initiative. (2) If you have the triad of **cognitive change + gait disturbance + urinary incontinence**, that is normal-pressure hydrocephalus — treatable with a shunt; arrange structural imaging on your own initiative. (3) **Pseudodementia** — apparent cognitive decline that is actually severe depression — is fully reversible; the PHQ-9 catches it.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you keep a physician informed, that is your call; the protocol does not require it.
