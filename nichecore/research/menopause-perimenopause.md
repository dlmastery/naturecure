## 1. Executive Summary

Perimenopause and menopause are conventionally siloed as an "oestrogen-deficiency" diagnosis treated with menopausal hormone therapy (oestradiol patch/gel + micronised progesterone, or oral combined), SSRI/SNRI for vasomotor symptoms (paroxetine), gabapentin, clonidine, or vaginal oestrogen for genitourinary syndrome of menopause (GSM). The integrative reframing is different: the menopausal transition is the **multi-system recalibration of a four-node network** — (i) **ovarian senescence + erratic then declining oestradiol/progesterone** with FSH/LH rises driving vasomotor instability (hot flashes, night sweats) via hypothalamic kisspeptin/neurokinin-B/dynorphin (KNDy) neuron dysregulation; (ii) **HPA-axis + cortisol-DHEA imbalance** amplifying mood, sleep and cognitive symptoms (DHEA falls ~80% between age 25 and 75; adrenal becomes the post-menopause backstop for steroid precursors); (iii) **gut-oestrobolome dysbiosis** (β-glucuronidase microbial activity controls enterohepatic oestrogen recycling — depleted oestrobolome accelerates total oestrogen loss); and (iv) **bone-cardiometabolic-cognitive vulnerability** (oestrogen loss → osteoclast unrestrained → bone loss 2–5%/yr early post-menopause; LDL/Lp(a) rise; visceral adiposity; insulin resistance; vascular endothelial dysfunction; hippocampal oestrogen-receptor decline). **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously dampens KNDy hyperactivity (black cohosh, red clover, Siberian rhubarb ERr 731, sage, soy isoflavones), supports HPA-adrenal (Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Yashtimadhu, Yoga Nidra), restores oestrobolome (sprouted millets, fermented foods, lignans, *Akkermansia*-supportive polyphenols), and reinforces bone-cardio-cognitive resilience (D3+K2 + magnesium + calcium-from-food, omega-3, strength training, weight-bearing exercise, evening primrose oil) addresses the upstream drivers — not the vasomotor or mood endpoint in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Ovarian senescence + KNDy-mediated vasomotor instability.** As ovarian follicle reserve falls, oestradiol becomes erratic (early peri), then declines persistently (late peri → post-menopause). FSH and LH rise (FSH >25 mIU/mL is the conventional menopause marker). In the arcuate nucleus, kisspeptin / neurokinin-B / dynorphin (KNDy) neurons normally restrained by oestradiol become hyperactive, projecting to the median preoptic nucleus to dysregulate thermoregulation — the molecular basis of hot flashes and night sweats. Key actors: oestrogen receptors α and β, KISS1, TAC3 (neurokinin-B), KOR (dynorphin receptor), FSH, LH, AMH (low to undetectable), inhibin-B. Targeted by **black cohosh (*Actaea racemosa* / Cimicifuga, EMA HMPC monograph — hepatic caution), red clover (Promensil 40 mg isoflavones, EMA HMPC), Siberian rhubarb root extract ERr 731 (TGA + Health Canada NHP), sage (*Salvia officinalis*, EMA HMPC for hot flashes), soy isoflavones (genistein + daidzein, fermented sources preferred), Shatavari, Ashokarishta, Liu Wei Di Huang Wan + Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan**. **Synthesis:** Modulating KNDy is the upstream brake on vasomotor symptoms.

**2.2 HPA-axis + cortisol-DHEA imbalance.** Ovarian steroid loss shifts steroidogenesis to the adrenals — DHEA-S becomes the post-menopause precursor pool. But adrenal DHEA peaks at 25 and falls 80% by 75, so menopause coincides with adrenal "thinning". Chronic stress + insufficient sleep + caffeine excess raise cortisol, suppress DHEA further, drive central adiposity, insulin resistance, mood disturbance, hippocampal atrophy. Key actors: CRH, ACTH, cortisol, DHEA/DHEA-S, glucocorticoid receptors, BDNF. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol −23–28% in RCT), Shatavari, Yashtimadhu (*Glycyrrhiza glabra*, cautious — see BP), Bacopa, Holy Basil, Maca (*Lepidium meyenii*), Yoga Nidra, MBSR, pranayama**. **Synthesis:** Without HPA-adrenal restoration, the other three nodes regress under life stress.

**2.3 Gut oestrobolome dysbiosis + lignan + SCFA depletion.** The oestrobolome — gut microbial genes encoding β-glucuronidase — controls enterohepatic recirculation of oestrogens. Healthy oestrobolome maintains some circulating oestrogen even after ovarian decline; dysbiotic oestrobolome accelerates total oestrogen clearance. Menopausal cohorts show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Bifidobacterium*; expanded *Bacteroides*. Lignan-converting bacteria (Eggerthella, Lactonifactor) convert dietary lignans (flax, sesame, whole grains) to enterolactone — a phyto-oestrogen modulator. Targeted by **flax seeds (15–20 g ground daily), sprouted millets, fermented foods, pomegranate, cranberry, green tea polyphenols, multi-strain probiotic 30–50 B CFU, L-glutamine, zinc-carnosine**. **Synthesis:** Restoring oestrobolome extends endogenous oestrogen circulation.

**2.4 Bone-cardiometabolic-cognitive vulnerability.** Oestrogen loss removes the brake on osteoclast-mediated bone resorption — bone loss 2–5%/yr in the first 5 years post-menopause. Endothelial nitric-oxide drops → vascular stiffness. LDL and Lp(a) rise; HDL-protective effect wanes. Visceral adipose accumulates. Hippocampal oestrogen receptors decline → memory and word-finding lapses ("brain fog"). Key actors: RANKL/OPG, osteoclasts, osteoblasts, oestrogen-receptor β in bone, ApoB, Lp(a), endothelial NOS, BDNF, oestrogen receptors in hippocampus. Targeted by **vitamin D3 4000–6000 IU + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 µg + magnesium glycinate 300–400 mg + calcium-from-food 800–1000 mg/day + boron 3 mg + omega-3 2 g, strength training 2–3×/wk + weight-bearing cardio 3×/wk + balance work, omega-3 EPA+DHA, Bacopa, Lion's Mane (*Hericium erinaceus*), evening primrose oil (GLA)**. **Synthesis:** Without bone-cardio-cognitive reinforcement, the long-tail disease burden compounds across decades.
