## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What menopause actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Perimenopause is not "estrogen deficiency" — it is the body's whole-system recalibration over 5–10 years. Naming the four parallel changes lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(STRAW+10 staging; KNDy hypothesis)*

### 1.2 The four changes — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: 15–20 g freshly-ground flax + 15 g sesame in food + sage tea after dinner. This week: vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 100 µg + magnesium glycinate 300 mg + omega-3 2 g + start 3×/week strength training. If hot flashes are dominant: add Siberian rhubarb ERr 731 (Estrovera) 4 mg AM. *(EMA HMPC sage 2017; EMA Black cohosh 2018; ERr 731 Heger 2006)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Perimenopause and menopause are conventionally siloed as an "oestrogen-deficiency" diagnosis treated with menopausal hormone therapy (oestradiol patch/gel + micronised progesterone), SSRI/SNRI for vasomotor (paroxetine), gabapentin, clonidine, or vaginal oestrogen for GSM. The integrative reframing is different: the menopausal transition is the **multi-system recalibration of a four-node network** — (i) **ovarian senescence + erratic-then-declining oestradiol/progesterone** driving vasomotor instability via hypothalamic kisspeptin/neurokinin-B/dynorphin (KNDy) neuron dysregulation; (ii) **HPA-axis + cortisol-DHEA imbalance** amplifying mood, sleep and cognitive symptoms; (iii) **gut-oestrobolome dysbiosis** controlling enterohepatic oestrogen recycling; and (iv) **bone-cardiometabolic-cognitive vulnerability** (osteoclast unrestrained → bone loss 2–5%/yr early post-menopause; LDL/Lp(a) rise; visceral adiposity; hippocampal oestrogen-receptor decline). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously dampens KNDy hyperactivity, supports HPA-adrenal, restores oestrobolome, and reinforces bone-cardio-cognitive resilience addresses the upstream drivers — not the vasomotor or mood endpoint in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four changes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four parallel changes drive every menopause symptom — KNDy neurons in the brain go hyperactive (hot flashes), the adrenals get overloaded (mood, sleep, fatigue), gut bacteria stop recycling oestrogen, and bone-heart-brain lose oestrogen's protective signal. *(Eghbali 2019 KNDy review; Lobo 2014; Baker 2017 oestrobolome)*

### 2.2 KNDy → hot flashes

> **For you, in plain words.** As ovarian oestrogen drops, brainstem KNDy neurons normally held in check by oestrogen go hyperactive — dysregulating thermoregulation. That's what hot flashes and night sweats actually are. Siberian rhubarb ERr 731, black cohosh, sage and red clover quiet them. *(Heger 2006 ERr 731 RCT; Bommer 2011 sage; EMA black cohosh 2018)*

**Quick start.** Siberian rhubarb ERr 731 (Estrovera) 4 mg AM + sage tea 2 cups + black cohosh (Remifemin) 40 mg twice daily if Estrovera alone isn't enough.

#### Open the science

As ovarian follicle reserve falls, oestradiol becomes erratic (early peri), then declines persistently (late peri → post-menopause). FSH and LH rise (FSH >25 mIU/mL is the conventional menopause marker). In the arcuate nucleus, kisspeptin / neurokinin-B / dynorphin (KNDy) neurons normally restrained by oestradiol become hyperactive, projecting to the median preoptic nucleus to dysregulate thermoregulation — the molecular basis of hot flashes and night sweats. Key actors: oestrogen receptors α and β, KISS1, TAC3 (neurokinin-B), KOR (dynorphin receptor), FSH, LH, AMH (low to undetectable), inhibin-B. Targeted by **black cohosh (Cimicifuga racemosa, EMA HMPC monograph — hepatic caution); red clover (Promensil 40 mg isoflavones, EMA HMPC); Siberian rhubarb ERr 731 (Estrovera, Health Canada NHP); sage (Salvia officinalis, EMA HMPC for hot flashes); soy isoflavones (effect modulated by equol-producer phenotype); Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan + Liu Wei Di Huang Wan + Er Xian Tang; Shatavari; Ashokarishta**. **Synthesis.** Modulating KNDy is the upstream brake on vasomotor symptoms.

### 2.3 Adrenal HPA imbalance

> **For you, in plain words.** When the ovaries quiet down, the adrenal glands become the backstop for hormone precursors — but adrenal DHEA peaks at 25 and falls 80% by 75. Chronic stress + bad sleep + caffeine excess steal what little is left. Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Holy Basil and Yoga Nidra rebuild the floor. *(Choudhary 2017 Ashwagandha-cortisol RCT; AYUSH Shatavari monograph)*

**Quick start.** Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM with dinner + Yoga Nidra 20–30 min at bedtime + Shatavari powder 5 g in warm milk + caffeine cut-off 14:00.

#### Open the science

Ovarian steroid loss shifts steroidogenesis to the adrenals — DHEA-S becomes the post-menopause precursor pool. But adrenal DHEA peaks at 25 and falls 80% by 75, so menopause coincides with adrenal "thinning". Chronic stress + insufficient sleep + caffeine excess raise cortisol, suppress DHEA further, drive central adiposity, insulin resistance, mood disturbance, hippocampal atrophy. Key actors: CRH, ACTH, cortisol, DHEA/DHEA-S, glucocorticoid receptors, BDNF. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol −23–28% across RCTs), Shatavari, Yashtimadhu (cautious — see BP), Bacopa, Holy Basil, Maca, Yoga Nidra, MBSR, pranayama**. **Synthesis.** Without HPA-adrenal restoration, the other three nodes regress under life stress.

### 2.4 Oestrobolome — gut estrogen recycling

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria control whether oestrogens get recycled back into your blood or eliminated. A healthy oestrobolome extends the oestrogen your body still has. Sprouted millets, fermented foods, flax lignans and pomegranate rebuild it. *(Baker 2017 oestrobolome; Kwa 2016 microbiome-oestrogen)*

**Quick start.** Sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets 60–80 g/day + ground flax 15–20 g + sesame 15–20 g + fermented food 100–150 g + pomegranate ¼ cup + multi-strain probiotic 30–50 B CFU AM.

#### Open the science

The oestrobolome — gut microbial genes encoding β-glucuronidase — controls enterohepatic recirculation of oestrogens. Healthy oestrobolome maintains some circulating oestrogen even after ovarian decline; dysbiotic oestrobolome accelerates total oestrogen clearance. Menopausal cohorts show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Bifidobacterium*; expanded *Bacteroides*. Lignan-converting bacteria (Eggerthella, Lactonifactor) convert dietary lignans to enterolactone — a phyto-oestrogen modulator. Targeted by **flax seeds (15–20 g ground daily), sprouted millets, fermented foods, pomegranate, cranberry, green tea polyphenols, multi-strain probiotic, L-glutamine, zinc-carnosine**. **Synthesis.** Restoring oestrobolome extends endogenous oestrogen circulation.

### 2.5 Bone, heart and brain vulnerability

> **For you, in plain words.** Oestrogen loss removes the brake on osteoclasts (bone loss 2–5%/yr in the first 5 years), the brake on LDL (cardio risk rises), and the support to hippocampal memory (brain-fog and word-finding lapses). Vitamin D + K2 + Mg + strength training + omega-3 are the protective stack. *(Cochrane bone-D meta-analysis; Cochrane omega-3 cardio; BJSM resistance-training reviews)*

**Quick start.** Vitamin D3 4000–6000 IU + K2 100–200 µg + magnesium glycinate 300–400 mg + omega-3 EPA+DHA 2 g + strength training 2–3×/week + Zone 2 cardio 2–3×/week.

#### Open the science

Oestrogen loss removes the brake on osteoclast-mediated bone resorption — bone loss 2–5%/yr in the first 5 years post-menopause. Endothelial nitric-oxide drops → vascular stiffness. LDL and Lp(a) rise; HDL-protective effect wanes. Visceral adipose accumulates. Hippocampal oestrogen receptors decline → memory and word-finding lapses. Key actors: RANKL/OPG, osteoclasts, osteoblasts, ER-β in bone, ApoB, Lp(a), eNOS, BDNF, ER in hippocampus. Targeted by **vitamin D3 + K2 (MK-7) + magnesium glycinate + calcium-from-food + boron + omega-3 + strength training + weight-bearing cardio + balance work + Bacopa + Lion's Mane + evening primrose oil**. **Synthesis.** Without bone-cardio-cognitive reinforcement, the long-tail disease burden compounds across decades.

#### Open the science

The HRT debate is genuinely active. The WHI 2002 trial scared most women off MHT because of the small increase in breast cancer and CV events in the combined arm — but that trial enrolled women on average 12 years past menopause and used conjugated equine estrogens + medroxyprogesterone acetate (synthetic, not bioidentical). The KEEPS trial 2014 (early-menopause, transdermal estradiol + micronised progesterone, <3 yr from final menses) found cognitive, mood and bone benefit with neutral CV. The KEEPS Continuation 2024 finding (NEJM) showed sustained safety. The protocol's stance: HRT/MHT is a legitimate option a woman can pursue at her own initiative in the early-menopause window — but the home path of phytoestrogens (ERr 731, black cohosh, red clover, soy isoflavones, sage), HPA support (Ashwagandha, Shatavari), bone reinforcement (D3+K2+Mg+Ca-food+strength training), and behavioural therapy (MBSR — Carmody 2011, CBT — Hunter MENOS) covers most users without needing the prescription.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Menopause is four things happening together — brain KNDy neurons hyperactive (hot flashes), adrenals overloaded (mood/sleep), gut bacteria stop recycling oestrogen, bone-heart-brain lose oestrogen's protection. Menopause is not "estrogen deficiency" — it is the body's whole-system recalibration over 5–10 years. Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) tiny brain cells called KNDy neurons that the ovaries used to keep quiet start hyperfiring — that's what hot flashes and night sweats actually are; (2) your adrenal glands become the backstop for hormone precursors, but adrenal DHEA falls 80% from age 25 to 75, so stress + bad sleep + caffeine overload them; (3) your gut bacteria control whether oestrogens get recycled or eliminated, and dysbiotic flora speeds elimination; (4) without oestrogen's protective signal, osteoclasts unrestrained drive bone loss 2–5%/year, LDL rises, and the hippocampus's oestrogen receptors decline causing brain fog.
