> **CRITICAL SAFETY — READ THIS FIRST.** Sudden hearing loss in one (or both) ears is an AUDIOLOGY EMERGENCY. The 72-hour window for high-dose oral corticosteroids and/or intratympanic dexamethasone is the difference between hearing recovery and permanent loss (Stachler 2012 + Plontke 2016). If you have sudden hearing loss + can't get an audiology / ENT appointment within 72 hours, GO TO ER and ask for an audiogram + Rx prednisone. The protocol below is a recovery ADJUNCT after the steroid window — never a substitute.
>
> **For you, in plain words.** Sudden Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SSHL) is a sudden, usually one-sided drop in hearing — over hours or noticed on waking — that comes from the inner ear or auditory nerve, not the ear canal or eardrum. It is **an emergency.** Spontaneous recovery rates run ~30-65% within two weeks if untreated (Mattox 1977); a substantial fraction become permanent if the 72-hour steroid window is missed. **The dominant intervention is high-dose oral prednisone (1 mg/kg, up to 60-80 mg, × 7-14 days then taper) within 72 hours of onset, or intratympanic dexamethasone if oral is contraindicated or as salvage** (Stachler 2012 systematic guidance; Wilson 1980 trial; Plontke 2016; Battaglia 2008 IT-DEX; Crane 2015 meta — A-grade equivalent salvage). ## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What SSHL actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** SSHL = a hearing drop of 30 dB or more across at least three contiguous audiometric frequencies, developing over 72 hours or less. The drop is **sensorineural** (inner ear / cochlea / auditory nerve / central pathway) — NOT conductive (ear canal blockage / fluid / wax / eardrum issue). Most cases (~90%) are idiopathic — no identified cause — even after full workup. *(Stachler 2012 systematic guidance; Mattox 1977 natural history)*

### 1.2 The five levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** TONIGHT: (1) call audiology / ENT — ask for "urgent SSHL same-day audiogram + steroid evaluation"; if no slot in 72 hours go to ER and ask for prednisone Rx + audiology referral. (2) STOP any ototoxic medications you may be on (aminoglycoside antibiotics — gentamicin, tobramycin; loop diuretics — furosemide; high-dose NSAIDs; high-dose aspirin; cisplatin chemotherapy; macrolides — azithromycin in some scenarios — discuss with prescribing clinician, do NOT abruptly stop life-saving meds without that conversation). (3) Run a free Mimi or hearWHO test on your phone — this is your baseline. (4) Home BP cuff + home blood glucose check — vascular + metabolic drivers are high-leverage modifiable risks. THIS WEEK: start omega-3, magnesium, zinc, vitamin C, alpha-lipoic acid; tinnitus-management masking apps if residual tinnitus has started (myNoise, Tinnitracks); CBT app (Woebot, MoodGym) — depression + anxiety + isolation hit hard. THIS MONTH: MRI of internal auditory canal scheduled (mandatory workup); follow-up audiogram at 1-2 weeks + 4 weeks + 3 months; communication strategies family-counselling. *(Stachler 2012; Plontke 2016; Battaglia 2008)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

SSHL is usually framed as "sudden hearing loss — try steroids and hope." A more honest reframe: SSHL is **the convergence of five intersecting systems** that must be addressed in parallel — (i) **the 72-hour neuroprotective window** (steroids reduce cochlear inflammation; the dominant outcome lever); (ii) **vascular and metabolic risk modulation** (diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, smoking are over-represented in SSHL cohorts and modifiable); (iii) **non-Bell's-mimic exclusions** (acoustic neuroma 10%, MS, pontine stroke, Lyme, syphilis, AIED — MRI + workup non-substitutable); (iv) **residual-symptom management** (tinnitus 50%+ overlap, vestibular overlap, depression + anxiety + social isolation are the durable outcomes that determine quality of life if hearing is not fully recovered); (v) **adaptation if permanent** (hearing aids, cochlear implant evaluation if severe-profound, communication strategies, family counselling, structured speech-rehabilitation). **Synthesis.** Steroid window + IT-DEX salvage + MRI + vascular/metabolic control + tinnitus + CBT + adaptation — five systems running in parallel for 12 months.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five systems — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five systems govern SSHL recovery — cochlear inflammation in the acute window, vascular + metabolic substrate, structural-pathology rule-outs (acoustic neuroma + MS + stroke + AIED), residual-symptom layer (tinnitus + vestibular + depression), and adaptive layer if hearing doesn't fully return. Naming them lets every Rx, drop, supplement and app aim at the right system. *(Stachler 2012; Saunders 1995; Andersson 2009 hearing-loss-depression)*

### 2.2 Cochlear inflammation — the 72-hour window

> **For you, in plain words.** Whatever the trigger — virus, vascular, autoimmune, idiopathic — the cochlea inflames; hair cells and stria vascularis come under metabolic stress; if inflammation isn't reduced quickly, cells die and hearing loss becomes permanent. High-dose steroids in the first 72 hours are the dominant intervention. *(Wilson 1980 first RCT; Stachler 2012; Plontke 2016)*

**The deeper layer.** Wilson 1980 (first prednisone RCT, n=67) demonstrated benefit vs control. Stachler 2012 systematic guidance synthesized accumulated evidence: oral prednisone 1 mg/kg/d (up to 60-80 mg) × 7-14 d then taper, started <72 hr from onset, has the strongest evidence; recovery rates ~50-80% with treatment vs ~30-65% spontaneous untreated (Mattox 1977). Battaglia 2008 (n=51 RCT) + Plontke 2016 + Crane 2015 meta-analysis demonstrated **intratympanic dexamethasone (IT-DEX) 4-10 mg/mL × 3-4 injections at 3-7 day intervals as primary or salvage** is non-inferior to oral steroids — useful for diabetic patients, glaucoma, peptic ulcer, psychiatric concerns where oral steroids contraindicated. Arastou 2013 small signal trend toward better outcomes with combined oral + IT-DEX. Antiviral therapy questioned per Stachler 2012 — no clear benefit. Pentoxifylline + plasma expansion: NULL per modern reviews. The window matters: recovery rates fall sharply after 72 hours, plateau by 7-14 days, and beyond that the rescue yield drops markedly (still try, but lower). **Synthesis.** The single largest outcome lever is whether the reader gets to audiology or ER and starts prednisone within 72 hours.

### 2.3 Vascular and metabolic substrate

> **For you, in plain words.** Diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, smoking, and cardiovascular disease are all over-represented in SSHL cohorts — the cochlea has a single end-artery blood supply and is vulnerable to microvascular insult. Tight glucose + BP + lipid control + smoking cessation is both treatment and prevention of recurrence. *(Lin 2010 diabetes + SSHL; Aimoni 2010 cardiovascular + SSHL)*

**The deeper layer.** The cochlea is supplied by the labyrinthine artery (single end-artery, branch of AICA — anterior inferior cerebellar artery), with minimal collateral circulation. Microvascular insult — diabetic microangiopathy, hypertensive vasculopathy, hyperviscosity, embolic — is one of the leading mechanistic hypotheses for SSHL. Lin 2010 (n=10K Taiwan cohort) — T2D 1.5-2× SSHL risk. Aimoni 2010 — cardiovascular disease + lipid abnormalities over-represented. Sickle cell crisis is a recognised vascular SSHL trigger. The home protocol layer: home BP cuff daily AM, home BG / CGM if diabetic / prediabetic, lipid panel periodically, smoking cessation, aerobic exercise, Mediterranean food pattern. **Synthesis.** Vascular + metabolic control is both acute-recovery support and recurrence prevention — fully OTC, fully home, fully sovereign.

### 2.4 Structural-pathology rule-outs

> **For you, in plain words.** ~10% of SSHL is the first symptom of a vestibular schwannoma (a slow benign tumour on the auditory-vestibular nerve). A small fraction is MS, pontine stroke, AIED (autoimmune inner ear disease), Lyme, syphilis, Cogan syndrome. **MRI of the internal auditory canal (IAC) is mandatory at some point in the workup — it is not optional.** *(Saunders 1995 ~10% acoustic neuroma rate; Stachler 2012)*

**The deeper layer.** Saunders 1995 + multiple subsequent series confirm ~10% SSHL turn out to have vestibular schwannoma — slow benign tumour arising from Schwann cells of the vestibular nerve. Audiometric clues: asymmetric SNHL (worse high-frequency on affected side), word recognition disproportionate to PTA, tinnitus + balance issues. MS — facial weakness + CNS signs + optic neuritis history. Pontine stroke — vascular risk + brainstem signs + CT/MRI urgent. AIED (autoimmune inner ear disease, Cogan syndrome) — bilateral progressive SNHL + vertigo + eye inflammation; steroid-responsive. Lyme — endemic regions, tick exposure, EM rash. Syphilis — endemic regions, serology. **Synthesis.** MRI of IAC + neurological exam + audiometry pattern analysis distinguish idiopathic SSHL (~90%) from the ~10% with identifiable structural pathology — and the rule-outs change the treatment pathway.

### 2.5 Residual-symptom layer (tinnitus + vestibular + depression)

> **For you, in plain words.** Even with a "good outcome" SSHL, 50%+ end up with permanent residual tinnitus, ~30% with some vestibular dysfunction, and a substantial fraction with clinically significant depression + anxiety + social withdrawal. **These residual symptoms are NOT optional decorations — they determine quality of life as much as the hearing recovery itself.** *(Mosnier 2011 SSHL outcomes; Andersson 2009 hearing-loss-depression; Hesser 2012)*

**The deeper layer.** Mosnier 2011 — long-term SSHL outcomes show high residual tinnitus prevalence even in audiometric "recoverers." Andersson 2009 + Hesser 2012 — internet-based CBT for hearing-loss-related distress B-grade. Sereda 2019 — MBSR for residual tinnitus B-grade. The clinical implication: a recovery protocol that addresses only the audiogram and skips the tinnitus + depression layers under-treats the actual person. Masking apps (myNoise, Tinnitracks notched-music if residual hearing permits) — see tinnitus-support sister dossier for full toolkit. CBT for hearing-loss-related depression-anxiety — Woebot, MoodGym, internet-CBT-i (Sleepio, Somryst). Vestibular rehab if vertigo overlap — see vertigo-dizziness sister dossier. **Synthesis.** Residual-symptom management runs parallel to acute steroid window, not after — start tinnitus management in week 1, CBT in week 2, vestibular rehab if needed by week 4.

### 2.6 Adaptive layer if permanent

> **For you, in plain words.** If hearing doesn't fully return, the protocol shifts from "rescue" to "adapt" — consumer hearing earbuds (Apple AirPods Pro hearing-aid mode, OTC hearing aids cleared by FDA 2022 as regulatory-path context), professional hearing aids fitting, cochlear implant evaluation for severe-profound loss, communication strategies, family counselling, auditory training apps, hearing loops, captioning + speech-to-text apps. *(Apple AirPods FDA OTC hearing-aid clearance 2022 regulatory-path context; cochlear implant outcomes lit)*

**The deeper layer.** Severity stratification: mild residual loss (25-40 dB) → hearing aids, OTC or fitted; moderate (40-70 dB) → fitted hearing aids; severe (70-90 dB) → fitted hearing aids ± CROS/BiCROS for asymmetric loss; profound (>90 dB) → cochlear implant evaluation (especially if bilateral). The OTC + consumer-tech layer: Apple AirPods Pro hearing-aid mode (FDA OTC hearing-aid clearance 2022 — regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor); Bose Hear consumer-emerging; Phonak Lyric extended-wear (FDA-cleared 2014 — regulatory context); Jabra Enhance, Eargo, Lexie. Auditory training apps — Brain HQ Hearing Module. Hearing loops + induction systems (T-coils). Captioning apps — Otter, Live Transcribe, Ava. **Synthesis.** Adaptation is a separable system from rescue; if month 3 audiogram shows persistent loss, the protocol pivots cleanly into adaptation without losing the gains from steroid + supplement + CBT layers.

### 3.4 The diet base layer

> **For you, in plain words.** No diet replaces the steroid window. The food pattern that supports cochlear recovery is the same that supports vascular + metabolic health: Mediterranean / anti-inflammatory + omega-3-rich fish + leafy greens + colourful vegetables + nuts + olive oil. **Reduce sodium** (vascular pressure on the cochlear end-artery). **Reduce alcohol** (multiple ototoxic mechanisms + sleep). **Eliminate liquid sugar.** *(PREDIMED 2013; Curhan 2014 omega-3 + hearing)*

**Quick start.** Week 1: cut liquid sugar, ultra-processed foods, deep-fried foods, excess sodium. Add 3 servings/week oily fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines, anchovies). Daily leafy greens + nuts + olive oil + colourful vegetables. Adequate hydration. Alcohol moderation or pause during acute phase. Coffee fine but watch jitters during steroid course.
