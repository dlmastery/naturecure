## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What rosacea actually is

**For you, in plain words.** Rosacea is a chronic inflammatory facial-skin condition that mostly affects the central face (cheeks, nose, forehead, chin). It has four overlapping sub-types — flushing-and-visible-vessels (ETR), papules-and-pustules (PPR), thickening-of-the-nose-and-cheeks (phymatous), and inflamed-eyes-and-lids (ocular). It is NOT acne, NOT dirty skin, NOT something you caused. Most adults have a mix of more than one sub-type.

**Quick start.** Apply tinted mineral SPF 50+ with iron oxide every morning (Avène Tinted Mineral Fluid, La Roche-Posay Anthelios Tinted Mineral, MDSolarSciences Mineral Tinted, EltaMD UV Elements Tinted). Switch to a barrier-first cleanser (Cetaphil, Vanicream, CeraVe Hydrating, Bioderma Sensibio Micellar). Add niacinamide 4-5% serum AM + PM. Start a trigger diary tonight. Photograph baseline.

#### Open the science

Prevalence ~5-10% adults; peak onset 30-50; female:male ~3:1 ETR/PPR; male predominance for rhinophyma. Multi-axial pathogenesis: (i) **innate-immune dysregulation** — overactive cathelicidin LL-37 cleaved abnormally by KLK5 (Yamasaki 2007); (ii) **vasomotor instability** — TRPV1/TRPA1 neurogenic flush; (iii) **Demodex folliculorum overgrowth** + *Bacillus oleronius* symbiont → TLR2 inflammation; (iv) **gut-skin axis** — SIBO + H. pylori + IBD (Drago 2008, Egeberg 2016, Yamasaki 2014); (v) **environmental triggers** — UV, visible light, heat, alcohol (red wine), spicy food, exercise, stress. Cardinal lesions: persistent erythema, telangiectasia, papules/pustules **without comedones** (differentiates from acne), phymatous thickening, ocular surface inflammation.

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

**For you, in plain words.** Four engines drive rosacea — innate-immune over-activation (cathelicidin LL-37), vasomotor instability (sun/heat/alcohol → vessel-dilation), Demodex mite overgrowth (papulopustular), and gut-skin axis (SIBO, dysbiosis). Each gets its own intervention layer.

**Quick start.** Hit all four. Iron-oxide mineral SPF + barrier (immune + vasomotor). Niacinamide + azelaic acid (immune). Trigger diary (vasomotor). For papulopustular with mite signal — tea-tree-oil lid wipes + (if severe) §10.7.3 Soolantra (Demodex). Gut: low-FODMAP if SIBO suspected + probiotic.

#### Open the science

The four-driver framework is the working model in modern dermatology (van Zuuren 2015 Cochrane systematic review, Tan 2017 ROSCO consensus, Drake 2018 NRS framework, EADV European Academy of Dermatology guidelines). Each driver has its own primary literature, its own intervention class, and its own measurable phenotype.

### 2.2 Innate-immune dysregulation (cathelicidin / LL-37)

**For you, in plain words.** Rosacea-prone skin makes too much of a natural antimicrobial called LL-37 (cathelicidin), and a kitchen-enzyme called kallikrein-5 chops it wrong, creating peptides that trigger redness, vessel growth, and inflammation. Niacinamide, azelaic acid, green-tea polyphenols and licorice extract calm this pathway. *(Yamasaki 2007; Bissett 2004; Liu 2006 azelaic acid)*

**Quick start.** Niacinamide 4-5% serum AM + PM (every market — The Ordinary, Paula's Choice, La Roche-Posay Effaclar). Azelaic acid 15% (Skinoria Aquilea + The Inkey List + The Ordinary in EU/UK/AU markets; in US azelaic 10% OTC + 15-20% Rx in §10.7.3). Green-tea polyphenol topical 2-4% (BeautyStat, The Inkey List).

#### Open the science

Yamasaki 2007 (Nature Medicine): rosacea skin has 10-fold elevated cathelicidin LL-37 and elevated kallikrein-5 (KLK5) — KLK5 abnormally cleaves cathelicidin into pro-inflammatory peptides (LL-37-derived 29-37, 30-37) that trigger erythema, vasodilation, neutrophil chemotaxis. This is the upstream "switch" of rosacea inflammation. UV-light + heat further activate KLK5. Targeted by **azelaic acid** (Liu 2006 + EADV — A-grade for papulopustular; mechanism: inhibits KLK5 + cathelicidin processing + anti-inflammatory), **niacinamide 4-5%** topical (Bissett 2004 + Draelos 2005 — B-grade for erythema + barrier; mechanism: NF-κB modulation + ceramide synthesis + sebum reduction), **green-tea polyphenols topical EGCG** (Elsaie 2009 — B-grade; anti-inflammatory + anti-angiogenic), **licorice root extract topical** (glabridin — small evidence; anti-inflammatory + skin-tone-evening), **omega-3 + EPA/DHA oral** (Bhargava 2016 — small signal for ocular rosacea), **sea buckthorn oil topical** (omega-7 + carotenoids — small skin barrier signal), **broad-spectrum SPF with iron oxide** (Antelmi 2019 — blocks visible light that activates KLK5).

### 2.3 Vasomotor instability (flush + telangiectasia)

**For you, in plain words.** Rosacea-prone skin has hair-trigger vessels — sun, heat, alcohol (red wine in particular), spicy food, exercise without cool-down, hot beverages and emotional stress all open the surface vessels, and over time the vessels stay opened (telangiectasia). Trigger-mapping + cool compresses + mineral SPF are the home tools. *(Drake 2018 NRS patient survey; Wilkin 1981 flush mechanism)*

**Quick start.** Start a 2-week trigger diary (date, what you ate/drank/did, flush 0-10 score). Identify your top 3. Then eliminate or modify. Cool wet washcloth or cooling face roller for acute flush. Avoid hot showers on face. Cool ambient temperature.

#### Open the science

Vasomotor mechanism: rosacea skin has up-regulated TRPV1 + TRPA1 thermo-sensors + neurogenic vasodilation; UV + heat + ethanol + capsaicin + spicy-aldehydes (cinnamaldehyde) directly activate these channels → CGRP + substance P release → cutaneous vasodilation. With repeated activation, vessels remodel and become persistently visible (telangiectasia). Drake 2018 NRS patient survey (n=1,066) ranked patient-reported triggers: sun (81%), emotional stress (79%), hot weather (75%), wine (52%), red wine specifically (52%, distinct from other alcohol), other alcohol (45%), spicy food (45%), exercise (56%), hot bath (51%), cold weather (46%), humidity (44%), hot beverages (36%), heavy exercise without cool-down (50%). Targeted by **iron-oxide tinted mineral SPF 50+** (Antelmi 2019 — blocks visible light too), **trigger-elimination diary**, **cool wet compresses + face roller** (acute flush), **mineral makeup + green-tinted colour-correcting primer** for visible-redness camouflage, **stress reduction** (MBSR — Pinheiro 2021 small signal for rosacea), **gentle exercise with cool-down + cool shower post-exercise**, **avoid very-hot showers on face**, **avoid sauna in active flare**, **alcohol-modification or elimination** (red wine especially), **spicy-food moderation per individual tolerance**, **ambient temperature control** (no fireplaces near face, AC in summer), **§10.7.3 clinic-route**: brimonidine 0.33% gel Mirvaso + oxymetazoline 1% cream Rhofade for short-acting erythema reduction (Fowler 2013 + Stein-Gold 2017 A-grade with rebound caveat), IPL Intense Pulsed Light (Liu 2014 B+), PDL pulsed dye laser (Tan 2013) for telangiectasia.

### 2.4 Demodex folliculorum overgrowth

**For you, in plain words.** *Demodex folliculorum* is a tiny mite that normally lives in everyone's facial follicles in small numbers. In rosacea-prone skin (especially papulopustular and ocular sub-types), it overgrows significantly. The mite itself + a bacterial symbiont (*Bacillus oleronius*) trigger immune inflammation. Tea-tree oil lid wipes (4-5% terpinen-4-ol), proper face-washing, and (if severe) §10.7.3 Soolantra are the tools. *(Forton 2018 Demodex review; Stein 2014 + Taieb 2015 ATTRACT ivermectin)*

**Quick start.** If pustular pattern or ocular involvement: tea-tree-oil lid wipes 50% (Cliradex, Eye Eco TheraTears, We Love Eyes, Blephadex) once nightly. Wash pillowcase weekly. Avoid heavy oily moisturisers that nourish Demodex. If severe + diagnosed → §10.7.3 ivermectin 1% Soolantra A-grade.

#### Open the science

Forton 2018 (J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol — Demodex in rosacea review): Demodex density (per cm² by standardised surface biopsy) is ~5-15x higher in rosacea skin vs controls (>5 mites/cm² is the typical threshold). Mechanism: mite chitin + B. oleronius LPS-like + heat-shock-protein-62 → TLR2 → IL-8 + cathelicidin amplification → papulopustular inflammation. Forton 2018 + Casas 2012 confirmed Demodex elimination correlates with rosacea clinical improvement. Stein 2014 (ATTRACT-1 + ATTRACT-2 phase III RCTs n=683 + n=688): topical ivermectin 1% (Soolantra) significantly outperformed vehicle for papulopustular rosacea over 12 weeks — IGA success 38% vs 12% in trial 1, 40% vs 19% in trial 2. Taieb 2015: 16-week comparison showed topical ivermectin non-inferior to topical metronidazole 0.75% with trend favouring ivermectin. Targeted by **tea-tree oil topical 5% gel** (Bassett 1990 in acne — for rosacea: lower concentration to avoid irritation, prefer specialised eyelid formulations like Cliradex 50% terpinen-4-ol-purified extract for Demodex blepharitis), **tea-tree oil lid wipes** for ocular/blepharitis overlap, **hot-compress lid hygiene** (Bruder MGDRx mask), **avoidance of heavy occlusive moisturisers**, **pillowcase + towel hygiene** (weekly hot wash 60°C), **§10.7.3 topical ivermectin 1% Soolantra** (Stein 2014 + Taieb 2015 ATTRACT A-grade), **§10.7.3 topical metronidazole 0.75-1%** (van Zuuren 2015 Cochrane A-grade).

### 2.5 Gut-skin axis + systemic inflammation

**For you, in plain words.** Rosacea is associated with several gut conditions — SIBO (small-intestinal bacterial overgrowth), H. pylori infection, IBD (Crohn's + UC), celiac disease, and gut dysbiosis. Treating the gut often calms rosacea. Low-FODMAP trial, probiotics, fermented foods (if tolerated), and Mediterranean diet are the home tools. *(Drago 2008 SIBO; Egeberg 2016 IBD-rosacea cohort; Spoendlin 2016)*

**Quick start.** Mediterranean diet baseline. If bloating + flush correlate: 4-6 week low-FODMAP trial → reintroduce. Probiotic multi-strain (Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium). Fermented foods (kefir, sauerkraut) if tolerated — test histamine tolerance first.

#### Open the science

Drago 2008 (Italian study n=113 rosacea vs n=60 control): SIBO prevalence significantly higher in rosacea (52% vs 5%); rifaximin eradication produced rosacea improvement. Egeberg 2016 (Danish nationwide cohort n=49,475 rosacea vs n=4.3M controls): rosacea associated with IBD (especially Crohn's), celiac disease, T1D. Yamasaki 2014 (Yamasaki + Gallo gut-skin axis review): cathelicidin LL-37 expressed in both skin and gut; gut barrier disruption upregulates skin cathelicidin. Holmes 2018 (Australian H. pylori review): some signal for H. pylori-rosacea association though heterogeneous. Targeted by **Mediterranean-style diet** (low-glycemic, anti-inflammatory, polyphenol-rich), **low-FODMAP trial 4-6 weeks then reintroduce** (if SIBO/IBS overlap), **probiotic** (Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium multi-strain 10-25 billion CFU; specific strains *L. paracasei* + *L. rhamnosus*), **fermented foods if tolerated** (kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi — check histamine tolerance), **bone broth / collagen / L-glutamine** for gut barrier, **H. pylori screen if dyspepsia + rosacea** (stool antigen via DTC), **celiac screen if symptoms suggest**, **anti-inflammatory diet** — reduce sugary drinks, refined carbs, processed seed oils, alcohol, smoking, **omega-3 EPA+DHA** 2-3 g (Bhargava 2016 small signal especially ocular rosacea).

### 4.1 Ayurveda — *Mukhdushika* / Pitta-skin

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

**For you, in plain words.** Eight foods/drinks drive rosacea — alcohol (especially red wine), spicy foods, hot beverages (hot temperature itself, not caffeine), high-histamine foods (in histamine-intolerant subset), refined sugar + ultra-processed carbs, hot soup (temperature), dairy in some sub-types, citrus (in some — TRPV1 activator).

#### Open the science

- **Alcohol — red wine #1** (histamine + sulphites + ethanol vasodilation).
- **Spicy foods (capsaicin, cinnamaldehyde)** — TRPV1/TRPA1 neurogenic flush.
- **Hot beverages** — temperature itself, not caffeine; switch iced.
- **High-histamine foods (histamine-intolerant subset only)** — aged cheese, fermented meats, vinegar-pickled, smoked fish, sparkling wine; test Cerascreen DAO.
- **Refined sugar + ultra-processed carbs** — gut dysbiosis + systemic inflammation.
- **Dairy + citrus + tomatoes + eggplant** — individual sensitivity, especially histamine-overlap.
- **Smoking** — independent risk + worsens vasomotor.
- **Chronic dehydration**.

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

**For you, in plain words.** Cool, gentle cooking. Ferment if histamine-tolerant. Soak grains/legumes. Use spices that DON'T flush (turmeric, coriander, fennel, cumin) — avoid chilli + cayenne + cinnamon-heavy + paprika-heavy.

### 10.5 Exercise / Sleep / Caffeine timing rules

**For you, in plain words.** Cool-down post-exercise. Cool shower not hot. Caffeine moderate (caffeine itself doesn't flush; hot temperature does). Sleep 7-9 hours.

#### Open the science

- Exercise: Zone 2 moderate; cool-down 10 min; cool shower post; avoid hot yoga.
- Caffeine: the caffeine molecule itself does not flush rosacea — the HOT temperature of hot coffee/tea does. Iced coffee + iced tea are usually fine for most users.
- Sleep: 7-9 h, cool bedroom (18-20°C), blackout, humidified 40-55%.
- Alcohol: red wine #1 trigger per Drake 2018; consider eliminating or rotating.
- Spicy food: individual tolerance; trigger diary identifies your threshold.

### 10.6 Strictly Avoid — specific to rosacea

**For you, in plain words.** Direct rosacea aggravators:

- **Topical corticosteroids on face** — cause steroid-rosacea + rebound.
- **Hot beverages, red wine** — Drake 2018 top triggers.
- **Hot yoga, Bikram, sauna in active flare**.
- **Aggressive scrubs, microdermabrasion, glycolic peels >5%**.
- **Fragrance + essential oils on face**; **witch hazel-alcohol, alcohol-denat toners, SLS cleansers**.
- **Peppermint, eucalyptus, camphor, menthol, cinnamon, capsaicin topicals on face**.
- **Strong retinoids in active flare**.
- **Oral niacin nicotinic acid (NOT niacinamide topical — different molecule)**.
- **Smoking, vaping**.
- **Pregnancy: isotretinoin (§10.7.3) TERATOGENIC — mandatory pregnancy prevention**. Most herbs caution.
- **Lactation**: most OTC topical caveat; consult Hale's.
