## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What acne actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Acne is not "bad skin" — it is the follicular signal that hormones, food and bacteria are out of balance. Naming the four drivers lets us aim at the real targets. *(Bhate & Williams 2013; Melnik 2015 mTORC1 acne)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Cut dairy whey + refined sugar + ultra-processed snacks from tonight. Start zinc picolinate 30 mg with dinner. Apply niacinamide 4% serum AM and bakuchiol 1% PM. ### 1.3 The deeper layer

#### Open the science

Acne is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" problem treated with topical retinoids, benzoyl peroxide, topical/oral antibiotics, hormonal pills and isotretinoin. The integrative reframing is different: acne is the **follicular presentation of a four-node systemic loop** — (i) androgen-driven sebum hypersecretion with altered sebum lipidome, (ii) follicular hyperkeratinisation occluding the sebaceous duct, (iii) *Cutibacterium acnes* (formerly *Propionibacterium acnes*) phylotype shift and *Malassezia* overgrowth on a dysbiotic skin microbiome, and (iv) insulin / IGF-1 / mTORC1 signalling amplified by high-glycaemic-load Western diets, dairy, gut dysbiosis and HPA-axis dysregulation. **Synthesis.** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously normalises insulin / IGF-1 / mTORC1, repairs the gut, modulates skin microbiome and selectively reduces 5α-reductase / androgen tone addresses the upstream drivers, not the comedone. *(Melnik 2015; Bhate & Williams 2013)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive acne — androgens make too much oil, the follicle clogs, the wrong bacteria takes over, and Western food drives insulin and IGF-1 through the roof. Each gets its own intervention layer. *(Melnik 2015; Dreno 2018 microbiome)*

### 2.2 Androgen-driven oil overflow

> **For you, in plain words.** Hormones (testosterone, DHT) turn up the volume on your oil glands and change the oil to a kind that clogs and inflames. Spearmint tea, saw palmetto and zinc bring the volume down. *(Grant 2010 spearmint; Pais 2010 saw palmetto DHT; Dreno 2013 zinc)*

#### Open the science

Free testosterone is converted to dihydrotestosterone (DHT) by 5α-reductase in sebocytes; DHT binds the androgen receptor (AR), upregulates sterol-response-element-binding-protein-1 (SREBP-1) and stearoyl-CoA desaturase, driving triglyceride + squalene synthesis. Squalene is then peroxidised (squalene peroxide is comedogenic and pro-inflammatory). Clinical: oily T-zone, jawline, chest, back; flares premenstrually, on high-androgen states (PCOS, anabolic supplements). Targeted by spearmint tea (anti-androgen), saw palmetto (5α-reductase inhibition), zinc picolinate (anti-androgen + barrier), green tea EGCG topical, *Vitex agnus-castus* (luteal-phase hormonal modulation). **Synthesis.** Damping androgen → sebum is the upstream brake on the entire cascade. *(Grant 2010; Pais 2010; Dreno 2013)*

### 2.3 Follicular plugging

> **For you, in plain words.** The skin cells lining each follicle stick together and plug the pore — this is the invisible micro-comedone that becomes every visible pimple. Bakuchiol and niacinamide gently open the follicle. *(Plewig 2000 microcomedone; Dhaliwal 2019 bakuchiol RCT)*

#### Open the science

IL-1α and altered linoleic-acid : oleic-acid ratio in sebum drive infundibular keratinocyte cohesion failure — corneocytes pile up, plugging the follicle. The micro-comedone is the precursor of every visible lesion. Targeted by topical bakuchiol (plant retinoid analog), topical niacinamide 4%, topical 2% salicylic acid (BHA), oral zinc, omega-3 (restores linoleic acid). **Synthesis.** Open the follicle without stripping the barrier and the inflammatory cascade loses its substrate. *(Plewig 2000; Dhaliwal 2019 Br J Dermatol)*

### 2.4 Bacterial and yeast shift

> **For you, in plain words.** A bacterium called *C. acnes* shifts to an inflammatory strain; in some people, a yeast called *Malassezia* over-grows (fungal acne). Tea tree oil, manuka honey, neem and skin-microbiome probiotics restore balance. *(Bassett 1990 Australia tea tree; Dreno 2018 microbiome)*

#### Open the science

Healthy follicles are colonised by *C. acnes* phylotype II/III; acne-prone follicles shift toward phylotype IA1, which produces porphyrins, LPS-like toxins, biofilm and triggers TLR2/4 → NLRP3 inflammasome → IL-1β. Concurrent *Malassezia furfur/restricta* overgrowth produces the small uniform monomorphic papules of "fungal acne" (Malassezia folliculitis). Targeted by topical tea tree oil 5%, *Indigo naturalis* topical, neem topical, manuka honey mask, oral berberine + AMPK pathway, oral probiotic (*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG, *L. paracasei*); for fungal sub-type, zinc pyrithione 1% wash + reduction of skin-fed yeasts. **Synthesis.** Restoring skin microbial balance is the immune-de-escalation lever. *(Bassett 1990; Dreno 2018)*

### 2.5 Insulin / IGF-1 / mTORC1 + gut-HPA

> **For you, in plain words.** Western food — refined sugar, dairy whey, ultra-processed snacks — spikes insulin and a growth signal called IGF-1, which tells your oil glands to make more oil and your follicles to plug. Plus chronic stress feeds the loop. Low-glycaemic millet diet, dairy elimination, berberine and ashwagandha all bring it down. *(Smith 2007 RCT low-GI; Melnik 2015 mTORC1; Cordain 2002 Western diet acne)*

#### Open the science

Western diets high in refined dairy whey, high-glycaemic carbohydrate and ultra-processed foods raise insulin and IGF-1, which inhibit FoxO1 (a transcriptional brake on sebocyte lipogenesis) and activate mTORC1 — directly upregulating SREBP-1 and follicular hyperkeratinisation. Gut dysbiosis (depleted *Akkermansia*, *Faecalibacterium*) and chronic stress (cortisol → CRH on sebaceous glands → sebum) amplify the loop. Cohort data link acne severity to HOMA-IR. Targeted by low-glycaemic Siri Dhanyalu millet diet, dairy elimination, intermittent fasting (if appropriate), berberine, inositol (myo + d-chiro 40:1 for PCOS overlap), ashwagandha, Yoga Nidra. **Synthesis.** Without metabolic-gut-HPA repair, the follicular flare returns the moment topicals are stopped. *(Smith 2007; Melnik 2015; Cordain 2002)*

### When to call your doctor

> **For you, in plain words.** Six situations in acne are not "wait and see" — they need a dermatologist or relevant specialist (not the ER, but soon).

- **Severe nodulocystic acne** (deep, painful, draining nodules; multiple cysts) — refer to dermatologist; this is the isotretinoin (Roaccutane) candidate territory and the home protocol cannot match it. *(EMA isotretinoin; BAD acne guidelines)*
- **Sudden adult-onset acne, especially with hirsutism, irregular cycles, or male-pattern hair loss** — rule out PCOS, androgen-secreting tumour, late-onset CAH; full hormone workup + endocrinology referral. *(EMA PCOS; ESHRE/ASRM 2018)*
- **Visible scarring developing** — ice-pick, boxcar, or rolling scars → derm referral within weeks; early intervention with topical retinoids or in-clinic chemical peels prevents permanent disfigurement.
- **Severe psychological distress or social withdrawal** (DLQI ≥10), suicidal ideation linked to skin → mental-health referral + dermatology; isotretinoin has a black-box for mood, so this triad needs joint management. *(crisis lines: 988 US, Samaritans UK, iCall IN, Lifeline AU)*
