> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for long-term asthma control — chronic, between-flare living, not the emergency stuff. Lead with the boring high-evidence levers: a daily peak-flow diary, a clean indoor-air setup, and a slow nasal-breathing practice. Supplements come second, as supports — not as replacements for what your prescriber may already have you on. *(WHO Traditional Medicine Strategy 2025-2034; Bruton 2017 Cochrane on Buteyko; Martineau 2017 BMJ vitamin D meta)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO TCIM, India's Ministry of AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS), EMA HMPC herbal monographs, Australia TGA, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Men-Tsee-Khang Dharamsala, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, plus classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator or US disease association is cited as authority.** The single narrow factual exception is the regulatory-path note about montelukast's 2020 neuropsychiatric boxed warning in §10.7.3 — used as a factual safety signal, not as an evidence anchor.
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every supplement, food, breath practice and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No prescription, no clinic visit, no hospital admission is in the home regime.** Inhaled corticosteroids, biologics (omalizumab/Xolair, mepolizumab, benralizumab, dupilumab, tezepelumab), oral steroids and allergy immunotherapy are placed in §10.7.3 as referral context, never inside the daily protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users **already** on those medicines can see how this protocol interacts with them.
>
> **Red flag — severe exacerbation.** Peak flow below 50% of your personal best, struggling to speak in full sentences, neck and rib muscles pulling visibly, blue lips or fingertips, or a silent chest where wheezing has gone quiet — this is an emergency. Use your reliever inhaler and seek emergency care on your own initiative. This home stack does **not** replace acute care.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What chronic asthma actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic asthma is not a "flare problem" — it's a low-grade airway inflammation that's smouldering 24/7, and the goal of the home plan is to lower that background fire so the flares get rarer and milder. *(GINA 2024 global synthesis; Holgate 2015 Lancet Respiratory)*

### 1.2 The four root drivers

> **Quick start.** Buy a peak-flow meter tonight and a true HEPA air purifier for the bedroom this week; start nasal-only breathing during waking hours; clean your bedroom for dust mites and mould; cut indoor smoke, vape and strong fragrances; document your reliever-puffs-per-week as your living dashboard. *(Bruton 2017 Cochrane Buteyko; Sublett 2011 indoor air)*

### 1.3 Why "control," not "cure"

Chronic asthma is best framed as four interlocking root drivers — (i) **type-2 airway inflammation** with eosinophils, IL-4/IL-5/IL-13 and IgE in allergic and eosinophilic phenotypes; (ii) **bronchial smooth-muscle hyperreactivity** that over-constricts to cold air, exercise, viruses and irritants; (iii) **dysfunctional breathing** — chronic mouth-breathing, hyperventilation, low end-tidal CO₂ and a hair-trigger panic-breath response that worsens perceived symptoms; and (iv) **trigger and microbiome exposure** — indoor allergens, mould, occupational dusts, NSAIDs in aspirin-sensitive subtype, vitamin-D deficiency, gut and airway microbiome shifts. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs daily peak-flow self-monitoring, breath-retraining (Buteyko, pranayama), indoor-air hygiene and an anti-inflammatory diet — with a small, evidence-graded supplement layer on top — hits all four drivers without competing with whichever inhaler regime your prescriber has set.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers — airway inflammation, twitchy muscle, broken breathing pattern, and trigger load — explain why asthma keeps coming back. Naming each lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Holgate 2015; Bousquet 2008)*

### 2.2 Type-2 airway inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** In most adults with persistent asthma, immune cells called eosinophils are sitting in the airway lining, dripping inflammatory signals all day. That's the background fire. *(Wenzel 2012 Nature Medicine; Pavord 2018 Lancet)*

**The deeper layer.** Allergens and viral remnants drive epithelial alarmins (TSLP, IL-33, IL-25) that recruit ILC2s and Th2 cells. These pump out IL-4 (IgE class-switching), IL-5 (eosinophil expansion) and IL-13 (mucus + smooth-muscle changes). Eosinophil granule proteins (MBP, ECP, EDN) damage the epithelium. Targeted by **vitamin D** (Martineau 2017 BMJ moderate-grade meta showing fewer exacerbations), **omega-3 EPA/DHA**, **quercetin** (mast-cell stabiliser, weaker human asthma data), **curcumin**, **Vasaka / Adhatoda vasica** (Ayurveda, A-grade traditional bronchodilator), **Boswellia serrata** (Gupta 1998 small signal in asthma), and avoidance of indoor allergens via HEPA + dust-mite covers. **Synthesis.** Lowering type-2 inflammation is the upstream lever that lets every downstream therapy work.

### 2.3 Smooth-muscle hyperreactivity

> **For you, in plain words.** Even when inflammation is low, the muscle ring around the airways stays jumpy — it slams shut on cold air, exercise, viral colds or strong smells. *(Brusasco & Pellegrino 2003)*

**The deeper layer.** Airway smooth muscle in asthma shows increased mass, altered contractility, and a hair-trigger response to cholinergic, leukotriene and histamine signals. Vagal cholinergic tone often dominates, especially at night. Targeted by **magnesium glycinate / citrate** (oral mixed evidence; IV is clinic-A-grade for acute), **pranayama** (Bidwell 2012, Saxena 2009 — improved FEV1 and symptom scores), **Buteyko breathing** (Bruton 2017 Cochrane B+ for symptom control), warm-up before exercise, scarf-over-mouth for cold-air-induced EIB, **Lobelia inflata** (Eclectic tradition + homeopathic), and **Ma Huang / Ephedra** in TCM (CAUTION — stimulant, CV risk, legally restricted in many jurisdictions). **Synthesis.** Breath-retraining is the home tool that quiets this twitch without a single capsule.

### 2.4 Dysfunctional breathing pattern

> **For you, in plain words.** Many people with asthma also breathe wrong all day — through the mouth, too fast, too shallow. That alone makes the asthma feel worse than it is. *(Thomas 2005; Bruton 2017 Cochrane)*

**The deeper layer.** Dysfunctional breathing — habitual mouth-breathing, increased respiratory rate, paradoxical chest-vs-abdomen motion, hypocapnia — is documented in 30-60% of adults carrying an asthma diagnosis. Low end-tidal CO₂ causes cerebral vasoconstriction, dizziness, anxiety, and air-hunger that feels like an asthma flare but is not bronchospastic. Buteyko's central insight: chronic hyperventilation perpetuates bronchospasm. Targeted by **Buteyko nasal-only + reduced-volume practice**, **Papworth method** (diaphragmatic + relaxation), **Bhramari humming** (raises nasal NO ~15-fold per Lundberg 1995), **slow-paced 5-6 breaths/minute coherent breathing**, **Anulom Vilom**, taping the mouth at night (Itzhak Maoz protocols — controversial, present honestly). **Synthesis.** Of all home interventions, breath-retraining gives the most consistent ACT improvement in published RCTs.

### 2.5 Trigger and microbiome load

> **For you, in plain words.** What sits in your bedroom air, your gut, your cleaning cupboard and your workplace decides how often the airways are insulted. Lower the insults, lower the asthma. *(Bousquet 2008 ARIA; Stiemsma 2017 microbiome-asthma)*

**The deeper layer.** Indoor allergens (Der p 1 / Der f 1 dust-mite proteins, cat Fel d 1, mould spores, cockroach), outdoor air pollution (PM2.5, NO₂, ozone), occupational sensitisers (isocyanates, flour, latex, animal proteins), tobacco/vape smoke, NSAIDs in AERD (~10% of asthmatics), beta-blockers, paracetamol exposure in childhood, and an altered airway + gut microbiome (lower Bacteroidetes diversity, low SCFA producers) all feed into the type-2 axis. Targeted by **HEPA air purifier** (Coway, IQAir, Levoit — bedroom priority), **dust-mite-proof mattress and pillow covers**, **N95/KN95 on high-pollution days**, **smoking and vaping cessation** (single highest-leverage lever — varenicline/NRT noted in §10.7.3), **NSAID avoidance** in AERD, **weight loss** in the obesity-asthma phenotype, **occupational trigger investigation**, and a **fibre-rich + fermented-food** Mediterranean diet to repair the gut-lung axis. **Synthesis.** Trigger elimination is unglamorous and the single most underestimated lever in chronic asthma control.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Ayurveda — Vata-Kapha Shwasa

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda calls asthma *Tamaka Shwasa* and treats it as cold-mucus (kapha) driving wind (vata). Vasaka is the centrepiece herb — it's a real, A-grade traditional bronchodilator with monograph backing. *(Charaka Samhita, Chikitsa 17; AYUSH-CCRAS monograph)*

**Quick start.** Vasaka (Adhatoda) syrup 10 mL twice daily, Sitopaladi Churna 3 g with honey and ghee twice daily, Tulsi (holy basil) tea 2-3 cups/day. Kanakasava 15 mL with warm water at bedtime for night-cough phenotype. *(Bhavaprakasha; Sahasrayoga)*

#### Open the science

- **Vasaka / Adhatoda vasica** — *Sahasrayoga* lists it as the prime *Shwasahara* herb. Active alkaloid vasicine relaxes bronchial smooth muscle and is mucolytic; vasicinone has bronchodilator activity. AYUSH-GMP Vasaka syrups (Dabur, Himalaya, Baidyanath). Standard dose 5-15 mL of syrup or 250-500 mg of standardised extract twice daily.
- **Pippali / Long pepper (Piper longum)** — *Charaka, Chikitsa 17* names Pippali Rasayana for chronic respiratory. Modern data: piperine improves bioavailability of co-administered actives; mild bronchodilator signal.
- **Yashtimadhu / Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)** — demulcent, mild anti-inflammatory, glycyrrhizin gives steroid-sparing action in some classical regimens. Caution: hypertension and hypokalaemia at >3 g/day chronic.
- **Sitopaladi Churna** — classical respiratory powder (sugar, bamboo manna, Pippali, cardamom, cinnamon). Mucolytic and warming. 3 g with honey and ghee.
- **Talisadi Churna** — Talispatra-based, for chronic cough + asthma overlap.
- **Kanakasava** — fermented preparation containing Datura (controlled, in classical small dose) for night-cough phenotype; AYUSH-GMP product (Kerala Ayurveda, AVN, AVP). Do not exceed labelled dose — Datura at high dose is anticholinergic toxic.
- **Vasarishta** — Vasaka-based fermented preparation, 15-30 mL with water twice daily.
- **Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum)** — adaptogen with mild bronchodilator and immune-modulatory activity (Cohen 2014 review). Fresh leaves 4-6 daily or 300 mg standardised extract.
- **Bharangyadi Kashayam** — Kerala classical decoction for Tamaka Shwasa.

### 4.2 TCM — Lung Qi & Phlegm

> **For you, in plain words.** Chinese medicine sorts asthma by whether cold, heat or weakness dominates. Real classical formulas exist for each pattern; one major caution — Ma Huang (ephedra) is a powerful bronchodilator but carries unacceptable cardiac risk and legal restrictions in most countries. *(Huang Di Nei Jing; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020)*

**Quick start.** For cold-wheeze (clear sputum, worse in cold): Xiao Qing Long Tang (Minor Bluegreen Dragon). For heat-wheeze (yellow sputum, fever): Ma Xing Shi Gan Tang (without practitioner: a Plum Flower granule under TCM-pharmacist guidance). For chronic weakness: Yu Ping Feng San (Jade Windscreen) preventive year-round. *(Shang Han Lun; Tai Ping Hui Min He Ji Ju Fang)*

#### Open the science

- **Ma Huang / Ephedra sinica** — classical bronchodilator (the parent compound of pseudoephedrine). **CAUTION:** acceptable cardiovascular safety profile is only with experienced TCM-physician dosing; banned or restricted in many jurisdictions (US, Canada, EU). We document it for completeness; we do **not** include it in the home protocol.
- **Ding Chuan Tang (Arrest Wheezing Decoction)** — classical asthma formula; contains Ma Huang, ginkgo nut, mulberry bark, almond, scutellaria. Plum Flower granule.
- **Xiao Qing Long Tang (Minor Bluegreen Dragon)** — cold-rooted asthma with watery sputum. *Shang Han Lun*.
- **Bu Fei Tang (Tonify the Lung Decoction)** — chronic lung-qi deficiency with weak cough.
- **San Zi Yang Qin Tang (Three-Seed Filial Devotion)** — elderly cough-phlegm-asthma overlap; perilla, radish, mustard seeds. Gentle, well-tolerated.
- **Yu Ping Feng San (Jade Windscreen)** — preventive immune-barrier formula; astragalus, atractylodes, ledebouriella. Year-round use. Plum Flower / Min Tong granule.
- **Sang Bai Pi (mulberry root bark)** — single herb for cough with heat.
- **Loquat / Pi Pa Gao** — classical syrup, especially Nin Jiom Pei Pa Koa (over-the-counter at Asian groceries), demulcent for cough.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Indoor-air engineering

> **For you, in plain words.** What sits in your bedroom air decides how much asthma you have at 3 a.m. Buy a real HEPA purifier and dust-mite covers this week. *(Sublett 2011 review; Sublett 2010 RCT bedroom HEPA)*

**Quick start.** HEPA purifier rated for your bedroom square-footage (CADR ≥250 for a 200 sq ft room): Coway AP-1512HH, IQAir HealthPro, Levoit Core 400S, Blueair. Dust-mite-proof zip-encased mattress + pillow covers (Mission Allergy, AllerEase). Hot-wash bedding ≥60°C weekly. Vacuum with HEPA-filter vacuum twice weekly.

#### Open the science

Bedroom HEPA filtration is the single most reproducible non-pharm intervention for allergen-driven asthma. Cool-mist humidifier in dry climates with caution — over-humidification (>55% RH) breeds dust mite and mould; aim 40-50%. Avoid ultrasonic without distilled water (mineral aerosol). On high-pollution days (PM2.5 >35 µg/m³), N95/KN95 respirators outdoors. Indoor dust-mite and mould spore home test kits (Mold Inspection Network, ImmunoLytics) are sub-clinical-grade but useful baselines. Avoid scented candles, plug-in air-fresheners, fragranced cleaning products, incense, and indoor smoking/vaping.

### 5.2 Peak-flow self-monitoring

> **For you, in plain words.** A £15 plastic tube measures your airways once in the morning and once at night. Cheaper than any supplement and a dozen times more useful. *(Reddel 2009 GINA self-monitoring synthesis)*

**Quick start.** Mini-Wright peak-flow meter or Truzone (kids and adults). Personal-best = average of best-three readings over 2 weeks when feeling well. Track AM + PM. Green zone ≥80% personal best · Yellow 50-80% (reliever + reassess) · Red <50% (emergency).

#### Open the science

Twice-daily peak-flow with personal-best zoning detects worsening control 3-7 days before symptoms become severe. Add the Mir Spirobank Smart home spirometer for fuller FEV1/FVC tracking if budget allows. Pulse oximeter (Wellue, Masimo MightySat) for spot-checks during illness: SpO₂ <94% sustained is a red flag in asthma. Asthma Control Test (ACT) — 5 items, scored 5-25, ≥20 = controlled — done monthly is the living symptom dashboard.

### 5.3 Home red-light & sauna adjuncts

> **For you, in plain words.** Red-light therapy and infrared sauna are not asthma treatments, but they support the upstream gut-lung-microbiome axis and recovery from frequent colds. Optional, not core. *(Hamblin 2017 PBM review)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red MID or Joov Solo, 10-15 min on chest 3×/week — modest, optional. Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten, JNH Lifestyles) 20 min 2-3×/week if tolerated. **Caution:** stop and exit if any breathing tightness develops in the sauna.

### 5.4 Cold air & PEMF — present honestly

> **For you, in plain words.** Cold-water immersion is fashionable, but in asthma it can trigger bronchospasm. Skip it unless you tolerate it well. Home PEMF mats have no real asthma evidence. *(Anderson 2010 EIB review)*

#### Open the science

Cold-water immersion (Plunge, Edge Tub, ice-bath barrel) — many people with EIB experience cold-induced bronchospasm and should avoid sub-15°C exposure until well-controlled. Home PEMF (Bemer, Pulse Centers) — no credible asthma-specific evidence; documented here for completeness only.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods (daily)

> **For you, in plain words.** Eat for the inflamed airway — colourful Mediterranean foods, oily fish, ginger and turmeric in tempering, fermented foods for the gut-lung axis. *(Berthon 2013 Mediterranean diet & asthma)*

#### Open the science

- **150-200 g leafy greens** (spinach, kale, methi, amaranth) — magnesium, folate, polyphenols.
- **120-160 g oily fish** (salmon, mackerel, sardines, anchovy) 3×/week — long-chain omega-3.
- **2 servings allium** (garlic, onion, shallot) — quercetin, sulphur compounds.
- **1-2 cups berries** — anthocyanins, vitamin C.
- **30 g nuts** (walnut, almond) — magnesium, omega-3 (walnut), vitamin E.

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Sulphited wine, MSG-heavy ultraprocessed food, fizzy drinks, deep-fried, and (in aspirin-sensitive asthma) every NSAID — these reliably trigger flares.

#### Open the science

- **Sulphited foods** (dried fruit, wine, processed meats) — well-documented asthma trigger in sulphite-sensitive subset.
- **NSAIDs in AERD** — aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac strictly avoided.
- **Tartrazine** (E102) and other azo food colours — sensitivity in some.
- **Cold and carbonated drinks** — vagal trigger.
- **Deep-fried + industrial seed oils** — pro-inflammatory.
- **Refined sugar** — glycation + microbiome dysbiosis.
- **Alcohol >7 units/wk** — sulphites + bronchial irritant.
- **Trans fats, ultra-processed snacks, aspartame.**
- **Histamine-loading foods if mast-cell phenotype**: aged cheese, fermented soy, leftover fish, cured meats — case-by-case.

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

> **For you, in plain words.** How you cook your food affects your airway as much as what's in it. Tempering (tadka) makes anti-inflammatory spices fat-soluble.

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** What you breathe at work and at home decides how much asthma you carry. Eight specific things to swap or avoid this week.

#### Open the science

- **Indoor**: scented candles, plug-in air-fresheners, fragranced laundry detergent, fabric softener, aerosol cleaning sprays, bleach, ammonia, incense, mosquito coils, gas-stove fumes (NO₂ — vent or switch to electric where possible).
- **Bedroom**: dust-mite-proof covers, hot-wash bedding ≥60°C weekly, vacuum twice weekly with HEPA, no carpet if possible, no pet on the bed for cat/dog-sensitised users, humidity 40-50%.
- **Mould**: visible mould investigated; bathroom and laundry ventilation; under-sink leaks fixed.
- **Occupational sensitisers**: isocyanates (auto-body paint), flour (baking), latex (healthcare), grain dust (agriculture), wood dust (carpentry), persulphates (hairdressing), animal proteins (lab/vet) — workplace PPE, ventilation, or job-change conversation.
- **Outdoor pollution**: PM2.5 app (IQAir, AirVisual) — N95/KN95 above 35 µg/m³; avoid exercise near heavy traffic.
- **Tobacco and vape**: cessation is the single highest-leverage intervention; mainstream support tools (NRT patches, gum, lozenges) are OTC; varenicline is referral-route §10.7.3.
- **Personal care**: switch to fragrance-free, paraben-free, phthalate-free body wash, shampoo, laundry — sample brands: Earth Mama, All Free Clear, Method Free + Clear.
- **NSAIDs**: in AERD subtype, strict avoidance — paracetamol/acetaminophen low-dose is usually tolerated; coxibs (celecoxib) often tolerated under prescriber guidance.

#### 10.7.2 Behavioural & adjunctive therapies (A-grade non-pharm tools)

> **For you, in plain words.** Buteyko breathing, Papworth method, pranayama, MBSR, and smoking cessation are the highest-evidence non-pharmacological asthma tools. None of them require a prescription.

- **Buteyko breathing method** — Cochrane Bruton 2017 B+: improved symptom control and reliever use, smaller effect on FEV1. *(Bruton 2017 Cochrane; Buteyko Clinic Method app)*
- **Papworth method** — diaphragmatic + relaxation training. Thomas 2003 RCT in primary care.

- **NSAID avoidance** — strict in AERD (~10% of asthmatics aspirin-sensitive).
- **Weight loss** — 5-10% body-weight loss in obesity-asthma phenotype (Dixon 2018) improves ACT meaningfully.
- **Allergen avoidance** — see §6.5.
- **Cold-air avoidance** with scarf-over-mouth for EIB.

#### 10.7.3 Clinic-route options (if home protocol plateaus)

> **For you, in plain words.** If your home plan plateaus, here are the gold-standard conventional therapies your prescriber may already have you on or may add. We document them so the home protocol can sit alongside without conflict. **We do not recommend any of these.**

- **Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS)** — budesonide (Pulmicort), fluticasone (Flovent, Arnuity), mometasone (Asmanex), ciclesonide (Alvesco), beclomethasone (Qvar). GINA 2024 first-line for persistent asthma. A-grade gold-standard.
- **LABA (long-acting beta-agonists)** — formoterol, salmeterol, vilanterol; always combined with ICS, never as monotherapy.
- **ICS-formoterol MART (maintenance + reliever therapy)** — budesonide-formoterol (Symbicort), beclomethasone-formoterol (Fostair) — GINA 2024 strongly prefers this over SABA-only for adults; SYGMA and SMART trial evidence.
- **LAMA** — tiotropium (Spiriva Respimat) — add-on for severe asthma. NOVEL-START + PrACTICAL trials.
- **Leukotriene antagonists** — montelukast (Singulair) — useful in allergic + exercise-induced phenotypes. **Regulatory note: 2020 FDA boxed warning for neuropsychiatric adverse effects (mood, sleep, suicidality) — regulatory-path safety context, not evidence anchor.**
- **Biologics for severe asthma:**

  - **Anti-IL4Rα** — dupilumab (Dupixent) — Th2-high atopic.

- **Oral corticosteroid bursts** — prednisolone 30-40 mg × 5-7 days for exacerbations; never chronic if avoidable.
- **Bronchial thermoplasty** — refractory cases only.
- **Allergy testing + immunotherapy** — formal IgE testing, SCIT (subcutaneous) or SLIT (sublingual tablets) for allergic phenotype — Cochrane evidence supports SCIT for asthma; SLIT for AR; emerging for asthma.
- **Smoking cessation pharmacotherapy** — varenicline (Champix), bupropion.
- **Formal sleep medicine consult** — for OSA-asthma overlap.

## 14. Closing paradigm

The paradigm here: chronic asthma is a four-driver disease and the home protocol stacks evidence-graded layers against each driver — breath-retraining and bedroom hygiene as the unsung A-grade home cornerstones, a small clean supplement layer (vitamin D, omega-3, magnesium, curcumin, Boswellia, Vasaka) sitting on top, classical traditions (Ayurveda Vasaka-Sitopaladi, TCM Yu Ping Feng San preventive) honoured where they earn their place, and full transparency about what we don't recommend (Ma Huang, cold-immersion in active asthma, salt-lamps, chiropractic). The clinic-route options (ICS, MART, biologics, immunotherapy) are documented honestly in §10.7.3 so the user already on them sees no conflict, and so the user whose home protocol plateaus knows the gold-standard names. Sovereignty over the airway means: measure it daily, breathe it slowly, clean its room, feed it well, and stack only what the evidence justifies.
