## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What psoriasis actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Psoriasis is not "just a skin condition" — it is the visible signal that the IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17 axis is stuck "on", with a leaky gut feeding it, a fatty liver amplifying it, and a vitamin-D-starved Treg system that cannot apply the brake. ### 1.3 The deeper layer

#### Open the science

Psoriasis is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" diagnosis treated with topical corticosteroids, vitamin-D analogues (calcipotriene), tazarotene, clinic NB-UVB cabins, methotrexate, cyclosporine, apremilast, deucravacitinib, and an expanding biologic suite (anti-TNF: etanercept/Enbrel, adalimumab/Humira, infliximab/Remicade, certolizumab/Cimzia; anti-IL-17A: secukinumab/Cosentyx, ixekizumab/Taltz; anti-IL-17R: brodalumab/Siliq; anti-IL-23: guselkumab/Tremfya, risankizumab/Skyrizi, tildrakizumab/Ilumya; anti-IL-12/23: ustekinumab/Stelara). ## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive psoriasis — an immune axis (IL-23/Th17/IL-17) stuck "on", a leaky gut priming it, a fatty inflamed liver amplifying it, and a vitamin-D-starved Treg system unable to apply the brake. Each gets its own intervention layer. *(Ogawa 2018; Bhatia 2015 gut-psoriasis; Gisondi 2017 NAFLD-psoriasis)*

### 2.2 IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17 hyperproliferation

> **For you, in plain words.** The immune system is stuck on the IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17 channel, telling skin cells to turn over every 3-5 days instead of every 28 days — so plaques and scale build up. Curcumin, Boswellia, Indigo naturalis, Nigella and Berberine all damp this channel. *(Lowes 2014 Th17 review; Lin 2018 Indigo Taiwan)*

#### Open the science

Plasmacytoid dendritic cells sense LL-37–DNA/RNA complexes, secrete IFN-α, prime conventional DCs to release IL-23 and IL-12, which polarise naive CD4+ T-cells into Th17 producing IL-17A, IL-17F and IL-22. IL-17 binds keratinocyte IL-17RA/RC receptors, driving S100A7/8/9, β-defensins, CCL20, CXCL1/8 and TNF-α — keratinocyte turnover collapses from 28 days to 3-5 days, producing the silver-scaled plaque. Targeted by curcumin (Vollono 2019), Boswellia AKBA (Pedretti 2010), *Indigo naturalis* topical (Lin 2018 Taiwan RCT — Grade B for plaque psoriasis), EGCG, Berberine, Nigella sativa, *Andrographis paniculata*; topical 0.5% Indigo naturalis ointment and 1% Capsaicin cream (Bernstein 1986 capsaicin-psoriasis). **Synthesis.** Reducing Th17 tone is the upstream brake on plaque turnover. *(Ogawa 2018; Lin 2018)*

### 2.3 Gut-skin axis (gliadin → zonulin → LPS → Th17)

> **For you, in plain words.** Psoriasis starts in the gut for many — gliadin from wheat opens tight junctions, LPS from bacteria translocates, and the immune system gets primed for Th17 in the mesenteric lymph nodes. Severity tracks intestinal permeability. *(Bhatia 2015; Sikora 2019 gut-psoriasis)*

#### Open the science

Psoriatic cohorts show elevated zonulin, anti-gliadin antibodies in a sub-fraction, increased intestinal permeability and altered microbiota (depleted *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Bifidobacterium*; expanded Proteobacteria and *Salmonella enterica* lineage signatures). LPS translocation activates TLR4 on mesenteric DCs, biasing Th17 over Treg. Severity correlates with intestinal-permeability biomarkers and with comorbid IBD (Crohn's / UC overlap is up to 7x background). Targeted by gluten elimination (Wolters 2005 Italian sub-cohort responsive); zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, DGL, slippery elm, marshmallow root, fermented foods; *Akkermansia*-supportive polyphenols (pomegranate, cranberry, green tea); multi-strain probiotics with *L. plantarum* HEAL19 and *Bifidobacterium infantis* 35624. **Synthesis.** No topical or supplement layer holds if gluten and seed-oil intake keep the gut gate open. *(Sikora 2019; Wolters 2005)*

### 2.4 Metabolic-hepatic loop (NAFLD + insulin resistance)

> **For you, in plain words.** 50-70% of psoriasis patients have metabolic syndrome and many have non-alcoholic fatty liver (NAFLD/MASLD). The fatty liver and visceral fat produce inflammatory signals that amplify Th17 and keratinocyte plaque. Berberine, milk thistle, intermittent fasting and Zone 2 cardio target this loop. *(Gisondi 2017; Mantovani 2021 NAFLD-psoriasis)*

#### Open the science

50-70% of psoriasis cohorts meet metabolic syndrome criteria; NAFLD/MASLD prevalence reaches ~50% in moderate-severe disease. Visceral adipose tissue secretes leptin, resistin, IL-6, TNF-α and lowers adiponectin — all amplifying Th17 and keratinocyte inflammation. Hepatic steatosis couples to skin via shared inflammatory cytokines and shared CYP-substrate kinetics that complicate methotrexate use. Targeted by Berberine (Yin 2008 metformin-equivalent for metabolic syndrome), milk thistle silymarin (EMA HMPC monograph 2018), NAC, choline, *Picrorhiza kurroa* (Kutki — Charaka liver hepatic-protectant), sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millet rotation, optional 14:10 or 16:8 intermittent fasting (NOT in hypothyroid or HPA-depleted users), Zone 2 cardio, strength training. **Synthesis.** Without metabolic-hepatic repair, plaque recurs after any other lever is pulled. *(Gisondi 2017; Yin 2008 berberine; EMA HMPC silymarin 2018)*

### 2.5 Vitamin-D / Treg deficit + HPA stress

> **For you, in plain words.** Most psoriasis patients are vitamin-D-starved — and without enough vitamin D the FoxP3 Treg "brake cells" cannot suppress the Th17 surge. Add chronic stress and the brake gets weaker still. Vitamin D3 + K2 + magnesium + Ashwagandha + pranayama target this layer. *(Romanì 2018 vit D-psoriasis; Choudhary 2017 KSM-66)*

#### Open the science

Psoriasis cohorts cluster at 25-OH-D <30 ng/mL; vitamin D is required for FoxP3+ Treg differentiation that counter-balances Th17. Chronic stress (the Koebner trigger acknowledged in Charaka and confirmed in modern cohorts) elevates cortisol acutely, then dysregulates HPA, suppresses Tregs and erodes the skin barrier — the "stress-plaque-stress" loop. Vagal tone (HRV-rMSSD) is reduced in moderate-severe psoriasis. Targeted by vitamin D3 + K2 (titrated to 60-80 ng/mL), magnesium glycinate (cofactor), Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa monnieri*, L-theanine, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, consumer VNS, MBSR (Kabat-Zinn 1998 small RCT in psoriasis — meditation accelerated UV clearance). **Synthesis.** Without vitamin D / Treg reconstitution and cortisol normalisation, the other three nodes regress under life stress. *(Romanì 2018; Kabat-Zinn 1998)*

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Eleven over-the-counter molecules with named trial or formal monograph backing. Vitamin D3+K2, omega-3 fish oil and topical Indigo naturalis are the highest-yield three. Curcumin, Berberine, Boswellia, Nigella, EGCG and milk thistle add depth; gotu kola and aloe vera are topical adjuncts. **Fish oil for psoriasis** — honestly mixed evidence per Maurice 1987 — kept as Grade B. *(Maurice 1987 fish oil; Camargo 2014 vit D; Lin 2018 Indigo)*

### 3.2 The fish-oil honest caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Fish oil is widely sold as "the psoriasis oil" — but the evidence is honestly mixed. The Maurice 1987 trial showed benefit; later trials are heterogeneous. We keep it as Grade B because the omega-3 ratio is important for the metabolic and CV comorbidity story, but it is NOT the cornerstone. *(Maurice 1987; Cochrane reviews mixed)*

#### Open the science

Maurice 1987 (n=28 placebo-controlled) showed modest PASI improvement with 1.8 g EPA + 1.2 g DHA. Subsequent trials are mixed; meta-analyses show small effect sizes for psoriasis-specific outcomes. The omega-3 ratio matters for the metabolic-CV comorbidity layer in §2.4 even if the direct skin effect is modest. **Grade B** (not Grade A) — keep in protocol as a comorbidity-anchored daily, not as a primary plaque-clearer. *(Maurice 1987; Søyland 1993 EFA review)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Seven home-only options — (1) controlled AM sun (the highest-yield free intervention — UVB cleared psoriasis before any drug existed), (2) home narrowband UVB hand-held devices (Daavlin Levia/SolRx — Cochrane B+ for plaque BUT requires careful dosimetry), (3) Dead Sea salts bath (Israeli balneology RCTs), (4) home red-light + NIR PBM panels (limited psoriasis-specific RCT but plausible adjunct), (5) topical occlusion wraps post-cream, (6) home infrared sauna (stable phase only), (7) coal-tar shampoo for scalp. **NO clinic NB-UVB cabin, NO PUVA, NO 308 nm excimer, NO hospital infusion.** *(Bath-Hextall Cochrane phototherapy; Adler-Cohen 2012 Dead Sea)*

### 5.2 Controlled AM sun (the home NB-UVB substitute)

> **For you, in plain words.** Direct sunlight is the highest-yield free intervention for plaque psoriasis — UVB cleared psoriasis before any drug existed. Fitzpatrick I 5-10 min, II 10-15, III 15-20, IV 20-25, V 30, VI 30-40 min. Windows 09:00-11:00 AM and 15:30-17:00 PM. Avoid summer solar noon. Skip during acute flare; eye protection mandatory. *(Halpern 2000 UVB-psoriasis; AYUSH)*

### 5.3 Home narrowband UVB hand-held devices

> **For you, in plain words.** Daavlin Levia, SolRx, and similar 311-nm narrowband UVB hand-held units are consumer-purchasable. The Cochrane review supports NB-UVB for plaque psoriasis (Grade B+), and home use under careful dosimetry can substitute the clinic cabin — BUT careful dosing matters (start at 100-200 mJ/cm², slow ramp). Eye shield, no facial / genital exposure without prescriber-level guidance. **This is the most powerful home device for psoriasis but the most easy to mis-dose.** *(Bath-Hextall Cochrane phototherapy review; Koek 2009 home NB-UVB Netherlands)*

Devices: Daavlin Levia hand-held, SolRx 100/500 series, Philips BlueControl, Levia M-100. Cost: $400-1500.

### 5.4 Dead Sea bath salts (Israeli balneology)

> **For you, in plain words.** Israeli (Tel Aviv University) balneology data — magnesium chloride + bromide + potassium 250-500 g per bath, 20 min, 2-3 times a week. PASI reduction equivalent to mild topical regimens. **Climatotherapy at the Dead Sea itself is the gold-standard climatotherapy** but home-bath version is honest second-best. *(Adler-Cohen 2012 Dead Sea; Halevy 2001 Dead Sea climatotherapy)*

Brands: Premier Dead Sea, Westlab, Salt of the Earth, Mineral Cosmetics Israel.

### 5.5 Red-light PBM panel (stable phase only)

> **For you, in plain words.** 660 + 810-850 nm panel, 8-15 J/cm² per session, 10-15 min, 4-5 times a week. **Psoriasis-specific RCT evidence is limited** (Avci 2013 PBM-skin review covers general; Ablon 2018 psoriasis-PBM pilot positive). Skip during acute weeping or pustular flare. *(Avci 2013; Ablon 2018)*

Devices: MitoMID ($449), MitoMAX ($749), Joov Solo/Elite, Hooga HG500. Eye shield mandatory.

### 5.6 Home occlusion wraps post-cream

> **For you, in plain words.** Apply emollient + topical (Indigo naturalis 0.5%, Mahamarichyadi Taila, 777 Oil) to plaques, then wrap with damp cotton tubular bandages (Tubifast) under dry layer for 1-2 h or overnight. Substantially accelerates topical penetration. *(Volden 2001 occlusion-psoriasis; Devillers 2002 wet-wrap framework)*

### 5.7 Coal-tar shampoo + scalp wash

> **For you, in plain words.** Coal-tar shampoo (Neutrogena T/Gel 0.5% coal tar; PolyTar; Sebcur-T) is OTC, has decades of evidence for scalp psoriasis (Heydendael 2003), and works. Use 2-3 times a week. Smells. Stains. Works. *(Heydendael 2003 coal tar)*

### 5.8 Home infrared sauna (stable phase only)

> **For you, in plain words.** Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten, JNH, Clearlight) at 45-55°C, 15-20 min, 2 times a week, with cool rinse and re-moisturise within 3 min — **only in stable phase, NEVER during pustular or erythrodermic flare**. *(Beever 2009 IR sauna review)*

#### Open the science

The Sbidian 2021 Cochrane network meta-analysis (n=140 trials, 51,749 patients) ranks IL-23 biologics (risankizumab, guselkumab) and IL-17 biologics (ixekizumab, secukinumab) as the most effective at achieving PASI 90, with IL-23 class showing slight efficacy edge plus the cleanest long-term safety profile. Methotrexate remains the gold-standard small-molecule cornerstone (CHAMPION). Apremilast and deucravacitinib are oral alternatives. Clinic NB-UVB confirmed effective per Cochrane. Regulatory milestones (FDA approval dates: secukinumab 2015, ixekizumab 2016, guselkumab 2017, risankizumab 2019, deucravacitinib 2022) appear here as regulatory-path context only, NEVER as the trust anchor — the trust anchor is the named clinical trial. **Doctrine: all of the above are clinic-route options the user may sovereign-decide to pursue if the home protocol plateaus after 6 months. The protocol does not recommend any of them; it documents them honestly.**

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.2 Self-administered PASI and BSA

> **For you, in plain words.** PASI scores four body regions (head, trunk, upper limbs, lower limbs) on erythema + thickness + scale + area (0-72). BSA palm-method: one palm = 1% BSA. Both fully self-administrable. *(Fredriksson 1978 PASI; Finlay 1994 DLQI)*

**Procedure (every 4 weeks):** standardise lighting, distance, pose. Score PASI for each region: erythema 0-4, induration 0-4, desquamation 0-4, area 0-6, weighted by region (head 0.1, trunk 0.3, upper limb 0.2, lower limb 0.4). Total 0-72.

**Adjunct self-scoring:** **DLQI** quarterly (10-item quality of life); **PEST** quarterly (psoriatic arthritis 5-item screen); **PHQ-9** quarterly (depression 2-3x risk).

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Psoriasis has four causes — an immune axis stuck "on" (IL-23/Th17/IL-17), a leaky gut feeding it, a fatty inflamed liver amplifying it, and a vitamin-D-starved Treg system unable to brake it. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home; **smoking cessation + alcohol cut + gluten removal + topical Indigo naturalis + vitamin D + AM sun is the fastest visible 12-week move**. *(Naldi 2005; Lin 2018; Romanì 2018; Sbidian 2021)*

Psoriasis is not "just a skin condition". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) your immune system is stuck on the IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17 channel, telling skin cells to turn over every 3-5 days instead of every 28 days, so plaques and scale build up; (2) your gut wall has gaps that let gliadin from wheat and bacterial LPS prime the Th17 channel from the mesenteric lymph nodes; (3) your liver is often fatty (NAFLD/MASLD in up to half of moderate-severe patients) and produces inflammatory signals that amplify the loop; (4) your vitamin D is usually low — and without enough vitamin D the Treg "brake cells" cannot suppress the Th17 surge.

This protocol does **not** start with serial topical corticosteroids, methotrexate, cyclosporine, apremilast, deucravacitinib, a biologic injection, or a JAK inhibitor. Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers — starting with **smoking cessation, alcohol cut, gluten + ultra-processed elimination, vitamin D3 6000 IU, topical Indigo naturalis 0.5%, AM controlled sun, and home NB-UVB hand-held device (Daavlin Levia) with careful dosimetry**, then OTC supplements (omega-3, curcumin, Berberine, Boswellia, Nigella, EGCG, milk thistle, probiotic), then AYUSH + TCM internal stack (Khadirarishta + Manjishtadi + Liang Xue Si Wu Tang), then Mediterranean diet + weight loss for moderate-severe, then home red light + Dead Sea bath + sleep + breathwork + MBSR (which has a small but real RCT in psoriasis per Kabat-Zinn 1998). **No biologic. No clinic NB-UVB cabin. No PUVA. No 308 nm excimer. No methotrexate. No JAK inhibitor. No hospital admission.**

Expect a slow, steady re-balancing. **Smoking cessation alone is the single highest-leverage behavioural move** — Naldi 2005 showed clear dose-response. Around Week 12 your PASI often drops 40-50% for moderate plaque. Severe plaque psoriasis is a 4-6 month story. **If your psoriasis is over 90% body surface (erythrodermic), generalised pustular, palmoplantar pustular, or you have psoriatic arthritis joint disease** — stabilise with your existing therapy first; this protocol layers in slowly only once stable, and PsA joint disease genuinely warrants specialist evaluation.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a dermatologist or rheumatologist informed, that is your personal decision. The protocol does not require it. For erythrodermic / pustular / PsA-joint presentations, the protocol acknowledges those genuinely benefit from specialist evaluation.
