## 1. Executive Summary

Psoriasis is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" diagnosis treated with topical corticosteroids, vitamin-D analogues, clinic NB-UVB cabins, methotrexate, cyclosporine, apremilast and an expanding biologic suite (anti-TNF, anti-IL-17, anti-IL-23). The integrative reframing is different: psoriasis is the **skin presentation of a systemic IL-23 / Th17 axis stuck "on" against a leaky gut barrier and a fatty, glycated liver**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17A/F → keratinocyte hyperproliferation, (ii) gut-skin axis with gliadin- and LPS-driven priming of Th17 in mesenteric lymph nodes, (iii) metabolic-inflammatory loop (NAFLD + insulin resistance + visceral adiposity feeding adipokine inflammation, low adiponectin, high leptin/resistin), and (iv) HPA / autonomic dysregulation with vitamin D / Treg insufficiency. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously dampens IL-23/Th17, seals the gut, reverses metabolic-hepatic inflammation and restores vitamin-D-driven Treg tolerance addresses the upstream drivers — not the cosmetic plaque.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17A/F keratinocyte hyperproliferation.** Plasmacytoid dendritic cells sense LL-37–DNA/RNA complexes, secrete IFN-α, prime conventional DCs to release IL-23 and IL-12, which polarise naive CD4⁺ T-cells into Th17 producing IL-17A, IL-17F and IL-22. IL-17 binds keratinocyte IL-17RA/RC, drives S100A7/8/9, β-defensins, CCL20, CXCL1/8 and TNF-α — keratinocyte turnover collapses from 28 days to 3–5 days, producing the silver-scaled plaque. Targeted by curcumin, Boswellia AKBA, *Indigo naturalis* (Qing Dai), EGCG, Berberine, Nigella, Andrographis paniculatae; topical 0.5% Indigo naturalis ointment and 1% Capsaicin cream. **Synthesis:** Reducing Th17 tone is the upstream brake on plaque turnover.

**2.2 Gut-skin axis: gliadin → zonulin → LPS → Th17 priming.** Psoriatic cohorts show elevated zonulin, anti-gliadin antibodies (in a sub-fraction), increased intestinal permeability and altered microbiota (depleted *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Bifidobacterium*; expanded Proteobacteria, *Salmonella enterica* lineage signatures). LPS translocation activates TLR4 on mesenteric DCs, biasing Th17 over Treg. Severity correlates with intestinal-permeability biomarkers and with comorbid IBD (Crohn's / UC). Targeted by gluten elimination, zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, DGL, slippery elm, marshmallow root, fermented foods, *Akkermansia*-supportive polyphenols (pomegranate, cranberry), and multi-strain probiotics. **Synthesis:** No layer holds if gluten + seed-oil intake keeps the gut gate open.

**2.3 Metabolic-inflammatory loop (NAFLD + insulin resistance + visceral adiposity).** 50–70% of psoriasis cohorts meet metabolic syndrome criteria. Visceral adipose tissue secretes leptin, resistin, IL-6, TNF-α and lowers adiponectin — all amplify Th17 and keratinocyte inflammation. Hepatic steatosis (NAFLD/MASLD prevalence ~50% in moderate-severe psoriasis) couples to skin via shared inflammatory cytokines and shared CYP-substrate kinetics that complicate methotrexate use. Targeted by Berberine, Milk Thistle, NAC, Choline, *Picrorhiza kurroa*, sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets, intermittent fasting (if appropriate), Zone 2 cardio, strength training. **Synthesis:** Without metabolic-hepatic repair, plaque recurs after any other lever is pulled.

**2.4 Vitamin-D / Treg deficiency + HPA hyperactivity.** Psoriasis cohorts cluster at 25-OH-D <30 ng/mL; vitamin D is required for FoxP3⁺ Treg differentiation that counter-balances Th17. Chronic stress (Koebner trigger acknowledged in Charaka and modern cohorts) elevates cortisol acutely, then dysregulates HPA, suppresses Tregs and erodes the skin barrier. Targeted by Vitamin D3 + K2 (titrated to 60–80 ng/mL), Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa monnieri*, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, cold immersion. **Synthesis:** Without Vitamin D / Treg reconstitution and cortisol normalisation, the other three nodes regress under life stress.

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers to eliminate

- **Smoking and vaping** — strongest documented modifiable trigger in European psoriasis cohorts (dose-dependent severity). Cessation is the single highest-yield environmental intervention.
- **Alcohol** — direct trigger; beer-and-whiskey association strongest in male cohorts.
- **Streptococcal carriage** (guttate sub-type) — DTC throat swab (Everlywell strep test); chronic tonsillar carriage warrants gargle with **warm salt + turmeric + neem twice daily**.
- **Topical irritants** — sodium lauryl sulphate (SLS) shampoos and body washes, alcohol-based hand sanitisers (frequent use damages barrier), fragrance allergens. Swap to **fragrance-free, SLS-free, paraben-free** (Vanicream, Cetaphil, Eucerin, Klorane, Avalon Organics).
- **Mechanical Koebner triggers** — tight waistbands at belt-line, watch straps, jewellery clasps; avoid scratching, rough loofahs, harsh exfoliation.
- **Heavy-metal exposure** — occupational solvent (mechanic, painter), pesticide (farm worker) exposure correlates with severity. DTC heavy-metal urine panel (Doctor's Data) baseline if suspicion.
- **Indoor air** — VOCs/formaldehyde from new furniture (off-gas 2 weeks + HEPA + activated carbon). Mould → ERMI/HERTSMI-2 test (Mycometrics, EnviroBiomics) if recurrent flare correlates with home environment.
- **Hard water (chlorine + chloramine + minerals)** — install **shower filter** (Berkey, Aquasana, Sprite) — reduces transepidermal water loss and chemical irritation.
- **Sunscreen chemistry** — swap oxybenzone/octinoxate for **non-nano zinc oxide / titanium dioxide** mineral sunscreen (Badger, Thinkbaby, Suntribe).
- **Stress + sleep deprivation** — chronic <6 h sleep documented to increase plaque severity in Korean and Italian cohorts.
- **Lithium, β-blockers, antimalarials, IFN-α, terbinafine, ACE inhibitors** — known pharmacologic psoriasis triggers in users who may be on them; documented in §10.1 with the gap rule, not a "stop your meds" instruction.

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.0 Self-administered clinical scoring instrument

**PASI (Psoriasis Area and Severity Index)** — the standard quantitative measure, self-administrable at home with photo standardisation.

**Procedure (every 4 weeks):** standardise lighting (5000 K LED or north window, 08:00), distance (50 cm close-ups, 1.5 m whole-body), pose (5 fixed views — face front/left/right, anterior + posterior trunk, hands palm-up/down, feet, scalp parted, nail close-ups). Mid-grey background. Save JPEG + RAW to encrypted cloud (Proton Drive, iCloud E2E). Compute PASI: 4 body regions (head, upper limbs, trunk, lower limbs) × area% (0–6) × (erythema + induration + scale, each 0–4) × regional weighting; total 0–72.

**Adjunct self-scoring:**
- **BSA (Body Surface Area)** by palm method (1 palm = 1% BSA) — quick weekly self-track
- **DLQI** (Dermatology Life Quality Index, 10 items) baseline + Mo3/6/12 — UK/European-developed, non-US scale
- **PGA (Physician Global Assessment) — self-rated version** 0 (clear) to 5 (severe) baseline + Mo3/6/12
- **PsAID-12** (Psoriatic Arthritis Impact of Disease, EULAR) if PsA overlap — quarterly
- **PHQ-9** quarterly (psoriasis ↑ depression 2–3×); **GAD-7** quarterly
- **PSQI** (Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index) baseline + Mo3/6/12

**Apps:** DermAssist (Google), TrueDerm, Imagine (psoriasis-specific tracker by King's College London — non-US-affiliated).

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Psoriasis is not "just bad skin". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) a particular immune-signalling axis (IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17) is stuck "on" and tells your skin cells to multiply seven to ten times faster than normal, which is why the plaque builds up silver scale; (2) your gut wall has small gaps that let food fragments and bacterial toxins into your blood, which keeps that immune axis on hair-trigger; (3) a quiet metabolic fire is burning — insulin resistance, fatty liver, and visceral belly-fat all secrete inflammation that feeds the skin plaque; and (4) you are almost certainly low in vitamin D and high in cortisol, both of which weaken the immune brakes (Tregs) that should be turning the Th17 axis off.

This protocol does **not** try to silence one cytokine with a biologic, suppress your whole immune system with methotrexate, or sit you in a clinic light-cabin. Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the outside in — with anti-inflammatory foods, sprouted millets, fermented foods, bone broth, olive oil, wild fish, OTC botanicals (curcumin, Boswellia, Berberine, Indigo naturalis, Picrorhiza), home sunlight in safe windows, a home red-light panel, infrared sauna, cold-water immersion, Dead Sea bath salts, breathwork, yoga and sleep. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.** Everything you need fits in a kitchen, a bedroom and a yoga mat.

Expect a slow, steady re-balancing — not an overnight switch. The first thing you will notice (Weeks 4–6) is that spreading stops and the scale thins. Around Week 12 you may see plaques fade in colour and shrink at the edges. Real PASI 75 (75% improvement) is a 4-to-6-month story for plaque sub-type; PASI 90 is realistic at 9–12 months for adherent users. Nails and palms/soles are always slowest — give them a year.
