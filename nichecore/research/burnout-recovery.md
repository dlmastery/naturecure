## 1. Executive Summary

Burnout — recognised by WHO ICD-11 as a chronic workplace stress syndrome with three dimensions (exhaustion, cynicism/depersonalisation, reduced professional efficacy) — is conventionally siloed under "depression" and treated with SSRIs, time off, or an executive coach. The integrative reframing is different: burnout is the **physiological end-state of prolonged HPA-axis overdrive transitioning into HPA hypoactivity, paired with mitochondrial bioenergetic decline, neurotransmitter depletion, and identity-meaning collapse**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) HPA-axis transition from Stage 1 hyperactivity → Stage 2 flattening → Stage 3 hypoactivity with severely depleted total cortisol output and low DHEA-S, (ii) mitochondrial bioenergetic decline with reduced ATP, elevated lactate, impaired Complex I/IV activity, and oxidative damage, (iii) monoamine neurotransmitter depletion (dopamine, norepinephrine, serotonin) with reduced BDNF, and (iv) identity-meaning erosion (cynicism, disengagement, loss of professional self-efficacy) requiring behavioural-cognitive-relational repair, not pharmacology. **Synthesis:** An OTC, home-based protocol that restores HPA reserve, rebuilds mitochondria, replenishes monoamines/BDNF, and rebuilds meaning addresses the upstream drivers — not the symptom layer.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 HPA-axis transition from hyperactivity to hypoactivity.** Months-to-years of cortisol overproduction lead to glucocorticoid receptor desensitisation (FKBP5 upregulation), CRH receptor downregulation, eventual ACTH-cortisol output drop, and a collapsed DUTCH cortisol curve. Subjective: morning cannot-get-out-of-bed exhaustion, low resilience to additional stress, salt cravings, dizziness on standing, mid-afternoon energy crash, feeling "wired-and-tired" early then just "depleted". Key actors: CRH, ACTH, cortisol, GR/NR3C1, FKBP5, 11β-HSD1/2, DHEA-S. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66 (HPA-normalising RCTs), Rhodiola, *Eleutherococcus*, Tulsi, Bacopa, phosphatidylserine, small-dose DGL liquorice (with home BP monitor), DHEA / 7-keto-DHEA after baseline measurement, restorative sleep + non-negotiable rest, gentle morning sun + salt water. **Synthesis:** HPA reserve must be slowly rebuilt — adaptogens *normalise* rather than push.

**2.2 Mitochondrial bioenergetic decline.** Chronic cortisol + chronic inflammation degrade mitochondrial function: Complex I and IV activity drop, ATP production falls, lactate rises, ROS increase, mitochondrial DNA damage accumulates, mitophagy lags. Subjective: physical heaviness, exercise-induced post-exertional malaise (PEM-like), unrefreshing sleep, brain fog. Key actors: Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase), Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase), ATP synthase, NAD+, CoQ10, carnitine, α-lipoic acid, magnesium, PGC-1α. Targeted by CoQ10/ubiquinol, NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR), acetyl-L-carnitine, α-lipoic acid, magnesium glycinate, D-ribose, creatine, B-complex (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B12), red light photobiomodulation (660 + 850 nm), Zone 2 cardio rebuild. **Synthesis:** Mitochondria are the substrate for energy; without rebuilding them, adaptogens have nothing to act on.

**2.3 Monoamine + BDNF depletion.** Chronic stress depletes dopamine (reward, motivation), norepinephrine (alertness), serotonin (mood, well-being), and reduces BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor). Subjective: anhedonia, loss of motivation, low mood, social withdrawal. Key actors: tyrosine hydroxylase, DOPA decarboxylase, tryptophan hydroxylase, MAO-A/B, BDNF, TrkB. Targeted by **L-tyrosine** (dopamine precursor; only if not on SSRI/MAOI), **saffron** (*Crocus sativus*), **L-theanine**, **rhodiola** (MAO modulation), **Bacopa** (serotonin/BDNF), **Lion's Mane** (Hericium erinaceus, BDNF/NGF), **omega-3 EPA** (BDNF), aerobic exercise (BDNF), sun + nature exposure, social connection. **Synthesis:** Rebuilding monoamines + BDNF restores the neural substrate for motivation.

**2.4 Identity-meaning erosion / cynicism-disengagement.** The hallmark of burnout (vs simple stress) is the cynicism + reduced professional efficacy dimensions — these are *behavioural-cognitive-relational*, not biochemical. Persistent cortisol elevation amygdala-primes negativity bias; chronic exhaustion erodes self-efficacy beliefs; social withdrawal compounds. Targeted by **MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction, Jon Kabat-Zinn) 8-wk course**, **compassion-focused therapy (Paul Gilbert, UK)**, **acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT) self-guided**, **values clarification work** (Russ Harris ACT), **meaningful re-engagement** (gradual return to work in a renegotiated role), **social co-regulation** (face-to-face time with trusted others), **structured sabbatical-style rest** (2–8 wk full disengagement where life permits), and creative-expressive practices (journaling, music, art, gardening). **Synthesis:** No supplement reverses cynicism — only meaning + relational repair + rest does.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Burnout is not "weakness" or "not being able to handle it" — it is a real, measurable, three-dimensional syndrome that the World Health Organization formally recognises (ICD-11). Three things are happening at once inside you: (1) you are deeply, physically exhausted because your stress hormones have been overdriven for months or years and your mitochondria — your cellular power plants — are running on empty; (2) you have started to feel cynical, numb, or disconnected from the work that used to matter; and (3) you no longer believe you are effective at what you do. Underneath those three: your HPA axis (hypothalamus → pituitary → adrenal) is collapsed, your DHEA-S (resilience hormone) is depleted, your gut barrier has eroded, and your brain's BDNF (the molecule that keeps neurons plastic) has dropped.

This protocol does **not** rely on an SSRI to "lift the mood". Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the outside in — with **adaptogens** (Ashwagandha, Rhodiola, Bacopa, Tulsi) that nudge cortisol back to a healthy rhythm; **mitochondrial cofactors** (CoQ10/Ubiquinol, ALCAR, magnesium, B-vitamins, creatine, NAD+ precursors); **BDNF-supporting foods and supplements** (omega-3, Lion's Mane, saffron, berries, aerobic walking, sunlight); **phosphatidylserine** that quiets the evening cortisol tail; **vagal-nerve practices** (humming, gargling, cold splash, resonance-frequency breathing, optional Nurosym or Sensate); **deep structured rest** (a sabbatical or even just 2 weeks of full disconnection if life permits); **meaning work** (MBSR, ACT values clarification, social co-regulation, gradual renegotiated re-engagement); and **stage-appropriate exercise** (no HIIT in Stage 2/3 — rest is the medicine). **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.** Everything fits in a kitchen, a bedroom and a yoga mat.

Expect a slow, steady re-balancing — not an overnight fix. Stage 1 acute burnout typically recovers in 3–6 months; Stage 2 (with cynicism) in 6–9 months; Stage 3 (full HPA exhaustion) takes 9–18 months. The hardest single lesson: **rest is not laziness; it is the medicine.** The biggest single lever: a 2–4 week structured disengagement at the start, if life permits.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a physician informed, that is your personal decision. The protocol does not require it.
