## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What burnout actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Burnout is not weakness, and it's not the same as depression. Maslach defined a specific triad — exhaustion (you have nothing left), cynicism (you can't bring yourself to care), reduced efficacy (you can't make things happen). It is a recovery-debt syndrome. *(Maslach MBI; West 2014)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: phone in another room + screens off 21:00 + slow paced breathing 10 min + true day-off planned within 7 days. This week: take the Maslach MBI self-quiz + audit your calendar for energy-leak meetings + book a single 90-min ultradian-respect block per day + start ashwagandha KSM-66 300 mg twice daily. *(Sonnentag 2012; Chandrasekhar 2012)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story treats burnout as a personal-resilience failure to be fixed with a wellness app, or it pathologises it into "depression" requiring an SSRI. A more honest reframe (Maslach + Sonnentag + Demerouti): burnout is the output of (i) **chronic effort-recovery mismatch** where work demand exceeds replenishment capacity month after month, (ii) **HPA-axis Stage-2 flattening or Stage-3 depletion**, (iii) **loss of psychological detachment** (work intrusions into off-hours destroying recovery), and (iv) **collapsed job-resource scaffolding** (autonomy + meaning + social-connection + fair workload + recognition all eroded simultaneously). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs MBSR + CBT-burnout (B+ grade) with Sonnentag recovery-experience training, adaptogen HPA support, sleep + nutrient rebuild, AND **deliberate work-context redesign** (calendar audit, ultradian-respect 90-min blocks, true off-hours detachment) addresses all four drivers. Supplements alone will not fix burnout.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive burnout — effort-recovery mismatch, HPA flattening or depletion, lost psychological detachment, and collapsed job resources. Naming each lets every intervention aim at the right one. *(Demerouti JD-R model; Sonnentag 2012)*

### 2.2 Effort-recovery mismatch

> **For you, in plain words.** Meijman & Mulder's classical model: every effort spent must be recovered. If you spend more than you replace day after day, the deficit becomes burnout. Sleep, micro-recovery breaks, weekends and true holidays are the replacement supply. *(Meijman 1998 effort-recovery; Sonnentag 2012)*

#### Open the science

Effort-Recovery (E-R) model: any sustained work demand draws on physiological and psychological resources. Recovery requires functional time AWAY from the demand. Allostatic load (McEwen) accumulates when recovery is chronically inadequate. Demerouti's Job Demands-Resources model maps demands (workload, emotional load, role conflict, work-family conflict) against resources (autonomy, feedback, social support, growth opportunity). Burnout emerges when demands chronically exceed resources AND recovery opportunities are lost. Targeted by **Sonnentag's four recovery experiences** (psychological detachment, relaxation, mastery, control over leisure time), **mandatory true days off**, **ultradian work-rest blocks** (90-min focus + 20-min real break), **sabbaticals where feasible** (sectoral/government schemes), **annual leave actually taken**. **Synthesis.** This is the upstream lever — without recovery restoration, every other intervention leaks.

### 2.3 HPA Stage-2 flattening or Stage-3 depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** Months of chronic stress flatten or eventually deplete the cortisol curve. You wake tired, push through on caffeine, crash in the evening but can't sleep. Ashwagandha is the strongest restorative we have. *(Lopresti 2019 meta; Lennartsson 2013)*

#### Open the science

Chronic burnout populations show two patterns on DUTCH/ZRT cortisol testing: **Stage 2** flattened diurnal slope (Lennartsson 2013 — Swedish burnout cohort), and a smaller **Stage 3** with low total cortisol output. DHEA-S declines reliably (Lennartsson 2013). HRV (RMSSD) is consistently reduced. Targeted by **ashwagandha KSM-66** (Chandrasekhar 2012, Lopresti 2019), **rhodiola** (Panossian 2010 — particularly the stress-with-fatigue burnout overlap), **holy basil/Tulsi** (Cohen 2014), **eleuthero** (EMA HMPC), **Schisandra Wu Wei Zi** (TCM adaptogen — emerging data), **phosphatidylserine** for AM-racing-but-fatigued sub-type, **sleep restoration before everything else**. **Synthesis.** Adaptogens normalise the curve — they pull a high curve down and lift a flat curve up.

### 2.4 Lost psychological detachment

> **For you, in plain words.** Sonnentag's research is unambiguous — if you can't mentally disconnect from work in your off-hours, you don't recover, even if you sleep enough hours. Phone-in-another-room and a firm work-end-ritual restore detachment. *(Sonnentag 2012; Etzion 1998)*

#### Open the science

Sonnentag's recovery-experience research (2005-2020) identified four experiences that drive functional recovery: **psychological detachment** (mental disengagement from work), **relaxation** (low-arousal pleasant activities), **mastery** (challenging non-work activities — language learning, music, sport), **control** (autonomous choice over leisure time). Of these, **detachment is the single highest-leverage** — its absence predicts burnout independent of hours worked. Targeted by **phone-in-another-room** after work-end ritual, **no work email at home**, **single work-end ritual** (walk home from office in past, walk-around-the-block now if working from home), **separate work device from personal**, **email batching** (twice daily, not continuous), **calendar protected blocks**, **explicit out-of-office norms with team**. **Synthesis.** Detachment is the cheapest highest-leverage intervention and the one most often skipped.

### 2.5 Collapsed job-resource scaffolding

> **For you, in plain words.** Burnout is not just about you — it's about whether your role gives you autonomy, meaning, fair workload, social connection and recognition. When all five erode, no amount of self-care will fix it. Job-crafting and role redesign matter. *(Demerouti JD-R; Wrzesniewski job-crafting)*

#### Open the science

Demerouti's Job Demands-Resources model: burnout emerges from chronic demand-resource imbalance. Six core resources predict burnout protection: **autonomy** (control over how work is done), **meaning** (sense of purpose), **fair workload** (not chronically overwhelming), **social support** (colleague + supervisor), **growth opportunity**, and **recognition** (effort acknowledged). When resources collapse, demand becomes toxic regardless of personal resilience. Targeted by **job-crafting** (Wrzesniewski 2001 — actively reshaping tasks, relationships, and meaning within current role), **explicit boundary setting** (declining low-value meetings, blocking deep-work time), **role-conversation with manager**, **considering role change or sabbatical**, **rebuilding social-connection** outside work, **mentor/therapist for the harder organisational pieces**. **Synthesis.** Personal protocols carry 60-70% of the work; structural redesign carries the rest. Pretending it's all personal is gaslighting.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Nine over-the-counter molecules support HPA recovery in burnout — but no supplement replaces sleep, detachment, and calendar audit. Read the plain-English column first. Notice the Cochrane caveat on rhodiola (positive Panossian meta but heterogeneous trials). *(Panossian 2010; Lopresti 2019)*

### 3.2 Ashwagandha + Rhodiola — the cornerstone pair

> **For you, in plain words.** Ashwagandha KSM-66 cuts cortisol ~28% (Lopresti 2019 meta) and supports sleep + DHEA-S. Rhodiola is the AM stress-with-fatigue herb (Panossian 2010 meta — B-grade, heterogeneous but consistent direction). Pair AM rhodiola + AM/PM ashwagandha for 8-12 weeks. *(Chandrasekhar 2012; Panossian 2010)*

#### Open the science

Withanolides modulate GABAergic + HPA signalling — Lopresti 2019 meta (5 RCTs) showed ~28% cortisol drop and consistent stress symptom reduction at 8-12 weeks. Specifically in burnout: Langade 2019 (n=80) showed ashwagandha 600 mg/day improved sleep onset, sleep quality and refreshed-on-waking score. Rhodiola: Panossian 2010 meta pooled 11 trials — directionally consistent for stress + mental/physical fatigue overlap, though trial heterogeneity is a Cochrane-style caveat. Best for the stress-with-fatigue burnout overlap; AM only. **Caution: ashwagandha pregnancy excluded; hyperthyroidism can raise T4. Rhodiola can be over-stimulating in bipolar diathesis.** **Grade A** for ashwagandha cortisol reduction; **Grade B** for rhodiola stress + fatigue.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Ayurveda — restoring ojas

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda calls burnout *ojas-kshaya* (loss of vital reserve). The fix is rasayanas (rejuvenatives) — ashwagandha cornerstone, Brahmi for the mind, Shatavari for women, Triphala for gut-stress axis. *(Charaka Samhita Rasayana; AYUSH CCRAS)*

#### Open the science

Per Charaka Samhita Sutrasthana Ch. 1 (Rasayana), depletion-pattern illness (*kshaya*) is treated by rebuilding the seven *dhatus* (tissues) and restoring *ojas* (the subtle essence of vitality). **Classical rasayana protocol:** Ashwagandha churna 3-6 g/day (with milk if tolerated; Bhavaprakasha), Brahmi 500 mg twice daily (medhya rasayana), Shatavari for hormonal-burnout women 1-3 g/day, Triphala 3 g night for gut-stress axis. **Classical formulas:** Chyawanprash 1 tsp morning (rasayana cornerstone), Ashwagandharishta 15 ml twice daily (fermented adaptogen tonic), Saraswatarishta 15 ml twice daily (mind-tonic), Manasamitra Vatakam (Sahasrayoga — anxiety/insomnia compound). **Shirodhara** (medicated oil pour) is the signature burnout treatment in Kerala panchakarma centres; **home Padabhyanga** (foot oil massage with Brahmi or Ksheerabala oil at bedtime) is the consumer-accessible analogue.

### 4.2 TCM — qi, spleen, kidney exhaustion

> **For you, in plain words.** TCM sees burnout as a depletion of qi, often starting with spleen-qi (digestive + cognitive energy), then kidney-yin (deep reserve). Four formulas cover most patterns: Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang, Gui Pi Tang, Liu Wei Di Huang Wan, Si Jun Zi Tang. *(Huang Di Nei Jing; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020)*

#### Open the science

**Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang (补中益气汤 Tonify the Middle and Augment the Qi)** — Astragalus + Ginseng + Atractylodes + Liquorice + Dong Quai + Citrus + Cimicifuga + Bupleurum — gold-standard for spleen-qi deficiency exhaustion; lifts collapsed energy. **Gui Pi Tang (归脾汤 Restore the Spleen)** — Ginseng + Astragalus + Atractylodes + Poria + Longan + Polygala + Saussurea + Suan Zao Ren + Dong Quai — Heart-Spleen tonification for worry-fatigue-poor-sleep burnout triad. **Liu Wei Di Huang Wan (六味地黄丸 Six-Ingredient Pill with Rehmannia)** — Rehmannia + Cornus + Dioscorea + Alisma + Poria + Moutan — kidney-yin foundation for late-stage burnout depletion. **Si Jun Zi Tang (四君子汤 Four Gentlemen)** — Ginseng + Atractylodes + Poria + Liquorice — foundational qi tonic. **Schisandra/Wu Wei Zi (五味子)** — TCM adaptogen for liver-kidney axis. Sourcing: Plum Flower, Mayway, Min Tong granules; Kamwo Meridian Herbs NYC, Lhasa Karnak SF.

- **Pastured eggs 2/day** — choline, B12
- **Slow carbs 80-120 g cooked** — sprouted millets, quinoa, oats
- **Fermented food 1 portion/day** — kefir, kanji, sauerkraut
