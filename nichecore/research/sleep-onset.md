## 1. Executive Summary

Sleep-onset insomnia is conventionally siloed as a "primary insomnia" diagnosis treated with Z-drug hypnotics (zolpidem), benzodiazepine receptor agonists, off-label sedating antidepressants and DORAs. The integrative reframing is different: delayed sleep onset is the **behavioural-circadian-neurochemical signature of a sympathetic nervous system that has not handed the baton to the parasympathetic at lights-out**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) GABAergic underdrive with relative glutamate dominance at the wake-sleep transition, (ii) HPA-axis hyperactivity with a high-amplitude evening cortisol tail crushing endogenous melatonin onset, (iii) circadian misalignment (delayed dim-light-melatonin-onset, DLMO) from late blue-light exposure and inadequate morning bright light, and (iv) cognitive-emotional hyperarousal (rumination, planning, anticipatory anxiety) keeping the default-mode network ignited past sleep latency. **Synthesis:** An OTC, home-based protocol that upregulates GABA, lowers evening cortisol, advances DLMO via light + temperature + behaviour, and quiets the default-mode network through pranayama + Yoga Nidra addresses the upstream drivers — not the sedation endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 GABAergic underdrive at the sleep-onset gate.** Sleep onset requires inhibitory GABAergic projections from the ventrolateral preoptic nucleus (VLPO) to silence the ascending arousal system (locus coeruleus, raphe, tuberomammillary). Underdrive at GABA-A receptors — exacerbated by caffeine adenosine antagonism, alcohol rebound, magnesium deficiency, and chronic stress — keeps the orexin/hypocretin wake-promoting system tonic. Key molecular actors: GABA-A α2/α3 subunit, glycine receptors, adenosine A2A, orexin/hypocretin. Targeted by glycine, magnesium glycinate, L-theanine, apigenin (chamomile/passionflower flavone), valerenic acid (*Valeriana officinalis*), lemon balm rosmarinic acid, lavender linalool. **Synthesis:** Reinforcing endogenous GABA tone at lights-out is the chemical brake on hyperarousal.

**2.2 HPA-axis hyperactivity with cortisol tail.** Healthy sleep onset requires cortisol to fall through a nadir around 23:00 while melatonin rises. Late-day stress, evening exercise, late heavy meals, and chronic HPA dysregulation flatten the cortisol decline, producing an "evening cortisol tail" that antagonises melatonin and keeps core body temperature elevated. Key actors: CRH-ACTH-cortisol axis, glucocorticoid receptor, SCN-pineal melatonin pathway. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa*, magnolia/honokiol, phosphatidylserine, evening pranayama, 4-7-8 breath, Yoga Nidra, warm-bath-to-cool-room thermoregulation. **Synthesis:** Without flattening the evening cortisol tail, melatonin cannot rise on time.

**2.3 Circadian misalignment / delayed DLMO.** The suprachiasmatic nucleus tracks blue-wavelength light via intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells (melanopsin / OPN4). Late-evening screen light (≥30 lux blue) suppresses melatonin onset by 60–90 min; inadequate morning bright light (<1000 lux within 30 min of waking) fails to set the phase. Late chronotype patients (DSPD) carry PER3 / CRY1 polymorphisms biasing them to a 1–3 h late DLMO. Targeted by morning sunlight 5–10 min within 30 min of waking, blue-blockers from sundown, dim warm light after 21:00, low-dose physiological melatonin (0.3 mg) timed 4–5 h before desired sleep, and consistent rise-time. **Synthesis:** Phase advance of the SCN is structural, not pharmacological.

**2.4 Cognitive-emotional hyperarousal / default-mode rumination.** Functional MRI shows persistent default-mode-network and salience-network activation in primary insomnia past sleep latency, with elevated beta EEG and reduced sleep-spindle coherence. The mind keeps "doing the day's work" past lights-out. Targeted by stimulus-control behavioural therapy (bed = sleep only), cognitive shuffle, Yoga Nidra (iRest, Bihar School), 4-7-8 breathwork, body-scan Vipassana, journaling-before-bed (worry parking-lot), and the homeopathic *Coffea cruda* / *Nux vomica* picture. **Synthesis:** Behavioural and somatic anchors override cortical override.
