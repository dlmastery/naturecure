## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What allergic + immune comfort is

> **For you, in plain words.** This dossier covers the cluster of allergy + general immune-fragility patterns — seasonal allergic rhinitis (SAR — pollen), perennial allergic rhinitis (PAR — dust mite, pet, mold), allergic asthma overlap, urticaria tendency, the atopic-march family, and the year-round immune-fragile pattern. Distinct from acute viral URI (sister dossier `respiratory-seasonal`) and from chronic asthma. *(Bousquet 2008 ARIA; Brożek 2017 ARIA update)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight + this week: **saline nasal irrigation 2×/day** (NeilMed Sinus Rinse, distilled water only); **HEPA air purifier in bedroom** running continuously (IQAir / Coway / Blueair / Levoit); **dust-mite encasement on mattress + pillows** (AllerEase, Mission Allergy); **second-generation OTC antihistamine** (Xyzal/levocetirizine 5 mg evening OR Allegra/fexofenadine 180 mg AM — non-sedating; AVOID first-generation Benadryl/diphenhydramine — anticholinergic); **OTC nasal steroid** (Flonase / Nasacort — daily during pollen season; not as-needed; takes 1–2 weeks for full effect); **vitamin D 2,000–5,000 IU + K2 + zinc 15 mg + omega-3 1 g daily floor**. Add **quercetin 500 mg 2×/day during high-pollen weeks** (mast-cell stabilizer), **butterbur Petadolex 75 mg 2×/day during season** (PA-free preparation only — Schapowal 2002 equivalent to cetirizine), **nettle 300 mg 2–3×/day during season** (Mittman 1990 small signal). Daily Anulom Vilom + Bhramari + nasal-breathing reinforcement. Pollen tracker app (Pollen.com / Klarify / BreezoMeter). *(Cochrane Hayward 2012; Schapowal 2002; Mlcek 2016; ARIA Bousquet 2008)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story prescribes a second-generation antihistamine + nasal steroid + (if severe) injectable biologic + (if uncontrolled) allergy shots. The natural-medicine reality is that **antihistamines + nasal steroids are honest, useful, OTC household tools** — there's no purity in suffering — but they don't address the underlying IgE-mediated mast-cell-driven Th2-skewed inflammatory state OR the environmental load OR the gut-immune axis. A more honest reframe: allergic + immune-comfort patterns have **five mechanistic drivers** — (1) **environmental trigger load** (pollen, dust mite, pet, mold, indoor air, occupational), (2) **Th2-skew + mast-cell activation** (quercetin, butterbur, nettle, vitamin D, omega-3), (3) **mucosal barrier integrity** (saline rinses, gut microbiome, vitamin A, zinc), (4) **HPA-immune coupling** (stress + sleep are immune levers), (5) **chronic low-grade systemic inflammation** (Mediterranean diet, omega-3, polyphenols). A home protocol that pairs environmental control + mast-cell stabilization + barrier support + stress + nutrition addresses all five WITHOUT panicking into immunosuppressive biologics for mild-moderate disease that responds to layered home work. **Synthesis.** Antihistamines and nasal steroids are honest tools, kept in the home; the upstream work is environment + diet + breath + sleep.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five drivers — environmental trigger load, Th2-skew + mast-cell activation, mucosal barrier integrity, HPA-immune coupling, chronic low-grade systemic inflammation. Each maps to specific home layers. *(Bousquet ARIA 2008; Galli 2008 mast cells; Romagnani Th2 2004)*

### 2.2 Environmental trigger load

> **For you, in plain words.** The single biggest non-pharmacological lever for allergy is *not breathing the allergen*. Dust-mite encasements + HEPA purifier + carpet removal + mold remediation + pollen-window-closing + pet-bedroom-out reduce trigger load directly. *(Wood 1998 dust-mite encasement; Allen 2015 HEPA; Calderón 2015 mite avoidance)*

#### Open the science

Allergen avoidance — for dust mite, encase mattress + pillows + duvet in mite-impermeable encasements (AllerEase, Mission Allergy, Protect-A-Bed); wash bedding weekly at 60°C; reduce carpet (especially bedroom); humidity <50% (mites need >55% to thrive); remove soft toys from bed (or freeze 24 h weekly). For cats/dogs: out of bedroom + frequent washing + HEPA + air purifier reduces but never eliminates antigen. For pollen: track via Pollen.com / Klarify / BreezoMeter; shower + change clothes on return home; keep windows closed during peak hours (typically 05:00–10:00); evening shower removes pollen from hair. For mold: remediate water damage; reduce humidity <50%; HEPA + dehumidifier; consider DTC mold test (ERMI). For occupational: review industrial exposures (isocyanates for asthma, organic solvents, animal dander, baker's flour, latex). HEPA air purifier — Allen 2015 + EAACI guidelines support B-grade signal for symptom reduction. CADR (clean air delivery rate) matched to room size matters. Brands: IQAir HealthPro, Coway Mighty, Blueair Blue Pure, Levoit Core. **Synthesis.** Avoidance is the single largest free non-pharmacological lever — do this first.

### 2.3 Th2-skew + mast-cell activation

> **For you, in plain words.** Allergy = Th2-skewed immune response + IgE on mast cells. When allergen binds, mast cells degranulate (histamine + tryptase + leukotrienes + cytokines), driving sneezing + itch + congestion + bronchoconstriction. Antihistamines block H1 receptor; nasal steroids damp inflammation; natural mast-cell stabilizers (quercetin, butterbur, nettle, vitamin D, omega-3) work upstream. *(Galli 2008; Mlcek 2016 quercetin; Schapowal 2002 butterbur)*

#### Open the science

Th2-skew (IL-4 + IL-5 + IL-13) → IgE class-switch → IgE binds FcεRI on mast cells → re-exposure → mast cell degranulation. Quercetin — flavonoid that stabilizes mast cells (Mlcek 2016 review of in-vitro + clinical signal); inhibits histamine release + leukotriene synthesis. Dose: 500 mg 2×/day; better absorbed with bromelain or piperine. Butterbur (Petasites hybridus) — Schapowal 2002 RCT showed equivalence to cetirizine in seasonal allergic rhinitis; Schapowal 2004 equivalence to fexofenadine. **CRITICAL: use only PA-FREE (pyrrolizidine-alkaloid-free) preparation like Petadolex — wild butterbur is hepatotoxic.** Dose: 50–75 mg 2×/day during season. Nettle (Urtica dioica) — Mittman 1990 small RCT showed signal for seasonal allergic rhinitis (n=98, freeze-dried nettle leaf 300 mg). Bromelain — pineapple-derived enzyme; small signal for sinus inflammation (Helms & Miller 2006). Vitamin D — Litonjua 2014 review found mixed evidence for vitamin D in allergic disease; some signal for asthma exacerbations; correction if deficient is sane. Omega-3 — anti-inflammatory; Calder 2015 review; supports resolvin + protectin production. **Synthesis.** Mast-cell stabilization is the upstream lever; OTC antihistamines are honest downstream tools.

### 2.4 Mucosal barrier integrity

> **For you, in plain words.** Healthy nasal + airway + gut mucosa block allergens better. Saline rinses clear allergens + restore mucociliary clearance. Gut microbiome supports tolerance + reduces Th2-skew. Vitamin A + zinc + glutamine support epithelial integrity. *(Cochrane Hayward 2012 saline; Calder 2017 gut + immune; Maares 2016 zinc + barrier)*

#### Open the science

Saline nasal irrigation — Cochrane Hayward 2012 + Šlapak 2008 + Garavello 2010 — symptom reduction signal in allergic rhinitis; clears pollen + dust mite + mediators; restores mucociliary clearance. Use 240 ml warm distilled or boiled-cooled water + isotonic salt + bicarb 2×/day during season. NeilMed Sinus Rinse + NetiPot. **CRITICAL: distilled or boiled-cooled water only — never tap (rare amoebic risk).** Gut microbiome — diverse fiber-rich diet + fermented foods support Treg-supportive bacteria that reduce Th2-skew (Round & Mazmanini 2009). Probiotic specifics for allergy are strain-specific and variable — some signal for Lactobacillus rhamnosus + Bifidobacterium longum (Güvenç 2016 review); not universal, not guaranteed. Vitamin A — retinol supports IgA secretion + epithelial integrity (cod-liver oil 1 tsp = ~2,500 IU). Zinc — Maares 2016 review on zinc + barrier function; 15–30 mg picolinate daily. Glutamine — gut barrier support; 5–10 g daily during high-stress periods. Nasal-breathing reinforcement — mouth-breathing bypasses nasal filtering + humidification + nitric oxide; Triana 2018 mouth-breathing morbidity; tape-the-mouth at night, address chronic nasal obstruction. **Synthesis.** Barrier integrity is upstream of trigger response.

### 2.5 HPA-immune coupling

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress + poor sleep impair regulatory T-cell function + increase Th2-skew + worsen allergy. Daily breathwork + adequate sleep + MBSR + walking are immune levers. Rosenkranz 2013 small RCT MBSR + allergic inflammation. *(Rosenkranz 2013 MBSR + inflammation; Prather 2015 sleep + immune; Cohen 2007 stress + immune)*

#### Open the science

Chronic stress → elevated cortisol → eventual glucocorticoid receptor resistance → paradoxical Th2-skew + inflammation. Sleep deprivation → reduced regulatory T-cell function + elevated inflammatory markers. Rosenkranz 2013 — small RCT in allergic patients showed MBSR reduced experimentally-induced inflammatory markers (n=49). Prather 2015 — short sleep + URI susceptibility. Cohen 2007 — stress + URI. **Home approach:** daily MBSR / mindfulness 10–20 min, sleep 7–9 h (cross-reference sleep-onset + night-waking dossiers), daily walks + Zone-2 cardio, vagal-tone work (Bhramari + Anulom Vilom). **Synthesis.** Stress + sleep are immune levers — never just "supplement harder."

### 2.6 Chronic low-grade systemic inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** Background inflammatory state (driven by poor diet, omega-6 excess, ultraprocessed foods, microbiome dysbiosis, obesity, smoking) amplifies allergic response. Mediterranean diet + omega-3 + polyphenols + weight optimization + smoking cessation lower the baseline. *(Calder 2017; Casas 2014 Mediterranean diet + inflammation)*

#### Open the science

Mediterranean diet — Casas 2014 + PREDIMED data show reduced inflammatory markers; high fiber + polyphenols + olive oil + omega-3 fish. Omega-3 EPA/DHA — Calder 2017 review on anti-inflammatory + resolvin production; 1–2 g daily; oily fish 3×/week. Polyphenols (quercetin, EGCG, anthocyanins from berries) reduce NF-κB activation. Weight optimization — visceral adiposity drives inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α). Smoking/vaping cessation — single highest-leverage anti-inflammatory intervention for smokers. **Synthesis.** Allergy is downstream of inflammation; address upstream diet + lifestyle.

### 3.2 Quercetin + butterbur Petadolex — the cornerstone pair

> **For you, in plain words.** Quercetin 500 mg 2×/day during season (mast-cell stabilizer — Mlcek 2016 review) + butterbur Petadolex 50–75 mg 2×/day during season (PA-FREE preparation only — Schapowal 2002 equivalent to cetirizine). Take quercetin with bromelain or piperine for absorption. Stop butterbur if you're not using a PA-free certified product. *(Mlcek 2016; Schapowal 2002; Schapowal 2004)*

#### Open the science

Quercetin — flavonoid found in onions, apples, capers, tea. Mast-cell stabilization mechanism: inhibits histamine release from mast cells + basophils + inhibits leukotriene synthesis (Mlcek 2016 review of in-vitro + clinical signal). Bioavailability is low — combine with bromelain (300 mg) OR piperine (10 mg) for absorption. Phytosome form (Quercefit) shows improved bioavailability (Pawar 2024). Dose: 500 mg 2×/day during season; consider year-round at lower dose 250 mg/day for chronic atopy. Butterbur Petadolex — Schapowal 2002 RCT (n=125 seasonal allergic rhinitis) showed butterbur equivalent to cetirizine for symptom relief; Schapowal 2004 (n=330) equivalent to fexofenadine. **CRITICAL safety:** wild butterbur contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs) that are hepatotoxic + carcinogenic. ONLY use certified PA-free preparation — Petadolex (Weber & Weber, Germany) is the validated PA-free brand. Dose: 50–75 mg standardized petasin 2×/day during pollen season. **Synthesis.** Quercetin + Petadolex are the two highest-evidence mast-cell stabilization layers; OTC antihistamines remain honest household tools alongside.

### 5.2 Home allergy testing

> **For you, in plain words.** DTC home IgE-panel kits (Everlywell food + environmental, OmegaQuant inflammation) give a starting map of likely triggers — but ALL allergy testing has false positives + false negatives; clinical correlation matters. The most reliable test is the trigger-mapping diary + structured elimination + symptom correlation. *(Sicherer 2010 allergy testing accuracy)*

#### Open the science

DTC IgE-panel kits — Everlywell (food + environmental panels), Imaware, MyImaware, OmegaQuant (omega + inflammation), Pinnertest (food sensitivity — IgG, controversial). **Honest framing:** specific-IgE testing has decent sensitivity for environmental allergens but variable specificity; food IgE testing is poor predictor of clinical food allergy without OFC (oral food challenge). IgG food-sensitivity testing is NOT endorsed by major non-US allergy bodies (BSACI / EAACI / Japanese Society of Allergology) due to poor clinical correlation. **More reliable:** 2–4 week trigger-mapping diary (date, symptom, suspected trigger, environment); systematic elimination (single-trigger removal 2–4 weeks, then re-challenge); food-symptom diary for suspected food triggers; pollen-tracker apps correlated with symptom diary for seasonal pattern confirmation. Clinic-route skin-prick testing + specific-IgE panel via allergist remains gold standard per §10.7.3. **Synthesis.** Start with diary + elimination + DTC IgE if useful; escalate to allergist if pattern unclear.

### 10.6 Exercise × allergy

> **For you, in plain words.** Moderate exercise supports immune function (Nieman J-curve). During peak pollen days, exercise INDOORS with HEPA filter — outdoor exercise loads the airway with pollen. *(Nieman 2019)*

### 10.8 Red-flag escalation

> **For you, in plain words.** **Stop the home protocol + call emergency services immediately + use EpiPen if available** for: (1) throat tightness/hoarseness/stridor; (2) hives + breathing difficulty; (3) tongue/lip swelling; (4) sudden hypotension/faintness; (5) diffuse hives + GI + breathing; (6) wheeze + inability to speak full sentences. **Other escalations:** (7) severe asthma exacerbation peak flow <60% personal best → ER + reliever; (8) recurrent severe infections suggesting immunodeficiency workup; (9) persistent fever >5 days; (10) chronic sinusitis with bone-erosion / vision / neuro signs → emergency ENT; (11) angioedema with throat involvement; (12) reduced consciousness + fever any age. **For known anaphylactic food allergy — carry two EpiPens always + medic-alert bracelet + see allergist + this dossier is adjunctive only.**

## 11. Self-Administered Clinical Scoring

### 11.1 The four-instrument home assessment

> **For you, in plain words.** Four tools — TNSS daily during season, RQLQ baseline + monthly, VAS daily during flare, peak-flow + SpO2 if asthma overlap. *(Spector 2003 TNSS; Juniper 1999 RQLQ; asthma action plan)*

### 11.2 TNSS (Total Nasal Symptom Score)

> **For you, in plain words.** 4-item self-rate scale (nasal congestion, rhinorrhea, itching, sneezing) each 0–3; total 0–12. Score daily during season. Free PDF; widely used. *(Spector 2003)*

### 11.3 RQLQ (Rhinoconjunctivitis Quality of Life Questionnaire)

> **For you, in plain words.** 28-item validated quality-of-life scale for allergic rhinoconjunctivitis. Baseline + monthly during season. *(Juniper 1999)*

### 11.4 VAS itch + sneeze + congestion

> **For you, in plain words.** 0–10 visual analog scale daily for each symptom. Quick + practical. Pattern recognition over weeks shows protocol response.

### 11.5 Peak-flow + SpO2 (if asthma overlap)

> **For you, in plain words.** Establish baseline when well. <60% personal best peak flow OR SpO2 <94% = ER per asthma action plan. *(Reddel GINA plan; Berry 2017)*

### 11.6 Trigger-mapping diary

> **For you, in plain words.** Daily: weather, pollen count, indoor environment, foods, suspected triggers, symptoms 0–10, supplement compliance, sleep hours. Reveals personal pattern over 4–8 weeks.
