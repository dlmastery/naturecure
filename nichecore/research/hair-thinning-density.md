## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What hair thinning actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Hair thinning is rarely "just genetic." Four overlapping causes drive it — and all four can be worked on at home. *(Trüeb 2019; Sinclair 2018)*

### 1.2 The four causes — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Order a home blood panel (ferritin, vitamin D, thyroid, B12) this week. Add 1 ml of 1% rosemary essential oil in jojoba carrier to your scalp four nights a week. Do 4 minutes of daily scalp massage. Cut tight ponytails / braids / weaves. These four moves cover the highest-evidence layers before any supplement. *(Panahi 2015 Iran RCT; Koyama 2016 Japan; WHO ferritin 2020)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Conventional dermatology treats hair thinning as a scalp problem, throws topical minoxidil at it, and offers oral finasteride / dutasteride / spironolactone or surgical transplant if that fails. None of this asks *why* the follicles are miniaturising. A more honest reframe: chronic thinning is the **scalp presentation of four overlapping drivers** — (i) DHT-driven follicular miniaturisation in genetically susceptible follicles (androgenetic alopecia), (ii) micronutrient substrate floors (ferritin, vitamin D, zinc, B12, protein, omega-3) where any deficit silently throttles the anagen growth phase, (iii) scalp microbiome dysbiosis (Malassezia overgrowth, low-grade folliculitis) eroding the follicular environment, and (iv) HPA-axis stress, subclinical hypothyroidism, and PCOS-related hyperandrogenism in women that amplify shed and pattern expression. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that secures the micronutrient floor, dampens DHT signalling with botanical and topical layers, restores the scalp microbiome, and resets HPA + thyroid hits all four drivers in parallel — and the visible density change at 16-32 weeks is the consequence, not the goal.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive chronic thinning — DHT signalling, missing nutrients, an unhappy scalp ecosystem, and the stress-thyroid axis. Naming them lets every food, oil and supplement aim at the right one. *(Trüeb 2019; Sinclair 2018; Rushton 2002)*

### 2.2 DHT and the androgen-receptor axis

> **For you, in plain words.** In genetically susceptible follicles, testosterone gets converted to a stronger hormone (DHT) that shrinks the hair root with each cycle. Damping that conversion is the upstream lever. *(EMA saw palmetto HMPC monograph; Cho 2014 Korea pumpkin seed oil RCT)*

#### Open the science

Testosterone is converted to dihydrotestosterone (DHT) by 5α-reductase type II in dermal papilla cells of genetically susceptible follicles — frontotemporal + crown in men (Hamilton-Norwood pattern), diffuse central thinning in women (Ludwig pattern). DHT binds the androgen receptor and shortens anagen, lengthens telogen, and progressively miniaturises terminal hair → vellus hair → eventually no hair. Around 95% of pattern hair loss in men is androgenetic; about 70% of pattern thinning in women has an androgenetic component. Targeted by **oral saw palmetto** (Serenoa repens, EMA HMPC monograph for BPH; small AGA trials), **oral pumpkin seed oil** (Cho 2014 Korea RCT, n=76, +40% mean hair count at 24 weeks), **topical rosemary 1%** (Panahi 2015 Iran RCT, n=100, non-inferior to topical minoxidil 2% at 6 months with less scalp itch), **topical pumpkin seed oil**, **topical onion juice** (Sharquie 2002 Iraq RCT, alopecia areata + AGA), **topical green tea EGCG**, and **topical minoxidil 5%** (OTC). *(References: EMA HMPC Serenoa monograph 2015; Cho EBCAM 2014; Panahi Skinmed 2015; Sharquie J Dermatol 2002.)*

### 2.3 Micronutrient substrate floor

> **For you, in plain words.** The hair follicle is one of your body's most metabolically active tissues. If iron, vitamin D, zinc, B12 or protein are low, hair quietly thins regardless of what you put on your scalp. *(Rushton 2002; WHO ferritin 2020)*

#### Open the science

**Ferritin** is the most replicated nutritional driver — UK, Indian, Iranian and German cohorts consistently link ferritin <30 ng/mL to telogen effluvium and pattern hair loss severity in women; the Rushton hypothesis proposes ferritin 70+ ng/mL as optimal for hair growth (mechanistically strong; RCT evidence mixed). **Vitamin D** receptor is expressed in the follicle; low 25-OH-D correlates with AGA, alopecia areata and telogen effluvium. **Zinc** is the cofactor for keratin-synthesis enzymes and modestly inhibits 5α-reductase. **Biotin** is hyped — only useful if actually deficient (rare; biotin >2500 µg falsely depresses TSH and falsely elevates troponin on lab assays). **B12 + folate** drive follicle cell division. **Protein 1.2-1.6 g/kg** is mandatory for keratin substrate. **Omega-3** lowers scalp inflammation and supports sebaceous-gland health (Le Floc'h 2015 France RCT). Targeted by ferritin/Vit D/Zn/B12/methyl-folate/omega-3/collagen + dietary protein, all under DTC-lab measurement. *(References: Rushton Clin Exp Dermatol 2002; WHO ferritin cut-off 2020; Le Floc'h J Cosmet Dermatol 2015; Trink Brit J Dermatol 2013 Vitamin D in AA.)*

### 2.4 Scalp microbiome and barrier

> **For you, in plain words.** Your scalp is a tiny ecosystem of yeasts and bacteria. When the yeast (Malassezia) overgrows, the follicle gets inflamed, the barrier breaks, and hair thins. Gentle scalp hygiene fixes most of it. *(Borda 2015 Malassezia review; Koyama 2016 Japan scalp massage trial)*

#### Open the science

The scalp surface harbours Cutibacterium acnes, Staphylococcus epidermidis, Malassezia restricta and M. globosa. Dandruff / seborrhoeic dermatitis correlates with Malassezia overgrowth + scalp inflammation; chronic low-grade folliculitis (Folliculitis decalvans, Lichen planopilaris, Central centrifugal cicatricial alopecia [CCCA — predominantly Afro-textured hair with traction + chemical-relaxer + heat-tool history]) can drive scarring hair loss in genetically susceptible scalps. Targeted by **zinc pyrithione 1% wash**, **salicylic acid 2% scalp wash**, **tea tree 2-5% wash**, **neem decoction rinse**, **gentle low-pH sulphate-free cleansers**, **scalp oils** (Bhringraj/Eclipta alba, Brahmi, neem, rosemary), **traction reduction**, and **standardised 4-minute daily scalp massage** (Koyama 2016 Japan ex vivo + clinical trial — 24-week standardised manual scalp massage increased hair density measurably). *(References: Borda Curr Probl Dermatol 2015; Koyama Eplasty 2016; Tosti CCCA review.)*

### 2.5 HPA-axis, thyroid, PCOS, perimenopause

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress, an underactive thyroid and (in women) high androgens or low estrogen all amplify shedding. Test, don't guess. *(Choudhary 2017 Australia Ashwagandha cortisol RCT; Vincent 2013 hypothyroid alopecia review)*

#### Open the science

Chronic stress (work, illness, bereavement) accelerates catagen → telogen effluvium 2-3 months after the trigger (the classic "post-COVID shed", "post-illness shed", "postpartum shed"). Subclinical hypothyroidism (TSH 2.5-4.5 mIU/L with positive anti-TPO antibodies) is overrepresented in female pattern hair loss. PCOS / hyperandrogenism (elevated free testosterone, low SHBG) drives female AGA + hirsutism + acne. Perimenopausal estrogen drop unmasks androgenetic predisposition in women aged 45-55. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66** (Choudhary 2017 Australia n=64, cortisol -23% to -28%), **magnesium glycinate**, **Bacopa**, **Yoga Nidra**, **vagal-tone practices**, **thyroid cofactor floor** (selenium 200 µg, iodine cautiously, zinc), **myo-inositol + D-chiro-inositol 40:1** (PCOS), **spearmint tea** (Akdoğan 2007 Turkey + Grant 2010 UK — anti-androgen in women), and **DUTCH / Thriva hormone testing**. *(References: Choudhary Indian J Psychol Med 2017; Akdoğan Phytother Res 2007; Grant Phytother Res 2010; Vincent 2013.)*

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Fourteen over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on biotin (only if deficient), saw palmetto (small trials, modest effect), and pumpkin seed oil (one good Korean trial — try for 24 weeks). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Cho 2014; Panahi 2015)*

### 3.2 The honest evidence frame

> **For you, in plain words.** Topical minoxidil 5% (Rogaine/Kirkland/Mintop — OTC at any pharmacy) is the most-studied hair regrowth molecule. It works for roughly 40% of users. Rosemary 1% oil matched it in a head-to-head Iranian trial with less scalp itch. Oral saw palmetto has weaker, smaller trials than the prescription DHT-blockers but is sold OTC. Pumpkin seed oil has one good 24-week Korean trial. **Try each for 16-24 weeks — stop if no visible change at six months.** *(Panahi 2015 Iran; Cho 2014 Korea; EMA saw palmetto monograph 2015)*

### 4.3 TCM — Tuo Fa 脱发, Bai Fa 白发

> **For you, in plain words.** Chinese medicine reads hair loss as Kidney essence depletion and Blood deficiency. #### Open the science

Patterns: Kidney-essence (jing) depletion → premature greying + thinning; Liver-blood deficiency → dry brittle thin hair; Spleen-qi deficiency with damp → excessive shed; Blood-stasis on the scalp (post-trauma, post-illness) → patches. The properly prepared form (zhi he shou wu, processed with black bean) is significantly safer but not risk-free. Always source NMPA-certified processed form, never raw; stop immediately if you notice fatigue, dark urine, or yellowing. Lin 2015 hepatotoxicity review documents the pattern.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Topical minoxidil 5% (OTC) + topical rosemary 1% oil + daily 4-minute scalp massage is the strongest at-home biophysical bundle. Home low-level laser therapy caps and microneedling come second. Red-light helmets have one good trial; results are modest. *(Panahi 2015 Iran; Koyama 2016 Japan; Lanzafame 2014 LLLT RCT)*

**Quick start, tonight.**
1. Wash with a gentle, low-pH, sulphate-free cleanser; alternate with a zinc-pyrithione 1% or ketoconazole-free salicylic + tea-tree wash 2 nights a week.
2. Apply 1 ml topical minoxidil 5% OR 5 ml of the rosemary 1% / pumpkin seed oil blend to the dry scalp.
3. Do 4 minutes of standardised manual scalp massage — fingertip pressure, slow circles, every region of the scalp.

### 5.2 Topical scalp regime — wash, oil, massage

> **For you, in plain words.** A low-pH, sulphate-free wash 3 nights a week; an anti-yeast wash (zinc pyrithione 1% or tea-tree 2-5%) on the alternate nights; then 1 ml of topical minoxidil 5% OR 5 ml of the rosemary 1% / pumpkin seed oil blend; then 4 minutes of manual scalp massage. *(Borda 2015; Panahi 2015; Koyama 2016)*

### 5.3 Standardised scalp massage

> **For you, in plain words.** Fingertip pressure, slow circles, 4 minutes a day — the Koyama Japanese trial showed measurable density gain at 24 weeks. Free, low-risk, evidence-backed. *(Koyama 2016 Japan; English 2019 self-massage cohort)*

#### Open the science

Koyama 2016 (Japan, Eplasty) used a wearable scalp-massaging device 4 minutes/day for 24 weeks in healthy male subjects and measured hair-shaft thickness via image analysis — significant increase. English 2019 self-reported large internet survey of self-massagers showed perceived hair retention. Mechanism: mechanical stretch on dermal papilla cells modulates gene expression (HoxC13, IGF, BMP4), increases scalp microcirculation, and lowers scalp tension. Free, low-risk, take 4 minutes a day. *(Koyama Eplasty 2016; English Dermatol Online 2019.)*

### 5.4 Home low-level laser therapy caps (LLLT)

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red-light caps (Capillus, iRestore, Theradome) shine 650 nm light at the scalp for 30 minutes every other day. Honest read: one good trial showed modest density gain; expensive ($300-3,000); fits as one OPTIONAL layer for those who can afford it. *(Lanzafame 2014 RCT; Friedman 2017 cohort)*

#### Open the science

Lanzafame 2014 (n=44, Lasers Surg Med) — a 16-week randomised trial of a 655 nm laser device showed a 35-39% increase in terminal hair density vs sham. Friedman 2017 (Capillus cohort, US) confirmed similar effect sizes. Mechanism: photobiomodulation at 650-660 nm activates mitochondrial cytochrome c oxidase, increases ATP, prolongs anagen. Devices: Capillus 82, iRestore Elite, Theradome PRO. Capillus FDA cleared 2014 (regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor — the evidence anchor is Lanzafame 2014). Eye protection automatic with cap design; do NOT use if you have a head-or-face cancer history or take photosensitising drugs. Price $300-3,000 — fits as ONE optional layer for users who can afford it. *(References: Lanzafame Lasers Surg Med 2014; Friedman 2017.)*

### 5.5 Home microneedling derma-rollers

> **For you, in plain words.** A small derma-roller (0.5-1.5 mm) rolled over the scalp once a week creates micro-injuries that stimulate hair-growth signalling. Adds to minoxidil effectiveness in trials. **Do not use** in active infection, scarring alopecia, blood thinners. *(Dhurat 2013 India RCT, n=100)*

#### Open the science

Dhurat 2013 (India, Int J Trichology) — 100 men with AGA randomised to topical minoxidil 5% alone vs minoxidil + weekly 1.5 mm dermaroller; the dermaroller arm had 3-4× greater hair count at 12 weeks. Mechanism: micro-injury releases growth factors (PDGF, VEGF), increases minoxidil penetration, activates Wnt/β-catenin. Roller size 0.5 mm (beginner) to 1.5 mm (experienced); clean with isopropanol; once a week max; apply minoxidil or rosemary blend immediately after. **Do not use** in active scalp infection, scarring alopecia (worsens scarring), on blood thinners (warfarin, rivaroxaban). **Grade B**. *(Dhurat 2013 Int J Trichology.)*

### 5.6 Anti-yeast scalp washes

> **For you, in plain words.** Zinc pyrithione 1%, salicylic 2%, or tea tree 2-5% washes 2 nights a week reset the scalp microbiome — even without dandruff symptoms, this often helps hair density. *(Berger 2003 zinc pyrithione cohort; Satchell 2002 Australia tea tree RCT)*

Brands: Head & Shoulders Classic (zinc pyrithione 1%), Selsun Blue (selenium sulfide 1%), Paul Mitchell Tea Tree Special Shampoo (tea tree 2%), Nizoral 1% (ketoconazole, sold OTC in most markets — note: ketoconazole IS OTC at 1%; the 2% prescription strength is clinic-route). Alternate with low-pH sulphate-free daily cleansers (Vanicream, Free & Clear, AYUSH neem-based washes).

### 5.7 Red-light panel for scalp + sub-occiput

> **For you, in plain words.** A home red-light panel (660 nm + 810 nm) held 4-6 inches from the crown for 10-15 min, 4 nights a week — overlaps the same mechanism as LLLT caps at lower cost. Honestly graded C. *(Hamblin transcranial PBM review; mechanistic overlap with Lanzafame 2014)*

Devices: Mito Red Light MitoMIN/MID/MAX, Joovv Solo, Hooga panels. Wavelength 660+810 nm. 10-20 J/cm² per session. Eye protection mandatory; do NOT use with photosensitising drugs, active head/face cancer, or recent brain surgery.

### 5.8 Hair-friendly grooming

> **For you, in plain words.** Loose hairstyles, no tight ponytails or braids, wide-tooth comb instead of brush, microfiber towel not terry, no high-heat tools, silk pillowcase. Tension and friction damage hair shafts and traction-stress follicles. *(Khumalo 2007 BJD traction cohort)*

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Hair thinning has four causes — DHT signalling, missing nutrients, an unhappy scalp ecosystem, and stress-thyroid-PCOS imbalances. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home; shed drops in weeks, visible density in months. *(Trüeb 2019; Le Floc'h 2015; Panahi 2015)*

Hair thinning is not "just genetic — accept it." Four things drive it: (1) in genetically susceptible follicles, testosterone gets converted to a stronger hormone (DHT) that shrinks the hair root with each cycle; (2) iron, vitamin D, zinc, B12 or protein, if low, silently throttle the growth phase regardless of what you put on your scalp; (3) the scalp is a tiny ecosystem of yeasts and bacteria — when the yeast (Malassezia) overgrows, the follicle gets inflamed; (4) chronic stress, an underactive thyroid, perimenopause and PCOS all amplify shedding and pattern expression.

This protocol does **not** chase hair loss with prescription pills. It rebuilds each of the four layers — topical minoxidil 5% (sold over the counter at every pharmacy) OR a topical rosemary 1% + pumpkin seed oil blend (Iranian trial matched 1% rosemary against 2% minoxidil with less itch), an oral DHT-damping layer (saw palmetto, pumpkin seed oil), a substrate floor (iron only if ferritin <30, vitamin D to 50-70 ng/mL, zinc if low, B12 if vegetarian/65+/on metformin, daily protein 1.2-1.6 g/kg), scalp microbiome reset (zinc-pyrithione or tea-tree wash 2 nights a week, gentle low-pH cleanser), 4 minutes of daily standardised scalp massage (the Japanese Koyama trial showed measurable density gain at 24 weeks), MBSR for stress shed, Yoga Nidra at bedtime. Women with PCOS overlap layer spearmint tea + inositol; women with Hashimoto-positive layer the thyroid cofactor floor. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.**

Expect a slow, layered shift. **Shed count** drops first (weeks 2-8). **Visible density** lifts at month 3-4 (women with PCOS/Hashimoto correction often see the biggest gains). Telogen effluvium (post-stress, postpartum) self-resolves in 6-12 months with substrate restoration. Decades-old advanced pattern loss does NOT fully reverse; it stabilises and modestly thickens. Scarred follicles (lichen planopilaris, frontal fibrosing alopecia, advanced CCCA) do NOT regrow on any therapy — be honest with yourself.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you keep a physician informed, that's your call; the protocol does not require it.
