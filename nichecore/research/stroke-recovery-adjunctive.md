> **For you, in plain words.** You (or someone you love) had a stroke. Acute treatment (IV tPA/tenecteplase, thrombectomy, neurosurgery, ICU) happens in hospital — no home protocol replaces it. **This dossier is for the months and years AFTER the acute event.** It wraps around standard secondary-prevention Rx (antiplatelet, anticoagulant, statin, antihypertensive) + formal rehab (PT/OT/SLT) — never replaces them. Five levers: (1) BP <130/80 — single largest recurrence-prevention lever; (2) Mediterranean food pattern — A-grade secondary prevention; (3) structured rehab practice — CIMT for affected limb, speech apps, balance training; (4) post-stroke depression treatment (30-50% prevalence; untreated doubles mortality); (5) silent-AFib + OSA + glycemia screening — the comorbidity ring driving 70% of recurrences. *(PREDIMED 2013; Stroke Unit Trialists 2013 Cochrane; SPARCL 2006; ARISTOTLE 2011)*
>
> **The frame.** Anchored in WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034, India AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRYN, CCRUM, CCRS, PCIMH, ICMR), EMA HMPC, Australia TGA + NHMRC, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Tibetan Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, classical Ayurvedic/Chinese/Unani/Siddha/Tibetan/homeopathic texts. **No US regulatory body or disease association (ASA, AHA, AAN, NIH-NINDS-policy) is cited as authority.** US regulatory milestones (Vivistim FDA De Novo 2021, tenecteplase off-label) = regulatory-path context only. Evidence anchor = named trial (NINDS 1995, ECASS III 2008, HERMES 2016, DAWN, DEFUSE-3, EXCITE Wolf 2006, FLAME, FOCUS, ARISTOTLE, SPARCL, SPRINT, PREDIMED, Vivistim Tex 2021).
>
> **Sovereignty (with limits).** **Secondary-prevention prescriptions in stroke are non-negotiable** if your neurologist placed you on them. Acute interventions (tPA, thrombectomy, surgery), inpatient rehab, formal PT/OT/SLT, botox for spasticity, SSRIs for PSD, Vivistim VNS-paired rehab = §10.7.3 referral addendum.
>
> **Red flag — NEW stroke / TIA = 911 NOW.** **FAST** (Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to 911) + **BE-FAST** (Balance loss + Eye/vision change). Every 30 min delay ≈ 1 month brain-death equivalent. Recurrence 5-15% first year — post FAST + BE-FAST on the fridge. Seizure post-stroke = same-day neurology. Suicidal ideation + PSD = crisis lines (988 US, Lifeline AU, iCall IN, Samaritans UK) + same-day psychiatric urgency. DVT/PE post-stroke immobility = urgent. Aspiration pneumonia from dysphagia = urgent. **Five exceptions to "stay at home."**

## 1. Executive Summary

> **CRITICAL SAFETY — read first.**
>
> If you are reading this DURING an acute stroke event (sudden onset face droop / arm weakness / speech difficulty / severe headache / vision change), STOP and call your local emergency number now: 911 (US), 999 (UK), 112 (EU + India + AU + many others), 119 (Japan), 120 (China). Every 30 minutes you wait equals ~1 month of brain tissue lost.
>
> This dossier is for the recovery + secondary-prevention phase, ALONGSIDE your neurology + PT/OT/SLT team — never instead. Antiplatelet / statin / BP-Rx / anticoagulation are gold-standard secondary prevention; this protocol is an adjunct that runs in parallel.

### 1.1 What stroke recovery actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** A stroke is brain tissue dying from either a blocked blood vessel (ischemic — ~85%) or a burst blood vessel (hemorrhagic — ~15%). Recovery is the brain re-routing function around the dead tissue (neuroplasticity), supported by tight secondary-prevention so you don't have a second event. *(NINDS tPA 1995; Stroke Unit Trialists 2013)*

### 1.2 The five levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** This week: home BP cuff daily AM, target <130/80; cut liquid sugar and ultra-processed foods; start Mediterranean food pattern; 20-minute structured walking practice daily; FAST + BE-FAST poster on the fridge. This month: schedule home PT/OT/SLT sessions or app-based equivalents (TactusTherapy for aphasia, Constant Therapy, CIMT-home version for affected limb), screen for AFib with KardiaMobile and OSA with home pulse-ox, PHQ-9 monthly for post-stroke depression. *(SPRINT 2015 BP; PREDIMED 2013; Wolf 2006 EXCITE CIMT)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Stroke recovery is usually framed as acute rescue (tPA + thrombectomy) followed by "wait and see." A more honest reframe: post-stroke recovery is the **convergence of five daily systems** for 12-24+ months — (i) **secondary-prevention vascular control** (BP, AFib, lipids, glycemia, smoking, OSA — drives 70% of recurrences); (ii) **structured rehab practice** (CIMT, mirror-box, gait, aphasia, cognitive rehab); (iii) **Mediterranean / MIND food pattern** — A-grade secondary prevention; (iv) **PSD + fatigue treatment** (30-50% prevalence; untreated doubles mortality); (v) **inflammation + microbiome + sleep + stress**. **Synthesis.** Disciplined BP self-monitoring + Mediterranean + ≥3 h/d rehab practice (first year) + PSD treatment + supplement layer (omega-3 DHA, citicoline, B-vits if homocysteine elevated, magnesium, vitamin D) hits all five in parallel, letting the brain re-wire as completely as the lesion allows.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five systems — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five systems run post-stroke recovery — vascular secondary-prevention, structured rehab practice, food pattern, depression + fatigue, and chronic inflammation. Naming them lets every food, walk, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(SPARCL 2006; Wolf 2006 EXCITE; PREDIMED 2013; Hackett 2008 Cochrane PSD)*

### 2.2 Vascular secondary-prevention control

> **For you, in plain words.** The single biggest thing you can do is prevent stroke #2. Tight BP control, anticoagulation if AFib, statin, glycemia, smoking cessation and OSA treatment cut recurrence risk by 60-80%. *(SPRINT 2015; ARISTOTLE 2011; SPARCL 2006; OSA + stroke recurrence Yaggi 2005)*

**The deeper layer.** HTN dominant — every 10 mm Hg systolic reduction cuts recurrence ~30%; SPRINT 2015 BP <120/80 reduces all-cause mortality (caveats elderly/frail). AFib — ARISTOTLE 2011 DOACs reduce stroke + bleeding vs warfarin. Statins (SPARCL 2006 atorvastatin 80 mg) cut recurrence ~16%. Glycemic control + SGLT-2/GLP-1 era cuts CV/stroke recurrence. OSA treatment (CPAP) cuts recurrence in stroke-OSA overlap. Smoking cessation → non-smoker baseline within 5 y. **Synthesis.** Home protocol's #1 job: make BP, glycemia, OSA daily measurement habits.

### 2.3 Structured rehab practice (neuroplasticity)

> **For you, in plain words.** The brain re-wires itself around the dead tissue ONLY when you give it the right practice — high-repetition, task-specific, focused movement and speech and cognitive training for hours per day. *(Wolf 2006 EXCITE; Langhorne 2011 stroke rehab review)*

**The deeper layer.** Neuroplasticity dose-dependent: ≥3 h/d structured task-specific practice for first 12 months drives most recovery (Langhorne 2011 Lancet). CIMT (Wolf 2006 EXCITE NEJM — restrict unaffected limb 90% waking + intensive shaping of affected) = A-grade upper-extremity motor recovery. Mirror-box for neglect; gait training on treadmill with partial body-weight support (LEAPS Duncan 2011); speech therapy (Melodic Intonation, script, semantic feature analysis); cognitive rehab (attention + working memory + metacognitive strategy). Home apps (TactusTherapy, Constant Therapy) supplement formal PT/OT/SLT. **Synthesis.** Dose matters; window widest first 12 mo, plasticity continues for years.

### 2.4 The food pattern (Mediterranean / MIND)

> **For you, in plain words.** The Mediterranean food pattern cuts stroke recurrence by ~30% per PREDIMED 2013 + Lyon Heart Diet. The MIND diet (MIND = Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay) overlaps strongly and protects cognition. *(PREDIMED 2013 Estruch NEJM; Lyon Heart 1994 de Lorgeril; MIND Morris 2015)*

**The deeper layer.** PREDIMED (Estruch 2013 NEJM, n=7447): Mediterranean + EVOO or nuts cut major CV events ~30% vs low-fat control. Lyon Diet Heart (de Lorgeril 1994 Lancet): >50% recurrent-CV reduction. MIND (Morris 2015) = Mediterranean + DASH hybrid emphasising berries, leafy greens, fish, olive oil, whole grains, nuts; limits red meat, butter, cheese, fried foods, sweets — significantly slowed cognitive decline. Mechanism: polyphenols + omega-3 + hydroxytyrosol + dietary nitrate + reduced ultra-processed inflammation. **Synthesis.** Mediterranean / MIND is the platform every supplement + rehab session sits on.

### 2.5 Post-stroke depression + fatigue

> **For you, in plain words.** 30-50% of stroke survivors get clinical depression; ~50% report disabling fatigue. Untreated depression doubles mortality and slows rehab. *(Hackett 2008 Cochrane PSD; Lawrence 2013 post-stroke fatigue)*

**The deeper layer.** PSD prevalence 30-50% (Hackett 2014); doubles 1-year mortality, slows functional recovery. FLAME (Chollet 2011 Lancet Neurol fluoxetine 20 mg) showed motor recovery BEYOND depression treatment — FOCUS 2019 did NOT replicate motor signal but PSD treatment remains A-grade. CBT for PSD (Hackett 2008 Cochrane) = A-grade. Mindfulness for post-stroke fatigue (Lawrence 2013). Modafinil for fatigue (Bivard 2017 small RCT). **Synthesis.** PHQ-9 monthly mandatory; treat if ≥10 — CBT + SSRI coordinated.

### 2.6 Chronic inflammation + sleep + microbiome

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic inflammation, poor sleep and microbiome dysbiosis quietly worsen all four other systems and slow recovery. *(Hotamisligil 2017 metaflammation; Yaggi 2005 OSA + stroke)*

**The deeper layer.** Post-stroke inflammation (elevated CRP, IL-6, TNF-α) drives secondary neuronal injury. OSA (40-70% prevalence) drives recurrence + cognitive decline + depression. Sleep fragmentation impairs glymphatic clearance + synaptic consolidation. Microbiome dysbiosis worsens systemic inflammation. Targeted by oily fish 3-4×/week, EVOO 30 ml/d, fibre 40 g, fermented foods, polyphenols, CPAP for OSA, sleep hygiene, stress management. **Synthesis.** Slowest lever to move but determines long-term cognitive + functional trajectory.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 Ten OTC molecules — table

> **For you, in plain words.** Ten over-the-counter molecules with a non-US monograph or named trial behind them. The strongest signals are for omega-3 DHA (cognitive support), citicoline (the honest mixed signal — small early positives, ICTUS 2012 NULL), B-vitamins for homocysteine (mixed VITATOPS 2010 null for prevention but useful if elevated), vitamin D (replete if low), magnesium (modest BP signal). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; ICTUS 2012; VITATOPS 2010)*

### 3.2 The honest caveat — citicoline ICTUS 2012 NULL

> **For you, in plain words.** Citicoline was once heralded as a neuroprotective miracle based on small earlier trials. The largest RCT — ICTUS 2012 (n=2298) — showed NO benefit in acute ischemic stroke recovery. **We list citicoline because the evidence is honestly mixed: some smaller cognitive-recovery trials still positive, EMA continues to monograph it for use in some EU countries.** Try it for 12 weeks if you wish; stop if no shift on cognitive measures. *(ICTUS Davalos 2012 Lancet NULL; Secades 2016 cognitive signal)*

**The deeper layer.** Early small trials (Davalos 2002 + Cohen 2003) suggested citicoline reduced disability. ICTUS 2012 (Davalos, Lancet, n=2298) — the largest RCT — found NO significant benefit on mRS at 90 days. EMA continues to permit citicoline in some EU countries for cognitive impairment of vascular origin. Honest read: citicoline is a low-effect-size adjunct, never a cornerstone. We grade it C for stroke recovery.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 Home BP cuff (cornerstone)

> **For you, in plain words.** HTN management is the #1 stroke-recurrence-prevention lever. Daily AM home BP measurement is non-negotiable post-stroke. Target <130/80; <120/80 if your neurologist + primary care agree per SPRINT extension. *(SPRINT 2015 NEJM; SECONDARY-PREVENTION trials)*

Omron 5/7/10 series ($40-80). Two readings, one minute apart, average them. Log daily. If sustained >140/90 — same-week primary care. If sustained >180/110 — same-day urgent care.

### 5.2 KardiaMobile / Apple Watch (AFib screening)

> **For you, in plain words.** Silent AFib drives 20-30% of strokes. Weekly self-checks for AFib post-stroke catch the source you might be missing. *(CRYSTAL-AF 2014; STROKE-AF 2021)*

KardiaMobile 1L ($80) or Apple Watch Series 6+. Weekly 30-second rhythm strip. Any AFib detected = same-week cardiology + repeat workup for anticoagulation.

### 5.3 Home pulse-oximeter (OSA screen)

OSA prevalence in stroke survivors is 40-70%; treating OSA cuts recurrence. Overnight pulse-ox (Wellue O2Ring, ~$160) screens for desaturation events. If ODI (oxygen desaturation index) >5 — home sleep test → CPAP if confirmed.

### 5.4 Home glucose monitoring (metabolic-stroke overlap)

If T2D or T1D — CGM cornerstone (Dexcom G7, Libre 3, Stelo). See type-2-diabetes-active.md and type-1-diabetes-adjunct.md dossiers.

### 5.5 CIMT home version (affected limb)

> **For you, in plain words.** Constraint-Induced Movement Therapy — restrict the unaffected arm 90% of waking hours with a padded mitt; intensive shaping practice with the affected arm. A-grade for upper-extremity motor recovery. *(Wolf 2006 EXCITE NEJM)*

Padded mitt for unaffected hand (Saebo ConstraintGlove or DIY thick mitten) — wear 6-8 hours/day with structured practice tasks. Combine with mirror-box therapy ($40, Reflex mirror box) for unilateral neglect. Pair with formal OT.

### 5.6 Speech-aphasia practice apps

TactusTherapy ($50-200 per module — Language Therapy 4-in-1, Naming Therapy, Conversation Therapy); Constant Therapy ($25/mo subscription); Tactus AppRx — daily 30-60 min app-based speech practice supplements formal SLT. Caregiver involvement essential.

### 5.7 Cognitive rehab apps

CogMed (working memory training), Lumosity, BrainHQ Posit Science — daily 20-30 min cognitive practice. Combine with metacognitive strategy training (write down what you can't remember; use external cues; structured daily schedule).

### 5.8 Home transcranial DC stimulation (tDCS) — caution

Caputron + Halo Neuroscience consumer tDCS devices for motor rehab — emerging research, mixed evidence (FOCUS trial 2021 NULL for motor). Use cautiously, paired with formal PT — not as a standalone home intervention.

### 5.9 Home VR rehab

Saebo VR therapy, AbleNet, Neofect smart gloves, free VR therapy apps (REAL Therapy, VRehab) — engaging supplement to formal rehab. Combine with formal OT.

### 5.10 Vagus-nerve stimulation tVNS (transcutaneous)

Saypin, Mantis, Parasym — non-invasive transcutaneous VNS via tragus electrode 30 min/day. Emerging research (preliminary signal for paired motor rehab — mostly tested in clinic-deployed implanted VNS as below in §10.7.3).

### 5.11 Home rehab wearables for cardio + sleep + activity

Apple Watch / Whoop for HR + HRV + sleep tracking; Polar H10 chest strap for accurate HR; structured walking program with Pacer / Strava apps.

### 5.12 Red-light panel + sauna + cold

Mito Red Light, Joovv (10-20 min head + body 3-4×/wk); infrared sauna 20-30 min 3-4×/wk (caution — BP fluctuation post-stroke, hydrate well); cold immersion CAUTION post-stroke (BP spike risk — consult neurologist before starting).

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 The Mediterranean / MIND base layer

> **For you, in plain words.** PREDIMED 2013 cut major CV events 30% (including stroke); MIND 2015 slowed cognitive decline. Build the platform first. *(PREDIMED Estruch 2013 NEJM; MIND Morris 2015 Alzheimer Dement)*

### 6.2 Daily macros

- Carbohydrate: 40-50% from whole grains, legumes, vegetables
- Protein: 1.0-1.5 g/kg/d (more if older/sarcopenic)
- Fat: 30-40% emphasising EVOO, nuts, fish; minimal industrial seed oil
- Fibre: 30-40 g/d from food
- Sodium: <2.3 g/d (1.5 g/d if HTN — DASH target)

### 6.3 What to add daily

- 250 g leafy greens + cruciferous (MIND emphasises leafy greens A-grade)
- 100 g berries (MIND emphasises berries A-grade)
- 30 g raw nuts (PREDIMED emphasis)
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil (PREDIMED + MIND cornerstone)
- 1 fermented food/day
- Oily fish 3-4×/week (sardines, mackerel, salmon, herring — DHA cornerstone)
- Legumes 4 servings/week
- Whole grain 200 g/day

### 6.4 What to subtract (MIND-unhealthy list)

- Red meat <4 servings/week
- Butter/margarine <1 tbsp/d
- Cheese <1 serving/week
- Fried foods minimal
- Sweets/pastries <5 servings/week
- Liquid sugar (single biggest BP + glycemic lever)
- Industrial seed oils
- Trans fats
- Sodium >2.3 g/d (HTN driver)
- Excess alcohol >7 drinks/week

### 6.5 Sample 3-day rotation

**Day A — Mediterranean.** Breakfast: oatmeal with berries + walnuts + flax + olive oil drizzle. Lunch: grilled salmon + roasted vegetables + whole-grain bread. Dinner: chicken with lentils + leafy greens + EVOO + small whole-grain side.

**Day B — Indian-Mediterranean fusion.** Breakfast: vegetable upma with whole-grain millets + green chutney. Lunch: dal + brown rice + sabzi + cucumber raita. Dinner: fish curry (sardines) + quinoa + sauteed greens.

**Day C — Lower-glycemic.** Breakfast: omelette + avocado + sauteed greens + small fruit. Lunch: tuna salad + EVOO + greens + olives + nuts. Dinner: grilled meat + roasted cruciferous + small whole-grain portion + berries.

#### Open the science

The NINDS trial (NINDS rtPA Stroke Study Group 1995, NEJM, n=624) established IV tPA within 3 hours improves functional outcomes in acute ischemic stroke; ECASS III (Hacke 2008, NEJM) extended the window to 4.5 hours. The HERMES collaboration (Goyal 2016, Lancet meta, n=1287 across 5 RCTs) established endovascular thrombectomy A-grade for large-vessel occlusion. DAWN (Nogueira 2018, NEJM) and DEFUSE-3 (Albers 2018, NEJM) extended thrombectomy to 6-24 hours in selected patients with favourable imaging. ARISTOTLE (Granger 2011, NEJM, n=18,201) established apixaban superior to warfarin for AFib-stroke prevention with reduced bleeding. SPARCL (Amarenco 2006, NEJM, n=4731) established atorvastatin 80 mg cuts recurrent stroke ~16%. The Stroke Unit Trialists Cochrane review (2013) established organised stroke-unit care reduces death + dependency at one year. The EXCITE trial (Wolf 2006, NEJM, n=222) established CIMT A-grade for upper-extremity motor recovery 3-9 months post-stroke. The Vivistim VNS-REHAB trial (Tex 2021, Lancet, n=108) established paired vagus-nerve stimulation + rehab A-grade for chronic moderate-to-severe arm impairment. The FLAME trial (Chollet 2011, Lancet Neurology, n=118) showed fluoxetine improved motor recovery beyond depression treatment in acute ischemic stroke; the larger FOCUS trial (FOCUS Collaboration 2019, Lancet, n=3127) did NOT replicate the motor signal — the honest read is that SSRIs for PSD treatment remain A-grade, but the motor-recovery-beyond-depression claim is now uncertain. The VITATOPS trial (VITATOPS Trial Study Group 2010, Lancet Neurology, n=8164) showed B-vitamin supplementation did NOT reduce stroke recurrence in unselected patients — we surface this honestly because B-vitamins are over-marketed for stroke prevention. The ICTUS trial (Davalos 2012, Lancet, n=2298) showed citicoline did NOT improve outcomes in acute ischemic stroke — we surface this honestly because citicoline is over-marketed for neuroprotection. The PREDIMED trial (Estruch 2013, NEJM, n=7447) established Mediterranean diet A-grade for primary CV + stroke prevention. The MIND diet (Morris 2015, Alzheimer Dement) showed MIND-pattern adherence slowed cognitive decline.

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

> **For you, in plain words.** What is going on, what to expect, and what NOT to expect.

You had a stroke. Brain tissue died — that part is permanent — and now your brain is re-routing function around the damage. Neuroplasticity is real and works in proportion to (1) how aggressively you prevent stroke #2 (BP, AFib, statin, glycemia, OSA, smoking), (2) how much structured rehab practice you do (CIMT, speech apps, balance, cognitive rehab), (3) Mediterranean/MIND food pattern, (4) PSD + fatigue treatment, (5) sleep + stress. Supplements come fifth — they amplify, never replace.

What to expect: first 6 months = steepest recovery slope; first 12 months = most of the recovery; modest gains continue for years. Most survivors achieve mRS 0-2 with disciplined rehab + secondary prevention. PSD hits 30-50% — treat it. Post-stroke fatigue ~50% — manage but don't let it stop the rehab dose.

What NOT to expect: full pre-stroke baseline in every case. Some deficits permanent. The two highest-leverage things: (1) take secondary-prevention Rx every day without fail, (2) ≥3 h/d structured rehab for the first year.

The sovereign ethos: OTC, home-based, independent path wrapping around your stroke unit + neurologist + rehab team + secondary-prevention Rx. **Secondary-prevention Rx is non-negotiable; everything else is sovereign.**
