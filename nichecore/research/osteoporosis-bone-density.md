The single most powerful intervention is **heavy progressive resistance + impact training** — the LIFTMOR trial (Watson 2018, Australian) showed that postmenopausal women with low bone density who lifted heavy (deadlifts, squats, overhead press at 80–85% 1RM) plus did high-impact jumping actually *reversed* osteopenic-osteoporotic bone loss. Pair with vitamin D + K2 + magnesium + calcium-from-food + protein (1.2–1.6 g/kg) + fall-prevention balance work. We are honest where the science is mixed: calcium supplements (vs calcium from food) carry a cardiovascular signal (Bolland 2010); vitamin D alone in replete community-dwellers is null; strontium is restricted; vibration plates have a mixed signal. *(Watson 2018 LIFTMOR; Avenell 2014 Cochrane vitamin D + calcium; Bolland 2010 calcium-CV meta)*

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What osteoporosis actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Osteoporosis is NOT just "weak bones from aging." It is the silent **uncoupling of bone formation and bone resorption** — osteoclasts (bone-eaters) outpace osteoblasts (bone-builders), driven by oestrogen loss in women, testosterone loss in men, chronic glucocorticoid exposure, vitamin D + K2 deficiency, sarcopenia, and inflammatory disease overlap. The result is microarchitectural deterioration that doesn't hurt — until the fracture. *(Riggs 2002 NEJM; Black 2016 NEJM osteoporosis review)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: take vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 (MK-7) 100 µg + magnesium glycinate 400 mg + calcium-from-food (200 g leafy greens, 60 g sesame, 100 g sardines with bones, 100 g coconut yoghurt) + 30 g protein per meal. This week: start heavy resistance training 2–3×/week (deadlift / squat / overhead press at challenging load), add jumping protocol 50 jumps 3×/week, install a home grip-strength dynamometer ($25), order DTC CTX + P1NP + 25-OH-D + ferritin labs, calculate your FRAX 10-year fracture risk online. If postmenopausal: add Hadjod (Cissus quadrangularis) 500 mg BID and consider K2-MK7 200 µg. *(Watson 2018 LIFTMOR; Knapen 2013 K2-MK7; Stohs 2013 Cissus)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

Osteoporosis is conventionally siloed as a "bone mineral density disorder" treated with bisphosphonates, denosumab, anabolic PTH analogs, romosozumab, SERMs, or HRT. The integrative reframing is different: bone is a **dynamic five-node tissue network** — (i) **mechanical loading** (osteocytes sense strain via the lacunocanalicular system; without load, bone resorbs); (ii) **endocrine signalling** (oestrogen + testosterone restrain osteoclast RANKL; both fall with age — postmenopausal bone loss runs 2–5%/yr × 5 yr); (iii) **mineral-cofactor stack** (vitamin D3 + K2-MK7 + magnesium + boron + protein — without K2, calcium goes to arteries, not bone); (iv) **muscle-bone unit** (sarcopenia + osteoporosis travel together; resistance training restores both); (v) **gut-bone axis** (microbiome regulates calcium absorption, SCFA + bone formation; coeliac, IBD and gut inflammation accelerate bone loss). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously loads bone heavily, replaces the mineral-cofactor stack, restores muscle, supports gut absorption, and addresses fall risk addresses upstream drivers — not bone-density numbers in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five changes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five parallel changes drive bone loss — loss of mechanical loading, sex-hormone withdrawal, mineral + cofactor depletion, sarcopenia, and gut-bone axis disruption. Hitting one is not enough. *(Riggs 2002; Black 2016)*

### 2.2 Mechanical loading — the master switch

> **For you, in plain words.** Bone responds to load. Heavy resistance training + jumping signals osteocytes to lay down bone; sedentary life resorbs it. LIFTMOR (Watson 2018, Australian) showed that postmenopausal women with low BMD doing heavy deadlift + squat + overhead press + jump-chins actually *reversed* lumbar BMD loss vs control. Walking alone is not enough. *(Watson 2018 LIFTMOR; Tobias 2014 jumping; Beck 2017)*

**Quick start.** 2–3 strength sessions/week of compound lifts at progressively challenging load (deadlift, squat, overhead press, pull-up — work up to 80–85% 1RM after 8–12 weeks of preparation) + impact training 3×/week (50 jumps from low height, then progress) + 7000–10000 steps/day baseline.

#### Open the science

Osteocytes sense strain via the lacunocanalicular fluid system. Strain ≥1500 microstrain signals via Wnt/β-catenin, downregulates sclerostin, upregulates osteoblasts. Walking generates ~500–800 microstrain (below bone-forming threshold); heavy resistance + impact = 3000–5000 microstrain. LIFTMOR (Watson 2018 Australian RCT, postmenopausal low BMD): 28 wk twice-weekly supervised HiRIT + jump-chins → lumbar BMD +2.9% (vs control −1.2%); femoral-neck +0.3% (vs −1.9%); functional measures all improved; one adverse event. Targeted by **heavy resistance (deadlift, squat, OHP, row, pull-up at challenging load); jumping (Tobias 2014); weighted vests (Greenway 2015 small signal); tai chi (Zhao 2017); vibration plates mixed (LOAD)**. **Synthesis.** Heavy load is non-negotiable. Walking is necessary but not sufficient.

### 2.3 Sex-hormone withdrawal

> **For you, in plain words.** Oestrogen and testosterone restrain RANKL — the signal that activates osteoclasts. When sex hormones drop (postmenopause, andropause, surgical removal, GnRH suppression, glucocorticoids), RANKL spikes and osteoclasts run unchecked. Bone loss runs 2–5%/yr in the first 5 postmenopausal years. *(Riggs 2002 NEJM; Khosla 2010)*

**Quick start.** Postmenopausal: combine the bone protocol with the menopause-protocol KNDy-modulators (Estrovera, sage, black cohosh) for symptom relief without HRT. Men with low T (free T <250 pg/mL on DTC): ashwagandha 600 mg + zinc 15 mg + adequate protein + heavy resistance training (the testosterone-supporting natural stack — not testosterone replacement). All: vitamin D + K2 + magnesium + heavy resistance training.

#### Open the science

Oestrogen restrains RANKL — without oestrogen, osteoblast-derived RANKL surges, osteoclasts proliferate, resorption outpaces formation. Oestrogen also restrains IL-1/IL-6/TNF-α. Testosterone is directly osteoblast-anabolic + indirectly via aromatisation. Glucocorticoids reduce osteoblast lifespan + Ca absorption + increase osteoclast lifespan + 2°HPT. Targeted by **menopause-protocol KNDy modulators (Estrovera, black cohosh, sage); ashwagandha (Lopresti 2019 — T +14.7% in stressed men); soy isoflavones (Liu 2017 modest, equol-dependent); K2-MK7 (Knapen 2013)**. **Synthesis.** Sex-hormone-floor support is real but modest; loading remains the master switch.

### 2.4 Mineral-cofactor stack

> **For you, in plain words.** Bone needs calcium AND the cofactors that route calcium INTO bone (not arteries). Vitamin D3 absorbs calcium; vitamin K2 (MK-7) activates osteocalcin to deposit calcium into bone matrix; magnesium activates vitamin D + calms PTH; boron supports oestrogen + vitamin D recycling; protein provides the bone matrix scaffold; trace minerals (zinc, copper, manganese, silica) are osteoblast cofactors. *(Avenell 2014 Cochrane vit D + Ca; Knapen 2013 K2-MK7; Castiglioni 2013 Mg)*

**Quick start.** Vitamin D3 4000–6000 IU + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 µg + magnesium glycinate 400 mg + boron 3 mg + zinc 15 mg + protein 1.2–1.6 g/kg + calcium-from-food (sesame, leafy greens, sardines with bones, coconut yoghurt) — NOT calcium supplements as the default per Bolland 2010 CV risk signal.

#### Open the science

Calcium is necessary but not sufficient — Bolland 2010 BMJ meta flagged ~30% RR increase in MI with Ca supplements alone. Hence favour **calcium-from-food + K2-MK7** over isolated supplements. Vit D3 enables intestinal Ca absorption via VDR + CYP27B1 → calcitriol. K2-MK7 carboxylates osteocalcin (deposits Ca into bone) AND MGP (inhibits arterial calcification) — Knapen 2013 Dutch RCT MK-7 180 µg/day × 3 yr preserved femoral-neck BMD. Mg activates vit D + calms PTH. Boron supports E + vit D recycling. Protein provides ~50% bone matrix (collagen I). Targeted by **vit D3 (Avenell 2014 — modest in deficient); K2-MK7 (Knapen 2013 — B); Mg glycinate; boron 3 mg; trace minerals (Zn, Cu, Mn, silica from horsetail); protein 1.2–1.6 g/kg; Ca-from-food preferred (Bolland 2010)**. **Synthesis.** The **mineral-cofactor stack**, not isolated Ca, supports bone.

### 2.5 Muscle-bone unit + sarcopenia

> **For you, in plain words.** Sarcopenia (muscle loss) and osteoporosis (bone loss) are the same disease in two tissues. Heavy resistance training rebuilds both. Grip strength predicts fragility-fracture risk + all-cause mortality independent of BMD. *(Pillai 2017 sarcopenia-osteoporosis; Leong 2015 PURE grip strength)*

**Quick start.** Heavy resistance 2–3×/week + 1.2–1.6 g/kg protein (more if over 70) + leucine-rich protein in every meal + home grip-strength dynamometer baseline + creatine monohydrate 5 g/day (one of the few supplements with consistent muscle + bone signal in older adults).

#### Open the science

Muscle pulls on bone via tendons; bigger muscle = bigger bone signal. Sarcopenia (~1%/yr from 30, ~3%/yr from 60) reduces loading + fall protection. Leong 2015 PURE (n=139,691, 17 countries, data object) — grip predicts all-cause + CV mortality independent of BMI + activity. Targeted by **heavy resistance training; 1.2–1.6 g/kg protein (0.8 g/kg RDA is deficiency-prevention floor, not optimum); leucine 2.5–3 g per meal; creatine 5 g/day (Candow 2015); HMB 3 g/day (Wilson 2013 modest); ashwagandha (Wankhede 2015)**. **Synthesis.** Treat sarcopenia + osteoporosis as one disease.

### 2.6 Gut-bone axis

> **For you, in plain words.** The gut microbiome regulates calcium absorption and SCFA-driven bone formation. Coeliac disease, IBD, gut inflammation, and proton-pump inhibitors (PPIs) all accelerate bone loss. *(Nilsson 2018 probiotic-bone trial; Tu 2020 gut-bone review)*

**Quick start.** Multi-strain probiotic 30–50 B CFU AM + 30–40 g fibre/day + fermented foods 100 g/day. If on chronic PPI, evaluate whether you still need it; coeliac/IBD requires aggressive bone-protective protocol because absorption is impaired. Adequate vitamin D (PPIs reduce B12 + calcium absorption).

#### Open the science

Gut microbiota influence bone via (a) SCFA (butyrate, propionate, acetate — pro-osteoblast); (b) Ca absorption; (c) IL-6/TNF-α regulation; (d) bile-acid + steroid recycling. Coeliac causes 2° osteoporosis via villous atrophy + impaired Ca + D absorption. IBD carries elevated osteoporosis risk via inflammation + steroid use. PPIs reduce Ca absorption (needs acid stomach) + modest fracture signal. Targeted by **multi-strain probiotic; Nilsson 2018 Swedish RCT (L. reuteri 6475 reduced lumbar BMD loss); fibre + fermented foods; coeliac elimination; PPI deprescribing where appropriate**. **Synthesis.** Gut absorption is the bottleneck.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Resistance + impact is the device

> **For you, in plain words.** The single most powerful "device" for bone is a barbell + a place to jump. LIFTMOR replicated in supervised home/community settings with appropriate progression. Start with bodyweight 4–6 weeks, dumbbells 6–12 weeks, barbell after 12 weeks if able. *(Watson 2018 LIFTMOR; Beck 2017)*

### 5.2 Whole-body vibration plates

> **For you, in plain words.** Power Plate, Galileo and consumer vibration plates have a mixed signal — the LOAD trial in the Netherlands suggested no effect on hip BMD vs control after 12 months, but smaller trials show benefit at higher frequencies (25–35 Hz) for postmenopausal lumbar BMD. Useful adjunct for those unable to do high-impact loading; not a replacement for resistance training. *(LOAD trial Slatkovska 2011; Verschueren 2004)*

### 5.3 Weighted vests

> **For you, in plain words.** Wearing a weighted vest (start 2–4 kg, build to 5–10% body weight) during daily activity adds load. Greenway 2015 small signal for hip BMD in older adults. Cheap, accessible — useful adjunct. *(Greenway 2015)*

### 5.4 Tai chi + balance work

> **For you, in plain words.** Fall prevention IS fracture prevention. Tai chi (Zhao 2017 meta), single-leg balance work, and structured TUG/SPPB practice reduce fall risk 30–40% in older adults. *(Zhao 2017 meta; Sherrington 2017 Cochrane fall prevention)*

### 5.5 Home red-light + infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Photobiomodulation has mechanistic + early signal for osteoblast activity (in vitro + animal); home red-light panels are useful general adjuncts. Home infrared sauna 20 min 2–3×/week aids systemic inflammation + circulation. *(Hamblin 2018 PBM review; Beever 2009 IR sauna)*

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods — daily

> **For you, in plain words.** Mediterranean + Asian high-protein, high-mineral, high-fibre pattern. Calcium-from-food is preferred to supplements (Bolland 2010 CV signal with supplements). *(Bolland 2010 BMJ meta; Mediterranean cohorts)*

**Quick start.** Mandatory daily intake (target gram quantities):

- Protein (fish, eggs, lentils, tempeh, whey, paneer) — **1.2–1.6 g/kg/day spread across 3–4 meals**
- Leafy greens (spinach, kale, methi, dill, collards) — **200–300 g**
- Sesame seeds (esp. white, with skin) — **30 g** (extremely calcium-dense)
- Sardines / mackerel with bones — **100 g 3×/week** (calcium + vitamin D + omega-3)
- Cooked cruciferous (broccoli, bok choy, Brussels sprouts) — **150–200 g**
- Coconut yoghurt OR cultured A2 dairy (if tolerated) — **150 g**
- Natto (fermented soybeans) — **40–50 g** (extreme K2-MK7 source) — Asian-grocery channel
- Tempeh / tofu / fermented soy — **80–100 g**
- Pulses (lentils, chickpeas, mung dal) — **80–100 g cooked**

- Fermented foods (sauerkraut, kanji, kimchi) — **100–150 g**
- Extra-virgin olive oil — **30–40 g**
- Berries — **80–100 g**
- Bone broth — **250 ml**
- Almonds + walnuts — **30 g**

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Five categories that accelerate bone loss.

- **Excess sodium** (>2300 mg/day) → renal calcium loss → urinary calcium excretion ↑ → secondary hyperparathyroidism → bone resorption.
- **Excess phosphoric acid** (cola drinks) → calcium-phosphorus imbalance → bone loss signal in cohorts.
- **Excess alcohol** (>1 drink/day women, >2 men) → directly suppresses osteoblasts + reduces vitamin D activation + impairs calcium absorption + falls risk.
- **Smoking** — direct osteoblast toxicity + accelerated menopause + reduced oestrogen + reduced calcium absorption. Quit.
- **Excess caffeine** (>400 mg/day) → modest urinary calcium loss (compensated by adequate calcium-from-food intake).

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

> **For you, in plain words.** Sprout millets to increase mineral bioavailability (phytate reduction). Soak nuts + seeds + grains 8–10 h. Slow-cook bone broth 24–48 h. Ferment naturally (kanji, sauerkraut, dosa, idli). Ghee-temper turmeric + black pepper.

### 6.5 Environmental + occupational triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Five categories specific to bone-density preservation.

- **Excess glucocorticoid use** — if you are on prednisone or inhaled steroids long-term, work with your prescriber on the lowest effective dose + bone-protective stack.
- **Long-term proton-pump inhibitors (PPIs)** — reduce calcium absorption + modest fracture signal. If you can deprescribe (with prescriber), do so.
- **Sedentary occupations** — sitting all day reduces mechanical load on bone. Walking breaks every hour, standing desk, stairs.
- **Aluminium cookware + antacids** — high aluminium intake binds phosphate + interferes with bone mineralisation. Use stainless steel, cast iron, glass.
- **Falls hazards at home** — loose rugs, poor lighting, lack of stair handrails, bathroom slip risks. Mitigate aggressively — fall prevention IS fracture prevention.
