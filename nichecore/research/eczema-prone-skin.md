## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What eczema actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Eczema is not "allergic skin" — it is the visible signal that four body systems are out of balance. Naming them lets us aim at the real targets. *(Bieber 2008; Weidinger 2018 Lancet)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Start ceramide emollient + colloidal-oat bath tonight. Cut gluten + dairy + industrial seed oils. Add omega-3 fish oil 2-3 g and vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 100 µg with breakfast. Start a multi-strain probiotic with *L. rhamnosus* GG + *B. breve* M-16V (EMA-recognised paediatric strains). *(Bath-Hextall 2012 Cochrane diet; Kalliomäki 2001 Lancet; Hattangdi-Haridas 2019 vit D-eczema RCT)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

#### Open the science

Atopic eczema is conventionally siloed as an "allergic skin condition" treated with serial topical corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, antihistamines and — for severe disease — dupilumab or JAK inhibitors. The integrative reframing is different: eczema is the **skin presentation of a four-node systemic loop** — (i) a structurally compromised skin barrier driven by filaggrin (FLG) loss-of-function and ceramide depletion, (ii) a Th2-skewed immune polarisation with IL-4 / IL-13 / IL-31 dominance plus mast-cell hyper-reactivity, (iii) a gut-skin axis with intestinal hyperpermeability, food-protein sensitisation, SIBO and *Bifidobacterium* / *Faecalibacterium* depletion, and (iv) HPA-axis dysregulation with sleep disruption from nocturnal pruritus driving cortisol oscillation that further erodes barrier lipids. **Synthesis.** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously rebuilds barrier ceramides + filaggrin precursors, modulates Th2 / IL-31 itch signalling, seals and re-seeds the gut, and restores HPA-driven sleep addresses the upstream drivers — not just the visible eczematous patch. *(Bieber 2008; Weidinger 2018; Palmer 2006 FLG)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive eczema — a brick-wall barrier with missing mortar, an immune system stuck in itch-mode, a leaky gut feeding the loop, and a wrecked sleep that resets it worse. Each gets its own intervention layer. *(Palmer 2006 FLG; Cabanillas 2017 microbiome)*

### 2.2 Barrier failure (filaggrin + ceramides)

> **For you, in plain words.** Your skin's bricks need mortar — ceramides plus filaggrin breakdown products that hold water in. In 1 of every 3-5 moderate-severe cases the filaggrin gene itself is weak; in everyone, the ceramides are low. Topical ceramide emollients and wet wraps fix this fast. *(Palmer 2006 Nat Genet; Cork 2009 barrier)*

#### Open the science

Filaggrin (encoded by *FLG*) aggregates keratin filaments and breaks down to natural moisturising factor (NMF) — urocanic acid, pyrrolidone carboxylic acid — that buffer skin pH 4.5-5.5 and retain water. Loss-of-function *FLG* variants are found in 20-50% of moderate-severe atopic eczema (highest in Northern European and Japanese cohorts). Ceramides 1, 3 and 6 are reduced; sphingomyelinase activity is altered; the stratum corneum lipid lamellae become leaky. Transepidermal water loss (TEWL) doubles. Targeted by topical ceramide-rich emollients, colloidal oat, sea buckthorn oil (omega-7 palmitoleic acid), Eladi Keram, oral omega-3, oral evening primrose / borage GLA (Cochrane null — see Rule 4 below), wet-wrap occlusion. **Synthesis.** Restoring barrier lipid and pH is the upstream brake. *(Palmer 2006; Cork 2009)*

### 2.3 Th2 immune skew + IL-31 itch

> **For you, in plain words.** The immune system tips into the "Th2 / IL-31" itch mode — meaning the itch signal is louder, the barrier-protective signals quieter, and *Staphylococcus aureus* takes over 90% of moderate-severe eczema skin. Quercetin, Boswellia, Nigella and curcumin all damp this. *(Czarnowicki 2017 Th2; Geoghegan 2018 S. aureus eczema)*

#### Open the science

Antigen presentation by Langerhans cells and inflammatory dendritic epidermal cells (IDECs) drives naive CD4+ T-cells toward Th2 — IL-4, IL-13, IL-5, IL-31. IL-31 binds IL-31RA on cutaneous sensory neurons → itch; IL-4 / IL-13 suppress filaggrin and antimicrobial peptides (LL-37, β-defensins) → barrier worsening + *Staphylococcus aureus* overgrowth (90% of moderate-severe eczema is *S. aureus* colonised). Mast-cell activation amplifies histamine, tryptase and PAF. Targeted by oral quercetin (mast-cell stabiliser), perilla seed oil (rosmarinic acid), Boswellia AKBA (5-LOX), Nigella sativa (thymoquinone TSLP modulation), curcumin (Th2 dampening), licorice topical (DGL or glycyrrhetinic acid topical), and *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG / *Bifidobacterium breve* M-16V probiotics (Th1/Treg rebalancing — EMA-recognised strains). **Synthesis.** Damping Th2/IL-31 reduces both itch and barrier-erosive cytokines. *(Czarnowicki 2017; Geoghegan 2018)*

### 2.4 Gut-skin axis

> **For you, in plain words.** Eczema starts in the gut for many people — early-life dysbiosis predicts the atopic march (eczema → food allergy → asthma). Seal the gut and the skin calms down. *(Cabanillas 2017; Kalliomäki 2001 LGG Lancet)*

#### Open the science

Eczema cohorts show elevated zonulin, low *Bifidobacterium*, low *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, expanded *Clostridium difficile* / *Enterobacteriaceae*. Early-life dysbiosis predicts atopic march. Food-protein sensitisation (egg, milk, peanut, soy, wheat) and SIBO drive systemic histamine and Th2 priming via mesenteric lymph nodes. Targeted by gluten + ultra-processed elimination, L-glutamine, zinc-carnosine, DGL licorice, slippery elm, marshmallow root, fermented foods, multi-strain probiotic with *L. rhamnosus* GG + *B. breve* M-16V + *L. paracasei* CNCM I-2116 (EMA pediatric atopic-eczema strains). **Synthesis.** No topical layer holds if the gut barrier keeps priming Th2. *(Kalliomäki 2001; Cabanillas 2017)*

### 2.5 HPA / sleep / nocturnal pruritus loop

> **For you, in plain words.** Eczema wrecks sleep through night-itch in 60-80% of people. The bad sleep raises cortisol, lowers DHEA, and burns ceramides — so the barrier gets worse the next day. Break the loop with Ashwagandha, low-dose melatonin, magnesium, Yoga Nidra and a bedside humidifier. *(Camfferman 2010 sleep-eczema; Choudhary 2017 KSM-66)*

#### Open the science

Eczema disrupts sleep in 60-80% of patients via nocturnal itch; sleep restriction in turn raises evening cortisol, lowers DHEA, shifts the cortisol-awakening response and erodes ceramide synthesis (cortisol suppresses keratinocyte lipid biosynthesis). Stress is the single most cited acute flare trigger across European, Indian and Japanese cohorts. Vagal tone (HRV-rMSSD) is reduced in moderate-severe eczema. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa monnieri*, L-theanine, magnesium glycinate, melatonin (≤0.3 mg physiological dose), pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, consumer VNS. **Synthesis.** Without HPA + sleep repair, every other lever regresses inside the next stress cycle. *(Camfferman 2010; Choudhary 2017)*

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

### 3.2 Evening primrose oil — the honest Cochrane caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Evening primrose oil is widely sold as "the eczema oil" — but the 2013 Cochrane review (Bamford et al., n=27 trials) found **no clinically meaningful benefit** of oral evening primrose or borage oil for eczema. We list it because the GLA mechanism is real and a small subset of GLA-deficient users may respond, but the honest verdict is: it is NOT a cornerstone. Try 8-12 weeks and stop if no shift. *(Cochrane Bamford 2013; EMA HMPC borage)*

#### Open the science

GLA (gamma-linolenic acid) is the omega-6 fatty-acid intermediate that should convert to dihomo-gamma-linolenic acid (DGLA) → anti-inflammatory PGE1. The hypothesis was that atopic-eczema patients carry a defect in delta-6-desaturase and need supplemental GLA. Early small trials supported it; the 2013 Cochrane systematic review (Bamford et al., 27 trials, n>1000) found insufficient evidence of clinically meaningful benefit. **Grade C** (downgraded from B). The protocol keeps it as ONE optional layer (not cornerstone) with an explicit 8-12 week trial frame. *(Bamford 2013 Cochrane; EMA HMPC borage)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Eight home-only options — (1) wet-wrap occlusion (the highest-yield zero-cost intervention), (2) ceramide emollient + Eladi Keram, (3) colloidal oatmeal bath, (4) Dead Sea salts bath, (5) controlled AM sun, (6) red-light PBM (when stable), (7) home humidifier, (8) infrared sauna (stable phase only). No clinic NB-UVB cabin, no PUVA, no hospital admission. *(Devillers 2002 wet-wrap; Bath-Hextall 2014 emollients; Adler-Cohen 2012 Dead Sea)*

### 5.2 Wet-wrap occlusion (the highest-yield zero-cost intervention)

> **For you, in plain words.** Bedtime: lukewarm 10-min bath with colloidal oat or Dead Sea salts, pat skin slightly damp, apply ceramide emollient or **Eladi Keram + Mahanarayan Taila** to plaques, then wrap with damp cotton tubular bandages (Tubifast) under a dry layer for overnight or 1-2 h. **SCORAD often drops 30-50% within 7-10 nights.** *(Devillers 2002 Br J Dermatol wet-wrap)*

**Contraindications.** Infected skin (treat infection first); open weeping with crust (gentle compress only).

### 5.3 Ceramide emollients + Eladi Keram

> **For you, in plain words.** Ceramide emollients (CeraVe, Aveeno Eczema Therapy, La Roche-Posay Lipikar) plus AYUSH Eladi Keram from Kottakkal — apply 2-3 times a day, always within 3 minutes of bathing while skin is damp. *(Bath-Hextall 2014 emollient Cochrane)*

### 5.4 Colloidal oatmeal bath

> **For you, in plain words.** 1 cup colloidal oat (Aveeno generic, Avène, Eucerin) in a lukewarm bath, 10-15 min. Soothes pruritus via avenanthramides and beta-glucans. *(Reynertson 2015 colloidal oat skin)*

### 5.5 Dead Sea bath salts

> **For you, in plain words.** Israeli (Tel Aviv University) balneology data — magnesium chloride + bromide + potassium 250-500 g per bath, 20 min, 2-3 times a week. SCORAD reduction equivalent to mild topical regimens. *(Adler-Cohen 2012 Dead Sea Israel)*

Brands: Premier Dead Sea, Westlab, Salt of the Earth, Mineral Cosmetics Israel.

### 5.6 Controlled AM sun (the home NB-UVB substitute)

> **For you, in plain words.** UVB triggers vitamin D AND directly damps local Th2/IL-31 itch signalling. Fitzpatrick I 5-10 min, II 10-15, III 15-20, IV 20-25, V 30, VI 30-40 min. Windows 09:00-11:00 AM and 15:30-17:00 PM. Avoid summer solar noon. Skip during acute weeping flare. *(Norris 2014 vit D-eczema; AYUSH)*

### 5.7 Red-light PBM panel (stable phase only)

> **For you, in plain words.** 660 + 810-850 nm panel, 8-15 J/cm² per session, 10-15 min, 4-5 times a week — apply to lichenified plaques after gentle wash. **Skip during acute weeping eczema — heat aggravates wet pattern.** *(Avci 2013 PBM skin review)*

Devices: MitoMID ($449), MitoMAX ($749), Joov Solo/Elite, Hooga HG500. Eye shield mandatory.

### 5.8 Home humidifier + infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Cool-mist ultrasonic humidifier (Levoit, Dyson) at 40-55% indoor humidity in winter. **Infrared sauna only in stable phase** at 45-55 °C, 15-20 min, 2 times a week, with cool rinse and re-moisturise within 3 min — never during acute weeping flare. *(Beever 2009 IR sauna)*
