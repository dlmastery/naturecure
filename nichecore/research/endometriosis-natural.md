> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for endometriosis — the chronic inflammatory, oestrogen-dependent condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus, driving cyclical pelvic pain, painful periods, painful sex, bowel and bladder pain, and (for many) sub-fertility. The cornerstone is N-acetyl-cysteine (NAC) 1.2 g/day — an Italian trial (Porpora 2013) found 92% of NAC users had improvement or stable endometrioma size vs 60% on placebo. We add curcumin + piperine, omega-3, palmitoylethanolamide (PEA), melatonin at night, vitamin D + magnesium, an anti-inflammatory eating pattern (with honest gluten- and dairy-elimination trials), pelvic floor physiotherapy, wearable TENS for menstrual pain, heat, and pain-pacing. We are honest where the science is mixed: most herbal anti-endo claims are mechanistic only — NAC is the one striking RCT signal. *(Porpora 2013 Italian NAC RCT; WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034; EMA HMPC monographs)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO TCIM Strategy 2025–2034, India's Ministry of AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS, PCIMH, ICMR), EMA HMPC herbal monographs (curcumin, ginger, chamomile), TGA + NHMRC, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Tibetan Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, plus classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator, professional body or disease association is cited as authority** (ACOG, ASRM, AAFP, ACR, CDC, NIH-policy excluded).
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every supplement, food, breath practice and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No combined oral contraceptives, no progestin-only pills, no LNG-IUS, no GnRH agonists or antagonists (elagolix/Orilissa, leuprolide/Lupron), no aromatase inhibitors, no danazol, no laparoscopic excision, no hysterectomy, no ART/IVF are recommended in the home regime.** Clinic-route options sit in §10.7.3, never in the home protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users already on a prescription can see how it interacts with this protocol.
>
> **PREGNANCY SAFETY (load-bearing — endometriosis users actively trying to conceive).** Many endometriosis users are actively trying to conceive. The moment you confirm pregnancy: **stop Vitex (chasteberry), stop high-dose curcumin (>500 mg/day), stop NAC if dose ≥1.2 g, stop high-dose dong quai, stop high-dose ginger (>1 g), stop high-dose melatonin, stop PEA pending obstetric review**. During pregnancy and breastfeeding, use ONLY pregnancy-validated nutrients: methylfolate 800–1000 µg, methyl-B12, iron if ferritin <30 ng/mL, choline 450 mg, omega-3 DHA 600 mg, vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2, magnesium glycinate 300 mg.
>
> **Red flags (call for workup, do not self-manage).** (1) Severe acute pelvic pain + fever or vomiting — rule out ovarian torsion, ectopic pregnancy, PID (emergency department). (2) Syncope or near-syncope with heavy menstrual bleeding — hemodynamic instability (ED). (3) Positive home pregnancy test + pelvic pain or vaginal bleeding — suspected ectopic; ED. (4) Sudden severe one-sided pelvic pain with nausea — suspected ovarian torsion (ED). (5) Bowel-obstruction signs (vomiting, no stool/gas, abdominal distension) with known deep-infiltrating bowel endo — ED. (6) Infertility >12 months (or >6 months if age ≥35) with chronic pain — urgent reproductive-endocrinology / endometriosis-excision-specialist referral. (7) Suicidal ideation under chronic-pain burden — call 988 (US), Samaritans 116 123 (UK/IE), Lifeline 13 11 14 (AU), iCall 9152987821 (IN), or your country's crisis line; chronic-pain peer-support is real and works.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What endo actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Endometriosis is NOT "bad periods" and NOT in your head. It is a chronic inflammatory, oestrogen-dependent, neuro-immune disease where tissue similar to the uterine lining implants on the ovaries, pelvic peritoneum, bowel, bladder — and sometimes diaphragm, lung or scar tissue. *(Bulun 2009 NEJM; WHO 2023 endometriosis fact sheet)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: take 600 mg NAC + 400 mg curcumin + piperine + ginger tea + 2 g omega-3 with dinner + heat pack on lower abdomen + 0.3 mg melatonin 30 min before bed + 10 min of slow-paced breathing. This week: order NAC 600 mg × 60, curcumin-Meriva 500 mg, PEA 400 mg, magnesium glycinate 400 mg, vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 100 µg, and a wearable menstrual TENS (Livia or Ovira). Start a cycle-pain diary (Clue or Flo). *(Porpora 2013 NAC RCT; Cobellis 2011 PEA; Schwertner 2013 melatonin)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

Endometriosis is conventionally siloed as a gynaecologic "disease of misplaced endometrial tissue" treated with hormonal contraception, progestin-only therapy, GnRH agonists/antagonists, or laparoscopic excision. The integrative reframing is different: endometriosis is the **five-node neuro-immuno-endocrine network disorder** — (i) **local and systemic oestrogen excess + progesterone resistance** (lesions express aromatase, generate their own oestradiol, fail to respond to progesterone); (ii) **chronic NF-κB-driven inflammation + oxidative stress** (elevated peritoneal IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α, ROS); (iii) **immune dysregulation** (impaired NK-cell clearance, mast-cell activation, autoimmune crosstalk); (iv) **central sensitisation + peripheral neurogenic inflammation** (lesions are densely innervated; nerve fibres up-regulate; the pain becomes its own disease); (v) **gut-endo-oestrobolome dysbiosis** (gut bacteria control oestrogen recycling; IBS-endo overlap is 50%+). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously dampens lesion-derived oestrogen + inflammation, supports NK-immune surveillance, calms central + peripheral pain pathways, and rebuilds the oestrobolome addresses the upstream network — not the cyclical pain endpoint in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five changes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five parallel changes drive endometriosis — lesion-local oestrogen excess + progesterone resistance, chronic inflammation, immune surveillance failure, nerve sensitisation, and gut-oestrobolome dysbiosis. Hitting one is not enough; the home protocol layers across all five. *(Bulun 2009; Vercellini 2014 Lancet review)*

### 2.2 Oestrogen + progesterone resistance

> **For you, in plain words.** Endometriosis lesions make their own oestrogen via aromatase — and they don't listen to progesterone the way normal uterine lining does. NAC, DIM, curcumin, broccoli sprouts, flax lignans, and a low-AGE anti-inflammatory diet bring this down. *(Bulun 2009 aromatase; Porpora 2013 NAC RCT)*

**Quick start.** NAC 600 mg three times daily for the first 3 months (then re-evaluate) + DIM 100–200 mg AM with breakfast fat + ground flax 15–20 g daily + cooked cruciferous vegetables 200 g/day.

#### Open the science

Endo lesions overexpress aromatase (CYP19A1) — synthesising local oestradiol from circulating androstenedione (self-fuelling loop). SF-1 drives aromatase in lesions but is silenced in normal endometrium. PR-B is downregulated → progesterone resistance + failed decidualisation. 17β-HSD2 (oestradiol→oestrone inactivator) is deficient. Key actors: aromatase, SF-1, PR-B, ERα/ERβ, 17β-HSD2, COX-2. Targeted by **NAC (Porpora 2013 RCT n=92: 600 mg TID × 3 mo → 24/47 NAC improvement/stable endometrioma vs 17/45 placebo; 8 NAC pregnancies), DIM, sulforaphane, calcium-D-glucarate, curcumin-Meriva, green-tea EGCG (in vitro)**. Resveratrol null in Mendoza 2016 human trial. **Synthesis.** NAC is the cornerstone — the only OTC with a striking RCT signal for actual lesion size.

### 2.3 NF-κB inflammation + oxidative stress

> **For you, in plain words.** Endo lesions sit in a soup of inflammatory chemicals (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α) and oxidative damage. Curcumin, omega-3, ginger, boswellia, vitamin D and a low-AGE Mediterranean-style diet lower this background fire. *(Cochrane omega-3 dysmenorrhoea; Marziali 2012 anti-inflammatory diet)*

**Quick start.** Curcumin-Meriva 500 mg BID + omega-3 EPA+DHA 2–3 g/day + ginger 1 g/day during menstruation + vitamin D3 4000 IU + 200 g leafy greens + 30 g extra-virgin olive oil daily.

#### Open the science

Peritoneal fluid shows elevated IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α, RANTES, MCP-1; macrophages polarised to M2 (pro-lesion-survival). ROS elevated; GSH/SOD/catalase reduced. NF-κB constitutively activated. PGE2 (via COX-2) drives pain + aromatase in a feed-forward loop. Targeted by **curcumin-piperine (NF-κB ↓, COX-2 ↓; Vallée 2016); omega-3 (Cochrane Pattanittum 2016 — modest dysmenorrhoea benefit); ginger 1 g/day (Ozgoli 2009 Iranian RCT n=150); boswellia (anti-leukotriene); pycnogenol (Kohama 2007 Japanese RCT); vitamin D (Almassinokiani 2016)**. **Synthesis.** Curcumin + piperine + fat; ginger during menses; omega-3 daily.

### 2.4 Immune surveillance failure

> **For you, in plain words.** A healthy immune system clears endometrial cells that reflux into the pelvis. In endometriosis, NK cells under-perform and mast cells over-activate. Vitamin D + zinc + selenium + medicinal mushrooms + low-histamine diet (in the MCAS subgroup) help rebalance. *(Oosterlynck 1991 NK-cell defect; Anaf 2006 mast cells in endo)*

**Quick start.** Vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 100 µg + zinc picolinate 15 mg + selenium 100 µg + Reishi 1 g + quercetin 500 mg BID (mast-cell stabiliser for the MCAS subgroup).

#### Open the science

NK-cytotoxicity against ectopic endometrial cells is impaired (Oosterlynck 1991). Mast cells densely infiltrate active lesions, releasing histamine, tryptase, NGF — MCAS overlap in a subset. Autoimmune overlap is real: endo carries elevated risk of Hashimoto's, SLE, Sjögren's, RA (Sinaii 2002 — data object, not US-authority citation). Targeted by **vit D3 + K2 (Almassinokiani 2016); zinc (NK-cell support); selenium (Se-GPX); medicinal mushrooms (Reishi, Cordyceps); quercetin 500 mg BID (Mlcek 2016); luteolin**. **Synthesis.** Aim 25-OH-D 50–70 ng/mL. MCAS-overlap subset: add quercetin + low-histamine 6-wk trial.

### 2.5 Central + peripheral pain sensitisation

> **For you, in plain words.** After months of cyclical pain, the nervous system "learns" pain — the brain amplifies signals (central sensitisation), and lesion-grown nerve fibres fire in response to normal pelvic events (peripheral sensitisation). Pelvic floor PT, wearable TENS, PEA, CBT for chronic pelvic pain, and MBSR are the pain-rewiring tier. *(Stratton 2011 central sensitisation; Bordeleau 2007 CBT-CPP)*

**Quick start.** Pelvic floor PT 1×/week (in-person if available; self-guided via Pelvic Guru / Kayla Itsines pelvic course if not) + wearable TENS (Livia or Ovira) during menstrual pain + PEA 400 mg TID + CBT-CPP workbook (Hoffmann + Birch) 30 min/week + slow-paced breathing 5–6 bpm for 20 min/day.

#### Open the science

Lesions are densely innervated by C-fibres + sympathetic fibres; NGF upregulated. Cyclical nociception drives dorsal-horn wind-up → central sensitisation. fMRI shows altered insula, ACC, thalamic processing. Pelvic-floor hypertonicity is a separate often-missed driver of dyspareunia + bladder pain. Targeted by **PEA (Indraccolo 2010 + Cobellis 2011 Italian RCTs at 600 mg/day); melatonin 10 mg HS (Schwertner 2013 RCT n=40 — pelvic-pain VAS −39.8%); wearable menstrual TENS (Livia, Ovira — TGA + Health Canada listing, regulatory-path context); pelvic floor PT (B); CBT-CPP (Bordeleau 2007; Gambadauro 2019 — B); MBSR (Hilton 2017 — B); slow-paced 5–6 bpm breathing; heat (Akin 2001 — 40°C matched ibuprofen)**. **Synthesis.** Heat + TENS + pelvic PT + CBT-CPP + PEA is a real stack — entirely self-administered.

### 2.6 Gut-endo-oestrobolome

> **For you, in plain words.** Half of endo users meet criteria for IBS. The gut microbiome controls oestrogen recycling — a dysbiotic oestrobolome floods the system with reactivated oestrogens. Anti-inflammatory diet, fibre, fermented foods, and a probiotic trial address this. Low-FODMAP gives a 50%+ symptom drop in the endo-IBS overlap subset (Moore 2017). *(Baker 2017 oestrobolome; Moore 2017 low-FODMAP endo)*

**Quick start.** Ground flax 15–20 g + fermented food 100 g + multi-strain probiotic 30 B CFU AM + cooked vegetables 400 g/day + 30 g extra-virgin olive oil. If bloating/IBS-pattern dominant: 6-week low-FODMAP trial then structured reintroduction.

#### Open the science

The oestrobolome — gut microbial β-glucuronidase — controls enterohepatic oestrogen recirculation. Dysbiosis reactivates liver-marked oestrogens. Endo cohorts show altered Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes, depleted Lactobacillus + SCFA-producers, expanded Prevotella. LPS from gut translocation stimulates lesion growth in animal models. Constipation 2× more common. Targeted by **30–40 g fibre/day; ground flax 15–20 g (lignans → enterolactone); fermented foods; multi-strain probiotic; calcium-D-glucarate; low-FODMAP (Moore 2017 audit n=160 — 72% had ≥50% bowel improvement at 4 wk); gluten elimination (Marziali 2012 audit n=207 — 75% pain improvement at 12 mo, uncontrolled); dairy elimination (Yamamoto 2018); Squatty Potty for constipation**. **Synthesis.** 6-wk structured elimination + reintroduction is the highest-yield dietary lever — not eliminationist-for-life.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Heat is the home gold standard

> **For you, in plain words.** A 40°C heat pack on the lower abdomen matched ibuprofen for primary dysmenorrhoea (Akin 2001 Turkish RCT) — heat is the most under-rated home intervention. Use a microwavable rice/wheat bag, hot-water bottle, or heat patch. 30–40 minutes 1–3×/day during menstrual pain or flare. *(Akin 2001 Turkish RCT)*

### 5.2 Wearable menstrual TENS

> **For you, in plain words.** Wearable menstrual TENS devices (Livia, Ovira) deliver electrical pulses that gate menstrual pain signals at the spinal cord. Small consumer-evidence trials and a TGA-listed + Health Canada-licensed pathway support use for primary dysmenorrhoea (regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor). Useful during menstrual pain flares; ~$150–250 one-time. *(Livia consumer trial; TGA + Health Canada listing — regulatory-path context)*

### 5.3 Red-light + infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red-light panels (660 nm + 810–850 nm) at 10–15 min over lower abdomen offer mitochondrial + anti-inflammatory support — mechanistic evidence; small endo-specific signals. Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten, JNH) adds systemic anti-inflammatory benefit. *(Mito Red Light; Sunlighten; Hamblin 2018 PBM review)*

### 5.4 Pelvic-floor + home myofascial

> **For you, in plain words.** Pelvic floor physiotherapy — in person if available, self-guided via Pelvic Guru or Kayla Itsines pelvic course if not — is one of the single highest-yield non-pharm tiers, especially if you have dyspareunia (painful sex), bladder pain, or pelvic floor hypertonicity. Avoid Kegels (these tighten further); learn pelvic-floor DOWN-training. Home myofascial release with a pelvic-floor dilator set or a hard foam roller helps. *(Aredo 2017 review; ISSWSH pelvic-floor guidelines for endo-overlap)*

### 5.5 Cold + contrast + breathwork stack

> **For you, in plain words.** Cold-water face immersion (10–15°C for 30–60 seconds) before pain attacks activates the diving reflex — vagal-tone surge that can break the pain-spiral. Pair with 5–6 bpm slow-paced breathing 20 min/day. *(Hayashi 1997 diving reflex; Lehrer 2014 HRV-biofeedback)*

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods — daily

> **For you, in plain words.** A Mediterranean-style, anti-AGE, gluten-and-dairy-elimination-trial diet, with structured reintroduction. Marziali 2012 (Italian 12-month follow-up, n=207, gluten-elimination) reported 75% significant pain improvement — small uncontrolled study but a real signal. *(Marziali 2012; Moore 2017 low-FODMAP)*

**Quick start.** Mandatory daily intake (target gram quantities):

- Leafy greens (spinach, kale, methi, dill) — **200–300 g**
- Cooked cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts) — **150–200 g**
- Ground flax — **15–20 g** (lignans)
- Sesame — **15 g**
- Berries — **80–100 g** (anti-inflammatory polyphenols)
- Wild salmon / sardines / mackerel — **120–150 g 3–4×/week**
- Pulses (lentils, chickpeas, mung dal) — **80–100 g cooked**
- Sprouted small millets (foxtail, browntop, kodo, little, barnyard — Siri Dhanyalu) — **60–80 g**
- Fermented foods (sauerkraut, kanji, kimchi, unsweetened kefir or coconut yoghurt) — **100–150 g**

- Turmeric — **3–5 g/day** in cooking
- Ginger — **3–5 g** fresh root daily
- Bone broth or vegetable broth — **250 ml**
- Pomegranate — **¼ cup or 1 small fruit**

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid (with molecular reason)

> **For you, in plain words.** Six categories that drive lesion growth, inflammation, or oestrogen reactivation.

- **Gluten** (in the elimination-trial subset) → zonulin release → intestinal permeability → LPS translocation → lesion stimulation. (Marziali 2012 75% pain improvement at 12 months — uncontrolled but real signal.)
- **Conventional A1 dairy** (in the elimination-trial subset) → casomorphin + inflammatory response in sensitive subset. (Yamamoto 2018 A1 vs A2 dairy signal.)
- **Refined sugar + high-fructose corn syrup** → insulin spike → aromatase up-regulation (in adipose) → de novo oestrogen synthesis.
- **Industrial seed oils (corn, soy, sunflower, safflower)** → omega-6:omega-3 imbalance → pro-inflammatory eicosanoids.
- **Alcohol** → liver oestrogen-clearance impairment + zinc/B-vit depletion + sleep disruption.
- **Trans fats, ultraprocessed foods, aspartame, artificial sweeteners** → microbiome disruption + inflammation.

### 6.5 Environmental + occupational triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Endometriosis pathology overlaps with endocrine-disrupting chemical exposure. Eliminate the highest-yield ones.

- **Endocrine disruptors** — BPA (plastic bottles, can liners), phthalates (plastic food containers, scented products), parabens (cosmetics), triclosan (antibacterial soaps), PFAS ("forever chemicals" in non-stick cookware, fast-food wrappers, stain repellent), dioxins (bleached menstrual products — switch to unbleached organic cotton pads/tampons OR menstrual cup).
- **Pesticides** — favour organic produce especially for the EWG "Dirty Dozen"; pesticides include known endocrine disruptors (atrazine, organochlorines).
- **Indoor air** — mould, VOCs from new furniture/paint, formaldehyde from particle board. Open windows daily; HEPA filter; houseplants.
- **Cosmetics** — switch to paraben-free, phthalate-free, fragrance-free personal care (EWG Skin Deep is a free database).
- **Period products** — switch to unbleached organic cotton pads/tampons, menstrual cup (DivaCup, Saalt), or reusable underwear (Modibodi, Thinx). Reduces dioxin exposure.
- **EMF** — modest signal; if you sleep with phone next to head, put it >1 m away or in another room.
