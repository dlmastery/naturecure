## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What low mood actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Low mood and depression-like presentations are not "weakness" or "all in your head". They are a real, measurable convergence of neurotransmitter depletion, inflammation in the brain, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural-cognitive patterns of avoidance and rumination. All five layers can be rebuilt at home. *(Cuijpers 2020 behavioural activation Cochrane; Su 2018 omega-3 meta; Cryan & Dinan 2012 gut-brain)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: write down one small thing that used to give you pleasure and schedule it for tomorrow (10 minutes; this is behavioural activation, the A-grade evidence layer). Cut alcohol completely for two weeks. Go outside within 30 minutes of waking for 10 minutes of direct sunlight. This week: download a self-applied CBT-for-depression workbook free from your library (David Burns *Feeling Good* or Greenberger & Padesky *Mind Over Mood*). Book a direct-to-consumer thyroid + B12 + vitamin D + ferritin + omega-3 panel — hypothyroidism, B12 deficiency, low ferritin and low vitamin D all mimic depression. If your PHQ-9 is ≥15, OR you have any suicidal thoughts, call the crisis line in §11.3 BEFORE starting any supplement layer. *(Cochrane Cuijpers 2020; Hofmann CBT meta; Bot 2017 B12-folate-depression)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story shrugs depression off as "a chemical imbalance you need an SSRI for" — which is, at best, one slice of a five-driver picture, and the STAR-D trial (Insel 2006, n=4,041) showed that only ~30% of patients achieve remission on first-line SSRI, with ~50% remaining symptomatic after four sequential medication trials. A more honest reframe: persistent low mood is the output of (i) **monoamine + BDNF + neurogenesis depletion** (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, BDNF, hippocampal neurogenesis all down), (ii) **chronic neuroinflammation** with elevated IL-6 + TNF-α + hs-CRP shifting tryptophan into the kynurenine pathway away from serotonin, (iii) **mitochondrial bioenergetic dysfunction** with reduced ATP and reduced PGC-1α underlying anhedonia + fatigue, (iv) **gut-brain axis dysbiosis** with reduced Lactobacillus/Bifidobacterium, altered SCFA, and elevated LPS, and (v) **behavioural-cognitive-relational** patterns (rumination, avoidance, loss of meaning, social withdrawal). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **behavioural activation + CBT/MBCT** (the A-grade frontline) with monoamine support (saffron, omega-3 EPA), anti-inflammatory layers (curcumin, EPA), mitochondrial rebuild (CoQ10, NAD+, B-complex), gut-brain repair (psychobiotics, fermented foods), and a measured cofactor floor (vitamin D, B12, folate, iron, magnesium, zinc) hits all five drivers in parallel.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-driver)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive depression — depleted neurotransmitters, brain inflammation, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural patterns of avoidance and rumination. Naming them lets every food, breath, supplement and behavioural shift aim at the right one. *(Cuijpers 2020; Su 2018; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

#### Open the science

The five-driver model converges literature from Maes 2011 (inflammation-depression), Su 2018 (omega-3 EPA meta), Cryan & Dinan 2012 (gut-brain), Duman 2016 (BDNF + neurogenesis), Manji 2003 (mitochondria + bipolar). Cochrane Cuijpers 2020 (behavioural activation, n=53 RCTs) and Cochrane Hofmann 2012 (CBT meta) underpin the behavioural pillar. **Synthesis.** No single mechanism explains all of depression; the home protocol intervenes at each layer in parallel.

### 2.2 Monoamine + BDNF depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** Depression involves reduced serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine AND reduced BDNF (the protein that grows new brain connections). Saffron, omega-3 EPA, exercise, Lion's Mane and sunlight all rebuild this substrate. *(Akhondzadeh 2005 saffron RCT; Hausenblas 2013 saffron meta; Erickson 2011 exercise + BDNF)*

#### Open the science

Key actors: TPH2 (tryptophan hydroxylase, serotonin synthesis), tyrosine hydroxylase (dopamine synthesis), MAO-A/B, SERT, DAT, BDNF, TrkB, mTOR. Hippocampal neurogenesis is reliably reduced in depression (Sahay & Hen 2007). Targeted by **saffron** (Akhondzadeh 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40; Hausenblas 2013 meta, 6 trials n=230, ES equivalent to SSRI for mild-moderate); **omega-3 EPA ≥1 g/d** (Su 2018 meta, n=26 RCTs); **Lion's Mane** (Hericium erinaceus — NGF + BDNF in animal models, Mori 2009 small Japanese RCT); **aerobic exercise** (Blumenthal SMILE 1999/2007 — exercise non-inferior to sertraline; Schuch 2016 meta); **morning sunlight** (Lambert 2002 serotonin synthesis sunlight correlation); **social connection** (Cacioppo loneliness-depression literature). **Synthesis.** This is the neurochemical lever.

### 2.3 Neuroinflammation + kynurenine shunt

> **For you, in plain words.** Inflammation in the body raises chemicals that pull tryptophan AWAY from making serotonin and TOWARD making neurotoxic kynurenine. Lowering inflammation lifts mood through this same lever. Omega-3 EPA, curcumin and a Mediterranean diet are the highest-evidence anti-inflammatory layers. *(Maes 2011 IDO-kynurenine; Su 2018; Lopresti 2014 curcumin-depression meta)*

#### Open the science

Elevated IL-6, TNF-α, hs-CRP, IFN-γ correlate with depression severity (Dowlati 2010 meta). IDO (indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase) is activated by inflammation, shunting tryptophan to neurotoxic quinolinic acid, an NMDA agonist. Targeted by **omega-3 EPA ≥1 g/d** (anti-inflammatory; Su 2018 meta); **curcumin BCM-95 or Meriva 500 mg BID** (Lopresti 2014 meta, 6 RCTs, moderate signal vs placebo); **resveratrol**; **Boswellia AKBA**; **Mediterranean-MIND diet** (Jacka SMILES 2017 RCT n=67 — diet intervention reduced depression scores by ~32%); **gluten elimination trial if marker-positive**; **gut-permeability repair**; **micronutrient floor** (zinc, magnesium, vitamin D); **reduced visceral adiposity**; **smoking cessation**. **Synthesis.** Lowering systemic inflammation is the upstream anti-depressant lever — often the missing piece in SSRI non-responders.

### 2.4 Mitochondrial bioenergetic failure

> **For you, in plain words.** Anhedonia (the inability to feel pleasure) and fatigue track mitochondrial energy failure — the cellular power plants are not making enough ATP. CoQ10, NAD+ precursors, B-vitamins, magnesium, creatine and red-light therapy rebuild this substrate. *(Manji 2003 bipolar-mito; Wallace mito-aging literature; Hamblin photobiomodulation 2017)*

#### Open the science

Depression — particularly anhedonia + fatigue dimensions — correlates with reduced mitochondrial ATP, reduced Complex I/IV activity, reduced PGC-1α, oxidative damage. Key actors: Complex I, Complex IV, ATP synthase, CoQ10, NAD+, carnitine, α-lipoic acid, magnesium, PGC-1α. Targeted by **CoQ10/Ubiquinol 100-300 mg**; **NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR) 250-500 mg**; **acetyl-L-carnitine 1-2 g** (Wang 2014 ALC depression meta); **α-lipoic acid 300-600 mg**; **magnesium glycinate 400 mg**; **creatine 3-5 g** (Lyoo 2012 creatine augmentation RCT); **B-complex** with methylated forms (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6 P-5-P, B9 5-MTHF, B12 methylcobalamin); **transcranial photobiomodulation 810-1064 nm** (Cassano 2018 Vielight pilots; Schiffer 2009 — emerging signal); **Zone 2 cardio rebuild**. **Synthesis.** Restoring mitochondria substrates the energy + reward systems.

### 2.5 Gut-brain axis dysbiosis

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria make ~90% of your body's serotonin precursors and most of your short-chain fatty acids. Depressed cohorts reliably show lower Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium and higher gut permeability. Psychobiotics + fermented foods + Mediterranean fibre rebuild this. *(Cryan & Dinan 2012 Nature; Messaoudi 2011 RCT; Jacka SMILES 2017)*

#### Open the science

Depressed cohorts show altered microbiome — reduced Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Faecalibacterium; increased zonulin + LPS; altered tryptophan-serotonin-kynurenine balance; reduced SCFA. The gut produces ~90% of body serotonin and signals to the brain via vagal afferents, SCFA (butyrate), and immune mediators. Targeted by **multi-strain psychobiotics** — Lactobacillus helveticus R0052 + Bifidobacterium longum R0175 (Cerebiome blend; Messaoudi 2011 French RCT n=55 — anxiety + depression + cortisol reduction); **Lactobacillus rhamnosus JB-1**; **fermented foods** 100-150 g/day (kefir, sauerkraut, kanji, kimchi, dosa batter — Hilimire 2015 Oregon cohort fermented-food-anxiety); **prebiotic fibre** 25-35 g/day; **dietary tryptophan** (turkey, eggs, pumpkin seeds, oats); **elimination of ultra-processed food and chronic alcohol**; **L-glutamine 5 g**; **zinc-carnosine** if hyperpermeable. **Synthesis.** Often the slowest-moving but most durable layer.

### 2.6 Behavioural-cognitive-relational layer

> **For you, in plain words.** Rumination, avoidance, loss of meaning and social withdrawal are not symptoms TO be fixed by supplements — they are themselves causal drivers that lock the depression in. Behavioural activation (re-engaging in pleasurable + meaningful activities, even when you don't feel like it) has Cochrane A-grade evidence. *(Cochrane Cuijpers 2020 behavioural activation n=53 RCTs; Dimidjian 2006 BA vs paroxetine; Jacobson 1996)*

#### Open the science

This is the highest-evidence non-pharmacological layer for depression. Targeted by **behavioural activation** (Jacobson 1996; Dimidjian 2006 BA non-inferior to paroxetine for severe depression, n=241; Cuijpers 2020 Cochrane meta n=53 RCTs); **CBT-for-depression** (Beck cognitive therapy; Hofmann 2012 Cochrane meta); **MBCT (Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy)** — Segal/Williams/Teasdale 8-week program (Kuyken 2016 meta — equivalent to maintenance antidepressants for relapse prevention); **iCBT/internet-delivered CBT** (Andrews 2018 meta n=64 studies); **Woebot/Wysa app-based** (small trials); **ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy)** — Harris *The Happiness Trap*; **IPT (Interpersonal Therapy)**; **values clarification + meaningful re-engagement**; **social co-regulation** (face-to-face time with trusted others — load-bearing); **nature exposure** (forest bathing / Shinrin-yoku — Park 2010 Japanese trials); **gratitude practice** (3 things daily); **creative-expressive practices**. **Synthesis.** Behavioural and relational shifts are non-negotiable — supplements alone do not restore meaning.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest cautions on **St John's Wort (EXCLUDED — CYP3A4 wrecks contraceptives, SSRIs, warfarin, statins; serotonin-syndrome risk)**, **5-HTP (EXCLUDED — serotonin syndrome risk with antidepressants too high for home self-administration)**, **L-tyrosine + Rhodiola + SAMe (use ONLY if no bipolar-spectrum history — they can precipitate mania)**, and **kava (EXCLUDED — rare severe liver toxicity)**. *(EMA SJW monograph 2018; EMA SJW SmPC; EMA kava 2002; Linde 2008 Cochrane SJW)*

### 3.2 Saffron — the strongest single-trial signal

> **For you, in plain words.** Saffron (Crocus sativus stigma extract, standardised affron or Iranian Novin Saffron) has the strongest single-trial evidence in this category. Iranian Akhondzadeh trials (2005, 2007) showed equivalence to fluoxetine 20 mg and imipramine 100 mg for mild-moderate depression at 30 mg/day over 6 weeks. Hausenblas 2013 meta pooled 6 trials with consistent effect. EMA herbal monograph 2022. Try 28-30 mg once daily for 6-8 weeks. **Caution**: 4-hour gap from SSRIs/SNRIs (serotonergic). *(EMA saffron monograph 2022; Akhondzadeh 2005 n=40; Hausenblas 2013 meta)*

#### Open the science

Active compounds: **crocin** (carotenoid glycoside) and **safranal** (volatile aldehyde). Mechanism: monoamine oxidase inhibition, serotonin reuptake modulation, NMDA antagonism, HPA effects. Trials: Akhondzadeh 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40 (equivalence); Akhondzadeh 2007 vs imipramine, n=30 (equivalence); Moshiri 2006 vs placebo; Noorbala 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40; Kashani 2017 RCT in postpartum depression, n=60 (significant benefit); Modabbernia 2012 in perimenopausal depression; Hausenblas 2013 meta of 6 trials confirming consistent effect size; Lopresti 2014 meta confirming. Standardised brand: **affron** (Pharmactive, Spain), **Novin Saffron**, **Bonusan Saffron**. **Grade A** for mild-moderate depression — the highest-graded single botanical in this dossier.

### 3.3 Omega-3 EPA — the inflammation lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Omega-3 EPA (specifically EPA, not DHA) at >1 g/day has moderate signal for depression, especially in inflammation-driven and SSRI-resistant cases. Su 2018 meta-analysis confirmed signal. Pair with a low-omega-6 diet. *(Su 2018 omega-3 EPA meta; UK SACN omega-3 2020; EMA fish oil monograph)*

#### Open the science

The EPA:DHA ratio matters — meta-analyses (Su 2018; Mocking 2016) converge on EPA-dominant formulations being more efficacious than DHA-dominant for depression. EPA >1 g/day for 8-12 weeks. Mechanism: PUFA membrane incorporation; eicosanoid balance shift (PGE2 → PGE3); reduced IL-6/TNF-α; BDNF support. **Grade B** as monotherapy, **Grade A** as antidepressant adjunct (Sarris 2016 omega-3 augmentation meta).

## 5. Biophysical & Sensory Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence non-pharmacological layer for depression is **behavioural activation + CBT/MBCT** — read §10.7.2 first. For SAD specifically, the single highest-evidence biophysical layer is the **10,000-lux bright light box for 20-30 minutes within an hour of waking** (Lam 2016 CAN-SAD trial showed equivalence to fluoxetine in non-seasonal MDD too). Devices come after that. *(Cochrane Cuijpers 2020 BA; Lam 2016 CAN-SAD trial; Cochrane Pjrek 2020 bright light)*

**Quick start, tonight.**
1. Schedule one 10-minute "pleasure activity" for tomorrow morning (walk, music, calling a friend, garden time) — write it down BEFORE bed. This is behavioural activation.
2. Set your phone for an outdoor light walk within 30 minutes of waking.
3. If winter or SAD-pattern: order a 10,000-lux bright light box this week.

### 5.2 Bright light therapy (10,000 lux) — the SAD A-grade

> **For you, in plain words.** A 10,000-lux bright light box for 20-30 minutes within an hour of waking is A-grade evidence for SAD AND has equivalent-to-fluoxetine effect in non-seasonal MDD (Lam 2016 CAN-SAD trial). The single highest-evidence biophysical depression layer. *(Cochrane Pjrek 2020 bright light; Lam 2016 CAN-SAD trial JAMA Psychiatry)*

**Practical.** Brands: Northern Light Technologies Boxelite ($200), Carex Day-Light Classic Plus ($120), Philips HF3520 dawn simulator + bright box ($150), Lumie Vitamin L ($175), Lumie Brazil ($240). 10,000 lux at eye level, 16-24 inches away. Use peripheral vision; do NOT stare directly into the light. 20-30 min AM, ideally within 30 min of waking. **CAUTION**: bipolar-spectrum users — bright light can precipitate hypomania/mania; consult a clinician on your own initiative if you have any bipolar history. **Grade A** for SAD; **Grade B** for non-seasonal MDD.

### 5.3 Dawn simulator alarm

> **For you, in plain words.** A dawn-simulator alarm gradually brightens over 30 minutes before your wake time, mimicking natural sunrise — Wirz-Justice trials and Glickman pilots show real morning-mood benefit, especially for SAD and atypical depression with morning lag. *(Wirz-Justice 1996; Glickman 2006)*

### 5.6 Self-applied CBT for depression (A-grade!)

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-applied CBT for depression has Cochrane A-grade evidence — Hofmann 2012 meta of 269 trials. Self-applied behavioural activation has Cochrane A-grade (Cuijpers 2020, n=53 RCTs) and was shown non-inferior to paroxetine for severe depression (Dimidjian 2006, n=241). Free workbooks, free apps, free public-library access. *(Cochrane Hofmann 2012; Cochrane Cuijpers 2020; Dimidjian 2006)*

**Self-guided routes.**
- **Workbooks** (library-free): David Burns *Feeling Good* and *Feeling Good Handbook*; Greenberger & Padesky *Mind Over Mood*; Wright/McCray *Breaking Free from Depression*; Christopher Martell *Behavioural Activation for Depression*.
- **Apps** (free/freemium): Woebot (free, conversational CBT for depression), MoodKit, Wysa (free tier), MoodGYM (free, Australian National University), Beating-the-Blues (UK NHS-derived, free in many regions), This Way Up (Australia, low-cost).
- **ACT (acceptance and commitment therapy)** — Russ Harris *The Happiness Trap* + ACT Companion app.

> **For you, in plain words.** The 8-week Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy program (Segal/Williams/Teasdale) is Cochrane A-grade for preventing relapse in recurrent depression — equivalent to maintenance antidepressants. **How to do it.** Segal/Williams/Teasdale *Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Depression* (book + audio); Williams/Penman *Mindfulness: A Practical Guide to Finding Peace in a Frantic World* (UK Oxford); 30 min/day for 8 weeks + 10 min/day maintenance. > **For you, in plain words.** Japanese forest bathing — Park 2010 trials showed reduced cortisol, lower BP, and mood lift after 2-hour forest walks. Free, accessible. *(Park 2010 Japan forest bathing trials)*

2-3 hours in mature forest, walking slowly, 1-2×/week. Hokkaido University and Chiba University trial programs. **Grade B** for mood + cortisol.

> **For you, in plain words.** Janssen 2016 Finnish trial showed whole-body hyperthermia equivalent to SSRI for depression at 6 weeks (n=30). Home sauna 60-80°C, 20 min, 3×/week. *(Janssen 2016 hyperthermia-depression; Laukkanen 2018 Finnish sauna)*

> **For you, in plain words.** Alcohol (the single biggest dietary depression amplifier — wrecks REM, depletes B-vits, rebounds as morning depression); chronic high-dose caffeine; ultra-processed food daily; chronic anti-cholinergic OTC sleep pills (long-term dementia signal); chronic isolation and screen-only social life; doomscrolling at night. *(WHO alcohol harm; UK MHRA anti-cholinergic burden 2022; Lassale SMILES 2019)*

> **For you, in plain words.** A Mediterranean-MIND-Indian-millet base of oily fish, leafy greens, berries, olive oil, sprouted millets, fermented foods, and tryptophan-rich proteins. Jacka SMILES 2017 trial showed Mediterranean dietary intervention reduced depression scores by ~32% over 12 weeks (n=67). *(Jacka SMILES 2017 RCT; Lassale 2019 MD-depression meta; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

**Quick-start summary.** **No** alcohol (it wrecks REM and rebounds as morning depression), **no** ultra-processed food daily, **no** caffeine after 14:00 (sleep loss compounds depression). See §6.2 for daily-gram list.

### 6.4 Preparation methodology

### 6.6 Environmental & occupational triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic workplace stress, social isolation, screen-only social life, untreated sleep apnea, mould exposure, low natural-light exposure, chronic-pain comorbidity — fix the environment before scaling supplements. *(WHO indoor air 2009; UK MHRA anti-cholinergic 2022; Hunt 2018 social-media RCT)*

- **TL;DR.** Chronic workplace stress / toxic culture — psychological-safety audit; consider boundaries or job change; track Maslach Burnout Inventory. *(Maslach Burnout Inventory)*
- **TL;DR.** Social isolation — schedule 2-3 in-person social contacts weekly; the loneliness-depression link is causal (Cacioppo). *(Cacioppo loneliness-depression literature)*
- **TL;DR.** Untreated sleep apnea — order a home sleep test (Lofta WatchPAT) if BMI >30, snoring, witnessed apnoea, morning headaches. Sleep apnea masquerades as depression. *(Lofta validation; Schroder 2005 apnea-depression)*
- **TL;DR.** Mould + VOCs — ERMI/HERTSMI-2 test if water-damage history; HEPA-plus-carbon purifier. *(WHO indoor mould 2009)*
- **TL;DR.** Low daylight exposure — at least 30 min outside between 06:00 and 11:00. *(Lambert 2002 sunlight-serotonin)*
- **TL;DR.** Anti-cholinergic OTC sleep pills (Benadryl, ZzzQuil, Unisom, Tylenol PM) — stop chronic use; long-term dementia signal. *(UK MHRA anti-cholinergic burden 2022)*
- **TL;DR.** Doomscrolling and social media — Hunt 2018 Penn trial showed 3-week social-media reduction lifts depression scores. *(Hunt 2018 Penn RCT)*
- **TL;DR.** Endocrine-disrupting plastics, parabens, phthalates — switch to glass, clean personal care. *(EFSA BPA 2023)*
- **TL;DR.** Nicotine + vaping — chronic depression amplifier; cessation lifts mood after withdrawal period. *(Boden 2010 smoking-depression)*

### 7.5 Sleep & circadian

> **For you, in plain words.** Lights-out 22:00-22:30; screens off 21:00; bedroom 18-20°C and blackout; morning sunlight within 30 min of waking (critical for depression); no caffeine after 14:00; no alcohol. Atypical depression with hypersomnia needs the SAME morning anchor + restricted sleep window (not more sleep). *(Health Canada melatonin; AYUSH daily-rhythm; Riemann sleep-depression)*

Target sleep score (Pittsburgh) under 5; track Insomnia Severity Index if insomnia overlaps. CBT-i (Sleepio, Somryst, free workbook) for insomnia subtype.

### 7.6 Exercise periodization — A-grade antidepressant

> **For you, in plain words.** Mon/Wed/Fri Zone-2 cardio (you can still talk), Tue/Thu strength, Sat tai chi or qigong or partnered dance, Sun nature walk. Aerobic exercise has Cochrane A-grade evidence for depression — Blumenthal SMILE trial showed non-inferiority to sertraline. *(Cochrane Cooney 2013; Blumenthal SMILE 1999, 2007; Schuch 2016 meta)*

**Weekly.** Zone 2 cardio 30-45 min at 60-70% max HR, Mon/Wed/Fri; strength 30-45 min compound lifts, Tue/Thu; tai chi/qigong or partnered dance 30-45 min on Sat; nature walk 60-90 min on Sun.

**Monthly.** Week 1 — full intensity + TCM overlay. Week 2 — moderate. Week 3 — deload (≤70% volume) + extra sleep / sauna / Yoga Nidra. Week 4 — full + TCM re-cycles.

**Caveat**: in severe depression with profound fatigue, START with 5-10 minute outdoor walks daily and BUILD UP. Behavioural-activation framing: schedule, then do, regardless of motivation. **Optional 14:10 time-restricted eating (10:00-20:00)** — NOT for underweight, severely depressed with reduced appetite, pregnant/breastfeeding.

### 7.7 Behavioural activation — A-grade

> **For you, in plain words.** Behavioural activation (BA) — gradually re-engaging in pleasurable + meaningful activities — has Cochrane A-grade evidence equivalent to or better than antidepressants for mild-moderate depression. The single most important behavioural lever in this dossier. *(Cuijpers 2020 Cochrane n=53 RCTs; Dimidjian 2006 BA vs paroxetine; Jacobson 1996)*

**How to do it.**
1. Each Sunday, write 3-5 small activities for the week that USED to give you pleasure or meaning (walk, coffee with friend, garden, music, hobby).
2. Schedule them on specific days/times in your calendar.
3. Do them ON SCHEDULE — even if you don't feel like it. Motivation follows action, not the other way around.
4. Rate each activity 0-10 for mood-before and mood-after.
5. Build up gradually (don't start with "run a marathon").

Resources: Christopher Martell *Behavioural Activation for Depression*; David Burns *Feeling Good* (Chapter on activity scheduling); free BA workbooks at UK NHS, This Way Up Australia. **Grade A**. *(Cuijpers 2020; Dimidjian 2006)*

### 7.8 Social co-regulation — non-negotiable

> **For you, in plain words.** Loneliness and depression are bidirectionally causal (Cacioppo). At least 2-3 in-person social contacts per week is the floor. Phone/text-only is NOT enough — co-regulation is physiological. *(Cacioppo loneliness-depression literature; Holt-Lunstad social-relationships-mortality meta)*

Practical: schedule weekly coffee/meal with a trusted person; join a regular group (sports league, choir, book club, hiking group, spiritual community); volunteer 2-4 hours/week.

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.1 What to measure, in plain words

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-administered scoring at home — PHQ-9, BDI-II, MADRS self-rated, DASS-21 depression subscale, EPDS if perinatal, SPAQ if seasonal, plus a daily 0-10 mood + anhedonia + energy slider. ### 11.2 Self-administered depression scoring

> **For you, in plain words.** The main score is PHQ-9, a 9-question self-quiz scored 0-27; 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-19 moderately severe, ≥20 severe. **Question 9 asks specifically about thoughts of self-harm — if you answer YES (any score above 0), call the crisis line in §11.3 IMMEDIATELY.** All scoring done at home. *(Spitzer 2001 PHQ-9)*

Primary: **PHQ-9** — 9-question self-scored; 0-27. **5-point drop = clinically meaningful.** Cadence: baseline / week 4 / 8 / 12 / month 6 / 9 / 12. **Question 9 (self-harm thoughts) — any positive score triggers §11.3 crisis routing IMMEDIATELY.**

Secondary: **Beck Depression Inventory II (BDI-II)** — 21-item severity questionnaire, 0-63. Bands 0-13 minimal, 14-19 mild, 20-28 moderate, 29-63 severe. Baseline + week 12 + month 6 + month 12.

Severity-tracking: **MADRS (Montgomery-Åsberg) self-rated** — 10 items; sensitive to change.

Dimensional: **DASS-21 depression subscale** — pairs with anxiety + stress subscales for mixed presentations.

Perinatal: **EPDS (Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale)** — 10 items for pregnancy and postpartum; ≥10 moderate, ≥13 severe; **Q10 (self-harm thoughts) triggers §11.3 IMMEDIATELY**.

Seasonal: **SPAQ (Seasonal Pattern Assessment Questionnaire)** — pattern + severity over the year.

Tertiary: **0-10 slider** for mood, anhedonia, energy, sleep, appetite, daily.

Optional adjuncts: daily HRV (HeartMath, Oura, Whoop); weight + waist; tracking in Daylio, Bearable, MoodKit, or paper journal.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Depression has five drivers — depleted neurotransmitters + BDNF, brain inflammation, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural patterns of rumination and avoidance. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home; energy and sleep often lift first; mood is a 6-12 month story. *(Cuijpers 2020; Akhondzadeh 2005; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

Depression is not "weakness" and it is not "all in your head" — it is a real, measurable convergence of neurotransmitter depletion, brain inflammation, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural-cognitive patterns. Five things drive it: (1) your serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine and BDNF (the brain-growth protein) are reduced; (2) inflammation is pulling tryptophan away from making serotonin into making neurotoxic kynurenine; (3) your mitochondria — your cellular power plants — are producing less ATP, which presents as anhedonia + fatigue; (4) your gut bacteria — which make ~90% of your body's serotonin precursors — are out of balance; (5) rumination, avoidance, social withdrawal and loss of meaning lock the depression in.

This protocol does **not** chase depression with off-label nerve drugs or stack of antidepressants. It rebuilds each of the five layers — **behavioural activation** (the A-grade evidence layer — gradually re-engaging in pleasurable + meaningful activities even when you don't feel like it), **self-applied CBT or MBCT** (the A-grade cognitive lever), **saffron + omega-3 EPA + curcumin** (the monoamine + anti-inflammatory layer), **Ashwagandha + holy basil + B-complex + vitamin D** (the HPA + cofactor layer), a Mediterranean-millet-fermented-food diet, home bright light + sauna + exercise + social co-regulation. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital in the home regime.**

Expect a slow, layered shift. **Energy and sleep** lift first (weeks 2-4), not always mood. By weeks 8-12 most users' PHQ-9 drops by 5 points or more — a meaningful change. Real change is a 6-to-12-month story. SAD responds fast (2-4 weeks) to bright light. Postpartum depression often needs iron + B12 + vitamin D correction first. Recurrent depression needs MBCT for relapse prevention.
