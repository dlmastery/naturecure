## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What low mood actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Low mood and depression-like presentations are not "weakness" or "all in your head". They are a real, measurable convergence of neurotransmitter depletion, inflammation in the brain, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural-cognitive patterns of avoidance and rumination. All five layers can be rebuilt at home. *(Cuijpers 2020 behavioural activation Cochrane; Su 2018 omega-3 meta; Cryan & Dinan 2012 gut-brain)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: write down one small thing that used to give you pleasure and schedule it for tomorrow (10 minutes; this is behavioural activation, the A-grade evidence layer). Cut alcohol completely for two weeks. Go outside within 30 minutes of waking for 10 minutes of direct sunlight. This week: download a self-applied CBT-for-depression workbook free from your library (David Burns *Feeling Good* or Greenberger & Padesky *Mind Over Mood*). Book a direct-to-consumer thyroid + B12 + vitamin D + ferritin + omega-3 panel — hypothyroidism, B12 deficiency, low ferritin and low vitamin D all mimic depression. If your PHQ-9 is ≥15, OR you have any suicidal thoughts, call the crisis line in §11.3 BEFORE starting any supplement layer. *(Cochrane Cuijpers 2020; Hofmann CBT meta; Bot 2017 B12-folate-depression)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story shrugs depression off as "a chemical imbalance you need an SSRI for" — which is, at best, one slice of a five-driver picture, and the STAR-D trial (Insel 2006, n=4,041) showed that only ~30% of patients achieve remission on first-line SSRI, with ~50% remaining symptomatic after four sequential medication trials. A more honest reframe: persistent low mood is the output of (i) **monoamine + BDNF + neurogenesis depletion** (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, BDNF, hippocampal neurogenesis all down), (ii) **chronic neuroinflammation** with elevated IL-6 + TNF-α + hs-CRP shifting tryptophan into the kynurenine pathway away from serotonin, (iii) **mitochondrial bioenergetic dysfunction** with reduced ATP and reduced PGC-1α underlying anhedonia + fatigue, (iv) **gut-brain axis dysbiosis** with reduced Lactobacillus/Bifidobacterium, altered SCFA, and elevated LPS, and (v) **behavioural-cognitive-relational** patterns (rumination, avoidance, loss of meaning, social withdrawal). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **behavioural activation + CBT/MBCT** (the A-grade frontline) with monoamine support (saffron, omega-3 EPA), anti-inflammatory layers (curcumin, EPA), mitochondrial rebuild (CoQ10, NAD+, B-complex), gut-brain repair (psychobiotics, fermented foods), and a measured cofactor floor (vitamin D, B12, folate, iron, magnesium, zinc) hits all five drivers in parallel.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-driver)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive depression — depleted neurotransmitters, brain inflammation, mitochondrial energy failure, gut-brain dysbiosis, and behavioural patterns of avoidance and rumination. Naming them lets every food, breath, supplement and behavioural shift aim at the right one. *(Cuijpers 2020; Su 2018; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

#### Open the science

The five-driver model converges literature from Maes 2011 (inflammation-depression), Su 2018 (omega-3 EPA meta), Cryan & Dinan 2012 (gut-brain), Duman 2016 (BDNF + neurogenesis), Manji 2003 (mitochondria + bipolar). Cochrane Cuijpers 2020 (behavioural activation, n=53 RCTs) and Cochrane Hofmann 2012 (CBT meta) underpin the behavioural pillar. **Synthesis.** No single mechanism explains all of depression; the home protocol intervenes at each layer in parallel.

### 2.2 Monoamine + BDNF depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** Depression involves reduced serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine AND reduced BDNF (the protein that grows new brain connections). Saffron, omega-3 EPA, exercise, Lion's Mane and sunlight all rebuild this substrate. *(Akhondzadeh 2005 saffron RCT; Hausenblas 2013 saffron meta; Erickson 2011 exercise + BDNF)*

#### Open the science

Key actors: TPH2 (tryptophan hydroxylase, serotonin synthesis), tyrosine hydroxylase (dopamine synthesis), MAO-A/B, SERT, DAT, BDNF, TrkB, mTOR. Hippocampal neurogenesis is reliably reduced in depression (Sahay & Hen 2007). Targeted by **saffron** (Akhondzadeh 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40; Hausenblas 2013 meta, 6 trials n=230, ES equivalent to SSRI for mild-moderate); **omega-3 EPA ≥1 g/d** (Su 2018 meta, n=26 RCTs); **Lion's Mane** (Hericium erinaceus — NGF + BDNF in animal models, Mori 2009 small Japanese RCT); **aerobic exercise** (Blumenthal SMILE 1999/2007 — exercise non-inferior to sertraline; Schuch 2016 meta); **morning sunlight** (Lambert 2002 serotonin synthesis sunlight correlation); **social connection** (Cacioppo loneliness-depression literature). **Synthesis.** This is the neurochemical lever.

### 2.3 Neuroinflammation + kynurenine shunt

> **For you, in plain words.** Inflammation in the body raises chemicals that pull tryptophan AWAY from making serotonin and TOWARD making neurotoxic kynurenine. Lowering inflammation lifts mood through this same lever. Omega-3 EPA, curcumin and a Mediterranean diet are the highest-evidence anti-inflammatory layers. *(Maes 2011 IDO-kynurenine; Su 2018; Lopresti 2014 curcumin-depression meta)*

#### Open the science

Elevated IL-6, TNF-α, hs-CRP, IFN-γ correlate with depression severity (Dowlati 2010 meta). IDO (indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase) is activated by inflammation, shunting tryptophan to neurotoxic quinolinic acid, an NMDA agonist. Targeted by **omega-3 EPA ≥1 g/d** (anti-inflammatory; Su 2018 meta); **curcumin BCM-95 or Meriva 500 mg BID** (Lopresti 2014 meta, 6 RCTs, moderate signal vs placebo); **resveratrol**; **Boswellia AKBA**; **Mediterranean-MIND diet** (Jacka SMILES 2017 RCT n=67 — diet intervention reduced depression scores by ~32%); **gluten elimination trial if marker-positive**; **gut-permeability repair**; **micronutrient floor** (zinc, magnesium, vitamin D); **reduced visceral adiposity**; **smoking cessation**. **Synthesis.** Lowering systemic inflammation is the upstream anti-depressant lever — often the missing piece in SSRI non-responders.

### 2.4 Mitochondrial bioenergetic failure

> **For you, in plain words.** Anhedonia (the inability to feel pleasure) and fatigue track mitochondrial energy failure — the cellular power plants are not making enough ATP. CoQ10, NAD+ precursors, B-vitamins, magnesium, creatine and red-light therapy rebuild this substrate. *(Manji 2003 bipolar-mito; Wallace mito-aging literature; Hamblin photobiomodulation 2017)*

#### Open the science

Depression — particularly anhedonia + fatigue dimensions — correlates with reduced mitochondrial ATP, reduced Complex I/IV activity, reduced PGC-1α, oxidative damage. Key actors: Complex I, Complex IV, ATP synthase, CoQ10, NAD+, carnitine, α-lipoic acid, magnesium, PGC-1α. Targeted by **CoQ10/Ubiquinol 100-300 mg**; **NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR) 250-500 mg**; **acetyl-L-carnitine 1-2 g** (Wang 2014 ALC depression meta); **α-lipoic acid 300-600 mg**; **magnesium glycinate 400 mg**; **creatine 3-5 g** (Lyoo 2012 creatine augmentation RCT); **B-complex** with methylated forms (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6 P-5-P, B9 5-MTHF, B12 methylcobalamin); **transcranial photobiomodulation 810-1064 nm** (Cassano 2018 Vielight pilots; Schiffer 2009 — emerging signal); **Zone 2 cardio rebuild**. **Synthesis.** Restoring mitochondria substrates the energy + reward systems.

### 2.5 Gut-brain axis dysbiosis

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria make ~90% of your body's serotonin precursors and most of your short-chain fatty acids. Depressed cohorts reliably show lower Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium and higher gut permeability. Psychobiotics + fermented foods + Mediterranean fibre rebuild this. *(Cryan & Dinan 2012 Nature; Messaoudi 2011 RCT; Jacka SMILES 2017)*

#### Open the science

Depressed cohorts show altered microbiome — reduced Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Faecalibacterium; increased zonulin + LPS; altered tryptophan-serotonin-kynurenine balance; reduced SCFA. The gut produces ~90% of body serotonin and signals to the brain via vagal afferents, SCFA (butyrate), and immune mediators. Targeted by **multi-strain psychobiotics** — Lactobacillus helveticus R0052 + Bifidobacterium longum R0175 (Cerebiome blend; Messaoudi 2011 French RCT n=55 — anxiety + depression + cortisol reduction); **Lactobacillus rhamnosus JB-1**; **fermented foods** 100-150 g/day (kefir, sauerkraut, kanji, kimchi, dosa batter — Hilimire 2015 Oregon cohort fermented-food-anxiety); **prebiotic fibre** 25-35 g/day; **dietary tryptophan** (turkey, eggs, pumpkin seeds, oats); **elimination of ultra-processed food and chronic alcohol**; **L-glutamine 5 g**; **zinc-carnosine** if hyperpermeable. **Synthesis.** Often the slowest-moving but most durable layer.

### 2.6 Behavioural-cognitive-relational layer

> **For you, in plain words.** Rumination, avoidance, loss of meaning and social withdrawal are not symptoms TO be fixed by supplements — they are themselves causal drivers that lock the depression in. Behavioural activation (re-engaging in pleasurable + meaningful activities, even when you don't feel like it) has Cochrane A-grade evidence. *(Cochrane Cuijpers 2020 behavioural activation n=53 RCTs; Dimidjian 2006 BA vs paroxetine; Jacobson 1996)*

#### Open the science

This is the highest-evidence non-pharmacological layer for depression. Targeted by **behavioural activation** (Jacobson 1996; Dimidjian 2006 BA non-inferior to paroxetine for severe depression, n=241; Cuijpers 2020 Cochrane meta n=53 RCTs); **CBT-for-depression** (Beck cognitive therapy; Hofmann 2012 Cochrane meta); **MBCT (Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy)** — Segal/Williams/Teasdale 8-week program (Kuyken 2016 meta — equivalent to maintenance antidepressants for relapse prevention); **iCBT/internet-delivered CBT** (Andrews 2018 meta n=64 studies); **Woebot/Wysa app-based** (small trials); **ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy)** — Harris *The Happiness Trap*; **IPT (Interpersonal Therapy)**; **values clarification + meaningful re-engagement**; **social co-regulation** (face-to-face time with trusted others — load-bearing); **nature exposure** (forest bathing / Shinrin-yoku — Park 2010 Japanese trials); **gratitude practice** (3 things daily); **creative-expressive practices**. **Synthesis.** Behavioural and relational shifts are non-negotiable — supplements alone do not restore meaning.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest cautions on **St John's Wort (EXCLUDED — CYP3A4 wrecks contraceptives, SSRIs, warfarin, statins; serotonin-syndrome risk)**, **5-HTP (EXCLUDED — serotonin syndrome risk with antidepressants too high for home self-administration)**, **L-tyrosine + Rhodiola + SAMe (use ONLY if no bipolar-spectrum history — they can precipitate mania)**, and **kava (EXCLUDED — rare severe liver toxicity)**. *(EMA SJW monograph 2018; EMA SJW SmPC; EMA kava 2002; Linde 2008 Cochrane SJW)*

### 3.2 Saffron — the strongest single-trial signal

> **For you, in plain words.** Saffron (Crocus sativus stigma extract, standardised affron or Iranian Novin Saffron) has the strongest single-trial evidence in this category. Iranian Akhondzadeh trials (2005, 2007) showed equivalence to fluoxetine 20 mg and imipramine 100 mg for mild-moderate depression at 30 mg/day over 6 weeks. Hausenblas 2013 meta pooled 6 trials with consistent effect. EMA herbal monograph 2022. Try 28-30 mg once daily for 6-8 weeks. **Caution**: 4-hour gap from SSRIs/SNRIs (serotonergic). *(EMA saffron monograph 2022; Akhondzadeh 2005 n=40; Hausenblas 2013 meta)*

#### Open the science

Active compounds: **crocin** (carotenoid glycoside) and **safranal** (volatile aldehyde). Mechanism: monoamine oxidase inhibition, serotonin reuptake modulation, NMDA antagonism, HPA effects. Trials: Akhondzadeh 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40 (equivalence); Akhondzadeh 2007 vs imipramine, n=30 (equivalence); Moshiri 2006 vs placebo; Noorbala 2005 vs fluoxetine, n=40; Kashani 2017 RCT in postpartum depression, n=60 (significant benefit); Modabbernia 2012 in perimenopausal depression; Hausenblas 2013 meta of 6 trials confirming consistent effect size; Lopresti 2014 meta confirming. Standardised brand: **affron** (Pharmactive, Spain), **Novin Saffron**, **Bonusan Saffron**. **Grade A** for mild-moderate depression — the highest-graded single botanical in this dossier.

### 3.3 Omega-3 EPA — the inflammation lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Omega-3 EPA (specifically EPA, not DHA) at >1 g/day has moderate signal for depression, especially in inflammation-driven and SSRI-resistant cases. Su 2018 meta-analysis confirmed signal. Pair with a low-omega-6 diet. *(Su 2018 omega-3 EPA meta; UK SACN omega-3 2020; EMA fish oil monograph)*

#### Open the science

The EPA:DHA ratio matters — meta-analyses (Su 2018; Mocking 2016) converge on EPA-dominant formulations being more efficacious than DHA-dominant for depression. EPA >1 g/day for 8-12 weeks. Mechanism: PUFA membrane incorporation; eicosanoid balance shift (PGE2 → PGE3); reduced IL-6/TNF-α; BDNF support. **Grade B** as monotherapy, **Grade A** as antidepressant adjunct (Sarris 2016 omega-3 augmentation meta).
