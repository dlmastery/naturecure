> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) — the irregular periods, the chin and upper-lip hair, the acne, the weight that won't shift, the cycles that won't ovulate. Lead with inositol + berberine for insulin, magnesium and B6 for the nervous system, spearmint tea for androgens, sprouted millets and bitter greens for the gut. Supplements come second to food and strength training. We are honest where the science is mixed — and honest that home tools cannot replace specialist workup for fertility once you have actively tried to conceive for a year. *(WHO Traditional Medicine Strategy 2025-2034; Cochrane inositol-PCOS 2018; Wei 2012 berberine RCT)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO TCIM Strategy 2025–2034, India's Ministry of AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS, PCIMH, ICMR), EMA HMPC herbal monographs, TGA + NHMRC, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Tibetan Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, plus classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator, professional body or disease association is cited as authority** (ACOG, AACE, NAMS, ADA, AMA, AAFP, CDC, NIH-policy excluded).
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every supplement, food, breath practice and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No combined oral contraceptive, no metformin, no spironolactone, no clomiphene/letrozole, no GLP-1 agonist, no anti-androgen drug, no clinic procedure, no ovarian drilling is recommended in the home regime.** Clinic-route options sit in §10.7.3, never in the home protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users already on a prescription can see how it interacts with this protocol.
>
> **PREGNANCY SAFETY (load-bearing).** If you are pregnant, trying to conceive, or breastfeeding — many of these herbs are contraindicated. **Stop Vitex, berberine, high-dose curcumin, saw palmetto, Ashokarishta, Kanchanara Guggulu and Pushyanuga the moment you confirm pregnancy.** During pregnancy and breastfeeding, use ONLY pregnancy-validated nutrients: methylfolate 800-1000 µg, methyl-B12, iron (if ferritin low), choline 450 mg, omega-3 DHA 600 mg, vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2, magnesium glycinate 300 mg, iodine (cautious if anti-TPO+). The §11.5 cycle-tracking section explains how to know.
>
> **Red flags (call for workup, do not self-manage).** (1) Cycle absent >90 days AND not pregnant AND not on hormonal contraception — long-term unopposed oestrogen risk to the uterine lining; consider transvaginal ultrasound. (2) Suspected pregnancy — confirm with home test; stop herbs. (3) Heavy bleeding soaking >1 pad/hour for several hours, or persistent severe pelvic pain — these are not normal PCOS and need workup. (4) Severe acne with cystic, scarring lesions, or new-onset rapid virilisation (deep voice, clitoromegaly) — rule out androgen-secreting tumour.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What PCOS actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** PCOS is not "just irregular periods" — it is the visible apex of four overlapping body systems that have drifted out of balance. Naming all four lets every food and supplement aim at the right one. *(Rotterdam consensus 2003; Indian PCOS cohort studies)*

### 1.2 The four causes — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Start sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets + bitter greens tonight + Ovasitol (myo + d-chiro inositol 40:1) and berberine with meals starting tomorrow + 2 cups of spearmint tea midday and evening + strength training 3×/week. These five moves cover the highest-evidence layers. *(Unfer 2014 inositol meta-analysis; Wei 2012 berberine RCT; Akdoğan 2007 spearmint RCT)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

PCOS is conventionally siloed as a "reproductive-endocrine" diagnosis treated with combined oral contraceptives, metformin, spironolactone, clomiphene/letrozole and (when fertility is at stake) ovarian drilling or assisted reproduction. The integrative reframing: PCOS is the **ovarian and dermatologic presentation of a four-node systemic network** — (i) **insulin resistance + hyperinsulinaemia** driving theca-cell androgen output, SHBG suppression and anovulation; (ii) **hyperandrogenism** (theca CYP17, 5α-reductase) producing hirsutism, acne, hair loss and follicular arrest; (iii) **chronic low-grade inflammation** (elevated hs-CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, advanced glycation end-products) amplifying ovarian dysfunction; and (iv) **gut-androgen axis dysbiosis** (depleted *Akkermansia*, expanded β-glucuronidase-producers) that recycles enterohepatic androgens. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously restores insulin sensitivity, lowers androgen tone, dampens inflammation and rebuilds the gut-androgen axis addresses the upstream drivers — not the cosmetic, menstrual or fertility endpoints in isolation.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers explain almost every PCOS symptom — insulin not listening, too much androgen, slow-burn inflammation, and out-of-balance gut bacteria that recycle hormones back into your blood. *(Unfer 2014; Vrbíková 2012; Lindheim 2017 microbiome)*

### 2.2 Insulin resistance → ovary

> **For you, in plain words.** When your cells stop listening to insulin, your pancreas pumps out more — and that excess insulin tells your ovaries to make too much testosterone, which stops ovulation. Inositol + berberine are the home cornerstones. *(Unfer 2014; Wei 2012)*

**Quick start.** Ovasitol (myo + d-chiro inositol 40:1) 2 g AM + 2 g PM + Berberine 500 mg with each meal + strength training 3×/week + sprouted millet at every meal.

#### Open the science

Skeletal-muscle and adipocyte insulin resistance forces pancreatic β-cells to hyper-secrete insulin. Hyperinsulinaemia acts on theca-cell IGF-1R and insulin receptors, amplifying LH-driven CYP17A1 (17α-hydroxylase / 17,20-lyase) activity → testosterone, androstenedione, DHEA. Insulin suppresses hepatic SHBG synthesis, raising free testosterone, and disrupts the LH/FSH ratio (>2:1 typical), arresting antral follicle maturation. Key actors: INSR, IGF-1R, CYP17A1, AKR1C3, SHBG, AMH. Targeted by **myo-inositol + d-chiro-inositol 40:1 (Ovasitol-style), berberine, alpha-lipoic acid, magnesium glycinate, cinnamon (Ceylon, not Cassia), chromium, gymnema, low-glycaemic Khadar-Vali Siri Dhanyalu millet diet, Zone 2 cardio + strength training**. Cochrane 2018 systematic review of inositol for PCOS found improvements in ovulation, cycle regularity and metabolic markers; Wei 2012 RCT (n=89) found berberine equivalent to metformin for menstrual regularity at 3 months. **Synthesis.** Restoring insulin sensitivity is the upstream brake on the entire cascade.

### 2.3 Androgen excess → hair, acne, hair loss

> **For you, in plain words.** Excess testosterone gets converted into DHT (the stronger version) on your skin and scalp — that's what drives the chin hair, the acne and the scalp thinning, and what stops eggs from maturing. Spearmint tea, zinc and saw palmetto turn that conversion down. *(Akdoğan 2007 spearmint RCT; Iranian zinc-PCOS RCT 2016)*

**Quick start.** Spearmint tea 2 cups midday + evening + Zinc picolinate 15 mg with dinner + Saw palmetto 320 mg AM if hair loss/hirsutism dominant.

#### Open the science

Excess theca androgens convert peripherally via 5α-reductase to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), driving pilosebaceous unit hyperactivity (terminal hair on chin, upper lip, sideburn, chest, abdomen; sebum and acne; androgenetic scalp pattern). Intra-ovarian androgens prematurely lutein-ise granulosa cells and arrest follicular dominance → polycystic morphology + anovulatory cycles. Key actors: androgen receptor (AR), 5α-reductase types 1 and 2, AKR1C3, SHBG, AMH (chronically elevated 2–6× normal). Targeted by **spearmint tea (Akdoğan 2007 Turkish RCT n=21 — significant testosterone reduction over 30 d), zinc picolinate (5α-reductase inhibition), saw palmetto (EMA HMPC monograph), reishi, licorice + peony classical TCM pair (Shakuyaku-kanzo-to — multiple Japanese Kampo RCTs lowering testosterone in PCOS), Shatavari, Ashokarishta, Kanchanara Guggulu**. **Synthesis.** Lowering androgen tone reverses the cosmetic and follicular dysfunction.

### 2.4 Slow-burn inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** PCOS bodies show small but persistent inflammation that damages egg quality and feeds androgen output. Omega-3 fish oil, NAC and a Mediterranean-millet diet quiet it. *(Iranian PCOS-omega-3 meta-analysis 2017; Iranian NAC-PCOS RCTs)*

**Quick start.** Omega-3 EPA+DHA 2 g/day with food + NAC 600 mg twice daily + steam/boil/poach (cut deep-fry and char-grill) + low-AGE cooking.

#### Open the science

PCOS cohorts show elevated hs-CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, MCP-1, advanced glycation end-products (AGEs) and oxidative stress markers (MDA, 8-OHdG). Visceral adipose tissue (often present even in lean PCOS as ectopic fat) secretes leptin, resistin, IL-6 and lowers adiponectin. AGEs cross-link ovarian follicle basement membrane, impair granulosa function and amplify androgen output. Targeted by **omega-3 (EPA+DHA 2 g), NAC, curcumin, quercetin, resveratrol, low-AGE cooking, Mediterranean overlay, Ashwagandha, Triphala**. **Synthesis.** Without inflammation control, every other lever regresses.

### 2.5 Gut-androgen axis

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria can recycle hormones back into the blood instead of clearing them — making PCOS worse. Sprouted millets, fermented foods and a daily probiotic rebuild the right flora. *(Lindheim 2017; Liu 2017 PCOS-microbiome)*

**Quick start.** Sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets 60–80 g cooked/day + fermented food 100–150 g/day (kanji, sauerkraut, A2 dahi) + multi-strain probiotic 30–50 B CFU AM.

#### Open the science

PCOS cohorts show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*; expanded *Bacteroides*, *Escherichia/Shigella*, β-glucuronidase-producers (enterohepatic deconjugation re-cycles androgens and oestrogens back into circulation). Bile-acid pool altered (↓ secondary BAs that signal through FXR/TGR5 → metabolic dysregulation). Targeted by **multi-strain probiotic 30–50 B CFU, sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets (foxtail, barnyard, kodo, little, browntop), fermented foods (kanji, sauerkraut, A2/coconut kefir), pomegranate + cranberry + green tea (Akkermansia-supportive polyphenols), L-glutamine, zinc-carnosine**. **Synthesis.** Sealing the gut and re-flora-ing the microbiome cuts androgen recycling and inflammation at the root.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** PCOS is four things happening together — your cells stop listening to insulin, the extra insulin tells your ovaries to make too much testosterone, silent inflammation damages egg quality, and gut bacteria recycle hormones back into your blood. This protocol rebuilds each layer at home. *(Unfer 2014; Wei 2012; Lindheim 2017)*

PCOS is not just "irregular periods" or "cosmetic acne and hair". Inside your body, four things are happening together: (1) your cells aren't listening to insulin well, so your pancreas pumps out too much insulin, which tells your ovaries to make too much testosterone; (2) that extra testosterone causes the chin/upper-lip hair, the acne, the scalp thinning, and stops your eggs from maturing each month; (3) silent inflammation simmers in the background and damages your egg quality; and (4) your gut bacteria are out of balance in a way that recycles hormones back into your blood instead of clearing them.

This protocol does **not** put you on the pill to hide the cycle, or metformin to manage one symptom, or spironolactone for hair, or clomiphene to force ovulation. Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the inside out — with sprouted millets, fermented foods, OTC supplements (inositol, berberine, NAC, omega-3, vitamin D, magnesium, spearmint tea, Ceylon cinnamon), red-light therapy on your abdomen, sauna, cold water, strength training, breath-work and sleep. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital.** Everything you need fits in a kitchen, a bedroom and a yoga mat.

Expect a slow, steady re-balancing — not a one-month switch. First (Weeks 4–6) energy stabilises and PMS lightens. Around Week 12, many users get a spontaneous period — sometimes the first in years. Real cycle regularity, hair changes and fertility windows are a 6-to-12-month story. Existing terminal facial hair takes 12–24 months to thin meaningfully (those follicles are already terminal — new growth is what changes first).

You are sovereign over your own body. If you choose to keep a physician informed — especially if you're planning to conceive, or your cycles stay absent >90 days, or you have severe scarring acne, or you have rapid virilisation — that is your personal decision. The protocol does not require it, but it also does not pretend to replace specialist workup when it is genuinely indicated.
