## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What anxiety actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Anxiety isn't weakness or "all in your head" — it is a real, measurable mismatch between the brain's threat-detection system (amygdala + HPA axis) and its braking system (prefrontal cortex + GABA + vagus nerve). You can rebuild the brake at home. *(Etkin & Wager 2007; Holzel 2011)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: 6 rounds of 4-7-8 breath before bed + cut caffeine after 14:00 + stop scrolling at 21:00 + write tomorrow's three biggest worries on paper. This week: download a self-applied CBT-for-anxiety workbook (free at most public libraries) + start 5-6 breaths/minute coherent breathing 10 min/day + book a direct-to-consumer thyroid + ferritin + vitamin D panel. *(Cochrane Hofmann CBT meta; HRV-biofeedback Lehrer 2014)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story shrugs anxiety off as "a chemical imbalance you need an SSRI for" — which is at best one slice of a five-driver picture. A more honest reframe: chronic anxiety is the output of (i) an over-tuned **amygdala threat alarm**, (ii) a depleted **GABAergic + serotonergic + vagal brake**, (iii) a **hyperactive HPA cortisol axis** driven by chronic stress and sleep loss, (iv) a **gut–brain axis** in dysbiosis (low SCFA, low tryptophan, high LPS), and (v) **biological cofactor depletion** (magnesium, B6, B9, iron, vitamin D, omega-3 DHA). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs paced breathing + CBT (the A-grade frontline) with HPA support, GABAergic herbs, gut-axis repair, and a cofactor floor hits all five drivers in parallel.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five causes drive anxiety — over-tuned threat alarm, weak braking system, runaway stress hormones, gut-brain dysbiosis, and missing nutrient cofactors. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Etkin & Wager 2007; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

### 2.2 Over-tuned amygdala alarm

> **For you, in plain words.** The amygdala is the brain's smoke detector. In anxiety, it fires too easily, too long, too loud. Daily cognitive practice (CBT, mindfulness) physically retrains it. *(Holzel 2011 MBSR amygdala-shrinkage trial; Etkin & Wager 2007)*

#### Open the science

The amygdala is hyper-reactive in generalised anxiety, panic, and social anxiety on fMRI; prefrontal top-down inhibition is weakened. Holzel 2011 (MBSR 8-week trial) showed measurable amygdala grey-matter reduction. Cima 2012, Cuijpers 2014, Hofmann 2012 (Cochrane CBT meta-analyses) all confirm the cognitive lever. Targeted by **CBT-for-anxiety**, **MBSR / MBCT**, **acceptance & commitment therapy (ACT)**, **graded exposure**, and **HRV biofeedback**. **Synthesis.** This is the highest-evidence lever in the entire dossier — higher than any supplement.

### 2.3 Weak GABA / serotonin / vagus brake

> **For you, in plain words.** The brain's "off switch" runs on GABA, serotonin and the vagus nerve. Chronic stress, alcohol and benzodiazepine tolerance burn it out. Lavender (Silexan), L-theanine, magnesium and slow-paced breathing rebuild it. *(EMA Silexan monograph 2017; Kasper 2014 Silexan vs lorazepam)*

#### Open the science

GABAergic tone falls with chronic stress, alcohol use, and benzodiazepine receptor desensitisation. Serotonergic 5-HT1A signalling drops with chronic HPA activation. Vagal tone (measured as HRV) falls with poor sleep and chronic stress. Targeted by **Silexan lavender oil capsules** (EMA HMPC 2017; Kasper 2014 RCT vs lorazepam, n=539), **L-theanine** (Japanese green-tea-derived; Kakuda 2011), **magnesium glycinate + threonate** (Boyle 2017 review), **glycine 3 g pre-bed**, **passionflower (Passiflora incarnata)** (Akhondzadeh 2001 RCT vs oxazepam), **slow-paced breathing 5-6 breaths/min** (Lehrer 2014 HRV-biofeedback meta), and **vagal toning** (humming/Bhramari, cold splash, gargling). **Synthesis.** This is the cleanest mechanistic lever for OTC supplements.

### 2.4 Runaway HPA cortisol axis

> **For you, in plain words.** The hypothalamus-pituitary-adrenal axis pumps out cortisol when you're stressed. When it doesn't switch off — chronic stress, sleep loss, caffeine — anxiety becomes a baseline state. Ashwagandha is the strongest natural cortisol-lowerer we have. *(Lopresti 2019 ashwagandha meta-analysis; Chandrasekhar 2012)*

#### Open the science

The HPA axis becomes dysregulated under chronic stress — flattened diurnal slope, elevated cortisol awakening response, blunted feedback inhibition. This drives sympathetic dominance, insulin resistance, sleep disruption, and amygdala hyper-reactivity. Targeted by **Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)** — KSM-66 or Sensoril extracts (Chandrasekhar 2012 RCT; Salve 2019; Lopresti 2019 meta-analysis pooled 5 RCTs showing ~28% cortisol reduction); **Rhodiola rosea** (Bystritsky 2008 small open-label; Cropley 2015 RCT); **Phosphatidylserine** (Hellhammer 2004 PS-cortisol blunting); **Holy basil (Tulsi)** (Saxena 2012 Indian RCT); **circadian sleep hygiene**; **morning sunlight + caffeine cut-off at 14:00**. **Synthesis.** Without HPA reset, the other layers leak.

### 2.5 Gut–brain dysbiosis

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria make ~90% of your body's serotonin precursors and most of your short-chain fatty acids. Dysbiosis (low diversity, low Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium) is now reliably linked to anxiety. Psychobiotics + fermented food rebuild it. *(Cryan & Dinan 2012 Nature; Messaoudi 2011 RCT)*

#### Open the science

The gut microbiome modulates the HPA axis via vagal afferents, SCFA signalling (butyrate to brain), and tryptophan metabolism (kynurenine pathway). Anxiety patients show reduced SCFA, lower Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium, higher LPS endotoxemia. Targeted by **Lactobacillus helveticus R0052 + Bifidobacterium longum R0175** psychobiotic (Messaoudi 2011 French RCT, n=55, anxiety + cortisol reduction); **Lactobacillus rhamnosus JB-1** (Bravo 2011 mouse model + emerging human data); **fermented foods** (Mediterranean kefir, sauerkraut, kanji, kimchi); **prebiotic fibre** 25-35 g/day; **dietary tryptophan** (turkey, eggs, pumpkin seeds); **elimination of ultra-processed food and alcohol**. **Synthesis.** Often the slowest-moving but most durable layer.

### 2.6 Cofactor depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** Anxiety is fuelled by missing nutrients — magnesium (calms the nerves), B6 (makes GABA + serotonin), B9 folate, B12, iron (low ferritin = restless agitation), vitamin D, omega-3 DHA. Measure first; correct deficiencies; don't blanket-supplement. *(Boyle 2017 magnesium-anxiety review; UK SACN omega-3 2020)*

#### Open the science

Magnesium is a cofactor for >300 enzymes including GABA synthesis and NMDA modulation; deficiency is common in chronic-stress phenotypes (Boyle 2017 review). B6 (P-5-P form) is a rate-limiting cofactor for GABA and serotonin synthesis. Iron deficiency (ferritin <30 ng/mL) presents with restless agitation and insomnia — often mistaken for primary anxiety. Vitamin D deficiency (25-OH-D <30 ng/mL) is strongly associated with anxiety in observational cohorts. Omega-3 DHA is membrane-essential for neurotransmission; UK SACN 2020 confirms 250 mg EPA + DHA/day floor. Targeted by **measure-first ferritin, B12, vitamin D, RBC magnesium**, then correct. **Synthesis.** Often the highest-leverage missed driver.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest cautions on kava (liver-tox), 5-HTP (NEVER with antidepressants), and St John's Wort (excluded from this protocol entirely under §10.1). *(EMA Silexan monograph 2017; EMA kava warning 2002; EMA SJW monograph + SmPC interactions)*

### 3.2 Silexan lavender — the EMA-monograph star

> **For you, in plain words.** Silexan (Dr Willmar Schwabe's oral lavender oil capsule, sold as Lasea in Europe) has the strongest single-trial evidence in this category — a head-to-head trial against lorazepam (Kasper 2014, n=539) showed non-inferiority for generalised anxiety with no sedation and no dependence. EMA herbal monograph 2017. Try 80 mg once daily for 8-10 weeks. *(EMA Silexan monograph 2017; Kasper 2014; Kasper 2018 meta-analysis)*

#### Open the science

Silexan is a standardised steam-distilled essential oil of Lavandula angustifolia, encapsulated for oral use. Mechanism: VDCC (voltage-dependent calcium channel) modulation in pre-synaptic neurons; 5-HT1A binding. Trials: Kasper 2014 (n=539, vs lorazepam, non-inferiority); Kasper 2018 meta-analysis (5 RCTs, n=1,172, Cohen's d ~0.3-0.5 for GAD-7 reduction vs placebo); Möller 2019 (vs paroxetine non-inferiority). EMA HMPC monograph 2017 grants well-established-use status. **Grade A** for generalised anxiety symptom reduction in this specific product.

### 3.3 Ashwagandha — the cortisol-lowerer

> **For you, in plain words.** Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera, KSM-66 standardised root extract) is the most-tested adaptogen for anxiety + cortisol. Lopresti 2019 meta-analysis (5 RCTs) showed ~28% cortisol reduction and consistent anxiety-score drops over 8-12 weeks. Indian-AYUSH and Australian-TGA monographed. *(Chandrasekhar 2012; Lopresti 2019 meta; Choudhary 2017 Australia)*

#### Open the science

Withanolides (withanoside IV, withanoside VI, withaferin A) modulate GABAergic and cortisol signalling. Chandrasekhar 2012 (RCT, n=64, KSM-66 300 mg BID, cortisol -28%, PSS -44%); Salve 2019 (n=60, 250-600 mg, anxiety + sleep improved); Lopresti 2019 (meta n=5 RCTs); Choudhary 2017 (Australian trial, sleep + cortisol). Standardised to ≥5% withanolides. **Caution: pregnancy (excluded), hyperthyroidism (can raise T4), autoimmune (theoretical immune modulation), serious sedative interaction.** **Grade A** for cortisol reduction; Grade B for anxiety symptom reduction.

## 5. Biophysical & Sensory Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence layer for anxiety is paced breathing + cognitive practice (CBT or MBSR). Devices come second. *(Lehrer 2014 HRV meta; Hofmann 2012 Cochrane CBT meta)*

**Quick start, tonight:**
1. Sit for 10 minutes and breathe at exactly 5-6 breaths per minute (5-second inhale, 5-second exhale) — set a phone timer.
2. Download a self-applied CBT workbook (David Burns "Feeling Good" or "Mind Over Mood" by Greenberger & Padesky — both library free).
3. If you have racing thoughts in bed, do 6 rounds of 4-7-8 breath (inhale 4 sec, hold 7, exhale 8).

### 5.2 Slow-paced coherent breathing (5-6 bpm)

> **For you, in plain words.** Breathing at 5-6 breaths per minute (down from your usual 12-18) maximises heart-rate-variability and activates the vagal brake within minutes. Lehrer's HRV-biofeedback meta-analysis pooled 24 trials — consistent anxiety reduction. *(Lehrer 2014 meta; Russo 2017)*

**Practical.** Set a phone timer or use free apps (Breathwrk free tier, Awesome Breathing, BreathBall). 5 seconds in, 5 seconds out = 6 bpm. 10 min twice a day. **Grade B**. Optional hardware: HeartMath Inner Balance ($179) — pulse-coupled visual feedback that scores your coherence.

### 5.3 HRV biofeedback hardware

> **For you, in plain words.** HeartMath, Lief, Polar H10 + Elite HRV, Whoop, Oura — sensors show your real-time vagal tone, and you learn to raise it. 10-20 min twice a day. *(Lehrer 2014; HeartMath Institute meta)*

### 5.4 Self-applied CBT for anxiety (A-grade!)

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-applied CBT for anxiety has Cochrane-level evidence equivalent to or better than most prescription anxiolytics. Free workbooks, free apps, free public-library access. The single highest-evidence lever in this dossier. *(Cochrane Hofmann 2012; Andrews 2018 iCBT meta)*

**Self-guided routes.**
- **Workbooks** (library-free): David Burns *Feeling Good* and *Feeling Good Handbook*; Greenberger & Padesky *Mind Over Mood*; Edmund Bourne *The Anxiety and Phobia Workbook*; Padesky *Mind Over Mood for GAD*. Read 1 chapter/week + complete exercises.
- **Apps** (free/freemium): Woebot (free, conversational CBT), MoodKit, Wysa (free tier), MoodGYM (free, Australian National University), Beating-the-Blues (UK NHS-derived, free in many regions), This Way Up (Australia, low-cost). Sleepio app for the sleep-anxiety overlap.
- **ACT (acceptance and commitment therapy)** — Russ Harris *The Happiness Trap* + ACT Companion app.
- **Honest framing**: a credentialed therapist gets better outcomes for many — especially for trauma, panic with agoraphobia, OCD overlap, and severe GAD. The user decides; the protocol does not require it. **Grade A**. *(Hofmann 2012; Andrews 2018; Karyotaki 2017)*

### 5.5 MBSR + MBCT (mindfulness-based)

> **For you, in plain words.** The 8-week Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction programme (Jon Kabat-Zinn) and MBCT (Segal/Williams/Teasdale) both have Cochrane-level evidence for anxiety + depression relapse prevention. Self-applicable via the workbook + audio. *(Hofmann 2010 MBSR meta; Kuyken 2016 MBCT)*

**How to do it.** Kabat-Zinn *Full Catastrophe Living* + audio (free at most public libraries); Segal/Williams/Teasdale *Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy* workbook. 30 min/day for 8 weeks + 10 min/day maintenance. Free MBSR courses offered by UMass Medical (Palouse) and many other global health systems. **Grade A** for anxiety symptom reduction.

### 5.6 Home light therapy (10,000 lux)

> **For you, in plain words.** Bright-light box in the morning (10,000 lux for 20-30 min) and a dawn-simulator alarm reset your circadian clock — critical if your anxiety has a winter-mood, sleep-disruption, or jet-lag dimension. *(Lieverse 2011 light therapy; Glickman 2006 dawn simulator)*

**Practical.** Brands: Northern Light Technologies Boxelite ($200), Philips HF3520 dawn simulator ($150), Lumie Bodyclock Spark ($110). 20-30 min within 30 min of waking, eye level ~16 inches away. **Eye protection: do not look directly into the box; use peripheral vision.** **Grade A** for circadian/SAD anxiety; **B** for general anxiety with circadian dimension.

### 5.7 Blue-light blocking glasses (evening)

> **For you, in plain words.** Wear blue-blocking glasses (amber or red lenses) from 21:00 onwards to protect your own melatonin production — the cheapest anxiety-friendly sleep aid we have. *(Burkhart 2009; van der Lely 2015)*

**Brands.** BluTech, Swanwick, BlockBlueLight, Uvex (under $40). **Grade B** for sleep onset; indirect anxiety lever. **Combine** with screens off 21:00 ideally; if screens stay on, the glasses are a partial workaround.

### 5.8 Cold exposure / cold splash

> **For you, in plain words.** A 30-60 second cold face splash or shower triggers the vagal mammalian dive reflex — fastest acute anxiety reset we have. *(Buijze 2016 Dutch cold-shower trial)*

**Practical.** Cold-water face splash at the sink — 30 sec, AM + PM. Build to a 60-90 sec cold shower (Wim Hof Method or simpler — turn the dial to cold for the last 60 sec). **Do not do** if you have significant cardiovascular disease (consult on your own initiative). **Grade B** for acute vagal activation; **C** for chronic anxiety reduction.

### 5.9 Home sauna + contrast

> **For you, in plain words.** 20 min in a home sauna at 60-70°C, 3×/week, followed by a 30-60 sec cold splash — supports vagal tone, sleep, and stress resilience. *(Finnish Laukkanen 2018 sauna cohort)*

Sunlighten, HigherDOSE blanket; Plunge tub or bathtub + ice. **Do not** in pregnancy, with significant heart disease, or with alcohol.

### 5.10 Weighted blanket

> **For you, in plain words.** A 5-9 kg (10-20 lb) weighted blanket triggers deep-pressure proprioception that calms the autonomic nervous system — small trials show real anxiety and sleep benefit. *(Ekholm 2020 Swedish weighted-blanket trial n=120)*

Gravity Blanket, Bearaby, Layla, Hush. ~10% of body weight. **Grade B** for anxiety + insomnia.

### 5.11 Home CES (cranial electrotherapy stimulation)

> **For you, in plain words.** Alpha-Stim is a consumer-purchasable cranial electrotherapy device (~$895) with FDA-cleared status for anxiety/insomnia/depression — small RCT support; emerging-tier evidence. *(Bystritsky 2008 Alpha-Stim RCT; Kirsch 2014 meta — regulatory-path: FDA Class III cleared, not evidence anchor)*

**Practical.** Alpha-Stim AID (anxiety/insomnia/depression) — earlobe clips, 20-60 min/day. **Grade C** with emerging signal. **Do not** use with pacemaker or implanted electronic device. **Caveat**: significant cost, modest-to-moderate effect size.

### 5.12 Home transcutaneous VNS

> **For you, in plain words.** Consumer ear-clip vagus-nerve stimulators (Nurosym, Pulsetto, Truvaga, Parasym) deliver gentle electrical buzz to the auricular branch of the vagus — emerging signal for anxiety. 20-30 min/day. *(De Ridder 2014 pilot; Nurosym pilots)*

**Practical.** Nurosym (~$700) clinical-grade home device; Pulsetto (~$300); Truvaga (~$200); Apollo Neuro (vibration not electrical) ~$349. **Do not** with pacemaker, vagal instability, or active seizure disorder. **Grade H** — emerging.

### 5.13 Yoga + body-scan

> **For you, in plain words.** Cramer's 2018 meta-analysis pooled 8 RCTs of yoga for anxiety — moderate evidence for symptom reduction. The body-scan + restorative postures matter more than the gymnastic ones for anxiety. *(Cramer 2018 yoga-anxiety meta)*

Iyengar restorative + Yoga Nidra + viparita karani. See §7.2-7.3 for the asana-and-pranayama map.

### 5.14 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Alcohol (false calm + rebound anxiety + REM destruction); high-dose caffeine; energy drinks; chronic benzodiazepines (dependence + paradoxical anxiety on taper); anti-cholinergic OTC sleep pills (Benadryl/ZzzQuil — long-term dementia signal); social-media doomscrolling (amygdala primer). *(UK MHRA benzo review; UK MHRA anti-cholinergic burden 2022)*

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.1 What to measure, in plain words

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-administered scoring at home — GAD-7, BAI, PDSS-SR, ISI, plus a daily 0-10 worry/tension slider. Direct-to-consumer labs cover thyroid (anxiety mimics hyperthyroidism), B12, iron (low ferritin presents as restless agitation), vitamin D, magnesium, plus a sleep-apnea check. *(Spitzer 2006 GAD-7; Beck 1988 BAI; Shear 1997 PDSS; Bastien 2001 ISI)*

### 11.2 Self-administered anxiety scoring

> **For you, in plain words.** The main score is GAD-7, a 7-question self-quiz scored 0-21; ≥10 = moderate, ≥15 = severe. All scoring done at home. *(Spitzer 2006 GAD-7)*

Primary: **GAD-7** — 7-question self-scored questionnaire; 0-21; ≥10 moderate anxiety; ≥15 severe. **5-point drop = clinically meaningful.** Cadence: baseline / week 4 / 8 / 12 / month 6 / 9 / 12.

Secondary: **Beck Anxiety Inventory (BAI)** — 21-item severity questionnaire, 0-63. Bands 0-7 minimal, 8-15 mild, 16-25 moderate, 26-63 severe. Baseline + week 12 + month 6 + month 12.

Panic-specific: **Panic Disorder Severity Scale (PDSS-SR)** — 7 items for panic frequency/severity. Baseline + monthly if panic subtype.

Insomnia overlay: **Insomnia Severity Index (ISI)** — 7 items; ≥15 moderate-severe insomnia. **Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI)** — target <5.

Tertiary: **0-10 slider** for worry, tension, and sleep-interference, daily.

Optional adjuncts: daily HRV (HeartMath, Oura, Whoop); 7-day average home BP cuff; weight + waist; tracking in Bearable or paper.

## 12. Implementation Timeline (12 months, self-tracked)

### 12.1 The year, in plain words

> **For you, in plain words.** A five-phase year — adaptation (weeks 1-4) → settling (5-8) → first real shift (9-12) → consolidation (months 4-6) → long-term (6-12). Caffeine-sleep-driven subtypes move fastest (weeks 2-4); generalised worry needs CBT consistency over 12 weeks. *(Hofmann 2012; Lopresti 2019)*

- **TL;DR (Weeks 1-4 — Adaptation).** Half-doses days 1-3 to test tolerance; baseline GAD-7 + BAI + DTC labs + home BP. Combo 1 + cofactor floor + coherent breathing + start CBT workbook from day 1; Combo 3 from day 7. *(EMA dose-titration; Hofmann)*
- **TL;DR (Weeks 5-8 — Settling).** Full dose; sleep usually lifts first; HRV baseline ↑; week 8 re-check ferritin, B12, vit D if low. *(Lopresti 2019)*
- **TL;DR (Weeks 9-12 — First real shift).** GAD-7 drops 4-8 points (5 = meaningful); finish CBT workbook chapters; full DTC re-check. *(Spitzer 2006; Hofmann 2012)*
- **TL;DR (Months 4-6 — Consolidation).** Maintenance doses; prune non-responders; add TCM cycling weeks 1 & 4; MBSR or MBCT 8-week course if not already done. *(NMPA monographs; Hofmann 2010)*
- **TL;DR (Months 6-12 — Long-term).** Quarterly self-scores; seasonal supplement rotation; annual labs. Realistic expectations: GAD-7 typically -8 to -12 points over 12 months on a consistent protocol; caffeine-driven anxiety often resolves; generalised worry softens but doesn't disappear. *(Hofmann 2012; Lopresti 2019)*

**No physician check-in is mandatory.** If you choose to keep a physician informed, that is your personal decision.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Anxiety has five drivers — over-tuned threat alarm, weak braking system, runaway cortisol, gut-brain dysbiosis, missing cofactors. Anxiety is not "weakness" and it is not "all in your head" — it is a real, measurable mismatch between your brain's threat-detection system and its braking system. Five things drive it: (1) your amygdala (the smoke detector) is firing too loud and too often; (2) your GABA-serotonin-vagus brake is depleted; (3) your stress hormone cortisol won't switch off; (4) your gut bacteria — which make most of the body's serotonin precursors — are out of balance; (5) cofactors like magnesium, B6, B9, B12, iron, vitamin D and omega-3 are missing.

This protocol does **not** chase anxiety with off-label nerve drugs or chronic benzodiazepines. It rebuilds each of the five layers — paced 5-6 breaths-per-minute breathing (the A-grade vagal lever), a self-applied CBT workbook (the A-grade cognitive lever), Silexan lavender capsules + L-theanine + magnesium (the GABA layer), Ashwagandha + holy basil + phosphatidylserine (the cortisol layer), a Mediterranean-millet-fermented-food diet with measured cofactor correction (the gut + cofactor layer), home red-light + sauna + weighted blanket + HRV biofeedback. **No prescription.
