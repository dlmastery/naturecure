## 1. Executive Summary

Anxiety-like presentations are conventionally siloed under "Generalised Anxiety Disorder" or "Panic Disorder" and treated with SSRIs, SNRIs, benzodiazepines (short-term), or buspirone — frequently as a first move and as long-term maintenance. The integrative reframing is different: chronic anxiety-like states are the **emergent expression of a sympathetic-dominant nervous system layered on neurochemical depletion, gut-brain axis disruption, and cognitive-behavioural patterns of avoidance and rumination**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) GABAergic underdrive paired with glutamate excitotoxicity (the "off-switch" weak, the "on-switch" stuck), (ii) HPA-axis hyperactivity with elevated cortisol awakening response and exaggerated reactivity to stressors, (iii) gut-brain axis dysbiosis where altered microbiome (low *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Faecalibacterium*) shifts vagal afferent + neurotransmitter precursor balance toward anxiogenesis, and (iv) cognitive-behavioural rumination + avoidance keeping the default-mode network ignited and reinforcing threat-detection circuits. **Synthesis:** An OTC, home-based protocol that upregulates GABA, normalises HPA, restores gut-brain microbiota, and provides behavioural-cognitive practices (CBT-informed self-work, ACT, mindfulness, polyvagal practices) addresses the upstream drivers — not just the symptom.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 GABAergic underdrive + glutamate excitotoxicity.** Anxiety-like states are characterised by reduced GABA-A receptor function (often via α2/α3 subunit hypofunction) and elevated glutamate excitotoxicity (via NMDA receptor hyperactivity). Subjective: racing thoughts, inability to "switch off", muscle tension, hyperarousal, easy startle. Key actors: GABA-A α2/α3, glycine receptors, NMDA receptor, kynurenine pathway. Targeted by L-theanine, magnesium glycinate, apigenin (chamomile/passionflower), valerian, lemon balm, lavender (Silexan), magnolia, taurine, glycine, GABA precursors (Bacopa, Ashwagandha). **Synthesis:** Restoring GABA tone is the chemical brake on hyperarousal.

**2.2 HPA-axis hyperactivity.** Chronic anxiety drives elevated CAR, exaggerated cortisol reactivity to stressors, and elevated evening cortisol. The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis is locked in over-drive, amplifying sympathetic tone, blood pressure, and threat-detection. Key actors: CRH, ACTH, cortisol, glucocorticoid receptor, FKBP5. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66, Rhodiola (low-moderate dose), Bacopa, Tulsi, phosphatidylserine 100 mg PM, evening Yoga Nidra, 4-7-8 breath. **Synthesis:** Without HPA normalisation the sympathetic system stays primed.

**2.3 Gut-brain axis dysbiosis.** Cohort studies of anxiety disorders show altered microbiome composition with reduced *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*; reduced SCFA production; increased intestinal permeability (zonulin) and LPS translocation. Microbiota shape vagal afferent signalling and serotonin/GABA/BDNF synthesis (90% of body serotonin is in the gut). Key actors: vagus nerve afferents, enteric nervous system, microbiota-derived SCFA, tryptophan-kynurenine, zonulin/occludin, LPS-TLR4. Targeted by multi-strain probiotics (Lactobacillus rhamnosus, *L. plantarum*, *L. helveticus*, *B. longum* 1714 — "psychobiotic" strains documented in UK + Irish trials), prebiotics (GOS, inulin, resistant starch), fermented foods, zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, gluten-free trial, polyphenol-rich foods, omega-3 EPA. **Synthesis:** Sealing the gut and shifting microbiota tone is the mind-body axis intervention.

**2.4 Cognitive-behavioural rumination + avoidance.** Anxiety is amplified by rumination (default-mode network ignition), threat-monitoring hypervigilance (salience network elevation), and avoidance (reinforcing the threat schema by preventing disconfirmation). Targeted by **mindfulness-based interventions** (MBSR, MBCT — UK-developed, non-US-association), **CBT-informed self-help** (Beck, Padesky — international; Australian *Mind Over Mood* workbook), **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)** (Hayes; Australian Russ Harris *The Happiness Trap* self-guided), **interoceptive exposure self-work** (gentle controlled exposure to anxiety sensations to break the threat schema), **polyvagal-theory-grounded somatic practices** (Stephen Porges; Deb Dana self-guided), **breathing practices** (4-7-8, Bhramari, resonance-frequency), **Yoga Nidra**, and **interpersonal co-regulation** with trusted others. **Synthesis:** Behavioural and cognitive shifts are non-negotiable — supplements alone do not change the meaning the mind assigns to body signals.
