### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

#### Open the science

Vitiligo is conventionally siloed as a "dermatology" diagnosis — a cosmetic depigmentation treated with topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, clinic-based NB-UVB cabins and the new topical JAK inhibitors. The integrative reframing is different: vitiligo is the **skin presentation of a systemic NRF2/GPX4 collapse on a permeable gut barrier**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) iron-dependent lipid peroxidation (ferroptosis) of melanocytes, (ii) gliadin-zonulin-mediated intestinal permeability priming a chronic CD8+ T-cell attack on melanocyte antigens, (iii) microbiome dysbiosis with loss of *Akkermansia*/*Faecalibacterium*/SCFA producers, and (iv) HPA-axis hyperactivity that accelerates depigmentation under life stress. **Synthesis.** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously upregulates NRF2/GPX4, seals the gut, restores SCFA-producing flora and downregulates cortisol addresses the upstream drivers rather than the cosmetic endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive expanding white patches — rusting pigment cells, a leaky gut, missing friendly bacteria, and chronic stress. Each node has its own intervention layer. *(Yamaguchi & Hearing 2019; Fasano 2012 zonulin; Bzioueche 2021 skin microbiome)*

### 2.2 Pigment-cell rusting (ferroptosis)

> **For you, in plain words.** Your pigment cells (melanocytes) make their own colour but also their own poison (peroxide). When the internal shield (GPX4) fails, the cells rust away. NRF2 activators turn the shield back on. *(Yamaguchi & Hearing 2019; Friedmann Angeli 2014 GPX4)*

#### Open the science

Melanogenesis generates H₂O₂, quinones and superoxide; IFN-γ and IL-1β from infiltrating CD8+ T-cells amplify mitochondrial ROS, deplete glutathione and downregulate **GPX4**. Loss of GPX4 with master TF **NRF2** and antiporter **System Xc⁻ (SLC7A11)** drives iron-dependent ferroptosis. Presentation: expanding, sharply demarcated achromic macules. Targeted by NRF2 activators (polydatin, quercetin, sulforaphane, *Polypodium leucotomos*, *Nigella sativa* thymoquinone) + glutathione-cycle support (NAC, alpha-lipoic acid, selenium) + home red light. **Synthesis.** Reinforcing GPX4/NRF2 is the upstream brake on melanocyte loss. *(Yamaguchi & Hearing 2019; Dell'Anna 2007 Italy; Middelkamp-Hup 2004 Madrid)*

### 2.3 Leaky gut autoimmunity

> **For you, in plain words.** Gluten opens microscopic gaps in the gut wall. Bacterial fragments leak through and keep the immune system on hair-trigger — which is what attacks the pigment cells. Seal the gut and the attack loses fuel. *(Fasano 2012 zonulin; Sander 2008 leaky gut autoimmunity)*

#### Open the science

Gliadin binds CXCR3 on intestinal epithelium, releasing **zonulin**, which triggers MyD88 → EGFR/PAR2 → tight-junction disassembly. LPS, undigested macromolecules and tTG-modified peptides enter the lamina propria, sustaining CD8+ priming. Targeted by strict gluten elimination, zinc-carnosine, L-glutamine, DGL liquorice, slippery elm and fermented foods. **Synthesis.** No layer holds if gluten + seed-oil intake keeps the gate open. *(Fasano 2012; AYUSH grahani-roga monograph)*

### 2.4 Friendly-bacteria depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** When the gut bacteria that make short-chain fatty acids (the calming signals that hold the immune system back) die out, the autoimmune attack escalates. Sprouted millets, fermented foods and bitter greens feed them back in. *(Bzioueche 2021; Ni 2020 Akkermansia)*

#### Open the science

Indian, Chinese and European metagenomics show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Blautia*, *Roseburia* and expanded *Proteobacteria*. Loss of butyrate / propionate / acetate reduces GPR41/43 Treg signalling. Targeted by *Momordica charantia* polysaccharides, sprouted millets, fermented foods and live-strain probiotics. **Synthesis.** SCFA recovery is the immune-tolerance lever. *(Bzioueche 2021; Ni 2020; AYUSH dhanya rasayana)*

### 2.5 Stress-HPA acceleration

> **For you, in plain words.** Stress is a documented vitiligo trigger — patches often appear or spread on the rubbing edge of a stress event (the Koebner phenomenon). Cortisol burns up the antioxidant shield and worsens the leaky gut. Ashwagandha, Yoga Nidra and humming-breath bring it down. *(van Geel 2014 Koebner; Choudhary 2017 KSM-66 cortisol)*

#### Open the science

Cortisol elevates ROS, suppresses Tregs, disrupts the gut barrier. Targeted by Ashwagandha KSM-66, Bacopa, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, home cold immersion. **Synthesis.** Without cortisol normalisation, the other three nodes regress under life stress. *(van Geel 2014; Choudhary 2017 Australia; AYUSH rasayana monographs)*

The 311 nm narrowband UVB clinic cabin remains the most-evidenced repigmentation modality (Hamzavi 2004; Bhatnagar 2007). ### When to call your doctor

> **For you, in plain words.** Six situations in active vitiligo are not "wait and see" — they need a dermatologist or relevant specialist (not the ER, but soon).

- **Rapidly-spreading new patches over weeks** — rule out an alternative diagnosis (post-inflammatory hypopigmentation, pityriasis alba, tinea versicolor, leprosy, halo nevi) and consider phototherapy referral. *(VETF 2007; British Association of Dermatologists)*
- **Co-occurring thyroid symptoms** — fatigue, hair loss, cold intolerance, neck enlargement or weight change → full thyroid antibody panel + endocrinology referral (Hashimoto / Graves overlap is common). *(WHO TCIM endocrine; EMA thyroid)*
- **Severe psychological distress, depression, or social withdrawal** — VIS-22 or DLQI ≥10, suicidal ideation, or work/relationship impairment → mental-health referral; the cosmetic load is real. *(crisis lines: 988 US, Samaritans UK, iCall IN, Lifeline AU)*
- **Patches in genital, oral, lip, periorbital or peri-anal mucosa** — these areas have specific phototherapy protocols and need dermatology + ophthalmology (peri-orbital) review before any home topical experimentation.
- **Pre-PUVA, NB-UVB, or excimer phototherapy patient considering self-treating** — coordinate with the prescribing dermatologist before stacking the dossier's topical bakuchiol + sun-bath regime (additive phototoxicity risk).
- **New autoimmune symptoms** — joint pain, alopecia areata patches, mouth ulcers, diarrhoea + weight loss (celiac), persistent fatigue with hyperpigmentation in non-vitiliginous areas (Addison) → relevant specialist referral.
