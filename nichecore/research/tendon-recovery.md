## 1. Executive Summary

Tendon pain is conventionally siloed as a "sports-med / ortho" path treated with NSAIDs, corticosteroid injection, PRP, shockwave at clinic, and ultimately tenotomy / open repair. The integrative reframing is different: tendinopathy is **not "inflammation" (tendinITIS is a misnomer) — it is a chronic, non-resolving failed-healing response** in which collagen turnover, tenocyte metabolism, and matrix water content all drift away from the strong parallel-aligned collagen-I architecture toward disorganised collagen-III, neovascularisation, neurogenic ingrowth and altered glycosaminoglycan ratios. Four convergent root mechanisms — (i) failed collagen turnover (collagen-I underbuilt; collagen-III over-deposited) with collagen peptide + vitamin C cofactor deficit, (ii) tenocyte oxidative + AGE stress (insulin resistance, hyperglycaemia, and dyslipidaemia directly accelerate tendinopathy — diabetic tendons fail predictably), (iii) **load-management failure** — wrong dose, wrong tempo, wrong rest (or, paradoxically, too much rest after an irritation event), and (iv) low-grade soft-tissue inflammation from poor sleep, high cortisol and low Treg / vitamin-D milieu. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously provides matrix substrate, dampens AGE/oxidative stress, prescribes a **progressive heavy-slow-resistance loading program (HSR)** and corrects sleep + metabolic substrate addresses the upstream drivers — not the cortisone-shot endpoint.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

**2.1 Failed collagen turnover — Type I underbuilt, Type III over-deposited, GAG mismatch.** Healthy tendon is 65–80% water + 20–30% dry-weight collagen, of which 95% is type I (strong parallel fibres) and a small fraction is type III (more elastic, less load-bearing). Tendinopathy shifts this ratio: type III rises, fibres lose parallel alignment, proteoglycans (decorin, aggrecan) shift toward a cartilage-like profile. Tenocytes are starved of the precursors needed to lay down properly hydroxylated, glycosylated type I collagen. Targeted by hydrolysed collagen peptides 15 g + vitamin C 500–1000 mg taken 30–60 min before loading session (Shaw / Baar Edinburgh / Australian Institute of Sport protocol), gelatin 15 g as alternative, hyaluronic acid (oral), MSM, silica, copper (lysyl oxidase cofactor for cross-linking), manganese, zinc. **Synthesis:** Tendon cannot rebuild without the building blocks delivered at the right time.

**2.2 Tenocyte oxidative + AGE stress (metabolic substrate).** Diabetic tendons fail. Cohorts show 2–3× tendinopathy and 3–5× rupture risk in T2D, in part from non-enzymatic glycation of collagen — Advanced Glycation End-products (AGE) cross-link collagen abnormally, making tendon stiff, brittle and prone to micro-failure. Hyperlipidaemia (LDL deposition in tendon — tendon xanthoma is the visible extreme) and statin therapy independently weaken tendon. Oxidative stress in tenocytes drives ferroptosis-like senescence. Targeted by gluten + sugar elimination, omega-3 (EPA-rich), curcumin phytosome, Boswellia AKBA, NAC, alpha-lipoic acid, magnesium glycinate, vitamin D, vitamin K2, sprouted millets, *Centella asiatica* (gotu kola — classical collagen-synthesis remedy), *Withania somnifera*. **Synthesis:** Metabolic-AGE control is non-negotiable for chronic non-resolving tendinopathy.

**2.3 Load management — too little OR too much, wrong tempo.** Tendon adapts to mechanical load via mechanotransduction (tenocyte cilia + integrin signalling → IGF-1 / TGF-β / collagen-I gene expression). Both under-load (immobilisation → matrix degradation) and over-load (volume + intensity beyond tendon-cell synthesis rate) drive tendinopathy. The strongest evidence base for non-surgical tendon recovery — Alfredson eccentric heel-drop, Beyer / Kongsgaard Heavy Slow Resistance (HSR), Silbernagel progressive — all centre on **slow tempo (3-3-3), heavy progressive load (60–85% 1-RM), 3 sessions/week, 12-week minimum**. Plyometric work is added later. Targeted by structured HSR program (per body region in §7.6), tempo-eccentric Alfredson protocol for Achilles, isometric mid-range hold (45 s × 5) for acute pain modulation, gradual return to sport at week 12–16. **Synthesis:** Load is the medicine. No supplement substitutes for properly dosed mechanical load.

**2.4 Soft-tissue inflammation + HPA / sleep / vitamin-D milieu.** While tendinopathy is not classical inflammation, the surrounding paratenon, peri-tendinous tissue and the chronic low-grade inflammatory cytokine pool (IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β) do bias healing direction. Vitamin D <30 ng/mL is over-represented in tendinopathy cohorts. Chronic stress / sleep <7 h elevates cortisol which suppresses collagen synthesis. Targeted by vitamin D3 + K2, Ashwagandha KSM-66, *Bacopa monnieri*, pranayama, Yoga Nidra, vagal practices, sleep hygiene. **Synthesis:** Without sleep + vitamin D + cortisol control, HSR + collagen peptides plateau.

### 6.5 Environmental & occupational triggers to eliminate

- **Sudden return to high-load activity after deconditioning** — single biggest tendon-failure trigger. Use the protocol's progression chart, never skip a stage.
- **Wrong shoes** — replace worn shoes (sole compression >50% spent). For Achilles: heel lift / drop ≥6 mm during acute phase. For plantar fascia: arch-support insoles + supportive house shoes (no barefoot on hard floors). Hoka, Brooks, Asics — generous heel toe drop.
- **Poor ergonomics** — for tennis elbow: forearm-vertical mouse, split keyboard, lighter racket grip + correct backhand technique; for hamstring: stop sitting on hard chairs, use coccyx cushion; for rotator cuff: monitor at eye level, no sleeping on involved shoulder; for plantar: cushioned house slippers.
- **Repetitive over-use** — texting (de Quervain), guitar without rest (forearm tendon), running on concrete (Achilles + plantar).
- **Cold dampness** — heated insoles, wool socks, warmers (silk + wool sleeves).
- **BPA, phthalates** — glass / stainless steel.
- **Smoking, vaping, alcohol** — Mg / B-vit depletion, microvascular vasoconstriction; smoking is a documented independent tendinopathy risk factor.
- **Statin therapy** (if on it for cholesterol) — tendinopathy + rupture is a documented class effect; coordinate with prescriber if you are already on one. The protocol does not recommend stopping a statin.
- **Fluoroquinolone antibiotics** — documented tendon rupture risk for up to 6 months post-course (especially Achilles). If you must take one for a prescribed indication, **pause HSR** during and for 2 weeks after; light loading only.
- **Sleep ergonomics** — pillow positioning to protect involved tendon (e.g., shoulder pillow for rotator cuff).
- **Light pollution / blue light at night** — protects melatonin which supports overnight collagen synthesis.

### 7.6 Exercise periodization (weekly + monthly) — **HSR / Eccentric protocols are the medicine**

**THIS IS THE LOAD-BEARING SECTION FOR TENDON RECOVERY.** Supplements support; load rebuilds.

**Per body region — the gold-standard protocol:**

- **Achilles mid-portion (Alfredson)**: 3 × 15 reps eccentric heel-drop on slant board with knee straight + 3 × 15 with knee bent, **2 sessions/day, 7 days/week, 12 weeks**, progressively weighted vest from week 2.
- **Achilles insertional**: same Alfredson but **NO full dorsiflexion** (stop at neutral); heel lift in shoes.
- **Patellar (Kongsgaard HSR)**: 3 sessions/week of single-leg squat 3-3-3 tempo at 70–85% 1-RM, 4 sets × 6–15 reps progressing over 12 weeks. Squat with foot on 25° decline board.
- **Lateral epicondyle (tennis elbow)**: eccentric wrist extension with dumbbell or band, 3 × 15 BID, 12 weeks; counter-force strap during activity.
- **Medial epicondyle (golfer's elbow)**: eccentric wrist flexion, same protocol.
- **Rotator cuff (supraspinatus)**: external rotation with band at 0° abduction → 30° → 90°; full-can / empty-can within pain limits; scapular retraction + serratus push-up; 3×/week, 12 weeks.
- **Gluteus medius / GTPS**: side-lying clam + lateral band walk + single-leg bridge + Bulgarian split squat; 3×/week, 12 weeks.
- **Hamstring proximal**: Romanian deadlift + Nordic hamstring eccentric + glute-ham raise; avoid sitting on hard surfaces.
- **Plantar fasciopathy (Rathleff high-load)**: heel raise with toe extended (towel under toes) on step, 3-3-3 tempo, 3 × 12 every other day, 12 weeks.
- **De Quervain**: thumb-spica splint + isometric thumb extension; gradual eccentric; 6–12 weeks.

**Universal HSR rules:**
- **Pre-load gelatin block 15 g + vitamin C 50 mg, 60 min before session** (Shaw / Baar 2017).
- **Pain monitoring scale 0–10**: pain during exercise ≤5/10 is acceptable; pain settles within 24 h is acceptable; rising pain over days means deload by 25%.
- **3-3-3 tempo** (3 s concentric, 3 s isometric, 3 s eccentric).
- **Progression** by 5–10% per 2 weeks if tolerated.
- **NO cold immersion within 2 h of HSR** (cold blunts mTOR + collagen synthesis).
- **Isometric mid-range hold (5 × 45 s)** as acute pain modulator when tendon is too sore for HSR — borrowed from Rio / Cook 2015.

**Weekly cadence (general)**
- **Mon / Wed / Fri** — HSR loading session (45 min, gelatin pre-load)
- **Tue / Thu** — Zone 2 cardio (45 min) avoiding involved tendon stress (e.g., swimming or cycling instead of running for Achilles)
- **Sat** — mobility + restorative + foam-roll
- **Sun** — long walk / yin yoga / Yoga Nidra

**Monthly cadence**
- **Week 1** — full HSR; TCM cycling (Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang or Shen Tong Zhu Yu Tang)
- **Week 2** — moderate HSR + progress 5%
- **Week 3** — deload (≤70% volume) + extra sleep + extra sauna
- **Week 4** — full HSR; TCM cycling re-cycles

Optional 14:10 IF — **not** during peak HSR load weeks (energy availability matters for tenocyte synthesis).

### 10.6 Strictly Avoid — specific to tendon pathology

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

Tendon pain is one of the most misunderstood injuries in modern medicine. The old word "tendinitis" implies inflammation, but the actual story inside a chronic painful tendon is different: it is a **failed healing response**. Four things are happening together: (1) your body is trying to lay down new collagen but it's laying down the wrong kind (type III instead of strong type I) because the building blocks and the timing are off; (2) the metabolic background — sugar, AGE cross-links, sometimes insulin resistance — is making your tendon brittle and stiff at a chemical level; (3) the loading dose, tempo and progression of your activity is either too little or too much (or the wrong tempo), and the tendon never gets the signal to remodel correctly; and (4) sleep, vitamin D and cortisol are tilting healing in the wrong direction.

This protocol does **not** try to mask the pain with chronic NSAIDs, escalate toward repeated cortisone shots (which weaken tendons over time), or rush you toward PRP and tenotomy. Instead, it rebuilds each of those four layers from the inside out — with bone broth, **collagen peptides (or a flavoured gelatin block) taken 60 minutes before every loading session along with vitamin C** (this is the Shaw / Baar protocol from Edinburgh that doubled collagen synthesis in engineered tendons), Centella asiatica (the classical Ayurvedic / Kerala collagen plant), MSM for sulphur, copper for lysyl oxidase (the enzyme that cross-links collagen properly), classical Ayurvedic and TCM tendon oils, home red-light therapy, and most importantly **a structured Heavy-Slow-Resistance program** done 3 sessions a week for at least 12 weeks at a 3-second-up / 3-second-hold / 3-second-down tempo. **No NSAID for the chronic phase. No cortisone shots. No surgery.**

Expect a slow, steady re-build. The first thing you will notice (Weeks 4–6) is that morning stiffness shortens and the first painful step in the morning becomes less painful. Around Week 12 your VISA score will measurably improve. Real tendon-matrix recovery is a 6-to-12-month story.
