> **For you, in plain words.** "Dizziness" is a catch-all for conditions that feel similar but have very different causes + fixes. Identify **which sub-type** you have. ~50-70% true vertigo = BPPV (a tiny calcium crystal that the self-Epley maneuver re-positions in 60 sec). ~5-10% = Meniere's (episodic vertigo + hearing loss + ear fullness + tinnitus). ~10-20% = vestibular migraine. Rest = vestibular neuritis (post-viral), PPPD (chronic anxiety-vertigo loop), cervicogenic, orthostatic hypotension (~20%), rarer central causes (brainstem stroke, MS, vestibular schwannoma). This dossier teaches self-triage (Dix-Hallpike + supine roll-test), A-grade Epley + Semont + Brandt-Daroff self-maneuvers, daily vestibular rehab, sub-type-tuned diet, and red-flag recognition. *(Hilton 2014 Cochrane Epley A-grade; Hillier 2015 Cochrane VRT A-grade; Bhattacharyya 2017 BPPV guidelines)*

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What vertigo + dizziness actually are

> **For you, in plain words.** "Vertigo" is the false sensation of spinning when you're not moving. "Dizziness" is a broader word that also includes light-headedness (BP drop on standing), disequilibrium (unsteadiness), and pre-syncope (about to faint). These are not the same — and the cause matters more than the label. *(Bisdorff 2009 Barany Society classification)*

### 1.2 The five sub-type-driven levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: triage yourself with the Dix-Hallpike test (look up a VEDA-quality video; if it triggers vertigo with rotatory nystagmus on one side → posterior-canal BPPV). If BPPV positive → do the Epley maneuver tonight (A-grade Cochrane Hilton 2014 — gold standard). If not BPPV: start a 4-week daily vestibular rehabilitation practice (Cawthorne-Cooksey + gaze stabilisation — A-grade Cochrane Hillier 2015) regardless of cause. This week: home BP cuff to rule out orthostatic hypotension, migraine-trigger diary for vestibular migraine, sodium-and-fluid log for Meniere's, weekly PHQ-9 / GAD-7 for PPPD. *(Hilton 2014 Cochrane Epley A-grade; Hillier 2015 Cochrane VRT A-grade)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Vertigo is usually framed as "inner-ear thing — take meclizine." A more honest reframe: vertigo + dizziness = the symptomatic output of seventeen distinct sub-types running through **five overlapping vestibular systems** — (i) **otoconia + semicircular canal** (BPPV); (ii) **endolymph + perilymph fluid balance** (Meniere's); (iii) **vestibular nerve + brainstem-cerebellar** (neuritis, schwannoma, MS, central); (iv) **cortical processing + anxiety-prediction loop** (PPPD, chronic vestibular migraine); (v) **autonomic + cardiovascular + cervical** (orthostatic, AFib-syncope, cervicogenic). **Synthesis.** Disciplined sub-type triage (Dix-Hallpike → Epley for BPPV; sodium-fluid for Meniere's; trigger elimination for vestibular migraine; CBT for PPPD; cervical PT for cervicogenic) + structured VRT daily + supplement layer (D for BPPV recurrence; Mg+CoQ10 for vestibular migraine; ginger for motion) + balance training (tai chi A-grade falls) targets the exact mechanism instead of suppressing the symptom.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five systems — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five systems run vertigo + dizziness — otoconia (BPPV), endolymph (Meniere's), vestibular nerve (neuritis / schwannoma / MS / stroke), cortical anxiety loop (PPPD / migraine), and autonomic-cervical inputs (orthostatic / AFib / cervicogenic). Naming them lets every maneuver, food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Bisdorff 2009; Strupp 2013)*

### 2.2 Otoconia + semicircular canal (BPPV)

> **For you, in plain words.** Tiny calcium crystals (otoconia) live on a gel mat inside your inner ear. When one breaks loose and floats into a semicircular canal, it triggers vertigo every time you move your head — especially rolling over in bed or looking up. The Epley maneuver moves the crystal back to where it belongs. *(Bhattacharyya 2017 BPPV international guidelines; Hilton 2014 Cochrane)*

**The deeper layer.** BPPV ~50-70% of all true vertigo. Posterior-canal (most common) = brief (≤1 min) rotatory vertigo + rotatory-vertical nystagmus on Dix-Hallpike. Horizontal-canal less common (supine roll-test). Anterior-canal rare. Risks: head trauma, prior episode (~50% recurrence), aging, vitamin D deficiency (Talaat 2015 small RCT in deficient: D repletion cut recurrence). Targeted by **self-Epley** (A-grade Cochrane Hilton 2014); Semont alternative; Brandt-Daroff residual; Lempert/barbecue for horizontal canal; D repletion if deficient. **Synthesis.** BPPV = highest-leverage sub-type — Epley resolves in 1 session for most.

### 2.3 Endolymph + perilymph fluid balance (Meniere's)

> **For you, in plain words.** Meniere's disease is hydrops — excess fluid pressure in the inner ear — that causes episodes of vertigo lasting hours, with hearing loss, tinnitus, and ear fullness on the affected side. Low-sodium diet + fluid management is the home cornerstone. *(Sajjadi 2008 Meniere; Hallpike 1938 classical)*

**The deeper layer.** Meniere's ~3-5% of vertigo overall but most disabling chronic form. Diagnosis: ≥2 spontaneous episodes 20 min-12 h + sensorineural hearing loss + tinnitus or aural fullness. Endolymphatic hydrops postmortem; causal role debated. Targeted by **low-sodium <1.5-2 g/d** (Santos 1993 + Greco 1989 B-grade); reduce caffeine + alcohol; sleep + stress; betahistine (EU-only Rx; Cochrane Murdin 2016 NULL — surface honestly); diuretics (Thirlwall 2006 Cochrane modest); intratympanic gentamicin refractory (A-grade Patel 2016 vertigo control + hearing-loss caveat); intratympanic dexamethasone (Garduño-Anaya 2005). **Synthesis.** Diet + sodium + stress + sleep give meaningful control; refractory escalates.

### 2.4 Vestibular nerve + brainstem-cerebellar integration

> **For you, in plain words.** Vestibular neuritis (post-viral inflammation of the vestibular nerve), labyrinthitis (inflammation including hearing), and central causes (cerebellar stroke, MS, brainstem stroke, vestibular schwannoma) all involve the nerve + brain side. The trick is distinguishing them — and never missing a posterior-circulation stroke. *(Strupp 2013; Kattah 2009 HINTS exam)*

**The deeper layer.** Vestibular neuritis = sudden severe vertigo days, no hearing change, post-viral URI; HINTS (Head Impulse positive one side, unidirectional horizontal nystagmus, no skew) = reassuring peripheral. Labyrinthitis adds hearing loss. Central (HINTS fail — Head Impulse normal both sides, direction-changing nystagmus, vertical skew) = ER + MRI. Vestibular schwannoma = slow benign tumor on vestibular nerve with progressive unilateral hearing loss + tinnitus + occasional vertigo — MRI + neurosurgery/gamma-knife. Targeted by **VRT** (Cochrane Hillier 2015 A-grade unilateral peripheral); short-term vestibular suppressants only (Strupp 2011 — >3 days delays central compensation); steroids for neuritis (Strupp 2004 NEJM modest). **Synthesis.** Differential diagnosis is the lever; once peripheral confirmed, VRT is the durable fix.

### 2.5 Cortical processing + anxiety-prediction loop (PPPD + chronic vestibular migraine)

> **For you, in plain words.** Persistent postural-perceptual dizziness (PPPD) is a chronic functional dizziness driven by a brain that has learned to over-monitor balance and over-predict threat — often triggered by an initial vestibular insult that then persists. Vestibular migraine is migraine that presents as vertigo without head pain. Both respond to CBT + SSRI + vestibular rehab. *(Staab 2017 PPPD diagnostic criteria; Holmberg 2006 CBT-PPPD; Lempert 2012 vestibular migraine criteria)*

**The deeper layer.** PPPD (formerly PPV / chronic subjective dizziness / phobic postural vertigo) = Barany Society formally recognised (Staab 2017) — chronic dizziness >3 mo, worse upright or in complex visual environments, anxiety-driven. CBT (Holmberg 2006; Schaaf 2014) B+; SSRIs (Staab 2002 sertraline; Horii 2007 paroxetine) B+. Vestibular migraine (MAV) ~10-30% of migraine sufferers; episodes minutes-hours with/without head pain, classical migraine triggers (food, stress, sleep, hormones). Targeted by **trigger elimination** + **Mg 400 mg/d + CoQ10 100-300 mg + B2 400 mg/d** (migraine-prophylaxis extrapolated B-grade); standard migraine prophylaxis clinic-route. **Synthesis.** PPPD + vestibular migraine = most under-diagnosed sub-types; naming them is the lever.

### 2.6 Autonomic + cardiovascular + cervical (orthostatic, AFib, cervicogenic)

> **For you, in plain words.** ~20% of people who say "I'm dizzy" actually have orthostatic hypotension — BP drops when they stand. Some have AFib-driven syncope-vertigo overlap. Some have cervicogenic dizziness from neck dysfunction. These are NOT inner-ear problems and don't respond to inner-ear treatments. *(Freeman 2011 orthostatic hypotension; Reiley 2017 cervicogenic dizziness)*

**The deeper layer.** Orthostatic hypotension = systolic BP drop ≥20 mmHg or diastolic ≥10 mmHg within 3 min of standing — common in elderly, dehydration, autonomic dysfunction, medications. AFib drives pre-syncope / syncope episodes that the patient describes as "dizzy spells." Cervicogenic dizziness from C0-C3 dysfunction, whiplash, TMJ-cervical overlap — controversial diagnosis but real cervical-PT response in selected patients. POTS (postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome) — heart rate rises ≥30 bpm on standing without BP drop. Targeted by: **home BP cuff supine + standing readings**; KardiaMobile for AFib screening; hydration + sodium for POTS-orthostatic; structured cervical PT for cervicogenic; medication review for medication-induced orthostatic. **Synthesis.** Always screen BP supine + standing before assuming inner-ear cause.

### 3.2 The honest caveat — betahistine Cochrane NULL

> **For you, in plain words.** Betahistine (Serc) is the most-prescribed Meniere's drug in Europe — widely used for decades. The 2016 Cochrane review (Murdin et al.) found NO clear benefit for vertigo-episode reduction in Meniere's. **We mention it honestly because it's prescribed everywhere in Europe and India, but the largest evidence review says it doesn't reliably reduce vertigo episodes.** It's an Rx (not in the home protocol), placed in §10.7.3 with the honest caveat. *(Murdin 2016 Cochrane NULL)*

### 3.4 The diet base layer (sub-type-specific)

> **For you, in plain words.** Diet for vertigo depends on sub-type. Meniere's = low-sodium <2 g/d. Vestibular migraine = migraine-trigger elimination (chocolate, aged cheese, MSG, nitrates, red wine, aged meats, caffeine). PPPD = general Mediterranean + anti-inflammatory + sleep + stress. *(Santos 1993 + Greco 1989 sodium Meniere; classical migraine-trigger lit)*

**Quick start.** Build a 4-week food + symptom diary. If Meniere's pattern → strict low-sodium <2 g/d trial (reads labels — most processed foods exceed this fast). If vestibular migraine → eliminate top triggers for 4 weeks then re-challenge one at a time. If PPPD → Mediterranean + magnesium + structured sleep.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 Self-administered Epley maneuver (the cornerstone)

> **For you, in plain words.** The single most powerful home intervention in vertigo. If your vertigo is positional + triggered by rolling over in bed or looking up — and the Dix-Hallpike test triggers it on one side — you have posterior-canal BPPV, and the Epley maneuver resolves it in 1-3 sessions for ~80% of users. **A-grade Cochrane evidence (Hilton 2014).** *(Hilton 2014 Cochrane; Bhattacharyya 2017 BPPV international guidelines)*

**Quick start.** Find a VEDA-quality (Vestibular Disorders Association) Epley video. The maneuver: (1) Sit upright on bed with head turned 45° toward affected side; (2) Lie back quickly so head hangs over edge of bed, still turned 45°; (3) Hold 30-60 sec until vertigo + nystagmus subside; (4) Turn head 90° to other side (now 45° to opposite side); (5) Hold 30-60 sec; (6) Roll body onto side, head turned 45° downward; (7) Hold 30-60 sec; (8) Sit up slowly. Repeat 1-3× per day until vertigo resolves. Sleep upright (45°) for 2 nights after successful maneuver.

### 5.2 Semont maneuver (alternative for posterior canal)

If Epley doesn't work or is awkward for cervical-spine limited patients, the Semont (Liberation) maneuver is an A-grade alternative — Cochrane Hilton 2014 lists it equivalent to Epley for posterior-canal BPPV.

### 5.3 Brandt-Daroff exercises (residual habituation)

After successful Epley, residual subjective dizziness often persists for days-weeks. Brandt-Daroff exercises (sit-lie-flip-sit on each side, 5 reps × 3 sessions/day) habituate the vestibular system to position changes. Useful supplement to Epley.

### 5.4 Lempert / barbecue roll (horizontal canal BPPV)

For horizontal-canal BPPV (supine roll-test positive), the Lempert "barbecue roll" maneuver — log-roll 360° in 90° increments — is the home intervention. Less common than posterior-canal but worth knowing.

### 5.5 Vestibular rehabilitation therapy (VRT) — daily practice

> **For you, in plain words.** The A-grade Cochrane evidence (Hillier 2015) for unilateral peripheral vestibular dysfunction (vestibular neuritis, post-BPPV, age-related). Daily 20-30 min of structured exercises drives central compensation. *(Hillier 2015 Cochrane VRT A-grade)*

Core exercises (Cawthorne-Cooksey + modern VRT):
- **Gaze stabilisation (VOR adaptation)** — fix eyes on a target while moving head side-to-side, then up-down, increasing speed over weeks
- **Habituation exercises** — repeated position changes that provoke mild symptoms to drive central adaptation
- **Balance training** — Romberg, sharpened Romberg, single-leg stance, eyes-open then eyes-closed
- **Gait training** — walking with head turns, walking on foam, walking with eyes closed for short distances (safety!)
- **Visual scanning** — saccades and pursuits to retrain eye-head coordination

Apps: VEDA resources, VertiGO!, Vestibular First exercises, Bertec Vision Advantage for clinic use.

### 5.6 Tai chi for balance + falls prevention

A-grade evidence (Wayne 2015 + Tousignant 2013) for balance improvement + falls prevention. Weekly tai chi class + home practice is the cornerstone for elderly + bilateral vestibular hypofunction.

### 5.7 Home video-Frenzel goggles (consumer-emerging)

Consumer-emerging — clinical Frenzel goggles remove visual fixation to reveal subtle nystagmus. Home versions (smartphone-attached video goggles) are emerging for self-assessment of nystagmus during Dix-Hallpike. Not yet mainstream but worth knowing.

### 5.8 Home BP cuff (orthostatic screen)

Omron 5/7/10 ($40-80). Measure BP supine (lying 5 min), then standing at 1 min and 3 min. Drop ≥20 mmHg systolic or ≥10 mmHg diastolic = orthostatic hypotension. ~20% of "dizziness" patients have OH, not inner-ear.

### 5.9 KardiaMobile / Apple Watch (AFib + syncope screen)

For dizziness + pre-syncope + syncope episodes — weekly rhythm check catches AFib + bradyarrhythmia.

### 5.10 Home pulse-oximeter (OSA screen)

OSA + vertigo overlap is under-recognised. Overnight pulse-ox (Wellue O2Ring) screens for desaturation events.

### 5.11 Symptom tracking apps + diaries

Migraine Buddy, N1-Headache, Vertigo Diary apps — track frequency, duration, triggers, severity. Essential for vestibular migraine + Meniere's pattern recognition.

### 5.12 Red-light, sauna, cold (general adjuncts)

Mito Red Light, Joovv (10-20 min head/neck 3-4×/wk); infrared sauna 20-30 min 3-4×/wk (caution dehydration triggering Meniere's); cold immersion CAUTION (BP spike + orthostatic — only after orthostatic screen + clearance).

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Sub-type-specific diet

> **For you, in plain words.** Diet for vertigo depends on sub-type. Meniere's = strict low-sodium <2 g/d + reduce caffeine + alcohol. Vestibular migraine = migraine-trigger elimination. PPPD = general Mediterranean + anti-inflammatory + sleep + stress.

### 6.2 Meniere's-specific diet

- Sodium <1.5-2 g/d (read every label)
- Caffeine ≤1 cup/day or eliminate
- Alcohol minimal or eliminate
- Hydration steady — 2-3 L/d, avoid swings
- Avoid MSG (often triggers)
- Avoid large amounts of any food at one sitting (fluid load matters)

### 6.3 Vestibular migraine — elimination phase

Eliminate for 4 weeks then re-challenge one at a time:
- Aged cheese (tyramine)
- Chocolate
- Aged/cured meats (nitrates)
- Red wine + alcohol
- MSG-containing foods
- Aspartame
- Caffeine — taper carefully (rebound headache)
- Nuts (variable individual sensitivity)
- Citrus + bananas (variable)
- Skipped meals (huge migraine trigger)
- Dehydration

### 6.4 PPPD + general Mediterranean

- 250 g leafy greens + cruciferous daily
- 100 g berries daily
- 30 g raw nuts daily
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil daily
- Oily fish 3-4×/week
- Legumes 4 servings/week
- Whole grains 200 g/day
- Fermented food daily
- No liquid sugar
- Minimal ultra-processed food

### 6.5 General daily floor (all sub-types)

- Mediterranean base layer
- Magnesium-rich foods (leafy greens, nuts, seeds, dark chocolate)
- Vitamin-D-rich foods + sun exposure
- Adequate protein for elderly (sarcopenia + falls prevention)
- Steady hydration (Meniere's especially)

- **High-dose Ginkgo + ginger + omega-3** combined if on anticoagulant — bleeding risk.
- **Bhastrika + Kapalbhati** if migraine-prone or HTN — BP/migraine trigger.
- **Inversion yoga poses** in BPPV active phase — symptom provocation.
- **Cold immersion** without BP screen — orthostatic risk.
- **Driving** with uncontrolled vertigo — formal evaluation first.
- **Alcohol** in Meniere's + vestibular migraine — trigger.
- **High salt** in Meniere's — single biggest dietary lever.
- **MSG + aspartame + tyramine-rich aged foods** in vestibular migraine.
- **High-dose niacin (>1 g/day)** — flushing + BP.
- **St. John's Wort** with SSRI for PPPD — serotonin syndrome.
- **Ephedra / Ma Huang** — CV + BP risk.
- **Pregnancy:** Vitex, high-dose Bacopa untested, Berberine, high-dose vitamin A — flag with obstetric care.
- **"Vertigo cure" supplement marketing** that promises one-pill solution — there isn't one; sub-type triage is the lever.

#### Open the science

The Cochrane Hilton 2014 review of canalith-repositioning procedures (Epley + Semont) established A-grade evidence for posterior-canal BPPV resolution — single-session resolution rates ~80%. The Cochrane Hillier 2015 review of vestibular rehabilitation therapy established A-grade evidence for unilateral peripheral vestibular dysfunction (post-vestibular-neuritis, post-BPPV residual, age-related). The Cochrane Murdin 2016 review of betahistine for Meniere's found NO clear evidence of benefit for vertigo episode reduction despite widespread EU use — we surface this honestly because betahistine is over-prescribed in EU and India. The Strupp 2011 review noted vestibular suppressants (meclizine, dimenhydrinate) delay central compensation if used beyond 72 hours and should be limited to acute symptom relief. The Wayne 2015 review + Tousignant 2013 RCT established tai chi A-grade for falls prevention in elderly. The Patel 2016 RCT of intratympanic gentamicin for refractory Meniere's established A-grade vertigo control but with the honest caveat of induced sensorineural hearing loss + 100% vestibular ablation. The Staab 2002 case series of sertraline for PPPD (then called chronic subjective dizziness) established the SSRI signal for PPPD that subsequent reviews have supported. The Talaat 2015 small RCT in vitamin-D-deficient BPPV patients showed D repletion cut recurrence — useful for BPPV recurrence prevention if you're D-deficient. The Lempert 2012 international consensus criteria + Cherchi 2018 review established vestibular migraine as a distinct entity requiring migraine-trigger elimination + standard migraine prophylaxis.

## 12. Implementation Timeline (12 months)

### 12.1 Weeks 1-4 — Adaptation + sub-type triage

> **For you, in plain words.** Triage your sub-type in week 1. If active BPPV → Epley immediately. Start daily VRT regardless of cause. Identify Meniere's vs vestibular migraine vs PPPD vs orthostatic via symptom diary. *(Hilton 2014; Hillier 2015)*

- Day 1: Self-Dix-Hallpike + supine roll-test. If posterior-canal BPPV → Epley tonight. Home BP cuff set up.
- Day 1-3: Half-dose supplement ramp (Mg, CoQ10); full dose D3/K2, B2.
- Week 1: Symptom + trigger diary started. Sodium log if suspecting Meniere's. Daily VRT app 20-30 min started.
- Week 2: Tai chi or balance class started. PHQ-9 + GAD-7 monthly. Weekly KardiaMobile.
- Week 3-4: Full supplement doses. Diet sub-type-tuned (low-sodium / trigger-elim / Mediterranean).
- DTC labs: baseline A1c + lipid + B12 + ferritin + 25(OH)D + TSH + ANA (if autoimmune Meniere suspicion).

### 12.2 Weeks 5-8 — Stabilisation

- Tolerance confirmed. DHI tracked monthly.
- VRT progression — gaze stabilisation + habituation + balance.
- If PPPD pattern emerging → start CBT-PPPD workbook (Holmberg protocol) + consider SSRI consult.

### 12.3 Weeks 9-12 — Clinical shift

- Repeat DHI + Vertigo Symptom Scale + FGA at week 12.
- Repeat DTC labs.
- Identify sub-type clearly — escalate to otology / neurology if atypical.

### 12.4 Months 4-6 — Consolidation

- Maintenance protocol locked.
- If Meniere's not controlled with diet → consult otology re: intratympanic dexamethasone (preserves hearing) before gentamicin.
- If vestibular migraine not controlled with magnesium + CoQ10 + trigger-elim → consult neurology re: standard migraine prophylaxis.

### 12.5 Months 6-12 — Long-term

- Quarterly DHI + DTC labs.
- Annual full panel + audiometry if Meniere's.
- Tai chi class long-term for falls prevention.

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

> **For you, in plain words.** What is going on, what to expect, and what NOT to expect.

You have vertigo or dizziness. Figure out which sub-type — the cause matters more than the label. ~50-70% = BPPV; self-Epley resolves in 1-3 sessions for ~80%. ~5-10% = Meniere's (low-sodium + fluid management). ~10-20% = vestibular migraine (trigger elim + Mg + CoQ10 + B2). Vestibular neuritis (post-viral) or PPPD (chronic anxiety-vertigo loop) → VRT + CBT ± SSRI. ~20% = orthostatic hypotension or AFib-syncope — NOT inner-ear. Triage matters.

What to expect: BPPV with Epley resolves in days. Meniere's with strict sodium reduces frequency within weeks. Vestibular migraine with trigger elim + Mg reduces episodes 4-8 weeks. PPPD with CBT + VRT + SSRI improves over 2-6 months. Vestibular neuritis compensates over 4-12 weeks with VRT. Elderly falls improve with tai chi + balance over 8-12 weeks.

What NOT to expect: one-pill cure. Vestibular suppressants help acutely but delay compensation if >3 days. Betahistine has Cochrane-NULL evidence for Meniere's despite EU prescription pattern. The home protocol works because it targets mechanism per sub-type — A-grade self-Epley + A-grade VRT + A-grade tai chi + dietary cornerstones are genuinely effective.

The sovereign ethos: OTC, home-based, independent. **No Rx required.** Red flags (central vertigo, sudden hearing loss + neuro signs, thunderclap headache, syncope + chest pain) → ER or specialist immediately. Sovereign over your own body.
