> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for women whose body and mind shift in the 7–14 days before a period — bloating, breast pain, irritability, mood crash, cramps, cyclical migraine, or in the more severe PMDD form, true 1-week-of-the-month depression. It is **not** an oral contraceptive protocol, not an SSRI protocol, not a clinic protocol. It is the layered, honest, evidence-graded path: cycle-aware nutrition, magnesium + calcium + vitamin B6 floor, chasteberry (Vitex) as the most-studied hormonal layer, Xiao Yao San for the textbook liver-qi-stagnation pattern, yoga and slow breath in the luteal phase, CBT for PMS and dialectical-skills for PMDD, and an honest conversation about where evening primrose oil (Cochrane null) and other over-marketed remedies sit. *(WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034; Schellenberg 2001 Vitex RCT BMJ; Thys-Jacobs 1998 calcium RCT)*
>
> **Pregnancy & trying-to-conceive safety (load-bearing — read this first).** **Pregnant women, women actively trying to conceive in the current cycle, and breastfeeding women must discontinue every herb in this dossier — including chasteberry/Vitex, dong quai, black cohosh, Xiao Yao San, Shatavari at high tonic doses, Ashoka, Lodhra, Pushyanuga Choorna, saffron, Schisandra, and all classical TCM/Unani/Siddha compound formulas.** Pregnancy nutrition is its own science — only pregnancy-validated nutrients are appropriate: **methylfolate 400–800 µg, choline 450 mg, iron only if ferritin low (target 70 ng/mL+), vitamin D3 2000–4000 IU, omega-3 DHA 200–300 mg, iodine 150 µg, magnesium glycinate 300 mg, B12 methylcobalamin 1000 µg**. If you are uncertain whether you may be pregnant (late period, unusual cycle, recent unprotected intercourse), pause every herb until a home urine pregnancy test confirms negative.
>
> **Red flags — when this is not just PMS (rule-out, not self-manage).** (1) **Unusual or heavy bleeding** between periods, after intercourse, or soaking through a pad/tampon every hour for more than two consecutive hours — rule out fibroids, polyps, endometrial pathology. (2) **Suspected pregnancy** with cyclical-feeling symptoms — home test, then a beta-hCG draw if test-line is faint. (3) **Persistent severe pelvic pain** outside the menstrual window or pain so severe it stops walking, vomits, or fails ibuprofen 600 mg — rule out endometriosis, ovarian torsion, ovarian cyst rupture, pelvic inflammatory disease. (4) **Suicidal ideation in the luteal phase** (the classical PMDD "I want to die for one week of the month" pattern) — this is real, treatable, and a known PMDD presentation; the home protocol layers help but a crisis line (988 in US, Samaritans UK 116 123, Lifeline AU 13 11 14, iCall India 9152987821) is the right first call in the moment. (5) **New cyclical neurological symptoms** — sudden visual loss, weakness on one side, severe new headache "worst of life" — rule out cerebrovascular event. (6) **Galactorrhoea** (nipple discharge unrelated to breastfeeding) with cycle irregularity — rule out prolactinoma.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What PMS actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** PMS is not "in your head" — it is a real, cyclical, hormone-and-neurotransmitter shift in the 1–2 weeks before bleeding. PMDD is its most severe form (about 3-8% of menstruating women). The body is not broken; it is responding too strongly to normal hormonal change. *(Yonkers 2008 international review; Halbreich 2003 PMDD epidemiology)*

**Quick start.** Track this cycle and the next on the **Daily Record of Severity (DRSP)** — paper or in Clue / Flo / Oura. If symptoms cluster in the 7–14 days before bleeding and resolve within 4 days of bleeding starting, you have a positive prospective PMS/PMDD pattern. This is the only diagnostic that matters; everything else is downstream of cycle-mapped self-tracking.

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: magnesium glycinate 300 mg with dinner, calcium 600 mg AM + 600 mg PM with food, vitamin B6 (P-5-P form) 50 mg AM, vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 100 µg AM. This week: start a 2-cycle DRSP log, cut alcohol to ≤4 drinks/week, reduce caffeine to one coffee before 11:00. This month: chasteberry (Vitex agnus-castus) 20–40 mg standardised extract once daily AM for the next 3 full cycles before judging effect. *(Schellenberg 2001 BMJ Vitex RCT; Thys-Jacobs 1998 calcium 1000 mg RCT; Wyatt 1999 B6 meta-analysis)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

PMS and its severe form PMDD are conventionally treated with cyclical luteal-phase SSRIs (sertraline 50-100 mg, fluoxetine 20 mg from day 14), continuous OCPs (skipping placebo to suppress cycling), spironolactone for fluid, GnRH agonists with add-back for refractory cases, and in extreme PMDD with pharmacological failure, hysterectomy + BSO. The integrative reframing: PMS is a **four-node network** — (i) **luteal-phase progesterone-allopregnanolone-GABA sensitivity** (GABA-A receptors respond paradoxically to rising allopregnanolone, producing irritability instead of calm); (ii) **oestrogen-dominance + impaired hepatic clearance** (sluggish phase-2 detox, gut β-glucuronidase reabsorption, NAFLD aromatase push oestradiol up relative to progesterone); (iii) **HPA-axis stress amplification + sleep loss + serotonin trough** (stress + sleep restriction worsen every luteal item; serotonin synthesis dips luteally); (iv) **micronutrient floor failure + cyclical inflammation** (silent Mg, Ca, B6, vitamin D, omega-3, iron deficiencies independently amplify symptoms). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that simultaneously calms the GABA-allopregnanolone sensitivity, drains oestrogen-dominance, lowers HPA amplification, and refills the cofactor floor produces a real DRSP-mapped reduction for most women — without SSRIs or OCPs — and §10.7.3 documents the clinic-route honestly.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers explain almost every PMS symptom. The brain's calming system overreacts to the luteal-phase hormone shift, the liver and gut do not clear oestrogen fast enough, stress amplifies it all, and four key nutrients sit silently low. Each of the four has a targeted home layer. *(Halbreich 2003; Bäckström 2003 allopregnanolone-GABA; Lustyk 2009 CBT for PMS meta-analysis)*

### 2.2 Luteal GABA-allopregnanolone

> **For you, in plain words.** In the second half of your cycle, progesterone rises and gets converted into a calming brain chemical called allopregnanolone. For some women, the brain's calming receptors paradoxically over-react and produce irritability and low mood instead of calm. Magnesium, taurine, L-theanine, slow breathing, and CBT calm the system. *(Bäckström 2003; Lustyk 2009; Carter 2018 magnesium-GABA review)*

**Quick start.** Magnesium glycinate 300 mg PM + L-theanine 200 mg as needed during luteal-week irritability spikes + Bhramari breathing 5 minutes 2× daily across the luteal week + slow paced breathing 5–6 breaths/min for 10 min before bed.

#### Open the science

Allopregnanolone (3α-hydroxy-5α-pregnan-20-one), a neurosteroid derivative of progesterone, rises across the luteal phase and modulates GABA-A receptors (specifically α2β3γ2 subunits in the limbic system). In a subset of women — likely those with specific GABA-A subunit polymorphisms — receptor sensitivity inverts at high allopregnanolone concentrations, producing dysphoria, irritability and anhedonia instead of the expected anxiolytic effect (Bäckström 2003; Andréen 2009). The mid-luteal-week serotonin trough compounds this. Key actors: allopregnanolone, GABA-A α2β3γ2, 5-HT 1A/2A receptors, BDNF, prefrontal-limbic connectivity, monoamine oxidase A. Targeted by **magnesium glycinate (NMDA-antagonist + GABA-A positive modulator), taurine (GABA-A modulation), L-theanine (α-wave + GABA), saffron (Crocus sativus — Crocin 5-HT modulation, Agha-Hosseini 2008 Iranian RCT), Bhramari + slow paced breathing (vagal-GABA), CBT for PMS (Lustyk 2009 meta — A-grade), DBT-distress-tolerance skills for PMDD irritability, MBSR (Lustyk 2009)**. **Synthesis.** This is the symptom-load node — calming it brings rapid weekly relief; it does not fix the underlying hormone metabolism (that is §2.3).

### 2.3 Oestrogen dominance + liver-gut clearance

> **For you, in plain words.** Your liver and gut have to clear the previous cycle's oestrogen each month. When phase-2 liver detox is sluggish, when gut bacteria reactivate already-cleared oestrogen, and when NAFLD or belly fat add aromatase activity, oestrogen ends up too high relative to progesterone — driving bloating, breast pain, headache, irritability. Cruciferous vegetables, DIM, milk thistle, fibre, and the right gut bacteria fix this. *(Fowke 2000 cruciferous-2OH-E1 cohort; Bradlow 1999 DIM)*

**Quick start.** 200 g cooked cruciferous vegetables daily (broccoli, cabbage, kale, Brussels sprouts, bok choy, cauliflower — rotate), DIM (diindolylmethane) 100–200 mg AM with food, calcium-D-glucarate 500 mg AM, ground flaxseed 30 g daily, milk thistle silymarin 300 mg BID.

#### Open the science

Oestradiol (E2) is metabolised in the liver via phase-1 CYP1A1/1A2/1B1 to 2-hydroxyestrone (2-OH-E1 — protective), 4-hydroxyestrone (4-OH-E1 — DNA-damaging) or 16α-hydroxyestrone (16α-OH-E1 — pro-proliferative). Phase-2 conjugation via COMT (catechol-O-methyltransferase) and UGT glucuronidation prepares oestrogens for biliary and renal excretion. Gut β-glucuronidase (produced by certain Clostridia, Bacteroides species — the "oestrobolome") deconjugates oestrogens back into the enterohepatic circulation, raising systemic oestrogen burden. Visceral adiposity and NAFLD/MASLD raise aromatase (CYP19A1) activity, converting testosterone and androstenedione to oestradiol. Key actors: CYP1A1/1A2/1B1, COMT, UGT1A1, β-glucuronidase, aromatase. Targeted by **cruciferous vegetables → I3C → DIM (Bradlow 1999), calcium-D-glucarate (β-glucuronidase inhibition), milk thistle (phase-2 support), ground flaxseed (lignan SHBG modulation), 30+ g/day fibre (oestrogen excretion), Triphala (gut-liver clearance), Bupleurum-containing TCM formulas (Xiao Yao San)**. **Synthesis.** Without oestrogen-clearance work, the calming layer in §2.2 only blunts symptoms; this fixes the underlying ratio.

### 2.4 HPA amplification + serotonin trough

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress amplifies every luteal symptom — and the luteal phase itself drops serotonin synthesis, contributing to the mood crash. Ashwagandha, Holy Basil, CBT, MBSR, exercise, and luteal-week sleep protection fix this layer faster than any pill. *(Choudhary 2017 Ashwagandha cortisol RCT; Lustyk 2009 CBT meta; Vaghela 2019 exercise PMS meta)*

**Quick start.** Ashwagandha KSM-66 600 mg PM + Holy Basil (Tulsi) tea 2 cups + CBT for PMS workbook or app (Clue Plus, Moodfit) + lights out 22:30 in the luteal week + Vaghela-style aerobic exercise 30 min × 4–5/week.

#### Open the science

Cortisol amplifies premenstrual symptoms via glucocorticoid-receptor modulation of GnRH, allopregnanolone-GABA sensitivity, and serotonin reuptake. Sleep restriction in the luteal week worsens every prospective DRSP item. Serotonin synthesis dips luteally (Halbreich 2003); tryptophan-to-serotonin conversion requires vitamin B6 (P-5-P), magnesium, iron, vitamin C. Key actors: HPA axis (CRH-ACTH-cortisol), 5-HT1A/2A receptors, serotonin transporter, BDNF, monoamine oxidase A. Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66 (cortisol -23-28% across Choudhary 2017, Lopresti 2019, Salve 2019 RCTs), Holy Basil, Rhodiola, Bacopa, Saffron, 5-HTP cautious (50-100 mg PM only — not with SSRIs), magnesium, B6, vitamin D, omega-3 EPA, CBT for PMS (Lustyk 2009 meta — A-grade), DBT-distress-tolerance for PMDD, MBSR (Lustyk 2009 — A-grade), aerobic exercise (Vaghela 2019 meta — A-grade), CBT-i for luteal-phase insomnia (Sleepio app)**. **Synthesis.** This is where the CBT/MBSR work moves the prospective DRSP needle as hard as any herb.

### 2.5 Cofactor floor + cyclical inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** Four nutrients — magnesium, calcium, vitamin B6, vitamin D — are silently low in most women with PMS, and refilling them is the single most cost-effective layer. Omega-3 EPA blunts the cyclical inflammation. Iron only if your ferritin is genuinely low. *(Thys-Jacobs 1998 calcium 1000 mg RCT; Wyatt 1999 B6 meta-analysis; Walker 1998 magnesium-PMS RCT; Khajehei 2015 vitamin D PMS RCT)*

**Quick start.** Calcium citrate 1200 mg/day (600 mg AM + 600 mg PM, food) + magnesium glycinate 300 mg PM + vitamin B6 (P-5-P) 50 mg AM (NEVER >100 mg long-term — peripheral neuropathy risk) + vitamin D3 4000 IU + K2 AM + omega-3 (EPA 1000 + DHA 500 mg) AM. If ferritin <30 ng/mL via DTC lab: gentle iron bisglycinate 25 mg every other day with vitamin C, 1 hour from tea/coffee.

#### Open the science

Thys-Jacobs 1998 randomised n=466 women to calcium 1200 mg/day vs placebo for 3 cycles and showed a 48% reduction in DRSP score (vs 30% placebo) — A-grade evidence. Walker 1998 RCT showed magnesium 200 mg/day reduced fluid retention. Wyatt 1999 meta-analysis of 9 trials of vitamin B6 (≤100 mg/day) showed twofold improvement in PMS symptoms vs placebo. Khajehei 2015 RCT showed vitamin D 50,000 IU monthly improved PMS scores in deficient women. Omega-3 EPA (Sohrabi 2013 RCT, n=124) reduced PMS depression, nervousness, anxiety. Iron deficiency (ferritin <30) independently amplifies fatigue and mood symptoms (Soppi 2018 ferritin-mood review). Targeted by **the cofactor floor stack (§6.6)**. **Synthesis.** Refill the floor first; everything else works better on a refilled floor.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules — each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first, and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on evening primrose oil (Cochrane null), B6 above 100 mg (peripheral neuropathy risk), and 5-HTP (don't combine with SSRIs). *(EMA HMPC chasteberry monograph; AYUSH/CCRAS Ashoka monograph; Cochrane EPO 2013)*

### 3.2 The chasteberry (Vitex) cornerstone — the honest read

> **For you, in plain words.** Chasteberry (Vitex agnus-castus) is the most-studied single hormonal herb for PMS. Schellenberg 2001 in BMJ (n=170 German women, double-blind RCT) showed a 50% reduction in PMS score vs 23% on placebo over 3 cycles. The Cochrane 2017 review concluded the evidence is moderate (B-grade) but consistent. It is **strictly contraindicated in pregnancy, with hormonal contraception, with dopamine antagonists (some antipsychotics, metoclopramide), and in women with hormone-sensitive cancers**. Take it for 3 full cycles before judging. *(Schellenberg 2001 BMJ; Cerqueira 2017 Cochrane; EMA HMPC monograph 2010)*

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six healing traditions described cyclical menstrual disorders centuries before "PMS" or "PMDD" existed. We honour them — and we are honest that modern high-quality randomised trials are sparse for most classical formulas, though Xiao Yao San in TCM has accumulating Chinese RCT support. *(Charaka Chikitsa 30; Huang Di Nei Jing Su Wen 1; Avicenna's Canon Book III; Siddha Pen Noi; Tibetan Four Tantras; Boericke)*

### 4.2 Ayurveda — Yonivyapad / Ritu Vyatha

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda's framework for cyclical menstrual disorders centres on Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus — the queen of women's herbs), Ashoka (Saraca indica — uterine tonic), Lodhra (Symplocos racemosa), Yashtimadhu (licorice), Pushyanuga Choorna for menorrhagia, and Dashamoolarishta + Saraswatarishta as rasayana classical fermented decoctions. Triphala supports the gut-liver clearance of the §2.3 oestrogen-dominance layer. *(Charaka Chikitsa Sthana ch. 30 Yonivyapad; Sushruta Sharira; AYUSH Shatavari + Ashoka monographs)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 Priority order — what works at home

> **For you, in plain words.** Home tools that move the dial: cycle tracking (the foundational instrument), home heating-pad over lower abdomen for cramping, infrared sauna 2–3×/week (sweat + circulation), gentle cold-water face-immersion for mood spikes, home red-light over lower abdomen for cyclical discomfort, and HRV biofeedback for the autonomic amplification. *(Akin 2004 heating-pad RCT; Lehrer 2003 HRV biofeedback review)*

### 5.2 Cycle tracking — the foundational instrument

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-leverage tool — without prospective cycle-mapped tracking, you cannot tell PMS from baseline mood and cannot judge whether any intervention is working. Pick one and use it for at least 2 full cycles before adding any herb. *(Steiner 2003 PSST; Halbreich 2003)*

**Quick start.** Clue (free, evidence-based, EU-headquartered), Flo (broader feature set), Natural Cycles (FDA De Novo cleared as contraception in some regions — regulatory-path context, not endorsement), or Oura ring + Oura cycle insights (passive temperature + HRV). Log: bleeding days, DRSP 11 items, sleep, mood, energy, cravings, breast tenderness, headache.

### 5.3 Heating-pad + abdominal warmth

> **For you, in plain words.** A simple heating pad over the lower abdomen reduces dysmenorrhoea pain comparably to ibuprofen in head-to-head trials. The cheapest, most under-used home tool. *(Akin 2004 RCT; Akin 2001 heating-pad-vs-ibuprofen)*

**Quick start.** Sunbeam Xpress-Heat, Pure Enrichment PureRelief XL, or simple hot-water bottle — 40°C, 30 minutes, lower abdomen, days 1–2 of menses or as needed for cramping.

### 5.4 Infrared sauna + sweat clearance

> **For you, in plain words.** Two to three home infrared sauna sessions a week (40 min, 60–65°C) support circulation, mood, and the §2.3 oestrogen-clearance system through sweat. Avoid in the heaviest bleeding days; otherwise good through the cycle. *(Beever 2009 IR-sauna review; Hannuksela 2001 sauna physiology)*

**Quick start.** Sunlighten Solo home capsule, JNH Lifestyles Joyous 2-person, Clearlight Sanctuary — 40 min, 3×/week, hydrate well.

### 5.5 Home red-light over lower abdomen

> **For you, in plain words.** A small home red-light panel (660 nm + 850 nm) over the lower abdomen for 10–15 minutes during the luteal week may reduce cyclical discomfort through circulation + mitochondrial support. Evidence is early but plausible and home-safe. *(Hamblin 2017 PBM review)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red Light MitoMIN, Hooga HG500, Joov Solo — 660 + 850 nm, 10–15 min, lower abdomen, 6 inches distance, luteal-week daily.

### 5.6 HRV + Lief/HeartMath biofeedback

> **For you, in plain words.** Heart-rate-variability biofeedback (slow-paced breathing at 5–6 breaths/min coached by a wearable) is B-grade for stress and a known PMS amplifier. Ten minutes daily for the full luteal week. *(Lehrer 2003 HRV biofeedback meta; Goessl 2017 HRV-anxiety meta)*

**Quick start.** HeartMath Inner Balance ($99 sensor + app), Lief patch, Polar H10 + Elite HRV app, Oura ring + Welltory.

### 5.7 Acupressure + self-shiatsu

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-applied acupressure on SP6 (above the inner ankle), LI4 (web of thumb), and CV4-CV6 (lower abdomen midline) for 3–5 minutes each is a documented Chinese-medicine self-care layer for cyclical discomfort. *(Chen 2013 Cochrane on acupressure for dysmenorrhoea; Wong 2010)*

> **For you, in plain words.** Endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) directly worsen oestrogen-dominance — clean the inputs and the §2.3 layer improves. *(Hauser 2015 EDC review; Vandenberg 2009 BPA)*

- **Occupational**: hair dyes, salon chemicals, pesticide exposure, organic solvents — minimise and use PPE.
- **Mould & VOCs**: damp-test home; remediate; HEPA filter in bedroom.
