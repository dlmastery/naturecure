## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What AFib actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Atrial fibrillation is when the two small top chambers of the heart shake like jelly instead of squeezing. The pulse goes irregular, the heart works harder, blood pools in a little corner of the atrium, and clots can form. *(Hindricks 2020 ESC guidelines mechanism overview; Camm 2010 ESC AFib)*

### 1.2 The five drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Buy a consumer single-lead ECG this week (KardiaMobile, Apple Watch SE/Series 4+, or Withings ScanWatch). Cut alcohol — ideally to zero, certainly under one drink/day. Lose 10% bodyweight if you carry extra around the middle. Sleep 7-8 hours and screen yourself for sleep apnea with STOP-BANG. Walk 30-45 minutes a day at conversational pace; do not train for marathons. *(LEGACY Pathak 2014; Voskoboinik 2020 NEJM; Holmqvist 2015; Andersen 2013 endurance-overtraining)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

The mainstream model is "you have AFib, here's a thinner and a rhythm pill". A more honest reframe: most non-valvular AFib is the endpoint of **five overlapping drivers** — (i) **atrial substrate disease** (the left atrium stretches and scars from years of pressure, alcohol, sleep apnea, obesity); (ii) **autonomic imbalance** (sympathetic over-drive, low HRV, poor baroreflex; some AFib is vagally driven and fires at night, some is sympathetically driven and fires on exertion); (iii) **inflammation + oxidative stress** that re-wires atrial electrical signalling; (iv) **electrolyte and mineral starvation** — chronically low magnesium and potassium make atrial cells trigger-happy; (v) **structural triggers** — sleep apnea, hyperthyroidism, mitral regurgitation, post-op cardiac surgery. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **weight loss + alcohol cessation + OSA treatment + Mediterranean diet + moderate (not extreme) exercise + tobacco-zero** (the LEGACY/Voskoboinik/Holmqvist frontline) with a tight cofactor floor (magnesium taurate, potassium, vitamin D, omega-3 EPA, vitamin K2 MK7), targeted botanicals (Arjuna, hawthorn, Dan Shen, taurine), self-administered ECG, and trauma-informed nervous-system work hits all five drivers in parallel.

### 1.4 What this dossier is NOT

> **For you, in plain words.** This is not a substitute for a stroke-prevention decision. If your CHA2DS2-VASc score is high, the trial evidence for anticoagulation is overwhelming — apixaban/Eliquis cut strokes ~21% versus warfarin and bleeding ~31% in ARISTOTLE (n=18,201). That conversation belongs in §10.7.3. *(Granger 2011 NEJM ARISTOTLE)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five drivers — a stretched and scarred left atrium, an over-revved nervous system, inflammation, mineral starvation, and structural triggers. Naming them lets every food, breath, and supplement aim at the right one. *(Heijman 2018 AFib pathophysiology review; Nattel 2017 Lancet AFib)*

### 2.2 Stretched scarred atrium

> **For you, in plain words.** Years of high blood pressure, extra weight, sleep apnea and heavy alcohol stretch the left atrium until it fibrose — like leather instead of fresh muscle. Reverse the stretching by removing the cause and the AFib often recedes. *(LEGACY Pathak 2014; Abed 2013 obesity-AFib)*

#### Open the science

Sustained left-atrial pressure from hypertension, OSA-driven negative intrathoracic pressure, mitral valve disease and obesity stretches the atrial myocyte sarcomere. Mechano-transduction activates TGF-β1 → CTGF → atrial fibrosis. Fibrosis disrupts the normal anisotropic conduction of the atrial wall, creating re-entry circuits that sustain AFib. The LEGACY trial (Pathak 2014, n=355 obese AFib patients, Adelaide Australia) showed that **≥10% bodyweight loss reduced AFib recurrence by ~50% versus controls**, and reversed left-atrial volume on imaging — substrate disease can be REVERSED. The REVERSE-AF and ARREST-AF studies (Pathak 2015) extended this to a multi-risk-factor intervention. Targeted by **weight loss**, **OSA-CPAP** (Holmqvist 2015), **BP control**, **alcohol cessation** (Voskoboinik 2020), and **MAGNETIC** Mediterranean-diet adherence. **Synthesis.** Atrial substrate disease is reversible — the protocol's most important lever.

### 2.3 Over-revved nervous system

> **For you, in plain words.** Some people's AFib fires when they relax (a vagal pattern — often at night, after meals); others' fires when they're stressed (a sympathetic pattern — exercise, anxiety). Both respond to slow breathing, daily walking, and lower alcohol. *(Coumel 1996 autonomic AFib; Lakkireddy 2013 yoga RCT)*

#### Open the science

The autonomic nervous system modulates atrial electrophysiology profoundly. Vagally-mediated AFib (Coumel pattern) fires in young athletic men, at night, post-prandially, or after alcohol — vagal acetylcholine release shortens atrial refractory period via I-KAch channels, creating substrate for re-entry. Sympathetically-mediated AFib fires on exertion, stress, hyperthyroidism, or post-coffee — β-adrenergic activation raises I-CaL, accelerates spontaneous depolarisation, and shortens cycle length. Most chronic AFib is mixed. Targeted by **slow paced breathing 5-6 bpm** (Lakkireddy 2013 yoga, n=49, Kansas; AFib symptom burden halved), **HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath, Inner Balance, Lief), **MBSR** (Lakkireddy 2013), **regular Zone-2 cardio** (rebuilds baroreflex), **stopping alcohol** (Voskoboinik 2020), and **stopping high-dose stimulants**. **Synthesis.** Autonomic re-balancing is the daily lever for paroxysmal AFib.

### 2.4 Inflammation and oxidative stress

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic low-grade inflammation makes the atrium electrically unstable. The same kitchen and lifestyle that protects against heart attacks (Mediterranean diet, fish, olive oil, walking) calms AFib too. *(PREDIMED 2018 secondary cardio; Mariani 2014 omega-3 AFib mixed)*

#### Open the science

Atrial myocytes from AFib patients show elevated NF-κB, TNF-α, IL-6, and CRP versus sinus-rhythm controls. NLRP3 inflammasome activation has been mapped specifically to AFib substrate (Yao 2018 China). Oxidative stress (NOX2/NOX4-driven superoxide) uncouples atrial myocyte calcium handling and shortens refractory period. Targeted by **Mediterranean diet** (PREDIMED 2018 Spain — primary prevention of CV events; secondary AFib signal weaker but plausible), **omega-3 EPA** (mixed — see §3 honest grading; REDUCE-IT Bhatt 2019 showed CV benefit with icosapent ethyl but signal for incident AFib was actually slightly INCREASED — present honestly), **curcumin** (mechanistic; small AFib post-op signal), **olive polyphenols**, and **Pycnogenol**. **Synthesis.** Anti-inflammation is one layer of many — keep it as cofactor, not as cornerstone.

### 2.5 Electrolyte starvation

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronically low magnesium and potassium make the atrium fire off-rhythm. Cheap, free-of-side-effects, and one of the most under-recognised drivers. *(Schwalfenberg 2018 Mg review; Khan 2013 Mg post-op AFib meta-analysis)*

#### Open the science

Magnesium is a cofactor for Na/K-ATPase and for sodium-potassium pump function. Low intracellular Mg shortens atrial refractory period and raises ectopic activity. Cardiac surgical units routinely IV-load magnesium pre-operatively to reduce post-op AFib (POAF) — Khan 2013 meta-analysis confirms ~20% reduction. Chronic Mg deficiency is endemic in Western diets (most adults consume <60% of RDA). Potassium follows the same logic — low-K serum is arrhythmogenic. Targeted by **magnesium taurate or glycinate** (300-600 mg/d elemental, B-grade for AFib if Mg-deficient; Schwalfenberg 2018), **dietary potassium** (bananas, leafy greens, beans, baked potato — 3,500-4,700 mg/d), **electrolyte-rich whole-food diet**, and **stopping loop diuretics** (which deplete both — clinic-route conversation only). **Synthesis.** Test once via DTC labs; if Mg-RBC <5 mg/dL or potassium low-normal, fix it.

### 2.6 Structural triggers

> **For you, in plain words.** Sleep apnea, an overactive thyroid, valve problems, and recent heart surgery are the four most-missed triggers. Self-screen for OSA tonight with STOP-BANG; ask for a DTC TSH if your AFib is new. *(Holmqvist 2015 OSA-CPAP; Cappato 2018 hyperthyroid-AFib)*

#### Open the science

OSA generates massive negative intrathoracic pressure swings (down to -80 cmH2O during obstruction), stretching the atrium and producing surges of sympathetic outflow on arousal. Untreated OSA roughly doubles AFib incidence and slashes ablation success rates. CPAP reverses much of this. Hyperthyroidism (free T4 high, TSH suppressed) is found in ~5-10% of new AFib — and the AFib often resolves once euthyroid. Mitral regurgitation stretches the atrium directly. Post-operative AFib (POAF) after CABG or valve surgery is common (~30-40%) and often self-limiting. Targeted by **STOP-BANG self-screen → home sleep study → CPAP**, **DTC TSH + free T4 testing**, and **echocardiogram referral if structural disease suspected**. **Synthesis.** Screen these four — fixing the trigger often fixes the AFib.

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

### 3.2 The honest omega-3 caveat (load-bearing — Rule 4)

> **For you, in plain words.** Omega-3 is heart-supportive — REDUCE-IT (Bhatt 2019 NEJM, n=8,179, icosapent ethyl 4g/d) cut major CV events by 25%. But the *same* trial showed a small INCREASE in incident AFib in the EPA arm (3.1% vs 2.1%). STRENGTH (Nicholls 2020, EPA+DHA carboxylic acid) was overall null. So we keep omega-3 EPA (1-2 g/d from cold-water fish or molecularly distilled supplement) in the protocol for general cardio-protection — but NOT as an AFib-specific therapy, and NOT at REDUCE-IT-level 4g/d in someone whose AFib is paroxysmal-vagal. *(Bhatt 2019 REDUCE-IT; Nicholls 2020 STRENGTH; Mariani 2014 omega-3 AFib meta)*

#### Open the science

Marine omega-3 EPA modulates inflammation, lowers triglycerides, stabilises membrane phospholipids and was hypothesised to be anti-arrhythmic. Mariani 2014 meta-analysis showed mixed signals for AFib recurrence (some primary-prevention benefit, no secondary-prevention). The REDUCE-IT trial's AFib signal-up is now well-recognised at high-purity EPA doses ≥4 g/d. The protocol therefore frames omega-3 as **cardio-cofactor, not AFib-monotherapy** — 1-2 g/d EPA+DHA from oily fish or a clean supplement (Nordic Naturals Ultimate Omega, Carlson Elite, Bare Biology UK).

### 6.2 What to avoid (with molecular reason)

- **Alcohol** — single largest modifiable AFib trigger ("holiday heart"). Voskoboinik 2020 NEJM RCT: abstinence reduced recurrence 53% vs 73% in moderate drinkers. **Target: zero, certainly under 1 drink/day, never binge.**
- **Energy drinks + high-dose caffeine** — sympathetic surge, ectopic-beat trigger.
- **Ultra-processed foods + industrial seed oils** — omega-6/3 imbalance, inflammation.
- **Refined sugar + high-fructose corn syrup** — insulin resistance, visceral fat.
- **Trans fats** — endothelial dysfunction.
- **Excess sodium >2,300 mg/d** — raises BP.

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

Sprouting (8-10h soak + 8-12h germinate), fermentation, slow-cooking, bone broth, ghee tempering. **Do not deep-fry.** Pressure cook beans to reduce lectins.

### 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

Atrial fibrillation is when the two top chambers of your heart shake instead of squeezing — your pulse goes irregular, sometimes fast, sometimes scary, and your heart works harder than it should. The mainstream model is a blood thinner and a rhythm pill. There is a place for both — especially the blood thinner if your stroke risk is high — but the conversation that rarely gets started in a 12-minute visit is: most non-valvular AFib has five fixable drivers, and the highest-evidence trial we have on AFib reversal (LEGACY, Adelaide 2014) is about losing 10% of your bodyweight. Add to that: cut alcohol to zero (Voskoboinik 2020 NEJM showed abstinence halved recurrence), treat the sleep apnea you may not know you have, walk daily but stop training for marathons, and feed the heart the minerals it actually needs (magnesium, potassium, omega-3, vitamin D + K2). This is months-long work — not an overnight reversal. Some episodes will still come; you will track them on a $89 home ECG and learn your own triggers. **This is an independent, over-the-counter, home-based protocol. No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery.** If you are already on a blood thinner or a rhythm drug, §10.1 shows you how this stack interacts. If you keep a cardiologist in the loop, that is your call. The protocol does not require it. The protocol does require honesty — including about ginkgo, omega-3 high-dose, and hawthorn mortality nulls.
