## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What liver disease actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** "Liver disease" usually means one of two things — alcohol-related fatty liver (ALD) or non-alcohol-related metabolic fatty liver (MASLD / MASH, formerly NAFLD / NASH). Both start as fat accumulation in liver cells, both can progress to fibrosis and cirrhosis if ignored, and both are reversible at the fat-only and early-fibrosis stages. *(Younossi 2018 MASLD global epidemiology; WHO alcohol 2022)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Reduce alcohol to ≤7 drinks/week (women) or ≤14 (men), with at least 2 alcohol-free days. Lose 7-10% of body weight if BMI >25 (Vilar-Gomez 2015 — MASH resolves in ~90% at this target). Cut sugary drinks and ultra-processed food. Walk 30 min after dinner. Take 1.5 g silymarin (milk thistle) twice a day and consider TUDCA 250 mg/day. Order a DTC liver panel (ALT, AST, GGT, ALP, bilirubin, albumin) at baseline and week 12. These five moves cover the highest-evidence layers. *(Vilar-Gomez 2015; WHO alcohol 2022; Saller 2007 silymarin meta)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

The mainstream model is "your enzymes were a bit high, watch them". A more honest reframe: liver dysfunction is the endpoint of **four overlapping drivers** — (i) **alcohol-driven oxidative damage** (acetaldehyde + CYP2E1 reactive oxygen species + glutathione depletion); (ii) **metabolic dysfunction** (insulin resistance + visceral adipose → free fatty acids flooding the liver → de novo lipogenesis → steatosis → MASH); (iii) **gut-liver axis dysbiosis + LPS endotoxaemia** (leaky gut → bacterial fragments cross to liver → Kupffer cell inflammation); (iv) **failure of hepatic glutathione recycling and Phase 1/2 detox capacity** when overwhelmed. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs **alcohol reduction + weight loss + Mediterranean / low-fructose / high-coffee diet + sleep + exercise** (the highest-grade frontline) with a tight cofactor floor (B-complex, methylated folate, choline, betaine, zinc, magnesium, vitamin E for MASH only), targeted botanicals (silymarin, TUDCA, NAC, berberine, turmeric, schisandra), self-measured FIB-4 and DTC liver panel hits all four drivers in parallel — and tells the truth that for advanced fibrosis (stage 3+) or cirrhosis, the home stack ADDS to clinic-route care, never replaces it.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers — alcohol toxicity, metabolic fatty-liver coupling, gut-leakiness, and detox-system overwhelm. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Younossi 2018; Friedman 2018 MASH review)*

### 2.2 Alcohol-driven oxidative damage

> **For you, in plain words.** Alcohol turns into acetaldehyde — a direct cell poison — and burns through the liver's main antioxidant (glutathione). The single highest-yield intervention is drinking less or stopping. *(Lieber 2004 alcohol-liver review; Cederbaum 2012 CYP2E1)*

#### Open the science

Ethanol metabolism: alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) → acetaldehyde (toxic) → aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH) → acetate. At higher chronic intakes, CYP2E1 is induced — it generates reactive oxygen species (ROS) that deplete glutathione, oxidise lipids and damage mitochondria. Asian-flushing phenotype (ALDH2*2 variant — ~36% of East Asians) accumulates acetaldehyde and has higher cancer risk. Targeted by **alcohol reduction** (the only A-grade intervention), **NAC** (glutathione precursor — Smilkstein 1988 acetaminophen IV), **silymarin** (Saller 2007 Cochrane mixed), **selenium**, **zinc**, **thiamine (B1)** — mandatory in any heavy drinker to prevent Wernicke-Korsakoff, **methylated folate + B12 + B6**, and **glutathione precursors**. **Synthesis.** The herb stack is real but a small fraction of what dropping alcohol does.

### 2.3 Metabolic fatty-liver coupling

> **For you, in plain words.** Fat around the organs + insulin resistance + sugary drinks + lack of exercise → fat builds up in the liver (MASLD), then it inflames (MASH), then it scars (fibrosis). Reversible at the early stages with weight loss alone. *(Younossi 2018; Vilar-Gomez 2015 weight-loss MASH RCT)*

#### Open the science

MASLD (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease, formerly NAFLD) is the hepatic manifestation of metabolic syndrome. Mechanism: visceral adipose → free fatty acid flux to liver → hepatic insulin resistance → de novo lipogenesis (driven by fructose) → triglyceride accumulation → steatosis. Progression to MASH involves lipotoxicity, ER stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, inflammasome activation, and stellate-cell-driven fibrosis. Targeted by **weight loss 7-10%** (Vilar-Gomez 2015 — MASH resolves in 90% at 10% loss; fibrosis regresses in 45%), **fructose reduction** (Schwarz 2017 UCSF kids isocaloric), **Mediterranean diet** (Cochrane Akinkugbe 2018), **coffee 3-4 cups/day** (Saab 2014 meta — protective), **exercise — both aerobic and resistance** (Hashida 2017 meta), **vitamin E 800 IU/day for non-diabetic biopsy-proven MASH** (PIVENS Sanyal 2010 — A-grade for that specific use), **GLP-1 agonists** (SEMA-NASH 2021 — Rx, §10.7.3), and **resmetirom** (MAESTRO-NASH 2024 — Rx, §10.7.3).

### 2.4 Gut-liver axis + LPS endotoxaemia

> **For you, in plain words.** A leaky gut leaks bacterial fragments into the bloodstream that travel to the liver and stoke inflammation. Targeted by fermented foods, polyphenol-rich plants, soluble fibre, and high-quality probiotics. *(Aron-Wisnewsky 2020 gut-liver review; Loguercio 2014 VSL#3 Italy MASH RCT)*

#### Open the science

The portal vein delivers gut-derived material directly to the liver. In dysbiosis with increased intestinal permeability ("leaky gut"), lipopolysaccharide (LPS) from Gram-negative bacteria reaches Kupffer cells via TLR4, driving chronic low-grade inflammation, hepatic insulin resistance, and progression of fatty liver to MASH. Alcohol acutely increases gut permeability. Targeted by **fermented foods** (kanji, kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi), **soluble fibre** (oats, psyllium), **polyphenols** (berries, dark chocolate, green tea, pomegranate), **probiotics** (VSL#3 / Visbiome — Loguercio 2014 Italy; multi-strain Lactobacillus), **L-glutamine 5 g/day**, **zinc carnosine**, and **avoidance of NSAIDs and unnecessary antibiotics**.

### 2.5 Detox-system overwhelm

> **For you, in plain words.** The liver runs a two-phase detox factory (Phase 1 activates and Phase 2 conjugates). Either phase can be overwhelmed by chronic alcohol, paracetamol, polypharmacy, mould, plastics, or just nutrient deficiency. Targeted by glutathione precursors (NAC), cruciferous veg, and a tight micronutrient floor. *(Liska 1998 detox review; Higdon 2007 cruciferous)*

#### Open the science

Phase 1 = CYP450 enzymes (CYP3A4, CYP2E1 alcohol-induced, CYP1A2 coffee/cruciferous-induced) make compounds more polar (and often more reactive). Phase 2 = conjugation with glutathione (GSH), glucuronide, sulphate, glycine, acetyl, methyl groups — neutralises the activated intermediate. Bottlenecks: low GSH (alcohol, paracetamol, malnutrition), low methylation (low folate, low B12, low choline, MTHFR variants), low sulphation (low cysteine, low molybdenum). Targeted by **NAC**, **glycine 5-10 g/day**, **methylated folate + B12 + B6**, **choline 425-550 mg/day**, **betaine (trimethylglycine, TMG) 1-3 g/day**, **selenium**, **molybdenum 75-150 µg/day**, **glutamine**, **broccoli sprouts + sulforaphane**, **green tea EGCG**, **R-alpha-lipoic acid**.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve OTC molecules with non-US monograph or named-trial backing. Honest mixed-evidence notes on silymarin (Cochrane Saller 2007 mixed), curcumin for MASH (small Indian trials positive, larger meta mixed), and resveratrol (mostly null for liver). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; Cochrane Saller 2007)*

### 3.2 Silymarin — the honest Cochrane caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Milk thistle (silymarin / silybin) is the most-studied liver herb. Cochrane Saller 2007 found it generally well tolerated but evidence for hard liver-disease outcomes was mixed; specific signal in early alcoholic liver disease and as adjunct in chronic hepatitis. We keep it as one layer (not the cornerstone) and frame as "8-12 week try, re-measure liver enzymes; continue only if ALT/GGT drift down". *(Cochrane Saller 2007; Loguercio 2012 Italy MASH RCT n=138)*

**The deeper layer.** Silymarin is a complex of flavonolignans (silybin A/B, isosilybin, silydianin, silychristin) from *Silybum marianum* seeds. Mechanism: membrane stabilisation, scavenges free radicals, GSH preservation, inhibits hepatic stellate cell activation. Loguercio 2012 (n=138, Italian MASH trial) found silymarin + vitamin E + phosphatidylcholine reduced ALT and fibrosis markers. Saller 2007 Cochrane summary: well tolerated, signal in alcoholic liver disease and as hepatitis adjunct, no firm conclusion on hard endpoints. **Grade B** in alcoholic liver and as MASH adjunct; **Grade C** for primary hard-endpoint outcomes.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Home options are limited compared to clinic FibroScan — the highest-yield "biophysical" levers for liver are weight loss, exercise, alcohol reduction, and sauna. DTC liver enzyme panels are your tracking instrument. CGM is useful for the metabolic-liver coupling sub-type. *(Vilar-Gomez 2015; Laukkanen 2018; McPherson 2017 FIB-4)*

### 5.2 DTC liver enzyme panels

> **For you, in plain words.** Order a full liver panel from LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, Thriva (UK), Cerascreen (EU), True Test India, Walk-In Lab, InsideTracker, Function Health — ALT, AST, GGT, ALP, total + direct bilirubin, albumin, total protein. $50-100/panel. Track at baseline / week 12 / month 6 / month 12. *(McPherson 2017 FIB-4)*

### 5.3 FIB-4 calculator — your home fibrosis estimator

> **For you, in plain words.** FIB-4 is a simple calculator using age, AST, ALT and platelet count. Score <1.3 = low fibrosis risk; 1.3-2.67 = indeterminate; >2.67 = high risk — pursue FibroScan or clinic workup. Free FIB-4 calculators online (Hepatitis C Trust UK, MDCalc). *(McPherson 2017)*

### 5.4 Home elastography — FibroScan referral

> **For you, in plain words.** True at-home elastography devices do not exist yet — FibroScan and MRI elastography are clinic-route. But many primary-care clinics in UK/EU/Australia/India now offer self-referral FibroScan via Lofta-style services or direct hepatology booking. Liver stiffness <7 kPa low; 7-9 kPa indeterminate; >12 kPa significant. *(McPherson 2017; EASL Eslam 2020)*

### 5.5 Home CGM for metabolic-liver coupling

> **For you, in plain words.** A 14-day CGM (Stelo, Lingo, Libre, Dexcom) shows you which foods spike your glucose — useful for MASLD/MASH because metabolic dysfunction drives liver fat. Once-per-quarter is plenty. *(Hall 2018 Stanford CGM)*

### 5.6 Home weight + waist + body composition

> **For you, in plain words.** Weekly weigh-in (same time, same conditions), monthly waist measurement, optional home body-composition scale (Withings Body Cardio, Tanita) — track the 7-10% loss target. *(Vilar-Gomez 2015)*

### 5.7 Wearables — sleep, HRV, RHR

> **For you, in plain words.** Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch, Garmin, Polar — track sleep architecture (poor sleep drives MASLD), HRV (drinking trashes HRV) and resting heart rate (RHR rises after alcohol). *(Cappuccio 2011 sleep-MASLD; Plotnikoff 2017 alcohol HRV)*

### 5.8 Sauna — Finnish Laukkanen cohort

> **For you, in plain words.** Regular sauna 4-7×/week associated with lower fatty-liver progression in Finnish cohort work; pair with cold splash for vagal tone. 20 min at 50-70°C. *(Laukkanen 2018; Beever 2010 review)*

**Practical.** Sunlighten Solo, HigherDOSE infrared blanket, JNH Lifestyles, Therasage. **DO NOT use** sauna with decompensated cirrhosis, ascites, or severe portal hypertension; **DO NOT use** with active alcohol intoxication (dehydration + arrhythmia risk).

### 5.9 Cold immersion + contrast hydrotherapy

> **For you, in plain words.** 30-60 second cold splash after sauna; mechanistic vagal benefit; alcohol withdrawal (especially severe) may complicate cold tolerance. *(Tipton 2017 cold immersion review)*

### 5.10 Slow-paced breathing devices (alcohol-craving adjunct)

> **For you, in plain words.** RESPeRATE-style 6-bpm breathing or free apps reduce craving intensity for some users; HRV biofeedback (HeartMath, Lief, Polar+EliteHRV) is documented in alcohol-recovery research as a real adjunct. *(Penzlin 2017 HRV AUD; Eddie 2014 mindfulness-craving)*

### 5.11 Red-light therapy panels (optional)

> **For you, in plain words.** 660 + 810-850 nm panels (Mito Red Light, Joov, Hooga) have mostly skin and mitochondrial evidence; liver-specific evidence is mechanistic-only. Optional adjunct, not cornerstone. *(Hamblin 2018 PBM review)*

### 5.12 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Skip "liver detox" colonics, coffee enemas, parasite cleanses, and bodybuilder pre-workout cocktails. Skip excessive home sauna in cirrhosis or ascites. Skip aggressive cold-plunge in active withdrawal. Skip megadose vitamin A and iron supplements. *(Teschke 2013 HILI)*

### 13.5 Patient-facing primer

If you have just been told you have fatty liver, mildly raised liver enzymes, or that your drinking is causing damage — take a breath. The liver is one of the most regenerative organs in the body. At the fat-only and early-fibrosis stages, what you eat, drink and weigh can reverse the damage entirely.

The honest hierarchy: alcohol reduction is the single biggest lever. Weight loss to a healthy range is the second. Mediterranean-pattern eating with 3-4 cups of unsweetened coffee, fewer sugary drinks, daily walking, sleep, and stress management is the third. Everything else — milk thistle, TUDCA, NAC, berberine — is helpful but is downstream of those three.

This is not a "stop drinking by yourself with no support" protocol. If you are drinking heavily every day, cold-turkey withdrawal can be dangerous and sometimes fatal. The §10.7.3 medically-supervised detox path is a real option — naltrexone (Vivitrol monthly) and acamprosate are evidence-based Rx that match or beat AA for many people. AA and SMART Recovery and MBRP are evidence-based behavioural paths. Reframe, Sunnyside, Tempest and Hello Sunday Morning apps work for moderate drinkers wanting to reduce without stopping. You decide your path. We give you the honest evidence.

What to expect: ALT and GGT usually drop noticeably within 4-12 weeks of meaningful alcohol reduction. Fatty liver on imaging reverses in 12-24 weeks. Stage 1-2 fibrosis can regress in 6-18 months with full stack adherence. Cirrhosis is mostly not reversible — but progression can be halted, and Hepatocellular carcinoma surveillance becomes important.
