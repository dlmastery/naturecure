## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What this protocol is

> **For you, in plain words.** Insulin resistance is not "almost diabetes" — it is the upstream metabolic state that produces fatty liver, belly fat, PCOS, high triglycerides, low HDL, high blood pressure and most heart attacks years before glucose ever shows up high. You can work on it at home. *(WHO TCIM 2025-2034; Reaven 1988 Banting Lecture)*

**Quick start.** Tonight: cut all liquid sugar, all wheat / refined grain and all industrial seed oils. This week: buy a home glucose meter, weigh in fasted, measure your waist (target <94 cm men / <80 cm women), and start a 10-minute walk after every meal. These four moves are the cheapest, fastest insulin-sensitivity gains available.

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Four upstream causes drive insulin resistance — fat-stuffed muscles, fat-stuffed liver, inflamed belly fat, and a damaged gut microbiome. Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Petersen 2017; Lim 2011; Hotamisligil 2017; Cani 2007)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

#### Open the science

Insulin resistance (IR) is conventionally siloed as a "pre-diabetes risk factor" treated by a step-wise pharmaceutical cascade — metformin → GLP-1 receptor agonist → SGLT-2 inhibitor → eventually insulin — applied at thresholds that lag pathology by 5-15 years. The integrative reframe is fundamentally different: IR is the **whole-body metabolic-inflammatory state of an evolutionarily-mismatched cell membrane, mitochondria, hepatocyte and microbiome**, driven by four convergent root mechanisms — (i) skeletal-muscle AMPK suppression with GLUT4 translocation failure and ectopic lipid (intramyocellular diacylglycerol → PKC-θ → IRS-1 Ser307 inhibitory phosphorylation), (ii) hepatic de novo lipogenesis fed by fructose + refined carbohydrate plus impaired hepatic insulin clearance (~50% of systemic insulin disposal is hepatic in health), (iii) adipose tissue inflammation with leptin / adiponectin / resistin / TNF-α dysregulation and NLRP3 inflammasome activation, and (iv) gut microbiome dysbiosis with LPS endotoxaemia driving hepatic TLR4 / NF-κB and SCFA depletion. **Synthesis:** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously reactivates AMPK, drains hepatic fat, reduces visceral adipose inflammation and restores SCFA-producing microbiome addresses the upstream drivers — not the downstream glucose number. *(Petersen 2017 Cell Metab IMCL; Lim 2011 Diabetologia hepatic-fat drainage; Hotamisligil 2017 Nature inflammation-IR; Cani 2007 Diabetes LPS-IR)*

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive insulin resistance — muscle that has stopped listening to insulin, a liver clogged with fat, belly fat acting as an inflammation factory, and a gut bacteria population that has lost the calming, butyrate-making species. Naming them lets every part of the protocol aim at the right one. *(Petersen 2017; Lim 2011; Hotamisligil 2017; Cani 2007)*

### 2.2 Muscle AMPK has gone quiet

> **For you, in plain words.** Your muscles have stopped responding to insulin's "open the glucose door" signal because they are stuffed with stored fat and you haven't asked them to work hard. *(Petersen 2017 Cell Metab)*

#### Open the science

Sedentary lifestyle, chronic carbohydrate excess and mitochondrial dysfunction lower the AMP:ATP ratio chronically, deactivating AMPK and its downstream targets (ACC, PGC-1α, mitochondrial biogenesis). Intramyocellular diacylglycerol accumulates, activates PKC-θ, phosphorylates IRS-1 at Ser307 inhibitorily, blocks PI3K-Akt-AS160 signalling and traps GLUT4 in intracellular vesicles. Targeted by **Berberine HCl / dihydroberberine, *Gymnema sylvestre*, *Momordica charantia*, *Coccinia indica*, exercise (Zone 2 + resistance), creatine 5 g, time-restricted eating**. **Synthesis:** Restoring AMPK + emptying intramyocellular triglyceride is the upstream lever on muscle insulin signalling. *(Petersen 2017; Lan 2015 J Ethnopharmacol; Westcott 2012 ACSM strength)*

### 2.3 Your liver has filled with fat

> **For you, in plain words.** Fructose and refined carbohydrate get turned into fat right inside the liver. A fatty liver makes more sugar than a healthy one — even when you are fasting — and stops clearing insulin properly. *(Lim 2011 Diabetologia; Stanhope 2009 J Clin Invest fructose)*

#### Open the science

Dietary fructose and refined carbohydrate enter the liver, are converted to acetyl-CoA, then via ACC → malonyl-CoA → palmitate → triglyceride through SREBP-1c / ChREBP. Hepatic steatosis impairs insulin clearance, hyperinsulinaemia worsens lipogenesis (the loop), and inflammatory hepatocytes release CRP, fetuin-A and DAMPs. Loss of hepatic insulin clearance inflates peripheral insulin and worsens IR. Targeted by **Berberine, *Silybum marianum* (Milk Thistle / silymarin), *Picrorhiza kurroa* (Kutki), choline 425-550 mg, betaine TMG 1-2 g, omega-3 EPA-rich, fructose elimination, alcohol elimination, sprouted Siri Dhanyalu millets, time-restricted eating, Newcastle-style sustained low-calorie phase**. **Synthesis:** Without hepatic fat drainage, IR is uncorrectable from peripheral interventions alone. *(Lim 2011 Diabetologia; Loguercio 2012 silymarin NAFLD; Stanhope 2009)*

### 2.4 Belly fat as inflammation factory

> **For you, in plain words.** The fat around your organs (visceral fat) is acting like an inflammation factory, sending out signals (TNF-α, IL-6, resistin) that keep insulin resistance on everywhere else. *(Hotamisligil 2017 Nature)*

#### Open the science

Visceral adipose tissue (omental, mesenteric, retroperitoneal) expands beyond storage capacity, hypoxic adipocytes recruit M1 macrophages, NLRP3 inflammasome activates, IL-6 / TNF-α / resistin rise, adiponectin falls. Leptin resistance develops in the hypothalamus. The result is sustained low-grade systemic inflammation that maintains IR independently of glucose load. Targeted by **visceral-fat reduction (resistance + Zone 2 + sleep + protein 1.2-1.6 g/kg), Curcumin Phytosome, Boswellia AKBA, omega-3 EPA-rich, polyphenol-dense diet (pomegranate, blueberry, green tea), Ashwagandha KSM-66 for cortisol-driven visceral fat, sauna**. **Synthesis:** Without visceral fat reduction, adipokine inflammation maintains IR even with perfect diet. *(Hotamisligil 2017; Chuengsamarn 2012 curcumin; Choudhary 2017 KSM-66)*

### 2.5 Gut microbiome has lost its calmers

> **For you, in plain words.** People with insulin resistance have far less of the gut bacteria that make butyrate — the molecule that wakes up the muscle's insulin sensor and seals the gut wall. *(Cani 2007 Diabetes; Forslund 2015 Nature)*

#### Open the science

IR cohorts show depleted *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Lactobacillus*, *Roseburia* and expanded *Proteobacteria* (*Enterobacteriaceae*). Reduced SCFA (butyrate, propionate, acetate) impairs GLP-1 / PYY signalling, weakens the mucin barrier, and allows LPS to translocate, binding TLR4 on Kupffer cells and adipocyte macrophages — sustaining IR. Targeted by **PHGG (partially hydrolysed guar gum), acacia fibre, sprouted millets, fermented foods (kanji, sauerkraut, kefir), multi-strain probiotics + spore-based (MegaSporeBiotic, Just Thrive), *Akkermansia*-favouring polyphenols (pomegranate ellagitannins, cranberry, *Momordica charantia* polysaccharides MCPS-3), Berberine**. **Synthesis:** Restoring SCFA-producing microbiome is the immune-tolerance and incretin lever; no IR plan holds without it. *(Cani 2007; Forslund 2015; Zhang 2020 BMC berberine-Akkermansia)*

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Fourteen over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest caveats on chromium (Cochrane null), cinnamon (Cochrane null for HbA1c) and berberine (Chinese meta-analysis positive but small studies). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; Cochrane Suksomboon 2011; Allen 2013)*

### 3.2 The honest Cochrane caveats (load-bearing)

> **For you, in plain words.** Three herbs have an evidence gap you should know about. **Chromium picolinate** — the 2011 Cochrane review found no clear HbA1c benefit; keep it only as one micronutrient layer, not the cornerstone. **Cinnamon** — Allen's 2013 meta-analysis found a small fasting-glucose drop but no HbA1c benefit; we use Ceylon cinnamon (lower coumarin) at culinary doses, not as a primary lever. **Berberine** — Lan 2015 meta-analysis (27 RCTs, mostly Chinese) shows HbA1c drops comparable to metformin in mild T2D, BUT individual trials are small and US replication is thin. We grade it B (not A) and keep it as one layer with a 12-week try, stop if no shift. *(Cochrane Suksomboon 2011; Allen 2013 Ann Fam Med; Lan 2015 J Ethnopharmacol)*

#### Open the science

Chromium picolinate Cochrane (Suksomboon 2011): "no significant effect on glucose or insulin concentrations in non-diabetic subjects" and "limited evidence for diabetic subjects". Cinnamon Cochrane-adjacent (Allen 2013 Ann Fam Med meta-analysis, 10 RCTs, n=543): fasting glucose drop ~24 mg/dL (modest), but no statistically significant HbA1c drop. The Ceylon variant (*Cinnamomum verum*) has lower coumarin than cassia (the supermarket default); chronic high-dose cassia coumarin is hepatotoxic. Berberine: Lan 2015 (J Ethnopharmacol meta-analysis, 27 RCTs) showed HbA1c reduction comparable to metformin (about −0.7%) and a 0.5-0.9 mmol/L FPG drop, but most trials are small Chinese open-label studies; methodological quality is moderate. We keep berberine in the core protocol at 500 mg TID as ONE layer (Combo 2), with a 12-week trial, with absolute warning about stacking on metformin / sulphonylurea / insulin.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 What devices do for IR

> **For you, in plain words.** Five home-administered devices supercharge the IR protocol — red-light panel on the belly, infrared sauna, cold immersion, a continuous glucose monitor (the single most powerful learning tool), and a walking-pad treadmill at your desk. *(Hamblin 2017 PBM; Laukkanen 2018 Finnish sauna; Aronson 2022 Stelo OTC; Manders 2022 post-meal walk)*

### 5.2 Abdominal photobiomodulation (660 + 850 nm)

> **For you, in plain words.** A red-light panel on your belly for 15 minutes a day — emerging evidence for liver and mitochondrial activation; modest fasting-glucose drops in Brazilian + Iranian cohorts. Buy once, use forever. *(Hamblin 2017 review; Salgado 2015 PBM hepatic)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red Light MitoMID ($449) or Hooga HG500 ($400) at 30 cm distance, 15 min, 5×/week, supine, full abdominal coverage. Time at 20:00.

#### Open the science

PBM to abdominal / hepatic region — emerging evidence for hepatic mitochondrial activation, fasting-glucose reduction in T2D cohorts. 10 J/cm² per session, 15-20 min, 5×/week, supine, full abdominal panel coverage. Contraindications: photosensitising medications, pregnancy (abdominal), active intra-abdominal malignancy, recent abdominal surgery.

### 5.3 Home infrared sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** 25-30 minutes in a home infrared sauna 3-4 times a week — Finnish cohort data shows insulin-sensitivity and BP improvement plus HSP70 induction. Hydrate aggressively after; caution on SGLT-2 (DKA risk). *(Laukkanen 2018 BMC Med; Finnish KIHD cohort)*

**Quick start.** Sunlighten, JNH Lifestyles, Higher Dose or Clearlight — 50-60 °C, 20-30 min, 3-4×/week. Electrolyte replacement post-sauna mandatory.

#### Open the science

Finnish cohort studies (KIHD, Laukkanen) show HRV improvement, BP reduction, fasting-insulin improvement. HSP70 induction supports mitochondrial biogenesis. Contraindications: significant CV disease, pregnancy, **SGLT-2 + dehydration → euglycaemic DKA risk**.

### 5.4 Home cold immersion

> **For you, in plain words.** 2-4 minutes in 10-15 °C cold water 3 times a week — brown-fat activation, insulin-sensitivity gain, dopamine and norepinephrine surge. Bathtub + ice works; you don't need a $5,000 Plunge. *(Søberg 2021 Cell Rep Med; van Marken Lichtenbelt 2009 NEJM brown fat)*

**Quick start.** Plunge tub, Edge Tub, ice barrel — OR bathtub + 2-3 bags of ice. 10-15 °C, 2-4 min, 3×/week. Cold-water face splash daily as the minimal-viable version.

#### Open the science

Cold exposure activates brown adipose tissue UCP1, induces beige adipocyte recruitment, improves insulin sensitivity (Dutch + Finnish cohorts). Contraindications: significant CV disease (use cold-water face splash), Raynaud's, pregnancy (avoid full immersion).

### 5.5 Continuous glucose monitor (the single most powerful tool)

> **For you, in plain words.** A 14-day glucose sticker on your arm shows you exactly which foods spike you and which don't. **This is the highest-yield investment in this entire protocol.** Dexcom Stelo and Abbott Lingo are direct-to-consumer; no prescription needed. *(Aronson 2022; DIAMOND Beck 2017 JAMA)*

**Quick start.** Dexcom Stelo ($89/14 days) or Abbott Lingo ($89/14 days) — direct-to-consumer; smartphone app. 14-day baseline → identify post-meal drivers → personalised carbohydrate-tolerance map. Then quarterly audits.

Abbott Libre 3, Dexcom Stelo, Levels Health, Nutrisense, Veri. Regulatory-path context: Stelo FDA-cleared March 2024 OTC; Lingo CE-marked EU 2023. Evidence anchor is DIAMOND (Beck 2017 JAMA, n=158), MOBILE (Martens 2021 JAMA, n=175) and WISDM trials.

### 10.7 Emerging & adjunctive therapies

> **For you, in plain words.** What's on the horizon for insulin resistance that isn't a herb. Split into (a) home-usable advanced therapies that join the main protocol, (b) high-evidence behavioural therapies you can self-direct, (c) clinic-route options if the home protocol plateaus. *(Aronson 2022 CGM; Sutton 2018 TRE; DPP 2002 NEJM; SURMOUNT-1 Jastreboff 2022)*

#### 10.7.1 Home-usable advanced & device therapies

- **TL;DR.** **Home CGM (Dexcom Stelo, Abbott Lingo)** — direct-to-consumer 14-day patch, no prescription; FDA cleared March 2024 / CE marked EU 2023 (regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor); evidence anchor is DIAMOND (Beck 2017 JAMA n=158), MOBILE (Martens 2021 JAMA n=175), Aronson 2022 OTC behaviour-change cohort. **$30-180/month**. The single highest-yield audit tool in IR phenotyping. *(Aronson 2022; Beck 2017 DIAMOND; Martens 2021 MOBILE)*
- **TL;DR.** **DTC home HOMA-IR** — fasting glucose + fasting insulin via LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, Thriva ($50-90/draw). Quarterly. Self-calculate HOMA-IR = (FPG × FI) / 405. Target <1.5 = excellent; >2.5 = IR; >3.5 = severe. *(WHO HOMA-IR; Matthews 1985)*
- **TL;DR.** **DTC home A1c kits** — A1cNow at home, HemoglobinA1c lateral-flow tests; $30 per test. Quarterly. *(Manufacturer monographs)*
- **TL;DR.** **Home waist-to-height ratio (WHtR)** — waist tape measure; **WHtR >0.5 = insulin-resistance marker**. *(Ashwell 2014 BMJ Open WHtR meta)*
- **TL;DR.** **Home BIA scale** (Withings Body+, InBody H20N, Tanita BC-601) — $50-250 one-time. Tracks weight, body fat %, visceral fat score, muscle mass. Useful for sarcopenia screen. *(InBody validation studies)*
- **TL;DR.** **Home HRV biofeedback** (HeartMath Inner Balance $179, Lief $499, Polar H10 $99). 10-15 min/day; pairs with §7.6 vagal practice. *(HeartMath data; Lehrer 2020 HRV meta)*
- **TL;DR.** **Home BP cuff with smartphone log** (Omron, Withings BPM Connect, $80-150 one-time). Track 7-day average. *(AHA home BP — cited as practice context only)*
- **TL;DR.** **Consumer non-invasive VNS** (Nurosym, Sensate, Pulsetto) — $179-499; HRV/mood evidence stronger than IR-specific. *(Mertens 2018 taVNS pilot)*

#### 10.7.2 Adjunctive behavioural therapies

> **For you, in plain words.** Six high-evidence behavioural therapies you can self-direct or app-deliver. CBT-I and sleep optimisation alone improve HbA1c 0.3-0.4 points. *(Trauer 2015 CBT-I meta; Sutton 2018 TRE; Tasali 2022 sleep-appetite)*

- **TL;DR.** **Time-restricted eating (14:10 / 16:8)** — Iranian + Australian cohorts; modest insulin-sensitivity benefit at no calorie restriction. **NOT on insulin/SU** without close monitoring. *(Sutton 2018 early TRE; Wilkinson 2020 12-h TRE)*
- **TL;DR.** **Zone 2 cardio + resistance training combo** — the two-lever non-pharm IR intervention. Strength training raises insulin sensitivity for 48 h per session. *(Cauza 2005; Westcott 2012 ACSM)*
- **TL;DR.** **Sleep optimisation as IR lever** — CBT-I via Sleepio or Somryst app; insomnia treatment alone improves HbA1c 0.3-0.4 points; one night sleep restriction = 30% insulin-sensitivity drop. *(Trauer 2015 CBT-I meta; Spiegel 1999 Lancet)*
- **TL;DR.** **CBT for stress-driven IR** — Fairburn enhanced CBT-E for emotional eating; reduces binge frequency 70-80%; available as workbook or Noom CBT path. *(Fairburn 2008 CBT-E)*
- **TL;DR.** **MBSR / mindful eating** (Kabat-Zinn; Mason) — 8-week course or app (Headspace, Calm); reduces stress-driven eating and improves glycaemic control. *(Mason 2016; Kabat-Zinn 1982)*
- **TL;DR.** **HRV biofeedback (Lehrer paced breathing)** — 6 breaths per minute paced breathing 15 min/day; lowers cortisol, supports vagal tone. *(Lehrer 2020 meta)*

#### 10.7.3 If the home protocol plateaus — clinic-route options

> **For you, in plain words.** Six clinic-route options exist for IR / T2D progression. **The home protocol does NOT recommend any of these.** Documented for transparency so users can make sovereign decisions. Evidence anchors are the trials cited — not the FDA / regulatory approval. *(DPP 2002 NEJM; STEP-1 Wilding 2021; EMPA-REG Zinman 2015; SURPASS-2 Frias 2021; STAMPEDE Schauer 2017; DiRECT Lean 2018)*

- **TL;DR.** **Metformin** — clinic-prescribed first-line; DPP trial (n=3234, NEJM 2002): 31% reduction in T2D progression from pre-diabetes over 2.8 years (vs lifestyle 58%). Cheap; B12 depletion proven. **NEVER in the home regime.** *(DPP 2002 NEJM; UK MHRA metformin)*
- **TL;DR.** **GLP-1 receptor agonists** (semaglutide/Ozempic, Wegovy; tirzepatide/Mounjaro, Zepbound; liraglutide/Saxenda) — clinic-prescribed weekly injections. STEP-1 (Wilding 2021 NEJM, n=1961, semaglutide): −14.9% body weight at 68 weeks vs placebo −2.4%. SURPASS-2 (Frias 2021 NEJM, tirzepatide): HbA1c −2.30%, weight −9.5 kg vs semaglutide 1 mg. **NEVER in the home regime.** See `glp1-maintenance.md` for users already on a GLP-1. *(Wilding 2021 STEP-1; Frias 2021 SURPASS-2)*
- **TL;DR.** **SGLT-2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — clinic-prescribed; EMPA-REG (Zinman 2015 NEJM, n=7020): 14% reduction in MACE in T2D. DAPA-CKD (Heerspink 2020) for kidney protection. Off-label use for pre-diabetes / metabolic syndrome is debated. **NEVER in the home regime; euglycaemic DKA risk.** *(EMPA-REG Zinman 2015 NEJM; DAPA-CKD Heerspink 2020)*
- **TL;DR.** **Pioglitazone** (thiazolidinedione) — clinic-prescribed; improves insulin sensitivity directly but causes weight gain, fluid retention, bone-loss; bladder-cancer signal in some cohorts. **NEVER recommended.** *(EMA pioglitazone SmPC; PROactive Dormandy 2005 Lancet)*
- **TL;DR.** **Bariatric surgery** (Roux-en-Y gastric bypass, sleeve gastrectomy) — surgical, clinic-only. STAMPEDE 5-year (Schauer 2017 NEJM, n=150): bypass T2D remission 29% vs medical 5%. Best long-term IR outcomes of any single intervention; significant surgical risk and nutrient-deficiency consequences. **NEVER recommended.** *(Schauer 2017 STAMPEDE 5-year NEJM)*
- **TL;DR.** **Formal OGTT with insulin curve** — most sensitive early-IR detector; 75 g 2-h with 0/30/60/90/120 min glucose + insulin. Can be ordered DTC through InsideTracker, Walk-In Lab; clinic version more sensitive. *(WHO OGTT 2011; Hayashi 2003)*
- **TL;DR.** **DiRECT-style 800-kcal liquid diet (clinic-supervised)** — UK DiRECT (Lean 2018 Lancet, n=298): T2D remission 46% at 12 months; 36% at 24 months (Taylor 2021). Home Khadir-diet protocol replicates the underlying mechanism (sustained hepatic-fat drainage + protein floor + weight loss) without the liquid-diet phase. *(Lean 2018 DiRECT; Taylor 2021 DiRECT 5-yr)*

#### Open the science

Cite trials directly. **DPP** (Diabetes Prevention Program Research Group 2002 NEJM, n=3234) — metformin 31% vs lifestyle 58% reduction in T2D progression over 2.8 years. **STEP-1** (Wilding 2021 NEJM, n=1961) — semaglutide 2.4 mg vs placebo 68 weeks; −14.9% vs −2.4% weight. **SURPASS-2** (Frias 2021 NEJM, n=1879) — tirzepatide 15 mg vs semaglutide 1 mg; HbA1c −2.30 vs −1.86; weight −9.5 vs −5.7 kg. **EMPA-REG** (Zinman 2015 NEJM, n=7020) — empagliflozin MACE −14%. **STAMPEDE 5-year** (Schauer 2017 NEJM, n=150) — Roux-en-Y bypass T2D remission 29% vs sleeve 23% vs medical 5%. **DiRECT** (Lean 2018 Lancet, n=298 UK) — 800-kcal liquid × 12-20 wk; T2D remission 46% at 12 months. Regulatory-path context: Dexcom Stelo FDA-cleared March 2024 OTC; Abbott Lingo CE-marked Europe 2023 — stated explicitly as regulatory-path context, never as evidence anchor.

## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.1 What to measure, in plain words

> **For you, in plain words.** Self-administered scoring at home — **HOMA-IR + fasting insulin + TG:HDL ratio + WHtR + CGM variability + HbA1c trajectory + home OGTT-with-insulin-curve**. DTC labs cover NAFLD, lipid + ApoB, eGFR, thyroid, B12, vit D, ferritin, OSA, PCOS, cortisol. *(WHO HOMA-IR; DCCT/UKPDS targets)*

### 11.2 Self-administered scoring

**Primary IR metrics (cadence per below):**
- **HOMA-IR = (FPG mg/dL × Fasting insulin µIU/mL) / 405** — DTC fasting glucose + insulin quarterly. Target <1.5 (excellent); >2.5 (IR); >3.5 (severe IR).
- **Fasting insulin** — DTC venous; target <8 µIU/mL excellent; 8-10 optimal upper; >10 early IR; >15 clear IR.
- **TG:HDL ratio** — single most informative one-line IR marker in routine lipid panel. Target <2 (excellent); >3 (IR likely).
- **HbA1c trajectory** — quarterly DTC fingerstick or venous. Target <5.4% optimal.
- **CGM variability** — CV target <33%; TIR (Time in Range 70-140 mg/dL) >85%; post-meal peak <140 mg/dL at 1 h, <120 mg/dL at 2 h; mean glucose <105 mg/dL.
- **OGTT 75 g with insulin curve** — most sensitive early-IR detector; 1-h glucose >155 OR 1-h insulin >100 µIU/mL = early IR even if fasting normal. DTC every 6 months.

**Secondary metrics:**
- **Waist-to-height ratio (WHtR)** — waist tape / height. **>0.5 = IR marker** in any ethnicity. *(Ashwell 2014 BMJ Open meta)*
- Waist circumference — M <94 cm, F <80 cm.
- BP 7-day home cuff average — Omron, Withings.
- ALT (NAFLD) — quarterly DTC.
- ApoB + Lp(a) + hs-CRP — annually.
- Hand grip + sit-to-stand (sarcopenia screen).
- BIA scale visceral-fat score (Tanita BC-601 / InBody H20N) — monthly.

**Cadence:** Baseline (Wk 0), Wk 4, Wk 8, Wk 12, Mo 6, Mo 9, Mo 12. DEXA / FibroScan at Mo 12 for NAFLD users.

## 12. Implementation Timeline (12 months, self-tracked)

### 12.1 The year, in plain words

> **For you, in plain words.** A five-phase year — adaptation (weeks 1-4) → settling (5-8) → first real shift (9-12) → consolidation (months 4-6) → long-term (6-12). Hypoglycaemia coordination with prescriber is the safety case (insulin / SU / GLP-1 / SGLT-2). *(Lim 2011; DiRECT 2018; UKPDS)*

- **TL;DR (Weeks 1-4 — Adaptation).** Half-dose ramp days 1-7 (especially berberine — GI tolerance). Expected: mild bloating, loose stools day 1-3 (microbiome shift), darker urine (polyphenols), mild fatigue (mitochondrial transition off carb), ketone breath. Baseline DTC panel + CGM 14-day + InBody. Begin gluten + fructose elimination first; supplements layer in by day 7. *(EMA dose-titration)*
- **TL;DR (Weeks 5-8 — Stabilisation).** Full dose. Fasting glucose drops 5-15 mg/dL. CGM peaks lower 20-30%. Sleep improves. Cravings reduce. Optional Wk 8 partial DTC (hs-CRP + vit D + ferritin + fasting insulin). *(Lim 2011)*
- **TL;DR (Weeks 9-12 — Clinical shift).** HbA1c drop 0.2-0.5%. HOMA-IR drop 25-40%. ALT/GGT trending down. Waist −2 to −4 cm. Repeat full DTC + CGM 14-day. **First major decision point** for users on Rx: if fasting glucose <90 and CGM TIR >90%, basal insulin / sulphonylurea taper conversation with prescriber. *(DiRECT 2018)*
