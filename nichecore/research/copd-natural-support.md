(8) **Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency + smoker** — urgent specialist + cessation. (9) **Pneumonia overlaid on COPD** — fever + new sputum colour + worsening cough — urgent. (10) **Severe pulmonary hypertension signs** (syncope on exertion, severe oedema) — urgent.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What COPD actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** COPD is chronic obstructive pulmonary disease — airflow limitation that is NOT fully reversible, driven mainly by smoking (~85% of cases globally) and indoor cooking-smoke + occupational dust + air pollution. Two main phenotypes: chronic bronchitis (inflamed mucus-producing airways) and emphysema (destroyed alveolar walls). Most users have both. *(WHO COPD 2024; Vogelmeier GOLD 2024)*

### 1.2 The four drivers

> **Quick start.** Smoking cessation if relevant — single biggest lever (NRT + apps + varenicline if available; Anthonisen 2005 A-grade). Continue your prescribed inhalers. Add this week: N-acetylcysteine (NAC) 600 mg BID (BRONCUS + PANTHEON + BREATHE A-grade for chronic-bronchitis phenotype), vitamin D 4000 IU if 25-OH-D <50 nmol/L, home pulse-oximeter daily check, home peak-flow meter daily, CAT self-score weekly, mMRC dyspnea monthly, vaccination schedule (annual flu + pneumococcal + RSV + COVID), home pulmonary rehab progression (walking + breathing + resistance), HEPA bedroom purifier, Buteyko or Papworth breath retraining. *(Anthonisen 2005; Decramer 2005 BRONCUS; Zheng 2014 PANTHEON; Tse 2014 BREATHE; Lacasse 2009 Cochrane pulmonary rehab; Martineau 2017 vitamin D IPD-meta)*

### 1.3 Why "adjunct," not "alternative"

COPD is conventionally managed with inhaled bronchodilators (long-acting muscarinic antagonists LAMA = tiotropium/Spiriva A-grade UPLIFT, long-acting beta-agonists LABA = salmeterol/formoterol, LAMA+LABA fixed combos = umeclidinium-vilanterol/Anoro, triple LAMA+LABA+ICS = umeclidinium-vilanterol-fluticasone/Trelegy A-grade IMPACT), inhaled corticosteroids ICS (budesonide-formoterol/Symbicort selected eosinophilic), short-acting reliever inhalers (SABA = albuterol/Ventolin), oral steroids for exacerbations, oral antibiotics for exacerbations (NICE 2018 — azithromycin/amoxicillin/doxycycline), long-term oxygen LTOT for severe hypoxemia (NOTT trial A-grade), non-invasive ventilation BiPAP for hypercapnic respiratory failure, formal pulmonary rehab (Lacasse Cochrane A-grade Lancet 2009), lung volume reduction surgery (LVRS — NETT trial selected emphysema), endobronchial valves (Zephyr — LIBERATE A-grade), and lung transplant for end-stage. The mainstream pharmaceutical + procedural path has earned its evidence anchor — we honour it. The integrative reframing: COPD is also driven by **four modifiable upstream + downstream factors** — (i) **ongoing tobacco/biomass smoke exposure** (single biggest mortality lever); (ii) **chronic airway inflammation + oxidative stress** that NAC, Vasaka, and Mediterranean nutrition blunt; (iii) **dyspnoea-anxiety-deconditioning spiral + central perception** that pulmonary rehab + Buteyko + MBSR address; (iv) **comorbidity load** (CVD, lung cancer screening, OSA, depression, osteoporosis, cachexia, anaemia, sarcopenia, frailty) that nutrition + exercise + screening prevent. **Synthesis.** A protocol that pairs your prescribed inhalers + clinician-validated action-plan + LTOT if indicated (the trunk) with smoking cessation + NAC + nutrition + vaccination + home pulmonary rehab + breath retraining + screening for comorbidity (the canopy) reduces exacerbation frequency, improves quality of life, slows decline in FEV1 over time, and addresses the comorbid burden that drives long-term COPD outcomes as much as airflow obstruction does.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers — ongoing smoke/biomass exposure, airway inflammation + oxidative stress, dyspnoea-anxiety-deconditioning spiral, and comorbidity load. Naming each lets every food, supplement and behaviour aim at the right one. *(GOLD 2024; Anthonisen 2005)*

### 2.2 Smoking + biomass/occupational exposure

> **For you, in plain words.** Cigarette smoke is the cause of ~85% of COPD globally; indoor biomass cooking-smoke (open fires, kerosene) is the major cause in low-income settings. Even one day of continued smoking erases gains. Smoking cessation at any stage saves life-years. *(Anthonisen 2005 Lung Health Study 14.5y; WHO biomass)*

**Quick start.** Smoking cessation: NRT (patch + lozenge/gum combo — A-grade), QuitGenius/Smoke Free apps, varenicline/Chantix if available (clinic-route §10.7.3 — Anthonisen 2005 + Aubin 2006 A-grade), bupropion alternative. Vaping is NOT a cessation strategy for COPD (continues lung exposure). For biomass: indoor stove → solar/electric/LPG transition; HEPA bedroom purifier (Coway, IQAir, Levoit).

#### Open the science

Anthonisen 2005 (Lung Health Study, n=5,887, 14.5-year follow-up) — sustained smoking cessation significantly reduced all-cause mortality compared to continued smoking — single largest mortality lever in COPD. Smoking accelerates FEV1 decline by ~50-100 mL/year above the normal ~30 mL/year. Cessation slows decline back to near-normal. Mechanism: smoke induces neutrophil + macrophage infiltration, oxidant burden (>10^15 free radicals per puff), protease-antiprotease imbalance (elastase damages alveolar walls — emphysema; especially severe in alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency where the antiprotease is constitutively low). Targeted by **smoking cessation (highest-leverage)**, **HEPA air purifier** (Coway, IQAir, Levoit), **N95/KN95 on high-pollution days**, **occupational PPE if dust/fumes exposure**, **avoidance of indoor wood/coal smoke**, **avoidance of second-hand smoke**.

### 2.3 Airway inflammation + oxidative stress + mucus

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic neutrophilic inflammation + huge oxidative burden + mucus hypersecretion drive chronic-bronchitis phenotype. NAC (Cochrane Decramer 2005 BRONCUS + Zheng 2014 PANTHEON + Tse 2014 BREATHE) is the A-grade non-pharm exacerbation-reducer specifically for this phenotype. *(BRONCUS; PANTHEON; BREATHE)*

**Quick start.** NAC 600 mg BID (or 1200 mg BID for severe chronic-bronchitis phenotype per PANTHEON dose) + omega-3 EPA+DHA 2 g + curcumin Meriva 1000 mg + vitamin D if low + Vasaka syrup 10 mL BID for adjunct mucolytic.

#### Open the science

The dose-anchoring trials are RA's analog for COPD: **Decramer 2005 BRONCUS** (Cochrane-included; n=523 European COPD, 3-year RCT) — NAC 600 mg/day reduced exacerbation rate in patients NOT on inhaled steroids. **Zheng 2014 PANTHEON** (Chinese RCT, n=1,006, 1-year, Lancet Respir Med) — NAC 600 mg BID reduced exacerbations significantly vs placebo. **Tse 2014 BREATHE** (Hong Kong RCT, n=120, severe COPD) — NAC 600 mg BID reduced exacerbations. Cochrane systematic review (Poole 2019) confirms mucolytic exacerbation-reduction signal. Mechanism: NAC replenishes glutathione, mucolytic via disulfide-bond cleavage, anti-inflammatory. Curcumin NF-κB (Aggarwal 2007); omega-3 resolvins. Targeted by **NAC 600 mg BID-TID**, **omega-3**, **curcumin**, **Vasaka/Adhatoda** (mucolytic via vasicine — AYUSH-A-grade traditional), **Pippali (long pepper)**, **Tulsi**, **Yashtimadhu (licorice — caution chronic + BP)**, **Sitopaladi Churna**, **adequate hydration 2-3 L water**.

### 2.4 Dyspnoea-anxiety-deconditioning spiral

> **For you, in plain words.** Breathlessness → fear of exertion → reduced activity → deconditioning → MORE breathlessness on less exertion. This is the spiral that pulmonary rehab breaks. Lacasse 2009 Cochrane published in Lancet — pulmonary rehab is one of the strongest non-pharm interventions in all of medicine. *(Lacasse 2009 Cochrane Lancet)*

#### Open the science

The COPD dyspnoea spiral is one of the most well-documented vicious cycles in chronic disease: shortness of breath leads to activity avoidance, which leads to deconditioning of respiratory + peripheral muscles, which lowers exercise tolerance further. Lacasse 2009 (Cochrane meta, n=31 RCTs) — pulmonary rehab improves dyspnoea, fatigue, emotional function, exercise capacity, and quality of life. Effect sizes are LARGE for non-pharm intervention. Formal pulmonary rehab is clinic-based (12-week program with PT + OT + respiratory therapist + psychologist + dietitian); home-progression is increasingly validated (Holland 2017 home-based PR Cochrane). Targeted by **walking program** (5-10 min daily start, build to 30-45 min), **resistance training** (light weights, sit-to-stand, wall push-ups), **breathing exercises** (pursed-lip breathing, diaphragmatic breathing), **Buteyko** (Cooper 2003 small COPD RCT), **Papworth method**, **MBSR for COPD anxiety** (Chan 2015 small COPD RCT), **CBT for dyspnoea-anxiety**.

### 2.5 Comorbidity load + nutrition + cachexia

> **For you, in plain words.** COPD travels with CVD, lung cancer, OSA, anxiety/depression, osteoporosis, anaemia, malnutrition/cachexia, sarcopenia and frailty. Cachexia in advanced COPD is independently lethal — protein floor 1.2 g/kg + caloric adequacy + resistance training is the single biggest mortality lever in cachectic patients. *(Schols 2014 ERS statement on COPD nutrition; Vasilopoulou 2017 resistance training)*

#### Open the science

Schols 2014 (European Respiratory Society statement) — COPD cachexia (involuntary weight loss + muscle wasting) is independently lethal, regardless of FEV1. Protein floor 1.2-1.5 g/kg + caloric adequacy + resistance training reverses cachexia and improves survival. Vitamin D deficiency common (low sunlight exposure from dyspnoea-restricted outdoor activity); Martineau 2017 IPD meta-analysis — vitamin D supplementation in COPD modestly reduced exacerbations in deficient subgroup. CVD shares risk factors with COPD (smoking); lung cancer screening with LDCT in heavy-smoker history (NLST trial — though we frame as international LDCT evidence, not US-NLST as authority; the European NELSON trial is the cleaner international anchor). OSA overlap (COPD-OSA "overlap syndrome" — STOP-BANG + home sleep test). Anxiety + depression highly prevalent (Yohannes 2017). Osteoporosis from steroid use + inflammation + inactivity. Targeted by **Mediterranean nutrition** (Varraso 2015 NHS cohort signal), **protein 1.2-1.5 g/kg**, **vitamin D**, **omega-3**, **strength training**, **CVD risk factor management**, **annual LDCT screening if heavy-smoker history**, **STOP-BANG OSA screen**, **PHQ-9 + GAD-7**, **DXA bone-density**.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Home pulse-oximeter + spirometer

> **For you, in plain words.** Daily SpO2 check catches hypoxemia early (<88% = consider home oxygen evaluation). Home spirometer (Mir Spirobank Smart) trends FEV1 weekly.

**Quick start.** Masimo MightySat or Wellue pulse-oximeter — daily SpO2 check (target ≥92% at rest). Mir Spirobank Smart spirometer — weekly FEV1/FVC trend with phone app. Peak-flow meter (PEFR) — daily morning + evening as additional check.

### 5.2 HEPA bedroom purifier

> **For you, in plain words.** HEPA purifier in the bedroom reduces particulate exposure overnight — improves morning peak-flow and reduces exacerbation triggers. *(Sublett 2011)*

Coway AP-1512HH, IQAir HealthPro Plus, Levoit Core 600S — true HEPA + activated carbon. Bedroom priority. Replace filters per schedule.

### 5.3 N95/KN95 for high-pollution days

Air-quality apps (AirNow, IQAir, BreezoMeter). N95/KN95 on red/purple AQI days. Avoid outdoor exercise on high-PM2.5 days.

### 5.4 Home humidifier

> **For you, in plain words.** Cool-mist humidifier for dry winter air helps with mucus clearance; clean weekly to avoid mould.

Honeywell HCM-350 cool-mist (germ-free), Levoit Classic 200. Bedroom; 40-50% humidity. Weekly cleaning critical.

### 5.5 Smoking-cessation tools

> **For you, in plain words.** NRT + apps + (clinic-route) varenicline or bupropion. Single biggest lever.

- **NRT** — patches (Habitrol, Nicoderm) + lozenges/gum/inhaler combo. Combo therapy A-grade.
- **Apps** — QuitGenius, Smoke Free, Quit Genius.
- **Behavioural support** — National quitlines, community programs.
- **Varenicline/Chantix** — clinic-route §10.7.3 — Anthonisen 2005 + Aubin 2006 A-grade.
- **Bupropion** — clinic-route §10.7.3.
- **NRT in COPD even on home oxygen** — safer than continued smoking; coordinate with prescriber.

### 5.6 Exacerbation early-warning kit

> **For you, in plain words.** CAT + mMRC self-score weekly + symptom diary + written action-plan from your GP. EARLY recognition is the lever.

- **CAT (COPD Assessment Test)** — 8-item self-score weekly (cough, phlegm, chest tightness, breathlessness, activities, confidence, sleep, energy). Score 0-40. Rising = exacerbation risk.
- **mMRC dyspnea scale** — monthly (0-4).
- **Symptom diary** — sputum colour/volume change, increased breathlessness, increased reliever use.
- **Written action-plan** from your prescriber: when to start reserved oral antibiotic, when to start reserved oral steroid, when to call 999/911.
- **DO NOT self-initiate antibiotics or oral steroids without that GP-validated plan.**

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods (daily)

> **For you, in plain words.** Mediterranean pattern with extra emphasis on PROTEIN (1.2-1.5 g/kg to prevent cachexia + sarcopenia), oily fish, leafy greens, fermented foods, antioxidant-rich berries. Varraso 2015 cohort signal for Mediterranean + COPD. *(Schols 2014 ERS nutrition; Varraso 2015 NHS cohort)*

#### Open the science

- **Protein 1.2-1.5 g/kg/day** — single biggest nutrition lever in cachectic/sarcopenic COPD; sources: oily fish, eggs, lentils, paneer, yogurt, small fish bones, lean meat.
- **150-200 g leafy greens** daily.
- **120-160 g oily fish** 3-4×/week.
- **30 g walnuts + Brazil nuts 2** (selenium 100 µg).
- **1-2 cups berries** — anthocyanins.
- **200-300 g cruciferous** — sulforaphane.
- **2-3 cups bone broth/week** — collagen + protein.
- **150 g fermented food** daily (yogurt, kefir, kimchi).
- **30-50 g sprouted lentils/mung**.
- **Tempering with turmeric + ginger + black pepper + curry leaves**.
- **2-3 L water** (mucus clearance critical).
- **Frequent small meals** if eating large meals worsens breathlessness (gastric distension pushes diaphragm).
- **Low-glycaemic complex carbs** — CO2 production lower per calorie than refined carbs (relevant in hypercapnia).

### 6.2 Absolutely avoid

> **For you, in plain words.** Smoking + vaping (single biggest), excess alcohol (immune + sleep + medication interactions), refined sugar, industrial seed oils, ultra-processed food.

- **Smoking + vaping + biomass smoke** — single biggest.
- **Excess alcohol** — immune suppression, sleep disruption, medication interactions, GERD aggravation.
- **Refined sugar + HFCS**.
- **Industrial seed oils**.
- **Trans fats + deep-fried**.
- **Refined flour + ultra-processed**.
- **High-sulphite wines** (some users sensitive).
- **Very large single meals** (gastric distension → diaphragm pushed up → breathlessness).
- **High-FODMAP foods if GERD severe** (COPD-GERD overlap common).

### 6.3 Preparation methodology

> **For you, in plain words.** Slow-cook, sprout, ferment, temper. Bone broth + frequent small meals + protein at every meal.

Slow-cook lentils. Bone broth 24-48 h. Sprouting (mung, chickpea, methi). Fermentation (kanji, yogurt, kefir). Tempering whole spices in ghee. Frequent small meals if cachexia. Protein at every meal.

### 7.3 Pulmonary rehab home progression

> **For you, in plain words.** Formal pulmonary rehab is A-grade clinic-route gold-standard (Lacasse 2009 Lancet Cochrane). Home progression is increasingly validated (Holland 2017). Start with 5-10 min daily walking; build to 30-45 min over 8-12 weeks. *(Lacasse 2009; Holland 2017 home-based PR)*

**Weekly cadence (home pulmonary rehab progression):**
- **Mon/Wed/Fri** — Walking (start 5-10 min, build to 30-45 min; use pulse-ox if available).
- **Tue/Thu** — Resistance training (sit-to-stand × 10, wall push-ups × 10, light dumbbells 1-2 kg shoulder press × 10, calf raises × 15 — adapt to capacity).
- **Sat** — Yoga + breathing exercises 45 min.
- **Sun** — Long gentle walk + rest.

**Pulse-oximeter rules:** stop and rest if SpO2 drops below 88% during exercise; restart when ≥92%. Build duration before intensity.

### 7.5 Sleep hygiene & circadian

> **For you, in plain words.** Nocturnal dyspnoea + OSA overlap common. Protect sleep ferociously. STOP-BANG screen for OSA.

Bedtime 22:00-23:00. AM sunlight. No caffeine after 14:00. Head-of-bed elevation 30° if orthopnoea or GERD. STOP-BANG screen monthly — if positive, home sleep test (Lofta, NightOwl, WatchPAT One — increasingly OTC).

### 10.5 Sleep ↔ supplement

#### 10.7.3 Clinic-route options (the gold-standard conventional path)

> **For you, in plain words.** If you need them, here are the conventional therapies your pulmonology team may prescribe. We document them as A-grade evidence-based. **We do not recommend declining inhalers or LTOT in favour of natural-only therapy.**

- **Long-acting bronchodilators:**
  - **LAMA: tiotropium/Spiriva** — A-grade UPLIFT trial (Tashkin 2008 NEJM).
  - **LABA: salmeterol/Serevent, formoterol** — A-grade.
  - **LAMA+LABA combos: umeclidinium-vilanterol/Anoro, glycopyrrolate-indacaterol/Utibron, tiotropium-olodaterol/Stiolto, aclidinium-formoterol** — A-grade TONADO/SHINE trials vs monotherapy.
- **Triple LAMA+LABA+ICS:**
  - **Umeclidinium-vilanterol-fluticasone/Trelegy** — A-grade IMPACT trial (Lipson 2018 NEJM).
  - **Glycopyrrolate-formoterol-budesonide/Breztri** — A-grade ETHOS trial.
- **ICS-LABA: budesonide-formoterol/Symbicort** — A-grade for selected eosinophilic phenotype.
- **Short-acting reliever (SABA): albuterol/Ventolin** — symptomatic, frequent use = exacerbation marker.
- **Oral prednisolone for exacerbations** — 30-40 mg × 5-7 days (REDUCE trial — 5 days non-inferior to 14 days).
- **Oral antibiotics for exacerbations** — NICE 2018 — azithromycin/amoxicillin/doxycycline first-line.
- **Home oxygen LTOT** — for severe hypoxemia SpO2 ≤88% chronic — NOTT trial A-grade mortality reduction.
- **Non-invasive ventilation BiPAP** — for hypercapnic respiratory failure (acute exacerbation or chronic stable).
- **Formal pulmonary rehab** — A-grade Lacasse 2009 Lancet Cochrane — 12-week program (PT + OT + respiratory therapist + psychologist + dietitian).
- **Lung volume reduction surgery (LVRS)** — NETT trial (Fishman 2003 NEJM) — selected upper-lobe emphysema with low exercise capacity.
- **Endobronchial valves (Zephyr)** — A-grade LIBERATE trial (Criner 2018) — selected hyperinflated emphysema.
- **Lung transplant** — for end-stage; LAS-listed.
- **Smoking-cessation Rx:**
  - **Varenicline/Chantix** — A-grade Anthonisen 2005 + Aubin 2006 (combo with NRT often superior).
  - **Bupropion** — alternative.
  - **NRT combo (patch + lozenge/gum)** — A-grade.
- **Formal pulmonology + smoking-cessation specialist consultation** — high-leverage.

## 12. Implementation Timeline (12 months)

### 12.1 Month 0-1 — foundations

Continue your prescribed inhaler regimen. Continue LTOT if prescribed. Start smoking cessation if relevant (NRT + app + GP discussion re: varenicline) — single biggest lever. Buy pulse-oximeter + peak-flow meter + home spirometer + HEPA purifier. Establish baseline CAT, mMRC, FEV1, SpO2, weight. Co-author written action-plan with GP. Start Combo 1 (NAC + omega-3 + D3 + Mg AM). Start home pulmonary rehab walking 5-10 min daily.

### 12.2 Month 2-3 — full stack

Add Combo 2 (NAC second dose, Vasaka, curcumin). Add Ayurvedic anchor (Sitopaladi + Pippali + Tulsi). Walking up to 20-30 min daily. Add resistance training 2×/week. Start MBSR or CBT-dyspnoea app daily.

### 12.3 Month 4-6 — refinement

Reassess CAT, mMRC, FEV1, 6-MW, BODE. Walking 30-45 min. Resistance training 2-3×/week. Add TCM cycling (Bu Fei Tang or Yu Ping Feng San) weeks 1 & 4. If well-controlled, **in conversation with pulmonologist**, may consider stepping back inhaler at user's pace if A-grade DMARD analog (rare in COPD — usually maintenance lifelong).

### 12.4 Month 7-12 — maintenance

Quarterly CAT + BODE + DTC labs (CBC, LFTs, ferritin, 25-OH-D, lipid, HbA1c, TSH). Annual LDCT if heavy-smoker history. Annual DXA if steroid history. Vaccination schedule maintained (annual flu, RSV, COVID per WHO; pneumococcal as scheduled).

### 12.5 Quarterly self-audit

CAT, mMRC, FEV1 trend, SpO2 trend, exacerbation count, reliever use, weight, BMI, supplement adherence %.

### 12.6 Annual deep audit

DTC labs: CBC, LFTs, ferritin, 25-OH-D, lipid, HbA1c, TSH, B12. Annual LDCT if heavy-smoker history. Annual DXA if steroid history. Annual ophthalmology (cataract risk on chronic ICS/steroid). Spirometry FEV1 decline rate. BODE trend.

### 13.5 Patient-facing primer (load-bearing)

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a chronic lung disease caused mainly by smoking (or indoor biomass cooking-smoke in low-income settings), with chronic airway inflammation that produces breathlessness on exertion that progressively limits activity. The conventional path — long-acting inhalers (LAMA like tiotropium, LAMA+LABA combos, triple-therapy for frequent exacerbators), short-acting reliever inhaler, home oxygen if you're hypoxemic, pulmonary rehabilitation, smoking-cessation pharmacotherapy if needed — is genuinely A-grade evidence-based and saves lives. We do NOT recommend declining inhalers or home oxygen in favour of natural-only therapy. What this protocol adds is the layer that conventional care under-delivers: smoking cessation as the single biggest lever (the Lung Health Study followed people for 14.5 years and the quitters lived significantly longer), N-acetylcysteine 600 mg twice daily (three large RCTs — BRONCUS, PANTHEON, BREATHE — show A-grade exacerbation reduction in chronic-bronchitis phenotype), vitamin D if you're deficient (Martineau 2017 IPD-meta), home pulmonary rehab progression you build over weeks (walking + breathing exercises + resistance training), Buteyko-style breath retraining, MBSR for the dyspnoea-anxiety spiral, nutrition support to prevent the cachexia that kills more advanced-COPD users than airflow obstruction does (protein 1.2-1.5 g/kg + caloric adequacy + Mediterranean pattern), classical Ayurvedic Vasaka (a real bronchodilator with monograph backing), and proactive comorbidity screening (CVD, lung cancer LDCT if heavy-smoker history, OSA, depression, osteoporosis). You need a written exacerbation action-plan co-authored with your GP — do not self-initiate antibiotics or oral steroids without that plan. You are sovereign over your body and your healing path; the protocol does not require you to be in any particular clinic, but it does respect that inhalers genuinely work and that smoking cessation literally adds years of life. Red flags: severe exacerbation (cyanosis, accessory muscle use, silent chest, SpO2 <88%, altered consciousness), pneumothorax, massive haemoptysis, new ankle swelling — these need urgent assessment.

## 14. Closing paradigm

The paradigm here: COPD is a chronic progressive lung disease where the conventional path of inhaled bronchodilators (LAMA + LABA + ICS combos), reliever inhaler, home oxygen for severe hypoxemia, pulmonary rehab, smoking-cessation pharmacotherapy, and the surgical/valve options for selected emphysema is genuinely A-grade evidence-based and we honour this — we explicitly recommend continuing your prescribed regimen. The integrative adjunct layer (smoking cessation per Anthonisen 2005 14.5y Lung Health Study — single largest mortality lever, NAC 600 mg BID per BRONCUS + PANTHEON + BREATHE Cochrane — the strongest non-pharm exacerbation reducer in chronic-bronchitis phenotype, vitamin D in deficient subgroup per Martineau 2017 IPD-meta, omega-3, curcumin, magnesium, classical Ayurvedic Vasaka with AYUSH monograph + Sitopaladi + Pippali, TCM Bu Fei Tang + Yu Ping Feng San cycling, home pulmonary rehab progression per Holland 2017, Buteyko breath retraining per Cooper 2003, MBSR for dyspnoea-anxiety per Chan 2015, nutrition support to prevent cachexia per Schols 2014 ERS, vaccination schedule per global consensus, HEPA bedroom purifier, proactive comorbidity screening for CVD + lung cancer LDCT + OSA + depression + osteoporosis) reduces exacerbation frequency, slows FEV1 decline, improves quality of life and may extend survival beyond what inhalers alone achieve. We are honest about what we do not recommend: Ma Huang/Ephedra (CV risk + legal restriction), beta-carotene high-dose in smokers (ATBC + CARET lung cancer harm), vaping for "cessation" in COPD users (continues lung exposure), opioids/sedatives without respiratory monitoring, smoking while on home oxygen (FIRE risk absolute). A clinician-validated written exacerbation action-plan is mandatory before self-initiating antibiotics or oral steroids — surface this honestly. **No FDA / NIH-NHLBI / ATS / GOLD-US-arm / AAFP / CDC is cited as authority** on whether something works — GOLD international framework is used as global synthesis only. Sources: WHO TCIM, AYUSH/CCRAS/CCRH/CCRYN/CCRUM/CCRS/PCIMH/ICMR, EMA HMPC, TGA + NHMRC, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia, Health Canada NHP, Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard, IMPCOPS, NICE UK as global synthesis, NELSON trial for LDCT international anchor, classical Charaka/Bhavaprakasha/Huang Di/Shang Han Lun/Avicenna. Sovereignty over your lungs means: stop smoking (the biggest single lever there is), continue your inhalers, layer NAC + nutrition + pulmonary rehab + breath retraining + vaccination, screen the comorbidities, and reject the binary "drugs only" or "natural only" framings — the truth is integration.
