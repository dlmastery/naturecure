## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What emotional eating actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Emotional eating isn't weakness or "lack of willpower" — it is the predictable output of four broken loops in the body: cortisol won't switch off, your blood sugar is whipsawing, your dopamine reward system is over-fired, and your gut bacteria aren't sending fullness signals. You can rebuild each loop at home. *(Adam & Epel 2007 cortisol-eating; Stice & Yokum 2016 dopamine; Cryan & Dinan 2012 gut-brain)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: eat dinner sitting down, no screen, chew each bite 20 times + write tomorrow's three biggest stressors on paper + caffeine cut-off 14:00. This week: protein + fibre at every meal (target 30 g protein breakfast, 15 g fibre lunch) + 10-min post-meal walks + download a mindful-eating app (Eat Right Now by Judson Brewer, or Am I Hungry? by Michelle May) + book a direct-to-consumer thyroid + ferritin + HbA1c + vitamin D panel. *(Kristeller MB-EAT meta; Brewer 2018 Eat Right Now trial; Cochrane mindful-eating)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer

The mainstream story silos emotional eating as "a willpower problem" or "you need lisdexamfetamine for BED" — which misses four upstream drivers. A more honest reframe: persistent emotional eating is the output of (i) **HPA-axis hyperactivity** (chronic cortisol → visceral fat → leptin resistance → carb-seeking via NPY), (ii) **blood-sugar volatility** (refined-carb spike → insulin surge → reactive hypoglycaemia at 90-180 min → adrenaline-driven "I need sugar NOW"), (iii) **dopamine reward dysregulation** (hyperpalatable ultra-processed food → mesolimbic supra-physiological spike → D2 downregulation → tolerance → escalating intake), and (iv) **gut-brain satiety failure** (microbiome dysbiosis → low GLP-1/PYY/leptin sensitivity → "still hungry after a full meal"). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs mindful-eating training + CBT-E + DBT-distress-tolerance (the A-grade behavioural layers) with HPA-quieting adaptogens, blood-sugar-stabilising fibre + Gymnema + Berberine, dopamine re-sensitisation, and microbiome repair hits all four drivers in parallel — without chasing weight loss via GLP-1 injections.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four causes drive emotional eating — runaway cortisol, whipsawing blood sugar, over-fired dopamine, and a quiet gut that can't say "I'm full". Naming them lets every food, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(Adam & Epel 2007; Stice & Yokum 2016; Cryan & Dinan 2012)*

### 2.2 Runaway HPA cortisol axis

> **For you, in plain words.** Chronic stress, sleep loss and worry pump cortisol that you feel as PM/evening eating, comfort-food cravings, and visceral belly fat. Ashwagandha is the most-tested natural cortisol-lowerer; pranayama and Yoga Nidra are A-grade non-drug levers. *(Chandrasekhar 2012; Lopresti 2019 meta; Adam & Epel 2007)*

#### Open the science

Chronic psychosocial stress and sleep deprivation elevate cortisol with flattened diurnal slope. Cortisol drives visceral adipose deposition (Bjorntorp 2001), insulin resistance, and CNS reward sensitisation to high-calorie palatable foods ("comfort food" circuit; Adam & Epel 2007 *Physiol Behav*). Leptin signalling becomes blunted; NPY rises and drives carbohydrate seeking (Heilig 2004). Targeted by **Ashwagandha KSM-66** (Chandrasekhar 2012, Lopresti 2019 meta — cortisol -28%, food cravings ↓), **Rhodiola SHR-5**, **Bacopa**, **Magnolia + Phellodendron (Relora)** (Talbott 2013 stress-eating cohort), **pranayama (Anulom Vilom, Bhramari)**, **4-7-8 breath before meals** (Weil protocol), **Yoga Nidra** (Bihar School), **cold-water face splash**, **HRV biofeedback** (Lehrer 2014 meta), **consumer non-invasive VNS** (Nurosym, Sensate), **sleep restoration to ≥7 h**. **Synthesis.** Without HPA quieting, cravings recur at every life-stress spike.

### 2.3 Whipsawing blood sugar

> **For you, in plain words.** Refined-carb meals spike insulin, then crash blood sugar 90-180 minutes later. The crash feels like shakiness, anxiety, and "I need something sweet RIGHT NOW". Fibre + protein-first eating + Gymnema + Berberine stop the crash. A 2-week home CGM trial often reveals the pattern. *(Mitrou 2010 vinegar; Baskaran 1990 Gymnema; Cochrane berberine reviews)*

#### Open the science

Post-prandial glucose spikes from refined carbs + sugar trigger an insulin surge that overshoots, producing reactive hypoglycaemia 90-180 min later with counter-regulatory adrenaline + glucagon + cortisol release. This cascade is felt as shakiness, anxiety, irritability, "I need sugar NOW", brain fog. Chronic repetition cements the behavioural craving loop and primes insulin resistance. Targeted by **fibre-anchoring meals** (15+ g fibre/meal), **protein-first / vegetable-first eating order** (Shukla 2015 NY trial), **vinegar pre-meal** (15 ml ACV in 200 ml water; Mitrou 2010 *Diabetes Care*), **Gymnema sylvestre 200-400 mg pre-meal** (Indian RCTs — blunts sweet taste perception 60-90 min; Baskaran 1990, Shanmugasundaram 1990), **berberine 500 mg three times daily with meals** (Chinese metabolic-syndrome RCTs; Yin 2008 meta — equivalent to metformin for HbA1c reduction), **cinnamon 1-3 g (Ceylon only)** (Khan 2003 Pakistani RCT), **post-meal 10-min walk**, **eliminating refined sugar + HFCS + ultra-processed food**, **consumer CGM 2-week trial** (Stelo, Lingo, Levels, Nutrisense, Veri, Dexcom personal — direct-to-consumer in most jurisdictions). **Synthesis.** Stable blood sugar removes the metabolic driver of "I need something sweet NOW".

### 2.4 Over-fired dopamine reward

> **For you, in plain words.** Hyperpalatable ultra-processed foods (engineered to maximise the sugar-fat-salt "bliss point") hijack your dopamine system the way addictive drugs do. Tolerance builds; you need more for the same reward. A 2-4 week ultra-processed palate reset is the most powerful single move you can make. *(Stice & Yokum 2016; Volkow 2013 food-addiction overlap; Hall 2019 ultra-processed RCT)*

#### Open the science

Hyperpalatable ultra-processed foods (engineered to maximise the sugar-fat-salt bliss-point; Moss 2013 *Salt Sugar Fat*) drive supra-physiological mesolimbic dopamine release exceeding evolutionary food rewards. Repeated exposure → D2 receptor downregulation → tolerance → escalating intake required for the same hit (Stice & Yokum 2016 *Curr Opin Behav Sci*). Hall 2019 NIH metabolic ward RCT showed ultra-processed diet drove ~500 kcal/day higher intake vs matched unprocessed (US-origin trial used as evidence object, not authority). Mirrors substance-use disorder neurobiology in fMRI (Volkow cohorts treated as evidence objects). Targeted by **L-tyrosine 500-1000 mg AM** (dopamine precursor; Israeli + Dutch stress-cognitive trials), **L-theanine + caffeine AM** (smoothed reward; Kakuda 2011), **N-acetylcysteine (NAC) 600 mg twice daily** (modulates nucleus accumbens glutamate; Australian Berk + Brazilian addiction trials), **morning sunlight + outdoor exercise** (dopamine + circadian), **novelty engagement** (new hobby, new music — natural dopamine), **cold immersion** (acute dopamine surge; Šrámek 2000 Czech cold-water trial showed 2.5x dopamine), **gradual reduction of ultra-processed exposure** (palate reset 2-4 weeks), **Buddhist mindful-eating practices** (chew 20-30×; engage all senses; gratitude pause; Brewer 2018 Eat Right Now), **DBT-distress-tolerance** for high-craving moments (Linehan; Telch 2001 BED trial). **Synthesis.** Re-sensitising dopamine to whole-food rewards is the addiction-pattern lever.

### 2.5 Gut-brain satiety failure

> **For you, in plain words.** Your gut bacteria control whether you feel full. Ultra-processed food and antibiotics wreck the bacteria that release the "stop eating" hormones GLP-1 and PYY. Fibre 35-45 g/day + fermented foods + 30+ plants per week rebuild them. *(Cryan & Dinan 2012 Nature; Spector ZOE Predict; Cani 2007 Akkermansia)*

#### Open the science

Microbiome composition shapes satiety. *Akkermansia muciniphila* induces GLP-1 (Cani 2007 Belgian work). *Lactobacillus gasseri* modulates leptin sensitivity. SCFA producers (*Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, *Eubacterium*) activate FFAR2/3 → PYY + GLP-1 release. Vagal afferents from gut to NTS signal fullness. Dysbiosis + ultra-processed-food-driven Proteobacteria expansion + LPS leak distort these signals — you don't *feel* full. Targeted by **fibre anchoring 35-45 g/day** (most adults get 12 g — this gap is enormous; UK SACN 2015), **fermented foods 100-150 g daily** (kanji, sauerkraut, A2 kefir, kimchi, coconut kefir, fresh A2 dahi; Wastyk 2021 Stanford fermented-food trial showed 19-strain diversity increase), **prebiotic foods** (chicory root, jicama, garlic, onion, leek, green banana resistant starch), **multi-strain probiotic 20-50B CFU** (Visbiome, Bio-K+, Florastor, Megaspore), **polyphenol diversity** (30+ plants/week — Tim Spector ZOE Predict target), **bone broth + L-glutamine + zinc-carnosine for barrier**, **eliminating emulsifiers (carrageenan, polysorbate-80, CMC) + artificial sweeteners (sucralose, aspartame) + seed oils + gluten in sensitive individuals**. **Synthesis.** Microbiome-driven satiety recovery removes the "I'm still hungry after a full meal" signal.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Fifteen over-the-counter molecules, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest cautions on chromium (Cochrane null for cravings), garcinia/HCA (Cochrane null for weight, still heavily marketed), and 5-HTP (NEVER with SSRIs/SNRIs/MAOIs — serotonin syndrome risk). *(Cochrane Pittler 2002 chromium; Cochrane Onakpoya 2011 garcinia; EMA serotonin-syndrome guidance)*

### 3.2 Ashwagandha — the cortisol-lowerer

> **For you, in plain words.** Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera, KSM-66 standardised root extract) is the most-tested adaptogen for the cortisol that drives stress-eating. Lopresti 2019 meta-analysis (5 RCTs) showed ~28% cortisol reduction and Choudhary 2017 showed food-craving reduction in stressed overweight adults. AYUSH and Australian TGA monographed. *(Chandrasekhar 2012; Lopresti 2019 meta; Choudhary 2017 Australia)*

#### Open the science

Withanolides (withanoside IV, withanoside VI, withaferin A) modulate GABAergic and cortisol signalling. Chandrasekhar 2012 (RCT, n=64, KSM-66 300 mg twice a day, cortisol -28%, PSS -44%); Choudhary 2017 (Australian trial in stressed overweight adults, n=52, food-craving score reduced, weight + waist dropped). Standardised to ≥5% withanolides. **Caution: pregnancy (excluded), hyperthyroidism (can raise T4), autoimmune (theoretical immune modulation), sedative additivity.** **Grade A** for cortisol reduction; Grade B for food-craving reduction.

### 3.3 Gymnema sylvestre — the sweet-taste blocker

> **For you, in plain words.** Gymnema (Meshashringi) is an Ayurvedic herb whose gymnemic acids bind directly to sweet-taste receptors on the tongue and block sweet perception for 60-90 minutes. Take 15 minutes before a sweet-trigger meal. Indian RCTs and AYUSH-CCRAS monograph. *(Baskaran 1990; Shanmugasundaram 1990; CCRAS Meshashringi monograph)*

#### Open the science

Gymnemic acids structurally resemble glucose and competitively bind T1R2/T1R3 sweet-taste receptors, blunting sweet taste perception for 60-90 min. Also has insulin-sensitising effects via β-cell preservation in animal models. Baskaran 1990 (Indian RCT in type-2 diabetes); Shanmugasundaram 1990 (insulin-resistance trial). AYUSH-CCRAS monograph for Meshashringi. Standardised to 25% gymnemic acids. **Grade B** for sweet-craving reduction and adjunct glycaemic control.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six healing traditions named "false hunger from disturbed mind" centuries before modern psychiatry. ## 5. Biophysical & Behavioural Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** The single highest-evidence layer for emotional eating is NOT a supplement — it is **mindful-eating training (MB-EAT) + CBT-E + DBT-distress-tolerance**. Devices come second (HRV biofeedback, CGM, weighted blanket, sleep tracker). *(Kristeller MB-EAT meta; Fairburn CBT-E; Telch 2001 DBT BED)*

**Quick start, tonight:**
1. Eat dinner sitting down, no screen, chew each bite 20-30 times. Notice taste, texture, fullness signals.
2. Before any unplanned eating, ride the urge 90 seconds with paced breathing (5-second inhale, 5-second exhale) — most urges peak and fall in 90 sec.
3. Download **Eat Right Now** (Judson Brewer, free trial then paid) OR **Am I Hungry?** (Michelle May, free workbook).

### 5.2 MB-EAT — Mindfulness-Based Eating Awareness Training

> **For you, in plain words.** Jean Kristeller's MB-EAT is an 8-12 week mindful-eating training programme with A-grade meta-analytic evidence for binge eating, emotional eating, and obesity. The Brewer Eat Right Now app delivers it in self-guided form. *(Kristeller 2014 meta; Brewer 2018 Eat Right Now RCT)*

**Practical.** Kristeller workbook (*The Joy of Half a Cookie*); Brewer's Eat Right Now app (smartphone, self-guided 28-day programme); Mindful Eating-Conscious Living workshops (online). 20-30 min daily for 8-12 weeks. **Grade A** for emotional eating + binge eating.

### 5.3 CBT-E — Enhanced Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

> **For you, in plain words.** Christopher Fairburn's CBT-E (Oxford) is the gold-standard psychological treatment for ALL eating disorders. Self-guided workbook + free Oxford CREDO online training resources. *(Fairburn CBT-E; Fairburn 2009 Lancet)*

**Practical.** Fairburn *Overcoming Binge Eating* (2nd edition, library-free); CREDO Oxford CBT-E online resources; CCI (Centre for Clinical Interventions, Australia) free CBT workbooks for binge eating. **Grade A** — the highest-evidence non-drug intervention for eating disorders. For severe BED or bulimia, a credentialed therapist is preferred (the user decides).

### 5.4 DBT for distress-tolerance and binge prevention

> **For you, in plain words.** Marsha Linehan's Dialectical Behaviour Therapy adapted by Debra Safer (Stanford) for binge eating — Cochrane-level evidence for BED. Self-guided workbook works for milder cases. *(Telch 2001 DBT-BED RCT; Safer 2009 DBT-Eating Disorders workbook)*

**Practical.** Safer, Telch & Chen *Dialectical Behavior Therapy for Binge Eating and Bulimia*; DBT-skills app (free); CCI Australia DBT workbook free. **Grade A** for BED. Distress-tolerance skills (TIPP — Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation) are the acute craving intervention.

### 5.5 Intuitive Eating & HAES

> **For you, in plain words.** Tribole & Resch's *Intuitive Eating* is the most-translated framework for re-learning hunger and fullness cues without diet rules. B-grade evidence (Van Dyke 2014 review). Pairs with Health at Every Size (HAES). *(Tribole & Resch Intuitive Eating; Van Dyke 2014 review)*

**Practical.** Tribole & Resch *Intuitive Eating* (4th edition + workbook); Christy Harrison *Anti-Diet*; HAES community resources. **Grade B**. **Honest caveat**: Intuitive Eating is better for restriction-recovery cases; for active dopamine-reward addiction patterns to ultra-processed foods, an explicit palate-reset window often works better first.

### 5.6 Self-compassion training

> **For you, in plain words.** Kristin Neff's self-compassion practice reduces the shame-binge-shame loop that drives emotional eating in many. B-grade evidence; free self-compassion exercises and guided audio. *(Neff 2011 Self-Compassion; Adams & Leary 2007 self-compassion eating)*

**Practical.** Neff *Self-Compassion: The Proven Power of Being Kind to Yourself*; self-compassion.org free audio; Mindful Self-Compassion 8-week course (in-person or online). **Grade B** for emotional eating + body-image distress.

### 5.7 Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) for BED

> **For you, in plain words.** Wilfley's IPT for binge eating disorder — A-grade Cochrane-level evidence equivalent to CBT-E for BED. Focuses on the interpersonal triggers behind eating. *(Wilfley 2002 IPT-BED; Cochrane Hilbert 2019)*

### 5.11 Sleep tracker as appetite-regulation lever

> **For you, in plain words.** Spiegel & Van Cauter showed one night of <5 h sleep raises hunger hormone ghrelin 28% and lowers satiety hormone leptin 18%. Track sleep with Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch, RingConn, Fitbit — protecting sleep is direct cravings-protection. *(Spiegel 2004; Taheri 2004)*

> **For you, in plain words.** A 5-9 kg weighted blanket triggers deep-pressure proprioception that calms the autonomic nervous system — small trials show real anxiety and sleep benefit. Anxiety + sleep are upstream of emotional eating. *(Ekholm 2020 Swedish trial)*

> **For you, in plain words.** A 30-60 second cold face splash or shower triggers vagal mammalian dive reflex AND a 2.5x dopamine spike that competes with food reward. Fast acute craving reset. *(Buijze 2016; Šrámek 2000 Czech cold trial)*

**Practical.** Cold-water face splash at the sink — 30 sec, AM + PM + at any craving moment. Build to 60-90 sec cold shower. **Do not do** if you have significant cardiovascular disease. **Grade B** for acute vagal + dopamine reset.

> **For you, in plain words.** 20 min in a home sauna at 60-70°C, 3×/week, followed by 30-60 sec cold splash — supports vagal tone, sleep, and stress resilience. *(Finnish Laukkanen 2018 sauna cohort)*

### 5.16 What to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Restrictive diets (fuel the binge-restrict cycle); compensatory exercise after eating (eating-disorder pattern); diet pills (DMAA, ephedra, phentermine — cardiac risk); ultra-low-calorie cleanses; "detox" teas with senna (laxative-abuse pattern); social-media "what I eat in a day" content (drives comparison + restriction). *(WHO obesity 2024; Tribole & Resch; eating-disorder literature)*

> **For you, in plain words.** A Mediterranean-MIND base with explicit **protein-first, fibre-anchored** structure at every meal. 30 g protein at breakfast collapses morning hunger; 15 g fibre at lunch stops the 3 PM crash. ## 11. Clinical Scoring + Comorbidity Screening

### 11.1 What to measure, in plain words

### 11.2 Self-administered scoring

> **For you, in plain words.** The main score is EES (Emotional Eating Scale, Arnow 1995) — 25 items scored 0-4; track at home; ≥17 = elevated. Plus daily 0-10 craving VAS. *(Arnow 1995)*
