## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What T1D actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Your immune system attacked the insulin-making beta cells in your pancreas. That damage is permanent — no food, supplement, fast or breath in this document brings them back. What you CAN do is run the world around your insulin so it works as cleanly as possible, smoothing your time-in-range and protecting your eyes, kidneys, nerves and heart for decades. *(DCCT 1993 NEJM; EDIC follow-up Nathan 2005)*

### 1.2 The five levers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** Tonight: CGM on (Dexcom G7, Libre 3, or Medtronic Guardian) + linked to phone. This week: structured carb-counting + correction-factor diary; cut liquid sugar; 10-20 min walk after every meal. This month: trial Mediterranean/lower-carb pattern (60-120 g carbs/d per CGM response); add resistance training 2x/week with pre-exercise basal -30 to -50% from 60-90 min prior; re-run A1c at 12 weeks via DTC. Highest-evidence non-insulin levers in T1D. *(DIAMOND 2017 JAMA; TYPEONEGRIT Lennerz 2018; ISPAD 2022)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

T1D is usually framed as a one-way arithmetic problem: count carbs, dial insulin, accept the chaos. A more honest reframe: T1D is the **end-organ consequence of one absolute permanent deficit (no endogenous insulin) running through five surrounding daily systems** — (i) **food pattern** (size + predictability of post-prandial excursions); (ii) **insulin-delivery system** (MDI → CSII pump → hybrid closed-loop); (iii) **movement pattern** (exercise = sensitivity friend AND hypoglycemia trigger); (iv) **sleep, stress, circadian + dawn phenomenon**; and (v) **autoimmune comorbidity ring** (celiac, Hashimoto's, Addison's, vitiligo). **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs CGM-paired learning + structured carb-counting + resistance + Zone 2 with pre-exercise basal reductions + sleep/vagal repair + thoughtful OTC + traditional-medicine layer hits all five systems in parallel — letting your insulin work as cleanly and precisely as possible.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (5-node)

### 2.1 Five systems — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Five systems surround your insulin — food pattern, insulin delivery, movement, sleep + stress, and the autoimmune-comorbidity ring. Naming them lets every meal, walk, breath and supplement aim at the right one. *(DCCT 1993; ISPAD 2022; Hotamisligil 2017 inflammation)*

### 2.2 Food pattern

> **For you, in plain words.** Carb-counting accuracy and the fat-and-protein content of each meal determine whether your insulin lands cleanly or you spike + crash. Lower-carb, higher-fibre meals give the cleanest CGM curves. *(Lennerz 2018 TYPEONEGRIT; Krebs 2016 low-carb T1D RCT)*

**The deeper layer.** Every gram of carb must be matched by an insulin dose calculated against an I:C ratio that varies by meal-of-day, prior exercise, illness, menstrual cycle, alcohol, sleep, stress. Fat + protein blunt and extend the curve (pizza effect — late-rising glucose 3-6 h post-meal) — requires split-dose or extended bolus. Lower-carb patterns (50-150 g/d) reduce carb-counting error magnitude (10% error on 30 g = 3 g vs 12 g on 120 g) and meaningfully smooth time-in-range per TYPEONEGRIT (Lennerz 2018, n=316) + Krebs 2016 small RCT. **Synthesis.** Smaller, predictable carb loads with adequate fat, fibre and protein give your insulin the best landing.

### 2.3 Insulin delivery system

> **For you, in plain words.** Multiple-daily-injections (MDI) with a pen, an insulin pump (CSII), or a hybrid closed-loop pump-and-CGM combo each give different precision. The CGM-paired hybrid closed-loop is the modern gold standard for time-in-range. *(iDCL Brown 2019 NEJM; STeP-On; FLAIR; Bionic Pancreas Pilot 2022)*

**The deeper layer.** Three-tier progression: (1) **MDI** = long-acting basal (glargine/Lantus, detemir/Levemir, degludec/Tresiba) + rapid-acting prandial (aspart/Novolog, lispro/Humalog, glulisine/Apidra, faster aspart/Fiasp, Lyumjev) — entry standard; (2) **CSII** = pump (Tandem t:slim X2, Omnipod 5, Medtronic 780G, iLet) delivering rapid-acting only at programmable basal + user boluses; (3) **Hybrid closed-loop** = pump + CGM + algorithm auto-adjusting basal every 5 min — A-grade time-in-range vs MDI/CSII per iDCL (Brown 2019 NEJM), STeP-On, FLAIR, iLet Pivotal (Russell 2022). DIY-loop (Loop, AndroidAPS, OpenAPS) = off-label hobbyist closed-loop with strong real-world signal. **Synthesis.** Every step up = more time-in-range with less mental load; coordinate delivery upgrades with your endocrinologist.

### 2.4 Movement pattern

> **For you, in plain words.** Exercise opens GLUT4 channels in muscle and gives you 24-72 hours of better insulin sensitivity — but it also causes hypoglycemia during and post-exercise, sometimes overnight. The skill is pre-exercise basal reduction + intra-exercise carb backup + post-exercise CGM-paired vigilance. *(Riddell 2017 Lancet T1D exercise consensus; ISPAD 2022)*

**The deeper layer.** Aerobic/Z2 lowers glucose during + after (sensitivity uplift + muscle uptake); resistance raises during (catecholamine surge) but improves overnight sensitivity. Lift-before-cardio in combined sessions protects against intra-session hypo. Pre-exercise basal-rate reduction (pump: temp basal -30 to -50% from 60-90 min prior; MDI: lower the relevant bolus) is the core tool. Intra-exercise carb backup (15 g fast carb per 30-min if pre-BG <150 mg/dL). Set CGM low-glucose alarm 70 mg/dL for 12 h post-exercise (overnight late-hypo prevention). **Synthesis.** With right insulin + carb logistics, exercise is the single biggest sensitivity amplifier.

### 2.5 Sleep, stress, circadian, dawn

> **For you, in plain words.** A bad night's sleep raises tomorrow's insulin requirements by ~20%. Cortisol from chronic stress drives hyperglycemia. The dawn phenomenon spikes glucose 3 a.m.-7 a.m. as growth hormone and cortisol rise. *(Spiegel 1999 sleep restriction + insulin sensitivity; Bolli 1984 dawn phenomenon)*

**The deeper layer.** Sleep <6 h acutely cuts insulin sensitivity 18-30% (Spiegel 1999 Lancet) — bigger effect in T1D given absent autonomic compensation. Stress cortisol drives hepatic glucose + muscle IR; meditation/MBSR/breath/sleep attenuate. Dawn phenomenon (3-7 a.m. GH+cortisol surge) handled automatically by hybrid-closed-loop; MDI users adjust basal timing (split degludec AM+PM) or 5 a.m. alarm correction. **Synthesis.** Sleep ≥7 h + stress reduction + clean circadian rhythm meaningfully reduce daily insulin requirements.

### 2.6 Autoimmune comorbidity ring

> **For you, in plain words.** T1D rarely travels alone — celiac, Hashimoto's, Addison's, vitiligo and pernicious anemia all cluster with it. Screening and treating the comorbidities silently improves your glycemia. *(Triolo 2011 T1D autoimmune cluster; Kakleas 2015 paediatric T1D autoimmune review)*

**The deeper layer.** ~10% biopsy-positive celiac (Cohn 2014); ~25% Hashimoto antibodies (Kakleas 2015); ~1-2% Addison's (life-threatening if missed); ~5% vitiligo. Undiagnosed celiac → unexplained hypos (carb malabsorption) + B12/iron/D/Mg deficiencies. Untreated hypothyroidism → weight + IR. Pernicious anemia (autoimmune B12) sneaky in long-standing T1D. **Synthesis.** Annual antibody screen (anti-TTG, TSH + anti-TPO, B12 + intrinsic-factor, ACTH+cortisol if symptomatic) is the autoimmune-ring base layer.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 Twelve OTC molecules — table

> **For you, in plain words.** Twelve over-the-counter molecules with a non-US monograph or named trial behind them. The honest framing: every one of these is a **marginal smoother** on top of insulin — never a substitute. The strongest signal is for vitamin D (if deficient), magnesium (if deficient) and B12 (depleted in long-standing T1D / metformin overlap / celiac overlap). *(EMA monographs; AYUSH/CCRAS; Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020; Cochrane T1D vitamin D mixed)*

### 3.2 The honest caveat — there is no "natural cure" for T1D

> **For you, in plain words.** Methika (Fenugreek), Karela (Bitter Melon) and Gurmar (Gymnema) are marketed in some quarters as "natural insulin" or "diabetes cures." In Type 1 diabetes they are NOT. In T1D — where your pancreas makes no insulin — these herbs can give you a 5-15% marginal smoothing of post-meal glucose curves if your insulin dose is matched correctly. They cannot replace insulin. If you take any of them, REDUCE your insulin dose to match (not the other way around — never skip insulin to "let the herb work"), and watch your CGM for hypoglycemia. *(ISPAD 2022; CCRAS Methika monograph contextualised)*

### 3.4 The Mediterranean / structured-lower-carb base layer

> **For you, in plain words.** Mediterranean / structured-lower-carb is the platform. TYPEONEGRIT (Lennerz 2018, n=316) documented mean A1c 5.67% with low hypos on low-carb T1D. *(Lennerz 2018; Krebs 2016; PREDIMED 2013)*

**Quick start.** Daily floor: 250 g leafy greens + cruciferous, 150 g berries (carb-counted), 30 g raw nuts, 30 ml EVOO, 4 legumes/week (carb-counted), oily fish 3-4×/week, no liquid sugar, no seed oils. Carb-count every meal; smallest predictable load. See §6.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Ayurveda

> **For you, in plain words.** Classical Ayurveda recognises *Madhumeha* ("honey-urine") and within it distinguishes *Sahaja* (congenital / juvenile-onset = T1D-like) from *Apathyaja* (lifestyle-acquired = T2D-like). For T1D-like Sahaja Madhumeha, the classical view is that *Vata-pradhana* prakriti is more vulnerable and that insulin therapy is non-negotiable in the modern context. The OTC formulas here are AYUSH-GMP-certified and self-orderable; classical mercurial / metallic Rasa preparations (Vasanta Kusumakara Rasa, Madhumeha Chintamani Rasa) are excluded from the home protocol because they require expert Rasa-shala pharmacy preparation and have lead / mercury contamination risk. **None of these substitutes for insulin.** *(Charaka Samhita Chikitsa Sthana Ch. 6; CCRAS Ayurveda Formulary of India)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home only

### 5.1 Continuous glucose monitor

> **For you, in plain words.** A small sensor on the back of your upper arm that tells you what every meal, walk, sleep night and stress event does to your blood sugar in real time, with 5-minute granularity. In T1D this is **non-negotiable A-grade**. *(DIAMOND 2017 JAMA; JDRF-CGM-RCT Tamborlane 2008 NEJM)*

**Quick start.** Choose: Dexcom G7 (10-day, smallest, most accurate, best alarms); Abbott FreeStyle Libre 3 (14-day, also excellent); Medtronic Guardian (paired with Medtronic 780G pump). Pair to phone app + smartwatch for trend-arrow visibility. Aim for time-in-range (70-180 mg/dL) >70% within 12 weeks; time-below-range <70 mg/dL <4%; coefficient of variation <36%.

### 5.2 Home A1c kit

> **For you, in plain words.** DTC lab kits let you check your 3-month average at home — useful between endocrinologist visits. *(LetsGetChecked Ireland regulated; Everlywell DTC frameworks)*

Order quarterly: LetsGetChecked HbA1c, Everlywell HbA1c, Thriva UK. Capillary blood spot — mail in — result in 5-7 days. ~$50-80 per kit.

### 5.3 Home ketone meter

> **For you, in plain words.** When you are sick, vomiting, or your CGM shows persistent hyperglycemia >250 mg/dL — check ketones. A urine strip is the floor; a blood beta-hydroxybutyrate meter (Precision Xtra or KetoSens) is the gold standard for DKA prevention. *(ISPAD sick-day rules)*

**Quick start.** Urine ketone strips at home — Ketostix or Bayer Ketone Test Strips ($10-15 box of 50). Blood ketone meter — Abbott Precision Xtra ($30 meter + $2/strip) or KetoSens ($40 meter + $1/strip). If blood beta-hydroxybutyrate >1.5 mmol/L AND glucose >250 mg/dL — escalate. If >3.0 mmol/L — ER NOW.

### 5.4 Home BP cuff

> **For you, in plain words.** HTN is a T1D comorbidity that compounds nephropathy and retinopathy risk. Measure daily AM. *(ACCORD BP; SPRINT contextualised)*

Validated home cuff — Omron 5/7/10 series ($40-80). Target <130/80 for T1D-with-microalbuminuria; <140/90 baseline.

### 5.5 Home lipid panel

DTC lipid panel quarterly via LetsGetChecked, Everlywell or Thriva — track LDL, ApoB, Lp(a), triglycerides, HDL. T1D ages CV risk forward; track it early.

### 5.6 Foot inspection mirror

> **For you, in plain words.** Diabetic foot is amputation risk; daily 30-second self-inspection prevents the wound that costs you a toe. *(IWGDF 2019)*

$10 one-time. Inspect plantar surface every AM. Any wound that won't close in 7 days = podiatry same week. Any redness + warmth + swelling = same-day urgent.

### 5.7 Red-light panel (PBM)

Mito Red Light, Joovv, or PlatinumLED panels — 10-20 min abdomen / pancreas region 3-4×/week. C-grade evidence (small Brazilian trials show insulin-sensitivity signal); harmless adjunct.

### 5.8 Infrared sauna

Sunlighten, Higher Dose, or Plunge Sauna — 20-30 min 3-4×/week. B-grade evidence for endothelial / BP / insulin-sensitivity in T2D extrapolated for T1D. CGM closely after — sweating + dehydration can drop glucose.

### 5.9 Cold immersion

Plunge, Renu Therapy or DIY chest-freezer — 2-5 min 38-50 °F 2-3×/week. C-grade in T1D; cold = catecholamine surge = transient hyperglycemia → CGM after, expect 30-50 mg/dL rise. Useful for adrenal-stress tone.

### 5.10 Hybrid closed-loop awareness (home-deployed)

The hybrid closed-loop systems — Tandem Control-IQ + Dexcom G7, Omnipod 5 + Dexcom G7, Medtronic 780G + Guardian 4, iLet Bionic Pancreas — are consumer-prescribed semi-automated systems used in the home. The evidence (iDCL Brown 2019 NEJM; STeP-On; FLAIR; iLet Pivotal 2022) is A-grade for time-in-range improvement and is referenced in §10.7.3.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 The food pattern

> **For you, in plain words.** A Mediterranean / lower-carb pattern with 50-150 g/d carbs (your CGM tells you what your sweet spot is) gives the cleanest insulin curves. The TYPEONEGRIT users averaged ~36 g carbs/day with A1c 5.67% and low hypos — that's the ceiling case. *(Lennerz 2018 TYPEONEGRIT)*

### 6.2 Daily macros

- Carbohydrate: 50-150 g/d (tune via CGM)
- Protein: 1.2-1.8 g/kg/d
- Fat: balance — emphasise EVOO, nuts, oily fish; avoid industrial seed oils
- Fibre: 30-40 g/d from food

### 6.3 What to add daily

- 250 g leafy greens + cruciferous
- 150 g berries (carb-counted)
- 30 g raw nuts (almonds, walnuts, brazil nuts for selenium)
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil
- Legumes 4 servings/week (carb-counted)
- Oily fish 3-4×/week (sardines, mackerel, salmon)
- 1 fermented food/day (kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi)

### 6.4 What to subtract

- Liquid sugar (sweetened drinks, juice, sweet tea) — single biggest CGM lever
- Ultra-processed foods
- Industrial seed oils (soy, corn, cottonseed)
- Trans fats
- Refined wheat and white rice
- High-glycemic "natural" sweeteners (agave, date syrup, honey large quantity) — same insulin demand as sugar

### 6.5 Sample 3-day rotation (with carb counts)

**Day A — Mediterranean.** Breakfast: 2 eggs + 100 g sauteed greens + 50 g berries + 10 g chia (carb ~12 g). Lunch: 150 g grilled salmon + 200 g roasted vegetables + 30 g chickpeas (carb ~18 g). Dinner: chicken thigh + cauliflower rice + 100 g blueberries (carb ~15 g). Snacks: 30 g almonds + 50 g cheese.

**Day B — Indian-modified.** Breakfast: 2 idli (50 g carb each — pre-bolus 20 min) + sambar + coconut chutney + boiled egg. Lunch: 1 jowar roti (25 g carb) + dal + sabzi + raita. Dinner: paneer-vegetable curry + sauteed greens + small portion brown rice (30 g carb).

**Day C — lower-carb / TYPEONEGRIT-style.** Breakfast: omelette + avocado + greens (carb ~6 g). Lunch: tuna salad + EVOO + nuts (carb ~10 g). Dinner: grilled meat + roasted cruciferous + small berry portion (carb ~15 g). Snacks: nuts + cheese + olives.

### 6.6 Fasting + meal timing — T1D-specific caveats

> **For you, in plain words.** Time-restricted eating (TRE) in T1D is possible but tricky — basal insulin during fasting still drives hypoglycemia risk. Coordinate any fasting trial with your endocrinologist; never on a sulfonylurea (irrelevant for T1D) and never without CGM. *(Furmli 2018 case series T2D fasting; small T1D pilots emerging)*

- **Licorice >100 mg glycyrrhizin/d** — raises BP, lowers K; especially caution Addison overlap.
- **Ephedra / Ma Huang** — CV + glycemic.
- **St. John's Wort** with glycemic herb or psychiatric Rx — unpredictable drug levels.
- **High-glycemic "natural sweeteners"** (agave, date syrup, honey) — same insulin demand as sugar.
- **Bhastrika + Kapalbhati** if HTN — BP spike.
- **Pregnancy:** Vitex, high-dose Fenugreek, Berberine, high-dose vit A — obstetric coordination.
- **DIY-loop without engineering competence** — pump/CGM/phone failures = severe hypo or DKA.

## 13.5 Patient-facing Primer

> **For you, in plain words.** What is going on, what to expect, and what NOT to expect.

You have Type 1 diabetes. Your immune system attacked the insulin-making beta cells, and the damage is permanent — no food, herb, fast or breath brings them back. **Insulin is life-saving. You stay on it.** This dossier runs the world around your insulin so it works cleanly: CGM so you SEE every meal/walk/sleep/stress event, structured carb-counting + dose-adjustment, Mediterranean/lower-carb pattern, resistance + Z2 with pre-exercise basal reduction, sleep + stress repair, and an honest OTC + traditional layer that may marginally smooth glycemia.

What to expect: first six weeks = CGM learning + pattern shift. Week 12 = 0.5-1.5% A1c drop + 10-15-point TIR improvement for committed users. Month 6 = TIR ≥70% with reduced hypo exposure. Hybrid-closed-loop upgrade (endo-coordinated) is the biggest delta-improvement tool.

What NOT to expect: a "cure" or insulin independence. Skipping insulin = DKA = death. Anyone selling a "natural cure" for T1D is selling you a coffin. Real cure-class research = islet transplantation (limited durability), pancreas transplant (ESRD overlap), stem-cell beta cells (Vertex VX-880 — research only 2024-2025) — track via your endocrinologist.

The sovereign ethos: OTC, home-based, independent path that wraps around your insulin + endo team. **Insulin is non-negotiable; everything else is sovereign.** Keep your endo + diabetes-educator + RD + annual eye + podiatry in the loop — CGM data is the shared language.
