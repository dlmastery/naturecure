> **For you, in plain words.** This is a safe, over-the-counter, at-home plan for osteoarthritis — the joint-wear disease that affects knees, hips, hands, spine and shoulders. Lead with the unglamorous high-evidence levers: 5-10% weight loss, structured strength + aqua exercise, tai chi, and cognitive-behavioural pain therapy. Supplements come second. We are honest where the science is mixed — including arthroscopic surgery for degenerative meniscal tears, which the best trials say does NOT beat structured physio. *(WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034; Messier 2013 JAMA IDEA trial; Bartels 2007 Cochrane on aqua-therapy)*
>
> **The frame.** Grounded in WHO TCIM, India's AYUSH (CCRAS, CCRH, CCRUM, CCRS), EMA HMPC monographs, Australia TGA + NHMRC, China NMPA + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020, Health Canada NHP, Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard Dawakhana, IMPCOPS Chennai, plus classical Ayurvedic, Chinese, Unani, Siddha, Tibetan and homeopathic texts. **No US regulator or US disease association is cited as authority.** Where OARSI international guidance is referenced, it is as global synthesis with explicit framing.
>
> **Sovereignty.** Every supplement, food, breath practice and home device here is something you can buy and use yourself. **No prescription, no clinic visit, no surgery is in the home regime.** Topical and oral NSAIDs, intra-articular cortisone, hyaluronic acid injections, in-person physical therapy, and partial/total joint replacement are placed in §10.7.3 as referral context, never inside the daily protocol. The drug column in §10.1 exists only so users **already** on chronic NSAIDs can see how this protocol interacts.
>
> **Red flag — septic or crystal joint.** A single hot, red, exquisitely swollen joint with fever — especially if rapidly worsening over hours to a day — needs joint aspiration to rule out septic arthritis or acute gout/pseudogout. This is the one acute scenario the home stack cannot address. Seek emergency assessment on your own initiative.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What OA actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** Osteoarthritis isn't just "wear and tear" — it's a low-grade systemic inflammation of the whole joint (cartilage, bone, synovium and ligament) driven heavily by body weight, muscle weakness, and gut-immune cross-talk. *(Berenbaum 2013 OA inflammation; Hunter 2019 Lancet)*

### 1.2 The four root drivers

> **Quick start.** Step on a body-composition scale tonight; aim for 5-10% weight loss over 6 months if BMI ≥27; add 3×/week resistance training and 2×/week aqua-walking; start curcumin + Boswellia + collagen peptides; book a tai chi class. These five moves cover the highest-evidence layers. *(Messier 2013 IDEA; Wang 2009 NEJM tai chi; Bannuru 2018 curcumin OA network-meta)*

### 1.3 Why "systemic," not "the bad knee"

OA is best framed as four interlocking root drivers — (i) **mechanical overload** of cartilage from excess weight, weak surrounding muscle, and altered joint mechanics; (ii) **low-grade inflammation** with synovial macrophage activation, IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α and chondrocyte senescence; (iii) **metabolic syndrome / adipokine signalling** — leptin, adiponectin, and visceral fat as cartilage hostile environments (this is why hand OA also tracks with BMI); and (iv) **central pain sensitisation** — the brain learns chronic pain even when the joint settles, especially when sleep is broken and mood is low. **Synthesis.** A home protocol that pairs weight loss, structured exercise (aqua + strength + tai chi), an anti-inflammatory diet, evidence-graded supplements (curcumin, Boswellia, collagen peptides, omega-3), and cognitive-behavioural pain therapy — hits all four drivers simultaneously.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four drivers — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four drivers — mechanical overload, joint inflammation, metabolic syndrome, and central pain sensitisation — explain why OA progresses. Naming each lets every food, exercise and supplement aim at the right one. *(Berenbaum 2013; Glyn-Jones 2015 Lancet)*

### 2.2 Mechanical overload + muscle weakness

> **For you, in plain words.** The cartilage in your knee is taking 3-6 times your body weight every step. Lose 5 kg, save 15-30 kg of joint load with every footfall. *(Felson 1997; Messier 2005)*

**The deeper layer.** Quadriceps weakness precedes radiographic knee OA progression by years (Slemenda 1997). Hip abductor weakness drives medial-knee compartment loading. Walking biomechanics in obesity is altered toward higher impact loads. Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) and frailty compound the load problem. Targeted by **structured resistance training** (compound lifts, leg press, step-ups, glute work — A-grade Bartholdy 2017), **aqua-therapy** (Cochrane Bartels 2007 A-grade — unloading + resistance), **tai chi** (Wang 2009 NEJM A-grade for knee OA), **walking poles** (Crapo 2018 A-grade for unloading), **Iyengar yoga**, **5-10% weight loss** (Messier 2013 JAMA IDEA — single highest-leverage intervention). **Synthesis.** Weight loss + muscle is the upstream brake; without it, every supplement is a footnote.

### 2.3 Joint inflammation + chondrocyte senescence

> **For you, in plain words.** Even in OA, immune cells in the joint lining are pumping out inflammatory signals — and old, "rusted" cartilage cells stop repairing and start damaging. *(Sokolove 2013; McAlindon 2014 OARSI)*

**The deeper layer.** Synovial macrophages (M1-skewed) release IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α; chondrocytes shift to a catabolic phenotype with elevated MMP-13 and ADAMTS-5 degrading aggrecan and type-II collagen. Cellular senescence with SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype) drives the catabolic loop. Targeted by **curcumin** (Bannuru 2018 network-meta — comparable to NSAIDs short-term in OA), **Boswellia serrata** (Sengupta 2010, 2011; Kimmatkar 2003 — improved WOMAC), **omega-3 EPA/DHA** (Goldberg 2007 meta — A-grade for joint pain), **collagen peptides** (Clark 2008 + Dressler 2018 B-grade), **MSM** (Brien 2008 review — moderate), **ginger** (Bartels 2015 small signal), **SAMe** (Soeken 2002 — variable), **glucosamine + chondroitin** (GAIT 2006 — mixed; positive in moderate-severe knee OA, null in mild). **Synthesis.** Inflammation is the upstream signal cartilage hears; quieting it is the second lever after weight.

### 2.4 Metabolic syndrome + adipokine signalling

> **For you, in plain words.** OA is also a metabolic disease. Belly fat releases inflammation that hits joints — which is why even hand OA tracks with body weight, not just knee load. *(Yusuf 2010; Berenbaum 2017 Curr Opin Rheumatol)*

**The deeper layer.** Visceral adipose tissue secretes leptin, resistin, and inflammatory cytokines; adiponectin (anti-inflammatory) drops. Type-2 diabetes, dyslipidaemia and hypertension cluster with OA progression. Insulin resistance impairs chondrocyte glucose handling. Targeted by **Mediterranean / anti-inflammatory diet** (Veronese 2017 + 2019 OA cohort data), **omega-3**, **berberine 500 mg TID** for metabolic overlap (B-grade for metabolic syndrome), **HIIT** + Zone 2 cardio, **time-restricted eating 14:10** if metabolically appropriate, **vitamin D3** (mixed OA data but reasonable in deficiency), **magnesium**. **Synthesis.** Treating OA without treating metabolic syndrome is like mopping with the tap running.

### 2.5 Central pain sensitisation + sleep + mood

> **For you, in plain words.** When joint pain has been there for years, the brain rewires to feel it louder — even when the joint is calmer. Sleep loss and low mood amplify the volume. *(Arendt-Nielsen 2010 OA central sensitisation; Smith 2018 sleep + pain meta)*

**The deeper layer.** Quantitative sensory testing in knee OA shows widespread hyperalgesia and reduced descending pain inhibition. Catastrophising and pain-related fear independently predict disability. Sleep fragmentation lowers pain threshold by 30-50% the next day. Targeted by **cognitive-behavioural therapy for chronic pain (CBT-CP)** (Eccleston Cochrane A-grade), **mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR)** (Hilton 2017 A-grade meta), **pain-neuroscience education** (Lorimer Moseley A-grade), **sleep optimisation** (CBT-i if needed), **graded activity pacing**, **Iyengar yoga** + restorative yoga, **Yoga Nidra**, **acupuncture** (Cochrane Manheimer 2018 modest), **HRV biofeedback**. **Synthesis.** Central pain is the fourth lever — and the most under-treated in conventional OA care.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Ayurveda — Sandhivata

> **For you, in plain words.** Ayurveda calls knee/hip OA *Sandhivata* — vata accumulating in the joints, with depleted ojas and dryness in cartilage. Boswellia (Shallaki) and Guggul-class formulas are the centrepieces. *(Charaka Samhita, Chikitsa 28; Sushruta Samhita, Nidana 1)*

**Quick start.** Yogaraja Guggulu 2 tablets twice daily + Shallaki (Boswellia) 300 mg twice daily + Maharasnadi Quath 15 mL twice daily + Mahanarayan oil daily self-massage of affected joint + warm-water dam (compress). *(Bhavaprakasha; Sahasrayoga)*

#### Open the science

- **Shallaki / Boswellia serrata** — *Bhavaprakasha* lists it as the prime joint herb. Modern AKBA (acetyl-11-keto-β-boswellic acid) is the standardised active.
- **Yogaraja Guggulu** — classical multi-herb compound with Commiphora mukul resin as base; A-grade traditional anchor for Sandhivata. Singh 2003 RCT showed improvement comparable to ibuprofen in knee OA.
- **Trayodashang Guggulu** — for vata-dominant joint with neurological overlap (sciatica).
- **Kaishore Guggulu** — when inflammatory features dominate (heat, swelling).
- **Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)** — adaptogen + mild anti-inflammatory; muscle strength and joint pain RCTs (Ramakanth 2016). KSM-66 600 mg/day.
- **Rasna (Pluchea lanceolata)** — classical anti-Sandhivata herb in Maharasnadi Quath.
- **Sahacharadi Taila** — external warm-oil massage for joint stiffness.
- **Maharasnadi Quath / Kashayam** — Kerala classical decoction.
- **Mahanarayan taila** — multi-herb sesame-oil base for daily self-massage (abhyanga).
- **Janu Basti (knee) / Greeva Basti (cervical)** — home version: ring of dough on joint filled with warm Mahanarayan oil 30 min. Powerful classical local therapy.
- **Nirgundi (Vitex negundo)** — leaves used externally as poultice.
- **Shunthi (Zingiber officinale, dry ginger)** — internally with hot water for digestive-vata.
- **Murivenna** — soft-tissue and ligamentous trauma oil.

- **Causticum** — elderly contractures; tendons shortened; worse in dry cold.
- **Apis mellifica** — warm, swollen, stinging joint pain.
- **Colchicum autumnale** — OA with gout overlap; small-joint pattern.

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered only

### 5.1 Heat & cold

> **For you, in plain words.** Heat for chronic stiff joints, cold for acute swelling and post-exercise. Sunbeam wraps and ice packs are cheap, effective, and entirely under your control. *(Brosseau 2003 Cochrane thermotherapy)*

**Quick start.** Sunbeam moist-heat wrap 20 min before stretching/exercise for stiff joints; ice packs 15-20 min after exercise on a flared joint. Alternate hot-cold contrast bath for hand OA.

#### Open the science

Superficial heat (heat wraps, hot baths, paraffin baths for hand OA) — Cochrane Brosseau 2003 supports modest pain and stiffness relief. Cold packs after exercise or during flare. Paraffin wax bath for hand OA (Ayhan 2009 — improved grip strength + pain). Avoid heat on acutely inflamed joint with significant swelling.

### 5.2 Red-light & PEMF

> **For you, in plain words.** Home red-light/near-infrared panels and PEMF mats have modest but real OA evidence — they're nice add-ons, not the core. *(Tomazoni 2015 PBM knee OA; Bagnato 2016 PEMF knee OA)*

**Quick start.** Mito Red MID or Joov Solo — 10-15 min on the joint, 3-5×/week. PEMF mat (Bemer, Pulse Centers, Healthy Wave) 15 min daily for knee/hip OA.

#### Open the science

PBM at 660 + 810-850 nm shows modest WOMAC improvement in knee OA RCTs (Tomazoni 2015). Home pulsed electromagnetic field (PEMF) — Bagnato 2016 RCT in knee OA showed improvement on WOMAC. Bemer mat 15-30 min daily, or home Pulse Centers unit, or budget Healthy Wave mat. **PEMF contraindication: pacemaker / implanted electrical device, pregnancy.**

### 5.3 Compression, support & TENS

> **For you, in plain words.** Compression sleeves for proprioception, walking poles for unloading, TENS units for symptomatic relief. All consumer-grade, all evidence-supported. *(Cudejko 2018 knee bracing meta; Crapo 2018 poles RCT)*

**Quick start.** Bauerfeind GenuTrain for knee, McDavid or Tommie Copper sleeve for elbow/wrist, walking poles (Black Diamond, Leki) for hip/knee OA, iReliev or Omron TENS for 20-30 min flare-day relief.

#### Open the science

Knee bracing (unloader brace for medial-compartment knee OA, off-loading sleeve for general knee OA) — Cudejko 2018 meta supports modest WOMAC improvement. Walking poles unload knee/hip by up to 20% per step (Bohne 2007). TENS — Cochrane Rutjes 2009 — short-term symptomatic relief; not disease-modifying.

### 5.4 Home cryotherapy & sauna

> **For you, in plain words.** Cold packs for daily use; home infrared sauna 2-3×/week is a systemic adjunct for inflammation and recovery. *(Beever 2009 infrared sauna review)*

#### Open the science

Cold packs (gel packs, ice in cloth, frozen-peas) — 15-20 min, several times daily during flare. Home infrared sauna (Sunlighten Solo, JNH Lifestyles, Clearlight) 20-30 min 2-3×/week — heat-shock proteins, mild HRV improvement, vasodilation; modest pain effect. Caution: hypotension, dehydration.

### 5.5 Aqua-therapy access

> **For you, in plain words.** A community pool is the single best piece of OA gym equipment ever built. Two sessions a week is the prescription. *(Bartels 2007 Cochrane aqua-therapy A-grade)*

#### Open the science

Aqua-therapy / hydrotherapy — A-grade Cochrane evidence (Bartels 2007, Bartels 2016) for knee and hip OA — improved WOMAC pain, function, and walking distance. Water unloads weight while providing variable resistance. Local pool access (YMCA, community centre, masters swim) is the protocol. Self-guided aqua-walking, gentle aqua-aerobics, gentle laps — all count.

### 5.6 Home gait & strength self-monitoring

> **For you, in plain words.** Track your steps, your grip strength, and (if possible) gait quality — these are the numbers that move with OA.

#### Open the science

- Pedometer / step counter (any phone, Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin) — aim 6,000-10,000 steps/day, build gradually (Whitfield 2022 OA step-count cohort).
- Hand-grip dynamometer (Camry, Jamar) — track grip every 4-6 weeks as marker of systemic sarcopenia.
- Smart insoles (Nurvv, Naboso barefoot for proprioception) — emerging, optional.
- 6-minute walk test — measure once per month; standard OA functional outcome.
- Self-WOMAC monthly.
- Sit-to-stand test (30 seconds, how many?) — lower-body strength surrogate.

## 6. Dietary Protocol

### 6.1 Mandatory foods (daily)

> **For you, in plain words.** Eat for the inflamed joint AND the visceral fat — Mediterranean with Indian-spice tempering, oily fish, leafy greens, berries, bone broth, fermented foods. *(Veronese 2017 Mediterranean OA; Calder 2017 nutrition + inflammation)*

#### Open the science

- **150-200 g leafy greens** (spinach, kale, methi, amaranth) — magnesium, folate, polyphenols.
- **120-160 g oily fish** 3×/week — omega-3.
- **30 g nuts** (walnut, almond, pistachio).

### 7.6 Exercise periodization (the A-grade core)

> **For you, in plain words.** Exercise is the single most powerful OA medicine — bigger effect than any supplement. Three layers: aqua, strength, and movement therapy. *(Fransen 2015 Cochrane exercise + knee OA; Bartels 2007 aqua A-grade)*

**Weekly:**
- **Mon** — Aqua-walking + aqua-aerobics 45 min.
- **Tue** — Strength training 45 min (lower-body emphasis — leg press, step-ups, glute bridge, calf raise, seated row).
- **Wed** — Tai chi 45-60 min (group class or online).
- **Thu** — Strength training 45 min (upper-body emphasis — row, push-up, overhead press, biceps + triceps).
- **Fri** — Aqua-walking + aqua-aerobics 45 min.

**Monthly cadence:**
- Week 1 — full volume.
- Week 2 — moderate.
- Week 3 — deload 70% volume, more sleep.
- Week 4 — full volume.

#### 10.7.3 Clinic-route options (if home protocol plateaus)

> **For you, in plain words.** If your home plan plateaus, here are the conventional therapies your prescriber may add. We document them so the home protocol can sit alongside without conflict. **We do not recommend any of these.**

- **Topical NSAIDs** — diclofenac gel (Voltaren) — A-grade Cochrane Derry 2016 — first-choice analgesic in OA. Lower systemic toxicity than oral NSAIDs.
- **Oral NSAIDs** — ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, celecoxib (COX-2 selective; PRECISION trial showed celecoxib non-inferior CV safety vs ibuprofen/naproxen at moderate dose). Always with PPI consideration for chronic use.
- **Acetaminophen / paracetamol** — modest analgesic effect (Machado 2015 BMJ — small effect); often the safer baseline.
- **Intra-articular cortisone injection** — symptomatic relief 4-12 weeks. **Caution: Sheth 2017 JAMA + McAlindon 2017 JAMA showed quarterly cortisone over 2 years accelerated cartilage loss vs saline.** Limit to occasional flare control, not quarterly.
- **Intra-articular hyaluronic acid (viscosupplementation)** — Synvisc, Euflexxa, Durolane — mixed Cochrane evidence; some patients benefit.
- **Platelet-rich plasma (PRP)** — variable evidence; small RCTs positive, larger meta mixed.
- **Prolotherapy** — variable evidence.
- **In-person physical therapy** — OARSI gold-standard for symptomatic OA; home strength + aqua + tai chi from §7 covers most of the same ground.
- **Imaging — MRI** — only when clinically indicated; not for routine OA.
- **Arthroscopic meniscectomy for degenerative meniscal tears** — **HONESTY: ESCAPE 2018 + Katz 2013 NEJM + Sihvonen 2013 NEJM (FIDELITY) — null vs PT for degenerative meniscal tear in older adults. Massively over-prescribed for OA; OARSI 2019 explicitly recommends against. Surface this honestly.**
- **Partial / total knee replacement** — A-grade when conservative exhausted, advanced disease, severe disability. TOPS / KAT trials. Robotic-arm-assisted arthroplasty (Mako) emerging.
- **Total hip replacement** — A-grade outcome data; consider earlier in hip OA due to better outcomes than late.
- **Genicular nerve radiofrequency ablation** — emerging for severe knee OA.
- **Stem-cell injection** — variable evidence; expensive; not first-line.

WOMAC baseline, body-comp scale, walking poles, knee brace if knee OA, Combo 1 + Combo 3 start, weight-loss work begins (Mediterranean + Indian-tempering food pattern), tai chi class or app, aqua-therapy 2×/week.

### 13.2 Month 2-3 — full stack

Add Combo 2, Yogaraja Guggulu evening, MSM if soft-tissue overlap, glucosamine + chondroitin if moderate-severe knee OA, CBT-CP or MBSR app daily, smoking cessation if relevant.

### 13.3 Month 4-6 — refinement

Reassess WOMAC, 6-MWT, grip strength; if controlled, consider NSAID taper in conversation with prescriber; if not controlled, audit adherence + add TCM cycling overlay (Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang).

### 13.4 Month 7-12 — maintenance

Quarterly WOMAC, monthly 6-MWT, annual DXA if osteoporosis risk, joint-replacement conversation if persistent severe disability after 6+ months of compliant home protocol.

### 13.5 Quarterly self-audit

Weight, BMI, waist, BP, WOMAC, 6-MWT, grip, NSAID-days-per-month, supplement adherence %.

### 13.6 Annual deep audit

DTC labs: 25-OH vit D, ferritin, CBC, fasting glucose + HbA1c, lipid panel, TSH, hs-CRP, uric acid; RF + anti-CCP if RA suspicion.

## 14. Closing paradigm

The paradigm here: osteoarthritis is a four-driver systemic disease and the home protocol stacks evidence-graded layers against each driver — weight loss, structured exercise (aqua + strength + tai chi) and cognitive-behavioural pain therapy as the unsung A-grade home cornerstones, a clean supplement layer (curcumin, Boswellia, collagen peptides, omega-3, vitamin D, MSM) sitting on top, classical traditions (Ayurveda Yogaraja Guggulu and Shallaki, TCM Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang, Iyengar yoga + tai chi) honoured where they earn their place, and full transparency about what we don't recommend (arthroscopic meniscectomy for degenerative tears, megadose MSM, magnetic bracelets, chiropractic for OA). The clinic-route options (topical and oral NSAIDs, hyaluronic acid, PRP, joint replacement when warranted) are documented honestly in §10.7.3 so the user already on them sees no conflict, and so the user whose home protocol plateaus knows the gold-standard names. Sovereignty over your joints means: measure them monthly, move them daily, feed them well, sleep them well — and stack only what the evidence justifies.
