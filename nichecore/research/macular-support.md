## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 What macular health actually is

> **For you, in plain words.** The macula is the small bullseye at the centre of your retina that handles reading, faces, and fine detail. Age-related macular degeneration is the slow oxidation + drusen-deposit + complement-driven thinning of this region. It runs on a spectrum from no-AMD-but-genetic-risk through early dry AMD → intermediate dry AMD (where AREDS2 supplementation has the only A-grade nutrition trial) → advanced (dry geographic atrophy OR wet neovascular AMD). The leverage is highest at the early end. *(Mitchell 2018 AMD review; AREDS2 2013)*

### 1.2 The four drivers — at a glance

> **Quick start.** This week: (1) **stop smoking on your own initiative** — by far the biggest modifiable AMD risk factor; (2) **buy UV400 wraparound sunglasses with side coverage** and wear them all bright outdoor time; (3) **eat the Mediterranean Eye Diet** (leafy greens daily, oily fish 2×/wk, nuts, olive oil, berries, beans, whole grains, low red meat); (4) **print the free Amsler grid PDF, tape it to the bathroom mirror, self-test daily — one eye at a time**; (5) **if you have intermediate AMD (clinically confirmed), start the AREDS2 nutrient stack** — lutein 10 mg + zeaxanthin 2 mg + vitamin C 500 mg + vitamin E 400 IU + zinc 80 mg as zinc oxide + copper 2 mg; (6) **measure baseline omega-3 index** (OmegaQuant) and lift to 8-12% with diet ± high-DHA fish oil. These six moves cover the highest-leverage levers. *(AREDS2 2013; Merle Mediterranean Eye Diet 2019; Khan 2006 smoking + AMD; AYUSH Triphala monograph)*

### 1.3 The deeper layer — for those who want it

Macular health with aging is conventionally siloed as either "watch and wait" (early AMD) or, once wet AMD is diagnosed, as monthly-to-quarterly **anti-VEGF intravitreal injections** (ranibizumab/Lucentis, aflibercept/Eylea, brolucizumab/Beovu, faricimab/Vabysmo) — which preserve vision in many but require lifelong clinic visits, do not reverse damage, and carry small injection-related risks (endophthalmitis, retinal detachment). For geographic atrophy (advanced dry), the 2023-2024 complement-pathway therapies (pegcetacoplan/Syfovre, avacincaptad pegol/Izervay) slow lesion growth on OCT but have not yet shown clear functional vision benefit and carry their own injection-related risks. The integrative reframing is different: AMD is the macular expression of **four convergent root drivers** — (i) **oxidative damage** to the macular pigment + photoreceptors, accelerated by UV/visible blue light and smoking, (ii) **complement-driven chronic inflammation** (the CFH and C3 genetic risk pathways), (iii) **macular pigment depletion** (lutein and zeaxanthin in the central macula filter blue light and quench reactive oxygen species), and (iv) **systemic vascular + metabolic insufficiency** — hypertension, dyslipidaemia, smoking-induced endothelial dysfunction, diabetic microangiopathy overlap. **Synthesis.** A natural, OTC, home-based protocol that simultaneously eliminates the smoking + UV + blue-light load, restores macular pigment (AREDS2 in intermediate AMD; lutein + zeaxanthin + Mediterranean Eye Diet across the spectrum), supports anti-inflammatory + omega-3 status, and controls vascular risk hits all four drivers in parallel — independent of whatever clinic-route stage the user is at. The most important home-monitoring tool is the **daily Amsler grid** — the simplest, cheapest, and highest-leverage early-warning test ever devised for macular change.

## 2. Mechanistic Network (4-node)

### 2.1 Four causes — the picture

> **For you, in plain words.** Four mechanisms drive macular aging — oxidation + light damage, complement-driven inflammation, macular pigment depletion, and systemic vascular damage from smoking and metabolic disease. Naming them lets every food, supplement, sunglass and device aim at the right one. *(Mitchell 2018; Anderson 2010 complement in AMD; Bone 2007 macular pigment; Khan 2006 smoking; SanGiovanni 2009)*

### 2.2 Oxidative + light damage

> **For you, in plain words.** The macula sits behind a clear cornea and lens — it gets a daily dose of high-energy blue and UV light. Combined with the macula's enormous oxygen turnover (it has the highest oxygen consumption per gram of any human tissue), this is a constant oxidative load. UV + blue-light damage compounds across decades. *(Beatty 2000 oxidative theory; Sui 2013 UV + AMD)*

#### Open the science

The retina has the highest oxygen consumption per gram of any human tissue (~7× the brain). UV-A, UV-B and high-energy visible (HEV, blue) light photo-oxidise retinal phospholipids, generate reactive oxygen species in RPE cells, and accumulate lipofuscin (the brown wear-and-tear pigment). Smoking adds a vast systemic oxidative load (a single cigarette delivers ~10^14 oxidant molecules). Targeted by **UV400 wraparound sunglasses with side coverage** (the largest single environmental lever), **lutein + zeaxanthin in the central macula** (filter blue light + quench singlet oxygen), **AREDS2 nutrient stack** (vitamin C + vitamin E + lutein + zeaxanthin + zinc + copper), **smoking cessation**, **avoidance of unfiltered welding arcs / direct sun gazing / unprotected snow glare**. **Synthesis.** Reducing the daily photo-oxidative load is the largest environmental lever in this dossier.

### 2.3 Complement-driven inflammation

> **For you, in plain words.** Your immune system has an "old guard" arm called the complement cascade. Genetic variants in CFH (Complement Factor H) and ARMS2 — present in ~30-40% of people of European ancestry — make this cascade over-active in the macula, driving drusen formation, RPE damage, and the slow march of dry AMD. The new complement-inhibitor injections (pegcetacoplan, avacincaptad pegol) target this directly in advanced dry AMD. *(Anderson 2010; Edwards 2005 CFH variant; Klein 2005 CFH AMD)*

#### Open the science

CFH (Complement Factor H) Y402H variant, ARMS2/HTRA1 polymorphisms, and C3 variants confer significantly elevated AMD risk. Drusen — the yellow extracellular deposits between RPE and Bruch's membrane — contain complement components C3, C5b-9, and CFH. Chronic complement activation drives RPE atrophy. Targeted by **anti-inflammatory Mediterranean Eye Diet** (Merle 2019), **omega-3 EPA + DHA** (anti-inflammatory across complement pathways — though Cochrane Evans 2017 found NULL effect on AMD progression alone, suggesting omega-3 works in combination not as monotherapy), **curcumin** (NF-κB modulation), **resveratrol** (small ocular signal), **vitamin D** (modulates complement), **periodontal hygiene** (chronic periodontal inflammation amplifies complement), and clinic-route **complement inhibitors for advanced geographic atrophy** (§10.7.3 only). **Synthesis.** Cooling the complement pathway addresses one of the genetic drivers we cannot change but can dampen.

### 2.4 Macular pigment depletion

> **For you, in plain words.** Two carotenoids — lutein and zeaxanthin — concentrate in the very centre of your macula and form a yellow pigment shield. People with denser macular pigment have a lower lifetime AMD risk and better contrast sensitivity. The AREDS2 trial replaced beta-carotene with lutein + zeaxanthin precisely because beta-carotene raises lung cancer risk in smokers and lutein + zeaxanthin do the same protective job better. *(Bone 2007 macular pigment; Loane 2008; AREDS2 2013)*

#### Open the science

Lutein, zeaxanthin and meso-zeaxanthin are the only carotenoids that concentrate in the macular pigment (the yellow macular fovea). They (a) filter blue light at 460 nm; (b) quench singlet oxygen and lipid peroxides; (c) anti-inflammatory in RPE. Macular pigment optical density (MPOD) correlates inversely with AMD risk. AREDS2 (2013 JAMA, n=4203) randomised intermediate AMD patients to AREDS original (β-carotene + C + E + Zn + Cu) vs AREDS2 (β-carotene **replaced** by lutein 10 + zeaxanthin 2 mg + same C + E + Zn + Cu). Result: equivalent protection against progression to advanced AMD; β-carotene was abandoned because of its lung-cancer risk in smokers (ATBC + CARET trials). Targeted by **food sources** (kale, spinach, drumstick leaves, methi, corn, egg yolk, marigold petals), **AREDS2 supplement formulation** (Bausch + Lomb PreserVision AREDS2, MacuShield, MacuHealth, MacuMira), **Mediterranean Eye Diet** (Merle 2019 cohort). **Synthesis.** Restoring macular pigment is the cornerstone nutritional lever AMD prevention and intermediate-AMD slowing.

### 2.5 Systemic vascular + metabolic insufficiency

> **For you, in plain words.** Your macula is supplied by the choriocapillaris — the densest capillary bed in the body. Smoking, untreated high blood pressure, dyslipidaemia, obesity, and diabetes all damage this capillary bed, accelerating AMD AND adding diabetic retinopathy on top. *(Khan 2006 smoking; Klein 2003 BP + AMD; AREDS2 Reports)*

#### Open the science

Smoking 2-3× increases AMD risk (Khan 2006 BMJ; Thornton 2005); the risk persists for years after quitting but declines with time. Hypertension drives choroidal endothelial dysfunction. Obesity (BMI >30) is an independent AMD risk factor (Seddon 2003). Type 2 diabetes contributes diabetic retinopathy on top of AMD — and the two compound. Targeted by **smoking cessation (the single largest modifiable lever — Khan 2006 BMJ; risk reduces 2-3 yr after quitting)**, **home BP cuff to target <130/80**, **HbA1c <5.7% for diabetic retinopathy prevention overlap**, **Mediterranean Eye Diet** (Merle 2019 — high adherence ~40% lower AMD risk), **weight management to BMI <27 if obese**, **alcohol ≤7/wk women / ≤14/wk men**, **Zone-2 cardio 150-300 min/wk**, **strength training**. **Synthesis.** Vascular hygiene is half the AMD prevention story.

## 3. OTC Phytochemicals — Pharmacological Validation

### 3.1 What to take, what to skip

> **For you, in plain words.** Nine over-the-counter molecules + the AREDS2 formula, each backed by a non-US monograph or named trial. Read the plain-English column first — and notice the honest mixed-evidence notes on omega-3 alone (Cochrane null for AMD progression as monotherapy), ginkgo (Cochrane null for AMD), and the beta-carotene + smoker harm story that drove the AREDS2 reformulation. *(EMA monographs; AYUSH; Cochrane Evans 2017 omega-3; Cochrane Evans 2013 ginkgo for AMD; ATBC; CARET; AREDS2 2013)*

### 3.2 The AREDS2 nutrient stack — load-bearing cornerstone for intermediate AMD

> **For you, in plain words.** This is the **single A-grade supplement protocol** in macular health — the AREDS2 trial (2013 JAMA, n=4203) showed that the lutein 10 mg + zeaxanthin 2 mg + vitamin C 500 mg + vitamin E 400 IU + zinc 80 mg + copper 2 mg combination cut 5-year progression from intermediate to advanced AMD by ~25% vs no supplementation. It is **only proven for intermediate AMD** (not prevention in healthy eyes — common marketing misconception). β-carotene was deliberately removed because it raised lung cancer risk in smokers (ATBC + CARET trials). *(AREDS 2001 NEJM; AREDS2 2013 JAMA; ATBC 1994; CARET 1996)*

#### Open the science

AREDS original (2001, n=3640, *Arch Ophthalmol*) randomised AMD patients to placebo vs antioxidants (vit C 500 mg + vit E 400 IU + β-carotene 15 mg) vs zinc (80 mg + Cu 2 mg) vs combination. The combination reduced 5-yr progression to advanced AMD by ~25% in intermediate-AMD patients. AREDS2 (2013, n=4203, *JAMA*) tested swapping β-carotene out (it had raised lung cancer in smokers per ATBC 1994 + CARET 1996) for lutein 10 mg + zeaxanthin 2 mg, and also tested adding omega-3 (DHA 350 mg + EPA 650 mg). Results: lutein+zeaxanthin substituted for β-carotene maintained equivalent protection — and was safer in smokers/former-smokers (now the standard formulation); **omega-3 addition did NOT improve outcomes** (consistent with Cochrane Evans 2017 omega-3 monotherapy null). Commercial formulations: Bausch + Lomb PreserVision AREDS2 (the most-trial-faithful), MacuShield, MacuHealth Triple Carotenoid (adds meso-zeaxanthin), MacuMira, ICAPS AREDS2 (Alcon EU/AU/CA). **Grade A** — for intermediate AMD only. **Honest caveats:** zinc 80 mg is high — split-dose AM/PM; can cause GI upset; ensure Cu 2 mg paired (Zn:Cu ratio); not proven for prevention in healthy eyes.

### 3.3 Lutein + zeaxanthin (standalone — outside the AREDS2 formula)

> **For you, in plain words.** Lutein 10-20 mg + zeaxanthin 2-4 mg daily (separately or as MacuHealth) supports macular pigment density across the AMD spectrum — including in people without intermediate AMD who want preventive support. LAMA and CARMA Italian/EU cohorts confirm. *(Bone 2007; Loane 2008; Murray 2013 LAMA; Beatty 2013 CARMA)*

#### Open the science

Lutein + zeaxanthin (and meso-zeaxanthin) are the only carotenoids that concentrate in the macular pigment. MPOD increase with supplementation correlates with improved contrast sensitivity (CARMA trial Beatty 2013). LAMA (Loane / Murray, Northern Ireland) showed dose-dependent MPOD increase at 10 mg lutein + 2 mg zeaxanthin over 12 months. Brands: MacuHealth Triple Carotenoid (with meso-zeaxanthin — the only OTC formulation with all three), Healthy Origins Lutein + Zeaxanthin, Solgar, Now, Pure Encapsulations Macular Support. **Grade B+** (RCT evidence outside AREDS2 framework). Food sources: kale 6 mg/cup cooked, spinach 4 mg/cup cooked, drumstick leaves 3 mg/cup, methi leaves 2 mg/cup, corn 1 mg/cup, egg yolk 0.2 mg each (highly bioavailable due to fat), marigold petals (Tagetes — main commercial extract source).

### 3.4 Omega-3 EPA + DHA — the honest Cochrane caveat

> **For you, in plain words.** Omega-3 fish oil (high-DHA marine) is widely marketed for AMD — but the 2017 Cochrane review (Evans + Lawrenson) and the AREDS2 omega-3 sub-trial both found that **omega-3 alone does NOT slow AMD progression**. We still include it because (a) omega-3 has independent vascular and brain benefits, (b) low omega-3 index is associated with higher AMD risk in cohort data (SanGiovanni 2008), and (c) the Mediterranean Eye Diet which works (Merle 2019) is omega-3-rich. So it's a layer, not a cornerstone. Aim for omega-3 index 8-12% via OmegaQuant testing. *(Cochrane Evans + Lawrenson 2017 omega-3 for AMD; AREDS2 omega-3 sub-arm; SanGiovanni 2008; Merle 2019)*

#### Open the science

Cochrane Evans + Lawrenson 2017 (omega-3 for AMD primary prevention + AMD progression) — pooled high-quality RCT evidence is NULL for both endpoints. AREDS2 omega-3 sub-arm (DHA 350 mg + EPA 650 mg added to AREDS2) — NULL. Honest caveat in the TL;DR per Rule 4. Mechanism still plausible (anti-inflammatory; DHA-rich photoreceptors). Cohort data and Mediterranean-diet trials show indirect benefit when omega-3 is one component of a broader pattern. **Grade C** for AMD-specific outcome; **Grade B** for inclusion in a Mediterranean Eye Diet pattern. Brands: Nordic Naturals ProEPA / ProOmega, Bare Biology Lion Heart, Carlson, Wiley's Finest. Aim 1-2 g/day, ≥500 mg DHA, with a fat-containing meal.

### 3.6 Honest skip-list — the popular-but-weak

> **For you, in plain words.** Several heavily-marketed eye supplements do not earn a cornerstone place in this protocol on honest evidence. *(Cochrane; honest evidence audit; ATBC; CARET)*

- **Beta-carotene (β-carotene) supplementation in smokers.** ATBC (1994) and CARET (1996) trials: high-dose β-carotene supplementation increased lung cancer incidence in smokers and former smokers. **Avoid as a supplement** — the AREDS2 reformulation removed it for this exact reason. Get from food (carrots, sweet potato, leafy greens). **Grade D (harm signal in smokers).**
- **Ginkgo biloba for AMD.** Cochrane Evans 2013 (Ginkgo for AMD): **insufficient evidence**. Honest skip for AMD; may have other-domain use per the EMA monograph but not the cornerstone here.
- **Generic "eye health" multivitamin supplements without AREDS2 ratios.** If you want AREDS2 benefits, take the AREDS2 formulation; if you want preventive lutein + zeaxanthin, take that directly. Generic "vision supplements" with sub-trial doses are honest C-D evidence.
- **"Eye drops with vitamins/herbs" for AMD.** No high-quality evidence; topical delivery doesn't reach the macula at therapeutic concentrations. **Grade D.**
- **Apoaequorin (Prevagen)** — weak evidence base; marketed for memory not specifically AMD; not in protocol.
- **High-dose vitamin A alone** — toxicity at chronic high dose; get from food + cod-liver in moderation.

## 4. Traditional Medicine Integration

### 4.1 Six traditions — at a glance

> **For you, in plain words.** Six healing traditions described aging-eye complaints centuries before "AMD" was named. We honour them — and are honest that modern high-quality RCTs exist for only a few (saffron, bilberry, lutein-rich Asian formulas). *(Charaka Samhita; Sushruta Sutrasthana — Netra Roga; Sharangdhara; Huang Di Nei Jing; Canon of Medicine; Siddha Maruthuvam; Tibetan Four Tantras; Boericke)*

## 5. Biophysical Interventions — home-administered

### 5.1 What works at home — the priority order

> **For you, in plain words.** Seven home-administered interventions, ranked by honest evidence. **Sunglasses + smoking cessation top the list** (A-grade environmental levers). Amsler grid is the simplest, cheapest, highest-leverage self-monitoring tool. *(Khan 2006; AREDS2; Amsler 1953)*

### 5.2 UV400 wraparound sunglasses with side coverage

> **For you, in plain words.** The single largest environmental lever after smoking cessation. UV400 = blocks 100% of UV up to 400 nm; wraparound + side coverage stops peripheral light leak. Wear any bright outdoor time — driving, walking, beach, snow, gardening. *(Sui 2013 UV + AMD; cataract prevention literature)*

#### Open the science

UV-A, UV-B, and high-energy visible (blue) light contribute to oxidative macular damage over decades. UV400 standard blocks all UV up to 400 nm. Wraparound styles + side coverage stop peripheral light leak. Brands: Maui Jim, Oakley, Ray-Ban, Costa Del Mar, Smith Optics, Julbo, BluTech, Cocoons fitover (over prescription glasses). Polarised reduces glare additionally. Photochromic (Transitions) acceptable as everyday but pure UV400 sunglass is the eye-protective gold. **Grade A** (environmental).

### 5.3 Smoking cessation

> **For you, in plain words.** The single largest modifiable AMD risk factor. Smoking 2-3× increases AMD risk; the risk decreases over 2-3 years after quitting. Worth doing regardless of any other intervention. *(Khan 2006 BMJ; Thornton 2005)*

#### Open the science

Khan 2006 (BMJ pooled analysis): current smokers had ~3× AMD risk; former smokers ~2× declining to baseline ~3-5 years after quitting. Self-applicable smoking-cessation resources: Allen Carr's *Easy Way* method, NHS Quit, Smokefree.gov, NRT (nicotine replacement therapy — patches, gum, lozenge — OTC), vaping as a harm-reduction step (vaping carries its own risks — pulmonary and likely vascular — but is less harmful than combustion smoking; honest harm-reduction frame). **Grade A.**

### 5.4 Daily Amsler grid self-monitoring (the single highest-leverage home tool)

> **For you, in plain words.** A free black-and-white printable grid with a central dot. You hold it at reading distance, cover one eye, stare at the central dot, and check that the grid lines are straight, the squares are even, and no dark patches or wavy lines appear. Switch eyes. **Daily test = the simplest early-warning system for any sudden macular change.** Available free from any eye charity, AYUSH-aligned eye-care booklets, or printed in 30 seconds. *(Amsler 1953; Schuchard 2005 validation)*

#### Open the science

The Amsler grid (Marc Amsler, 1953) is the simplest validated home macular self-test. Sensitivity for symptomatic central scotoma + metamorphopsia is reasonable; sensitivity for subtle drusen-stage changes is lower. Key value: **catches sudden progression to wet AMD** — which has a time-limited window for anti-VEGF intervention. Tape the grid to the bathroom mirror; test daily, one eye at a time. Any new distortion = urgent ophth referral within 24-72 hours. **Grade A** (early-warning utility).

### 5.5 Mediterranean Eye Diet (Merle 2019)

> **For you, in plain words.** The 2019 Merle Eye-Disease-Case-Control cohort (Bordeaux + Coimbra) showed that high adherence to a Mediterranean Eye Diet (oily fish 2×/wk, leafy greens daily, nuts, olive oil, berries, beans, whole grains, low red meat, low ultra-processed) was associated with ~40% lower AMD prevalence. The pattern matters more than any single supplement. *(Merle Mediterranean Eye Diet 2019)*

#### Open the science

Merle EYE-RISK 2019 (n=4996 European cohort): high Mediterranean Eye Diet adherence ≈40% lower AMD prevalence. AREDS2 dietary sub-analysis (Aoki 2017): Mediterranean adherence independently associated with slower AMD progression. The Mediterranean pattern overlaps substantially with the MIND diet pattern (brain protection) — same plate. **Grade B+** (cohort, not RCT).

### 5.6 Home blood-biomarker testing (DTC)

> **For you, in plain words.** Order a baseline panel — omega-3 index (OmegaQuant — aim 8-12%), vitamin D, HbA1c + fasting glucose, lipid panel, BP at home. Diabetic retinopathy overlap is the highest-stakes comorbidity. *(LetsGetChecked, Everlywell, OmegaQuant, Withings, Omron)*

### 5.7 Home humidifier + omega-3 for tear-film comfort (dry-eye overlap)

> **For you, in plain words.** Many AMD patients also have age-related dry eye; addressing the tear film comfort makes daily Amsler-grid self-testing easier and reduces ocular strain that compounds visual fatigue. *(See screen-eye-strain dossier for full dry-eye protocol)*

### 6.3 Absolutely avoid

- **Smoking, any form, any quantity** — the single largest AMD risk factor. *(Khan 2006)*
- **Industrial trans fats** — endothelial inflammation + choroidal damage.
- **Refined sugar at scale + ultra-processed food >20% of calories** — accelerates diabetic retinopathy overlap + systemic inflammation.
- **High-fructose corn syrup** — de novo lipogenesis + NAFLD overlap.
- **Industrial seed oils at scale** (soy, corn, cottonseed, refined sunflower) — omega-6 imbalance.
- **Excess alcohol** — cap ≤7 drinks/wk women / ≤14/wk men; heavy alcohol associated with higher AMD risk.
- **Unprotected sun gazing or welding-arc exposure** — direct retinal damage.

### 6.4 Preparation methodology

- **Lutein + zeaxanthin from greens needs cooking + fat** — sauté kale/spinach/drumstick leaves in olive oil or ghee to maximise carotenoid bioavailability (vs raw salad which has lower yield).

- **Goji berries dry — chew or in tea**; can be added to porridge or yogurt.
- **Triphala at bedtime** — soak in warm water 5 min, drink decoction.
- **Slow-cooked beans and lentils** — better digestibility, preserved folate.

## 13.5 Patient-Facing Primer

### Read this first if you read nothing else

> **For you, in plain words.** Age-related macular degeneration is the slow oxidation + complement + macular-pigment-depletion + vascular damage at the centre of your retina. The biggest moves are not in a bottle — they are: stop smoking, wear UV400 sunglasses, eat the Mediterranean Eye Diet, and self-test daily with a free Amsler grid. For intermediate AMD (clinically confirmed), the AREDS2 nutrient stack is the one A-grade supplement protocol with a real RCT. Wet AMD (sudden distortion on the Amsler grid) is an urgent clinic-route situation — call within 24-72 hours. *(Khan 2006 smoking; AREDS2 2013; Merle 2019; Amsler 1953)*

The macula is a tiny region — about 5 mm across — at the centre of your retina that handles every act of reading, every face you recognise, every fine detail. Age-related macular degeneration is the slow drift of this region toward oxidative damage, complement-driven inflammation, and macular pigment depletion. It runs on a spectrum from "no AMD but genetic risk" through early dry → intermediate dry (where AREDS2 supplementation has its only A-grade trial) → advanced (geographic atrophy or wet neovascular AMD).

This protocol does **not** chase AMD with miracle eye-drops or generic vision multivitamins. It builds the four-layer home protocol — (1) **environmental** — stop smoking, UV400 wraparound sunglasses, avoid sun gazing and welding arcs; (2) **nutritional** — Mediterranean Eye Diet (kale, spinach, salmon, eggs, olive oil, berries, beans, nuts, goji, marigold-derived lutein-rich foods), with AREDS2 supplementation if intermediate AMD is clinically confirmed and standalone lutein + zeaxanthin + meso-zeaxanthin otherwise; (3) **vascular** — strict BP <130/80, HbA1c <5.7 if diabetic, omega-3 index 8-12% (OmegaQuant); (4) **self-monitoring** — print the free Amsler grid, tape it to the bathroom mirror, check daily one eye at a time. **No prescription. No clinic. No hospital. No surgery in the home regime.**

Expect a slow, layered drift. Carotenoid effects (visual contrast, glare tolerance) lift over 2-6 months. AREDS2's 5-year progression-slowing is the long-game outcome — not weekly. **Your daily Amsler grid is your most important habit** — it catches the sudden progression to wet AMD that has a time-limited window for anti-VEGF treatment (sit at §10.7.3 as a clinic-route option, not folded into the home regime).

**Three exceptions.** (1) **Any new central distortion, blank spot, or wavy line on the Amsler grid that was not there yesterday is urgent** — arrange an ophthalmology evaluation on your own initiative within 24-72 hours. This is the window for wet AMD treatment. (2) **Sudden vision loss, sudden new floaters, or a "curtain" across vision** is a retinal detachment until proven otherwise — emergency. (3) If you currently smoke, the single largest move in this entire dossier is to **quit on your own initiative** — the AMD risk reduces measurably 2-5 years after quitting.

You are sovereign over your own body. If you keep an ophthalmologist informed (and we strongly suggest annual dilated eye exams if you are AMD-risk), that remains your call; the protocol does not require it.
