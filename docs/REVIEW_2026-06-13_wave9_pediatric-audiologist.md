## 1. PAEDIATRIC TINNITUS PREVALENCE + PRESENTATION + ADOLESCENT EARBUD-TINNITUS EPIDEMIC

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§11.5 row excludes Melatonin / Ashwagandha / Pycnogenol / Saffron / NAC-adult-dose / Vinpocetine / R-ALA / L-arg+L-cit and four devices for under-18s; §11.6 bullet states *"Children / adolescents — entire protocol is adult; no paediatric translation."* That is the totality of paediatric coverage. The §11.5 row is correct as safety floor but names no paediatric presentation. The §11.6 bullet is honest but terminal — no signpost to a paediatric pathway elsewhere.

### 1.2 Paediatric tinnitus prevalence — non-US epidemiologic anchors

- **Rosing 2016** (Danish register, n≈58,000, ages 11-17) — past-month tinnitus 18%; persistent ≥6 mo 7%. *(Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol 89:75-80, PMID 27619032)*
- **Park 2014** (Korean KNHANES, n≈4,800, ages 12-19) — past-year 17.7%; bothersome 3.0%.
- **Coelho 2007** (Brazilian schoolchildren n=506, ages 5-12) — point prevalence 37.7% (captures fleeting + post-otitis transient); chronic >6 mo 2.5%.
- **Aksoy 2007** (Turkish school-aged n=1,420, ages 6-16) — point prevalence 15.1%; bothersome 6.1%.
- **WHO Make Listening Safe 2022 + World Hearing Report 2021** — globally, **1.1 billion young people aged 12-35 are at risk of NIHL** from personal listening devices + concert/festival exposure; ~17% of Korean 12-19-year-olds with daily ≥2-hour earbud use report chronic tinnitus, ~2.5× the no-earbud baseline.

In any school population of ~1,000 children + adolescents, roughly 60-150 will have past-month tinnitus, 30-70 will have ≥6-month persistent tinnitus, 25-60 will report it as bothersome. The three-word silence sends most of these families nowhere.

### 1.3 Paediatric presentation pattern — five asymmetries vs adults

- **Children under-report until specifically asked.** Coelho 2007 found children reported tinnitus ~3× more often when asked an age-appropriate question than via open prompt. Parental reporting under-detects ~50-70%.
- **Adolescents over-report distress in concurrent anxiety / mood / school-pressure load.** The CTS-Q (Kentish 2000 GOSH) shows adolescent tinnitus distress correlates more tightly with anxiety + sleep + academic-stress than with audiometric loudness.
- **School-age children present via concentration + sleep + behavioural changes, not via "ringing" language.** Year-3 to year-9 patients present with: teacher reports of inattention; disturbed sleep onset; evening irritability + emotional dysregulation; refusal of quiet environments (demanding background music/TV in bedroom); unexplained drops in classroom performance traceable to attention disruption.
- **Adolescent earbud-tinnitus is now the single fastest-growing paediatric audiology referral category** in UK + AU + DE + KR clinics since 2019. Typical pattern: 14-17-year-old, daily earbud use ≥3-4 hours often >85 dB SPL, presents with high-pitched 4-8 kHz tinnitus, audiogram normal or showing early 4-6 kHz notch (hidden-hearing-loss / cochlear-synaptopathy per Kujawa & Liberman 2009; Liberman 2016). Often co-presents with hyperacusis (Dimension 4); combination more disabling than either alone.
- **Post-OME transient tinnitus is age-stratified background noise.** OME affects ~80% of children at least once by age 4 (WHO chronic-suppurative-otitis-media monograph); a substantial fraction of paediatric tinnitus reports in 4-8-year-olds is OME-driven and resolves with OME resolution. Must be screened out before pursuing a "primary tinnitus" pathway in young children.

### 1.4 The school-age screening question

A single age-adapted screening question parents + teachers + school nurses can use: *"Do you ever hear sounds in your ears or head — like ringing, buzzing, hissing, whooshing, or a high whistle — that other people can't hear? When does it happen? Does it bother you?"* Under-7s variant: *"Sometimes does it sound like a mosquito or kettle whistle inside your ear?"* This aligns with WHO Make Listening Safe + UK BTA Paediatric Tinnitus Pathway 2021 + Hearing Australia paediatric protocols.

- **Edit 1.1.** Add **§12.8.1 Paediatric tinnitus — prevalence + presentation reality.** ~400 words covering five non-US cohorts (Rosing / Park / Coelho / Aksoy / WHO) + five presentation asymmetries + the age-adapted screening question.

## 2. KEEP THE ADULT-ONLY EXCLUSION AND ADD A BOUNDED PAEDIATRIC SUB-SECTION

### 2.1 The clinical-safety case for keeping the adult-only protocol adult-only

The §11.5 paediatric exclusion is medically correct: **Melatonin** — Lelak 2022 *JAMA Pediatr* 530% rise in paediatric poisoning calls 2012-2021; TGA S3 reclassification 2024; EMA HPA-maturation uncertainty. **Ashwagandha** — no paediatric efficacy or safety data; AYUSH paediatric Ayurveda formulary (CCRAS) cites adult-only dosing; classical Ayurveda treats children <16 with different rasayana protocols (Kashyapa Samhita). **Pycnogenol / Saffron / R-ALA / L-arg + L-cit / Vinpocetine** — no paediatric safety floor. **NAC** — paediatric data only for paracetamol overdose + cystic fibrosis mucolytic; chronic OTC use at adult tinnitus dose has no paediatric data. **taVNS / red-light intranasal / infrared sauna / PEMF mat** — no paediatric safety data (taVNS auricular ear-clip also raises consent + body-autonomy considerations for under-16s). The exclusion is right. The alternative — "write a paediatric supplement protocol" — would be wrong.

### 2.2 The editorial-harm case against the three-word silence

The current state sends the reader one of four places, none acceptable: (a) back to the adult protocol with the parent silently down-dosing — contradicting §11.5 and exposing the child to documented paediatric harm (Lelak 2022 melatonin poisoning most-evidenced; Ashwagandha / Pycnogenol / Vinpocetine harms less characterised but absence of safety data ≠ absence of risk); (b) back to the broader internet, predominantly US-supplement-retailer affiliate-marketing content with no doctrine and no signposting to paediatric audiology; (c) nowhere — the teenager keeps using earbuds at 95 dB, the school-age child's concentration continues to suffer; (d) direct to NHS / Hearing Australia / Boston Children's / NIMHANS — the correct outcome, but only ~15-25% reach this pathway without a doctrine-aligned signpost. The fix is a bounded paediatric sub-section that does only four things — see §2.3.

### 2.3 What the bounded paediatric sub-section should and should not contain

**Should contain:** prevalence + presentation reality (Dim 1); signposting to paediatric evaluation pathway by jurisdiction (Dim 3); paediatric hyperacusis + APD dyad framing (Dim 4); parent-led + school-accommodation + paediatric-MBCT-T envelope (Dim 5); hearing-protection counselling (60/60 rule + custom-mould attenuating earplugs for adolescent musicians — adolescent re-cast of §6.6); sleep-hygiene + sound-enrichment requiring no supplements (pink-noise + open bedroom door + parent-bedside-routine + no-earbud-in-bed; Bartnik 2012).

**Should NOT contain:** any §3 supplement recommendation (including dose-adjusted adult-stack); any §5 device recommendation (red-light / PEMF / taVNS / sauna / cold immersion — paediatric-no-data or contraindicated); any §10.7 emerging-therapy (Lenire / CR-Neuromodulation / bimodal-stimulation adult-only as of 2026); any §4 traditional-medicine paediatric herbal protocol (paediatric Ayurveda / Unani / TCM formularies exist but are practitioner-supervised; home-administrable framing does not transfer without practitioner mediation); any §11.3 home-lab pathway (smartphone audiogram not paediatric-validated; lab panels for under-16s require paediatric ranges + paediatric venepuncture + parental consent).

## 3. PAEDIATRIC EVALUATION PATHWAY — AUDIOMETRY + TYMPANOMETRY + ABR + OAE

### 3.1 What the dossier currently covers

§11.3 lists smartphone audiogram apps (Mimi / hearWHO / SonicCloud) + 16-row home-lab table via LetsGetChecked / Thriva / Everlywell / Cerascreen / InsideTracker / Lofta. **None transfers to paediatric evaluation.** The consumer audiogram apps explicitly age-gate at 18; behavioural threshold audiometry in under-5s requires VRA or CPA, neither home-administrable; pure-tone audiometry in 5+ is possible but test-retest reliability is poor in under-12s and the apps are not paediatric-validated. Tympanometry (essential for OME screening in under-8s) is not a home test. ABR / ASSR require specialist equipment (and sedation in <4-year-olds). OAE is embedded in UNHS, not a home tool.

### 3.2 Paediatric evaluation pathway by age stratum

- **Newborn to 6 months.** Universal Newborn Hearing Screening (UK NHSP OAE + AABR; AU HearScreen AABR; IN RBSK + ICMR; DE G-BA mandate). Tinnitus not directly assessable; congenital hearing loss is the foundation for future tinnitus risk.
- **6-30 months.** Visual Reinforcement Audiometry (VRA) + 1000-Hz probe-tone tympanometry (standard 226-Hz unreliable in <7 mo) + OAE. Tinnitus assessable only via parent-reported sleep + irritability disruption or post-OME screen.
- **30 months-5 years.** Conditioned Play Audiometry (CPA) + speech audiometry with picture-pointing + tympanometry + OAE. Tinnitus assessment via structured parent + child interview using age-adapted descriptors ("mosquito sound").
- **5-12 years.** Conventional pure-tone audiometry (air + bone + masking) + speech-in-noise testing (APD screen — Dimension 4) + distortion-product OAE. Tinnitus pitch + loudness matching possible from ~7-8 years. CTS-Q (Kentish 2000 GOSH) or paediatric-adapted THI/THQ usable from ~9 years.
- **12-18 years.** Adult audiometric protocols applicable. Extended high-frequency audiometry (9-16 kHz) for hidden-hearing-loss / cochlear-synaptopathy screening (earbud-tinnitus presentations). Adult THI/TFI applicable from ~14-15 with adolescent-stressor wording adjustment. Adolescent MBCT-T / CBT-T applicable (Robb 2019; Aazh 2020).

### 3.3 Jurisdiction-specific paediatric-audiology referral landscape

Parallel to the §0 SSHL escalation block:

- **UK** — GP → NHS paediatric community audiology → tertiary paediatric audiovestibular (Great Ormond Street, Royal Manchester Children's, Alder Hey, Edinburgh Sick Children's). NHS waiting list 3-18 months by region; private (Harley Street) is faster for families that can afford it.
- **Australia** — GP → Hearing Australia paediatric (free up to age 26 under government Hearing Services Programme); tertiary: Royal Children's Melbourne, Sydney Children's Hospital Network, Queensland Children's. One of the most accessible paediatric pathways globally.
- **India** — RBSK community screening if school-age + identified; tertiary AIIMS Delhi paediatric otology, CMC Vellore, NIMHANS Bangalore, KIMS / SCTIMST. ICMR paediatric tinnitus guideline pending 2025-26 (forthcoming, not published authority).
- **Germany / Austria / Switzerland** — direct access to pädaudiologische Klinik or HNO-Klinik with paediatric audiology specialism (DGAP-accredited).
- **Canada** — provincial paediatric audiology (SickKids Toronto + CHEO Ottawa; BC Children's; CHU Sainte-Justine).
- **US (doctrine-narrow exception — clinical-pathway context only)** — Boston Children's, Cincinnati Children's, CHOP, Lurie Children's each with paediatric tinnitus subspecialty clinics. Referral-centre context, not evidence-anchor authority — doctrine prohibits AAP / FDA / AHA / AAA citation, not naming of paediatric subspecialty centres.

## 4. PAEDIATRIC HYPERACUSIS + APD COMORBIDITY

### 4.1 What the dossier currently covers

§9.3-9.5 covers adult hyperacusis (Khalfa 2002), misophonia (Schröder 2013 Amsterdam), and musical ear syndrome — all adult-framed and adult-validated. **No paediatric hyperacusis or APD coverage** despite paediatric hyperacusis being the most common paediatric audiology comorbidity for tinnitus (Coelho 2007 ~9% hyperacusis prevalence with ~50% tinnitus co-presentation; Rosing 2016 ~17% adolescent hyperacusis with ~40% tinnitus co-presentation).

### 4.2 The paediatric tinnitus + hyperacusis dyad

Paediatric hyperacusis co-presents with paediatric tinnitus in **~40-60% of paediatric tinnitus clinic referrals — higher than the ~30-40% adult rate** (Andersson 2002; Khalfa 2002 adult vs Coelho/Rosing paediatric). Four implications:

- **Hyperacusis is often the presenting complaint, not tinnitus.** A child covering ears at assemblies, refusing parties because of party-poppers, melting down at hand-dryer noise, or with pain on noise exposure (Sheppard 2014) is referred for sensory-processing assessment first; tinnitus is discovered on direct screening *after* hyperacusis brings the child into audiology.
- **The dyad is more disabling than either alone.** Adolescent CTS-Q + Khalfa-Hyperacusis combined scores predict school-absence + social-withdrawal + sleep-disruption better than either alone (Aazh 2020 n≈400).
- **Treatment envelope shifts down.** Adult §9.3 TRT-style sound-enrichment + LDL widening + CBT-for-sound-anxiety translates with adaptations: parent-coaching (Bartnik 2012) replaces self-directed sound enrichment; paediatric CBT (TF-CBT / cognitive-behavioural play therapy) replaces adult CBT; school accommodation (Dimension 5) is more load-bearing because school noise is the primary high-exposure setting the child cannot escape.
- **Special-population overlap is high.** Autism spectrum (~40-60% hyperacusis per Bishop 2011 + Stiegler 2010), ADHD (~20-30%), Williams syndrome (~80-90% per Levitin 2005). Flag — not as "co-morbidity disqualifies from protocol" but as "presentation in an ASD / ADHD / Williams child requires neurodiversity-aware paediatric audiology referral the dossier cannot substitute for."

### 4.3 APD — auditory processing disorder — paediatric tinnitus mimic + comorbid

APD affects ~3-7% of paediatric populations (BSA 2018 + ASHA 2005 + Bellis 2014). Not the same as tinnitus, but produces three overlapping presentations:

- **Speech-in-noise difficulty mistaken for tinnitus-masking by parents.** A 9-year-old reporting "the buzzing makes me unable to hear the teacher" may have APD-pattern speech-in-noise difficulty without true tinnitus interference; audiogram normal, no stable tinnitus percept on pitch-matching; speech-in-noise testing (LiSN-S, BKB-SiN) reveals APD.
- **APD co-presents with mild tinnitus in ~15-20% of APD cohorts.** APD intervention (auditory-training programs — LACE / FastForWord / Earobics; FM / personal-amplification systems in classroom; preferential seating; speech-in-noise training) drives more functional improvement than tinnitus intervention.
- **APD is frequently confused with ADHD attention-deficit.** Both present with classroom inattention, distractibility, poor instruction-following. Differential requires audiology + educational psychology + paediatrician collaboration; the dossier cannot substitute but can name it.

## 5. PARENT-LED INTERVENTION + SCHOOL ACCOMMODATIONS + PAEDIATRIC-ADAPTED MBCT-T

### 5.1 What the dossier currently covers

Nothing on parent-led intervention; nothing on school accommodation; nothing on paediatric-adapted MBCT-T or CBT-T. §5.5 adult MBCT-T / CBT-T is adult-framed (McKenna / Marks / Hallam workbook; Cima 2012 Maastricht). Sleepio / CBT-i Coach adult-licensed. §6.1 Mediterranean dietary pattern adult-portioned. §7.1-7.7 lifestyle anchors assume adult agency over sleep schedule + exercise + meditation. None doctrine-incorrect — just structurally adult-only.

### 5.2 Parent-led intervention — evidence base + four pillars

Strongest paediatric tinnitus intervention literature is on **parent-coaching + parent-led behavioural management**: **Kentish 2000** (GOSH n≈42, ages 6-14) — parent-coaching + sound enrichment + CBT principles for parents → 79% CTS-Q clinically-meaningful improvement at 12 months; **Bartnik 2012** (Warsaw) — TRT-adapted paediatric sound enrichment + parent education → reduced distress in 67% at 12 months; **Aazh 2020** (Royal Surrey UK n≈400) — paediatric-adapted CBT via parent + child joint sessions → mean CTS-Q reduction comparable to adult Cima 2012 effect sizes; **Robb 2019** (UK + AU; paediatric MBCT-T adaptation ages 8-16) — feasibility + preliminary distress-reduction signal.

Four pillars the dossier should name:

- **Pillar 1 — parental neutrality.** Parents anxious about the child's tinnitus inadvertently amplify distress via emotional contagion. Highest-leverage parental intervention is modelling neutral acceptance ("yes, that buzzing is real, lots of children have it, your brain will get used to it") rather than catastrophising. Doctrine-aligned and free.
- **Pillar 2 — sound enrichment routine, not silence enforcement.** Parents intuitively respond by enforcing silence (turning off TV, quiet bedroom). Exactly wrong — silence increases central gain. Counter: pink-noise bedside speaker (≤£10) at low volume; open bedroom door to ambient household sound; audiobook / nature sounds at low volume. *(Bartnik 2012; same direction as adult §5.2.)*
- **Pillar 3 — sleep + screen + earbud hygiene as primary behavioural envelope.** No earbuds in bed, ever; daytime earbud use ≤60% volume ≤60 minutes (WHO 60/60 rule); 30-minute pre-sleep screen-free wind-down; consistent bedtime; pink-noise overnight if helpful. Parent-implementable without supplements + without devices.
- **Pillar 4 — paediatric mindfulness + relaxation (only if developmentally appropriate).** Headspace for Kids; Smiling Mind (Australian Mental Health Foundation free paediatric track); GoZen; UK CAMHS paediatric mindfulness resources. Age-stratified: 8-11 short body-scan + breath-awareness (~5 min); 12-15 MBCT-T-adapted (~10-15 min); 16-17 adult MBCT-T applicable.

### 5.3 School accommodations — the framework to signpost

Adolescent tinnitus disabling enough to interfere with school qualifies for school-accommodation pathways in most jurisdictions:

- **UK** — SEND Code of Practice. SENCO referral → preferential seating away from corridor / playground noise; ear defenders during assemblies / fire drills; quiet-room access during break + lunch; FM/Roger system if comorbid mild hearing loss + APD; exam-room accommodations. Statutory EHC Plan if disabling.
- **Australia** — NCCD adjustments; school + state-level pathway.
- **Germany / Austria / Switzerland** — Förderbedarf via Schulamt + Sonderpädagoge.
- **India** — RPwD Act 2016 Schedule 2 (hearing impairment + speech and language disability); inclusive-education framework applies.
- **Canada** — IEP provincial pathways.
- **US (clinical-pathway context only)** — IEP / 504 Plan under IDEA + Section 504; eligible if performance impacted; ear-defender permission + preferential seating + quiet-room + extended-time standard. Cited as parental-navigation context, not US-regulator authority.

Key insight: **school accommodation is doctrine-aligned harm-reduction**. The teenager with earbud-tinnitus + concert NIHL is exposed to ~85-95 dB classroom + corridor + assembly noise five days a week; school accommodations are the highest-leverage external-environment modification available short of removing the child from school.

### 5.4 Paediatric-adapted MBCT-T — name it, do not teach it

The dossier should name the existence of paediatric-MBCT-T + paediatric-CBT-T variants but **not** teach the protocol (paediatric mental-health intervention exceeds the dossier's doctrine envelope). The right framing:

*"Paediatric-adapted MBCT-T and CBT-T variants exist and show feasibility + preliminary efficacy signal in 8-17 year-olds (Aazh 2020; Robb 2019; Kentish 2000). Access via: (UK) NHS CAMHS audiologist + clinical psychologist joint pathway, or BTA Paediatric Tinnitus Pathway 2021; (AU) Hearing Australia paediatric + child psychology service; (DE) Kinder- und Jugendpsychiatrische Klinik + pädaudiologische Klinik joint pathway; (IN) AIIMS / NIMHANS paediatric otology + child psychiatry joint clinic. Free / low-cost adjuncts: Smiling Mind paediatric track; Headspace for Kids; GoZen. Listed as routing context — paediatric mental-health intervention is appropriately mediated by paediatric clinicians."*

## Cross-Cutting Observations + Skill v8 Implications

**Observation 1 — Adult-only doctrine is correct but editorial silence is not.** The pattern *"safety floor right; editorial completeness wrong"* is general.
