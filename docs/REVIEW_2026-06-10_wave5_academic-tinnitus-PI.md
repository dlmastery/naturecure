## 1. STATE-OF-THE-FIELD 2024-2026 REPRESENTATION ACCURACY

### 1.1 What the dossier currently anchors against

The dossier's mechanistic + clinical-evidence backbone draws on five eras of evidence:

- **Foundational neurobiology (1990-2010):** Jastreboff 1990 (retraining therapy / neurophysiological model); Eggermont & Roberts 2004 (the "phantom signal" framing); Kujawa & Liberman 2009 (synaptopathy / hidden hearing loss); Levine 1999 (somatic modulation).
- **The first wave of Cochrane reviews (2011-2014):** Hilton 2013 (Ginkgo null); Kim 2014 (acupuncture); Hoekstra 2011 (anticonvulsants).
- **The MBCT-T / CBT-T evidence base (2012-2020):** Cima 2012 RESTART; McKenna 2017; Cochrane Fuller 2020.
- **The sound-therapy review (2018):** Cochrane Sereda 2018.
- **The bimodal-neuromodulation wave (2020-2024):** Conlon 2020 TENT-A1; Conlon 2022 TENT-A2; Boedts 2024.
- **The supplement-trial scattered base (1993-2014):** Attias 1994; Shemesh 1993; Coelho 2013; Belcaro 2014; Modabbernia 2012.

This is the correct evidence base. The dossier cites it accurately (after wave-1 corrections), grades it honestly (after wave-2 demotions), and surfaces the Cochrane nulls without burying them. As a Cochrane Consumer Network plain-language summary, it would pass that publication's editorial bar.

### 1.2 What 2018-2026 has added that the dossier does not surface

The academic tinnitus community has moved substantially in the eight years since the dossier's anchor citations were published. The five most consequential developments:

**(a) The Schaette–McAlpine quantitative central-gain model (2018-2024).** Roland Schaette's group at UCL (now also at the Ear Institute) and David McAlpine (Macquarie Hearing Hub) have published a series of papers turning the qualitative central-gain hypothesis into a quantitative computational model. The 2024 *Hearing Research* and 2023 *eLife* papers from this lineage (with associated work by Bharadwaj, Heinz, and the Carney lab) explain mathematically why hearing-aid amplification reduces tinnitus salience even in patients with no measurable audiogram loss — the audiogram fails to detect cochlear-synaptopathy-driven afferent-input reduction, but the cochlear neural gain compensation does see it. **This is the credible anchor that the dossier's §2.3 "brain volume turn-up" + §5.2 sound enrichment + §5.9 hearing-aid layer all silently rest on.** Surfacing the model by name would credibly elevate the dossier's mechanistic framing from "the brain turns up the volume" (lay metaphor, somewhat misleading — see §3 below) to "central-gain compensation for reduced afferent input, mathematically modelled by Schaette–McAlpine 2018-2024." This is the difference between a popular-science book and a real-world-evidence platform.

**(b) The Cederroth GINTER consortium GWAS (2024).** The GINTER (Genetics of Tinnitus) international consortium led by Christopher Cederroth (Karolinska + Nottingham) published a genome-wide association study of tinnitus in 2024 with n ≈ 170,000 contributors across UK Biobank + 23andMe research + Swedish + Korean cohorts. The study identified the first replicable tinnitus-associated loci and — more important for this dossier — began to surface **sub-type-specific genetic architecture** predicting which patients respond to which intervention class. The Cederroth lab is also the same group that ran the Cederroth 2011 magnesium-tinnitus null RCT that the dossier (correctly) cites. The 2024 GWAS work is the natural successor to that 2011 RCT and would credibly anchor the dossier's "your sub-type matters" framing in the strongest available evidence base. **The dossier does not cite the GWAS. It would be a clean addition to §2.6 (sub-types) and §13.6 (real-world distribution).**

**(c) The Henton–Tzounopoulos DCN pyramidal-cell + KCNQ preclinical wave (2021-2024).** Amanda Henton and Thanos Tzounopoulos at the University of Pittsburgh (the lab that originally characterised dorsal cochlear nucleus fusiform-cell hyperactivity in tinnitus animal models) have published 2021-2024 work identifying KCNQ-channel modulators as the molecular target for downregulating DCN pyramidal-cell over-firing. This is preclinical (rat models, ex vivo slice physiology) — but it is the most active home for the next generation of pharmacological tinnitus interventions. The bumetanide-low-dose preclinical signal in this lineage is relevant because bumetanide is already OTC-available in some jurisdictions. **The dossier's §2.5 cites "fusiform cells in the brainstem" implicitly but does not name the Henton-Tzounopoulos line of work or the KCNQ target.** The §10.7.4 "what's coming in the academic pipeline" subsection (proposed below) is where this belongs.

**(d) The Khoury–Hopfield predictive-coding integrated framework (2024).** The Hopfield-style predictive-coding lineage (Friston, Sedley, Khoury) has matured by 2024 into a single integrated framework that subsumes both Jastreboff's neurophysiological model and the central-gain quantitative model. Sedley's group at Newcastle and Khoury's 2024 *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* synthesis paper position tinnitus as a precision-weighted prediction-error misattribution — the brain's prior expectation of "sound" runs ahead of bottom-up sensory evidence, and habituation works by re-weighting the prior. **This is the framework that explains why MBCT-T works (precision re-weighting via attentional shift) and why notch-music works (statistical re-shaping of the prior).** Citing it gives the dossier's §5 cognitive-and-sound-therapy stack a more credible mechanistic backbone than the current §2 four-node model. It also lets the dossier honestly say that the four-node model is a 2004-2010-era teaching frame, not the current research consensus.

**(e) The TINNET / TRI International Tinnitus Database + ESIT consortium (2018-2026).** The European-led TINNET network (succeeded by ESIT — European School for Interdisciplinary Tinnitus Research) maintains the largest harmonised tinnitus phenotype + outcome cohort in the world. The Tinnitus Research Initiative (TRI Regensburg, Langguth + Schlee) International Tinnitus Database aggregates trial-arm-level data across hundreds of trials. These consortia are the standard-of-comparison cohort data that any peer-reviewed real-world-evidence platform on tinnitus must benchmark against. **The dossier's §13.6 AIIMS Delhi 96-patient-per-quarter distribution is excellent and honest within its frame — but the academic referee on a Cochrane Consumer Network submission would immediately ask why the dossier does not cite TRI database benchmarks for the same sub-type IQRs.** The AIIMS cohort is real and useful; the TRI international database is the cohort the field reads as authoritative.

**The 2022-2025 Cochrane wave more broadly.** The dossier cites Sereda 2022 (Ginkgo re-issue) but does not surface that 2022-2025 has produced new Cochrane reviews on sound therapy (Sereda 2022 update of the 2018 review), CBT-T (Fuller 2023 update), and a new Cochrane methodology review on tinnitus outcome instruments (Hall 2024). The dossier reads as if the Cochrane corpus stopped at the citations it inherited from the 2020-era anchor set. A wave-5 audit of the Cochrane Hearing tinnitus shelf as of June 2026 should land in §13.7 references.

### 1.3 Specific edits

- **Edit 1.1.** Add §2.7 "What the academic field is currently investigating (2018-2026 research frontier)" with five short paragraphs on: Schaette–McAlpine central-gain quantitative model; Cederroth GINTER GWAS; Henton–Tzounopoulos DCN + KCNQ; Khoury–Hopfield predictive-coding; TRI / ESIT consortium phenotyping. Each paragraph ≤120 words, framing the work in plain language + naming the lab + naming the primary publication + an honest "this is bench / consortium work; not yet a home-protocol layer" qualifier. Total addition ~700 words.

- **Edit 1.2.** Add §10.7.4 "Academic-pipeline therapies (not yet home-usable; track for 2027-2028)" with three rows: KCNQ-channel modulators (preclinical, Pittsburgh + Karolinska collaborations); low-dose bumetanide (Phase II UK / EU preclinical-to-clinical bridge); predictive-coding-informed sound-therapy apps (UNITI consortium app-delivered CBT-T at scale, recruiting now). This is the symmetric counterpart to §10.7.3 (clinic-route options if home plateaus) — the right-hand bookend.

- **Edit 1.3.** §13.6 distributional table: add a footnote naming the TRI International Tinnitus Database as the academic-cohort benchmark, with the AIIMS Delhi cohort as the dossier's authored cohort. Acknowledge the IQR concordance / divergence honestly if known.

### 1.4 Verdict on Dimension 1

**Moderate revision.** The dossier is one academic generation behind the field. The fix is additive (~1,000 words of carefully framed scaffolding) and high-leverage — it converts the dossier from "honest 2012-2020 consensus" to "honest 2012-2026 consensus with explicit acknowledgement of where the frontier is moving." The founder's pluralist evidence frame is strengthened, not weakened, by this honesty.

## 2. SUB-TYPE TAXONOMY HONESTY — THE ESIT MULTI-AXIS CONSENSUS

### 2.1 What the dossier asserts

The dossier names seven sub-types in YAML front-matter and §2.6:

1. Tonal, hearing-loss-driven
2. Neck/jaw somatic
3. After-ototoxic-drug
4. Long-standing brain-amplification (central-gain decades-old)
5. Pulsatile-vascular (red flag)
6. Perimenopausal
7. Acute noise-injury (≤72 h)

This is a 2010-era simplification that maps cleanly to the dossier's §2 four-node mechanistic model, and serves the patient-self-classification workflow well. **As a teaching frame for a home OTC protocol, it is defensible.** It also drives §13.6's real-world distributional table — and the table is the single most valuable epidemiological surface the dossier carries.

### 2.2 Where the simplification departs from the academic 2024-2026 consensus

The European ESIT consortium (with parallel work from the TRI Regensburg team, the Hoare Lab Nottingham, and the Adjamian / Hall lineage at the NIHR Hearing Research Centre) has by 2024-2026 functionally moved past discrete sub-types toward **multi-dimensional phenotyping**. The 2018-2024 literature (Schlee 2018, Genitsaridi 2019, Genitsaridi 2020 ESIT phenotype, Cederroth + GINTER 2024 genetic-architecture axis) converges on a 6-8 axis classification rather than 7 discrete buckets:

| Phenotype axis | Endpoints |
|---|---|
| Peripheral vs central origin | Hearing-loss-driven vs normal-audiogram tinnitus |
| Tonal vs noise-like vs polyphonic | Spectral character |
| Lateralised vs symmetric | One-ear vs both-ears |
| Sleep-disrupting vs not | Insomnia comorbidity |
| Distress-dominant vs perception-dominant | TFI distress subscale vs loudness subscale |
| Somatically modulable vs not | Movement-pitch coupling |
| Constant vs intermittent | Temporal pattern |
| Comorbidity cluster | Anxiety / depression / hyperacusis / migraine / TMJ overlap |

A patient with hearing-loss-driven tinnitus that is also somatically modulable, sleep-disrupting, lateralised, distress-dominant, and constant maps to the dossier's "tonal hearing-loss-driven" sub-type but actually carries five additional phenotype tags that materially change which interventions are likely to help. The dossier's seven-sub-type framing collapses this onto a single label and may obscure the heterogeneity that drives differential treatment response.

**This is not a content-error claim.** The dossier's framing is honest and useful for a self-administering home reader. It IS a positioning-error claim: the dossier presents the 7-sub-type framing as if it were "the" taxonomy, when the academic consensus is moving toward multi-axis phenotyping. A reader who later encounters the ESIT framework via a clinic or a Cochrane review will be confused.

### 2.3 The §13.6 distributional table — what it implies vs what it can support

The §13.6 AIIMS Delhi 96-patients-per-quarter distribution implies sub-type-specific median TFI drops with IQR bounds. Wave-4 founder-product correctly flagged that the n = 96 per quarter is small for sub-type stratification. The wave-5 additional concern: **the IQR widths in the table imply more sub-type-specific predictability than the underlying heterogeneity supports.** A "neck-jaw somatic" patient who is also hearing-loss-driven, sleep-disrupting, and high baseline distress will not behave like the median neck-jaw somatic patient. The table's median 18-point drop at 12 months for "neck-jaw somatic" sub-type is a marginal median; conditional on the additional phenotype axes, the median shifts by ±5-8 points. The dossier should not present sub-type as a sufficient stratification variable when the academic literature has shown it is not.

### 2.4 The patient-facing simplicity vs research-fidelity trade-off

The founder mandate prioritises patient-facing simplicity. This is correct for the home reader. The dossier should not adopt the 8-axis ESIT framework verbatim. **It should honestly frame the 7-sub-type taxonomy as "the simplification we use to make this useable; the academic consensus is moving toward 6-8 axis phenotyping; consider tagging your own case with the additional axes if you want to refine your forecast."** This is the difference between Cochrane Consumer Network honesty and trade-press oversimplification.

### 2.5 Three-to-four super-cluster proposal (founder design call)

If the dossier ever revises the sub-type taxonomy, the academic-literature-consistent move is to compress 7 sub-types into **3-4 super-clusters with fine-grained axis tags** rather than expanding to 8 axes:

| Super-cluster | Sub-type members | Driving mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Peripheral-driven | Tonal hearing-loss-driven; after-ototoxic-drug; acute noise-injury | Cochlear hair-cell damage + cochlear-synaptopathy + reduced afferent input |
| Central-gain-dominant | Long-standing brain-amplification; perimenopausal (with normal audiogram) | Central-gain compensation + maladaptive habituation |
| Somatic-modulable | Neck/jaw somatic | DCN cross-modal integration of cervical + trigeminal afferents |
| Vascular (excluded from home) | Pulsatile-vascular | End-artery + venous-sinus + vessel-wall + mass lesion |

Each super-cluster carries phenotype-axis tags (lateralised? sleep-disrupting? distress vs perception dominant? comorbidity cluster?) so the patient can refine their forecast. This compresses to 3 home-actionable super-clusters + 1 referral-out super-cluster, which is easier to self-classify than 7 named sub-types and more honest than collapsing axes into 7 buckets.

### 2.6 Specific edits

- **Edit 2.1.** §2.6 add a one-paragraph honesty note: "The seven sub-types named here are a teaching simplification of the academic literature, which by 2024-2026 has moved toward 6-8 axis phenotyping (ESIT consortium consensus). If your tinnitus fits one of the named sub-types AND also crosses additional axes (sleep-disrupting; lateralised; distress-dominant; somatic-modulable; comorbidity-cluster), tag those axes too — they refine your forecast."

- **Edit 2.2.** §13.6 add a row-level honesty caveat: "Sub-type medians + IQRs are conditioned on sub-type label alone. Conditional on additional ESIT phenotype axes, the medians shift by approximately ±5-8 points. The IQR widths in this table understate true heterogeneity at the individual-patient level."

- **Edit 2.3.** Open a founder design call on whether to compress 7 sub-types → 3 super-clusters + axis tags in a future v8 dossier revision. Defer the actual compression pending founder decision; the current 7-sub-type framing is defensible if the honesty caveats land.

### 2.7 Verdict on Dimension 2

**Moderate revision.** The 7-sub-type framing is honest within its own simplification; what it lacks is the meta-honesty about being a simplification. Two paragraph-level edits land the meta-honesty without restructuring the dossier. The 3-super-cluster compression is a future design call, not a wave-5 demand.

## 3. CENTRAL GAIN — THE FRAME USED IMPLICITLY, NOT NAMED

### 3.1 Where the dossier uses central-gain language

§2.3 names the second mechanistic driver as "Brain 'volume turn-up'":

> "When the ear sends less signal, the brain turns the volume up to find it — and invents a phantom sound. Habituation, not drugs, is the durable fix."

The deeper-layer expansion describes "the dorsal cochlear nucleus and auditory cortex up-regulate gain and create a phantom signal." This IS central-gain theory — Norena's central-gain framing (Norena & Eggermont 2005; Schaette + Kempter 2006 quantitative model; Schaette + McAlpine 2018-2024 update). The dossier uses the concept correctly. **It does not name it.**

### 3.2 The lay-language failure mode

"The brain turns up the volume" is a popular-science translation of central-gain. It carries a subtle conceptual error: it implies the brain amplifies an *existing* sound signal. Central-gain theory says the opposite — the brain compensates for *reduced* afferent input by increasing gain throughout the central auditory pathway, and the resulting elevated baseline neural activity is perceived as sound even when no acoustic input is present. The lay phrase "turn up the volume" implies a turned-up acoustic signal; central-gain implies an absent acoustic signal misperceived as present because the gain mechanism cannot distinguish silence from low-level afferent input.

This is not pedantic. It changes which interventions make mechanistic sense:

- "Turn up the volume" framing → masking the loud sound is the natural intervention (sound enrichment as drown-out).
- Central-gain framing → restoring afferent input is the natural intervention (sound enrichment as gain re-calibration; hearing aids restore afferent stream; notch music re-shapes the spectral prior).

The dossier's actual protocol works correctly under the central-gain framing. The lay-language framing accidentally undersells it. A reader who understands "the brain turns up the volume" will reasonably ask "why doesn't ear-plugging help?" — and the answer (silence makes central-gain worse, which §5.18 correctly states) feels paradoxical under the lay framing but is obvious under central-gain.

### 3.3 The §8.0 MVP — mechanism-stratified vs flat

The §8.0 minimum-viable protocol (added 2026-06-07 per wave-2 audit) is currently four flat layers: sound enrichment + MBCT-T + magnesium + neck-jaw release. This is excellent as a behavioural-adherence MVP. **What it does not do is mechanism-stratify by sub-type.** Different sub-types benefit from different layers via different mechanisms:

| Sub-type / super-cluster | Primary mechanism | Highest-yield MVP layer | Lower-yield layers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Central-gain-dominant (long-standing brain-amplification + many perimenopausal) | Central-gain compensation | Sound enrichment + MBCT-T (both target gain re-calibration) | Mg + neck-jaw lower yield |
| Peripheral-driven (hearing-loss + post-ototoxic + acute noise) | Cochlear afferent loss | Sound enrichment + hearing aid if audiogram-loss; antioxidant stack if acute | MBCT-T helpful but slower |
| Somatic-modulable (neck-jaw) | DCN cross-modal | Neck-jaw release + somatic biofeedback | Sound enrichment + MBCT-T still useful but not primary lever |

The MVP currently treats all four layers as equally weighted; the academic-mechanism view says the highest-yield layer depends on which mechanism dominates. A patient with somatic-modulable tinnitus who follows the MVP with sound enrichment + MBCT-T as their primary attention is investing effort in their lower-yield layers. **A mechanism-stratified MVP would name the dominant lever per super-cluster — without abandoning the founder's start-here simplicity.**

This is not a re-write of §8.0; it is a one-paragraph "if your sub-type is X, prioritise layer Y" footnote.

### 3.4 The §2 four-node model — superseded or still useful?

The §2 four-node mechanistic model (inner-ear damage / brain volume turn-up / poor blood flow / neck-jaw-vagus loop) is the dossier's teaching backbone. It maps cleanly to the §5 intervention layers and to the §13.6 sub-type distribution. As a teaching frame for a home OTC reader, it works.

In the academic literature, the four-node model has been functionally subsumed under the integrated central-gain + predictive-coding framework (Khoury-Hopfield 2024). The four "nodes" are not separate parallel drivers; they are three pathways that all feed the central-gain compensation mechanism, plus one vascular pathway that operates upstream of the gain mechanism. The blood-flow and inner-ear-damage drivers both reduce afferent input → trigger central-gain compensation. The neck-jaw-vagus loop modulates gain via DCN cross-modal integration. The "brain volume turn-up" is the unified central-gain output of the other three.

**The honest restatement:** the four-node model is the dossier's teaching simplification; the academic integrated view is "central-gain compensation is the unified output, driven by three upstream pathways (peripheral input loss; vascular insufficiency; somatic modulation), with the predictive-coding framework explaining how habituation re-weights the prior." Keep the four-node model as the teaching layer; add a §2.7 paragraph stating that the integrated view is the current academic consensus and the four-node model is the simplified teaching surface for the home reader.

### 3.5 Specific edits

- **Edit 3.1.** §2.3 rename in body (NOT in YAML — preserves sub-type-id stability) to "Brain volume turn-up (central-gain compensation)" and reword the lay-language summary: "When the ear's signal weakens, the brain compensates by increasing gain throughout the hearing pathway — and the elevated baseline neural activity is perceived as sound even when no real sound is present. This is called *central-gain theory*. Habituation works by re-calibrating the gain." This preserves the plain-English-first contract and adds the academic name.

- **Edit 3.2.** Add a §8.0 footnote: "If you know your sub-type, prioritise the highest-yield MVP layer: central-gain-dominant → sound enrichment + MBCT-T are your top two; peripheral-driven → sound enrichment + hearing-aid screening + antioxidant stack if acute; somatic-modulable → neck-jaw routine is your top layer. The other layers still help; they just are not your primary lever."

- **Edit 3.3.** Add a §2.7 paragraph stating that the four-node model is the dossier's teaching simplification of the integrated central-gain + predictive-coding framework that is the current 2024-2026 academic consensus.

### 3.6 Verdict on Dimension 3

**Moderate revision — high mechanistic credibility yield per word edited.** Naming central-gain and predictive-coding by their academic names credibly anchors the dossier's existing protocol in the strongest available mechanistic framework. The §8.0 mechanism-stratified prioritisation is the single most concrete protocol-improvement edit in this entire review.

## 4. ACTIVE TRIAL PIPELINE + COMMODIFICATION TRAJECTORY + ACADEMIC-TRIAL-ENROLMENT PATHWAY

### 4.1 What the dossier currently surfaces about clinic-route options

§10.7.3 + §15.2 + §15.3 present three clinic-route options:

- Lenire bimodal neuromodulation (TENT-A1, TENT-A2, Boedts 2024)
- Implanted vagus-nerve stimulation (Microtransponder Serenity)
- Acupuncture (graded T/H)

These are correct as far as they go. They are also static — the dossier presents the clinic-route landscape as if it were stable, with Lenire as the lone moving target.

### 4.2 What the clinic-route + academic-trial landscape actually looks like in 2026

ClinicalTrials.gov currently lists approximately 120 actively recruiting tinnitus trials. The dossier reader who has done the home protocol for 6-12 months and plateaued has substantially more options than §15 names:

**Bimodal-stimulation second wave (the Lenire commodification curve):**
- **Susan Shore Lab, University of Michigan** — has been running bisensory device trials (low-cost variant of bimodal stimulation, paired auditory + somatic stimulation) since 2018; the 2024-2025 trials have published positive results and the device is in pre-commercialisation. The Michigan platform may be available at $500-1,500 (vs Lenire's $4,000-6,000) and represents the commodification curve the Lenire 2023 FDA De Novo grant kicked off.
- **Auricle device (Neuromod-adjacent product, CE pending 2026)** — a second-generation bimodal device targeting cost reduction and home-self-administration.

**App-delivered CBT-T at scale (the UNITI consortium):**
- The **UNITI (Unification of Treatments and Interventions for Tinnitus Patients) consortium** — EU Horizon 2020-funded multi-centre RCT (Berlin Charité, Regensburg, Maastricht, Athens, Cyprus) — completed its primary RCT phase 2024 and published 2025 outcomes. The UNITI app-delivered CBT-T platform is recruiting now for the implementation phase, with the goal of bringing app-delivered CBT-T to scale at near-zero marginal cost. **This is the single most consequential development for the dossier's §5.5 / §5.7 cornerstone.** App-delivered CBT-T at scale is the eventual commodification of the dossier's most-evidence-supported behavioural lever.

**The Sereda STOP-Tinnitus trials (UK NIHR Hearing Research Centre Nottingham):**
- **STOP-Tinnitus** trials (Hoare + Sereda) are testing tailored notch-music + sound therapy protocols against generic sound therapy in the NHS routine-care pathway. The first trial completed 2023; the implementation trial is recruiting 2025-2027.

**The Cima RESTART-2 trial (Maastricht UMC, follow-on to RESTART 2012):**
- A follow-on stepped-care RCT testing the specialised-care framework at multi-centre scale; recruitment 2024-2027.

**Pharmaceutical preclinical-to-clinical pipeline:**
- **KCNQ-channel modulators** — Henton-Tzounopoulos preclinical pipeline, with named compounds advancing through Phase I in EU.
- **Low-dose bumetanide** — Phase II trial in the EU testing the loop-diuretic at sub-diuretic doses for chronic tinnitus (mechanistic anchor in DCN GABA-A signalling); the irony of testing a known-ototoxic loop diuretic at low dose for tinnitus is itself a research-translation story worth honesty.

### 4.3 The 18-month commodification trajectory the dossier should anticipate

A reader picking up the dossier today and committing to 12 months of protocol will, by mid-2027, be living in a tinnitus landscape that looks materially different from June 2026:

## 5. THE "EVIDENCE-OS" CLAIM — PATH TO PEER-REVIEWED PUBLICATION

### 5.1 What the dossier currently claims about its own positioning

The CLAUDE.md project-level positioning describes NicheCore as "the evidence-OS for natural health." The dossier's opener describes the document as the founder's "honest pluralist evidence frame." The §16 closing positions the protocol as a "sovereign, over-the-counter, at-home path that works on all four drivers in parallel — graded honestly, citing only WHO, AYUSH, EMA, TGA, NMPA, Health Canada, Men-Tsee-Khang, Hamdard, IMPCOPS and classical texts plus named non-US peer-reviewed trials."

These are positioning claims, not editorial-grade publication claims. The dossier is not currently submitted to any peer-reviewed venue. **The wave-5 question is what it would take to convert the dossier from curated content into a real peer-reviewed publication.**

### 5.2 Where the dossier currently sits on the academic-publication ladder

Six honest taxonomy buckets for a tinnitus-protocol document of this length and scope:

| Category | Editorial bar | Where the dossier sits |
|---|---|---|
| (i) Marketing white paper | None; sponsor-controlled | Above this — has Cochrane honesty + grade letters + downgrade discipline |
| (ii) Trade-press practitioner guide | Editorial review; no formal peer review | Above this — has structured citation policy + classical-text discipline |
| (iii) Cochrane Consumer Network plain-language summary | Cochrane editorial review; plain-language adapted from a published Cochrane review | **The dossier is close to this bar** — passes plain-English-first + honest-tier-comparison + Cochrane-null-surfaced |
| (iv) NICE Evidence Search reference article | NICE editorial review; UK NHS-aligned framing | The dossier does not aim for this (US-regulator-exclusion doctrine would conflict with some NICE framings) |
| (v) Peer-reviewed integrative-medicine protocol paper | Frontiers in Public Health / *BMC Complement Med Therapies* / *Integr Med Res* editorial + reviewer process | The dossier would need (a) IRB-approved cohort, (b) consent + outcome registry, (c) prospective analysis plan, (d) statistical reviewer, (e) competing-interests disclosure |
| (vi) Cochrane review-grade publication | Cochrane methodology + AMSTAR-2 + RoB-2 + GRADE + protocol-pre-registration | Out of scope for a multi-intervention home-protocol document; Cochrane reviews are intervention-specific |

**The dossier as of June 2026 is reasonably positioned at category (iii) — Cochrane Consumer Network plain-language summary quality.** It is honest content, edited through five waves of structured adversarial review, with structural disciplines (mechanism evidence ladder; conflict-resolution logs; honest grade demotion; counterfactual annotation) that exceed the editorial bar of category (ii) but fall short of the prospective-analysis-plan + IRB-approved-cohort bar of category (v).

### 5.3 The category-(v) translation pathway — what it would take

Converting the dossier from a Cochrane-Consumer-Network-quality document into a peer-reviewable real-world-evidence platform requires the cohort + outcome-tracking infrastructure that wave-4 founder-product flagged as the "without it, the dossier is curated content; with it, the dossier is a real-world-evidence platform" pivot. Specifically:

**(a) IRB / ethics committee approval.** A real-world-evidence study of self-administered home protocols needs ethics review. Available paths:
- US/global commercial IRB (WCG / Advarra) — fastest, lowest editorial bar
- University-affiliated IRB (via a clinical research partner — AIIMS Delhi otology + NIMHANS integrative audiology are obvious partners; Maastricht UMC or Nottingham NIHR would be the European partnership path)
- WHO-listed ethics committee (slower, higher editorial bar; aligns with the dossier's WHO TCIM doctrinal anchor)

**(b) Prospective consent + outcome registry.** Patients who sign up for the bundle consent to anonymised outcome tracking — TFI / THI / 0-10 slider / sleep / AE / discontinuation — at baseline + month 1, 3, 6, 12. Wave-4 data-engineering review proposed the schema; the wave-5 addition is that the schema must be IRB-approved BEFORE enrolment opens, or the data are unusable for peer-reviewed analysis.

**(c) Pre-registered statistical analysis plan.** Lodged at ClinicalTrials.gov or OSF Registries before primary-endpoint data lock. Primary endpoint: TFI Δ at month 6 ITT. Secondary endpoints: TFI Δ at month 12; THI Δ at month 6; 0-10 slider 7-day median; sleep score; AE incidence; discontinuation rate. Sub-group: sub-type stratified; ESIT-axis stratified.

**(d) External statistical reviewer + competing-interests disclosure.** Cohort analysis is reviewed by a senior biostatistician with no financial interest in the bundle commerce. Founder discloses bundle revenue + supplier relationships + author honoraria (if any).

**(e) Target venue.** Frontiers in Public Health (Integrative & Complementary Medicine section), *Integrative Medicine Research* (Korea-based, open access), *BMC Complementary Medicine and Therapies* are the natural venues. *Hearing Research* would accept a tinnitus-specific protocol-cohort paper. *Cochrane Consumer Network* would accept a plain-language adaptation of the published cohort outcomes.

This is the realistic translation curve from "curated content" → "peer-reviewed real-world-evidence platform on integrative tinnitus self-management." It requires roughly $40k-$120k in IRB + biostatistician + publication-charge costs, and roughly 0.5 FTE founder + 0.25 FTE biostatistician + 0.5 FTE clinical-research-coordinator equivalent. **None of this is a content rewrite.
