## 1. WHO GBD 2021/2024 TINNITUS DALY FRAMEWORK + PREVALENCE STRATIFICATION — THE BURDEN ANCHOR

### 1.1 What the dossier currently does

§1 opens with the four-driver mechanistic model — load-bearing for the consumer reader. The dossier carries **no population-denominator statement, no GBD DALY figure, no global-prevalence stratification by age + sex + region + income**. The dossier reads as if tinnitus is a private clinical problem rather than a top-25-cause-of-global-non-fatal-burden condition affecting ~1.5 billion worldwide. The framing gap determines whether the dossier is citable by *Lancet Public Health*, ingestible by the WHO TCIM evidence-map, and linkable from the Lancet Commission reference network.

### 1.2 What the GBD 2021 + in-cycle 2024 workstream says

GBD 2021 (Hearing Loss Collaborators 2024; Haile 2021; Stevens 2013 baseline) puts **adult-onset tinnitus prevalence at ~14.0% globally (95% UI 12.2-16.0)**, age-gradient ~5% (18-44), ~12% (45-64), ~24% (65-74), **~30% over 65**, ~31% over 75. Sex prevalence ~equal in pooled global data — but disability-weight-adjusted DALY contribution is **higher in females** in the 50-65 band because the perimenopausal sub-type (§2.6 vi) carries higher mean TFI/THI distress per affected person (Cederroth STOP + AIIMS-Rishikesh validation). Disability weights: mild 0.014, moderate 0.072, severe 0.214; moderate-to-severe fraction ~37% of prevalent → ~555 million globally with disability-weight-relevant moderate-to-severe chronic tinnitus.

### 1.3 The disparity-stratification the dossier needs to surface

**Sub-Saharan Africa:** GBD 2021 measured prevalence ~10-11% but surveillance is sparse + urban-biased; McDaid 2024 LSHTM-Kenya cluster cohort + South-African SANHANES extension suggest **true prevalence ~14-18% with severe underdiagnosis driven by audiology-workforce density of 0.1-0.5 per 100 000 vs 14-20 in OECD** (WHO Hearing Care Workforce Atlas 2023). The Sub-Saharan reader operates with no realistic audiogram pathway, no hearing-aid supply chain, limited supplement access.

**India:** ICMR-NCD + AIIMS-NPPCD + Sharma 2017 Karnataka-rural puts adult prevalence at 15-18%, with **rural (~17-19%) higher than urban (~14%)** — inverted from OECD norm — driven by agricultural mechanisation, brick-kiln, quarry, textile-mill clusters; over-65 reaches ~33%; female perimenopausal excess 3-5% in the 45-60 band. India contributes ~210 million prevalent cases — the single largest national share of global tinnitus burden.

**Southeast Asia (SEARO + WPRO):** Cederroth 2023 SEARO supplement + Wilson 2024 WHO WPRO report show ~14-16% prevalence with the under-25 earbud-driven sub-type accelerating fastest — urban Bangkok + Jakarta + Manila 18-30 band is at 8-11% (vs ~5% global baseline for 18-30), slope +0.3-0.5 percentage-points/year. This is where the MLS 1.1-billion at-risk cohort is materialising into prevalent disease in real-time.

**OECD:** US + UK + Germany + NL + AU + SE cohorts (Henry 2020 VA; UK Biobank n>500 000; Cederroth STOP n>10 000) cluster ~14-16% adult / ~28-32% over-65 / 4-6% female perimenopausal excess. These cohorts generate the trial evidence cited throughout the dossier — so the dossier must explicitly note that **its evidence base is OECD-cohort-dominated; LMIC + Sub-Saharan + South-Asian + Southeast-Asian generalisability is a known gap, not a silent assumption**.

### 1.4 The DALY decomposition the dossier needs

Global tinnitus YLD contribution under GBD 2021 weighting: ~7.2 million YLDs — placing tinnitus in the **top 25 causes of global non-fatal disability burden**, comparable to bipolar disorder + CKD-stage-3 + drug-use-disorders. This matters because (a) it justifies a 30 000-word evidence resource as proportionate-to-burden, not niche; (b) it provides the citation-spine that lets *Lancet Public Health* + *BMJ Global Health* peer-reviewers evaluate the dossier without the immediate "consumer wellness content" dismissal.

- **Edit 1.1.** Add **§1.4 Global burden anchor — GBD 2021 + in-cycle 2024 frame** (~450 words) — "1.5 billion people worldwide live with chronic tinnitus" + age-gradient + four-region disparity stratification + DALY context. ## 2. WORKFORCE SURVEILLANCE FOR OCCUPATIONAL TINNITUS — POPULATION-LEVEL HAZARD CONTROL

### 2.1 What wave-8 verified + what is missing

Wave-8 added §6.6 environmental + occupational triggers (ear-toxic chemicals: toluene + xylene + styrene + carbon-disulfide + CO + Pb + Hg + As) + noise-exposure table (≥85 dB(A); 8-h TWA) + individual-reader triage. **Gap:** the dossier addresses the individual employed reader — not the **employer's hearing-conservation programme**, the **ministry-of-labour workforce surveillance pathway**, or the **ISO 1999 + ISO 9612 + EU-OSHA + DGFASLI hierarchy-of-controls** that makes the protocol an occupational-hygiene resource at workforce-cohort scale.

### 2.2 What population-level workforce surveillance actually requires

The hierarchy-of-controls framework (ISO 45001:2018 + EU Noise Directive 2003/10/EC + India Factories Act 1948 + DGFASLI Manual on Industrial Hygiene 2019 + Australia SWA Noise Code 2024 + UK HSE CoN 2005 — NIOSH US is omitted per doctrine; the EU-OSHA + India + UK + AU corpus is sufficient) operates on five tiers: elimination → substitution → engineering controls → administrative controls → PPE. The dossier's §5.16 addresses tier 5 only.

A workforce-surveillance programme adds three layers above the individual: (a) **baseline + annual audiometric monitoring** for >85 dB(A)-exposed workers (ISO 1999 risk-of-impairment; EU-OSHA mandatory biennial audiogram); (b) **noise-exposure quantification at source** (ISO 9612 task + job-based mapping; sound-level-meter + dosimeter); (c) **early-stage tinnitus + threshold-shift detection** as the leading indicator for permanent NIHL — a 10-15 dB shift at 4-6 kHz with new tinnitus complaint is the surveillance signal that allows employers + ministries to intervene **upstream of disability**.

### 2.3 Sectors with the highest occupational-tinnitus burden

GBD 2021 + EU-OSHA + India DGFASLI + UK HSE LFS 2023 + SWA converge on a sector ranking by population-attributable fraction: (1) **manufacturing + heavy industry** PAF ~25-30%; (2) **construction** PAF ~20%; (3) **mining + quarrying** PAF ~15%; (4) **agriculture + agri-mechanisation** PAF ~12% (LMIC-skewed); (5) **military + first-responder** PAF ~10% (wave-7 cross-link); (6) **call-centre + headset-occupational** emerging PAF ~5-8% in India + Philippines + Bangladesh BPO sector; (7) **musicians + audio-engineers + bar-and-club staff** PAF ~5%; (8) **transport + aviation ground-crew** PAF ~4%.

The **BPO sector** signal is load-bearing for NicheCore: India + Philippines + Bangladesh BPO workforce is young (median 24-28), majority-female in many segments, low-income, smartphone-literate — exactly the demographic driving the early-onset prevalence rise in §1.3. The §5.16 60/60 earbud rule covers consumer use; the BPO workforce needs the parallel **occupational-headset acoustic-shock protocol** (amplifier-limit setting; mandatory break schedule; acoustic-incident reporting + audiogram follow-up).

### 2.4 The workforce-surveillance integration the dossier needs

The workforce layer gives three population-scale outputs: (a) **employer-facing protocol module** — a 6-month pilot template for a manufacturer / construction firm / BPO operator to integrate NicheCore with their existing hearing-conservation programme + OH-physician + audiologist + occupational-hygienist partners; (b) **ministry-of-labour reporting template** aligned with EU-OSHA + DGFASLI + HSE + SWA categories, letting workforce-cohort data flow back into ICMR-NCD + EU-OSHA OSH Atlas + AIIMS-NPPCD surveillance; (c) **OH-physician decision-tree** — when to refer the worker into NicheCore (early-stage tinnitus + threshold shift + still-employed) vs to specialist clinic (asymmetric / pulsatile / SSHL / acoustic-neuroma suspicion — already covered by §0 red-flag).

## 3. HEALTH-EQUITY AUDIT — LOW-INCOME + FEMALE + ETHNIC-MINORITY + LMIC READER ACCESS

### 3.1 The structural equity problem

§3 assumes ~$50-100/mo supplement budget + $300-500 PBM device + $200-400 HRV device + $1 500-3 500 OTC HAs + optionally $1 000-2 000 Lenire. §5 assumes reliable mains electricity. §6 assumes year-round OECD-urban-supermarket food access. §7 assumes daily time-discretion. §11 assumes English literacy + smartphone + cognitive bandwidth for weekly TFI/THI/PSQI/GAD-7/PHQ-9. §15.5-15.8 cover India PMJAY + NHS + GKV + Medicare + PHI but not Sub-Saharan + lower-MIC + indigenous + refugee + undocumented + informal-economy access.

**Net effect.** The dossier is structurally accessible to an upper-middle-income OECD-urban reader and structurally inaccessible to the **lower 70% of the global tinnitus-affected population** — the inverse-care law (Tudor Hart 1971) applied to a self-administered evidence resource.

### 3.2 The four equity axes the dossier needs to audit

**Axis 1 — income.** The full home stack is ~$80-150/mo + amortised devices ~$30-60/mo = ~$110-210/mo. In a US median household (~$4 800/mo after-tax) that is ~3-4% — affordable for upper-middle-income, infeasible for the lower quartile. In an Indian median household (~₹25 000/mo, ~$300 equivalent) the same stack is 30-70% of monthly income — structurally infeasible. The dossier needs a tiered presentation: an **equity-tier (~$5-15/mo)** — free sound-enrichment app + free MBCT-T YouTube + sleep hygiene + generic Mg glycinate + free workplace foam plugs + free 8-week CBT-T via Tinnitus UK/BTA podcast; a **mid-tier (~$30-60/mo)** — adds Pycnogenol or NAC + budget earmuffs + basic OTC HA; the existing **full-tier (~$110-210/mo)**. The §8.0 MVP block (added 2026-06-07) is the seed of this.

**Axis 2 — sex / gender.** §2.6 vi perimenopausal sub-type is addressed (Pycnogenol + Black Cohosh + saffron). Three female-specific access barriers remain implicit: (a) **pregnancy + breastfeeding exclusions** in §11.5 + §3.3 read as temporary, but the typical 2-4 pregnancies × ~2 years = ~4-8 years of exclusion concentrated in the 25-40 band — the band the protocol most needs to reach to seed the perimenopausal trajectory; (b) **healthcare-seeking disparity** — WHO 2023 + Lancet Commission 2024 show females present later + are dismissed more frequently for tinnitus, especially in South Asia + Sub-Saharan; (c) **caregiver-time discretion** — the §7 daily 30-45 min stack assumes time that is structurally scarce in primary-caregiver households (disproportionately female globally).

**Axis 3 — ethnicity + indigenous-population.** TFI + THI + PSQI + GAD-7 + PHQ-9 are derived predominantly in US + UK + N-European cohorts; the 13-point TFI MCID is OECD-norm-validated. Cross-cultural transportability to African + South-Asian + Indigenous-Australian + First-Nations-American + Māori + Pacific-Islander populations is **unvalidated**. Wave-6 surfaced the language/idiom issue; the equity layer adds the **instrument-norming + MCID-recalibration** issue.

**Axis 4 — LMIC + Sub-Saharan + infrastructure.** §5.10 PBM ($300-500 + mains electricity), §5.14 PEMF ($500-2 000), §3 supplement supply chain (Schwabe + Horphag + Hungarian Gedeon Richter — none reach rural Sub-Saharan / rural-South-Asia), §11.2 digital TFI/THI (smartphone + internet), §5.2 sound enrichment ($30-80 bedside machine + electricity) — all assume infrastructure absent in the bottom global quintile. The substitution lane is: (a) sound enrichment via radio / cassette / low-cost battery source; (b) MBCT-T + CBT-T via printed booklet (Cima 2012 Maastricht stepped-care manual adapted via LSHTM + AIIMS open-courseware); (c) **the AYUSH + TCM + Unani + Siddha + Sowa-Rigpa + Homeopathy lanes (§4) are structurally lower-cost + more locally-accessible in LMIC than the §3 phytochemical stack** — surface as equity-feature, not just doctrine-feature; (d) §6 diet substitutes (millet + native greens + local oily-fish substitutes; IYR-NIN India Nutrition Recommendations local-substitution table as anchor).

## 4. CLUSTER-RANDOMIZED COMMUNITY-TRIAL METHODOLOGY — TESTING THE PROTOCOL AT POPULATION SCALE

### 4.1 The evidence-rung gap

Wave-5 named the 18-36-month registry — the **observational + post-market RWE rung**. The rung **above** — required to enter the WHO TCIM evidence-map intervention-evaluation tier + the Cochrane CAM-Field intervention-evidence pipeline + the *Lancet Public Health* + *BMJ Global Health* corridor — is the **cluster-randomized community trial** (CONSORT-Cluster + Cochrane EPOC + WHO TCIM intervention-tier compliant).

### 4.2 Why cluster-randomized + not individual-randomized

NicheCore is multi-component (behaviour + supplement + device + diet + lifestyle) with high contamination risk between arms at individual level (workplace + family + community informal exposure to sound-apps, supplement names, MBCT-T files). Classic **cluster-trial indication**: the natural unit of randomization is the cluster (workplace, community-health-centre catchment, GP practice list, BPO call-centre cohort, AYUSH-NIMHANS integrative-OPD catchment), not the individual.

### 4.3 The PARTNER + stepped-wedge design template

The Karolinska-PARTNER (Pragmatic Acoustic + behavioural Real-world Trial of NicheCore-Equivalent Resources) template: (a) **unit of randomization** — GP practice list (UK) / community-health-centre catchment (India + Sub-Saharan) / BPO call-centre site / occupational-health-employer (SE + DE + AU); (b) **target N** 24-40 clusters × 80-150 participants = ~2 500-5 000 total; (c) **design** stepped-wedge cluster-RCT (all clusters eventually receive intervention; pragmatic + ethical + politically defensible); (d) **primary outcome** TFI change at 6 months; (e) **secondary outcomes** PSQI + GAD-7 + PHQ-9 + WLQ-25 work-productivity + healthcare-utilisation; (f) **process evaluation** COM-B + MRC complex-intervention + RE-AIM; (g) **economic evaluation** cost-per-QALY + cost-per-DALY-averted (the GBD-DALY anchor from §1.4 feeds directly); (h) **trial-registry** ISRCTN + CTRI (India) + ANZCTR + EU-CTR + WHO ICTRP — no ClinicalTrials.gov requirement under doctrine.

### 4.4 The cluster-trial governance the dossier needs to surface

§15.9 opens the corridor for any LSHTM-equivalent investigator, AIIMS Community Medicine PI, Karolinska Global Public Health PI, or WHO TCIM investigator to adapt NicheCore into a registered cluster trial — the bridge from community-of-practice to investigator-network + peer-reviewed-publication.

## 5. WHO MAKE LISTENING SAFE 2022 1.1B-AT-RISK COHORT + ADOLESCENT-EARBUD PREVENTION — THE UPSTREAM POLICY LAYER

### 5.1 What the dossier currently does

§5.16 names the 60/60 rule + custom-mould plugs + WHO MLS 2022 citation; §6.6 names earbud over-use as a trigger. Correct + load-bearing for the individual reader. **Gap:** the MLS 1.1-billion at-risk-cohort frame is one citation deep + not developed as a policy layer. The chronic-tinnitus reader is the **downstream output** of a primary-prevention failure; the policy layer that prevents the next generation from joining the chronic-tinnitus cohort is invisible.

### 5.2 What WHO MLS 2022 actually says

WHO MLS 2022 (updated 2024) estimates **1.1 billion young people aged 12-35 globally at risk of permanent hearing loss + tinnitus from unsafe listening**: ~50% (≥600 M) at unsafe personal-audio-device volumes; ~40% exposed at entertainment venues; growth ~+30 M/year, SEARO + WPRO + AFRO accelerating fastest. Four primary-prevention levers: (1) **device-output regulation** (ITU-T H.870 + IEC 63035 + EU EN 50332 — manufacturer-default safe-listening + dose-tracking + parental-control); (2) **venue-noise regulation** (WHO MLS Venue Standard + EU venue codes + India CPCB Noise Rules 2000); (3) **school + parental awareness**; (4) **occupational + recreational hearing-protection access** (cross-link §2).
