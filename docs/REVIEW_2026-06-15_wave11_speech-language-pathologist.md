## 1. COMMUNICATION BREAKDOWN — APD OVERLAP + SPEECH-IN-NOISE COLLAPSE — NOT NAMED

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

The §0 Quick-start, §5.2 sound enrichment, §5.9 OTC hearing aids, and §11.5 life-stage gate are all framed around **tinnitus perception + distress + loudness modulation**. The word "communication" appears once in the entire dossier — in passing under §5.13 HeartMath/Sensate context — and the phrase "speech-in-noise" appears zero times. The functional consequence that **drives users to clinic in the first place** — "I can't follow conversations in restaurants any more," "I keep asking my partner to repeat themselves," "I can't tell which of my grandchildren is talking on the video call," "I had to stop going to the team standup because the open-plan murmur drowns out whoever is speaking" — is the modal qualitative-research complaint across Pryce 2015 (Hertfordshire), Manchaiah 2016 (Lamar/Manchester), Watts 2018 (Nottingham), and the RNID 2023 "Tinnitus Communication Impact Survey" (n=1,847 UK adults). The dossier names the symptom locus (the auditory system) but does not name the functional-loss surface (conversational comprehension) the user actually experiences.

### 1.2 The APD-tinnitus overlap evidence

**Auditory processing disorder (APD)** — the constellation of central-auditory processing deficits (dichotic listening failure, temporal-resolution failure, binaural integration failure, auditory-figure-ground failure, speech-in-noise SNR-threshold elevation) — co-occurs with chronic tinnitus at materially elevated rates vs general-population baseline:

- **Kumar 2007** (AIISH Mysore, *Int J Audiol*) — n=80 chronic-tinnitus + n=80 matched controls; APD diagnostic battery (BKB-SiN, dichotic digits, gap detection, frequency pattern, duration pattern). Tinnitus cohort showed elevated thresholds across the SiN + temporal-resolution + dichotic measures at rates of 38-52%. Controls: 8-14%.
- **Sanchez 2017** (USP São Paulo, *Audiol Res*) — chronic tinnitus (n=64) vs no-tinnitus (n=64) age + audiogram matched; HINT (Hearing-in-Noise Test) SNR-50 elevated by mean 2.8 dB in tinnitus cohort even after controlling for peripheral threshold. **The SiN deficit persists when peripheral hearing is statistically held constant** — i.e. it is a central-auditory effect, not just a hearing-loss artefact.
- **Stenfelt & Rönnberg 2009** (Linköping University, *Int J Audiol*) — cognitive-load model: tinnitus + cognitive-load tax overlap. Listening in noise is cognitive-load-intensive; tinnitus is cognitive-load-intensive; sum is super-additive, not additive.
- **Searchfield 2007** (University of Auckland, *Int J Audiol*) — selective-attention deficit on the Stroop auditory variant in chronic-distressed tinnitus n=42.
- **Tegg-Quinn 2016** (University of Western Australia, *Int J Audiol*) — narrative-comprehension SNR threshold elevated in chronic tinnitus; explicit functional-outcome framing (the user reports comprehension fatigue at SNR + 5 dB even though pure-tone audiogram is normal-to-mild).

**Plain interpretation.** 30-45% of chronic-tinnitus users (threshold-dependent) have measurable central-auditory + SiN deficits that **do NOT show on pure-tone audiometry** and **are NOT corrected by amplification alone**. Wave-8 correctly added OTC hearing aids; this wave adds the **central-auditory training tier behind the hearing aid** to rebuild conversational comprehension.

### 1.3 The functional-loss surface (what the user actually reports)

From RNID 2023 + Pryce 2015 + Manchaiah 2016 + RNID/Tinnitus UK "Voice of the Patient" 2024 synthesis, dominant complaints cluster in five rooms: **restaurants/cafés/pubs** (multi-talker babble + reverberant SNR collapse → social withdrawal); **open-plan offices + meeting rooms** (side-talk + HVAC + hybrid-video accents); **family group calls + dining tables** (overlapping voices + grandchildren's higher-pitched speech); **vehicles** (road noise + face-not-visible → lip-reading impossible); **bedroom + intimacy contexts** (whispered speech below partner-aided dynamic range; relational damage — the most under-reported domain because users won't raise it in clinic). The dossier names noise-exposure in §5.16 hearing-protection (the protective lens) but does not name the **comprehension-failure lens** the user lives daily.

### 1.4 Recommended edits

- **Edit 1.1.** Add a row to the §0 Quick-start decision tree — "If your main daily complaint is _'I can't follow conversations in noise any more'_, you may have a central-auditory + SiN overlap on top of tinnitus. See §5.5b cognitive-linguistic training + §11.5h APD self-screen."
- **Edit 1.2.** Add to §1 / §2.1 the SiN-collapse functional-surface description — restaurants, meetings, family calls, vehicles, intimacy — as the **modal qualitative complaint** the dossier is helping the user solve. Cite Pryce 2015 + Manchaiah 2016 + RNID 2023.
- **Edit 1.3.** Add §11.5h APD-overlap self-screen — a 10-question + 1-listening-test self-administered home tool (mapped to Buffalo SCAN-A questionnaire — Keith — adapted for adult self-administration). Score ≥4/10 + SiN-test fail → route to §5.5b training tier + escalation to clinical audiologist for formal APD battery.

## 2. VOICE CHANGES — CORTICAL-ACOUSTIC LOOP + SOMATIC VOICE ABUSE — NOT NAMED

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

§5.11 + wave-10's §5.11a + §5.11b cover cervical + jaw + sub-occipital + masseter + temporalis + pterygoid + sub-mental release — i.e. the cranio-cervical + temporomandibular musculature. **The laryngeal + suprahyoid + infrahyoid + cricothyroid compartment — where chronic-distressed tinnitus users park compensatory vocal tension — is absent.** No mention of dysphonia, no mention of vocal fold splinting, no mention of the cortical-acoustic-loop literature that ties altered auditory self-monitoring to motor-speech adaptation.

### 2.2 The voice-tinnitus evidence base

- **Hocevar-Boltezar 2006** (Ljubljana University Medical Centre, *Folia Phoniatr Logop*) — n=70 chronic tinnitus + n=70 controls; perceptual + acoustic + stroboscopic voice assessment. Elevated rates of: muscle-tension dysphonia (MTD); jitter + shimmer; supraglottic compression; throat-clearing tic. Authors hypothesise altered auditory-feedback monitoring driving compensatory laryngeal effort.
- **Cohen 2012** (Vanderbilt Voice Center, *J Voice*) — retrospective chart review of patients presenting to specialist voice clinic; chronic-tinnitus co-morbidity was elevated vs base rate, particularly in the MTD-without-organic-lesion sub-cohort.
- **Roy 2005** (University of Utah, *J Speech Lang Hear Res*) — vocal-effort + throat-discomfort PROMs elevated in chronic-auditory-disorder cohort.
- **Behlau & Madazio** (Brazilian Centro de Estudos da Voz, *J Voice* + *CoDAS*) — cortical-acoustic loop framework: when auditory self-monitoring is disrupted (tinnitus masking the user's own voice partials), the speaker over-recruits laryngeal effort to "feel" the voice; chronic over-recruitment → MTD pattern.
- **Lombard effect literature** (Lombard 1911 original + Cooke & Lu 2010 *J Acoust Soc Am* contemporary review) — speakers in noise involuntarily raise vocal effort + fundamental frequency + intensity. Chronic-tinnitus users live in a permanent internal Lombard condition; the laryngeal cost is cumulative.

**Plain interpretation.** A non-trivial fraction of chronic-distressed users develops a secondary voice problem — sore throat, mid-afternoon vocal fatigue, hoarseness, throat-clearing tic, swallow tightness, occasional voice breaks. Not psychiatric somatisation; the mechanical consequence of months of compensatory laryngeal effort against disrupted auditory self-monitoring + cervical-bracing + masseter co-contraction. Wave-10 added cervical + masticatory compartments; wave-11 adds the **laryngeal + supra/infrahyoid + cricothyroid** compartment they sit on.

### 2.3 Self-administrable interventions (OTC + home, doctrine-aligned)

- **Voice self-screen + symptom log.** VHI-10 (Jacobson 1997) + VFI (Nanjundeswaran 2015) + RSI (Belafsky 2002) — all public-domain, self-administered; baseline + re-score weeks 6/12.
- **Resonant Voice + SOVT.** Verdolini Abbott LMRVT + Lax Vox method (Sihvo, Finland — OTC silicone tubes) for semi-occluded vocal tract work. Co-extensive with §6.5 Bhramari humming-bee — the dossier already half-recommends this without naming the SLT mechanism.
- **Laryngeal manual release — self-administered.** Aronson + Mathieson light-pressure circumlaryngeal release at thyrohyoid + cricothyroid spaces 2-3 min/day. Stemple VFE — four exercises, 2× daily × 6-12 weeks (Mathieson 2009 *J Voice* RCT supports MTD outcome).
- **Hydration + reflux-vigilance + breath support.** Water 30 ml/kg/day; OTC steam inhaler; LPR-reflux diet integration with §6; costal-diaphragmatic breath substrate of efficient phonation — already in §6.5 Bhramari + §13 HeartMath, needs explicit voice-linkage call-out.

### 2.4 Honest framing

- **Voice changes are NOT inevitable.** Most tinnitus users do NOT develop dysphonia. The MTD/vocal-fatigue sub-cohort is meaningful but not majority — ~15-25% per Hocevar-Boltezar + clinic-reality estimates from RCSLT joint-clinic data.
- **NOT a substitute for SLT assessment when persistent.** Hoarseness > 3 weeks, voice breaks, pain on phonation, dysphagia, haemoptysis, or progressive loss requires an ENT + SLT consult to rule out organic pathology — laryngeal lesion, vocal fold nodule/polyp, paresis, malignancy. This is **clinic-route content**, NOT home-protocol content; place in §10.7.3 referral routing alongside the existing Lenire/VNS clinic options.
- **Self-administrable tier is genuinely doctrine-aligned.** Voice-Handicap-Index + Stemple VFE + Lax Vox SOVT straw + Aronson light-pressure release are all home-administrable, no-prescription, low-risk. They fit the home regime cleanly.

### 2.5 Recommended edits

- **Edit 2.1.** Add §11.5i voice-symptom self-screen (VHI-10 + VFI + RSI scoring instrument, 5-min self-administration, baseline at week 0 + re-score weeks 6 + 12).
- **Edit 2.2.** Expand §5.11 / §5.11a to include a §5.11d laryngeal-compartment block — Stemple VFE 4-exercise daily 5 min protocol + Lax Vox SOVT straw work + self-administered Aronson light-pressure release at thyrohyoid + cricothyroid spaces; cross-link to existing §6.5 Bhramari (which is operationally Resonant Voice + SOVT already, just unnamed).
- **Edit 2.3.** Add a "voice-fatigue + dysphonia" row to §10.7.3 clinic-referral block — when self-scoring stays elevated after 12 weeks home protocol, refer to RCSLT (UK) / SPA (AU) / ISHA (IN) / ASHA-equivalent international SLT clinic.
- **Edit 2.4.** Add Hocevar-Boltezar + Cohen + Roy + Behlau + Verdolini-Abbott + Stemple + Aronson references to bibliography under a new "voice/SLT" sub-cluster.

## 3. COGNITIVE-LINGUISTIC + CENTRAL-AUDITORY TRAINING — BRAINHQ + LACE — ABSENT

### 3.1 Current dossier coverage

§5.5 names CBT-T (Cima 2012 RESTART; McKenna 2017) + MBCT-T (cognitive-affective layer). §5.6 names yoga + breath. §5.13 names HRV-biofeedback. **Cognitive-linguistic training — central-auditory + working-memory + selective-attention drills — is missing entirely.** This is not a niche category; it is co-equal in evidence weight with CBT-T for the working-memory + attention + comprehension sub-cohort and has been the silent half of evidence-based aural rehabilitation since the late 2000s.

### 3.2 The BrainHQ + LACE evidence base

- **LACE — Listening And Communication Enhancement.** Sweetow & Henderson Sabes 2010 *J Am Acad Audiol* 21(4):243-255 — multi-site RCT n=263; 20-session home computer-based central-auditory + cognitive + communication-strategy training; significant gains on SiN measures + self-perceived communication PROMs. Olson 2013 *Int J Audiol* replication. LACE is **home-administrable**, costs $99 per licence (consumer DVD + PC + web), runs 30 min/day × 20 sessions over 4 weeks. Doctrine-aligned.
- **BrainHQ — Posit Science.** Designed by Michael Merzenich (UCSF) — adaptive auditory + cognitive training; specifically the "Sound Sweeps," "Memory Grid," "Hawk Eye," "Recognition" + "Listen and Do" exercises target rapid temporal processing + working-memory + dichotic-attention + speech-discrimination — all of which the chronic-tinnitus + APD-overlap cohort underperforms on. Anderson 2013 (Northwestern, *PNAS*) showed older-adult auditory + cognitive gains from BrainHQ-class training. **Subscription $14/mo or $96/year** — doctrine-aligned consumer subscription.
- **Henshaw & Ferguson 2013** (Nottingham + NIHR BRC Hearing Health + RNID, *PLoS One*) — systematic review of computer-based auditory training for adult hearing loss + tinnitus overlap; signal present, effect sizes small-to-moderate, with strongest effect on trained tasks + modest near-transfer to untrained SiN.
- **Whitton 2017** (*Science Translational Medicine* 9(371) — Harvard/Mass Eye and Ear) — Amptify-precursor adaptive auditory + cognitive training; demonstrated working-memory + speech-in-noise gain in tinnitus + hearing-loss cohort. Amptify is now Bose-acquired clinical-grade — but the precursor evidence supports the principle.
- **Sound Storm + Acoustic Pioneer.** Consumer auditory-attention apps targeting frequency-discrimination + temporal-processing + dichotic listening. Free-to-low-cost. Useful as adjuncts; do not have RCT-level evidence but have face-validity + pedagogical-equivalence to LACE exercises.

### 3.3 Why this matters for the §5 home regime

§5.5 CBT-T + MBCT-T addresses the **emotional/affective + habituation** part of distress. It does NOT train the **cognitive-auditory machinery** that determines whether the user can functionally hold a conversation at dinner. Different intervention targets — both validated, both home-administrable, both co-equal. The dossier currently teaches users to **react less to the tinnitus**; cognitive-linguistic training teaches them to **hear better through the tinnitus** — especially paired with §5.9 OTC hearing aids (LACE was designed as a hearing-aid-adjunct; the synergy is the point).

### 3.4 Recommended edits

- **Edit 3.1.** Add **§5.5b cognitive-linguistic + central-auditory training** — LACE 4-week home programme ($99 once) + BrainHQ subscription ($14/mo) + optional Sound Storm + Acoustic Pioneer adjuncts. Frame as **co-equal with §5.5 CBT-T/MBCT-T**, not subordinate. ~400 words; include dosing (30 min/day × 4-8 weeks initial; 10-15 min/day maintenance); honest evidence-tier (B for SiN-trained gains, C for far-transfer to untrained tasks); contraindications none beyond standard screen-time + ergonomic; cost ~$200-300 for first year.
- **Edit 3.2.** Update §0 Quick-start — if the main complaint is conversational comprehension, route to §5.5b BEFORE §5.5 CBT-T (i.e. cognitive-linguistic before cognitive-affective when functional-loss is the dominant complaint).
- **Edit 3.3.** Update §5.9 OTC hearing-aid block — note that LACE was designed as a hearing-aid-adjunct; the combination outperforms either alone (Sweetow 2010).
- **Edit 3.4.** Add Sweetow & Henderson Sabes + Anderson + Henshaw & Ferguson + Whitton references.

## 4. COMMUNICATION-PARTNER TRAINING — CARE + ACE — ABSENT

### 4.1 Current dossier coverage

§7 covers yoga/pranayama + sleep + exercise. §10.6 covers ear-toxic drugs + workplace exposure. §11.5 covers life-stage gates (paediatric, pregnancy, elderly, CI, AFib, etc). §12.5 covers compliance + adherence. **The communication partner — the spouse, adult child, parent, housemate, colleague who shares 60-80% of the user's daily conversational hours — is treated as if they don't exist.** This is the same blind spot adult-aural-rehab literature spent 20 years correcting; the dossier inherits the user-only framing the field has moved past.

### 4.2 The communication-partner-training evidence base

- **Kramer 2005** (VU University Amsterdam, *Int J Audiol*) — RCT of partner-inclusive home aural-rehab training vs user-only training; partner-inclusive showed larger gain on Hearing Handicap Inventory (HHI) PROMs + communication-strategy adherence at 6 months.
- **Preminger 2007** (University of Louisville, *J Am Acad Audiol*) — partner-inclusive group aural rehab outperformed user-only on communication PROMs.
- **Hickson, Worrall & Scarinci 2007** (University of Queensland Communication Disability Centre, *Ear Hear*) — **Active Communication Education (ACE)** programme — 5-session group home-administrable curriculum for adult-onset hearing-loss + partner; significant gain on Self-Assessment of Communication (SAC) + Significant Other Assessment of Communication (SOAC). ACE has the strongest evidence base of any partner-inclusive aural-rehab programme; SPA (Australia) embeds it in adult-aural-rehab clinical-pathway documents.
- **CARE — Communication Approach to Restoring Engagement.** SPA + RCSLT-aligned model adapted from the **Calgary-Cambridge consultation framework** (Kurtz & Silverman 1996, University of Calgary + Cambridge) — taught to medical students globally for clinician-patient communication, here flipped to teach the communication partner the same micro-skills: face the speaker; reduce competing noise before talking; rephrase rather than repeat; pace + chunk; check comprehension; use repair-strategy invitations.
- **Manchaiah 2014 + 2016** (Lamar University Texas + University of Manchester) — qualitative + Q-methodology studies of partner experience in adult-tinnitus dyads; partners report distress, advice-helplessness, repetition-fatigue + relational strain at rates ~70-80% in chronic cases.

**Plain interpretation.** Adult-aural-rehabilitation moved from a "fix the user" frame to a "rehabilitate the conversational dyad" frame around 2005-2010. The dossier is in the older frame.

### 4.3 What a CARE/ACE block would look like

A new §5.17 block, ~500 words, home-administrable in an afternoon + maintained as weekly practice:

- **Partner self-screen.** SOAC + 10-item perceived-burden scale (Manchaiah-derived).
- **Six Calgary-Cambridge-adapted partner micro-skills:** (1) face-the-speaker (lip-reading is unconscious); (2) reduce competing noise FIRST (mute TV, close door); (3) rephrase don't repeat (repetition at louder volume hardens MTD; rephrasing shifts phonetic + semantic load); (4) pace + chunk (short sentences, pause for processing); (5) check comprehension non-punitively ("Did that land?"); (6) invite repair-strategy ("Tell me which bit dropped out").
- **Weekly 15-min dyad debrief** + ACE 5-session curriculum link (free materials via Queensland Communication Disability Centre + SPA + RCSLT).
- **Tinnitus-specific add-on.** During distress crest: keep low-volume murmur background on; reduce surprise auditory loads; use written-medium hand-off (note, text) for 24-48h; AAC tier extends this.

### 4.4 Recommended edits

- **Edit 4.1.** Add **§5.17 Communication-partner training** to §5 home regime — CARE 6-micro-skill + ACE 5-session curriculum link + dyad self-screen (SAC + SOAC) + weekly 15-min dyad debrief. ~500 words.
- **Edit 4.2.** Update §11.5 life-stage gate with a partner-screen row — "If you live with a daily communication partner, run §5.17 partner training in week 2-3 of the protocol; if you live alone, see §5.17 self-support adaptation."
- **Edit 4.3.** Add Kramer 2005 + Preminger 2007 + Hickson 2007 + Manchaiah 2014/2016 + Calgary-Cambridge references.
- **Edit 4.4.** Update §13.7 honest-comparison table with a "partner-inclusive aural rehab" row — Grade B for PROM gain.

## 5. AAC FOR CATASTROPHIC-TINNITUS + COGNITIVE-SHUTDOWN — ABSENT

### 5.1 The catastrophic-tinnitus sub-cohort

Henry PTM-Level-5 (Henry 2007 *J Rehabil Res Dev* 44(1):95-116 — VA-derived, internationally applicable) describes a small sub-cohort whose tinnitus distress + hyperacusis + cognitive shutdown collapses conversational speech for episodes of hours-to-days, sometimes weeks. Cima 2012 RESTART: ~2-4% of severe-distress cohort had ≥1 such episode over 12 months. RNID/Tinnitus UK 2024: ~3% report "days I couldn't speak or be spoken to." **The dossier addresses this user nowhere.** §5.5 CBT-T + §5.5 MBCT-T + §5.13 HRV are correct longitudinal interventions but assume intact conversational baseline; users in catastrophic crest can't practise CBT-T in the moment — bandwidth is gone.

### 5.2 AAC as episodic-prosthetic — doctrine-aligned

Augmentative + alternative communication (AAC) — the field within SLT/SLP that supports users whose speech production or comprehension is unavailable — has migrated from clinic-prescribed dedicated hardware (Tobii Dynavox, Tobii TD I series; Smartbox Grid Pad) to **consumer-grade OTC smartphone + tablet apps** that are doctrine-aligned: home-administrable, no-prescription, no-clinic-visit, transparent pricing. The AAC tier for the catastrophic-tinnitus sub-cohort is **episodic-prosthetic** (used during distress crests, put away in between) — explicitly NOT a permanent-disability assignment.

- **Proloquo2Go** (AssistiveWare, NL, iOS, $269); **Predictable** (Therapy Box, UK, iOS+Android, $159-249); **Grid Player** (Smartbox, UK); **GoTalk Now** (Attainment, iOS); **Dialogue+** (TalkRocket, iOS+Android).
- **Built-in iOS Live Speech + macOS Live Speech (iOS 17+ / Sonoma+)** — free, native, simplest entry: type → spoken via user's Personal Voice clone (banked in advance) or default voice.
- **Comprehension-side during crest** (user can speak but can't follow incoming): Otter.ai, Live Transcribe (Android, free), Live Captions (iOS 16+, free), Ava (freemium $20/mo) — live-caption ambient speech; user reads instead of hearing.

### 5.3 Honest framing — three guardrails

- **Episodic, not chronic.** AAC + live-caption is short-term prosthetic during crests, not a disability identity-shift. Hours-to-days per episode, then put away.
- **Not a substitute for §5.5 / §5.5b / §5.9.** Longitudinal intervention remains the §5 regime; AAC + caption is the survival bridge through worst hours.
- **Privacy.** Otter.ai + Ava process cloud-side; native iOS/Android captioning is on-device — prefer on-device for partner-conversation privacy.

### 5.4 Recommended edits

## 10. CLOSING

The dossier post-wave-10 is a meticulous map of symptom + supplement + sound + cognitive-affective + somatic + lifestyle + sleep + cardiovascular + occupational + vestibular + paediatric + geriatric + military + service-design terrain. What no prior wave could see is that users do not come to clinic for the ringing alone — they come because **they can't follow conversation with grandchildren at Sunday lunch**, because **their voice is hoarse by Wednesday from straining over their own tinnitus**, because **their partner is exhausted from repeating**, because **on the worst days they can't speak or be spoken to**. These are speech-and-language consequences of an auditory disorder.

Adding the cognitive-linguistic + voice + partner + AAC tiers shifts the dossier from a tinnitus-management protocol to a **communication-function restoration protocol that centres on a tinnitus driver** — the direction RCSLT + SPA + ISHA + Cochrane Communication Field literature has been converging toward since the mid-2010s. The skill v6.2.6 generalisation — a five-check communication-function-consequence gate for any ailment whose downstream pathway includes communication breakdown (dementia, Parkinson's, stroke recovery, head-neck cancer survivorship, MS, Hashimoto, heart-failure caregiver-burden, sleep-apnoea CPAP-partner-monitoring) — is the load-bearing carry-forward.
