## 1. CLINIC-WORKFLOW TRANSLATION — THE 30-DAY JOURNEY BETWEEN DOSSIER-READING AND CLINIC-ARRIVAL

### 1.1 What the dossier assumes

The dossier reads as if the patient is a sovereign agent in a clinical vacuum — start the §8.0 MVP tonight, self-administer TFI, follow §8.5, escalate to clinic only on red flags. Sovereignty is correct + load-bearing for doctrine. Empirical problem: ~60-70% of NicheCore-eligible readers will encounter an audiologist, GP, ENT, NHS Talking Therapies (IAPT) clinician, AYUSH consultant, vestibular physio, TMJ physio, or sleep-medicine consultant during the 12-month arc. The handshake is not designed.

Concrete clinic-workflow surfaces the dossier needs:

- **What to bring to your audiology appointment.** Printed TFI/THI/PSQI/GAD-7/PHQ-9 from §11.2; 7-day rolling-median slider; §11.3 DTC lab results; trigger-diary; supplement + device list. Saves the audiologist 15 min + turns intake into actionable triage. The dossier never says this.
- **What to ask the audiologist.** NICE NG197 + Ottawa Decision Guide require structured questions: *"Does my hearing-loss pattern match my self-diagnosed §2.6 sub-type? Is §5.9 hearing-aid layer indicated? Is my pitch/loudness match consistent with TinnitusPlay? Is the §11.7 placebo + natural-history framing applicable to where I am in my arc?"*
- **What to ask the GP** (different from audiologist). GP is gateway to NHS IAPT self-referral + SSHL same-day steroid + thyroid + B12 + ferritin + lipid + sleep-apnea referral + the controlled-drug discussion (gabapentin, pregabalin, clonazepam — that the dossier rightly does NOT recommend but GPs may offer).
- **What to bring to your AYUSH consultant.** Reader walking into AIIA Delhi or Kerala-Ayurveda OPD with §4.2-§4.7 in hand needs to surface self-identified prakruti + current §3.3 stack + the karna-purana eardrum-integrity gate BEFORE the consultation, not after.
- **Between-visit task-list.** Audiology research nurses run between-visit task-lists for every patient: bi-weekly TFI; daily slider; trigger-log; adherence-log; relapse-recovery 2-day rule; week-4 self-check; week-8 escalation decision. The dossier names elements but does not assemble them into the workflow the clinic uses.

### 1.2 The handshake is bidirectional

A Nottingham BRC nurse who sees a new tinnitus referral + wants to recommend NicheCore needs: one-pager (A4, FKGL ≤7, distilled red-flags + §8.0 MVP + §11 scoring + §13.6 sub-type ranges + §15 clinic-routes + §15.5-§15.8 payer table). The dossier is 13,000 words across 16 sections. No nurse prints it. BTA leaflets are FKGL 6-7; Karolinska STOP cohort uses a 1-page recruitment flyer; NHS IAPT uses a self-referral A4. NicheCore needs the same — 600-800 words distilled, doctrine-compliant, clinic-printable.

## 3. ADHERENCE INTERVENTIONS AUDIOLOGY NURSES ACTUALLY RUN — MI + CALGARY-CAMBRIDGE + NICE SDM + COMIT'ID

### 3.1 What wave-4 brought + what is missing

Wave-4 brought COM-B + Fogg Tiny Habits + identity-first framing + relapse-recovery 2-day rule + Gollwitzer implementation-intentions + Lally 66-day automaticity. Load-bearing + preserved. Gap: the day-to-day **MI + Calgary-Cambridge + NICE NG197 SDM** vocabulary that audiology research nurses run with high-distress + low-engagement patients in the 20-minute clinic consultation. This is the lived adherence-practice that bridges wave-4's behavioural-design theory + the bedside reality. None mirrored in dossier voice or ConversionStrip.

### 3.2 Miller-Rollnick MI-4 vocabulary the dossier needs to mirror

- **OARS** — Open questions + Affirmations + Reflective listening + Summaries. Dossier currently uses closed prompts (*"Start the §8.0 MVP tonight"*). MI rewrites as open questions: *"What part of your tinnitus would you most want to be different a year from now?"*
- **Change-talk vs sustain-talk.** MI listens for the patient's own change-talk (*"I do want to sleep better"*) vs sustain-talk (*"I have tried everything and nothing works"*). The dossier should not argue the reader out of sustain-talk; it should reflect it and surface their own change-talk. Concrete edit: §8.0 MVP intro currently says *"Try this for 8 weeks"* (directive). MI rewrite: *"If, after reading the doctrine + the §11.7 honest baseline, you find yourself wanting to try one small layer for 8 weeks — the one most likely to give you a small win, least likely to cost time or money — sound enrichment tonight. What would make that easier to start?"*
- **Decisional balance + importance/confidence rulers.** Audiology nurse asks pre-purchase + pre-MVP: *"On 0-10, how important is it to do this? How confident are you that you can sustain it?"* Importance <7 → dossier-reading is the next step, not purchase. Confidence <5 → layer is too ambitious for week-1. Dossier should surface these before the MVP table; ConversionStrip should surface them before Add-to-Cart.
- **Ambivalence as a feature.** MI treats ambivalence as the normal state of any patient considering a 12-month protocol. The dossier currently treats ambivalence as a problem to overcome via better evidence-presentation. MI flips this: ambivalence is honoured + reflected + worked WITH. Edit: add §8.0.5 ambivalence-honouring paragraph immediately before the MVP table.

### 3.3 Calgary-Cambridge framework — the consultation structure

Calgary-Cambridge (Silverman + Kurtz + Draper 1998 + 2013) is the dominant consultation framework in UK NHS audiology + GP training + NIHR research. Five tasks: initiating + gathering information + physical examination + explanation/planning + closing. Dossier is structured as 16-section evidence walkthrough, not Calgary-Cambridge consultation. Operationalisations:

- **Initiating — establishing the agenda.** Calgary-Cambridge opens: *"What would you most want us to focus on today?"* The dossier opens with §0 red-flag block (medical-safety first — correct). Add immediately after red flags + before §1 Executive Summary: a 60-second self-agenda-setter — *"What do you most want different about your tinnitus 6-12 months from now? Distress-reduction? Loudness-reduction? Sleep? Social-functioning?"* — calibrates which §13.6 sub-type-arc the reader actually targets.
- **Gathering — open-to-closed funnel.** §11.2-§11.3 IS the gathering-information layer. Reframe: open first (*"describe your tinnitus in your own words"*), TFI/THI second, targeted closed third (DTC labs + audiogram).
- **Explanation/planning — chunk-and-check.** Calgary-Cambridge requires small chunks + check understanding before continuing. Dossier dumps §3.3 12-molecule table + §10.1 22-drug-interaction matrix + §11.5 18-row life-stage gate in single tables. Chunk-and-check rewrite: each table needs a 2-3 sentence chunk-summary above it + a *"before you continue, does this match your situation?"* check.
- **Closing — safety-net + summary.** Calgary-Cambridge mandates a safety-net at close: *"If X happens, do Y."* §0 red-flag IS the safety-net. Re-summarise at §13.5 patient-facing primer + end of §15 clinic-route addendum to mirror consultation close.

### 3.4 NICE NG197 + Ottawa Decision Guide

NICE NG197 (Shared decision making, 2021) + Ottawa Decision Guide (Stacey 2020 Cochrane SDM review) frame the dossier's choice-architecture. Operationalisations:

- **Reasonable-patient standard** (Montgomery v Lanarkshire 2015) — patients entitled to all material risks + benefits. §13.7 + §11.5 operationalise this; missing: **decisional-conflict scale (Low DCS, O'Connor 1995)** at month-3 + month-6 to track decisional clarity.
- **Ottawa values-clarification grid** — patient ranks values (distress-reduction vs loudness-reduction vs cost-minimisation vs adherence-burden) before committing. Dossier currently presents the mix as evidence-ranked; it should present values-clarification first.

### 3.5 COMIT'ID core-outcome-set adoption

COMIT'ID (Fackrell + Smith + Hall 2018-2024) defined the cross-trial outcome-harmonisation set: tinnitus intrusiveness, ability to ignore, concentration, sleep, sense of control. The dossier's §11.2 scoring uses TFI + THI + PSQI + 0-10 slider + GAD-7 + PHQ-9 — none exactly mirrors COMIT'ID. Edit: §11.2 should add a COMIT'ID-aligned 5-item check-in alongside TFI; enables future registry to publish in COMIT'ID-harmonised format + connect with the international consortium wave-5 academic-PI named as the publication path.

### 4.2 Handover-friendliness — SBAR template

NHS + most clinical handover-frameworks use SBAR (Situation-Background-Assessment-Recommendation) for cross-discipline communication. Audiology research nurses use SBAR daily. Reader needs an SBAR template they can complete + give to any clinician:

- **Situation:** chronic tinnitus, X months/years, sub-type Y (§2.6), current TFI Z, distress/sleep/annoyance pattern.
- **Background:** noise/ototoxic/age/somatic/pulsatile/SSHL-recent/Ménière/VM history; current meds; §11.3 DTC labs; baseline audiogram if available.
- **Assessment:** self-identified §2.6 sub-type; §11.5 life-stage exclusions; current §8.0 MVP or §8.1-§8.5 adherence; §13.6 prognosis-arc-expectation.
- **Recommendation:** what the reader is asking — audiometry + tympanometry referral; ENT triage for asymmetric/pulsatile/SSHL/Ménière; NHS IAPT self-referral confirmation; AYUSH OPD eligibility + karna-purana eardrum-integrity gate; pharmacy supplement-interaction review.

~250 words. Once added, every clinician the reader sees gets the same structured handover. NicheCore becomes the most useful tinnitus patient resource in the NHS pathway.

## 5. PATIENT-EXPERIENCE RESEARCH METHODOLOGY — Q-METHODOLOGY + IPA + PPI CO-PRODUCTION

### 5.1 What wave-5 named + what was not operationalised

Wave-5 academic-tinnitus-PI flagged the 18-36 month registry path (Frontiers Public Health / BMC Complement Med Therapies / Integrative Medicine Research / Cochrane CAM-Field), correctly costed at $40-120k + 1.25 FTE, named methodology as placeholder. Not operationalised: the **patient-experience research methodology that runs alongside the quantitative cohort** — without it the registry produces a quantitative-only dataset that misses the patient-experience signal explaining adherence + retention + the qualitative meaning behind a 9-point TFI drop. Three methodologies all fit doctrine + are routinely run by audiology research nurses + qualitative methodologists:

### 5.2 Q-methodology (Stephenson 1953; Watts & Stenner 2012, Sage)

Cross-discipline social-science technique for empirically capturing patient-viewpoint diversity. Application: design 40-60 statement Q-set (drawn from BTA forum posts + Tinnitus Hub Tinnitus Talk corpus + Karolinska STOP qualitative interviews + AIIMS integrative case notes + the dossier itself); 30-50 participants Q-sort on -5 to +5 normal-distribution grid; factor analysis identifies 4-6 distinct patient viewpoints.

- **Participants** — 30-50 NicheCore readers stratified by §2.6 sub-type + §11.5 life-stage + §13.6 arc + region (UK + Sweden + India + Australia + Germany).
- **Administration** — 30-45 min via Q-SortWare / FlashQ / HtmlQ.
- **Output** — 4-6 viewpoint factors with narrative labels ("the resigned distressed user"; "the empowered home-protocol adopter"; "the AYUSH-tradition-anchored Indian reader"; "the high-distress NHS-audiology-pathway user"; "the veteran with PTSD-comorbid tinnitus"; "the perimenopausal cyclical user").

The Q output gives NicheCore the empirical patient-viewpoint taxonomy that drives §13.6 sub-type prognosis + §11.5 life-stage gate + ConversionStrip-tier-design + the audiologist-facing one-pager. Without it those structures are reasoned-from-evidence rather than reasoned-from-patient-experience.

### 5.3 Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis (IPA — Smith, Larkin & Flowers 2009, Sage)

Dominant qualitative methodology for chronic-illness lived-experience in UK clinical psychology + audiology. 8-12 semi-structured interviews (60-90 min) per sub-type group at month 6 or month 12; interview schedule around §2.6 sub-type identity + §11.7 honest-baseline narrative + §13.6 sub-type-arc expectation; IPA double-hermeneutic analysis; emergent themes per case → cross-case super-ordinate themes.

The IPA output gives lived-experience texture explaining why the same 9-point TFI drop feels like transformation for one reader + disappointment for another. Also gives the dossier its next-revision patient-voice editing pass — §13.0-§13.4 case composites (Ravi + others) should be IPA-informed not author-imagined; §13.5 primer should be IPA-co-edited with named PPI partners.
