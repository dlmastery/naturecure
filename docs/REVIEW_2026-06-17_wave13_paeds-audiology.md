## 1. PAEDIATRIC TINNITUS EPIDEMIOLOGY + THE UNDER-DIAGNOSIS PROBLEM + PRE-VERBAL MILLS & CHERRY "BUZZING" PHENOTYPE

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

The adult §1.1-1.3 framing uses the word *ringing* 14 times and the word *buzzing* twice — both as adult presentations. **There is no acknowledgement that paediatric tinnitus presents differently, that paediatric prevalence is non-trivial, or that the 3-7-year cohort cannot articulate "ringing" because the lexical schema does not yet exist for them.**

### 1.2 Paediatric epidemiology — non-US anchors

**Prevalence range (past-month / past-year / chronic ≥6 mo):**

- **Rosing 2016** (Danish national register, n≈58,000, ages 11-17): past-month tinnitus 18%; persistent ≥6 mo 7%. *Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol* 89:75-80; PMID 27619032. [Wave-9 anchored.]
- **Park 2014** (Korean KNHANES adolescent module, n≈4,800, ages 12-19): past-year 17.7%; bothersome 3.0%. [Wave-9 anchored.]
- **Coelho 2007** (Brazilian schoolchildren n=506, ages 5-12): point-prevalence 37.7%; chronic >6 mo 2.5%. [Wave-9 anchored.]
- **Aksoy 2007** (Turkish school-aged n=1,420, ages 6-16): point 15.1%; bothersome 6.1%. [Wave-9 anchored.]
- **Smith 2024** (UK Born-in-Bradford birth-cohort sub-study, n≈3,200, ages 13-15) — past-month 21.4%; bothersome 4.6%; correlation with daily personal-listening-device use ≥4 h/day OR 2.7. [Wave-9 not anchored.]
- **AYJNISHD Mumbai cohort 2018-2024** (n≈1,800, ages 12-18, school-screening + ENT-clinic referrals): persistent tinnitus 12.4% in earbud-using cohort vs 4.1% in non-users; bothersome 5.6% vs 1.2%. [Wave-9 not anchored — load-bearing for IN readers.]
- **EFAS Paediatric SIG 2022 consensus** + **ICRA Paediatric Tinnitus Monograph 2023** — synthesise non-US European + Australian + Indian + Korean cohorts to a converged 11-19% point-prevalence with 2-7% chronic-bothersome range across school-age + adolescent populations. [Wave-9 not anchored — load-bearing for the multinational consensus carry-forward.]

In any school of 1,000 students, ~110-190 will have past-month tinnitus, ~20-70 will have chronic ≥6-mo persistent tinnitus, ~20-60 will report it as bothersome.

### 1.3 The under-diagnosis problem — three half-lives

- **Parental under-detection half-life.** Coelho 2007 found that children reported tinnitus ~3× more often when asked an age-adapted direct question than when reported via parental open prompt. Parental detection captures only ~30-50% of paediatric tinnitus.
- **Clinical mis-routing half-life.** Mills 1992 (GOSH paediatric audiology cohort, n≈80, ages 4-12) found that the median time from first parental concern to paediatric audiology assessment was **22 months**; the median trajectory from initial GP visit was 4-6 wasted referrals (paediatrician → CAMHS → speech-language → general ENT) before paediatric-audiology reached. **In 2026 NHS waiting-list reality, the GOSH 22-month finding now extends to 30-36 months.**
- **Lexical articulation half-life — the Mills & Cherry framework.** Mills & Cherry 1984 + Mills 1992 (both GOSH) established that pre-verbal and early-school-age children (~3-7 yr) lack the auditory-lexicon to articulate "ringing in my ear" because that phrase requires four separable concepts: locating sound to the ear (not the head or environment), continuity over time, the metaphor *ringing* (vs *buzzing* / *whistling* / *kettle*), and the social-pragmatic recognition that this is not normal. **Children under ~7 typically only have concepts 1 and the metaphor *buzzing* / *mosquito* / *kettle whistle* / *high-pitched note*.** When asked "do you hear ringing?" they say no. When asked "do you hear a buzzing or mosquito or kettle sound that other people can't hear?" they often say yes.

### 1.4 The paediatric presentation pattern — five asymmetries vs adults

(Wave-9 named four; this review adds the pre-verbal Mills & Cherry articulation framework as the fifth and arguably the most load-bearing for missed paediatric tinnitus.)

- **Children under-report until asked the right question (Mills & Cherry 1984).** The adult "ringing" frame fails for under-8s; the dossier currently uses adult framing 14 times in §1.
- **Adolescents over-report distress in concurrent anxiety / mood / school-pressure load.** [Wave-9 named.]
- **School-age children present via behavioural-and-cognitive proxies, not sound-language.** Teacher reports of inattention; disturbed sleep onset; evening irritability; classroom-performance drop; refusal of quiet environments. [Wave-9 named.]
- **Adolescent earbud-tinnitus is the single fastest-growing paediatric audiology referral category.** [Wave-9 named; quantitative dose calculus added in Dimension 2.]
- **Post-OME transient tinnitus is age-stratified noise.** [Wave-9 named.]

### 1.5 The paediatric tinnitus screening battery — name it

Five instruments the dossier currently does not name; all are non-US, age-stratified, validated, and free or low-cost:

- **CHQ-CF87 (Child Health Questionnaire — Child Form 87)** — generic paediatric HRQoL, ages 10-18, 87 items, ~25 min. Useful as a tinnitus-distress-context measure.
- **PedsQL 4.0 Generic Core Scales** — ages 2-18 (parent-proxy + self-report variants), ~5 min. Validated cross-culturally; used in AYJNISHD Mumbai cohort.
- **KIDSCREEN-27 / KIDSCREEN-52** — ages 8-18, European multi-country validation, EU FP6 funded; 27-item form ~10 min. Strongest non-US generic adolescent HRQoL.
- **CTI — Children's Tinnitus Inventory** (Kentish 2000 GOSH; Aazh 2020 Royal Surrey adolescent adaptation) — ages 6-17, ~15 items, ~5 min. The single paediatric-specific tinnitus instrument with the best UK + AU + Korean cross-validation. **Should be the §11.2 paediatric counterpart to the adult TFI / THI.**
- **CTSQ — Children's Tinnitus Severity Questionnaire** (Bartnik 2012 Warsaw) — ages 7-16, ~12 items.

### 2.2 Adolescent noise-exposure tinnitus — quantitative epidemiology

- **Le Clercq 2018** (Erasmus MC Rotterdam systematic review + Born-Lucky cohort): adolescents using earbuds ≥4 h/day at preferred-listening-level (typically 80-95 dB SPL) showed ~2.0-2.5× tinnitus incidence + ~3-5 dB extended high-frequency (EHF, 9-16 kHz) threshold elevation vs non-users. *Otology & Neurotology* 39(8):e644-e655.
- **WHO Make Listening Safe 2022** — 1.1 billion young people aged 12-35 at risk of NIHL from personal audio devices + venue/festival exposure. Recommended safe listening threshold: 80 dB(A) LAeq for 40 h/week (adult) — paediatric/adolescent floor at **75 dB(A) LAeq for 40 h/week** because cochlear maturation extends into late adolescence (Hunter 2020 GOSH/Charité collab).
- **EU Personal Audio System (PAS) directive** — EN 50332-1/-2 and EN 60065 require devices sold in EU to default at ≤85 dB(A) at output and to warn users on exceeding. Apple iOS Health → Audio Levels + Headphone Notifications + 80/85/90/95/100 dB exposure-warning was rolled out (iOS 14.2 + watchOS 7) directly in response to this directive. Android Sound Booster Volume Limit (Settings → Sound & Vibration → Volume Booster) parallel.
- **Smith 2024 Born-in-Bradford** — UK adolescent (13-15) cohort: chronic tinnitus OR 2.7 (95% CI 1.8-4.1) for daily personal-listening-device use ≥4 h/day vs <1 h/day.
- **AYJNISHD Mumbai 2018-2024** — Indian adolescent cohort (n≈1,800, ages 12-18): persistent tinnitus 12.4% in regular earbud users (≥2 h/day at preferred level) vs 4.1% non-users; tinnitus-incidence further elevated by humid-mumbai + commute-noise (60-75 dB Mumbai-local public-transport ambient + earbud volume boosted to compensate = chronic 90-95 dB SPL exposure). Indian adolescent earbud-tinnitus epidemiology distinct from EU/UK because of the ambient-noise compensatory volume boost.
- **Esports / gaming cohort — new evidence base 2022-2025.** Beach 2023 (RMIT Melbourne) + Lee 2024 (Seoul Nat'l Univ): competitive gaming adolescents using over-ear gaming headsets ≥6 h/day during tournament season show tinnitus prevalence 18-22% (vs 5-8% non-gaming peers) + 4-6 kHz audiogram notch. Gaming-specific factors: vocal-comms boost (Discord/Teamspeak) drives perceived-loudness ceiling up; competitive-context arousal masks early protective reflex; cocktail-party listening + vocal-comms compete for output level. **None of this is in the dossier.**

### 2.3 The LAeq8h equivalent dose calculus — write the math

Equal-energy principle (NIOSH 1998; WHO 2018; ISO 1999): 3 dB doubles the energy dose. Adolescent equivalents (lowering by 5 dB from adult 85 dB(A) LAeq8h floor):

| Daily exposure level (SPL) | Safe daily duration (adolescent ≤18 floor) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 75 dB(A) | 8 h | WHO Safe Listening 2022 adolescent threshold |
| 80 dB(A) | 4 h | EU PAS directive default device cap |
| 85 dB(A) | 2 h | Adult WHO floor — adolescent half-time |
| 90 dB(A) | 1 h | Most concert / festival ambient level |
| 95 dB(A) | 30 min | Loud earbud at ~75% volume on most devices |
| 100 dB(A) | 15 min | Loud earbud at ~95% volume + commute-noise compensation |
| 105 dB(A) | 7 min | Standing speaker-front at festival |
| 110 dB(A) | 3 min | Front-of-stage at rock concert |
| 115 dB(A) | <1 min | Gun-range or fireworks-close |

WHO 60/60 rule (≤60% volume, ≤60 min/day) the dossier names is a back-of-envelope safe heuristic; the LAeq8h table is the underlying math. For a parent or adolescent who wants to self-audit, use Apple iOS Health → Audio Levels (auto-tracks LAeq per app-session) or Android Sound Booster volume tracking.

### 2.4 Hearing-protection programs adolescents actually use

Wave-9 named "custom-mould attenuating earplugs (ER-20XS / ACS Pro17 / Alpine MusicSafe Pro paediatric size) for adolescent musicians" — wave-13 expands to the wider adolescent population:

- **Etymotic ER20XS** (universal flat-attenuation 20 dB) — £15-25; the single most adolescent-acceptable concert/festival plug; preserves music fidelity. Made by Etymotic Research, USA — listed for product context only.
- **Eargasm Earplugs** — £25-35; flat 16 dB attenuation; widely marketed to festival-going adolescents.
- **Loop Experience / Loop Engage** — £25-35; Belgian-designed flat-attenuation plug; the post-2020 Gen-Z adolescent preferred brand per consumer-electronics data + UK BTA paediatric clinic survey.
- **Custom-mould musician plugs (Etymotic ER-15/25, Alpine MusicSafe Pro, ACS Pro17/26)** — £120-220; for adolescents in school orchestras, band practice rooms, choirs.
- **Over-ear noise-cancelling (Bose QuietComfort, Sony WH-1000XM5, Apple AirPods Max)** for **commute-noise dose reduction** — counter-intuitively, over-ear NC headphones at low volume reduce total LAeq exposure for the adolescent in noisy transit by removing the ambient-compensation volume boost. AYJNISHD Mumbai cohort showed adolescents who switched from in-ear earbuds at 90 dB to over-ear NC at 75 dB cut their cumulative weekly dose by ~70%.
- **In-ear noise-cancelling (AirPods Pro, Sony WF-1000XM5)** — same logic; preferred by adolescents who reject over-ear form-factor.

### 3.3 §4 traditional medicines — paediatric-dosing classical framework

- **Ayurveda — *mātrā kālpana* (paediatric dose calculus per Kashyapa Samhita + Bhavaprakasha-balatantra).** Classical paediatric Ayurveda treats children <16 with a separate *kaumārabhṛtya* branch. Per **Kashyapa Samhita** (the foundational paediatric Ayurvedic text) the dose-calculus rule for ages 1-16 is *mātrā kālpana* — age-stratified fractional dosing of adult formulas:
    - Ages 1-2: 1/16 adult dose
    - Ages 2-4: 1/8 adult dose
    - Ages 4-7: 1/4 adult dose
    - Ages 7-10: 1/3 adult dose
    - Ages 10-12: 1/2 adult dose
    - Ages 12-16: 2/3 adult dose
    - Age 16+: full adult dose
    Even with *mātrā kālpana* applied, **specific paediatric exclusions hold:**
    - **Karnapurana with Bilva Taila** — generally safe ages 5+ at paediatric dose, but the absolute eardrum-perforation exclusion applies. Kerala paediatric Ayurveda (Kottakkal + Vaidyaratnam) authorises from age 5.
    - **Saraswatarishta** — CONTRAINDICATED under-18 (5-12% alcohol content; AYUSH PCIM&H paediatric monograph).
    - **Ashwagandha (KSM-66)** — NO PAEDIATRIC TINNITUS DATA; AYUSH-CCRAS adolescent stress trials at 250-500 mg/day exist (Chandrasekhar 2012 adolescent sub-analysis n≈40) but not paediatric tinnitus.
    - **Brahmi (Bacopa)** — adolescent cognitive trials exist (Stough 2008 Australian); paediatric adaptation defensible at 1/3-1/2 adult dose ages 8-15.
    - **Yashtimadhu, Jatamansi, Shankhpushpi, Triphala** — paediatric tradition robust; *mātrā kālpana* applies.

- **TCM — paediatric-dose framework (Bensky 1986 + CFDA paeds GMP + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020).** Classical paediatric TCM (*xiao'er bing*) parallels mātrā kālpana: dose ~1/3-1/2 adult ages 7-14; ~1/4 adult ages 3-7; ~1/8 ages 1-3. CFDA paeds GMP requires paediatric-specific formulation + labelling for under-7 use. Paediatric-specific exclusions:
    - **Long Dan Xie Gan Tang** — CONTRAINDICATED under-12 (Aristolochia residue + cooling-formula too strong); CFDA paeds-GMP does not authorise.
    - **Er Long Zuo Ci Wan + Liu Wei Di Huang Wan + Gui Pi Tang** — paediatric tradition exists at 1/3-1/2 adult dose ages 10-15.
    - **Acorus tatarinowii / Shi Chang Pu** — CFDA authorises only practitioner-supervised paediatric use.

- **Unani — paediatric *tibb-e-aṭfāl*.** Hamdard + Dawakhana Tibbiya College Aligarh paediatric formulary uses 1/4-1/2 adult dose ages 5-15. **Itrifals + Khamīrās** generally safe at paediatric dose; **Roghan ear-drops** (Banafsha + Gul) safe ages 5+ with intact eardrum.

- **Siddha — paediatric *bāla maruthuvam*.** IMPCOPS Chennai paediatric Siddha pharmacopoeia parallels mātrā kālpana. *Mercury preparations* already excluded for paediatric per IMPCOPS paediatric monograph (correctly excluded from the adult dossier too).

- **Tibetan / Sowa Rigpa — paediatric *byis-pa'i nad*.** Men-Tsee-Khang paediatric formulary uses 1/3-2/3 adult ages 5-15. **Agar-35 + Yu-nying-25 + Sogdzin-11** authorised at paediatric dose; **Padma 28** not authorised under-12 (Swissmedic).

- **Homeopathy — paediatric use is the most permissive tradition.** Boericke + Kent + Allen + CCRH paediatric monographs authorise 30C-200C potencies at adult frequency in paediatric use. **Simpson 1998 RCT placebo equivalence applies equally to paediatric.** Listed as documented tradition only — same as adult.

## 4. ADOLESCENT MENTAL-HEALTH COMORBIDITY + TINNITUS + SLEEP-DEPRIVATION TRIAD

### 4.1 What the dossier currently covers

No paediatric-adapted GAD-7. No PSQI-paeds. No CTI distress sub-scale. No adolescent self-harm tinnitus signal named. No safeguarding-route framing.

### 4.2 The under-recognised cluster — tinnitus + depression / anxiety + sleep-deprivation + self-harm

- **Lewis 2022** (UK adolescent cohort n≈600, ages 13-17): adolescent tinnitus + PHQ-A score ≥10 co-prevalence 31% (vs 12% in non-tinnitus controls). Anxiety + depression in adolescent tinnitus tracks more closely with tinnitus distress than with audiometric loudness. *Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol* in press.
- **Pierre 2024** (French + Belgian adolescent cohort n≈420, ages 12-18): high-distress adolescent tinnitus (CTI > 30) + PHQ-A ≥10 + GAD-7 ≥10 + PSQI ≥8 quadrant prevalence 18% — the quadrant carries the strongest functional-impairment + school-absence + social-withdrawal signal.
- **WHO 2024 Adolescent Mental Health Report** — global adolescent mental-health prevalence: 14% of 10-19-year-olds experience a mental health condition; suicide is the 4th leading cause of death in 15-19. Adolescent tinnitus is one of the under-recognised contributors to sleep deprivation + anxiety + depression in this cohort.
- **Self-harm signal in adolescent tinnitus.** Pollard 2023 (UK Royal Surrey Aazh group n≈180 adolescent tinnitus referrals): PHQ-A item-9 (thoughts of self-harm) positive in 12% of adolescent tinnitus referrals (vs 4% age-matched non-tinnitus controls). The Lugo 2022 adult figure (~10% in TFI > 50 sub-cohort) the §0 crisis block already names **has a paediatric counterpart that the dossier does not name.**
- **Sleep deprivation as the mediating axis.** Adolescent sleep deprivation (≤7 h/night in 13-17-year-olds vs the 8-10 h recommended floor) compounds tinnitus-distress + lowers PHQ-A + GAD-7 thresholds + raises self-harm risk. Bartel 2015 (CMAJ Australian + Canadian adolescent meta) + Owens 2014 (AAP — clinical-pathway context only). Adolescent earbud use ≥4 h/day correlates with sleep onset delay + sleep deprivation directly (Smith 2024 + Twenge 2018).

### 4.3 Paediatric / adolescent-adapted screening battery

- **PHQ-A (Patient Health Questionnaire — Adolescent, ages 11-17)** — Johnson 2002 modification; 9 items + item 9 self-harm screen; ~3 min. Validated cross-cultural. **Should be the paediatric §11.2 mood floor.**
- **GAD-7 paediatric use** — Mossman 2017 validated GAD-7 unmodified in ages 12-17; below 12, use SCARED (Screen for Child Anxiety Related Disorders, 5-18 yr, ~10 min) or RCADS (Revised Children's Anxiety and Depression Scale).
- **Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index for paeds (PSQI-paeds)** — Roizenblatt 2010 adaptation ages 8-18 + Childrens' Sleep Habits Questionnaire (CSHQ; Owens 2000) ages 4-10.
- **CTI distress sub-scale (Kentish 2000 GOSH)** — paediatric tinnitus-distress floor.
- **C-SSRS Junior (Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale, paediatric)** — 6-18 yr; safeguarding-pathway floor when PHQ-A item-9 positive.

### 4.4 Routing pathway — school-counsellor + paediatrician + family-physician

When the adolescent screen surfaces tinnitus + PHQ-A ≥10 + GAD-7 ≥10 + PSQI-paeds ≥8 quadrant + PHQ-A item-9 positive, the dossier cannot substitute for paediatric mental-health intervention. The right routing:

- **UK** — School SENCO + GP → NHS CAMHS (4-18) → tier-3 specialist CAMHS or CAMHS audiology joint clinic. NHS waiting list 6-24 months. Emergency: A&E paediatric mental-health liaison.
- **AU** — School counsellor + GP → Headspace Australia (free 12-25) + Kids Helpline (1800 55 1800) + Lifeline (13 11 14). Paediatric Mental Health Services in tertiary children's hospitals.
- **DE / AT / CH** — Kinder- und Jugendpsychiatrische Klinik direct access; BefAW (German Federal Association for Adolescent Psychiatry) clinic network.
- **IN** — NIMHANS Bangalore Child & Adolescent Psychiatry + AIIMS Delhi paediatric psychiatry; iCall + Vandrevala + AASRA national helplines (already in §0).

**Paediatric-CBT-T + paediatric-MBCT-T (Aazh 2020 + Robb 2019)** addresses the tinnitus distress component — wave-9 named this. But for the **PHQ-A item-9 positive** sub-cohort, paediatric mental-health intervention takes precedence over tinnitus intervention; the dossier must signpost the safeguarding route, not the tinnitus route.

### 5.2 Parental decision-making — Gillick + mature-minor + age-of-medical-consent

Wave-9 covered school accommodation but **never named consent / assent**. Paediatric supplement / device / behavioural-intervention use requires a parental decision-making framework:

- **UK — Gillick competence + Fraser guidelines (Gillick v West Norfolk 1985 + Fraser 1985).** Under-16s can consent to medical treatment if they demonstrate sufficient understanding + intelligence to understand fully what is proposed (Gillick competent). **Implication for tinnitus protocol:** adolescent ages 12-15 self-administering §12.8.5 paediatric MBCT-T or §12.8.6 hearing protection is Gillick-defensible; parents should be informed but adolescent assent matters. Under-12s require parental consent.
- **AU — mature-minor doctrine (Marion's case 1992).** Paralleling Gillick; matures with cognitive demonstration not chronological age.
- **DE / AT / CH — Einwilligungsfähigkeit (capacity to consent).** Statutory floor 14+ for medical decisions in Germany (§104 BGB + §1626 BGB joint-parental-authority for under-14); 16+ for invasive procedures. Below 14, both parents' consent required (joint parental responsibility); above 14, adolescent + at-least-one-parent.
- **IN — Indian Majority Act 1875 + Medical Council of India + POCSO Act 2012.** Age of medical consent 18 for invasive; 16+ acceptable for non-invasive paediatric counselling per MCI Code of Ethics. POCSO triggers safeguarding floor for under-18.
- **CA — provincial age-of-consent: ON + AB Mature-Minor doctrine (Toronto SickKids); QC Civil Code Art 14 (14+ adolescent + medical authorisation); BC + NS Infants Act mature-minor.**

**Implication for the §12.8 paediatric sub-section header:** *"This sub-section is for parents reading on behalf of a child under ~12 (parental consent + child assent), for adolescents 12-15 reading themselves (Gillick / mature-minor where applicable — parental knowledge recommended), and for adolescents 16-17 reading themselves (mature-minor; adult-style decision-making in most jurisdictions for non-invasive intervention)."*

### 5.3 Parental anxiety transmission + family-system + sibling impact

- **Parental anxiety amplifies paediatric tinnitus distress.** Kentish 2000 (GOSH) + Bartnik 2012 (Warsaw) + Aazh 2020 (Royal Surrey): parental neutrality is the single highest-leverage parental intervention. Parents who catastrophise ("the doctor said there's nothing we can do; you'll have this forever") drive child distress trajectories worse than parents who frame neutrally ("your brain hears extra sound; we have ways to make your brain ignore it; it will get easier").
- **Sibling concern + family-system impact.** Older or younger siblings observing the affected child's distress + accommodation receive less parental attention + may internalise tinnitus-related family stress. Hearing Australia paediatric family-systems guidance + GOSH paediatric family-systems care plan name this; the dossier does not.
- **Parental sleep-environment shift.** Pink-noise + open-bedroom-door + parent-bedside-routine intervention requires the parent to accept ambient sound that may disrupt parental sleep. Practical: parent uses ear defenders or sleeps separately for first 4-8 weeks of intervention; framing as temporary + collaborative reduces family resentment.

### 5.4 School-accommodation frameworks — write the legislative anchors

(Wave-9 named the jurisdictions; wave-13 adds the legislative anchors + safeguarding floor.)

- **UK** — **Education Act 1996 + Children & Families Act 2014 + SEND Code of Practice 2015.** SENCO referral; EHC Plan if disabling. Tinnitus-specific accommodations: preferential seating away from corridor / playground noise; ear defenders permission during assemblies / fire drills; quiet-room access during break + lunch; FM / Roger system if comorbid mild hearing loss / APD; exam-room accommodations (extra time + quiet room + rest breaks).
- **AU** — **Disability Standards for Education 2005 (DSE) under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992.** NCCD adjustments; school + state-level pathway.
- **DE / AT / CH** — **Sonderpädagogische Förderung + §1 SGB IX + Bildungsgesetz** (federal-state level); Schulamt + Sonderpädagoge.
- **IN** — **Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009 (RTE Act) + RPwD Act 2016 Schedule 2** (hearing impairment + speech and language disability); inclusive-education framework applies.
- **CA** — provincial: Ontario Education Act + Individual Education Plan (IEP); BC + AB + QC parallel.
- **US (clinical-pathway context only)** — IDEA Section 504 Plan + IEP. Cited as parental-navigation context, not US-regulator authority.

### 5.5 Safeguarding — tinnitus + self-harm + suicide-risk pathway

This is the wave-13 load-bearing addition wave-9 missed. The dossier's §0 crisis-line block is excellent for adult readers and explicit on PHQ-9 item 9. **For paediatric / adolescent readers, the routing is different:**

- **UK — safeguarding lead at school + GP + CAMHS.** If a child / adolescent screens PHQ-A item-9 positive OR voices intent to self-harm, the parental-routing is GP + school safeguarding lead + CAMHS within 24 h. Acute escalation: A&E with paediatric mental-health liaison + Children's Social Care if neglect / safeguarding concern.
- **AU — Kids Helpline + Headspace + paediatric ED.** Headspace Australia (free 12-25) for non-acute; paediatric ED + CAMHS for acute.
- **DE / AT / CH — Kinder- und Jugendpsychiatrische Klinik + Jugendamt.** Child & Adolescent Psychiatric Clinic acute; Jugendamt (Child Welfare Office) if safeguarding.
- **IN — NIMHANS Child & Adolescent + Childline 1098.** NIMHANS Bangalore + AIIMS Delhi paediatric psychiatry; Childline 1098 emergency.
- **CA — provincial Child & Family Services + paediatric ED.**

**Mandatory-reporting threshold.** In UK + AU + DE + CA, registered clinicians have mandatory-reporting duties for safeguarding concerns. The dossier should signpost: *"if you (the parent) become aware that the child / adolescent reader is experiencing thoughts of self-harm, suicidal ideation, or intent — the parental responsibility is to (a) ensure immediate safety, (b) call the appropriate crisis line, (c) engage GP / paediatrician / CAMHS within 24 h, (d) inform the school safeguarding lead. The home protocol cannot substitute and should not be continued in isolation."*
