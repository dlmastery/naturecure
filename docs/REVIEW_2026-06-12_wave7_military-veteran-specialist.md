## 1. SERVICE-RELATED TINNITUS — THE BLAST / IED / SUSTAINED-GUNFIRE PHENOTYPE THE DOSSIER UNDER-COVERS

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§2.6 sub-type row 7 names: *"Brand-new, after loud noise (≤72h) — Concert/firearm/workplace; ringing within hours; §2.2 acute; Best window; Lorito 2008; Attias 1994."* §5.16 covers hearing-protection generically ("custom-mould earplugs at concerts, with power tools, at gun ranges and on motorcycles"). §8.5 acute noise-injury stack covers ≤24h post-exposure NAC + Mg + Zn + CoQ10. §3.3 magnesium-row cites Attias 1994 IDF recruits hearing-protection RCT. **These are correct citations for civilian acute noise-injury.** They under-specify the deployed-veteran exposure pattern in three ways.

### 1.2 Blast / IED / explosive-overpressure exposure is mechanistically distinct from concert / firearm exposure

The deployed-veteran tinnitus cohort across UK MoD (Op TELIC + Op HERRICK), US OEF/OIF/OND, Australian Op SLIPPER + Op OKRA, and Israeli Op-cycles is dominated by **explosive overpressure** as the primary exposure modality — not by repeated steady-state noise exposure (which is the civilian range / industry pattern that the Attias 1994 IDF magnesium-protection trial measured). The mechanistic differences are load-bearing across four blast-effect tiers:

- **Primary blast wave (overpressure peak).** A near-miss IED, RPG, mortar, or breaching charge generates a peak overpressure of 50-500+ kPa within 2-10 ms. The wave ruptures the tympanic membrane at peaks ≥35 kPa in 50% of exposed personnel (Garth 1995; Cho 2014 US Army cohort), disarticulates the ossicular chain in a subset, and exceeds the stapedial-reflex protective onset latency (25-150 ms). **Conductive + sensorineural + central injury simultaneously, in milliseconds.** The dossier's §2.2 inner-ear-damage framing covers the sensorineural component; it does not name the conductive + ossicular + TM-rupture components requiring **otoscopic surveillance + ENT referral within hours, not 72 hours**.
- **Secondary blast (fragmentation + sustained acoustic load).** Sustained acoustic load above 140 dB SPL for 100-500 ms — well beyond the dossier's §6.6 "85 decibels" threshold. Compounds primary overpressure into severe + asymmetric Kujawa-Liberman cochlear synaptopathy.
- **Tertiary blast (acceleration / impact / TBI layer).** Body displacement + helmet impact creates a **mild TBI / concussion overlay** with central injury that no peripheral stack reaches — see Dimension 3.
- **Quaternary blast (chemical / thermal cocktail).** JP-8 jet fuel, propellant combustion byproducts, depleted-uranium aerosol in specific theatres, burn-pit exposure in Iraq + Afghanistan AOR. Beyond the §6.6 occupational ototoxic list.

**Sustained gunfire as a separate modality.** Repeated small-arms-fire exposure (typical infantry training-cycle loads of 50-500 rounds per session, multiple sessions per week) generates a different cumulative-dose pattern than a single concert or workplace shift. Attias 1994 measured **steady-state range exposure with magnesium protection** — n=300, 2-month follow-up, threshold-shift outcome. That trial generalises to civilian range exposure; it does **not** generalise to a 12-month deployment cumulative-dose curve with episodic blast events superimposed.

### 1.3 The "acute" window is wrong for blast-injured veterans

The dossier's §8.5 "start ≤24h post-exposure" framing is calibrated for a civilian concert / workplace event with a clear before/after. A deployed veteran experiences **repeated sub-clinical acute events over months** (every patrol with small-arms contact, every breach, every near-miss) interleaved with **acute clinical events** (the specific IED at this specific date that they remember). The civilian framing of "acute window" is a single discrete event; the deployed pattern is a **cumulative + episodic curve over a 6-9 month tour**. By the time a veteran identifies "tinnitus," they are typically **months-to-years post-the-load-bearing-event** — outside any acute window, in a chronic + central-amplification phase that the dossier's §2.6 row 4 ("long-standing, brain-amplification") covers in spirit but does not name as service-related.

### 1.4 Specific edits

- **Edit 1.1.** Add **§2.6 row 8** (new sub-type): "Blast / IED / sustained-deployment exposure (service-connected)." Plain-words column: *"Tinnitus that started during or after military deployment with explosive or sustained-gunfire exposure — often combined with hearing loss, mTBI symptoms, sleep disturbance, and post-traumatic stress; the most-claimed disability in major militaries. Different recovery path from civilian concert/firearm tinnitus — requires the §1, §2, §3 veteran-specific sub-protocols (added wave-7)."* Clinical features: bilateral or asymmetric high-frequency loss, often with TM scarring; concurrent hyperacusis + sound sensitivity; PTSD or mTBI overlay common; dominant cause: §2.2 + §2.3 + central; realistic response: variable, depends on PTSD + mTBI co-treatment; citation: *(Yankaskas 2013 military-noise review; Henry 2017 VA tinnitus cohort; Theodoroff 2015 Portland VA blast-tinnitus characterisation; UK MoD Defence Audiology Service guidance 2021)*.

- **Edit 1.2.** Add **§2.8** subsection "Service-related tinnitus — the deployed-veteran phenotype." ~400 words covering: primary/secondary/tertiary/quaternary blast effects; the cumulative + episodic dose curve; the otoscopic surveillance requirement for any blast-exposed veteran (TM perforation, ossicular disruption); the **distinction between the steady-state-range exposure that Attias 1994 measured and the deployment-cumulative pattern**; honest acknowledgment that the dossier's acute 24h-window is operationally meaningless for veterans presenting months-to-years after the load-bearing event.

- **Edit 1.3.** Add **§8.5.5** "Veteran-specific acute + chronic stack." Acute layer is unchanged from §8.5 (NAC 1200 BID + Mg 400 + Zn 30 if low + CoQ10 200 × 7 days) but with explicit qualifier *"For active-duty personnel near a discrete blast event with documented TM perforation — present at unit medical facility for otoscopic assessment within 24h; the supplement layer is adjunct only, not a substitute for ENT review."* Chronic layer (for the months-to-years-post veteran): full §8.1-§8.4 protocol with three explicit modifications — (a) **dose-adjust melatonin / Ashwagandha if on a VA-prescribed sleep med** (zopiclone, zolpidem, trazodone, low-dose quetiapine — all common in deployed-veteran sleep stacks per US VA + UK Veterans-UK prescribing patterns); (b) **defer Bhramari + forceful pranayama if the veteran has post-blast vestibular dysfunction** (humming + breath-hold can trigger vertigo in superior-canal-dehiscence patterns common in blast injury); (c) **screen for tinnitus + sound-induced startle response** before introducing notch-music or binaural beats (can trigger flashback in PTSD-comorbid sub-cohort — see Dimension 2).

**Major revision.** The blast / IED / sustained-deployment phenotype is mechanistically distinct, the largest single-condition tinnitus sub-population in the OECD, and absent from the dossier's seven-sub-type taxonomy + §8.5 acute stack + §5.16 hearing-protection framing. Fix is additive (~600 words of new content); no civilian content needs to be retracted.

## 2. PTSD-COMORBID TINNITUS — THE MBCT-T / CBT-T SCAFFOLD DOES NOT HANDLE TRAUMA-DRIVEN AVOIDANCE

### 2.1 What the dossier currently ships

§5.5 covers CBT-T (Cochrane Fuller 2020, demoted to Grade B per wave-2 biostatistics). §5.7 covers MBCT-T + MBSR (Cima 2012 RESTART + McKenna 2017 — A-grade in earlier revisions, demoted to B per wave-2). §10.7.2 names CBT-T as gold standard, MBCT-T as comparable head-to-head, TRT-self-guided + ACT as secondary. §11.3 includes PHQ-9 + GAD-7 for depression/anxiety. **CBT-i Coach is named as a tool from the "free Veterans Affairs app" with the careful caveat "listed as a tool, not as a US authority"** (line 292) — this is the dossier's only acknowledgment of the veteran population, and it is buried in a footnote on a CBT-i product. The dossier has **zero scaffolding around PTSD-comorbid tinnitus** as a clinical entity.

### 2.2 PTSD prevalence in service-connected tinnitus is 40-60%, not 10-15%

The civilian tinnitus literature reports a 10-15% comorbidity with PTSD in chronic tinnitus populations (Fagelson 2007). The **deployed-veteran tinnitus cohort runs 40-60%** comorbid PTSD (Lew 2007 Tampa VA Polytrauma cohort 49%; Hoffer 2010 Tampa VA blast-injury cohort 53%; Theodoroff 2015 Portland VA n=1,000 post-deployment cohort 44%; Carlson 2019 UK Iraq + Afghanistan veterans 38%). The mechanism is bidirectional: tinnitus is one of the dominant **trauma-cue stimuli** for the PTSD nervous system (sound + ear + the moment of the explosion = a hard-wired retrieval cue), and PTSD-driven hypervigilance amplifies the central-gain mechanisms that drive chronic tinnitus salience.

### 2.3 Standard tinnitus CBT-T / MBCT-T can worsen PTSD-comorbid presentation if mis-sequenced

This is the load-bearing safety point in this dimension. Standard tinnitus CBT-T (Cima protocol, Henry workbook, the Sereda Tinnitus E-Programme) trains the patient to **expose themselves to the tinnitus sound, sit with it, reframe their relationship to it, and reduce avoidance**. For a non-PTSD nervous system, this is the intended therapeutic mechanism. **For a PTSD-comorbid veteran where tinnitus is itself a trauma-cue, naive exposure can trigger flashbacks, dissociation, panic-loop relapse, or in worst-case acute suicidal-ideation episodes**. The Portland VA tinnitus team (Henry, Theodoroff, Carlson) — the **same lab that developed the TFI instrument** that the dossier uses as its primary score — has published multiple papers from 2013-2024 specifically on this sequencing problem (Henry 2017 *American Journal of Audiology*; Theodoroff 2017 *Hearing Research* veteran-specific tinnitus management; Carlson 2024 PTSD-tinnitus integrated treatment manual).

**The correct sequencing in the veteran cohort** is one of the following four pathways:

1. **PTSD-stable + tinnitus-distress-dominant.** Tinnitus CBT-T / MBCT-T as standard. This is the simplest case; standard dossier protocol applies. ~25-35% of the comorbid sub-cohort.

2. **PTSD-active + tinnitus-distress-secondary.** Trauma-focused therapy first (Prolonged Exposure / PE, Cognitive Processing Therapy / CPT, EMDR — the three VA-mandated first-line PTSD treatments), tinnitus-focused work deferred 12-24 weeks until PTSD symptoms stabilise (PCL-5 ≥10 point drop). ~30-40% of the comorbid sub-cohort.

3. **PTSD-active + tinnitus-trauma-cue-coupled.** Integrated PTSD + tinnitus treatment using a manualised approach (PE + tinnitus management module, or CPT + Henry tinnitus workbook concurrent) delivered by a clinician trained in both. ~20-30% of the comorbid sub-cohort. Self-applied is not safe here; the protocol must defer to clinician-supervised care.

4. **PTSD-tinnitus-mTBI triple-comorbid.** All three must be addressed concurrently in the VA / DoD Polytrauma framework (Tampa, Richmond, Palo Alto, Minneapolis VA Polytrauma Rehabilitation Centers; UK DMRC Stanford Hall; Australian Greenslopes). The home OTC protocol is adjunct only — see Dimension 3.

### 2.4 The dossier's MBCT-T framing must be sub-type-gated for PTSD

§5.7 currently presents MBCT-T as universally applicable. **It is not.** Mindfulness-based interventions in PTSD-comorbid populations carry a small but real risk of **dissociation-amplification** in patients with high baseline dissociation scores (Lustyk 2009 review). The standard MBSR / MBCT clinical protocol screens out high-baseline-dissociation patients via the Dissociative Experiences Scale (DES) and refers them to alternative trauma-stable interventions before mindfulness exposure. The dossier's strictly-self-applied framing has no DES screen, no clinician-supervised gate, and no PTSD-specific MBCT-T variant (Polusny 2015 MBSR-for-veterans-with-PTSD does exist but uses a modified protocol — shorter sits, more grounding, peer-supervised groups).

### 2.5 What the dossier should ship

- **Edit 2.1.** Add **§2.9** subsection "Tinnitus + PTSD — the comorbid presentation." ~400 words covering: 40-60% prevalence in deployed-veteran cohorts; the bidirectional mechanism (tinnitus as trauma-cue; PTSD hypervigilance as central-gain amplifier); the four sequencing pathways (PTSD-stable / PTSD-active-tinnitus-secondary / PTSD-active-trauma-cue-coupled / triple-comorbid); the load-bearing warning that **standard tinnitus CBT-T / MBCT-T can worsen mis-sequenced PTSD presentations**.

- **Edit 2.2.** Add **§5.5.5 "PTSD-adapted CBT for tinnitus."** Names PE (Foa), CPT (Resick), EMDR (Shapiro), TF-CBT (Cohen et al.) as the four VA / NICE / NHMRC-recommended trauma-focused therapies; surfaces that these are **clinician-delivered**, not self-applied (consistent with doctrine — the dossier surfaces them as referral pathway, does not recommend them as home protocol). Names the **Carlson 2024 PTSD-tinnitus integrated treatment manual** as the literature anchor. Names the **PCL-5 (PTSD Checklist for DSM-5, Weathers 2013)** as the comorbidity screen, scoring threshold ≥33 = probable PTSD, with explicit honest framing that the PCL-5 was developed by the US National Center for PTSD (a VA-affiliated body — surface honestly; doctrine treats this as scientific-anchor not regulator-authority citation per the established pattern with Stachler 2012 AAO guidance in the SSHL dossier).

- **Edit 2.3.** Update **§11.3 comorbidity & lab screening** to add: PCL-5 (PTSD Checklist) + dissociative experiences screen (DES-II if high-PCL-5) for any user identifying as service-connected or with deployed-veteran history. Position alongside PHQ-9 + GAD-7.

- **Edit 2.4.** Update **§11.5 life-stage gate** to add a row: *"Active PTSD (PCL-5 ≥33) — defer self-applied MBCT-T / CBT-T cognitive layers until PTSD-focused therapy initiated (PE / CPT / EMDR / TF-CBT); supplement + sound-enrichment + somatic layers proceed cautiously with grounding modifications. Notch-music, binaural beats, and Tinnitracks-style frequency-targeted interventions can trigger trauma-cue retrieval in tinnitus-trauma-coupled sub-type — defer pending clinician guidance."*

- **Edit 2.5.** Update **§11.6 "Who this protocol is not for"** to add: *"Active PTSD with tinnitus as trauma-cue (you identify the moment of tinnitus onset with a specific traumatic event AND have current intrusion / flashback / nightmare symptoms triggered by hearing the tinnitus) — clinician-supervised trauma-focused care first; the home cognitive layers in §5.5-§5.7 are not safe self-applied here."*

### 2.6 Verdict on Dimension 2

**Major revision.** The dossier's self-applied CBT-T / MBCT-T framing is appropriate for civilian + PTSD-stable populations; it is **unsafe for the PTSD-active veteran cohort** where tinnitus functions as a trauma-cue. Fix is additive (~600 words) plus targeted §11.5 + §11.6 gates. The fixes preserve doctrine (clinician referrals are surfaced as pathways, not recommendations) and surface the Henry / Theodoroff / Carlson Portland VA tinnitus-PTSD evidence base that the dossier already implicitly draws on via TFI development citation.

## 3. MILD TRAUMATIC BRAIN INJURY (mTBI / CONCUSSION) TINNITUS — A DISTINCT CENTRAL-REORGANISATION PHENOTYPE

### 3.1 The dossier is silent on mTBI tinnitus

Searching the dossier for "TBI", "traumatic brain", "concussion", "post-concussive" returns **zero hits**. §11.3 comorbidity screening covers hearing loss, jaw + neck dysfunction, thyroid, B12, iron, vitamin D, sleep apnea, BP, cholesterol, depression, anxiety, ototoxic drug load, perimenopause, heavy metals, mould, perimenopause hormones, heart inflammation — **no mTBI / concussion / post-concussive screen**. §2.6 sub-type 4 ("long-standing, brain-amplification") is the closest civilian analogue but does not name TBI mechanism. This is a load-bearing gap for the veteran cohort.

### 3.2 mTBI tinnitus prevalence in deployed-veteran cohorts is 35-55%, often comorbid with PTSD + tinnitus (triple-comorbid)

Lew 2007 Tampa VA Polytrauma cohort n=126 reported mTBI in 88% of blast-exposed veterans; **62% of those had tinnitus**. Hoffer 2010 Tampa cohort confirmed similar rates. Theodoroff 2015 Portland VA n=1,000 cohort: 41% reported a history of head injury during deployment; the head-injury subgroup had 1.7× higher tinnitus severity scores than the non-head-injury subgroup at the same audiometric loss. UK MoD King's College / Stanford Hall cohorts (Fear 2010, Rona 2017) report consistent figures. The pattern is robust across militaries.

### 3.3 mTBI tinnitus is mechanistically distinct from peripheral noise-induced tinnitus

Standard noise-induced tinnitus follows the Kujawa-Liberman cochlear-synaptopathy → central-gain compensation model (§2.2 → §2.3) that the dossier covers correctly. **mTBI tinnitus has a distinct mechanism set**: (a) **diffuse axonal injury (DAI) of the central auditory pathway** — DTI imaging (Knight 2019; Bigler 2013) shows white-matter loss in lateral lemniscus + inferior colliculus + medial geniculate body tracts with normal audiograms; (b) **cortical reorganisation in primary + secondary auditory cortex** — fMRI + MEG studies (Folmer 2011; Lanting 2009) show altered tonotopic organisation **not predicted by the audiogram**; (c) **autonomic dysregulation overlay** (orthostatic intolerance, exercise intolerance, sleep-architecture disruption — Tan 2009; Esterov 2017); (d) **vestibular + oculomotor co-injury** — ~80% have vestibular dysfunction (Hoffer 2010), ~60% have oculomotor dysfunction (Master 2016); the dossier's §5.11 neck + jaw release covers only the cervicogenic axis; (e) **post-concussive sleep disorder** at 50-70% prevalence (Mathias 2012) — beyond standard CBT-i, often requires polysomnography to rule out post-traumatic sleep apnea + REM behaviour disorder.

### 3.4 The VA / DoD mTBI Clinical Practice Guideline maps the right stack

The 2021 VA/DoD CPG for Concussion / mTBI (parallel UK MoD Defence Medical Services mTBI guidance + Australian DVA Mental Health Treatment Cycle) defines a multi-layer rehabilitation stack: vestibular rehab (semi-circular-canal + otolith retraining); oculomotor rehab (saccade + vergence + dynamic visual acuity); cognitive rehab (BrainHQ / Posit Science / RehaCom — BrainHQ has VA-validated evidence); sleep stack (CBT-i first; polysomnography if symptoms persist); graded return-to-activity (Buffalo Concussion Treadmill Test sub-symptom-threshold protocol). **The dossier has zero coverage of any of these layers** and presents nothing about the mTBI-tinnitus connection.

### 3.5 Specific edits

- **Edit 3.1.** Add **§2.10** subsection "Tinnitus + mTBI (post-concussive)." ~450 words covering: 35-55% prevalence in deployed-veteran cohorts; the mechanism distinction (DAI + cortical reorganisation + autonomic + vestibular + oculomotor + sleep); the **TBI-tinnitus phenotype** distinction from peripheral noise-induced tinnitus (audiogram often normal; mechanism is central; standard sound-enrichment is necessary but not sufficient); the requirement for clinician-delivered vestibular + oculomotor rehab as load-bearing missing layers; honest framing that the home protocol's §5.11 covers cervicogenic but not vestibular-otolith dysfunction.

- **Edit 3.2.** Add **§2.6 row 9** (new sub-type): "Tinnitus after head injury or mTBI." Plain-words column: *"Tinnitus that started after a head injury, concussion, blast-overpressure event, or other traumatic brain insult — usually with concurrent dizziness, sound sensitivity, sleep disruption, attention problems, and fatigue. The audiogram is often normal because the injury is to the central wiring, not the cochlea. Standard sound enrichment helps but is not enough — requires clinician-delivered vestibular + oculomotor rehab as the load-bearing layer."* Dominant cause: central reorganisation + autonomic + vestibular; realistic response: variable, often slow (6-18 months); requires multidisciplinary care. Citation: *(Lew 2007 Tampa VA Polytrauma; Theodoroff 2015 Portland VA blast-tinnitus; VA/DoD 2021 mTBI CPG — surfaced as scientific synthesis, not regulator authority; Lanting 2009 fMRI tinnitus reorganisation; Hoffer 2010 vestibular-tinnitus blast comorbidity)*.

## 4. THE VETERAN-SYSTEM PATHWAY — UK MoD VETERANS-UK + US VA + AUSTRALIAN DVA + AFMS + IDF

### 4.1 The dossier has no veteran-system pathway at all

Searching for "veteran", "MoD", "VA", "DVA", "Veterans Affairs", "AFMS", "war pension", "Armed Forces Compensation Scheme", "service-connected", "disability rating" returns **near-zero hits** (the CBT-i Coach footnote line 292 is the only mention, careful with the "tool not authority" caveat). For the largest tinnitus sub-population in the OECD this is a structural gap.

### 4.2 Why veteran-system pathway matters even in an OTC + home-only doctrine

The doctrine excludes recommending specific prescription drugs, clinic-based treatments, or US-regulator authority citations. **It does not exclude surfacing pathways to compensation, specialist clinics, or peer-support networks that the user is entitled to.** The omission of these pathways in the veteran context is functionally a doctrine violation in the opposite direction — the dossier presents the user as fully sovereign + financially capable + clinically isolated, when in reality the service-connected veteran is **owed** specific access to specialist audiology, mTBI care, PTSD care, peer support, and a £67-£200/month (UK) to $137-$320/month (US) financial benefit specifically because their tinnitus is service-connected. **Failing to surface this is a financial-access doctrine failure** that the founder-product reviewer in wave-4 and the patient-advocate reviewer in wave-5 would land if scoped to the veteran cohort.

### 4.3 The five major veteran systems

- **UK MoD Veterans-UK + Armed Forces Compensation Scheme (AFCS) + War Pension Scheme.** Tinnitus is compensable under both pre-2005 War Pension and post-2005 AFCS. Tariff tiers (AFCS tinnitus tariff 14-15; noise-induced SNHL tiers 11-14) depend on severity + bilaterality + audiometric thresholds. Pathway: Veterans-UK (Norcross, Thornton-Cleveleys) with audiometric evidence from Defence Audiology Service or NHS audiology. Adjacent networks: NHS Op COURAGE veteran mental-health pathway; Combat Stress (Surrey + Audley Court + Hollybush House); Royal British Legion + SSAFA peer-support; King's Centre for Military Health Research at KCL.
- **US Department of Veterans Affairs.** Tinnitus is the **#1 service-connected disability** in the VA system (~2.9M veterans on disability rolls FY2023; 10% rating per CFR 38 4.87 Diagnostic Code 6260). Pathway: VA Form 21-526EZ + C&P audiology examination. Access to VA Audiology + Speech-Language-Pathology Service via primary care VA enrolment. Polytrauma Rehabilitation Centers (Tampa, Richmond, Palo Alto, Minneapolis, San Antonio) for triple-comorbid presentations. Doctrine treats VA as **access pathway**, not regulator authority (same pattern as the SSHL dossier with Stachler 2012 AAO guidance).
- **Australian Department of Veterans' Affairs.** Statements of Principles 113/2014 + 114/2014 under VEA + MRCA. Pathway via DVA claim with audiology evidence. Open Arms (formerly VVCS) for PTSD + family support. Greenslopes Private Hospital + RAH Adelaide as audiology anchors.
- **Indian Armed Forces Medical Services (AFMS) + MoD Pension Cell.** Tinnitus recognised as service-connected disability under Indian Army / Navy / Air Force Pension Regulations; disability percentage via Release Medical Board (RMB) or Invaliding Medical Board (IMB). Specialist access via Command Hospital Pune, R&R Delhi Cantt, AFCME Delhi, INHS Asvini Mumbai. ECHS covers post-retirement audiology + hearing aids. AFMC Pune as academic anchor — aligns with existing AIIMS Delhi integrative-otolaryngology framing.
- **Israel Defense Forces Medical Corps + MoD Rehabilitation Department.** IDF Hearing Conservation Programme tracks deployed audiometric data longitudinally; post-service tinnitus + hearing loss compensable. Tel HaShomer Hospital + Soroka Beersheba as audiology anchors — particularly relevant given Attias 1994 IDF is the magnesium-row anchor citation.

**Other major militaries** to be named in passing: Canadian VAC, German Bundeswehr Sanitätsdienst, French ONACVG, Republic of Korea MPVA.

### 4.4 The financial-access gap is real

The dossier's §9.2 monthly cost surfaces $390-$680/month full protocol + one-time $5,000-$15,000+ device + supplier outlay. A UK war pension at AFCS Tariff 14 (tinnitus with hearing loss) runs **£74-£186/month** as of 2026; the US VA 10% tinnitus rating runs **$171-$201/month**; Australian DVA tinnitus payments run **AUD$120-$320/month** depending on category. **The full dossier protocol consumes 2-4× the typical veteran tinnitus disability payment**. The wave-4 founder-product reviewer + wave-6 behavioural-pricing reviewer both rightly flagged the financial-accessibility ceiling for the civilian population; the veteran cohort hits this ceiling harder. The Tier-1 "Start Here Pack" $45-$75/month bundle proposed in §8.0 minimum-viable protocol is **the load-bearing accessibility lever for the veteran cohort** and should be explicitly named as such.

## 5. HYPERACUSIS + SOUND-SENSITIVITY IN THE VETERAN COHORT — THE EARPLUG-OVERUSE FRAMING IS A SAFETY INVERSION

### 5.1 What the dossier currently says

§5.16 hearing-protection prevention paragraph: *"Custom-mould ear plugs at concerts, with power tools, at gun ranges and on motorcycles. Plus a 60/60 rule for earbuds. Preventing more hair-cell loss is half the protocol."* §10.6 strictly-avoid list: *"earplug over-use in normal-noise environments."* §5.18 what-to-skip: *"Pure silence (sensory deprivation makes the brain 'turn up')."* The framing is correct for civilian over-protectors who develop sound-sensitivity from chronic over-use of foam plugs in normal sound environments. **It is a safety inversion for the startled-veteran cohort.**

### 5.2 The veteran hyperacusis + sound-sensitivity phenotype

Hyperacusis runs 25-50% in deployed-veteran tinnitus cohorts (Theodoroff 2015 Portland VA n=1,000 — 40%; UK Combat Stress audit 2019 — 33%; Israeli IDF post-deployment screen — 28-35%). The mechanism overlap with PTSD-startle and mTBI central reorganisation is bidirectional. The clinical presentation is distinct from civilian hyperacusis: **startle-magnitude amplification** (PTSD-comorbid veterans show elevated baseline acoustic-startle, Pole 2007 effect size ~0.5; a car backfire / dropped pan can trigger PTSD acute-stress cascade with sympathetic surge + dissociation + flashback + tinnitus amplification + sleep disruption for days); **hyper-vigilance to ambient sound** (the PTSD nervous system maintains sound-environment monitoring state — every ambient sound is threat-assessed; removing protection in a high-ambient environment increases cognitive load + autonomic activation even without acute startle); **sound-cue trauma retrieval** (specific sound spectra matching deployment-vehicle engines or weapon-handling can trigger episodic intrusion).

### 5.3 Why "don't over-protect" is unsafe for this cohort

The dossier's framing rests on the **central-gain-amplification model** — protective over-use → sensory deprivation → central up-regulation → worse tinnitus. This is correct for civilian over-protectors. **For the startled-veteran cohort the calculus is inverted**: the cost of an acute startle event with downstream PTSD cascade + tinnitus amplification + dissociation + suicidal-ideation risk **far exceeds** the central-gain cost of moderate protective use. A veteran with calibrated routine use of filtered tunable protection (Etymotic ER-15/25 musician plugs; Westone DefendEar tactical filtered plugs; SoundGear digital protection; 3M Peltor TEP-100) in known-trigger environments **is using protection therapeutically**, not over-protectively. The Portland VA + UK Combat Stress + Australian DVA hyperacusis management protocols (Henry 2017; Carlson 2020; Greenway 2018) explicitly allow **graduated sound-environment exposure with calibrated protection in high-startle settings**. Removing protection entirely is not the goal for this cohort; calibrated protection + sound-environment titration + trauma-focused therapy is.

### 5.4 The filtered + tunable protection landscape the dossier under-covers

§5.16 names Etymotic ER-20, Eargasm, Loop Experience as civilian options. These under-cover the veteran-relevant landscape: **Etymotic ER-15 / ER-25 / Music Pro electronic** (filtered, 15-25 dB attenuation without distorting speech); **Westone DefendEar Tactical / Hunter / TRU Custom** (electronic, situational sound + impulse blocking — gold standard for tactical / hunting / range); **SoundGear Phantom** (digital with built-in noise suppression + low-level sound amplification — designed for tactical + tinnitus-comorbid veterans); **3M Peltor TEP-100 / TEP-200 Tactical Earplug** (electronic, used by US + UK + AU militaries); **CAEarHEAR + Westone DefendEar Digital** (high-end customised digital). These are **calibrated tactical hearing protection** the dossier's recommendation list does not name.

### 5.5 Specific edits

- **Edit 5.1.** Update **§5.16 hearing protection** to add a sub-paragraph: *"For service-connected veterans + active-duty personnel + the PTSD-startle / hyperacusis comorbid cohort — the civilian 'don't over-protect' framing in §10.6 does not apply to you. Use calibrated filtered + tunable protection in known-trigger environments. Recommended categories: filtered musician plugs (Etymotic ER-15 / ER-25 / Music Pro electronic); electronic tactical earplugs (Westone DefendEar Tactical / 3M Peltor TEP-100); digital hearing protection with situational-sound pass-through (SoundGear Phantom). Custom-fit from Defence Audiology Service (UK), VA Audiology Clinic (US), DVA-affiliated audiology (Australia), Command Hospital audiology (India), or IDF Medical Corps audiology (Israel) is covered under service entitlement in most jurisdictions — see §10.7.6."*

- **Edit 5.2.** Update **§10.6 strictly-avoid** list to qualify the earplug-overuse entry: *"earplug over-use in normal-noise environments — civilian context, applies to those who developed sound-sensitivity from chronic over-use of foam plugs in normal sound environments. **NOT applicable to PTSD-comorbid / hyperacusis / startle-amplified veteran cohort — see §5.16 calibrated filtered + tunable protection for that population.**"*

- **Edit 5.3.** Add **§2.11** subsection "Hyperacusis + sound-sensitivity in the veteran cohort." ~300 words covering: 25-50% prevalence; the PTSD-startle + mTBI + tinnitus triple-mechanism; why the civilian "don't over-protect" framing inverts in the veteran cohort; the calibrated filtered + tunable protection landscape; the **graduated sound-environment exposure with trauma-focused therapy** evidence base (Carlson 2020; Greenway 2018).

- **Edit 5.4.** Update **§11.3 comorbidity screening** Khalfa HQ row to add a note: *"Veteran cohort — Khalfa HQ scores ≥28 + service-connected status flags hyperacusis-PTSD-startle triple-comorbid presentation; the calibrated-protection pathway in §5.16 applies, not the §10.6 civilian over-protector framing."*

### 5.6 Verdict on Dimension 5

**Major revision.** The dossier's earplug-overuse framing is **correct for civilians**, **dangerous for startled veterans**, and currently un-stratified. The fix is small (~400 words + targeted qualifications at three points: §5.16, §10.6, §11.3) and preserves the civilian guidance while protecting the veteran cohort.
