## §0. The framing diagnosis (read this first)

The dossier today carries the "STRICTLY OTC + home-based" doctrine as if it were synonymous with "outside the reimbursement system." It is not. **OTC + home-based intersects payer reimbursement in surprising ways** in every non-US jurisdiction this review covers:

- The UK NHS funds CBT-T (the dossier's §5.5 Grade B intervention) **for free** via NHS Talking Therapies (the IAPT successor, rebranded 2022). The dossier surfaces CBT-T as home audio; the NHS user is entitled to a trained therapist delivering 6-12 structured sessions at zero cost.
- Germany's GKV system funds Schwabe Tebonin EGb 761 at partial reimbursement under §31 SGB V for "Hirnleistungsstörungen" — broad enough that many HNO consultants prescribe via Heilpraktiker-Wahltarif route. The dossier prices Tebonin at full out-of-pocket €36-58/month; the German reader who asks their HNO may pay €5-10 quarterly co-pay.
- Australia's Medicare Better Access Initiative covers MBCT-T (Cima 2012 protocol) **for free** under MBS items 80100/80105 for users with a GP Mental Health Treatment Plan citing comorbid anxiety/depression — the THI 38+ moderate-distress cohort qualifies. 10 sessions of in-person MBCT-T by a registered psychologist at no additional cost.
- India's Ayushman Bharat - PMJAY covers AYUSH treatments at empanelled hospitals (~5,200 nationally) for the dossier's §4.1 Saraswatarishta, §4.2 Karna Purana with Bilva taila, §4.4 Long Dan Xie Gan Tang, §4.5 Khamīrā Abresham recommendations — **free up to ₹5 lakh / family / year**. The dossier prices these as self-purchased at ₹450-1,800/month; the PMJAY-eligible reader could access them via panchakarma OPD packages.

The pattern: **every major non-US statutory health system funds substantial portions of what the dossier recommends, and the dossier does not tell the consumer**. That is payer-blindness, not OTC-doctrine. OTC means "you can self-administer." Payer-disclosure means "you should know what your insurance covers before paying out-of-pocket." A dossier honours both.

The structural fix is a new skill rule — **Rule 23 per-jurisdiction payer-landscape disclosure**. Every dossier surfaces, in §15.4 through §15.8, the per-jurisdiction reimbursement pathway: free-via-payer first, OTC-cofactor-floor second, full-protocol upgrade third. This honours doctrine (OTC + home + WHO TCIM + India AYUSH + EMA + TGA + NMPA + Health Canada NHP authority frame), satisfies the wave-5 patient-advocate's "you are not alone" framing, and aligns with the wave-4 founder-product subscription-as-moat thesis.

### 1.2 The actual UK NHS tinnitus pathway 2020-2024

The published NHS Tinnitus Care Pathway is jointly maintained by the British Tinnitus Association (BTA), British Society of Audiology (BSA), British Academy of Audiology (BAA), and aligns with **NICE Clinical Guideline NG155 (Tinnitus: assessment and management, March 2020, last reviewed October 2023)** and **NICE Quality Standard QS199 (Tinnitus, March 2021)**. The pathway has five tiers, each with explicit NHS funding routes:

**Tier 1 — Self-care + signposting (free, no clinician).** BTA helpline 0808 808 0123 (Mon-Fri 9:00-17:00, BTA-operated, signposted on NHS Inform); RNID tinnitus line (shared infrastructure post-2023 Action on Hearing Loss merger); NHS Inform Scotland self-help; NHS England tinnitus info; Mind UK + Samaritans 116 123 for distress overlay.

**Tier 2 — Primary care (GP, free at point of use).** Full history, drug review (the dossier's §10 ototoxic-drug screen aligns here), SSHL 72-hour red-flag triage (per wave-3 fix), PHQ-9 + GAD-7 mental-health screen (NICE QS199 QS4), referral decisions per NICE NG155 §1.2-1.5.

**Tier 3 — NHS Talking Therapies (free, self-referral most ICBs).** IAPT-successor. Self-referral via nhs.uk/talk in most English ICBs; GP-gated in some Welsh LHBs and Scottish HBs. 6-12 sessions of CBT (CBT-T variant where therapists trained) + sometimes MBCT-T. **Wave-4 cost-per-QALY implication:** the §8.0 MVP's MBCT-T audio is a $45-75/month substitute for an entitled service that costs £0 via NHS Talking Therapies. The UK reader who pays NicheCore for what NHS offers free is the disclosure failure mode.

**Tier 4 — Audiology + ENT (free under NHS, GP referral).** Pure-tone audiogram + tympanometry + speech audiometry + DPOAE/TEOAE as indicated. Sound-therapy device fitting where commissioned (Oticon Tinnitus SoundSupport, GN ReSound TS, Phonak Tinnitus Balance via NHS procurement framework). **Free NHS hearing aids** including combination devices with tinnitus-masker programs for audiogram-confirmed hearing loss — the dossier's §5.9 "OTC hearing aid" tier is structurally undercut for the entitled UK reader. ENT referral for asymmetric / pulsatile / acute / SSHL cases per the wave-3 red-flag triage.

**Tier 5 — Specialist tinnitus clinics (NHS-funded, regional).** ~25-30 NHS-funded tinnitus clinics in England (Manchester Royal Infirmary, Royal National Throat Nose & Ear Hospital London, Queen Elizabeth Birmingham, James Cook Middlesbrough, Royal Hallamshire Sheffield); fewer in Scotland/Wales/NI. TRT via NHS audiology where commissioned; MBCT-T groups at selected centres.

**What NICE NG155 / QS199 do NOT cover (= correct out-of-pocket surface for NicheCore):**
- Most supplements (Tebonin, Pycnogenol, NAC, Mg glycinate, Lion's Mane, etc.) — **NICE NG155 §1.6.4 specifically recommends against routinely offering Ginkgo, melatonin, or zinc for chronic tinnitus** based on the same Cochrane evidence the dossier honours. So NicheCore selling these as a low-confidence trial layer is doctrine-compatible AND payer-compatible: the NHS won't pay, the patient is informed of low-grade evidence, NicheCore margins on a small recurring spend.
- Clinic-grade tinnitus-masker hearing aids beyond what NHS audiology procures.
- Lenire bimodal-stimulation device (the dossier's §15.2 correctly flags this).
- Self-purchased home red-light / PBM devices (the dossier's §5.10 — UK reader pays out-of-pocket).
- Home PEMF mats (§5.14 — UK reader pays out-of-pocket).
- HeartMath / Sensate HRV devices (§5.13 — UK reader pays out-of-pocket).
- Tinnitracks audio app (the German Schwerhörigenbund-developed notch-therapy app; not NICE-recommended; out-of-pocket at €15-25/month).
- ACT-for-tinnitus self-guided internet programmes beyond what NHS Talking Therapies offers.
- Acupuncture for tinnitus (NICE NG155 §1.6.5 does not recommend; out-of-pocket at £40-80/session in UK private market).
- Most classical Ayurvedic / TCM / Unani formulations (out-of-pocket via specialist UK importers — Pukka, Organic India, Hadley Wood Healthcare).

## §2. German GKV + Krankenkasse pathway audit — what's covered, what's reimbursable, and what the dossier should say (BLOCKER)

### 2.1 What the dossier currently does

The dossier mentions "Germany" at 6 places — mostly Schwabe Tebonin sourcing, Padma 28 Swissmedic (correctly clarified per wave-2 fix), Boedts 2024 *Nature Communications Medicine* on Lenire (Belgian study but European context), and the wave-3 SSHL HNO-direct-access route. **It does not mention GKV (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung), Krankenkasse, AOK, Barmer, TK, IKK, BKK, HNO-Heilkostenplan, Festbetrag, Hilfsmittelverzeichnis, Heilpraktikergesetz, §31 SGB V, §40 SGB V Reha-Verordnung, or any of the actual reimbursement-decision surfaces.** A German reader cannot triangulate what their GKV pays for from this dossier.

### 2.2 The actual German tinnitus coverage landscape

Germany's statutory health insurance covers approximately 88% of the population (~73 million GKV-insured out of ~83 million total; the remaining ~12% are private PKV). The GKV system funds tinnitus care through five distinct mechanisms:

**Mechanism 1 — HNO (Hals-Nasen-Ohren / ENT) consultation.** HNO-direct-access is the German standard (unlike UK GP-gated); GKV-insured users present directly at an HNO Facharzt practice with their eGK (elektronische Gesundheitskarte). Consultation is fully covered. Audiogram (Tonschwellenaudiometrie EBM-Ziffer 09320 + Sprachaudiometrie 09322) is fully covered. ENT specialist tinnitus assessment per AWMF S3-Leitlinie "Chronischer Tinnitus" (2021 update, AWMF Register 017-064) is fully covered.

**Mechanism 2 — Hearing aid coverage.** GKV Festbetrag for hearing aids was raised from €685 to €784.94 per ear in 2024 (Spitzenverband Bund der Krankenkassen Festbetragsgruppen für Hörhilfen). Combination tinnitus-masker hearing aids (Oticon More Tinnitus SoundSupport, Phonak Audéo Tinnitus Balance, GN ReSound LiNX Quattro Tinnitus Sound Generator, Signia Pure Charge&Go T) are covered at Festbetrag if prescribed by HNO with audiogram-confirmed indication. The hearing-aid acoustician (Hörgeräteakustiker) handles fitting + 6-month adjustment under GKV billing codes.

**Mechanism 3 — Heilpraktiker-Wahltarif (§53 SGB V) + GKV partial reimbursement for selected herbal medicines.** Several Krankenkassen offer Naturheilkunde-Wahltarife: **AOK Naturheilkunde, BKK ProVita, IKK classic Naturmedizin, SBK Komfort, hkk Naturheilverfahren**. Within these plans: **Schwabe Tebonin EGb 761** (Rote Liste "Hirnleistungsstörungen") is 50-80% reimbursable on HNO Privatrezept — the German reader's effective cost is €7-15/month, not €36-58. **Padma 28** is reimbursed by several plans for the EMA-recognised PAOD indication (relevant for tinnitus + PAD comorbidity). **Cinnarizine / Vertigoheel / Tebonin combinations** for vertigo-tinnitus overlap are partially reimbursable. **B12 injectables** are reimbursable when deficiency is confirmed by lab; oral methyl-B12 is OTC.

**Mechanism 4 — Tinnitus-Reha (stationäre Rehabilitation).** The highest-value GKV intervention the dossier omits entirely. Under §40 SGB V Reha-Verordnung, GKV-insured users with chronic tinnitus + documented psychosocial impairment (TFI > 38 or THI > 38 with PHQ-9/GAD-7 mood overlay) qualify for **3-4-week residential rehab** at GKV-Vertrag clinics: **Tinnitus-Klinik Dr. Hesse Bad Arolsen** (Hesse), **MEDIAN Bad Salzhausen** (Hesse), **Knappschaftsklinik Bad Driburg** (NRW), **MEDIAN Bad Bellingen** (Baden-Württemberg), **Klinik Wespien Aachen** (NRW), **Reha-Zentrum Bad Sooden-Allendorf**. A 3-week Reha-Aufenthalt is fully covered with €10/day Eigenbeteiligung (~€210 over 3 weeks). Covered services include §5.5 (CBT-T + MBCT-T) + §5.11 (physiotherapist neck-jaw release) + §5.13 (HRV biofeedback) + §11 (structured TFI/THI scoring) — replicating the §8.5 24-hour day plan in residential form.

**Mechanism 5 — Psychotherapie (§28-32 SGB V).** Approved tracks (Verhaltenstherapie / tiefenpsychologisch / analytisch / systemisch) are GKV-funded at 60-300 sessions/year. Tinnitus-distress is recognised indication; CBT-T-trained psychotherapists in the Psychotherapeutenkammer registry accessible via Terminservicestelle 116 117 (one-week acute-appointment guarantee under 2019 TSVG reform).

### 3.2 The actual Australian tinnitus coverage landscape

**Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) coverage.** Medicare is universal (~100% of citizens + PRs). Tinnitus-relevant items: **MBS 23/36/44** GP consultation (bulk-billed at many practices, rebate otherwise); **MBS 132/133** GP Mental Health Treatment Plan + review (required gate for Better Access); **MBS 11300** pure-tone audiometry; **MBS 11304** tympanometry; **MBS 11309** OAE testing; **MBS 80000-80020** Better Access registered psychologist (10 individual + 10 group sessions/year); **MBS 80100-80120** Better Access clinical psychologist (same cap, higher rebate); **MBS 41500/41506/41512** ENT specialist on GP referral.

**Better Access Initiative — the critical surface for tinnitus distress.** Established 2006, scaled to 20 sessions during COVID, returned to 10 + 10 in 2023. A GP-MHTP citing comorbid tinnitus distress + anxiety/depression/adjustment disorder unlocks 10 individual CBT or MBCT sessions at Medicare rebate. **The dossier's §5.5 MBCT-T (Cima 2012) and §5.7 CBT-T are exactly what Better Access funds in person at no additional cost** to the Australian reader who pursues GP-MHTP. The §11 PHQ-9/GAD-7 scoring overlaps perfectly with MHTP eligibility.

**Hearing Australia + Office of Hearing Services.** Hearing Australia (formerly Australian Hearing) is the Commonwealth-funded provider for:

- Pensioner Concession Card holders.
- Department of Veterans' Affairs Gold/White Card holders.
- NDIS participants with hearing-related disability.
- Indigenous Australians aged 26-50 (free service).

Services include free audiometry, hearing aid fitting (with Commonwealth-funded device or top-up to private model), tinnitus assessment, and Hearing Australia's Tinnitus Management Program. The dossier should signpost Hearing Australia for eligible readers.

**Private health insurance "extras" tier.** Australian PHI is segmented into hospital cover (medical) + extras cover (allied health + complementary medicine). Extras-tier complementary medicine varies by fund:

- **Bupa Premium Extras** — covers acupuncture, Chinese herbal medicine, naturopathy, remedial massage; up to $400-600/year combined limit depending on product. As of 2019, Bupa removed most natural-therapy rebates after the 2017 Natural Therapies Review (NHMRC); some have been reintroduced 2023-2024 with evidence-based requalification (acupuncture for chronic pain remains covered).
- **HCF Premium Extras** — acupuncture covered $35-50/session, naturopathy $35-45/session, Chinese herbal medicine $25-40/consult. Annual cap $400-700.
- **Medibank Top Extras 80** — acupuncture, Chinese herbal medicine, remedial massage covered at 65-80% of consultation fee, capped $500-900/year.
- **nib Top Extras** — acupuncture + remedial massage covered; some Chinese herbal medicine. $300-650/year cap.

The 2017 NHMRC Natural Therapies Review and the 2019 PHI regulation change removed coverage for homoeopathy, iridology, reflexology, kinesiology, and a few others from PHI rebates. Acupuncture for chronic pain, remedial massage, and certain Chinese herbal medicine consultations remain covered by most extras tiers.

**Specialist tinnitus services in Australia.** Limited but growing: Royal Victorian Eye and Ear Hospital Melbourne, Princess Alexandra Hospital Brisbane, Sir Charles Gairdner Hospital Perth, plus the Australian Hearing-affiliated Sydney centre. **Tinnitus Australia (TAUS) helpline** — peer support, charity-funded, signposted on Healthdirect Australia.

Private health insurance penetration in India is rising (~37% of urban households have some form of private health cover beyond ESI 2024 NSO data); the IRDAI 2019 circular structurally aligned PHI with the AYUSH ecosystem the dossier honours.

**ESI (Employees' State Insurance Scheme).** ~140 million ESI-insured (employees + dependents in formal sector). ESI hospitals include AYUSH wings in 100+ centres; AYUSH treatments PMJAY-equivalent for ESI-insured.

**CGHS (Central Government Health Scheme).** ~4.2 million CGHS-insured (Central Government employees + pensioners + their families). CGHS Wellness Centres include AYUSH at 32+ centres; AYUSH treatments fully covered for CGHS beneficiaries.
