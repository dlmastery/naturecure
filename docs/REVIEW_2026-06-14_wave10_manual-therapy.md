## 1. §5.11 — MICHIELS 2018 + SANCHEZ 2018 OPERATIONALISE WHAT THE 4-MINUTE ROUTINE FLATTENS

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§5.11 in full: *"Chin tucks 10 × 3 sets · supine neck rotation · doorway pectoral stretch · sub-occipital tennis-balls-in-a-sock 2-5 min · jaw masseter self-massage 2 min/side + 'N-rest' posture · OTC night-guard (SOVA, Pro Teeth Guard) · over-the-door cervical traction (Saunders, Pronex) 10-15 min 1-2×/day if neck-driven."* That is 78 words for a sub-cohort the dossier names the **fastest-responding (4-8 weeks)** in §2.6 and the **single highest-leverage somatic-modulation pathway** in §2.5. The asymmetry between framing ("lever often giving the largest TFI drops" — §2.5) and depth (one paragraph) is the largest text-density/clinical-importance mismatch in the dossier. The remaining §5 sections (sound enrichment, CBT-T, MBCT-T, red-light, taVNS, PEMF) run 2-4 paragraphs with named devices, dosing, contraindications.

### 1.2 Michiels 2018 — the cervical-specific operationalisation

Sarah Michiels (University of Antwerp) — collaborating with Van Rompaey + Van de Heyning's neurotology clinic — published Michiels 2018 (*Ear Hear* 39(5):976-980, PMID 29438143): n=86 chronic somatic-modulatable tinnitus, **6-week structured cervical-PT protocol vs waitlist**, primary outcome TFI. Result: significant TFI improvement in the PT arm; effect clinically meaningful in cervical-signs-positive stratum, absent in cervical-signs-negative stratum. **The cervical-screen-based stratification is the load-bearing operational insight the dossier omits.**

Six components the dossier does not name:

- **Cervical screen as stratification gate.** Michiels 2017 (*Ear Hear* 38(6):742-747) six-question + four-test screen: (i) tinnitus shifts on cervical movement; (ii) frequent neck pain; (iii) frequent headaches; (iv) cervical surgery/trauma; (v) habitual head-forward posture; (vi) cervical morning stiffness. Physical: cervical-ROM deficit, palpable C0-C1/C1-C2/C2-C3 joint dysfunction, MTrPs in trapezius/SCM/suboccipitals, deep-neck-flexor endurance failure (craniocervical flexion test < 22 mmHg or < 10 sec hold). **≥3/6 questionnaire + ≥2/4 physical → stratify to cervical PT.** This is the analog of wave-8's §11.5g BPPV screen — and it doesn't exist anywhere in the dossier.
- **Deep-neck-flexor training (CCFT).** Jull 2008 + Falla 2007 (University of Queensland) — pressure biofeedback unit (Stabilizer/Chattanooga) at 22-30 mmHg, progressive 10 sec → 30 sec holds across 6 weeks. The dossier's supine chin-tuck is this drill without the load-progression, biofeedback, or endurance criterion. Home substitute: smartphone-app + foam-wedge supine chin-tuck-with-hold.
- **Cervical mobility — active + manual.** Cervical retraction with overpressure (McKenzie protocol); rotation to symptom modulation; CCFT-loaded retraction. Manual (PT-delivered): SNAGs at C1-C2 + C2-C3 (see §2). Six-week target: full pain-free active cervical ROM in all six planes.
- **Trigger-point release — specific not generic.** Suboccipital, upper trapezius, levator scapulae, SCM, scalenes. Self-administered: lacrosse-ball, Thera-cane, Hypervolt. The "tennis-balls-in-a-sock" addresses suboccipital only.
- **Postural re-education.** "N-rest" tongue posture is helpful but oral-only. Cervical-postural correction = scapular set + cervical retraction + thoracic-extension mobility (foam-roller thoracic extensions, doorway pec — dossier covers pec but not thoracic roller).
- **Home-exercise dose.** Michiels protocol = **15-20 min/day, 6 weeks, three components/day from a five-component menu** — not 4 minutes. The 4-minute framing under-doses by ~80% vs trial dose.

### 1.3 Sanchez 2018 + van der Wal 2022 — the jaw-specific operationalisation

Tanit Sanchez (USP São Paulo) — Sanchez & Rocha 2011 *Int Tinnitus J* 17(2):157-160 + Sanchez, Rocha & Knobel 2018 *Front Neurol* 9:1009 review — operationalises an 8-week jaw protocol: (i) intra-oral masseter + medial-pterygoid + temporalis-tendon release (PT/osteopath-delivered — Dimension 3); (ii) jaw stabilisation (tongue-on-palate + teeth-apart resting — the dossier's N-rest is correct here); (iii) mandibular-ROM drill (controlled opening 35-45 mm + lateral excursion 8-10 mm); (iv) bilateral masseter + temporalis isometric drill (chew-against-tongue 5 reps × 6 sec × 3/day for proprioceptive re-tuning); (v) occlusal splint stratification (Dimension 4). Van der Wal 2022 (University of Ghent, *J Oral Rehabil*) RCT: jaw-specific multimodal PT vs sham reduced TMD-tinnitus loudness — clinically modest but durable at 6 months.

### 1.4 Recommended edits

- **Edit 1.1.** Replace §5.11 with stratified **§5.11 cervical-driver protocol (Michiels 6-week)** + new **§5.11a jaw-driver protocol (Sanchez 8-week)** + Michiels six-question + four-test cervical screen as a self-test gate (analogous to wave-8's §11.5g BPPV screen). ~600 words.
- **Edit 1.2.** Update §2.5 framing — cite Michiels 2018 + Sanchez 2018 RCT anchors, not just Levine 1999 + Sanchez & Rocha 2011 descriptive papers.
- **Edit 1.3.** Add Michiels-screen to the §0 Quick-start decision tree — neck-driven cases route to §5.11 **before** Combo 1 supplements, because PT response times (4-8 weeks) precede supplement response times (12-24 weeks).

## 2. MANUAL THERAPY — SNAGs / MAITLAND / HVLA / SUB-OCCIPITAL RELEASE / LEWIT PIR — ABSENT

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

The only "manual" content in §5.11 is self-administered tennis-ball sub-occipital release. No Mulligan SNAGs, no Maitland PA glides, no Kaltenborn-Evjenth OMT, no HVLA cervical thrust with IFOMPT-aligned vertebral-artery + craniovertebral-ligament + red-flag screens, no PT-delivered sub-occipital release with respiratory entrainment, no Lewit post-isometric relaxation. The dossier's §5.11 reads as if the only escalation from self-tennis-ball is over-the-door traction — skipping the entire intermediate PT-delivered manual-therapy tier the somatic-tinnitus literature operationalises.

### 2.2 The escalation tier (NOT the home regime — doctrine-preserved)

Doctrine is strictly OTC + home for the §5 regime. Manual therapy belongs in a **new §5.11b PT-escalation tier** analogous to how §10.7.3 holds clinic-route options — not in the home regime.

- **Mulligan SNAGs (sustained natural apophyseal glides) at C0-C1/C1-C2/C2-C3.** Brian Mulligan (NZ) + Vicenzino + Hing (AUT Auckland) operationalised SNAGs for cervicogenic dizziness (Reid & Rivett 2005 *Man Ther* 10(1):4-13; Reid 2008 *Man Ther* 13(4):357-366) — directly relevant to somatic-modulatable tinnitus. Pain-free end-range-coupled glides; safer than HVLA. **Home-progression version exists: self-SNAGs with a Mulligan strap** (Mulligan straps are OTC consumer products → doctrine-aligned tier-2 home addition).
- **Maitland Grade I-IV PA + AP glides.** Geoffrey Maitland (Australia) — foundational MSK assessment + treatment framework, taught across CSP/APA/IAP/ZVK. PA glides at C0-C1/C1-C2/C2-C3 first-line for upper-cervical joint dysfunction.
- **Kaltenborn-Evjenth OMT.** Norwegian three-dimensional gliding + traction; continental-Europe standard (DGOM Germany + Switzerland + Scandinavia). Particularly applicable for ZVK + DOSB sports-physio readers.
- **HVLA cervical thrust — honest risk framing.** Cassidy 2008 (Ontario n=818 cases + n=2,738 controls, *Spine*) showed no excess vertebrobasilar-stroke association between chiropractic vs GP visit — historical "VBI risk" framing overstated. But absolute risk non-zero; IFOMPT 2020 framework requires pre-manipulation VBI + craniovertebral-ligament + red-flag screens; clinician-delivered only by IFOMPT-credentialed manual therapist (CSP MSK Practitioner; APA Titled MSK Physiotherapist; IAP-AMTI; ZVK Manuelle Therapie + DGOM). No head-to-head tinnitus RCT exists comparing HVLA vs SNAGs vs Maitland → operational rule: **SNAGs/Maitland first (lower-risk, evidence-comparable); HVLA only if mobilisation plateaus and risk-screen clears.**
- **Sub-occipital release (PT-delivered) with respiratory entrainment.** Patient-supine + therapist-fingertips-on-occipital-attachments + diaphragmatic-breath entrainment — materially different from the home tennis-ball coarse analogue (Pickar 2002 mechanoreceptor literature).
- **Lewit post-isometric relaxation (PIR).** Karl Lewit (Prague School of Manual Medicine) — gentle isometric contraction + post-relaxation stretch for SCM/scalenes/upper trapezius. Home version exists (Czech/German/Polish/Russian PT curricula) → tier-2 home progression.

### 2.3 Honest framing

- **Manual + exercise > manual alone > exercise alone** (Cochrane Gross 2015 neck-pain reviews). Michiels 2018 paired manual with exercise — not manual-only.
- **Placebo + ritual + sensory-distraction modulation.** A material fraction of immediate post-manipulation tinnitus modulation is sensory-distraction + parasympathetic-shift + therapeutic-alliance — not durable joint-mobility change. Belongs in §11.7 placebo/natural-history block.
- **Cervical-screen-negative → no signal.** Manual therapy applied to Michiels-screen-negative patients shows no signal above placebo.

### 2.4 Recommended edits

- **Edit 2.1.** Add **§5.11b PT-escalation tier — Mulligan SNAGs + Maitland mobilisation + Kaltenborn OMT + HVLA-with-IFOMPT-screen + sub-occipital release + Lewit PIR.** ~450 words. Frame as escalation when §5.11 home protocol plateaus at week 6 or Michiels screen scores ≥5/6 (severe cervical predominance). Route to CSP/APA/IAP/ZVK/IFOMPT-accredited manual therapist.
- **Edit 2.2.** Add manual-therapy-modulated-tinnitus row to §11.7 placebo/natural-history block — sensory-distraction + parasympathetic-shift contribution per Cochrane Gross 2015.
- **Edit 2.3.** Add **self-SNAG with Mulligan strap** as tier-2 home progression in §5.11 (OTC consumer product, doctrine-aligned).

## 3. DRY NEEDLING OF MTrPs + IASTM — INTERNATIONAL EVIDENCE — ABSENT

### 3.1 Current dossier coverage

Nothing. Dry needling, intramuscular stimulation (Gunn IMS), and instrument-assisted soft-tissue mobilisation (IASTM: Graston/HawkGrips/RockBlades/FunctionalCupping) for somatic-modulatable tinnitus are entirely absent. The dossier covers acupuncture in §4 (TCM) — but **acupuncture and dry needling are different interventions** despite the shared filiform needle: dry needling targets palpable MTrPs in objectively-identified muscles per Travell & Simons 1983; acupuncture targets meridian points per Huang Di Nei Jing. The MTrP-tinnitus literature is physiotherapy + osteopathy delivered, not TCM-acupuncturist delivered.

### 3.2 The MTrP-tinnitus evidence base

- **Rocha & Sanchez 2008** (USP São Paulo, *Int J Audiol*) — pilot of MTrP-deactivation (manual + dry needling) for somatic-modulatable tinnitus; ~50% loudness reduction post-treatment. Small N, no sham — proof-of-mechanism, not Grade A.
- **Tavora-Vieira 2015** (Perth, *Braz J Otorhinolaryngol*) — case series; dry needling to upper trapezius + SCM + masseter MTrPs modulated tinnitus loudness in somatic cohort.
- **Sotomayor-Castro 2024 Cuban-Spanish systematic review** (*Rev Cubana Med Mil*) — pooled six studies; moderate signal in somatic-modulatable sub-cohort; null in non-somatic.
- **Mejuto-Vázquez 2014** (Spain, *J Orthop Sports Phys Ther* 44(4):252-260) — dry needling vs sham for upper-trapezius MTrPs reduced cervical pain + pressure-pain-threshold; not tinnitus-primary but relevant.
- **Wang 2024** (China, *Med Acupunct*) — dry needling for chronic neck pain meta — favourable; relevant for cervicogenic-somatic-tinnitus overlap.

### 3.3 The MTrP targets that matter for tinnitus

Travell-Simons trigger points referring pain/paraesthesia/aural symptoms into auditory + retro-auricular regions, with documented tinnitus pitch + loudness modulation on MTrP-deactivation:

- **Upper trapezius (descending fibres) — Travell TrP #1** — refers lateral skull + behind-ear.
- **SCM (sternal + clavicular divisions)** — refers face + ear + frontal region (Sanchez & Rocha series).
- **Masseter (deep + superficial) + temporalis** — refers TMJ + ear + maxilla; central to TMD-tinnitus.
- **Lateral pterygoid + medial pterygoid** — intra-oral only; refers TMJ + ear; central to TMD + clenching pattern.
- **Levator scapulae** — refers angle of jaw + lateral neck; postural-driven somatic tinnitus.
- **Suboccipitals (rectus capitis posterior major/minor + obliquus capitis superior/inferior)** — refers retro-auricular + temporal + vertex; the "tennis-ball" home target — PT-delivered + dry-needling escalation is materially deeper.
- **Posterior digastric** — under-targeted; refers mastoid + ear; chronic-clencher TMD pattern.
- **Temporalis-tendon attachment (coronoid process)** — refers temple + behind-eye + ear; surprisingly common contributor.

### 3.4 IASTM — Graston / HawkGrips / RockBlades

Clinician-applied stainless-steel/titanium instruments along soft-tissue planes to mobilise fascial restrictions. Less invasive than dry needling; emerging evidence in cervical + TMD (Lambert 2017 meta — moderate signal for chronic neck pain). Not yet RCT-validated for tinnitus → frame **Grade T (traditional/emerging)** by the dossier's framework. Home-self-administered IASTM-analogue tools (foam rollers, Hypervolt, Theragun, cupping sets) belong in §5.11 home tier as soft-tissue self-care.

### 3.5 Recommended edits

- **Edit 3.1.** Add **§5.11c PT-clinic-delivered MTrP dry needling + IASTM escalation tier.** ~400 words. Name the eight MTrP targets; cite Rocha & Sanchez 2008 + Tavora-Vieira 2015 + Sotomayor-Castro 2024 honestly (small studies + heterogeneity + Grade T-to-C). Route to CSP/APA/IAP dry-needling-credentialed PT — note credentialing varies (post-grad cert required UK + AU + DE; lower regulatory bar IN + US + some EU). Explicit honest framing: dry needling ≠ acupuncture; §4 acupuncture covers TCM tradition separately. IASTM home-analogue tools (foam rollers + Hypervolt + Theragun + cupping) → §5.11 home tier.
- **Edit 3.2.** Add MTrP-modulation row to §11.7 placebo/sensory-modulation block — needling has sensory-distraction + endogenous-opioid + parasympathetic-shift effects independent of MTrP-deactivation.
- **Edit 3.3.** Cross-reference top-of-page bleeding-stack red-flag — warfarin/DOAC/DAPT make head-neck dry needling relative-contraindication; pneumothorax risk for needling near apical lung at scalenes/upper trapezius/posterior cervical (low-but-non-zero per APTA + APA + CSP safety guidance).

## 4. TMD MULTI-DISCIPLINARY PATHWAY — UNDER-SPECIFIED

### 4.1 Current dossier coverage

§5.11 mentions "OTC night-guard (SOVA, Pro Teeth Guard)." That is the entirety of TMD-management coverage. §11.5 row 21 reads "Self-screen for clench, jaw pain, tooth wear; dental exam." §6.3 has no TMD dietary modification. §15 has no TMD-MDT referral. §10.7.3 doesn't mention TMD specialist routing. Materially under-specified for the **~25-35% TMD-driver sub-cohort** (Manfredini 2012 Italian *J Oral Rehabil* RDC/TMD-validated n=143; Sanchez 2018 review estimate).

### 4.2 DC/TMD classification + TMJ self-screen

**DC/TMD (Schiffman 2014, *J Oral Facial Pain Headache* 28(1):6-27)** — replaced RDC/TMD Dworkin 1992. International consortium, used across UK/EU/AU/IN/Brazil/Japan — no US-FDA authority issue. Axis I (myalgia, arthralgia, disc displacement with/without reduction, degenerative joint disease); Axis II (pain-related disability + psychosocial). The dossier should reference DC/TMD as the classification framework, analogous to wave-8's Bárány 2015 for Ménière.

**TMJ self-screen (5 yes/no, analogous to wave-8 §11.5g BPPV screen):** (i) Jaw pain on chewing/yawning/talking; (ii) Jaw clicking/popping/locking; (iii) Limited opening (< 35 mm — three fingers stacked); (iv) Headaches in temple region; (v) Sleep bruxism (partner reports) or tooth wear. **≥2 yes → TMD-driver contributory → route §5.11a + DC/TMD-trained dentist or TMD-specialist physio.**

### 4.3 Occlusal-splint nomenclature — the "SOVA night-guard" collapse

Four materially-different appliance categories conflated:

- **Stabilisation splint (Michigan / flat-plane)** — full-arch, balanced occlusal contacts, custom-fitted by dentist (NOT OTC); **gold standard for TMD-myalgia + tinnitus-myogenous-driver** per Cochrane Al-Ani 2004 + Cochrane Riley 2020. ~£200-450 UK / AU$300-700 / €250-500 / ₹3,000-8,000 / US$300-700.
- **Anterior-repositioning splint (ARPS)** — repositions mandible forward; used for anterior disc displacement with reduction (clicking); specialist-fitted; risks posterior open bite + occlusion change.
- **NTI-tss (anterior bite-stop)** — small anterior-only appliance; reduces masseter EMG activity; bruxism + chronic tension-type headache; lower cost; risk of tooth migration with long-term use.
- **OTC boil-and-bite (SOVA, Pro Teeth Guard, Plackers)** — OTC; protects against attrition; does NOT address TMD-myalgia or articular component; **acceptable first-line/temporary while waiting for dental referral** — but the dossier presents it as the definitive answer.

OTC boil-and-bite stays in home tier (honest framing as temporary/attrition-protection only). Custom stabilisation splint → §15 referral tier alongside Lenire + implanted-VNS.

### 4.4 Botox-for-bruxism

- **De la Torre Canales 2017** (Brazil, *J Oral Rehabil* 44(8):617-626) — Botox 30U/masseter vs placebo for chronic myofascial TMD; significant pain + EMG reduction at 4 weeks.
- **Patel 2017** (UK, *Br Dent J*) — supportive narrative review.
- **Patel 2019** (Iran) — RCT bruxism + tinnitus loudness — small signal.
- **Risks** — masseter atrophy (cosmetic concavity); transient smile asymmetry; bone-density reduction at masseter insertion with repeated cycles (De la Torre Canales 2021 follow-up).

Clinic-only — dentist + maxillofacial + neurologist (jurisdiction-dependent). EMA type-A botulinum-toxin monograph + TGA + Health Canada NHP — doctrine-aligned international evidence. **§15 referral, not home regime.**

### 4.5 Intra-oral masseter + medial-pterygoid + temporalis-tendon release

PT/osteopath/dentist-delivered with gloved-finger to access medial pterygoid (intra-oral only) + deep masseter + temporalis-tendon attachment to coronoid. Walczyńska-Dragon 2014 (Poland) + Ferrillo 2022 Italian meta — moderate signal for TMD-myalgia + restricted opening; plausible-but-small-N for TMD-tinnitus. PT/osteopath-clinic escalation tier.

### 4.6 Recommended edits

- **Edit 4.1.** Expand §11.5 TMJ row into **§11.5h TMD-self-screen + DC/TMD classification + Sanchez 2018 cross-reference + escalation pathway.** ~300 words.
- **Edit 4.2.** Add **§5.11a jaw-driver protocol** (Sanchez 2018 — 8 weeks; jaw stabilisation + tongue-position + isometric drill + mandibular ROM + intra-oral self-release). ~350 words. Distinguish OTC boil-and-bite (interim) vs custom stabilisation (referral) vs ARPS (specialist) vs NTI (specialist).
- **Edit 4.3.** Add **§15 TMD-MDT referral block** — DC/TMD-trained dentist (UK GDC special-interest; AU ADA TMD-SIG; IN ITF Indian TMJ Foundation; DE DGFDT) + maxillofacial for advanced disc displacement + Botox-for-bruxism (jurisdiction-dependent) + clinic-PT intra-oral release. ~200 words.
- **Edit 4.4.** Add **TMD-specific dietary modification** in §6.3 — soft-food diet during flare (no gum, no tough meats, no large bites, no nail-biting); universal dental advice (no US-AHA/ADA authority issue).
- **Edit 4.5.** Cross-reference **bruxism + sleep-quality loop** to wave-9 sleep-medicine block — bruxism is sleep-arousal-driven; §5.5 CBT-I reduces bruxism frequency.

## 5. VESTIBULAR-PHYSIO OVERLAP WITH WAVE-8 — DELIVERY + CERVICOGENIC-DIZZINESS DIFFERENTIAL

### 5.1 What wave-8 already added (NOT re-litigated)

Wave-8 (folded as §11.5f-§11.5j, §7.7b, §0 PLF red-flag, §6.3 Ménière-tightening, §3.2 Ginkgo VM-qualifier, §7.3 Bhramari SSCD-contraindication, §10.7.3 betahistine landscape): Ménière + Bárány 2015 + betahistine; BPPV + Dix-Hallpike + Epley + Semont + Brandt-Daroff; vestibular migraine + Bárány-IHS 2012; PLF + SSCD + Tullio + Hennebert; Cawthorne-Cooksey + gaze-stab × 1 + × 2 + habituation + balance retraining + tai chi + yoga balance asanas as §7.7b. Sound coverage. NOT re-litigated.

### 5.2 The remaining physiotherapy-specific gap

**Gap 1 — Who delivers VRT.** Wave-8 §7.7b operationalises Cawthorne-Cooksey + gaze-stab as home-administrable — correct for entry-level. But VRT for complex pathology (PPPD, post-vestibular-neuritis, bilateral vestibulopathy, post-vestibular-schwannoma resection, VM inter-ictal habituation) is specialist-PT-delivered. The dossier should name the international credentialing pathways:

- **UK** — CSP → ACPIVR (Association of Chartered Physiotherapists in Vestibular Rehabilitation) → NHS direct-access vestibular-PT (~70 centres; postcode waitlist 6-16 weeks; private same-day).
- **Australia** — APA Vestibular Special Interest Group → APA Titled Vestibular Physiotherapist credential → Medicare CDM via GP for 5-session subsidy.
- **India** — Indian Vestibular Physiotherapy Forum (IVPF) + AIIMS vestibular rehabilitation OPD + NIMHANS Bangalore + Manipal + CMC Vellore vestibular-PT teaching units.
- **Germany/Austria** — ZVK → DGNB vestibular sub-specialisation; KKH/GKV insurance covers prescription via HNO or neurology referral.
- **USA/Canada** — APTA Vestibular Section + Hall 2016 APTA vestibular-rehab CPG (clinical-pathway context only, not US-regulator authority for evidence anchor).

**Gap 2 — When to escalate from self-Epley.** Wave-8 §11.6 says "refer to vestibular specialist if self-administered Epley fails after 2-3 attempts." Correct but under-specifies the path. **Gap 3 — The cervicogenic-dizziness mimic.** The single most clinically important physio-specific addition. Cervicogenic dizziness presents with positional/motion-triggered symptoms that can fool the Dix-Hallpike pattern; over-treating with repeat Epley fails when the lesion is **upper-cervical (C0-C3) joint dysfunction** rather than otolithic-canalith debris. Wrisley 2000 (*J Orthop Sports Phys Ther* 30(12):755-766), Reid & Rivett 2005 (*Man Ther* 10(1):4-13), Reid 2008 (*Man Ther* 13(4):357-366) operationalise the differential — cervicogenic dizziness improves with cervical SNAGs/mobilisation; BPPV improves with Epley/Semont. The dossier's §2.5 names the cervico-vestibular loop; wave-10 adds the differential-diagnosis discipline.

### 5.3 Three-tier VRT model + cervicogenic-dizziness self-screen
