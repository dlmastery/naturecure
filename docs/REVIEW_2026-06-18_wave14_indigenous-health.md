## TL;DR VERDICT

The dossier's biggest blind spot is structural: it calls itself pluralistic across six traditions, but the six are all **classical literate-text traditions with written Sanskrit / Pali / Chinese / Arabic / Tibetan / Hahnemann texts**, while the entire continent of Aboriginal + Torres Strait Islander oral-tradition medicine, all of rongoā Māori, the FNHA + NCCIH + Mi'kmaw Two-Eyed Seeing framework, Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit, Sámi traditional medicine, Ainu Iyomante-cosmology medicine, Adivasi LHT (Local Health Traditions), and the WHO Pacific traditional-medicine framework are simply missing. Five-dimension fix package below; one skill Rule 25 update; eight rendered §-additions in the dossier. Severity: major. Doctrine impact: load-bearing — the six-tradition floor changes.

## 1. THE PARTIAL-PLURALISM CRITIQUE — THE SIX-TRADITION FLOOR REKONNED

### 1.1 What the six traditions actually are

Ayurveda (Sanskrit Charaka + Sushruta + Vagbhata + Bhavaprakasha + Sharangdhara) — written-text. TCM (Chinese *Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic* + *Shang Han Lun* + *Imperial Golden Mirror* + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020) — written-text. Unani (Greco-Arabic — Avicenna's *Canon* + Galen + Qarabadeen Qadri) — written-text. Siddha (Tamil — *Agastya Samhita* + Theraiyar texts) — written-text. Tibetan Sowa Rigpa (Tibetan *Four Tantras* / *rGyud bzhi*) — written-text. Homeopathy (Hahnemann's *Organon* + Boericke *Materia Medica*) — written-text. **All six are codified, transmitted through Sanskrit / Pali / Chinese / Arabic / Tibetan / 19th-century German printed texts, mediated by literate-clerical-vaidya-hakim-lama-doctor classes.** That is one specific family of traditional medicine: the **classical literate-text family**.

### 1.2 What the floor IGNORES

- **Aboriginal + Torres Strait Islander** traditional medicine — 65,000-year continuous oral tradition; knowledge held + transmitted by Elders + Traditional Healers (ngangkari Aṉangu + ngangkari APY + Yuggera + Kuku Yalanji + Wiradjuri + Arrernte + Mununjali + Yindjibarndi); plant medicine cataloguing through NACCHO + ACCHS network; Aboriginal Bush Traders + Coomealla Health Aboriginal Corporation as community-controlled commercial bridges; protected under the Native Title Act 1993 + Cultural Heritage protections. Tinnitus + ear-health work specifically through Telethon Kids Institute Kulunga Aboriginal Research Development Unit + Lowitja Institute + Hearing Australia Aboriginal Hearing Program.
- **Māori** rongoā Māori — Te Kāhui Rongoā Trust national practitioner body + Te Paepae Matua mō te Rongoā advisory framework; ACC-funded since 2020 (recognition by statutory accident-compensation system); whānau-based transmission; spiritual + physical + family + mental health integrated through Te Whare Tapa Whā framework.
- **First Nations + Inuit + Métis** medicine — FNHA-BC's First Nations Traditional Wellness + Métis-specific + Inuit-specific frameworks; Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit (IQ) as a knowledge system; Mi'kmaw Elder Albert Marshall's Etuaptmumk Two-Eyed Seeing as a methodology for integrating Indigenous + Western knowledges without privileging either; midewiwin medicine lodge traditions (Ojibwe + Anishinaabe); Plains medicine + Cree healing.
- **Native American + Alaska Native + Native Hawaiian** medicine — cited as comparator-context per doctrine; lā'au lapa'au (Hawaiian plant medicine) through Papa Ola Lokahi.
- **Sámi** traditional medicine — birch + Labrador-tea + cloudberry phytochemistry codified through SANKS + University of Tromsø Centre for Sámi Health Research; *gáktečuovga* (light-from-the-traditional-coat) health-equity framing.
- **Ainu** medicine — kamuy (spirit) + iyomante cosmology + plant-medicine catalogue through Ainu Association of Hokkaido health programme; recognised under the Ainu Policy Promotion Act 2019.
- **Adivasi + Indigenous India** — distinct from classical Ayurveda; AYUSH Tribal Health programme bridges; CCRAS Local Health Traditions (LHT) documentation; community-specific traditions across Jashpur + Bastar + Wayanad + Nilgiris + Niyamgiri + Andaman + Nicobar.
- **Pacific** traditional medicine — Fiji + Samoa + Tonga + Solomon Islands + Vanuatu + PNG + Pohnpei + Marshall Islands traditional-healer councils; WHO Pacific TM Integration Framework.

The collective coverage gap is enormous. The dossier's "we honour six healing traditions that described ear-ringing centuries before audiology existed" (§4.1) is true and incomplete — it honours six classical literate-text traditions and silently excludes everything else.

### 1.3 Why this is doctrine-impact

Wave-6 cross-cultural-localisation surfaced jurisdictional + cultural-translation gaps. Wave-3 classical-text scholar improved citation rigor *within* the six. **Wave-14 surfaces that the six are themselves a sub-family of traditional medicine — not the whole.** The skill currently mandates "six traditions ≥2 entries each." If the six are kept as-is, the floor is a partial-pluralism floor. If the floor is raised to eight, ten, or twelve traditions, the same epistemological respect must extend — but the tinnitus-specific RCT base is **intentionally thin** in many Indigenous contexts because communities have **refused Western-RCT methodology as extractive** (Linda Tuhiwai Smith *Decolonising Methodologies* 1999/2012; Margaret Kovach *Indigenous Methodologies* 2009; Shawn Wilson *Research Is Ceremony* 2008). **This is not absence-of-evidence — it is epistemological-respect-for-community-refusal-of-extraction.** The dossier must say this explicitly.

### 1.4 Three options

- **Option (a) — Expand the floor.** Raise six → eight or ten: add Aboriginal + rongoā Māori + FNHA First-Nations + Inuit + Sámi + Ainu + Adivasi + WHO-Pacific as additional explicit traditions with ≥2 entries each. Cost: significant per-dossier research lift; risk of token-quantity-without-substantive-engagement; benefit: maximises explicit inclusion.
- **Option (b) — Reframe the doctrine (recommended).** Rename the six floor as "**six classical literate-text traditions**" (which is what they actually are) + add a separate "**Indigenous oral-tradition + community-knowledge-holder framework**" parallel section (new §4.8) with epistemological-respect framing + per-community knowledge-holder routing + Nagoya-compliance statement + named Aboriginal NACCHO + rongoā Māori + FNHA Two-Eyed-Seeing + AYUSH Tribal Health + Sámi + Pacific entries even where tinnitus-specific RCT base is thin. Cost: load-bearing skill change; benefit: honest naming of what the six are + parallel respect for what they aren't.
- **Option (c) — Acknowledge the limit honestly without doctrine change.** Name the partial-pluralism gap explicitly in a new §0.6 pre-amble + point readers to community-specific knowledge-holders + commit to extending in future dossiers. Cost: low; benefit: minimal — risks the *colonising silence* the dossier currently inhabits.

**Recommendation: Option (b)** — it is the only option that honours both the classical-text traditions for what they are AND opens space for oral-tradition + community-knowledge-holder medicine on its own epistemological terms. Option (a) without (b) risks token-quantity-without-substantive-engagement; Option (c) without (b) leaves the colonising silence intact.

## 2. NAGOYA PROTOCOL COMPLIANCE + EXTRACTIVE-RESEARCH-PATTERN AUDIT

### 2.1 What Nagoya requires

The Nagoya Protocol on Access to Genetic Resources and the Fair and Equitable Sharing of Benefits Arising from their Utilization (CBD, adopted 2010, in force 12 Oct 2014; 140+ Parties as of 2026) requires for any use of genetic resources + associated traditional knowledge: (a) **prior informed consent (PIC)** from the source-community + source-country competent national authority; (b) **mutually-agreed terms (MAT)** spelling out access conditions; (c) **fair + equitable benefit-sharing** — monetary or non-monetary, channelled to source-community. CBD Article 8(j) requires Parties to "respect, preserve and maintain knowledge, innovations and practices of indigenous and local communities... and... equitable sharing of the benefits." Bonn Guidelines (2002) operationalised pre-Nagoya. Akwé:Kon Guidelines (2004) added cultural + environmental + social impact assessment standards. WHO Traditional Medicine Strategy 2025-2034 reinforces the source-community-equity principle.

### 2.2 What the dossier does

Every traditional-medicine citation is a use of traditional knowledge. The dossier cites:

- Ayurveda — Charaka + Sushruta + Vagbhata + Bhavaprakasha + Sharangdhara + Kottakkal + Vaidyaratnam + Baidyanath + AYUSH-CCRAS.
- TCM — *Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic* + *Imperial Golden Mirror* + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 + Plum Flower (Mayway) + Min Tong + NMPA-patent monographs.
- Unani — Avicenna's *Canon* + Hamdard (Hakim Mohammed Said) + Dawakhana Tibbiya College Aligarh + Qarabadeen Qadri.
- Siddha — Tamil Siddhar + IMPCOPS Chennai + Theraiyar texts.
- Tibetan — *Four Tantras* + Men-Tsee-Khang Dharamsala + Sowa Rigpa.
- Homeopathy — Hahnemann's *Organon* + Boericke + Schwabe.

The dossier surfaces **zero** Nagoya-compliance statement. Zero per-tradition source-community benefit-sharing commitment. Zero supplier-ecosystem audit on whether commercial Kottakkal + Plum Flower + Schwabe + Hamdard + Men-Tsee-Khang + IMPCOPS source-institutions hold ABS (Access + Benefit-Sharing) agreements with the source-knowledge-communities. The supplier ecosystem §9 lists prices + brick-and-mortar addresses + brand options without any audit dimension.

### 2.3 The convergent wave-13-meets-wave-14 finding

Wave-13's inverse-care-law theme (the protocol's $390-680/month full pack, $200+ home-device layer, OECD-urban-middle-class implicit reader all structurally exclude the readers who most need it) **meets the Nagoya finding** (the traditional-knowledge inputs come from source-communities that are not compensated, credited or accountable-to). The same extractive structural pattern flows in both directions: extract traditional knowledge from source-communities for commerce → sell to OECD-urban-middle-class readers → exclude both the source-communities AND the structurally-vulnerable target readers. The dossier currently inhabits the centre of this extraction without naming it.

### 2.4 Concrete fix — §0.6 Nagoya-compliance statement

New §0.6 pre-amble (~120 words):

> *Nagoya Protocol + Indigenous knowledge sovereignty.* Every herb, formula and tradition cited in this dossier carries source-community knowledge. We honour the Nagoya Protocol on Access and Benefit-Sharing (CBD 2014), CBD Article 8(j), and WHO Traditional Medicine Strategy 2025-2034 source-community-equity principle. Where this dossier recommends a commercial product (Kottakkal, Plum Flower, Schwabe, Hamdard, Men-Tsee-Khang, IMPCOPS, AYUSH-CCRAS-network), we have audited [see §9.x] whether the source-institution holds an ABS (Access + Benefit-Sharing) agreement with the source-knowledge-community. Where we cite Indigenous oral-tradition medicine [see §4.8], we route readers to the community-controlled access pathway (NACCHO, Te Kāhui Rongoā Trust, FNHA, AYUSH Tribal Health, SANKS, WHO Pacific TM Integration Framework), NOT to extract-and-sell phytochemical supply chains.

### 2.5 Concrete fix — §9.x supplier-ecosystem audit dimension

Add a column to the §9 supplier-ecosystem table: **Nagoya-ABS status** — disclosed (with link), undisclosed, not-applicable (non-traditional-knowledge supplier). This forces the supplier ecosystem to surface the gap and gives the founder an editorial signal to prefer ABS-compliant suppliers (Kottakkal + AYUSH-CCRAS-network + IMPCOPS + Men-Tsee-Khang generally publish more on the source-community-equity dimension than diaspora-commercial Plum Flower + Schwabe; this audit forces the choice to be visible).

### 2.6 Concrete fix — §16 community-level OCAP + research-benefit-sharing commitment

§16 currently closes with "independent healing paradigm." Wave-14 add: an explicit **community-level benefit-sharing commitment** — the storefront commits a percentage of revenue from traditional-medicine product lines (e.g. 2%) to source-community-controlled funds (AYUSH-CCRAS folk-medicine research; NACCHO ear-health programme; Te Kāhui Rongoā Trust; FNHA; SANKS). ## 3. INDIGENOUS HEALTH FRAMEWORK INTEGRATION

### 3.1 Beyond decoration

Adding Indigenous health frameworks as PARALLEL conceptual scaffolds, not as decoration, means the frameworks change how the protocol is read + applied. The dossier currently scaffolds on §11.5 life-stage gate + §11.6 cluster screen + §2 four-cause mechanistic network. Indigenous frameworks supply different scaffolds.

### 3.2 Te Whare Tapa Whā (Māori — Mason Durie 1982/1994)

The four-walls house: **taha tinana** (physical) + **taha hinengaro** (mental + emotional) + **taha whānau** (family + community + relational) + **taha wairua** (spiritual). If any wall is weak, the house wobbles. Tinnitus-application: the §11.5 life-stage gate covers taha tinana well, partial taha hinengaro through PHQ-9 + GAD-7, partial taha whānau through wave-6/9/13 supportive-household assumptions, **zero taha wairua**. Concrete add: §11.5b "four-walls integrity check" (Te Whare Tapa Whā for Māori readers; conceptually-equivalent four-dimension self-check for all readers — physical-mental-relational-meaning) — 1 row per wall + 1 plain-words reflection per wall.

### 3.3 Te Pae Māhutonga (Mason Durie 1999)

The Southern-Cross six-star: **mauriora** (cultural identity) + **waiora** (physical environment + clean water + safe air) + **toiora** (healthy lifestyles) + **te oranga** (participation in society) + **ngā manukura** (community leadership) + **te mana whakahaere** (autonomy + self-determination). This is an outcomes framework — not a list of symptoms to manage but a list of life-domains the protocol should support. Tinnitus-application: the dossier's "TFI < 25 maintenance" outcome is silent on mauriora + ngā manukura + te mana whakahaere; concrete add: §12 12-month timeline could include a "te mana whakahaere" milestone (autonomy + self-determination) at month 6 — "you understand your tinnitus pattern + drivers well enough to teach another person."

### 3.4 Etuaptmumk Two-Eyed Seeing (Mi'kmaw Elder Albert Marshall 2004+; Bartlett, Marshall + Marshall 2012)

Integrating Indigenous knowledge + Western science **without privileging either**. Method: "seeing through one eye with the strengths of Indigenous knowledges + ways of knowing and through the other eye with the strengths of Western knowledges + ways of knowing." Tinnitus-application: the dossier currently privileges the Western eye implicitly (Cochrane meta-analyses + EMA monographs + AYUSH-CCRAS + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 as the citation hierarchy; classical texts as backdrop). Two-Eyed Seeing is an explicit invitation to the reader to integrate the dossier's Western eye with their own community's knowledge eye on equal terms. Concrete add: §0.6 pre-amble names Two-Eyed Seeing explicitly + invites integration; §4.8.4 routes First-Nations readers to Two-Eyed Seeing practitioner networks.

### 3.5 Closing the Gap (AU 2008+ / Refresh 2020+) + FNHA Wellness Wheel (CA)

Closing the Gap = 19 socioeconomic + health targets + 4 Priority Reforms (formal partnerships + Aboriginal-community-controlled organisations + transforming government organisations + shared access to data). FNHA Wellness Wheel = mental + spiritual + emotional + physical wellness on the inside circle + people + family + community + nation + relationships on the outside circle, with respect + wisdom + responsibility + relationships as the four directions. Both are health-equity frameworks that the dossier should explicitly reference for Aboriginal + TSI + First-Nations readers respectively. Concrete add: §15 routing names Closing the Gap (AU) + FNHA Wellness Wheel (CA) as the reader's home framework if applicable.

### 3.6 Sámi gáktečuovga + WHO Pacific TM framework

Gáktečuovga (light-from-the-traditional-coat) — Sámi-specific health-equity framing tying traditional dress + identity + community membership + health to one another (cultural identity as a determinant of health). WHO Pacific TM Integration Framework — formal integration of traditional medicine into Pacific national health systems through joint TM-biomedical practitioner training + community-traditional-healer recognition. Concrete add: §15.x routing names SANKS + Sámiráđđi Health Committee (Sámi); WHO Pacific TM Integration Framework + Papa Ola Lokahi (Pacific + Hawaiian).

### 3.7 Why integration not decoration

The wave-3 disability-inclusion BLOCKER fix worked because non-auditory perceptual-pathway analogues were rendered as **operational** §5.20 protocol additions, not as decoration. Wave-14 Indigenous-framework integration must work the same way: Te Whare Tapa Whā becomes an operational §11.5b four-walls check; Te Pae Māhutonga becomes an operational §12 te-mana-whakahaere milestone; Two-Eyed Seeing becomes an operational §0.6 invitation + §4.8.4 First-Nations routing.

## 5. SELF-DETERMINATION + SOVEREIGNTY + DATA SOVEREIGNTY

### 5.1 What UNDRIP + CARE + OCAP require

UNDRIP Article 24 — Indigenous Peoples have the right to their traditional medicines + to maintain their health practices + access to health services without discrimination. UNDRIP Article 31 — Indigenous Peoples have the right to maintain + control + protect + develop their cultural heritage + traditional knowledge + traditional cultural expressions, including manifestations of their sciences + technologies + cultures, including genetic resources. CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (GIDA 2019; Carroll et al. 2020 *Data Science Journal*): **C**ollective benefit (data ecosystems shall be designed + function in ways that enable Indigenous Peoples to derive benefit from the data) + **A**uthority to control (Indigenous Peoples' rights + interests in Indigenous data must be recognised, and their authority to control such data be empowered) + **R**esponsibility (those working with Indigenous data have a responsibility to share how those data are used to support Indigenous Peoples' self-determination + collective benefit) + **E**thics (Indigenous Peoples' rights + well-being should be the primary concern at all stages of the data life cycle + across the data ecosystem). FNIGC OCAP® — Ownership + Control + Access + Possession (First Nations' inherent right over their own information).

### 5.2 What the dossier does — §11 reader-data architecture

§11 collects: TFI + THI + 0-10 loudness/annoyance slider + PSQI sleep quality + PHQ-9 + GAD-7 + ringing log + adherence trajectories + PEM slider (wave-12 ME/CFS-autonomic). Wave-5 infosec covered individual privacy + GDPR + UK-DPA + HIPAA-comparator + APP-AU. **None of the prior waves covered Indigenous-collective-sovereignty.** The CARE Principles + OCAP framework operate at the *collective* level, not the individual level — community-aggregated trajectories of Aboriginal + TSI + Māori + First Nations + Inuit + Métis + Adivasi + Sámi + Ainu + Pacific readers are themselves a form of Indigenous data that the source-community should have authority over.

### 5.3 The FAIR-vs-CARE tension

Wave-10 knowledge-graph + wave-9 data-engineering surfaced the FAIR (Findable + Accessible + Interoperable + Reusable, GO FAIR 2016) principles for the §11 / §16 data architecture. CARE complements not contradicts FAIR per GIDA framing — but the dossier currently treats reader data as FAIR-by-default + CARE-by-omission. Concrete add: §11.3 Indigenous-data-sovereignty acknowledgement + community-level OCAP opt-in.

### 5.4 Concrete fix — §11.3 Indigenous-data-sovereignty acknowledgement

New §11.3 paragraph (~100 words):

> *Indigenous data sovereignty.* The data you log against this dossier (TFI, THI, sleep score, PHQ-9, GAD-7, ringing log, adherence trajectory) is yours. If you identify as Aboriginal + Torres Strait Islander, Māori, First Nations + Inuit + Métis, Adivasi, Sámi, Ainu, or Pacific Islander, the **CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (Collective benefit + Authority to control + Responsibility + Ethics)** and where applicable **FNIGC OCAP** (Ownership + Control + Access + Possession) apply at the collective level.
