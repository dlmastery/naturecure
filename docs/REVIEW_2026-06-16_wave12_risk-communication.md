## 1. SANDMAN *HAZARD + OUTRAGE* AUDIT — THE TINNITUS READER-POPULATION'S HIGH-OUTRAGE LOW-TO-MODERATE-HAZARD PATTERN

### 1.1 What wave-1 surfaced but never named at framework level

Wave-1 medical reviewer flagged a misinformation-accusation pattern early in the dossier's evolution — the early Lipoflavonoid + off-label-gabapentin framings were under-rigorously phrased and would have read as fringe-natural-medicine misinformation to an evidence-trained clinician. Wave-2 biostatistics + wave-5 fact-checker repaired the *technical* layer (placebo arithmetic, Cima 2012 attribution, Lenire completer-vs-sham framing). What none of waves 1-11 named is that **the misinformation-accusation pattern is not primarily an evidence-rigour signal — it is a Sandman outrage signal**. The chronic-tinnitus reader operates in a hazard-perception space where the actuarial hazard is moderate (severe distress sub-cohort suicidality ~2-3× baseline per Lugo 2022, but most readers sit at lower distress) but the *outrage* is HIGH on every Sandman dimension: (a) **involuntary** (nobody chooses tinnitus); (b) **invisible** (no audiogram-confirmable severity for the median reader); (c) **uncertain prognosis** (12-month outcome distribution genuinely wide per §13.6 qualitative ranges); (d) **chronic** (vs acute); (e) **media-amplified** (Lenire FDA De Novo + Tinnitracks DiGA generated outsize media coverage relative to actuarial benefit); (f) **sufferer-controlled framing** (no objective biomarker, every patient is the authority on their own symptom); (g) **unfamiliar mechanism** (the four-driver framing in §2 is novel to most readers); (h) **distrust of mainstream care** (10-15 years of "incurable nerve damage" mis-framing in primary care has built a deep distrust the reader brings to any source).

Sandman 1993 + 2017 framework: when hazard is moderate but outrage is HIGH, **the communication challenge is not to scare the reader (hazard up) or to reassure the reader (hazard down) — it is to *manage the outrage* by acknowledging the legitimacy of the outrage signals while honestly contextualising actual hazard**. The dossier's current style is calibrated for an actuarial-hazard reader — it states evidence grades, qualifies trial designs, surfaces null findings, names contraindications — and assumes the reader can integrate this evidence-rigour with their internal hazard-perception. **The outrage-driven reader cannot do this integration without explicit outrage-acknowledgement**.

### 1.2 The four communications that need Sandman outrage-management

**(a) The Ginkgo Cochrane-null + EMA-not-indicated framing in §3.2.** This is the highest-outrage communication in the dossier. Ginkgo is the single most heavily marketed natural-medicine intervention for tinnitus globally. The reader arrives with a strong prior belief that Ginkgo helps. The dossier honestly states Cochrane Hilton 2013 null + Sereda 2022 re-issued null + EMA HMPC monograph for mild dementia not tinnitus + indication-mismatched. The honest framing is correct. **What is missing is the outrage-acknowledgement layer: "We know you have heard for years that Ginkgo is THE supplement for tinnitus. That framing came from supplement marketing, not from the science. The science has been clear since at least 2013 and was re-confirmed in 2022. We honour your prior belief and we are honest with you about what the science actually says. Your sovereignty is not violated by being told the truth."** Without this outrage-acknowledgement, the technically-correct Cochrane framing reads to the outrage-driven reader as the same dismissive "incurable nerve damage" message they have been receiving for years, and bounces off.

**(b) The Lenire D-grade + no-sham-comparator framing in §15.2.** This is the second-highest-outrage communication. Lenire was the first FDA-De-Novo-cleared device for tinnitus (March 2023), generating $50+M of media coverage; the manufacturer-promoted "7-9 out of 10 see real relief" framing is widely circulated; the reader arrives with a strong prior belief that Lenire is the breakthrough. The dossier honestly states TENT-A1 + TENT-A2 had no sham comparator; Boedts 2024 is a single-arm registry; ITT vs completer denominators differ; GRADE-strict starting point is observational/low-certainty; D-grade. **What is missing is the outrage-acknowledgement: "Lenire is an honest device built by a real company with a real trial programme. The 7-9-out-of-10 framing is technically the completer-responder rate at 12 weeks — but it is not the active-vs-sham gap, because no sham-controlled trial has been published yet. The device may be genuinely better than placebo; we just cannot know without a sham trial. Until then, the honest grade is D not B. We are not telling you Lenire doesn't work — we are telling you we cannot know yet."**

**(c) The "we do not recommend homeopathy" framing in §13.7 + §4.7.** This is the third-highest-outrage communication. The Indian + German + French reader-population includes a large homeopathy-using sub-segment. The dossier honestly states Simpson 1998 RCT — no benefit beyond placebo — and grades T (tradition documented, not active recommendation). **What is missing is the outrage-acknowledgement: "Homeopathy is a 200-year-old healing tradition that has comforted millions of people. The randomised trials have not shown it works for tinnitus better than placebo. We honour the tradition by documenting it; we are honest that we cannot recommend it as an active layer. If you want to use homeopathic remedies for the comfort-ritual + tradition-honouring value, that is your sovereign choice; we just cannot tell you the evidence supports it."**

**(d) The "no US-regulator authority" doctrine framing in §16.** This is a Sandman outrage signal for the US-resident reader sub-segment, who arrives expecting FDA/AHA/ADA/AAFP authority anchors and finds them deliberately absent. The dossier doctrine is correct (founder mandate; honest framing; non-US-jurisdiction-balance); but the US reader's outrage at the absence of familiar authority anchors needs acknowledgement. **The outrage-acknowledgement: "If you are a US reader, you may notice we do not cite the FDA, AHA, ADA, or your familiar disease associations as authorities. This is not anti-US — it is a deliberate global-balance choice. The evidence base in this dossier comes from WHO, EMA, India's AYUSH, China's NMPA, Australia's TGA, Health Canada NHP, plus named non-US peer-reviewed trials. The science is the same; we simply do not anchor it to one country's regulators."**

## 2. TINKER 7 CARDINAL RULES OF RISK COMMUNICATION — THE LEGITIMACY AUDIT

### 2.1 Tinker rule-by-rule audit of the dossier

Tinker 1996 + 2017 named seven cardinal rules of risk communication (refined from Covello + Allen 1988 EPA original): (1) **accept and involve the public as a legitimate partner**; (2) **plan carefully and evaluate efforts**; (3) **listen to the public's specific concerns**; (4) **be honest, frank, and open**; (5) **coordinate with other credible sources**; (6) **meet the needs of the media**; (7) **speak clearly and with compassion**. (Some versions list 8 with compassion separate from clarity — adopted here as the 8th rule for explicit auditability.)

**Rule 1 — Accept public as legitimate partner.** The dossier's "sovereignty" framing (14+ instances per wave-11 bioethics audit) is the *intent* of rule 1 — but sovereignty is a doctrinal posture, not a behavioural surface. Legitimate-partner *behaviour* is: (a) inviting reader-question + reader-experience contribution; (b) crediting reader-correction (wave-10 co-design surfaced this structurally); (c) treating reader-disagreement as data, not as ignorance. **Verdict:** dossier passes rule 1 at the *intent* layer, fails at the *behavioural-surface* layer; partial pass.

**Rule 2 — Plan carefully and evaluate efforts.** The §12 implementation timeline is a *clinical-protocol* time-axis (weeks 1-4 adaptation → 5-8 settling → 9-12 first real shift → months 4-6 consolidation → 6-12 long-term). It is NOT a *risk-communication* time-axis (how does the reader's understanding + trust + behaviour evolve over the 12-month engagement). The two axes are related but structurally different. **Verdict:** dossier fails rule 2 — no risk-communication plan over time + no risk-communication evaluation feedback loop.

**Rule 3 — Listen to the public's specific concerns.** Wave-10 service-design + wave-10 co-design surfaced this at the design-research layer; wave-9 audiology-research-nurse + wave-11 educational-psychologist surfaced this at the clinical-encounter layer. The *risk-communication listening loop* is structurally different: it asks what the reader *fears* (not what symptoms they have), what *information* they want (not what evidence we think they need), what *language* they use (not what clinical taxonomy we deploy). **Verdict:** dossier fails rule 3 — no risk-communication listening loop is structurally surfaced; wave-10 co-design is the closest adjacent and does not extend.

**Rule 4 — Be honest, frank, and open.** This is the dossier's strongest rule. Every Cochrane null is surfaced; every fabricated stat has been caught + corrected; the founder mandate explicitly demands honest framing over marketing. **Verdict:** dossier passes rule 4.

**Rule 5 — Coordinate with other credible sources.** Partial pass. The §16 authority list (WHO + EMA + AYUSH + TGA + NMPA + Health Canada + Men-Tsee-Khang + Hamdard + IMPCOPS + classical texts) is credible-source coordination. What is missing is the *user-facing* credibility framework: when the reader hears something contradictory from their GP/HNO/audiologist/family-elder, how do they triangulate? The dossier needs a meta-rule. **Verdict:** dossier partially passes rule 5; the credible-source coordination *intent* is there but the triangulation-guidance for the reader is not.

**Rule 6 — Meet the needs of the media.** Fails. There is no media-facing layer — no press-kit, no journalist Q&A, no scientific-spokesperson designation. For a 30 000-word evidence-OS that will inevitably attract media attention (especially when the cohort registry publishes per wave-5 academic-PI path), this is a structural gap. Also failed at the *family + carer + GP* communication layer — the reader needs a 1-page summary they can hand to their GP, spouse, or carer; none exists.

**Rule 7 — Speak clearly.** Strong pass at the prose-readability layer (wave-3 health-info-UX confirmed); strong pass at the plain-English-first-render layer (every section opens with For-you-in-plain-words then Quick-start then The-deeper-layer). Partial pass at the numeracy layer (see Dimension 3 — Gigerenzer + Spiegelhalter conventions not fully adopted).

**Rule 8 — Compassion.** Present in tone but not structurally embedded. The crisis-line block in §0 is the closest structural compassion-surface; the rest of the dossier is tone-compassionate but not protocol-compassionate. A "compassion at every escalation point" pattern is missing.

## 3. NUMERACY + UNCERTAINTY COMMUNICATION — GIGERENZER + SPIEGELHALTER + WYNNE

### 3.1 The technical-accuracy pass that wave-5 + wave-6 + §11.7 completed

Technical accuracy is now strong.

### 3.2 The communication-presentation gap

What none of waves 1-11 addressed is that **the Gigerenzer + Spiegelhalter + RSS conventions for *presenting* probabilistic information to lay readers are not adopted**. Specifically:

**(a) Natural-frequency framing not adopted.** Gigerenzer 2007 + 2014: a 30% placebo responder rate is more accurately communicated as "30 out of 100 readers who do nothing actively will still hit the clinically-meaningful threshold by week 12 from expectancy + ritual + time alone — that's 3 out of every 10". The percentage framing requires the reader to do a mental Bayesian conversion that ~70% of adults cannot reliably perform. Natural-frequency framing bypasses the conversion. The §11.7 honest-baseline pass uses percentages throughout; should be re-rendered in natural-frequency form for the reader-facing prose, with percentages retained in the citation + technical-tier columns.

**(b) Spiegelhalter ladder-of-uncertainty not adopted.** Spiegelhalter 2017 + Royal Statistical Society 2018: when a number carries uncertainty, communicate the uncertainty source (sampling vs measurement vs structural) + the uncertainty type (statistical vs deep) + the uncertainty direction (does the headline number over- or under-state the truth + which way). The §11.7 + §13.6 honest-baseline + qualitative-range passes do the first part (acknowledging uncertainty) but do not deploy the Spiegelhalter ladder-of-uncertainty conventions for *naming the uncertainty class*. Example: "the 70-80% Lenire completer responder rate at 12 weeks is statistical uncertainty (sampling + missing data) + structural uncertainty (no sham comparator + industry sponsorship) + measurement uncertainty (TFI MCID heterogeneity); the structural-uncertainty component is the largest." This is more informative than the current "no sham comparator + observational-grade GRADE starting point" framing.

**(c) Wynne expert-vs-lay-knowledge-asymmetry not surfaced.** Wynne 1991 + 1996 (Cumbrian-sheep-farmer post-Chernobyl studies; Lancaster CESAGen): expert-knowledge and lay-knowledge are not on a single hierarchy; lay-knowledge often carries situational + tacit + lived-experience content that expert-knowledge cannot replicate. For tinnitus specifically, the reader knows their own symptom variation, trigger patterns, distress-amplifiers, and sleep-impact in ways the dossier cannot. **The dossier needs a Wynne acknowledgement: "You know your tinnitus in ways we cannot. The dossier covers what is generalisable across populations; your lived-experience is the missing data we cannot supply. Triangulate."**

**(d) Bayesian reader-prior-belief mapping missing.** Spiegelhalter + Cambridge Winton Centre conventions: before presenting a probabilistic finding, name what the reader probably believes already + how this finding shifts that belief. For tinnitus the readers' prior beliefs cluster around three poles: (i) "tinnitus is nerve damage you have to live with" (mainstream-care-derived); (ii) "Ginkgo / Lipoflavonoid / Lenire is the breakthrough" (marketing-derived); (iii) "alternative medicine has the answer" (anti-mainstream-care derived). Each prior needs a different communication strategy. **The dossier needs a Bayesian-prior-belief map up front: "If you arrive believing X, this dossier will challenge that belief in ways A + B; if you arrive believing Y, this dossier will confirm-with-qualification C + D."**

**(e) The 30% placebo + 50-80% natural-history + 70-80% Lenire completer-responder sequencing.** These three numbers are individually-correct in the dossier but their *combined-reading* is not scaffolded. A reader who reads §11.7 (placebo) then §13.6 (natural history) then §15.2 (Lenire) walks away with three numbers that they cannot integrate. The integration the dossier needs to surface: **"For acute (≤4-week) tinnitus, ~5-8 out of 10 will spontaneously resolve by 6 months even with NO intervention (natural history). For chronic tinnitus, of every 10 readers who receive a credible placebo + expectancy + ritual for 12 weeks, ~3-5 will hit the clinically-meaningful threshold (placebo response in open-label conditions). For chronic tinnitus on Lenire specifically, ~7-8 out of every 10 completers hit the responder threshold at 12 weeks — but we don't know how many of those would have hit it on a credible sham anyway, because no sham trial exists. Honest read: the active-vs-sham gap is genuinely unknown; the observational signal is real but the confounding is real too."**

### 4.2 The Vaughan + WHO COMBI + AYUSH IEC + RKI playbook

**Vaughan + Tinker 2011** equity-of-access risk-communication framework (developed for chronic-disease populations in the US South + Sub-Saharan + South-Asian rural cohorts — cited here for methodological provenance, not as US-regulator authority): **the equity-of-access principle is that no reader should be excluded from life-saving information by their literacy, language, or technology access**. The framework names five communication-equity gates: (a) **language** — at minimum, top-10 reader-language translation; (b) **literacy** — at minimum, a Grade-6-reading-level version alongside the full text; (c) **modality** — at minimum, audio + video alongside text; (d) **channel** — at minimum, no-smartphone-required alternative; (e) **cultural-translation** — not just word-for-word but concept-by-concept (the §2 four-driver model is culturally novel even in OECD; in the AYUSH-vata or TCM-kidney-essence framework the four-driver model needs concept-translation, not word-translation).

**WHO COMBI (Communication for Behavioural Impact) 2012 + 2024** five-step framework: (1) assess situation + audience; (2) set behavioural objectives; (3) segment audience; (4) design strategy mix (mass + community + interpersonal + counselling); (5) plan implementation + monitoring. ### 5.2 The WHO Risk Communication in Emergencies (RCCE) standard

WHO Risk Communication and Community Engagement (RCCE) 2017 + 2024 named the canonical crisis-communication choreography: (a) **stop the action** — interrupt whatever the reader is doing in the protocol; (b) **name the crisis honestly** — without minimising or catastrophising; (c) **give one immediate action** — not a list, not options, one action; (d) **name what NOT to do** — explicit anti-actions to prevent harm; (e) **provide a re-entry pathway** — when + how to return to the protocol after stabilisation; (f) **leave no orphan-reader** — every reader who crosses the crisis threshold has a named landing-place. The dossier's current crisis-line block does (a) partially (the block is at the top so it interrupts before the protocol starts, not during); does (b) well (Lugo 2022 figure honestly framed as severe-sub-cohort-specific); does (c) partially (multiple crisis-line options without prioritisation); does (d) partially ("home OTC protocol can wait; your safety cannot" is one anti-action, but more are needed); does (e) poorly (no re-entry choreography); does (f) well (every reader sub-population — Deaf, LGBTQ+, adolescent — has a landing-place).

### 5.3 The crisis-communication choreography the dossier needs

**(a) Stop-the-action choreography for mid-protocol crisis-screening.** The dossier's §11 scoring instrument includes PHQ-9 + GAD-7 + Columbia C-SSRS (added per wave-4 trust-and-safety + wave-11 educational-psychologist). The §11.5 life-stage gate names PHQ-9 item-9 positive as a trigger. **The dossier needs an explicit stop-the-action choreography**: when PHQ-9 item-9 ≥1, the reader's UI experience must change — protocol-content greyed-out, crisis-line block surfaced, escalation choreography presented; not "return to crisis-line block above" prose, but an interrupt.

**(b) Prioritised one-immediate-action per crisis-class.** The current block lists 12+ crisis-line options. RCCE convention: **prioritise to one per crisis-class**. For severe-distress + suicidal-ideation + non-Deaf + adult: UK → Samaritans 116 123 first; Ireland → Samaritans 116 123 first; AU → Lifeline 13 11 14 first; IN → iCall 9152987821 first; DE → Telefonseelsorge 0800 111 0 111 first. For severe-distress + suicidal-ideation + Deaf + adult: SignHealth UK first (UK); National Deaf Therapy (US); etc. For 16-24 + suicidal-ideation: UK Papyrus HOPELINE first; AU Kids Helpline; IN iCall + NIMHANS; DE Nummer gegen Kummer. The reader needs the *one* line to call, not a menu to navigate while in crisis.

**(c) Named anti-actions.** Currently the block names "home OTC protocol can wait; your safety cannot" — strong. Add: **"do NOT take additional supplements while in crisis; do NOT stop any prescribed medication without prescriber involvement (especially psychiatric medication); do NOT make irreversible decisions about your tinnitus protocol during a crisis episode; do NOT use sound enrichment at volumes higher than baseline (some sub-types react paradoxically to volume increase during acute distress); do NOT engage with the dossier's discussion of suicidality figures (Lugo 2022 §0 + §11.7) while in crisis — that information is for stable readers planning their own response, not for crisis-readers."**

**(d) Re-entry pathway.** Currently the dossier does not name when + how to return to the protocol after stabilisation. **RCCE convention**: (i) when crisis-line contact has been made; (ii) when 24 hours have passed since the crisis-event peak; (iii) when a credentialed clinician has been engaged (CAMHS / Adult-MHS / Headspace / NIMHANS / Kinder- und Jugendpsychiatrie / VA Mental Health / DVA Open Arms); (iv) when the reader is in a safe physical + social environment; (v) when the reader has explicitly chosen to re-engage with the dossier (not by default).
