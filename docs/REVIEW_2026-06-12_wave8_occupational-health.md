## 1. INDUSTRIAL NOISE DOSE-RESPONSE — THE SIX-INDUSTRY OCCUPATIONAL PHENOTYPE THE DOSSIER COLLAPSES INTO ONE ROW

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§2.6 row 7: *"Brand-new, after loud noise (≤72h) — Concert/firearm/workplace."* Wave-7 added row 8 for blast/IED/deployment. §5.16 covers hearing-protection generically (concerts, power-tools, gun-ranges, motorcycles). §6.6 cites EU 2003/10/EC at 85 dB threshold + lists ear-toxic chemicals (toluene, xylene, styrene, n-hexane, lead, mercury, manganese, CO) generically. **These under-specify the industrial-noise dose-response curves that determine compensability, the ototoxic co-exposures that compound noise injury, and the industry-specific phenotype an OH physician adjudicates.**

### 1.2 Six occupational sub-cohorts with distinct dose-response curves

The wave-7 deployed-veteran cohort is dominated by acute high-peak overpressure. The civilian occupational cohort is dominated by **cumulative + episodic + chronic** exposure with industry-specific dose-curves + co-exposure overlays:

- **Construction (UK CITB ~2.2M; EU FIEC ~12M; IN CIDC ~52M).** LAeq,8h 85-95 dB(A) + peaks 105-130 dB(C) on demolition, pneumatic-hammer, masonry-cutting. Co-exposures: silica dust, cement alkalinity, HAVS vibration. CNEL ≥100 dB(A)-years drives compensable loss + tinnitus in 35-50% (Themann 2019; HSE RR720 2009).
- **Manufacturing — metal-fabrication / textiles / glass / paper (UK ONS ~2.6M; EU ~30M; IN MoSPI ~31M).** Steady-state 85-105 dB(A) over 8-12h shifts. Co-exposures: ototoxic-synergist solvents — toluene, xylene, styrene (ACGIH BEI 2024); n-hexane (neuropathy + cochlear). Tinnitus prevalence 24-38% in 10-year cohorts (Lie 2016 Norway n=10,349; Kim 2017 KOSHA; ICMR-NIOH 2019).
- **Oil & gas / petrochemical (UK NSTA ~154,000; EU ~600,000; IN MoPNG ~6M; Gulf ~1.5M).** Drilling 100-118 dB(A) with high-impulse drill-string + turbine/compressor 95-110 sustained. Co-exposures: BTEX solvents, H2S. The 14-on/14-off rotation creates a cyclical dose-recovery curve audiometric surveillance must time-match. Prevalence 28-44% in 15-year offshore cohorts (NORA NIOSH 2018; Norwegian PSA 2020).
- **Agriculture (UK NFU ~470,000 farms; EU CAP ~9M; IN MoA&FW ~146M).** Tractor 92-105; chainsaw 105-115; combine 95-100; livestock 95-105. Co-exposures: OP + carbamate pesticides (cholinesterase + cochlear stress), grain dust. Prevalence 35-55% in long-tenure cohorts (Crawford 2008 NIOSH; Sjöström 2018; ICMR-NIOH 2020 Punjab pesticide-noise interaction).
- **Music / live-sound / hospitality (UK MU ~30k + 250k venue staff; EU ~1.5M; global ~6M).** Sustained 100-110 dB(A) over 2-4h performance + rehearsal compound. Prevalence 40-60% by 10-year career mark (Schmuziger 2006; Halevi-Katz 2015; Phillips 2010). Hospitality sub-cohort (bar / venue / hostel-night-staff chronic 85-95 dB(A) ambient + impulse) is the most under-recognised OECD occupational-tinnitus cohort, typically un-enrolled in HCPs despite exposure above EU LEX,8h thresholds.
- **Mining + quarrying + tunnel-construction (UK BGS ~30k; EU EUROMINES ~400k; IN DGMS ~4M).** Underground drilling 100-115; shovel + dragline 95-105; conveyor + ventilation 90-95 sustained. Co-exposures: silica (cochlear oxidative-stress amplifier), diesel particulate, blasting overpressure (mechanistic overlap with wave-7 blast phenotype on blasting-days with chronic-recovery-impaired baseline). Prevalence 38-58% in 15-year underground cohorts (DGMS India 2021; Sharaf 2018; Kowalska 2017).

**The single §2.6 row 7 framing collapses six mechanistically + statutorily + co-exposure-distinct cohorts into one bucket.** Downstream implication: a construction worker reading the dossier with new tinnitus has no surface guidance that (a) their exposure is within the IIDB A10 prescribed-disease window, (b) their silica + cement co-exposure is mechanistically additive, (c) their CNEL should drive the workers'-comp claim timing, (d) EU 2003/10/EC's 80 / 85 / 87 dB(A) cascade defines a graduated employer-obligation their employer is statutorily bound to.

### 1.3 Ototoxic chemical co-exposures — industry-stratified

§6.6 lists *"toluene, xylene, styrene, n-hexane, lead, mercury, manganese, carbon monoxide"* generically. The dose-impactful industry-stratification: toluene + styrene dominate plastics + composite-fabrication (boat-building, wind-turbine-blade); xylene + ethylbenzene dominate paint + coating; n-hexane dominates shoe + leather + adhesive (rubber-glue tinnitus + neuropathy cluster well-documented in Indonesia + Vietnam + China shoe-factory cohorts); lead in battery + radiator-repair + indoor-shooting-range staff; mercury in chlor-alkali + dental-amalgam; manganese in welding fume; carbon monoxide in foundry + confined-space + furnace. The ACGIH 2024 TLV introduces **noise + ototoxic-solvent combined exposure adjustment factors** (toluene halves the safe LAeq,8h threshold from 85 to 80 dB(A) in styrene-co-exposed cohorts). The dossier's "fix the environment before scaling supplements" framing is correct in direction; the industry-stratified mechanism specifies the fix.

## 2. WORKPLACE-ACCOMMODATION FRAMEWORK — UK EQUALITY ACT + EU DIRECTIVE 2000/78/EC + AUSTRALIAN DDA + INDIAN RPWD ACT 2016

### 2.1 What the dossier currently ships

Searching for "accommodation", "reasonable adjustment", "Equality Act", "employer", "interactive process", "RPWD", "disability discrimination" returns **zero hits**. Wave-7 §10.7.6 adds Veterans-UK / VA / DVA on-ramps but does not extend to civilian employer-obligation. The §11 framing assumes the user is employed-with-no-friction or unemployed-with-flexibility. **For the worker whose tinnitus distress impairs concentration in a cognitive role, or whose audiometric loss exceeds a role-specific safety threshold (HGV, heavy machinery, aviation, marine pilotage, safety-critical communications), the workplace-accommodation framework is the single largest determinant of job retention.**

### 2.2 The four workplace-accommodation regimes

- **UK Equality Act 2010.** Tinnitus + hearing loss with substantial + long-term adverse effect = "disability" under §6 + Schedule 1. Triggers employer §20 duty to make "reasonable adjustments" (three-limb test: PCP; physical feature; auxiliary aid). ET / EAT-adjudicated examples: quiet-zone workstation, noise-cancelling headset, time-of-day shift adjustment, redeployment from noise-zone to office, modified-duty during tinnitus flares, written-priority over phone for severe loss. Access to Work (DWP) funds assistive tech (hearing-loop, FM, captioning).
- **EU Council Directive 2000/78/EC + national transpositions.** Framework Directive across 27 member states; tinnitus + hearing-loss disability triggers "reasonable accommodation" duty (UN CRPD-aligned). Transpositions: Germany AGG 2006; France Code du travail L1132-1 + L5213; Italy Legge 68/1999; Spain LISMI + RD Leg 1/2013; Sweden Diskrimineringslagen 2008:567; Netherlands Wet gelijke behandeling.
- **Australia DDA 1992 + Fair Work Act 2009 §351 + state Anti-Discrimination Acts.** DDA "disability" + employer duty unless "unjustifiable hardship". Fair Work Commission general-protections is the parallel + often-stronger route. JobAccess funds assistive tech.
- **India RPWD Act 2016.** §3 non-discrimination + §20 employment equality. Hearing impairment named in "specified disabilities" schedule; tinnitus + loss at ≥40% disability certification (District Medical Board, audiometric + functional + ENT) accesses statutory reservations + accommodation. ALIMCO hearing-aid funding for BPL workers; Group C + D public-sector reservation.

### 2.3 Coverage doctrine

Treat Equality Act 2010 + Directive 2000/78/EC + DDA 1992 + RPWD 2016 as **statutory pathway anchors, not scientific-evidence anchors** — same pattern wave-7 applied to Veterans-UK + DVA + AFMS, same pattern the dossier already applies to AYUSH + CCRAS + AIIMS + EMA HMPC + NMPA + Hamdard. US ADA Title I + Title V is named for comparative international scope only (multinational HR overlap) — pathway reference, not authority anchor — mirroring the dossier's existing line 794 *"FDA OTC hearing-aid clearance 2022 — regulatory-path context"*.

### 2.4 The accommodation pathway the worker needs

(1) Self-assessment of substantial + long-term adverse effect (TFI ≥38 sustained ≥12 months + day-to-day impact); (2) disclosure decision-tree — OH service (medical-in-confidence) vs line management (operational adjustment) under GDPR + DPA 2018; (3) interactive-process written request (quiet workstation + noise-cancelling headset + redeployment + flexible start + written-priority over phone + protected MBCT-T break + assistive-listening device) — the written request creates the audit trail; (4) OH referral pathway (UK employer OH or H&WS or GP; EU national; AU GP → certified OH → Fit-for-Work; IN ESIC OH Cell or AIIMS COEH); (5) statutory time-bars (UK ET 3 months minus one day; IIDB no bar but back-dating affected; AU Fair Work Commission general-protections 21 days; IN RPWD §20 via Disability Commissioner).

## 3. WORKERS'-COMPENSATION PATHWAY — UK IIDB A10 + EU NATIONAL TABLEAUX + AU SAFEWORK + IN ESIC + CA WSIB

For a worker whose tinnitus is mechanistically + temporally linked to industrial noise + chemical co-exposure, **the workers'-comp system is the financial-access lever — same role the veteran-system played in wave-7**.

### 3.2 The five major workers'-compensation regimes

- **UK IIDB — Prescribed Disease A10 Occupational Deafness.** Tinnitus recognised as part of A10 when paired with SNHL ≥50 dB averaged 1, 2, 3 kHz in each ear (worse ear ≥50 dB), with ≥10-year exposure in a Schedule 1 Part I listed occupation (boilermaker, mining, weaving, drop-forge, etc.). DWP medical adjudicator assesses %; weekly benefit £40-£200+. Adjacent: Veterans-UK AFCS for service cases (wave-7); employer's liability civil claim via Industrial Disease solicitor. **Time bar:** Limitation Act 1980 3-year runs from date of knowledge — late-presenting tinnitus from 30-year-old shipyard exposure remains actionable.
- **EU national tableaux.** **Germany — BK 2301 Lärmschwerhörigkeit** via sector Berufsgenossenschaft (BG BAU / BG ETEM / BGN), MdE % tier. **France — Tableau 42 Atteinte auditive provoquée par les bruits lésionnels** via CPAM / CARSAT, rente d'incapacité permanente. **Italy — INAIL Tabella voce 50 ipoacusia da rumore.** **Spain — RD 1299/2006 grupo 2 agente 1 hipoacusia provocada por ruido** via Mutua + INSS. **Netherlands — WIA disability-tier via UWV** (no schedule).
- **Australia — Safe Work Australia + state WC Acts (NSW / VIC / QLD / WA / SA / TAS WorkCover, SA RTWSA) + Comcare (federal).** Industrial-deafness schedule by state; permanent-impairment lump-sum + weekly payments. National WC Statistics 2021-22 confirm industrial deafness consistently top-5 by frequency.
- **India — ESIC §46 Disablement Benefit under ESI Act 1948** for insured persons ≤₹21,000/month, Schedule III Part B disease 28-32 (noise + chemical + radiation). Above the ceiling: **Employees' Compensation Act 1923 (amended 2017)** Schedule III via labour commissioner. PPD % via ESIC medical board. Unorganised-sector workers (~93% of labour force per NSSO) typically uncovered — DGFASLI surveys document chronic under-claim. The Indian financial-access gap is the most acute in this dimension.
- **Canada — provincial WCB (WSIB Ontario / WorkSafeBC / CNESST Québec / WCB AB+SK+MB+Maritimes).** NIHL + tinnitus claim policies; hearing-aid coverage; vocational rehab.

## 4. HEARING-CONSERVATION PROGRAMME + EAR-PROTECTION PROGRAMME — THE SOURCE-CONTROL LAYER THE DOSSIER UNDER-COVERS

### 4.1 What the dossier currently ships

§5.16 covers personal-purchase + custom-mould protection (wave-7-expanded with calibrated tactical landscape). §6.6 cites EU 2003/10/EC and the 85 dB threshold. **Correct for personal-recreational + veteran calibrated-protection. Does not describe the employer's HCP architecture that, per statute across UK + EU + AU + US + IN + CA, sits upstream of personal protection as the source-control layer.**

### 4.2 The hearing-conservation programme architecture across regimes

- **UK Control of Noise at Work Regulations 2005 + HSE L108 ACoP.** Three-tier action-level cascade: lower (LEAV) 80 dB(A) LEX,8h + 135 dB(C) peak; upper (UEAV) 85 + 137; limit (ELV) 87 + 140 (accounting for protection attenuation). Employer duties graduated by tier: risk assessment + information (LEAV); protection available + health surveillance + zone-signage (UEAV); engineering source-control mandatory (ELV-exceed risk). Baseline + annual audiometry by OH provider; HSE 2005 audiogram categories 1-4 drive referral.
- **EU Directive 2003/10/EC (Physical Agents — Noise) + national transpositions.** Mirror 80 / 85 / 87 dB(A) cascade. Germany TRLV Lärm; France Code du travail R4431; Italy D.Lgs 81/2008 Titolo VIII Capo II; Spain RD 286/2006; Sweden AFS 2005:16.
- **Australia AS/NZS 1269 + Model WHS Regulations Chapter 4 Part 4.1.** Standard LAeq,8h 85 dB(A) + peak 140 dB(C); state-specific audiometric testing; HCP elements: assessment + noise-survey + control + protection + training + records.
- **US OSHA 29 CFR 1910.95 + 1926.52 + MSHA 30 CFR Part 62.** 85 dB(A) action level; 90 dB(A) PEL. **Doctrine note:** OSHA / MSHA are US regulators — cite as pathway reference only for US-jurisdiction workers (mirrors the existing dossier line on FDA OTC hearing-aid clearance "regulatory-path context, not evidence anchor"), never as scientific authority.
- **India Factories Act 1948 §89 + Schedule II; Mines Act 1952 + Mines Rules 1955.** Permissible 90 dB(A) LAeq,8h (DGFASLI 2022 consultation drafting toward 85). Pre-employment + periodic audiometry; engineering controls + signage + protection. **Honest framing for Indian readers:** DGFASLI surveys 2020-22 document <20% HCP compliance in non-public-sector industry — a real coverage gap.

### 4.3 The HCP entry-pathway the worker needs

(1) **Establish that workplace exposure exceeds the lower action value** (80 dB(A) UK + EU; 85 AU + US; 90 → 85 IN). Free smartphone SLM apps (NIOSH SLM iOS validated ±2 dB; OpenNoise; Decibel X) for first-pass; calibrated Type-2 SLM via OH service or HSE noise-survey for audit-grade. Apps cannot measure C-peak impulse reliably. (2) **Enrol in employer HCP** (UK employer OH or HSE self-referral; EU national; AU state; IN ESIC OH cell or DGFASLI regional). (3) **Baseline + annual audiometry** (UK HSE Approved Hearing Tester; EU country; AU AS/NZS 1269-compliant via AudA; IN ESIC-empanelled or AIIMS COEH). (4) **HSE audiogram categorisation triggers downstream pathway** — Cat 1 routine; Cat 2 confidential worker warning + accommodation discussion; Cat 3 ENT + OP referral + formal accommodation; Cat 4 immediate engineering / admin control + accommodation + IIDB awareness.
