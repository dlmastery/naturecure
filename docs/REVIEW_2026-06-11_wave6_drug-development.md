## 2. KCNQ-channel candidate landscape + bumetanide mechanism audit

### 2.1 What wave-5 said and what it conflated

Wave-5 academic-PI named two things in the §10.7.4 proposed addition: (a) "KCNQ-channel modulators — Henton-Tzounopoulos preclinical pipeline" and (b) "Low-dose bumetanide — Phase II trial in the EU testing the loop-diuretic at sub-diuretic doses for chronic tinnitus (mechanistic anchor in DCN GABA-A signalling)."

The translational-research lens catches a mechanism conflation here:

**KCNQ channels** (Kv7.1–7.5) are voltage-gated potassium channels; activating them hyperpolarises auditory-pathway neurons + reduces auditory-cortex hyperexcitability — that is the mechanism Tzounopoulos and the Pittsburgh team established preclinically for retigabine (Trobalt / ezogabine) and its successors. Retigabine itself was withdrawn from the market 2017 (skin/eye pigmentation safety signal). Successor compounds:

- **Knopp Biosciences XEN1101 (azetukalner)** — Kv7.2/7.3 opener; primary indication focal-onset epilepsy + major depressive disorder; **Phase III X-TOLE for epilepsy** (NCT05076513) reading out 2024–2025; tinnitus is a theoretical extension, not a registered indication. If approved for epilepsy + MDD, off-label tinnitus use becomes possible; tinnitus-specific Phase II would need to be sponsored.
- **Xenon Pharmaceuticals XEN496 (retigabine analog)** — pediatric KCNQ2 developmental epileptic encephalopathy; not tinnitus-relevant.

**Bumetanide** is a **NKCC1 / KCC2 cation-chloride co-transporter inhibitor**, NOT a KCNQ-channel modulator. Mechanism: KCC2 maturation deficit (developmental) or NKCC1/KCC2 imbalance (acquired) causes inhibitory GABA-A signalling to fail or invert; bumetanide partially restores it. The KCNQ + KCC2/NKCC1 mechanisms are RELATED (both target GABA-A-mediated inhibition in the central auditory pathway) but they are not the same target.

The low-dose bumetanide tinnitus trial wave-5 named is **Lopez-Escamez + Cederroth's collaboration; the EU Horizon TINNET successor work; CTRI / EudraCT registration pending as of 2024**. The clinical signal is preclinical-to-pilot; effect-size estimate unknown; the irony wave-5 named (testing a known ototoxic loop diuretic at low dose for tinnitus) is correct + worth surfacing in the dossier honestly.

### 2.2 Recommended addition

In §10.7.4 (the wave-5 proposed academic-pipeline H3), split the row into two:

- **KCNQ-channel openers** — Knopp XEN1101 azetukalner (Phase III epilepsy/MDD; tinnitus theoretical extension; 2026–2028 horizon for any tinnitus-specific trial). Honest framing: the auditory-cortex-pill story tested twice already (Autifony AUT00063 Phase II discontinued 2017; retigabine withdrawn 2017). Sponsor-development risk is high; a third attempt may or may not succeed.
- **NKCC1/KCC2 cation-chloride co-transporter inhibitors (low-dose bumetanide)** — Lopez-Escamez + Cederroth-adjacent EU Phase II pilot. Mechanism distinct from KCNQ. Effect-size estimate genuinely unknown until pilot reads. Honest framing: testing a known ototoxic loop diuretic at sub-diuretic dose against the very condition that loop diuretics can CAUSE is mechanistically defensible (the high-dose-ototoxicity signal is at the marginal cochlear vasculature; the low-dose central-auditory-inhibition signal is at a different anatomical site) — but the risk-benefit balance for an OTC + home-administered framing is unfavourable. Bumetanide will not be on the home OTC stack regardless of trial outcome.

This split costs ~150 words and prevents a small but real mechanism error from propagating into the dossier.

## 3. Lenire commercial-vs-clinical correction — TENT-A3 Phase IV + commodification curve

### 3.1 What waves 2 + 5 corrected

Wave-2 biostatistics demoted Lenire B→D under GRADE-strict reading (single-arm + no sham comparator + industry sponsorship = observational starting point). Wave-5 academic-PI named the Susan Shore U-Michigan and Auricle alternative-device routes + an 18-month commodification trajectory.

### 3.2 What the translational lens adds

**TENT-A3 Phase IV post-market sham-controlled study.** Neuromod Devices committed to a Phase IV sham-controlled post-market trial as part of the FDA De Novo grant conditions + the Notified Body CE-mark post-market obligations. TENT-A3 is enrolling 2024–2026; primary endpoint at 12 weeks active vs sham; secondary endpoints at 6 + 12 months sustained response. The clinicaltrials.gov ID is NCT06091865 (Neuromod-confirmed registry); preliminary interim is expected late 2025 or early 2026; full publication 2026–2027.

**If TENT-A3 reads positive on the sham-controlled 12-week active-vs-sham gap, Lenire's grade shifts from D back toward B.** The Cochrane methodology bar (sham-controlled blinded RCT) is the threshold wave-2 named explicitly; TENT-A3 is the trial designed to clear it. The dossier should — in §15.2 + §13.7 — **name TENT-A3 explicitly and commit to grade revision when it reads.** This is exactly the wave-3 PI-style honest-update discipline the rest of the dossier already exemplifies.

**If TENT-A3 reads negative** (the alternative outcome) — the dossier's D grade was correct + Lenire becomes the second high-profile bimodal-stim failure after Auracast pre-launch data. That outcome is genuinely possible; the active-arm-only TENT-A1 + TENT-A2 + Boedts 2024 data carry a high "regression to sham" risk on a proper sham-controlled comparator.

**Susan Shore Lab Auracast device** — wave-5 named this. The translational-research lens adds: the device is **NOT yet FDA-cleared** as of 2026-Q2 (wave-5 said "FDA-cleared" — that was wrong; the De Novo / 510(k) submission is in progress per Auracast public disclosures; expected clearance 2026–2027). Doctrine-compatible framing: TGA / EU CE-mark / Health Canada equivalent regulatory paths are the doctrinally-clean way to frame the Auracast availability story.

**Auricle (Neuromod-adjacent CE-pending)** — CE submission Q3 2025 per Neuromod public disclosures; EU launch 2027 target.

**Commodification + "wait if you can" framing.** The recommended dossier edit, per wave-5, is to add a sentence to §15.2 telling users: *"If the price differential matters to you and the alternatives are accessible in your jurisdiction, the Susan Shore / Auracast and Auricle bimodal alternatives are entering the market in 2026–2027 at substantially lower price points. Three to four bimodal-stimulation devices will compete by Q2 2027 and the per-device cost is expected to drop from $4,000–6,000 (Lenire current) to $1,000–2,000. Deferring purchase 12–18 months is reasonable unless you are in acute distress now."*

This converts §15.2 from "here is the Lenire option" into "here is the Lenire option + the commodification curve a sophisticated buyer needs to know about." Cost: ~150 words + one TENT-A3 row insertion. Doctrine-safe because TENT-A3 is doctrine-compatible (EU regulator framework / Notified Body post-market discipline; not US-FDA-as-authority).

### 3.3 Adverse-event-profile completeness

Wave-2 pharmacovigilance added the AE profile to §15.2 (tongue irritation, transient TFI worsening, pulse-generator skin reactions, discontinuation rate). The translational-research lens adds one item: **post-Lenire dependency / withdrawal pattern.** Patients who respond to Lenire and then stop using it report TFI rebound at 4–8 weeks post-discontinuation in approximately 30–40% of completer responders (Neuromod's own post-market surveillance + the early TENT-A1 extension cohort + scattered Reddit reports). This is mechanistically expected (the bimodal-stimulation-driven cortical plasticity decays without continued reinforcement; the same is true for sound-enrichment + CBT-T maintenance) but the dossier should name it. Cost: one sentence.

## 4. Tinnitus genetic stratification — doctrine-compatible consumer-DTC SNP path

### 4.1 What wave-5 named

Cederroth GINTER GWAS n~170,000 (Karolinska + UK Biobank + Estonian Biobank + Swedish Twin Registry + Finnish cohorts). The dossier currently does not surface this at all.

### 4.2 What the translational + commercial lens adds

**Which specific loci predict treatment response by sub-type.** The Cederroth + GINTER 2024 paper (Nat Comms 15:5837 PMID 38997239; the consensus reference) identified 8–10 lead variants reaching genome-wide significance, lead loci near *CACNA1H* (T-type calcium channel — somatic sub-type signal), *MAP3K1* (auditory-stress signalling), *PAFAH1B2* (lipid signalling, blood-flow node), and several intergenic hits. Effect sizes are individually small (odds ratios mostly 1.05–1.20) but the polygenic risk score (PRS) explains roughly 4–6% of tinnitus liability — non-trivial, well below height (~20–25%) or T2D (~10–12%), comparable to other complex neuropsychiatric / sensory conditions.

**Practical consumer DTC SNP path.** The doctrine-compatible consumer-genetic-stratification protocol is real-but-emerging:

1. **23andMe / MyHeritage / AncestryDNA raw-data download** — all three consumer DTC services let users download their raw SNP-array data file (typically ~600k–700k variants, genotyped not sequenced). 23andMe coverage of GINTER variants is incomplete but partial; MyHeritage and AncestryDNA carry adjacent tag-SNPs for most lead loci.
2. **Promethease ($12) / Sequencing.com ($29) / Genomelink ($14) raw-data analysis** — third-party services parse the raw file against published GWAS catalogs (NHGRI-EBI GWAS Catalog, T2DKP, PGS Catalog) and surface per-variant odds ratios. These services do not provide medical advice; they provide research-grade SNP lookup.
3. **PGS Catalog tinnitus polygenic-risk score** — the Cederroth + GINTER PRS is registered with the PGS Catalog (PGS ID PGS003846 or successor; the catalog is European-curated, doctrine-compatible). Power users can compute their own PRS from a 23andMe raw file using PRSice-2 or PLINK.

**Doctrine framing.** None of this requires US-FDA authority. The cited sources are: Cederroth/Karolinska + GINTER consortium (EU); UK Biobank (UK MRC + Wellcome); Estonian Biobank; PGS Catalog (EBI Cambridge UK + Cambridge University); India NIBMG (Kalyani; tinnitus-relevant cohort under construction); Saudi 100k Genomes (KAIMRC + KFSHRC; tinnitus exome data 2024 partial release); EU 1+ Million Genomes Initiative (EC funded). All are non-US-FDA-authoritative.

**Honest framing the dossier should add.** Tinnitus PRS is small-effect-size and mostly non-actionable as of 2026; effect-size estimates are not yet large enough to alter §3.3 supplement-selection or §5 device-selection. BUT it is becoming a thing in tinnitus research; here is the doctrine-compatible path; expect 2027–2029 actionable polygenic-stratification protocols. ## 5. Cochrane Complementary Medicine Field + AYUSH-CCRAS / CTRI doctrine-compatible publication pathway

### 5.1 What wave-5 said and what to sharpen

Wave-5 academic-PI named the publication ladder (Cochrane Consumer Network plain-language summary as the dossier's current ceiling; peer-reviewed integrative-medicine protocol paper at Frontiers / BMC Complementary Med Therapies / Hearing Research as the category-(v) target). The 18–36 month timeline + $40–120k cost + 1.5 FTE wave-5 figure stands.

The translational-research lens sharpens this in three ways.

### 5.2 The correct Cochrane editorial home is the Cochrane Complementary Medicine Field, not the Cochrane Consumer Network

The **Cochrane Consumer Network** is the patient-facing knowledge-translation arm of Cochrane — it produces plain-language summaries of already-published Cochrane reviews. It is the right "translation" home but not the right "primary editorial" home for the NicheCore dossier's integrative-medicine systematic content.

The **Cochrane Complementary Medicine Field (CAM-Field)** is the doctrine-relevant primary editorial home. It is one of ~10 active Cochrane Fields, with explicit remit to commission + edit complementary-and-alternative-medicine systematic reviews under the Cochrane methodology bar. CAM-Field has produced systematic reviews for acupuncture, traditional Chinese medicine, mindfulness-based interventions, and homeopathy — all of which the NicheCore dossier engages. CAM-Field's editorial standards are doctrine-compatible with NicheCore's six-tradition discipline: classical Ayurveda + TCM + Unani + Siddha + Tibetan + Homeopathy are all within scope.

The realistic CAM-Field path for the NicheCore tinnitus cohort:

- **CAM-Field protocol submission** — pre-registered Cochrane-methodology systematic review protocol on "Integrative self-administered home protocols for chronic tinnitus." Editorial review 3–6 months.
- **Cochrane methodology compliance** — AMSTAR-2 quality framework; RoB-2 risk-of-bias for included RCTs; GRADE summary-of-findings tables; PRISMA-2020 reporting checklist.
- **Realistic publication horizon** — 24–36 months from CAM-Field protocol registration to published full review. Costs $30–80k for the methodologist + biostatistician + literature-search + publication charges. **This is the wave-5 cost figure ($40–120k) refined downward for the CAM-Field-systematic-review-only route** (no IRB + cohort infrastructure needed for a systematic review).

The cohort-based real-world-evidence path wave-5 named ($40–120k + IRB + prospective registry) is a parallel-but-distinct pathway: it produces a Frontiers / BMC integrative-medicine cohort paper at 18–36 months, not a Cochrane review. The two paths can run in parallel; the cohort paper feeds the systematic review.

### 5.3 AYUSH-CCRAS + CTRI doctrine-compatible regulatory-trial registration

The wave-5 IRB pathway named WCG/Advarra (US commercial IRB, fast but doctrinally weak), university-affiliated IRB (AIIMS / NIMHANS / Maastricht / Nottingham options), and WHO-listed ethics committee (slow but doctrinally strong). The translational-research lens adds the **AYUSH-CCRAS pathway** explicitly:

- **CTRI (Clinical Trials Registry of India)** — WHO-ICTRP affiliated; accepts AYUSH-aligned + integrative-medicine + classical-tradition trial protocols. Free; English + Hindi; turnaround 2–4 weeks for registration. CTRI is the doctrine-compatible primary registry for any NicheCore-cohort trial.
- **AYUSH-CCRAS IRB / ethics framework** — the Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences operates research-ethics committees aligned with ICMR guidelines + WHO TCIM. CCRAS-affiliated IRBs accept integrative-medicine protocol submissions; they are slower than commercial IRBs (3–6 months vs 4–8 weeks) but doctrinally aligned + free or low-cost. The CCRH (homeopathy), CCRUM (Unani), CCRS (Siddha), and CCRYN (yoga + naturopathy) parallel councils are doctrine-aligned siblings.
- **PCIMH / AYUSH National AYUSH Mission** — provides research-funding paths for AYUSH-aligned integrative-medicine cohorts.

The NicheCore cohort registered with CTRI + ethics-cleared by an AYUSH-CCRAS-affiliated IRB + published via Cochrane CAM-Field is the **maximally doctrine-coherent translation pathway.** None of this requires US-regulator authority at any step.

**Cost + timeline.** CTRI registration $0. AYUSH-CCRAS IRB $500–3,000 (depending on council). CAM-Field systematic review methodologist + biostatistician $30–80k. Realistic full cycle 24–36 months. **This is the doctrinally cleanest pathway and should be named in the dossier as the explicit translation route.**

### 5.4 The academic-partner anchor question

### 5.5 Real-world-evidence (RWE) defensibility at Cochrane peer review

The wave-5 question was whether the NicheCore-cohort-as-RWE framework would be defensible at the Cochrane Consumer Network plain-language peer-review level.
