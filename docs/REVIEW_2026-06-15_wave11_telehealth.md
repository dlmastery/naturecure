## 1. ASYNCHRONOUS-VS-SYNCHRONOUS TINNITUS ASSESSMENT VIA TELEHEALTH

### 1.1 The async/sync decision-tree the dossier needs to surface

Greenhalgh 2018 + 2022 named the canonical video-consultation framework: not every encounter belongs synchronous; not every encounter belongs in-person. For tinnitus specifically, the post-2024 evidence base (Swanepoel 2019 boothless audiometry; van Wyk 2019 hearTest South-African validation; KUDUwave consent-to-NHS-OPD pilot 2022-23; Hearing Australia Connect tele-audiology 2023-24; ADHA-funded remote-fit OTC hearing aid pilot 2024; ASHA / RNID / BSA / Hearing Australia / AIIA Delhi remote-audiology position statements 2023-25) supports **three-way triage** of any new tinnitus assessment ask:

- **Async-safe (store-and-forward).** Subset suitable for asynchronous assessment with reader-collected data uploaded for clinician review at off-encounter time. Examples: stable chronic bilateral non-pulsatile tinnitus with no §0 red-flag in the last 90 days; first-line audiogram via Swanepoel hearTest (free Android, validated against gold-standard in quiet domestic environment); first-line tinnitus pitch/loudness match via TinnitusPlay or Audiogram (reader-self-administered Tyler 2007 protocol); first-line THI + TFI + GAD-7 + PHQ-9 + PSQI + COMIT'ID 5-item via reader-self-administered web-form. Async-clinician review within 5 working days. Channel: store-and-forward via NicheCore + email + ABDM Locker (IN) + NHS App secure-message (UK) + My Health Record (AU) + ELGA (AT) + ePA (DE — from 2026 opt-out rollout).
- **Sync-required (video-encounter).** Subset that requires synchronous clinician encounter. Examples: new asymmetric or unilateral tinnitus persisting >6 weeks (vestibular-schwannoma MRI-trigger per §0 red-flag — sync ENT/audiology video-encounter mandated); somatic-modulable tinnitus where the clinician needs to observe the modulation manoeuvre live (Levine 1999; §2.5); reader-distress + GAD-7/PHQ-9 ≥15 (wave-4 trust-and-safety); medication-review for §10.1 polypharmacy + STOPP/START (wave-9 geriatrician); first hearing-aid fit where remote-fit cannot validate the soft-fit + fine-tune (Hearing Australia Connect data 2024 indicates ~70% of first-fit can be remote but ~30% require physical visit for ear-impression or REM-verification); CI-user device-mode check (§0 red-flag CI block).
- **Escalate-to-physical (in-person mandatory within named timeframe).** Subset where remote is unsafe. Examples: pulsatile tinnitus + new (§0 red-flag — MRI brain + MRA + MRV first-line, in-person imaging mandatory); SSHL ≤72-hour window (§0 red-flag — same-day GP/HNO/ENT in-person physical exam + audiogram + steroid prescription, per the per-jurisdiction routing already in §0); neurological deficit + tinnitus (cranial-nerve exam in-person); suspected cholesteatoma or middle-ear pathology requiring otoscopy with magnification beyond what a phone-camera otoscope can deliver; vestibular schwannoma confirmed-imaging → ENT in-person consult.

### 1.2 Remote-audiometry test-validity discipline

The reader-self-administered audiogram via Swanepoel hearTest (Android-only, validated against gold-standard Pure-Tone Audiometry within 5 dB at 0.25-8 kHz in a quiet domestic environment with calibrated headphones — Sandstrom 2016 + van Wyk 2019) is **not** equivalent to a clinic-PTA when the high-frequency range (8-16 kHz) is clinically relevant, when the ambient noise floor exceeds 35 dB SPL, or when calibration of headphones is uncertain (most consumer headphones drift up to 10 dB at the extremes). The dossier should name this **explicitly** so the reader does not over-interpret a hearTest result. ShoeBox + KUDUwave are higher-validity boothless options but require shipped hardware ($150-1,500); both have wave-3-aligned acceptable-error envelopes for clinical audiometry per ASHA + BSA 2023-25 statements (cited for clinical-pathway context only, not as US-regulator authority — ASHA position statements quoted as research-society guidance).

## 2. DiGA-CERTIFIED TINNITUS APPS + UK NICE TA902 SLEEPIO PRECEDENT

### 2.1 The reimbursed-app landscape the dossier names at tool-level but not at reimbursement-level

The dossier already names Tinnitracks (§5.4), myNoise (§5.2), Sereda Tinnitus E-Programme (§5.7 + §11), Sleepio (§5.7), and ReSound Tinnitus Relief app (§5.2). **What it does not name is that several of these are prescribed-and-reimbursed in named non-US health systems**, meaning a DE / AT / CH / UK / AU reader can in many cases access them at €0 / £0 / A$0 out-of-pocket — turning the wave-6 affordability finding inside-out for the digital-app layer specifically.

- **Tinnitracks.** DiGA-listed since 2020 (BfArM Verzeichnis ID listed; Hannover-based Sonormed GmbH). Reimbursed under GKV §33a SGB V as DiGA at €0 out-of-pocket on standard German statutory insurance after a Hausarzt or HNO prescription. Cost without DiGA-listing was ~€499/year direct-pay; under DiGA, fully reimbursed for 90-day course (renewable). Inclusion criteria: tonal subjective tinnitus with identifiable pitch-match in the 200-8500 Hz range; min 5-month chronicity. Switzerland (CH KVG) and Austria (AT ELGA pilot 2024) have parallel listing pathways with smaller coverage envelopes.
- **Kalmeda.** DiGA-listed since 2020 (BfArM Verzeichnis; Mynoise GmbH partnership separate). 12-week CBT-T mobile programme reimbursed under DiGA pathway at €0 GKV out-of-pocket. Plain-language German + English. Companion-style daily-exercise + therapist-style audio narration architecture.
- **Mynoise.** **Not** DiGA-listed as a standalone (free + premium tiers via Stéphane Pigeon; the dossier already names it correctly). Adjacent DiGA-listed apps include Meta-Health Tinnitus + Hörgesund + Tinnitus-Counselling that occupy similar sound-enrichment + CBT-T territory and have been periodically reviewed for BfArM Verzeichnis listing.
- **Sereda Tinnitus E-Programme.** UK BTA-developed, deployed within NHS Nottingham + Sheffield tinnitus services 2022-25; **not currently NICE-recommended-as-DTI** but the equivalent service-level commissioning is in place via NHS Talking Therapies (wave-10 §14.5) where local tinnitus-specific CBT-T pathways exist (~30% of trusts). Reader pays £0 within commissioned trusts; pays ~£40-90 direct outside.
- **Sleepio (UK NICE TA902 precedent).** Big Health Sleepio for insomnia (NOT tinnitus directly) was the **first** Digital Therapeutic to receive NICE Technology Appraisal recommendation (TA902, 2022 — NHS-funded for chronic insomnia adults). Sleepio is the dossier's named adjunct in §5.7 for the sleep-CBT-i overlap with tinnitus-distress. The TA902 precedent is load-bearing because it establishes the *route by which a non-DiGA digital therapeutic gets to NHS-reimbursement*: NICE TA, then ICB / CCG commissioning, then NHS App Library + ORCHA assessment. The companion-app MVP (Dimension 3) should be architected with the TA902 + DiGA pathways as plausible eventual reimbursement routes — not a near-term ask, but a strategic plausibility.
- **Calm + Headspace.** Reader-paying subscriptions; **NOT** DiGA-listed; **NOT** NICE-recommended-as-DTI; named in §5.5 as MBCT-T adjacent practice with honest framing (helpful, low-harm, reader-paid). Calm has UK NHS App Library inclusion 2024 and a separate Calm Health enterprise pathway that some US insurers + UK private-medical-insurance (Bupa, AXA Health) cover; the dossier should name these as reimbursement-adjacent without overclaiming.

### 2.2 The dossier's per-jurisdiction reader-instruction the reimbursed-app layer requires

A DE reader on GKV should be told: "Tinnitracks and Kalmeda are DiGA-listed — ask your Hausarzt or HNO for a DiGA-prescription; reader-cost is €0 under GKV; the prescription is renewable up to 90-day cycles." A UK reader on NHS should be told the Sleepio TA902 pathway covers sleep-CBT-i specifically; the Sereda Tinnitus E-Programme exists in commissioned-trust pathways; otherwise direct-pay at ~£40-90. An AU reader should be told there is no DiGA-equivalent reimbursement yet but Hearing Australia Connect bundles digital tools within MBS audiology pathways. An IN reader should be told eSanjeevani + AYUSH Grid covers the consultation; the apps remain reader-paid but the consultation that prescribes their use is free at point-of-use under PMJAY-AYUSH.

- **Module 3 — Cognitive habituation programme.** Screens: 8-week MBCT-T audio-led course (Kalmeda-style architecture; lessons + practices + reflections); CBT-T light-touch journal (thought-feeling-fact triangulation; ACT defusion exercises); MI self-talk mirror (wave-9 §5.5b); Q-methodology viewpoint-mirroring + IPA super-ordinate-theme-mirroring (wave-5 + wave-9). **Async data primitive:** journal entries + viewpoint-snapshots stored locally; sentiment-summary exportable on reader consent.
