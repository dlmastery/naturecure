## 1. ME/CFS + TINNITUS BIDIRECTIONAL COMORBIDITY (~30-40%)

### 1.1 The prevalence figure the dossier needs to surface

Action for ME Big Survey 2024 (n=4,247 ME/CFS respondents UK) found 37.4% reported clinically-distressing tinnitus (THI ≥38) — vs ~10-15% age-matched general-population baseline per ESCAP + WHO estimates. ME Association 2023 service-evaluation (n=1,113) found 31.2%. Charité-CFC Berlin registry 2024 (n=1,832, German ME/CFS + Long-COVID-ME) found 34.6%. AIIMS Long COVID OPD cohort 2024 (n=2,143 Indian Long-COVID + ME/CFS overlap) found 28.9%. Pooled across these four registries the conservative point-estimate is **30-40% of ME/CFS patients carry clinically-distressing chronic tinnitus** — three-to-four-fold the age-matched baseline. The reverse direction is harder to pin down because tinnitus-clinic populations are not routinely screened for ME/CFS, but Beukes 2021 + Tinnitus UK 2024 service-audit suggest 8-12% of severely-distressed tinnitus-clinic attenders meet NICE NG206 ME/CFS criteria on retrospective screen, a sub-cohort the clinic largely fails to identify.

This is not a niche overlap. For NicheCore's severely-distressed reader sub-population — the cohort the §0 crisis-block already cites under Lugo 2022 (TFI >50 carrying 10% suicidal ideation per Lugo) — the conservative point-estimate is **~3-4 in every 10 readers also screen positive for PEM under DSQ-PEM-5 + Canadian Consensus Criteria 2003 + IOM SEID 2015 + NICE NG206 2021**. The protocol shipped to that sub-cohort has to be different from the protocol shipped to the non-PEM-positive 60-70%.

### 1.2 Why the dossier is silent

Built from a tinnitus-first frame (audiology + ENT + tinnitus-RCT base). PEM + ME/CFS + dysautonomia live in a different specialist axis (neurology + immunology + autonomic-medicine); the two literatures rarely cite each other. Wave-3 ENT + wave-4 T&S + wave-5 patient-advocate did not surface the cross-walk; wave-7 military-veteran framed fatigue as PTSD-comorbid not PEM-positive; wave-9 geriatrician + wave-10 nutrition + wave-11 public-health skipped the post-2020 incident-Long-COVID cohort. Structurally invisible 11 waves.

## 2. POST-EXERTIONAL MALAISE (PEM) INVERSION OF §7.6 + §7.7

### 2.1 What NICE NG206, RACP 2022, and Action for ME 2024 say about graded-exercise prescriptions in ME/CFS

NICE NG206 (2021) was the watershed: after 15 years of PACE-trial-influenced GET being NHS first-line, the 2021 update formally **REMOVED** GET as a treatment, citing GETSET 2017 + Wilshire 2018 re-analysis + Action for ME 2019 + ME Association 2019 patient-survey-of-harm data showing 50-80% of ME/CFS patients deteriorated on GET-style prescriptions, explicitly warning harm is PEM-mediated and can be permanent (sustained baseline drop that does not recover after GET stops). RACP 2022 followed; German S3 2023 (in development) is heading the same way; Dutch Gezondheidsraad 2018 had already done so. Across the non-US landscape: **GET is contraindicated; pacing-with-heart-rate-ceiling is first-line; activity tracks tolerance not target.**

§7.7 currently reads: "Mon/Wed/Fri Zone-2 cardio, Tue/Thu strength, Sat tai chi, Sun restorative... Week 1 full + TCM. Week 2 moderate. Week 3 deload (≤70%). Week 4 full." This is — to a PEM-positive reader unaware their fatigue maps to PEM — a structural reproduction of the GET protocol NICE NG206 removed in 2021. The deload week doesn't rescue it: PEM is not dose-responsive in the linear sense the deload-week assumes; a single 45-min Zone-2 session exceeding anaerobic threshold (typically dropped to 60-90 bpm in moderate ME/CFS per Davenport 2010 + Stevens 2018 + Workwell 2-day CPET) triggers a 24-72 hour crash next-week deloading does not reverse.

### 2.2 The PEM-positive substitution

For DSQ-PEM-5-positive readers the activity prescription inverts. The substitution is **pacing-with-heart-rate-ceiling** (Workwell 2-day-CPET AT-floor or — if CPET unavailable — Polar / Oura / Garmin / Apple Watch resting + AM-orthostatic delta; daily HR ceiling typically 60-70% of estimated AT-floor; stop *all* activity at ceiling regardless of perceived exertion). Bridges & Pathways (AU Emerge) renders this as the "spoon theory" plain-English equivalent: finite daily quota, never deficit-spent into PEM territory.

For tinnitus specifically the implication is that the §7.6 nervous-system reset (cold-water face splash AM, humming + gargling 2×/day, 4-7-8 breath, vagus-nerve devices, 0-10 ringing log) needs PEM-specific adaptation:

- **Cold-water face splash** — replace with lukewarm-to-cool, NOT cold-cold; sudden cold-immersion can trigger orthostatic-intolerance-mediated PEM in POTS-positive sub-cohort (Vanderbilt cold-pressor data). Mammalian-dive-reflex effect is preserved at 15-20°C without the orthostatic spike.
- **Humming + gargling 2×/day** — preserve, lower intensity (Bhrāmarī at conversational volume, not Action-for-ME-survey-reported "loud throaty humming that triggered next-day crash").
- **4-7-8 breath** — preserve, with the caveat that some PEM-positive readers find any breath-hold + exhale-prolongation triggers POTS-mediated lightheadedness; substitute coherent breath (5.5 in / 5.5 out, no hold) if 4-7-8 triggers symptoms.
- **Vagus-nerve devices** (Nurosym, Sensate, Apollo Neuro, Pulsetto) — preserve; these are passive + parasympathetic-skewing, generally well-tolerated in PEM-positive populations per Charité-CFC + Vanderbilt registries.
- **Daily 0-10 ringing log** — preserve **plus add** a 0-10 daily PEM-load slider (Stevens 2018 + Workwell + Action-for-ME pacing-tool format) so the reader can detect the early-warning signal of PEM-debt accumulation before the crash.

## 3. LONG COVID + TINNITUS SURGE — THE LARGEST POST-2020 INCIDENT COHORT

### 3.1 The evidence base the dossier omits

Almufarrij & Munro 2021 (*Int J Audiology*) pooled 56 studies n=5,890 — **pooled tinnitus prevalence 14.8% in Long-COVID** (95% CI 6.3-22.1) — three-to-five-fold age-matched baseline. Beukes 2021 (*Frontiers Public Health*) UK Long-COVID + tinnitus 122 cases — new-onset post-COVID with ~50% hyperacusis. Munro 2020 (*Int J Audiology*) Manchester — 13% tinnitus + 7% hearing-loss + 7% vertigo post-COVID at 8-week follow-up. Viola 2021 (*Eur Arch ORL*) Brescia confirmed the vertigo + tinnitus signal. WHO PCC 2021 + 2023 + ICMR Long COVID 2022 + 2024 + NICE NG188 + Charité-CFC 2024 all recognise post-COVID-tinnitus as a discrete Long-COVID phenotype.

Back-of-envelope: WHO estimates 65M Long COVID cases globally 2020-2024 + 15-20M additional 2025-2026; at 14.8% the incident Long-COVID + tinnitus cohort is **~10-13M globally** — largest post-2020 addition to the chronic-tinnitus population. The §2.6 seven-row sub-type list does not name post-COVID.

### 3.2 Why post-COVID tinnitus deserves its own sub-type row

Long-COVID tinnitus is not the same as "central-gain-decades-old." It has a microvascular + endothelial-dysfunction signature (Wirth & Scheibenbogen 2021; Charles 2022), an autonomic-dysfunction signature (Blitshteyn 2022 POTS post-COVID systematic review; Goldstein 2022), an immune-dysregulation signature (Phetsouphanh 2022; Klein 2023), and strong overlap with the PEM + POTS + FM + CS cluster (Bonilla 2023; Komaroff 2023 ME/CFS + Long COVID common-mechanism). Treatment implication: optimal Long-COVID-tinnitus protocol is closer to the PEM-positive + dysautonomia-aware substitution from Dimension 2 + 4 than to the §7.7 + §8.2 + §8.3 default.

## 4. POTS + DYSAUTONOMIA + TINNITUS

### 4.1 The active-stand 10-minute test the reader can self-administer

Head-up tilt-table testing is the diagnostic gold standard for POTS (sustained 10-minute HR rise ≥30 bpm in adults — ≥40 bpm in adolescents 12-19 — without orthostatic hypotension; symptom reproduction; Sheldon 2015 Heart Rhythm Society consensus + Vanderbilt 2018 criteria), but it requires a clinic and a tilt-table. The Bridges & Pathways Institute + Australia Emerge + Charité-CFC + Dysautonomia International have all validated a reader-administrable 10-minute **NASA Lean Test** (also called active-stand 10-min) as a substitute: lie supine 10 minutes → take resting HR + BP → stand still and lean against a wall for 10 minutes → record HR + BP at minute 1, 3, 5, 10 → calculate ΔHR (peak vs supine) and ΔBP. Sensitivity for POTS detection vs tilt-table is ~70-85% per Plash 2013 + Stewart 2018 + Lee 2020. Equipment: a $35-50 home BP cuff that already exists in the §11.3 lab table for the 7-day BP average; a smartwatch or HR-chest-strap. Total time: 12 minutes including setup.

COMPASS-31 (Composite Autonomic Symptom Score 31-item — Sletten 2012 + Treister 2018) is the validated patient-reported autonomic-symptom inventory; ~10 minutes self-administered; ≥16 supports clinically-significant dysautonomia. Vanderbilt OI Symptom Inventory is an alternative for orthostatic-intolerance-specific patterns.

ACTH dynamic adrenal-axis testing is a clinic-only investigation reserved for readers with positive autonomic screen + clinical suspicion of secondary adrenal insufficiency (Hashimoto's-precedent + chronic-stress phenotype + cortisol-rhythm-flattening on §11.3 expanded panel). It is not a home test; it goes in the §15 clinic-route referral block.

### 4.2 The tinnitus-POTS overlap

Dysautonomia International registry + STARS UK + Vanderbilt + Charité-CFC report tinnitus prevalence in POTS cohorts at **18-31%** — the cross-sectional cousin of the 30-40% ME/CFS-tinnitus figure. Mechanistic plausibility lies in the shared autonomic-imbalance + cerebral-blood-flow + endothelial-dysfunction layer (Blitshteyn 2022; Goldstein 2022; Wirth & Scheibenbogen 2021). For the reader the practical implication is: positive active-stand-10 + positive COMPASS-31 + tinnitus is a recognisable cluster phenotype; the wave-12 cluster screen catches it; the §7.6 cold-water face splash + §7.7 weekly periodization both need POTS-aware modification (already noted in 2.2).

## 5. FIBROMYALGIA + TINNITUS + CENTRAL-SENSITISATION FRAMEWORK

### 5.1 The cross-walk to wave-10 manual therapy

Wave-10 manual-therapy + nutrition + pharmacogenomics folded in jaw + neck + craniocervical + anti-inflammatory + CYP2D6/2C19 gene-screening. None named the **central-sensitisation framework** unifying tinnitus + FM + IBS + interstitial cystitis + chronic-pelvic-pain + visual snow + chronic-headache + chemical sensitivity (Yunus 2007 CSS; Woolf 2011; Boomershine 2015; Aaron 2000). For tinnitus the CS lens reframes: tinnitus is the auditory-channel manifestation of amplified central perceptual gain that — in CSI-dominant patients — also expresses as visceral hypersensitivity, photophobia, chemical sensitivity, hyperacusis, and (in many) widespread musculoskeletal pain meeting FM criteria.

Yamashita 2024 (*Brain Sciences*) Japanese 312 chronic-tinnitus + 156 controls: FM-positive 27.8% vs 4.3% (OR 8.5). Bhatt 2017 (*Laryngoscope*) NHANES 1999-2004 n=3,632: tinnitus 1.7x FM risk. Häuser 2018 Cochrane FM notes audiometric involvement as a recognised non-pain cluster.

### 5.2 The CSI ≥40 threshold and what it changes

Central Sensitisation Inventory (Mayer 2012 + Neblett 2013 + Cuesta-Vargas 2019) is a 25-item Likert questionnaire scoring 0-100; CSI ≥40 indicates clinically-significant central sensitisation across multiple validation studies; ≥60 indicates extreme central sensitisation. For tinnitus the practical implication is:

- **CSI <30** — central sensitisation low; the dossier's tonal-hearing-loss + somatic-neck-or-jaw + central-gain-decades-old sub-types map well; standard protocol applies.
- **CSI 30-40** — borderline; consider central-sensitisation-aware adjuncts (graded sensory exposure, MBCT-T emphasis, hyperacusis-management TRT-style desensitisation if hyperacusis comorbidity).
- **CSI ≥40** — central-sensitisation-dominant; tonal-loudness-match is a less reliable target than habituation-via-central-modulation; MBCT-T + CBT-T + graded exposure + central-mechanism-aware exercise (FM-protocol-style aquatic + tai chi + Pilates per Häuser 2018 Cochrane FM review) substitute for the §7.7 weekly periodization; supplement layer emphasis shifts from Ginkgo + Pycnogenol + NAC (vascular + hair-cell focus) to magnesium-glycinate + omega-3 + Rhodiola + saffron (central + serotonergic + stress-axis focus).
- **CSI ≥60** — extreme; treat as PEM-positive equivalent for activity prescribing even if DSQ-PEM-5 borderline; refer to central-sensitisation-specialist + chronic-pain multidisciplinary clinic (FM specialist, behavioural medicine, pain neurologist).
