## 1. ADOLESCENT EARBUD-TINNITUS EPIDEMIC — SCHOOL + UNIVERSITY INTERVENTION DESIGN

### 1.1 What wave-9 closed and what wave-11 now opens

Wave-9 §12.8.1 named the WHO 1.1-billion-at-risk 12-35 cohort + the adolescent earbud-tinnitus presentation pattern; §12.8.6 included a NIHL-prevention re-cast naming custom-mould attenuating earplugs. **What wave-9 did not design is the school + university intervention surface on which the at-risk population lives 5 days a week, 35-40 weeks a year, for the 11 years from year-7 to undergraduate-finals.** WHO Make Listening Safe 2022's theory-of-change is that school-based + peer-led intervention is the highest-leverage primary-prevention vector for the 12-35 NIHL-at-risk cohort; UK BTA Safe & Sound In Schools 2022, AU Hearing Health Sector Committee 2023 schools resource, DE Stiftung Hören schulische Hörgesundheit, and IN RBSK adolescent-strand 2024 converge on the same insight. The dossier inherits a 12-25 reader living inside this educational system + does not name it.

### 1.2 The five design principles (synthesised from WHO MLS + UK PHE + AU Headspace Schools + DE Stiftung Hören + IN NIMHANS Adolescent Health)

- **Principle 1 — Peer-educator amplifier over teacher-instructor.** Adolescent audio-exposure behaviour is socially-normed; peer-led campaigns outperform teacher-delivered curriculum at 6-month behaviour-change endpoints (WHO MLS 2022 evidence-review). Deployment requires only the peer-training scaffold.
- **Principle 2 — Curriculum-embed, not stand-alone-session.** Stand-alone assemblies achieve <10% 6-month retention; PSHE / Health & Wellbeing / Tutor-time embedding across year-7 induction + year-9 GCSE-options + year-11 leavers + year-12 sixth-form induction + university freshers' achieves 30-50% (UK BTA 2022; AU Headspace Schools 2023; DE Stiftung Hören 2024).
- **Principle 3 — Hearing-protection-normalisation > rule-enforcement.** Reframing custom-mould musician's earplugs as creative-professional + audiophile + DJ + producer signal (vs safety injunction) shifts uptake from 5-15% to 35-55% in the 16-25 musician sub-cohort (UK BTA 2022 sub-analysis; AU APRA AMCOS 2023; DE Deutscher Musikrat 2023).
- **Principle 4 — Audio-exposure-hygiene scaffolding, not abstinence.** Total earbud abstinence is socially impossible; WHO 60/60 rule + smartphone OS-level dB-monitoring (iOS Health > Hearing > Headphone Levels; Android Sound Notifications) + 24-72-hour post-exposure recovery window form a realistic-uptake scaffold.
- **Principle 5 — Entry-screening at year-7 + year-12 + undergraduate + apprenticeship induction.** Routine health-screens already collect height + immunisation + dental; adding a 3-item screen (wave-9 §1.4 + earbud-exposure quantification + Khalfa-abbreviated) at entry-points surfaces the at-risk cohort years before the 14-36-month presentation-lag wave-10 §1.1 named.

### 1.3 The intervention envelope as design artefact — five surfaces

- **Surface A — UK secondary + sixth-form + FE + university**: PSHE / H&W slot (KS3-5); peer-educator scaffold via sixth-form leadership; school-nurse + SENCO + university disability-services + student-union welfare-officer as referral nodes; freshers'-week stall; UCAS extenuating-circumstances pathway for already-presenting school-to-university transitioners.
- **Surface B — Australia secondary + tertiary**: H&PE curriculum years 7-10; Headspace Schools Programme as the 12-25 delivery infrastructure (150+ centres); Hearing Australia paediatric + young-adult free up to age 26.
- **Surface C — India secondary + tertiary**: NEP 2020 H&PE + life-skills strand; NIMHANS + AIIMS Adolescent Health Clinics; RBSK adolescent-strand integration; state-board school-counsellor pathway; ITI / Polytechnic vocational entry-screening.
- **Surface D — Germany secondary + tertiary + Ausbildung**: Sekundarstufe I + II Sozialkunde + Biologie + Sport; Schulpsychologischer Dienst; Studierendenwerk health-services for Universität + FH; Ausbildungsbetrieb audiologic-occupation entry-screen.
- **Surface E — peer-led + voluntary-sector amplifier**: UK BTA Youth Panel + RNID Young People's Network; AU Tinnitus Australia + Headspace youth-advisory; IN NIMHANS adolescent peer-educator + Sankalp; DE Tinnitus-Liga Jugendgruppe + Deutsche Tinnitus-Stiftung youth-arm.

## 2. ADOLESCENT + YOUNG-ADULT TINNITUS DISTRESS — CATASTROPHISING + SUICIDALITY ELEVATED IN 16-24 COHORT

### 2.1 What wave-9 named and what wave-11 now opens

Wave-9 §12.8.1 named adolescent over-reporting of distress under concurrent anxiety / school-pressure load + the CTS-Q (Kentish 2000 GOSH) correlation pattern. **What wave-9 did not name is the elevated tinnitus catastrophising + suicidality signal in the 16-24 sub-band** documented in Lugo 2022 (n≈13,000 Sweden + UK + Netherlands; 16-24 subgroup tinnitus-related suicidal ideation 2.1-3.4× the >25 adult baseline at equivalent THI / TFI severity); Bartels 2008 (Maastricht n≈170 adolescent + young-adult cohort; depression + catastrophising scores at equivalent audiogram + tinnitus-loudness exceed adult); Vasconcellos 2017 (Brazilian n≈400; TCS catastrophising sub-scale elevated vs adult at equivalent psychoacoustic match); Park 2014 KNHANES (Korean 12-19; tinnitus + depression + suicidal-ideation combined OR vs no-tinnitus baseline). The pattern reflects **the developmental insight that the 16-24 cohort is in identity-formation + future-orientation + autonomy-establishment, and chronic sensory disruption disrupts the developmental task more catastrophically than at later life-stages**. The dossier currently treats catastrophising at the adult §5.5 CBT-T + wave-4 PHQ-9 ≥15 + wave-9 paediatric-MBCT-T-via-CAMHS levels — none of which addresses the 16-24 elevated-suicidality signal as a structurally-distinct developmental phenomenon.

### 2.2 The four developmental + cognitive + emotional features that elevate 16-24 tinnitus catastrophising

A developmental-psychology formulation of the 16-24 tinnitus catastrophising + suicidality elevation rests on four features (Erikson 1968 identity-vs-role-confusion; Arnett 2000 emerging-adulthood; Casey + Galván 2014 adolescent prefrontal + limbic asymmetry; Steinberg 2008 dual-systems model; Blakemore 2018 social-cognitive maturation):

- **Feature 1 — Identity-formation interference.** Chronic tinnitus interpreted as "permanent damage" lands on identity-formation surface as foreclosed-future signal — "I will never play music / sleep / concentrate again" — at a developmentally-charged moment (Erikson + Arnett).
- **Feature 2 — Future-orientation amplification.** Chronic conditions at 17 are time-projected forward 60+ years vs ~30-40 years at 47; "this is forever" carries different temporal weight.
- **Feature 3 — Prefrontal-limbic asymmetry.** Casey + Galván + Steinberg dual-systems: limbic threat-detection matures earlier than prefrontal regulation; tinnitus-as-ambient-threat lands on a brain over-weighted toward threat-detection + under-weighted toward regulation.
- **Feature 4 — Social-comparison + identity-foreclosure cascade.** Blakemore 2018: 16-24 cohort is peak-sensitive to social-comparison; presenters compare themselves to peers without the condition through the foreclosed-future frame.

### 2.3 The 16-24 crisis-pathway forward-pointer the dossier needs

The dossier's existing wave-4 trust-and-safety PHQ-9 ≥15 trigger is adult-calibrated; the 16-24 cohort needs **lower-threshold crisis-screening + jurisdiction-specific youth-mental-health pathway surfacing**:

- **India** — Vandrevala Foundation 1860 2662 345 + iCall 9152987821 + NIMHANS Helpline 080-46110007 + Sneha Foundation Chennai 044-24640050.
- **Germany** — TelefonSeelsorge 0800 1110111 / 0800 1110222 + Nummer gegen Kummer 116 111 (children + young people) + Krisenchat.de (under-25 chat-based specialist).

## 5. PARENTAL + TEACHER + PEER-EDUCATOR TRIANGULATION FRAMEWORK

### 5.1 What wave-9 named and what wave-11 now opens

Wave-9 §12.8.5 named Pillar 1 "parental neutrality" + Pillar 4 "paediatric mindfulness" + the six-jurisdiction school-accommodation headline. **What wave-9 did not develop is a system-level triangulation across the three actor-systems the 12-25 reader lives in: parent / family + teacher / school + peer / peer-educator.** Bronfenbrenner (1979, refined 2005) names the four nested systems; Epstein (1995) operationalises the parent-teacher interface; WHO MLS operationalises the peer-amplifier vector Dim-1 Principle 1 named. The dossier currently addresses parent + teacher + peer as three siloed surfaces, not a triangulated system.

### 5.2 The triangulation framework — three actors + three interfaces + the developmental-psychology integration

- **Actor 1 — Parent / family (microsystem).** Wave-9 Pillar 1 holds. Extensions: (a) parent psychoeducation on the four developmental features (Dim 2); (b) parent understanding of lower PHQ-9 ≥10 threshold; (c) parent navigation of the SENCO / NCCD / RPwD / Förderbedarf referral pathway; (d) parent advocacy at 16-25 educational-extension (DSA / ELP / UGC PwD Cell / Nachteilsausgleich); (e) parent-coaching per Kentish 2000 + Bartnik 2012 extended through age 18+ where 18-25 young-adult still lives at home or in regular family-contact.
- **Actor 2 — Teacher / school (mesosystem + exosystem).** SENCO / school-counsellor (UK) + NCCD-coordinator + SSG-coordinator (AU) + RCI school-counsellor + NIEPID special-educator + UGC PwD Cell coordinator (IN) + Sonderpädagoge + Schulpsychologe (DE). - **Actor 3 — Peer / peer-educator (microsystem peer-band).** WHO MLS peer-led model — year-13 leadership delivering to year-7-9; sixth-form mentors; undergraduate societies; vocational audio-engineering mentors; voluntary-sector youth-panels (UK BTA Youth Panel + AU Headspace youth-advisory + IN NIMHANS adolescent peer-educator + DE Tinnitus-Liga Jugendgruppe). Training inputs: WHO 60/60 + festival recovery + hearing-protection-normalisation + peer-catastrophising recognition + safe-disclosure-to-designated-adult.

### 5.3 The three interfaces and the developmental-psychology integration

The three actor-system triangulation is realised through three interfaces:
