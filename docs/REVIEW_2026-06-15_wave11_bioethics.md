## 1. BEAUCHAMP-CHILDRESS FOUR-PRINCIPLE AUDIT ON THE PROTOCOL-AS-OFFERING

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

The "sovereignty" frame ("you are sovereign"; "no physician check-in is mandatory"; "if you keep a physician informed, that is your call") appears 14+ times across §0, §13.5, §12.1, §16, and the YAML opener. Per-supplement contraindications are excellent (warfarin/DOAC, CKD eGFR-gated Mg, CI exclusions, pregnancy/lactation/paediatric, PTSD-comorbid, melatonin paediatric ban). §13.7 honestly documents "do not recommend" approaches.

### 1.2 The four-principle inversion

Beauchamp-Childress *Principles of Biomedical Ethics* 8th ed (2019) names four prima facie principles in equipoise: **autonomy**, **beneficence**, **non-maleficence**, **justice**. The dossier's current signature:

- **Autonomy — over-weighted.** "Sovereignty" framing positions the reader as fully self-governing. This is consistent with founder doctrine but ethically requires *substantive* — not just *procedural* — autonomy: information-sufficient, capacity-intact, external-pressures-absent, alternatives-available. Three of those four are partially failing (§1.3).
- **Beneficence — under-coded.** No *positive duty* clause: when a reader's sub-type (presbycusis-dominant 65+, severe SSHL-window, Lenire-eligible refractory) makes a *higher-evidence* intervention the right answer, the dossier mentions the alternative (§5.9, §0 SSHL, §15) but does not *positively counsel* the reader toward it when the home protocol is *not* their best path. Wave-9's "hearing-first, supplements-second for 65+" is correctly added to §2.6 + §5.0 but is not *positively prescribed*.
- **Non-maleficence — partially coded.** Per-supplement contraindications are excellent; **cumulative-stack iatrogenic risk** — 10 caps/day × 12 months × supplier-quality variance × interaction-matrix complexity × adherence-stress × opportunity-cost — is under-quantified. The wave-4 9% adherence figure is named but its *non-maleficence implication* is not: the 91% experience iatrogenic distress (self-blame, dose-creep, financial loss). Opportunity-cost-of-time (hours not spent on CBT-T / hearing-aid-fitting / SSHL-window-action) is invisible.
- **Justice — inverted at storefront layer.** The MVP £45-75 tier (wave-6 + wave-9 + wave-10) is an *operational fix*; the *Faden-Powers principle* (the worst-off should not be made worse off; benefits accrue to those who need them most) is not named. The £390-680 full protocol systemically benefits the global affluent. The dossier does not commit to MVP-as-default + full-protocol-as-opt-in-step-up as a *justice commitment*, not just a behavioural-pricing choice.

### 1.3 Substantive vs procedural autonomy — the four sub-conditions

- **Information sufficient.** §11.7 + §13.6 + §13.7 approximate this, but the caveats are 8,000 words away from the §13.5 primer the skim-reader actually reads. Substantive autonomy requires caveats to ride alongside the cornerstone claim.
- **Capacity intact.** Not screened. ~25-30% of 75+ readers have MCI/early-dementia (wave-9); ~10-15% of severe-distress readers carry active PHQ-9 item-9 ideation (Lugo 2022); ~5-10% PTSD-active (wave-7) — none can give substantively autonomous consent to a 12-month protocol without an intake capacity self-screen.
- **External pressures absent.** Not assessed. Vulnerable-elder financial-exploitation (~5-8% of 65+ per RNID + Action on Elder Abuse + Age UK), domestic-abuse correlation in hearing-impaired women (Women's Aid + DAHA), and the dossier-as-marketing pressure are unscreened.
- **Alternatives available.** Mentioned but not positively counselled (per §1.2 beneficence).

### 1.4 Cumulative-stack iatrogenic risk + opportunity-cost-of-time

Non-maleficence is framed per-supplement, not per-stack. Cumulative harms:
- **Interaction-multiplication.** 10-cap stack × 4 timed combos × 17 §10 interaction categories = ~170 pairwise self-administered checks; cognitive load is itself a harm.
- **Financial harm.** ~30-45% of readers (wave-6 + wave-10 budget cohort) try the full £390-680 protocol, drop out at month 2-3, having spent £200-400 on supplements then discarded.
- **Opportunity-cost.** 22 actions/day × 365 = ~8,000 micro-actions/year, ~250-400 hours of attention. For a reader whose sub-type is better served by a hearing-aid trial (~2 hours) + CBT-T (~12 hours), this is time mis-spent.
- **Self-blame harm.** A protocol 91% of readers cannot complete carries a structural self-blame signature.

### 1.5 Specific edits — Dimension 1

- **Edit 1.1.** Insert **§0.4 Beauchamp-Childress four-principle frame** (≤250 words) — autonomy + beneficence + non-maleficence + justice; commit to substantive (not procedural) autonomy; positive-counselling beneficence; cumulative-stack + opportunity-cost non-maleficence; MVP-as-default justice commitment.
- **Edit 1.2.** Update **§13.5 patient-facing primer** — add §11.7 placebo + natural-history caveats inline (≤100 words).
- **Edit 1.3.** Add **§8.0.6 cumulative-stack non-maleficence ceiling** — >6-cap daily stacks carry interaction-multiplication + cognitive-load + financial-load + opportunity-cost compounding per-supplement contraindications; stack-creep = warning, not signal to add.
- **Edit 1.4.** Re-anchor **§8.0 MVP** as the justice-principle default offer; full §8.1-§8.5 is opt-in step-up.
- **Edit 1.5.** Update **§5.9 + §5.0 + §0 SSHL block** with positive-counselling: "if 65+ bilateral high-frequency loss, your highest-leverage step is a hearing-aid trial — start there before this protocol"; "if in the SSHL 72-h window, escalate to GP/HNO/ENT *now*."

## 2. CONSENT + CAPACITY FOR SPECIAL-CATEGORY DATA (BELMONT *INFORMED-CONSENT* LAYER ABOVE WAVE-5 GDPR LEGAL LAYER)

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

Wave-5 covered GDPR Article 9 special-category-data *legal* compliance (lawful basis, DPIA, data-subject rights, cross-border transfer, breach notification). Wave-4 covered platform-liability *legal* compliance (medical disclaimer, advertising-substantiation, jurisdiction-scoped claims). The proposed 12-question checkout safety quiz (§11.5) covers per-sub-population exclusion gating.

### 2.2 The Belmont consent gap

Belmont Report (1979) §B requires three elements of informed consent: **information** (procedure, purpose, risks, benefits, alternatives, right to withdraw), **comprehension** (capacity adapted to age, condition, language), **voluntariness** (absence of coercion + undue influence). The 12-question quiz covers gating, not Belmont consent.

- **Information missing from the quiz:** 91% non-completion rate (wave-4); cumulative cost at full-protocol tier (~£4,700-£8,200/yr); opportunity-cost vs alternatives (CBT-T ~12 h; hearing-aid trial ~2 visits); §11.7 placebo + natural-history + instrument-heterogeneity caveats; the dossier-as-marketing acknowledgement.
- **Comprehension not screened.** Tinnitus-distressed + 65+ MCI + TFI >50 readers receive no capacity self-screen (Mini-ACE / GPCOG / MoCA self-version) before consenting to a 12-month protocol.
- **Voluntariness not assessed.** A reader arriving in distress, reading 18,000 words of confident prose, then meeting the Subscribe & Save CTA is being *gently influenced* in a way Belmont §B3 (undue influence: financial inducement, time-pressure, source-dependence) would flag.

### 2.3 Capacity for special-category data — the ethical layer above GDPR

Wave-5 GDPR Art 9 covered the *legal* lawful-basis question. Belmont + ICMR 2017 + AYUSH-CCRAS 2017 + NHMRC 2018 + CIOMS 2016 Guidelines 9-11 ask the *ethical* question: **does the subject have capacity to consent, and is consent layered + revocable + non-prejudicial?** A reader sharing tinnitus sub-type + PHQ-9 + GAD-7 + TFI + PHQ-9 item-9 + pregnancy + CI + warfarin/DOAC + CKD eGFR + PTSD + MCI + financial-exploitation + domestic-abuse-correlation status is sharing the most sensitive cluster of special-category data GDPR + India DPDP Act 2023 + Australia Privacy Act + ICMR 2017 recognise. Ethical consent requires:

- **Layered consent.** Per-category opt-in, not bundled.
- **Withdrawal-without-prejudice.** Withdraw any category at any time without losing protocol access.
- **Data-deletion honoured ethically.** GDPR Art 17 gives the legal right; ethical commitment requires deletion *and* downstream-researcher notification.
- **No-pressure-to-share.** Refusing any category still allows access to accessible layers; current quiz conditions checkout on completion.

### 2.4 Specific edits — Dimension 2

- **Edit 2.1.** Re-architect **§11.5 + storefront quiz** as **Belmont-compliant layered consent** — per-category purpose-risk-opt-in trios; bundled consent removed; per-category withdrawal exposed.
- **Edit 2.2.** Add **§0.5 information-sufficient consent block** (≤150 words) — 91% non-completion + cumulative cost + opportunity-cost + placebo + dossier-as-marketing acknowledgement, before the reader invests time.
- **Edit 2.3.** Add **§11.5.1 capacity self-screen** — single-question reality-check ("in the last week have you had trouble understanding written instructions, remembering appointments, or making financial decisions?"). YES → caregiver-mediated consent + safeguarding escalation. Anchor to AYUSH-CCRAS 2017 + ICMR 2017 + Indian MHA 2017 + UK Mental Capacity Act 2005.
- **Edit 2.4.** Add **§11.5.2 voluntariness self-check** — "are you reading this in financial distress, time-pressure, family-pressure, or a crisis state?" YES → crisis-line + delay-the-purchase + free-MVP. Belmont §B3.
- **Edit 2.5.** Add **§11.5.3 withdrawal-without-prejudice + data-deletion-honoured-ethically clause** — storefront commitment that any reader can withdraw any category, with downstream-notification cascade. Anchor to GDPR Art 17 + India DPDP Act 2023 + Australia Privacy Act + ICMR 2017 §6.

## 3. EQUITY-OF-ACCESS ETHICS (FADEN-POWERS + WHO UHC 2023 + CIOMS 2016 GUIDELINE 3)

### 3.1 What the dossier currently covers

Wave-6 health-economist + behavioural-pricing surfaced jurisdictional pricing variance + MVP £45-75 tier + default-bundle architecture. Wave-9 + wave-10 extended with food-budget MVP overlay (UK Healthy Start / Foodbank Australia / ICDS-Anganwadi / EU FEAD / Tafel / Canadian Food First). Wave-7 + wave-9 surfaced veteran-access (UK Combat Stress + Australian DVA) and 65+ public-health access (NHS / GKV / Medicare / Ayushman).

### 3.2 The Faden-Powers + WHO + CIOMS justice-principle inversion

Faden & Powers (2008) names six well-being dimensions (health, personal security, reasoning, respect, attachment, self-determination) public-health interventions must protect for the worst-off. WHO UHC 2023 anchors the no-financial-hardship principle. **CIOMS 2016 Guideline 3 (Equitable Distribution of Benefits and Burdens)** specifies that benefits must reach the populations bearing the research burden — and that an intervention drawing from populations in one region should not be inaccessibly priced for those populations.

Tinnitus prevalence is approximately uniform globally (UK 12-14%; India 15-18% in 50+; Australia 10-12%; LMICs comparable per Lancet Commission Hearing Loss 2024 with ~10-fold worse access to evidence-based care). The dossier draws ethical legitimacy from AYUSH-CCRAS + Chinese Pharmacopoeia + NMPA + EMA HMPC + TGA + Hamdard + IMPCOPS + Men-Tsee-Khang — but the storefront then prices the full protocol at £390-680/month, systemically excluding the populations the evidence draws from. **This is the Guideline-3 inversion.**

### 3.3 The MVP-as-default architecture as a justice-principle commitment

- **MVP-as-default storefront.** £45-75 MVP is the default offer; full protocol is opt-in step-up surfaced only at the §8.0 week-12 decision gate, not at first checkout.
- **PPP-adjusted pricing by jurisdiction.** Per wave-6 + WHO UHC + Lancet Commission, supplement pricing should be PPP-adjusted (UK £45-75 ≈ India ₹1,200-2,000 ≈ Indonesia Rp 200,000-350,000), not flat-price USD/GBP.
- **Free-version baseline always honoured.** Layers (1) sound enrichment + (2) MBCT-T audio + (4) neck-jaw-vagus release are *free* in every jurisdiction, accessible without account-creation or data-sharing.
- **Benefit-sharing with source communities.** Per CIOMS 2016 Guideline 3 + WHO TCIM Strategy 2025-2034 + Indian Biological Diversity Act 2002 + Nagoya Protocol 2010: revenue from Ayurveda + TCM + Unani + Siddha + Tibetan + Homeopathy formulations carries a benefit-sharing obligation to source-tradition stewards (AYUSH-CCRAS + CCRH + CCRUM + CCRS + PCIMH + Men-Tsee-Khang + Hamdard + IMPCOPS).

The cohort must *include* (not exclude) PTSD-active, severe-distress (TFI >50), 65+ MCI, food-insecure — with safeguarding — because exclusion inverts Guideline 3.
