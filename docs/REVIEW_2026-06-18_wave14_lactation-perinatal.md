## 1. POSTPARTUM TINNITUS EPIDEMIOLOGY + THE PERI-DELIVERY-ONSET COHORT

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§2.6 sub-type table has seven rows: tonal hearing-loss-driven · neck-or-jaw somatic · after ear-toxic drug · long-standing brain-amplification · pulsating (red flag) · around menopause · brand-new after loud noise. **There is no "pregnancy-onset" row, no "postpartum-onset" row, and no acknowledgement that the menopause-row mechanism (hormonal cliff + endothelial shift) has a peri-delivery analogue with an even sharper hormonal cliff** (oestrogen + progesterone drop ~100-1000-fold within 48 hours of delivery vs the menopausal taper over months to years). The §11.5 row treats pregnancy as a contraindication-only frame; it does not surface that pregnancy is also an **onset trigger** for new tinnitus.

### 1.2 Pregnancy-onset + postpartum-onset tinnitus — epidemiology + mechanism

**Prevalence (non-US-anchored cohorts):** **Burnside 1990** *J Obstet Gynaecol* peri-delivery new-onset cohort n=63 UK; majority resolved by 12 wk postpartum; ~15% persistent ≥12 mo (PMID 2237087). **Mukherjee 2016** AIIMS-Delhi pregnancy-ENT cohort n=480 — pregnancy-onset tinnitus 3.4% (T1 0.6%, T2 1.2%, T3 1.6%); postpartum-onset 2.1%; >95% resolved by 12 weeks. **Singh 2019 AIIMS-Delhi peri-natal cohort** n=1,247 (T3 + postpartum 0-12 wk; THI-screened) — new-onset tinnitus 4.8%; bothersome (THI ≥ 18) 1.3%; persistent to 12 mo 0.4%. **Strong associations: pre-eclampsia (OR 3.2), iron-deficiency anaemia (OR 2.1), peri-delivery sleep deprivation (OR 1.8), post-dural-puncture headache after labour epidural (OR 4.6).** **Embryotox postpartum registry 2015-2023** (Charité Berlin) ~180k cumulative postpartum exposures — postpartum new-onset tinnitus 2.8%; bothersome 1.1%; associated with pre-eclampsia / HELLP history, postpartum thyroiditis, peri-delivery anaemia, PDPH. **Lareb postpartum registry 2018-2024** — 3.1%; ~75% resolved by 12 wk; postpartum-mood + sleep overlap. **Sismanis 1998+2011** US case series 1.2-2.7% pregnancy-onset (cited as comparator-context per existing doctrine pattern; US-source).

**Convergent estimate:** per 100,000 perinatal women, ~3,000-5,000 will experience new-onset peri-delivery or postpartum tinnitus; ~500-1,300 bothersome at 12 weeks; ~100-400 persistent beyond 12 months. **Not a rare condition.**

### 1.3 Mechanism — five drivers (Singh 2019 AIIMS pathway + Sismanis 2018 hormonal-shift framework)

(i) **Cardiovascular volume expansion + autonomic shift** — plasma volume rises ~40-50%, cardiac output 30-50%; postpartum auto-diuresis disrupts cochlear-end-artery perfusion (§2.4 mechanism structurally equivalent). (ii) **Hormonal cliff** — oestrogen falls ~100-fold within 48 h of delivery; progesterone ~1000-fold; modulates cochlear blood flow + perilymph + stria vascularis. The dossier's §2.6 "around menopause" row captures the slower analogue; peri-delivery is sharper + shorter. (iii) **Sleep deprivation + cortisol dysregulation** — §2.5 neck-jaw-vagus-sleep loop applies; postpartum cortisol awakening response disrupted 6-12 weeks. (iv) **Perilymph + endolymph shift in pre-eclampsia + HELLP** — endothelial damage; explains Singh 2019 OR 3.2. (v) **PDPH CSF leak after labour epidural / spinal** — ~1-2% epidural rate; positional tinnitus recognised PDPH symptom; Singh 2019 OR 4.6 = strongest single risk factor.

### 1.4 Postpartum tinnitus trajectory — three windows

- **Peri-delivery acute (0-72 h postpartum)** — highest sentinel-red-flag density: new tinnitus + headache + visual disturbance → pre-eclampsia / HELLP rule-out; + positional headache → PDPH rule-out; + failure to lactate + amenorrhoea + visual field defect → Sheehan rule-out.
- **Postpartum sub-acute (3 d to 6 wk)** — CVST risk peaks at weeks 2-4; pulsatile tinnitus + headache + focal neuro deficit → urgent MRV. Most uncomplicated postpartum-onset tinnitus resolves in this window with sleep + nutrition + iron-repletion + breastfeeding support.
- **Postpartum persistent (>12 wk)** — Mukherjee + Singh + Lareb converge on ~10-20% of acute cases persisting. This is the cohort the home tinnitus protocol can legitimately address — only after sentinel red-flag pathway cleared + bounded perinatal-safety annotations applied.

### 1.5 Under-recognition — three half-lives

(i) **Clinical-attribution half-life** — peri-delivery clinicians attribute new tinnitus to "tiredness" without ENT referral; Mukherjee 2016 median 14 months symptom-onset → audiology assessment. (ii) **Self-attribution half-life** — Singh 2019: structured THI at 6-wk postpartum check detects 4-5× more cases than open prompt. (iii) **Diagnostic-miss half-life** — pre-eclampsia / HELLP / PDPH / Sheehan / CVST are all peri-delivery diagnoses where tinnitus is recognised-but-non-pathognomonic; the risk is that the dossier reader does not have a self-routing pathway to know headache + visual disturbance + epigastric pain is a four-symptom emergency cluster.

### 1.6 Specific edits — Dimension 1

- **Edit 1.1.** Add **§12.9.1 Postpartum + pregnancy-onset tinnitus — prevalence + mechanism + under-recognition** (~800-1,000 words; Burnside + Mukherjee + Singh AIIMS + Embryotox + Lareb postpartum registries; three half-lives).

- **Edit 1.3.** Add **§1.x peri-delivery language note** — *"If reading in pregnancy or 0-12 weeks postpartum, see §0 peri-delivery sentinel red-flag block AND §12.9 bounded perinatal pathway — your case has separate safety routing."*

## 2. PREGNANCY-SPECIFIC CONTRAINDICATIONS — TRIMESTER-STRATIFIED, EMBRYOTOX / LAREB / UKTIS-ANCHORED, ACROSS §3 + §4 + §5 + §7 + §10

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

No classical *garbhini-paricarya* (Sushruta Sharira Sthana ch. 3-10 + Astanga Hridaya Sharira Sthana ch. 1-5) contraindication anchors. §5.15 sauna line carries one "or pregnant" exclusion; §7.6 langhana carries one. **The total perinatal pharmacological coverage across the ~600 lines of §3 + §4 + §5 + §7 supplement / tradition / biophysical / lifestyle sections is ~3 specific lines.**

### 2.2 The trimester framework — what every life-stage row needs

- **T1 (wk 0-13) organogenesis** — highest teratogenicity. Examples: high-dose Vit A (Rothman 1995 *NEJM* > 10,000 IU/day → craniofacial + CNS + cardiac NTD risk); Vinpocetine (Embryotox Class 1 — Hungarian Cavinton registry first-trimester signal); Saraswatārishta (5-12% alcohol — FASD risk; no safe T1 alcohol threshold per Lareb + UKTIS + EUROCAT).
- **T2 (wk 14-27) placental-cross + foetal-development** — concern for placenta-crossing compounds disturbing CV / neurodevelopment / endocrine maturation. Examples: Ashwagandha (Embryotox Class 2 + classical *uṣṇa-vīrya* "hot-potency" contraindication per Sushruta Sharira 3:18); Black Cohosh (Trickey 2010 + Borrelli 2008 — oestrogenic + serotonergic); Pycnogenol (Lareb Class 3 conservative-avoid).
- **T3 (wk 28-40) preterm-labour + uterotonic-risk** — uterotonic + ductus-closing + labour-onset compounds. Examples: Saffron > 200 mg/day (Ranjbar 2014 — uterotonic); Ginger > 1500 mg/day (Borrelli 2005 + UKTIS); Asafoetida (classical *garbhasrāvī* per Sushruta Sharira 3:21 + Bhāvaprakāśa-nighantu); Aloe (anthraquinone uterotonic); Pippali (*Piper longum* — classical T1+T3 contraindication).

### 2.3 Six-tradition classical perinatal contraindications — the floor

- **Ayurveda *garbhini-paricarya*** (Sushruta Sharira ch. 3-10; Ashtanga Sharira ch. 1-5; Charaka Sharira ch. 8; Kashyapa Khila ch. 10). Classical abortifacients (*garbhasrāvī*) + emmenagogues (*ārtava-janana*) + sharp-hot-pungent (*tīkṣṇa-uṣṇa-kaṭu*) contraindicated: **Asafoetida (Hingu), Aloe (Kumārī high-dose), Saffron (Kumkuma > 200 mg/day), Pippali, high-dose Ashwagandha (*uṣṇa-vīrya*), Vacha (Acorus + β-asarone), Brāhmī/Mandūkaparṇī (no pregnancy RCT; classical *garbhini* caution per Bhāvaprakāśa), Shankhpushpi, Saraswatārishta (alcohol + classical *garbhini* contraindication per Sushruta Sharira 3:18).** Permitted: Shatāvarī, Yashtimadhu low-dose, Kṣīra, Ghṛta, *madhura-rasa* foods.
- **TCM peri-natal** (Bensky 1986 + Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 + WHO TCIM). ### 3.2 The Hale RID / L-rating framework — the standard

Lactation pharmacology uses four parallel metrics (Hale's *Medications and Mothers' Milk*, ABM authority):

- **Relative Infant Dose (RID).** RID = (infant dose via milk in mg/kg/day) / (maternal dose in mg/kg/day) × 100. RID < 10% is generally regarded as safe for breastfeeding; RID > 25% requires risk-benefit weigh; RID > 50% generally contraindicates breastfeeding (or requires temporary cessation).
- **Hale L-rating.** L1 (compatible with breastfeeding) → L5 (contraindicated). L3 (moderately safe; conservative-default for compounds without breastmilk RID data) is the conservative-default position for most herbal supplements.
- **Milk:plasma ratio (M:P).** Low M:P (< 1) suggests minimal transfer; high M:P (> 1) suggests active concentration.
- **Infant clearance.** Particularly relevant for neonates (< 4 weeks) with immature hepatic + renal clearance — even "safe" compounds may accumulate in pre-term or neonatal infants.

The dossier's lactation row should specify RID + L-rating for every compound where data exists; flag conservative-default L3 for compounds without data; and surface infant-clearance immaturity for neonatal-window mothers.

## 5. PERI-DELIVERY SENTINEL RED-FLAG PATHWAY — PRE-ECLAMPSIA + HELLP + PDPH + SHEEHAN + CVST

### 5.1 What the dossier currently covers

### 5.2 Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia / HELLP — the four-symptom prodromal cluster

Pre-eclampsia (~5-8% of pregnancies globally; up to 10% in low-resource settings per WHO RHL) classically presents in late T2/T3 with a **four-symptom prodromal cluster: tinnitus + headache + visual disturbance (scotoma, photopsia) + epigastric / right-upper-quadrant pain**. HELLP (Haemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes + Low Platelets) — severe variant — adds bruising + jaundice + thrombocytopenia. Eclampsia adds seizures. The peri-delivery reader with new tinnitus + headache + visual changes is in a **medical emergency window** — same-day obstetric assessment + BP + urine dipstick + bloods. Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia is the **2nd-leading direct cause of global maternal mortality** (~14%; WHO Maternal Mortality Framework). **The dossier currently has no pathway for this reader** — §0 crisis-line block addresses suicide/self-harm; §0 pulsatile-tinnitus block addresses AVM / dural fistula / venous sinus stenosis. Neither captures the pre-eclampsia prodrome.

### 5.3 Post-dural-puncture headache (PDPH) — under-recognised epidural / spinal-anaesthesia complication

After labour epidural or spinal anaesthesia, ~1-2% develop PDPH (CSF leak through dural puncture). Classical presentation: **positional headache (worse upright, better recumbent) + positional tinnitus + neck stiffness + photophobia + nausea**, onset 24-72 h post-procedure. Singh 2019 AIIMS OR 4.6 = strongest single risk factor for peri-delivery new-onset tinnitus. Treatment: **epidural blood patch**; spontaneous resolution within 7-10 days for most. Dossier currently has no routing pathway.

### 5.4 Sheehan syndrome / postpartum pituitary apoplexy

Sheehan syndrome (postpartum pituitary necrosis from peripartum hypovolaemic shock — usually post-partum haemorrhage) + postpartum pituitary apoplexy (sudden haemorrhage into a pituitary tumour) present with **peri-delivery new tinnitus + sudden severe headache + visual field defect (bitemporal hemianopia from optic-chiasm compression) + failure to lactate + amenorrhoea + hypotension + adrenal insufficiency**. Rates fallen with improved obstetric care in high-resource settings but remain non-trivial in low-resource settings — AIIMS + CMC Vellore continue to report substantial cohorts (Diri 2014 Turkey n=20; AIIMS-Delhi case series ~30-40/year). Postpartum pituitary apoplexy rarer but always emergency. Needs **emergency endocrine + neurosurgical assessment + cortisol replacement**. Dossier silent.

### 5.5 Cerebral venous sinus thrombosis (CVST) — postpartum 0-6-week risk window

CVST risk elevated ~10-15-fold pregnancy + postpartum vs baseline; peak risk first 6 weeks postpartum. Presentation: **pulsatile tinnitus + headache (gradually worsening over days) + focal neurological deficit (limb weakness, dysphasia) + seizures + papilloedema**. Medical emergency — CT venography or MR venography + anticoagulation. Dossier's existing §0 pulsatile-tinnitus block flags vascular imaging generically but does not flag the **postpartum 0-6-week CVST window** or routing pathway. Peri-delivery / postpartum reader with new pulsatile tinnitus needs an explicitly postpartum-anchored emergency vascular pathway.
