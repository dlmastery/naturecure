## 1. SLEEP ARCHITECTURE × TINNITUS — PSG / ACTIGRAPHY PHENOTYPE THE DOSSIER REFERENCES BUT DOES NOT OPERATIONALISE

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§11.7 references "sleep architecture" once in passing as part of the PSQI overlay. §11.3 includes PSQI + ISI. §7.5 targets PSQI <5. §10.5.5 includes a Mg-threonate + melatonin "vivid dreams / fragmented REM" AE row at ~24%. The sleep-architecture line is **not anchored to specific PSG-derived phenotypes** — a senior sleep consultant reads it as a sleep-hygiene checklist (lights-out, 18-20°C, blackout, side-sleep ear-up, bedside masker), not as a sleep-medicine pathway with PSG / actigraphy phenotype connecting to §2.3 brain volume-up.

### 1.2 The sleep-architecture signature of chronic tinnitus

The published PSG + actigraphy literature (Crönlein 2007 Regensburg n=80; Marks 2018 Cambridge MRC-CBU n=20 high-density EEG; Attanasio 2013 Rome n=40 PSG; Wallhäusser-Franke 2013 Heidelberg n=87) converges on five phenotype features:

- **Slow-wave (N3) suppression** — 25-40% N3% reduction vs age-matched controls. N3 is the dominant cortical state for thalamocortical down-regulation; suppressed N3 keeps auditory cortex partially "on," maintaining §2.3 gain-up.
- **REM fragmentation + increased REM micro-arousal index** — 30-50% higher micro-arousal density (<15 s, sub-AASM-scoring threshold). §10.5.5 Mg-threonate-+-melatonin AE lands on this baseline.
- **Reduced sleep-spindle density + altered K-complex morphology** — spindles are the canonical sensory-gating mechanism (Schabus 2007; Dang-Vu 2010); reduced density (Marks 2018) is the EEG-level translation of "the brain cannot gate out the phantom even during sleep" — mechanistic anchor for why §5.2 + §5.17 overnight enrichment works.
- **Cyclic alternating pattern (CAP) elevation** — 20-40% higher CAP rate (Terzano + Parrino 2002 Italian school); explains "I sleep 7 hours but still feel exhausted" complaints.
- **Sleep-onset phenotype dominates** — ~60-70% sleep-onset (≥30 min latency) vs ~30-40% sleep-maintenance; intervention stacks differ (sleep-onset = stimulus control + paradoxical intention + restriction; sleep-maintenance = cognitive restructuring + overnight sound enrichment).

### 1.3 The actigraphy translation for home self-tracking

PSG requires a lab; for OTC + home doctrine, consumer actigraphy is the self-administrable layer. **Validated devices:** Oura Ring Gen 3+ (De Zambotti 2019 / Cao 2022 — 79% epoch-level sleep/wake concordance, weaker N3); Whoop 4.0 (Stone 2021); Fitbit Sense / Charge 6 (Haghayegh 2019 — TST valid, stages weaker); Apple Watch Series 9+ WatchOS 11 (Roomkham 2018); Garmin Venu 3 (least validated of majors); Withings ScanWatch + Sleep Analyser mat (validated for OSA screening — bridges Dimension 2). **Honest read:** total sleep time + onset latency + efficiency = reasonably tracked; **stage estimates (N3 + REM) are NOT clinically valid against PSG** (60-80% concordance). **Useful for tinnitus tracking:** (a) sleep-onset latency week-over-week → highest-yield objective biomarker for tinnitus-distress-driven insomnia; >30 min consistently → CBT-I gateway; (b) sleep efficiency target ≥85%; <85% → CBT-I sleep-restriction module; (c) overnight HRV / RMSSD as §2.5 vagus-nerve proxy linked to §5.13.

## 2. OSA + TINNITUS BIDIRECTIONAL — CPAP-HELPS-TINNITUS + OSA-WORSENS-TINNITUS + AHI-STRATIFIED INTERVENTION

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

§6.6 lists "untreated sleep apnea — Lofta WatchPAT" as one bullet in a 9-row environmental-trigger list; §11.3 pairs Epworth + WatchPAT. The wave-5 fact-checker review noted *"Untreated sleep apnea worsens tinnitus distress; CPAP improves distress; CPAP does NOT reduce loudness or pitch-match"* — clarification logged but **not folded into §5 or §8 protocol body**. The dossier treats OSA as "yes, screen for it" without bidirectional pathway, AHI stratification, positional-OSA + MAD + Inspire landscape, or CSA + HFrEF crossover with §11.6.

### 2.2 The bidirectional clinical reality

- **OSA prevalence in chronic tinnitus is elevated 1.5-2.5×** (Lai 2018 Taiwan n=12,000; Chopra 2019 PSG-confirmed; Koo 2019 South Korea n=24,000). Nocturnal intermittent hypoxia + sleep fragmentation feed both §2.2 cochlear oxidative stress AND §2.3 brain volume-up via fragmented N3 sensory gating.
- **CPAP improves THI / TFI tinnitus distress but does NOT reduce loudness or pitch-match.** Nakayama 2019 n=42; Korkmaz 2017 n=52 — CPAP-adherent cohorts drop THI 18-24 points at 6 months; pitch-match unchanged. This is the wave-5 fact-checker clarification — CPAP fixes §2.3 (brain gain via architecture) and partly §2.2 (oxidative stress via hypoxia) but does not reverse structural §2.2 damage already accrued. High-leverage adjunct, not cure.
- **Reverse causation runs too.** Tinnitus distress → sleep fragmentation → worsens AHI 15-20% (more arousals destabilise upper-airway tone). CBT-T / MBCT-T (§5.5) reduces AHI 3-8 events/hour at 6 months in COMISA cohorts (Sweetman 2017 + 2019 Adelaide).

### 2.3 The AHI-stratified intervention ladder the dossier does not surface

For the dossier reader who screens Epworth ≥10 + STOP-Bang ≥3 + HSAT-positive:

- **AHI 5-14 (mild OSA).** First-line: **positional therapy** (Night Shift neck-band; Philips NightBalance; supine-avoidance pillow). WatchPAT differentiates supine-dominant from non-positional OSA; if supine-AHI > 2× non-supine, positional therapy is cheap (~$20-200) and high-leverage. Second-line: **mandibular advancement device (MAD)** — TGA-listed SomnoDent + ResMed Narval + ProSomnus; UK NHS MAD pathway via dentist with sleep training; AIIMS dental sleep medicine + Manipal MAD fitting in India. Cost: £300-800 UK NHS / €400-1200 EU / AU$1500-2500 / ₹15,000-40,000.
- **AHI 15-29 (moderate OSA).** First-line: **CPAP / APAP** (ResMed AirSense 11 / Philips DreamStation 2 / Fisher & Paykel SleepStyle) via prescribing sleep physician + DME. UK NHS community sleep clinic pathway; EU GKV / SS / SNS coverage; AU PBS + private; India AIIMS + KIMS + Manipal + Apollo (~₹35,000-80,000 plus mask). APAP (auto-titrating 4-20 cmH₂O) is default; fixed CPAP only post-titration study. Adherence is the load-bearing variable — published ≥4 h/night on ≥70% nights at 6 months is ~50-65% — link to §8.0 MVP framing.
- **AHI 30+ (severe OSA) + CPAP-intolerant.** **Inspire hypoglossal nerve stimulator** (implanted) for AHI 15-65 + BMI < 32 + non-circumferential collapse on DISE. CE-marked 2010, FDA 2014, TGA 2020, Health Canada Class IV 2019, MHRA approved. AHI reduction ~68% at 12 months (STAR Strollo 2014; ADHERE 2023). §15 clinic-route only. **CRITICAL contraindication: §5.14 PEMF mat + §5.10 red-light over the implanted pulse generator** — add to §11.5 alongside cochlear-implant row.
- **Central sleep apnea + Cheyne-Stokes in HFrEF.** Crossover with wave-8 cardiology §11.6 row 16. CSA in HFrEF is **NOT treated with CPAP / ASV first-line** — SERVE-HF (Cowie 2015) showed adaptive servoventilation INCREASED cardiovascular mortality in HFrEF + LVEF <45% + central-dominant SDB. HFrEF readers route via HF + sleep joint MDT, not consumer Lofta + CPAP. ESC HF 2023 SDB module is the framework reference.

## 3. CIRCADIAN + CHRONOTYPE-STRATIFIED INTERVENTION + MELATONIN PHASE-RESPONSE CURVE

### 3.1 What the dossier currently covers

§7.5 sets uniform "lights-out 22:00-22:30 + screens off 21:00 + morning sunlight." §8.5 applies the same wake-06:00 / Combo 1-08:00 / Combo 3-21:00 / lights-out-22:00 timetable to all readers. §3.3 melatonin row says 0.3-3 mg "30-60 min before sleep." §10.5 names "last caffeine 14:00 + Mg / Ashwagandha PM + melatonin at bedtime."

This is **circadian-uniform prescription** — a major sleep-medicine error. Reader DLMO spans ~4-6 h across chronotypes; the uniform 22:00 anchor is wrong by 2-4 h for definite-evening and 1-2 h in the opposite direction for definite-morning. For a sleep-onset-insomnia-dominant cohort, this is the difference between 20-min sleep onset and 90-min sleep onset with tinnitus in attendance.

### 3.2 The chronotype distribution the dossier ignores

Chronotype is measured via the **Morning-Eveningness Questionnaire (MEQ — Horne & Östberg 1976)** + **Munich Chronotype Questionnaire (MCTQ — Roenneberg 2003)** — self-administered, free, validated across UK + EU + AU + India. Five categories with approximate prevalence + DLMO: definitely morning ~10% / DLMO ~19:30; moderately morning ~20% / DLMO ~20:30; intermediate ~40% / DLMO ~21:30; moderately evening ~20% / DLMO ~22:30-23:30; definitely evening ~10% / DLMO ~00:30-02:00.

Tinnitus + insomnia comorbid cohorts skew **evening type 1.4× over baseline** (Crönlein 2007) — sleep-onset insomnia clusters in evening chronotypes forced by social schedule to attempt sleep before DLMO, and tinnitus distress amplifies in the pre-DLMO wake window. **The blanket 22:00 lights-out is actively harmful for the definite-evening ~10% and suboptimal for the moderate-evening ~20%.** Two doctrine-compatible fixes: (a) align lights-out to DLMO + 30-60 min and restructure social schedule, or (b) structured chronotherapy / phase-advance protocol (15-30 min/week earlier lights-out + AM bright-light + dim-evening + DLMO-anchored low-dose melatonin).

### 3.3 The melatonin phase-response curve the dossier needs

Melatonin 0.3 mg at 30-60 min before sleep — the dossier's default — is **the wrong dose-time for ~30-40% of readers** (the evening-type cohort). The Lewy / Burgess / Eastman phase-response curve (PRC) research at Oregon Health Sciences + Rush Sleep Disorders + UCL Surrey Sleep Research is the international sleep-medicine consensus on this:

- **Phase-advance (move sleep earlier — the goal for evening chronotype with social-schedule misalignment).** Low-dose melatonin 0.3-0.5 mg taken **5-7 hours before habitual DLMO** (i.e. early-to-mid-afternoon for evening chronotypes) phase-advances the circadian clock ~30-90 min/week. For a DE reader with DLMO at 00:30, this means melatonin at 17:30-19:30, NOT at 21:30. The dossier's 21:00 Combo 3 melatonin for this reader is wasted at best, phase-delaying at worst.
- **Phase-delay (move sleep later — rare in this cohort).** Melatonin in early morning (post-DLMO) phase-delays.
- **No phase shift / sleep-promoter only (the dossier's current implicit assumption).** Melatonin 0.3-3 mg taken 30-60 min before habitual sleep, for a reader whose habitual sleep time is already aligned with their DLMO. Works only for moderate / intermediate / definite morning chronotypes with no circadian misalignment.

The §3.3 melatonin row should be rewritten with a chronotype-conditional dosing instruction — defaults to current 0.3 mg / 30-60 min before sleep for M / MM / N chronotypes; adds a **chronotherapy module** for ME / DE chronotypes with 0.3-0.5 mg at "5-7 hours before your DLMO" (estimable from MCTQ self-administered output).

### 3.4 ICSD-3R circadian rhythm sleep-wake disorders — under-served cohort

The dossier does not screen for: (a) **Shift-work disorder** — ~10-15% of working-age readership; the 22:00 anchor + §8.5 master timetable are invalid for shift-workers; needs a §8.5b variant anchored to "your sleep-period start" with strategic-napping + caffeine-timing + bright-light-during-shift + dark-glasses-commute-home + DLMO-anchored low-dose melatonin. (b) **Delayed sleep-wake phase disorder (DSWPD)** — MCTQ-MSF ≥06:00 + distress + functional impairment + ≥3-month duration + failed earlier-schedule attempts; ~0.2-1.7% adult prevalence; over-represented in tinnitus-insomnia. (c) **Advanced sleep-phase disorder** — early lights-out + early-morning waking; ~1% adult; over-represented in elderly readers; 06:00 wake is *already past* their natural wake. (d) **Non-24-hour sleep-wake disorder** — rare in sighted (familial), common in blind (light-entrainment loss); cross-reference wave-3 disability-inclusion row; tasimelteon (Hetlioz) clinic-route reference covers EU + UK + AU + India, not US-only.

## 4. CBT-I SPECIFICALLY FOR TINNITUS-INSOMNIA — SLEEPIO + SOMRYST + NATIONAL IAPT INTEGRATION + COMISA + HYPERAROUSAL INDEX

### 4.1 What the dossier currently covers

§5.5 conflates CBT-T + CBT-I in a single sub-section title: *"Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for tinnitus (CBT-T) and CBT for insomnia (CBT-I)."* The body mostly addresses CBT-T (Cima 2012 Maastricht, Cochrane Fuller 2020) with one trailing sentence pointing at Sleepio + CBT-i Coach. §10.7.2 names Sleepio + Somryst as one app-based row. §11.3 lists ISI alongside PSQI.

This is below sleep-medicine bar in five ways: (a) CBT-T and CBT-I are different protocols that interact non-trivially; (b) NICE TA902 recommends Sleepio for adults with insomnia and is NHS-prescribable — load-bearing UK surface the dossier misses; (c) German DiGA reimburses Somnio + HelloBetter Schlafen at 100% via GKV Krankenkassen — wave-8 GKV framing should extend; (d) **COMISA — comorbid OSA + insomnia** — absent despite ~30-50% prevalence in tinnitus-clinic cohorts and Sweetman 2019 dual-protocol RCT; (e) the **Hyperarousal Index** (Regestein 1993 + Cox 2020) — 26-item self-screen validated for hyperarousal-driven sleep-onset insomnia — is the natural gateway for tinnitus-insomnia and is absent from §11.3.

### 4.2 The CBT-I-for-tinnitus differentiation the dossier needs

CBT-I has six modules: sleep hygiene, sleep restriction, stimulus control, cognitive restructuring, relaxation, paradoxical intention. **The tinnitus-specific adaptation** (Marks 2019 Cambridge MRC-CBU; Henry 2019 Portland VA — named as protocol-developer per doctrine-narrow exception; UK Tinnitus E-Programme Sereda et al) diverges in four ways: (a) **stimulus control modified** — standard CBT-I says leave the bedroom after 20 min; for tinnitus, silence amplifies (§5.18 already names this) — stay in bedroom with bedside sound enrichment + body scan, leave only on defined cognitive-rumination loop; (b) **cognitive restructuring** targets the tinnitus-catastrophising loop ("the ringing will never stop / it's getting worse / I'll never sleep again") on top of standard insomnia cognitions ("I won't be able to function tomorrow") — Cima-2012-style defusion + Henry-2019 tinnitus-specific restructuring; (c) **sleep restriction** to 5-6 h initial time-in-bed is harder with tinnitus because the consolidated window has higher tinnitus salience — pair with low-volume bedside enrichment throughout; (d) **hyperacusis-comorbid sub-cohort** (Khalfa-Hyperacusis-Index-positive) needs adapted relaxation audio + light-touch sound enrichment + gradual desensitisation rather than full-bandwidth sound therapy.

### 4.3 The international digital-therapeutics landscape the dossier needs to surface

- **UK NICE TA902** recommends Sleepio for adults with insomnia; NHS-prescribable via community NHS Talking Therapies; self-referral in most ICBs. Extends §15.6.
- **German DiGA.** Somnio (Mementor) digital CBT-I reimbursed 100% by all GKV Krankenkassen; HelloBetter Schlafen also DiGA-listed; prescribable by GP / Psychotherapeut / HNO. Extends §15.7.
- **Australia.** Sleepio TGA-listed (ARTG 296486 Class I); Somryst TGA-listed; Better Access Initiative covers clinician CBT-I via MBS 80100-80120. Extends §15.8.
- **India.** Sleepio + Somryst not directly available; AIIMS Delhi + NIMHANS Bengaluru + Apollo + KIMS deliver therapist-administered CBT-I; wave-6 PMJAY §15.5 should add AYUSH-derived sleep practices (Yoga Nidra structured 30-day; pratyahara-anchored ritual) as DGHS-recognised CBT-I-equivalent.
- **Canada.** Sleepio + Somryst available; OHIP / RAMQ coverage variable.

### 4.4 COMISA — the missing cohort

Sweetman + Lack + Bastien 2017-2019 Adelaide formalised COMISA (comorbid OSA + insomnia) as a distinct phenotype: ~30-50% prevalence in chronic-tinnitus + sleep-clinic cohorts; worse outcomes than either alone; CBT-I concurrent with CPAP titration improves CPAP adherence 38% (Sweetman 2019 RCT) AND insomnia ISI 5-8 points more than either alone. Tinnitus + OSA + insomnia comorbid readers route through Dimension 2 OSA pathway AND CBT-I pathway concurrently, not sequentially.

### 5.2 The dual-zone sleep-environment engineering landscape

For the shared bedroom where one partner has tinnitus + needs sound enrichment and the other does not, there is a documented engineering ladder:

- **Personal-zone bedside masker.** Tivoli SongBook or Sangean WR-22BT within 30 cm of the tinnitus partner's pillow on pink / brown / rain / fan. SPL drops ~6 dB per doubling of distance, so at 1.5 m the partner experiences ~12-15 dB lower SPL. Works for ~40-50% of partner conflicts.
- **Bone-conduction / pillow-speaker / sleep-headphones.** Shokz OpenSwim / OpenComm bone-conduction; Ozlo Sleepbuds (Bose Sleepbuds II resurrection 2024); Soundcore Sleep A20 (TGA + CE 2024); Philips SmartSleep headband; Sound Oasis SP-151 + Acoustic Sheep SleepPhones pillow-speakers. Near-zero leak to partner; ~60-70% resolution rate; ear-canal-irritation AE is real, and ear-canal devices are contraindicated with cochlear / middle-ear implants (cross-reference §11.5).
- **Acoustic-zoning.** Directional speaker pointed away from partner + acoustic panel between bed and partner side + rug + soft drapes for absorption. ~$30-200; partner-side SPL drops another ~3-6 dB.

### 5.4 Scheduled separate-bedrooms — the destigmatised pathway

Sleep medicine has openly recommended scheduled separate-bedrooms for 15+ years (Rosenblatt 2006; Troxel 2007 Pittsburgh; Drews 2017 Kiel; Richter 2016 ESRS). Operationally: same-bedroom for evening intimacy + 30-60 min wind-down; transition to separate-bedrooms at lights-out; morning reconnection. Western-couple prevalence ~25-30% at least occasionally; outcomes consistently positive. Frame as a sleep-quality + relationship-protective choice (wave-6 behavioural-pricing "graduation not failure" framing applies), NOT a relationship-status downgrade. Cultural-localisation per wave-6: South-Asian + East-Asian + Middle-Eastern joint-family arrangements add complexity — disclose that scheduled separate-bedrooms is one option, mattress-zone separation + sound-engineering can substitute. Transition protocol: discuss with partner in sleep-medicine framing; 2-week reversible trial; track both partners' PSQI + ISI week-1 vs week-3; reconnection rituals (morning coffee + evening wind-down together); re-evaluate at 12 weeks.
