## 1. MÉNIÈRE'S DISEASE — BIDIRECTIONAL WITH TINNITUS, BÁRÁNY 2015 CRITERIA, BETAHISTINE EU/AU/IN FIRST-LINE

### 1.1 What the dossier currently covers

§11.6 row 9 reads in full: *"Diagnosed Ménière's disease — modifies §6.3 salt restriction; clinic-route referral."* §3.3 mentions Ménière only in three homeopathy rows (Chininum Sulph, Salicylicum Acidum, Chenopodium). §13.7 row 7 excludes pulsatile-vascular but not Ménière. No Bárány 2015 criteria, no low-frequency fluctuating SNHL signature, no betahistine EU/AU/CA/IN formulary status, no <1500 mg sodium tightening, no diuretic landscape, no Meniett device, no intratympanic-steroid landscape. The **wave-3 ENT-audiology reviewer flagged this exact gap on 2026-06-08** (logged in `IMPROVEMENT_BACKLOG_WAVE3_2026-06-08.md` row 48) but the block was **not folded into the dossier text** in any wave-4 through wave-7 correction pass. This audit re-surfaces it with specialist vestibular-medicine specificity beyond what wave-3 ENT-audiology scoped.

### 1.2 The bidirectional clinical reality

Ménière's disease (endolymphatic hydrops) and chronic tinnitus are bidirectionally intertwined in three ways the dossier does not surface:

- **Tinnitus is a Bárány-criterion-required symptom of definite + probable Ménière's.** The 2015 Bárány / JSER / EAONO / KBS / AAO-HNS consensus criteria (Lopez-Escamez 2015 *J Vestib Res* 25(1):1-7) require *"fluctuating aural symptoms (hearing, tinnitus or fullness) in the affected ear"* as one of four diagnostic pillars. The Bárány consortium is an international scientific body — citing its criteria respects the no-US-regulator-authority doctrine.
- **~30-50% of chronic-tinnitus patients in tertiary vestibular clinics turn out to have unrecognised early-stage Ménière's** when the audiogram is repeated over 6-12 months and a low-frequency fluctuating SNHL pattern emerges. The dossier's §11.3 consumer-audiogram + smartphone-app framing cannot detect this fluctuation (wave-3 ENT-audiology Dimension-2 established the smartphone limitation).
- **~70-90% of Ménière's patients have tinnitus** (Havia 2002 Finnish n=243; Tyrrell 2014 UK Biobank). A patient reading the dossier as a tinnitus playbook and following it for 6 months without clinic audiometry with bone-conduction + low-frequency-fluctuation tracking risks delayed Ménière diagnosis — and the home OTC + low-salt stack performs reasonably in early Ménière's, masking the underlying diagnosis from patient and dossier.

### 1.3 Bárány 2015 self-screen for the dossier reader

Operationalise as a four-question self-screen for §11.5f: (1) *"Two or more attacks of room-spinning vertigo lasting 20 minutes to 12 hours?"* — vertigo episode requirement; (2) *"Has a hearing test shown low-frequency loss (worse at 250-500 Hz than 2000-4000 Hz) on the tinnitus side at or after an attack?"* — audiometric criterion; the probable-Ménière sub-question is the patient-experiential *"does your hearing come and go over hours-to-days in that ear?"*; (3) *"Does the affected ear feel 'full' or 'pressured' before or during attacks?"* — aural fullness; (4) *"Not better explained by another diagnosis your clinician has named?"* — exclusion clause (vestibular migraine the highest-yield differential per Dimension 3). ≥3 yes → high pre-test probability of definite or probable Ménière's → defer the home antioxidant + vasoactive stack until clinic audiometry with low-frequency-fluctuation tracking is in hand.

### 1.4 The betahistine jurisdiction landscape the dossier does not surface

The dossier's §10.1 names betahistine briefly as *"EU / Asia / Canada — not US-approved."* For the Ménière patient this is incomplete:

- **EU (DE / FR / IT / ES / NL / Nordic):** Betahistine 16 mg TID or 24 mg BID is **first-line for Ménière's** per EMA HMPC + national formularies (Serc / Vertin / Betaserc). The BEMED trial (Adrion 2016 *BMJ* — German multicentre n=221 sham-controlled RCT) was **null on the primary attack-frequency endpoint** — disclose alongside formulary status per Cochrane-honesty. Betahistine remains widely prescribed in Europe (well-tolerated; reported sub-population responders; decades-long observational base).
- **AU + NZ:** TGA ARTG (Serc / Vertin); not PBS-subsidised (~AUD $30-60/mo out-of-pocket).
- **Canada:** Health Canada DIN-approved (Serc); provincial coverage varies.
- **India:** CDSCO-approved (Vertin / Vergon / Stuvert); ~₹150-300/mo; available without prescription in many states (formal prescription via AIIMS / NIMHANS / Manipal / Apollo otology recommended).
- **UK:** MHRA-licensed (Serc); NHS-prescribable.
- **US:** Not FDA-approved as of 2026; compounding pharmacy off-label route only.

### 1.5 The sodium target tightening the dossier is one click loose on

§6.3 targets *"<2000 mg sodium/day"* generally. For Ménière's specifically, the Bárány Society + JSER + EAONO consensus + the Furstenberg regimen recommend **<1500 mg sodium/day** with **<1000 mg/day during active attacks**. The 500 mg/day delta is clinically load-bearing for endolymphatic hydrops fluid dynamics and is a doctrine-aligned dietary modification. §6.3 should add a Ménière-positive sub-bullet.

### 1.6 Low-pressure pulse generator (Meniett) — the home-clinic borderline

The Meniett (Medtronic Xomed; CE-marked; FDA-cleared 2003) delivers ~12 cm H₂O micropulses TID via a tympanostomy tube. Densert 2001 + Gates 2004/2006 RCTs show modest vertigo-frequency reduction; Cochrane (van Sonsbeek 2015) found **insufficient evidence**. Doctrine-borderline (tube is clinic procedure; daily delivery is home) — mention in §10.7.3 (clinic-route plateau option) with honest Cochrane disclosure, not in §10.7.1.

## 2. BPPV CO-PRESENTING WITH CHRONIC TINNITUS — DIX-HALLPIKE + EPLEY + SEMONT + HORIZONTAL-CANAL MANOEUVRES

### 2.1 What the dossier currently covers

Nothing. The dossier contains **zero mention of benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)** as of post-wave-7. No reference to Dix-Hallpike positional testing, Epley canalith-repositioning manoeuvre, Semont liberatory manoeuvre, Lempert/BBQ-roll for horizontal-canal BPPV, or the Brandt-Daroff home habituation exercises. This is a structural blind spot — BPPV is the **single most common cause of dizziness** in any audiovestibular clinic (point prevalence ~2.4% adult population; lifetime incidence ~10%; Bárány Society 2015 BPPV consensus, Bhattacharyya 2017 AAO-HNSF guideline update).

### 2.2 Why BPPV matters in a tinnitus dossier

- **Co-prevalence with chronic tinnitus is ~10-20%** in tertiary tinnitus clinic cohorts — well above population baseline (shared age + inner-ear vulnerability; post-vestibular-neuritis + post-stapes-surgery cohorts elevated).
- **BPPV is the highest-leverage intervention in vestibular medicine.** A single Epley resolves posterior-canal BPPV in **~70-85% of patients on the first attempt** (Hilton & Pinder 2014 Cochrane) and ~95% within three attempts. No other vestibular condition has a comparable single-session treatment effect. A tinnitus-with-BPPV patient who follows the §8 antioxidant stack for 6 months without ever getting an Epley misses the largest reachable improvement on the table — and the home stack does nothing for BPPV.
- **Home-confirmable + home-treatable after one clinic confirmation.** Once Dix-Hallpike confirms posterior-canal laterality, Epley is fully self-administrable (DizzyFIX device, smartphone Epley apps, Bárány + UK National Audiology + NIMHANS video libraries). Brandt-Daroff is pure home. Doctrine-aligned: highest single-intervention effect-size in vestibular medicine.

### 2.3 The BPPV self-screen for the dossier reader

§11.5g three-question screen (Bárány 2015 + AAO-HNSF 2017): (1) *"Brief (<1 min) spinning triggered by head-position changes — rolling over in bed, looking up to a shelf, lying down, getting up?"* — positional trigger; (2) *"Same position consistently triggers the same symptom?"* — reproducibility; (3) *"Episodes occur in days-to-weeks clusters that resolve then sometimes return?"* — clinical course. ≥2 yes → defer the home antioxidant stack as primary intervention; book audiovestibular clinic Dix-Hallpike (UK NHS direct-access audiology; AU Audiology Australia vestibular; IN NIMHANS / AIIMS / Manipal; DE HNO Schwindelambulanz; FR ORL bilan vestibulaire). Once posterior-canal laterality is confirmed, Epley becomes daily home practice (§7.7b per Dimension 5).

### 2.4 Dix-Hallpike + Epley + Semont + Lempert — the manoeuvre landscape

The dossier should name the families without prescribing unsupervised first-time use (Dix-Hallpike in undiagnosed vertebrobasilar insufficiency or cervical instability carries small but non-zero stroke risk):

- **Posterior-canal (~85% of BPPV).** Diagnostic: Dix-Hallpike (45° head rotation, supine + 20° extension; upbeating + torsional nystagmus 1-15 sec latency). Treatment: **Epley** 4-position canalith-repositioning (~70-85% first-attempt success); alternative **Semont liberatory** (equivalent efficacy; preferred where cervical limits Epley).
- **Horizontal-canal (~5-15%).** Diagnostic: supine roll test. Treatment: **Lempert (BBQ-roll)** 360° supine roll in 90° increments, or **Gufoni**.
- **Anterior-canal (rare, <5%).** Specialist-confirmed only — more often a central-pathology mimic than true peripheral BPPV.
- **Brandt-Daroff** (Brandt & Daroff 1980): pure home habituation; lower efficacy (~25-40%) but tolerated where Epley positioning fails.

- **Edit 2.1.** Add **§11.5g BPPV self-screen + clinic confirmation + home Epley adjunct.** ~300 words. Three-question self-screen; Dix-Hallpike clinical-confirmation requirement; named manoeuvre landscape with honest Cochrane signal (Hilton & Pinder 2014); home self-administration after clinical confirmation; DizzyFIX + smartphone-app + Bárány video-library landscape; Brandt-Daroff home protocol; integration with §7.7 vestibular rehab.
- **Edit 2.2.** Add BPPV to the §11.6 "who this protocol is not for" exclusion criteria — *"Acute positional vertigo (BPPV pattern per §11.5g) — do not start the home tinnitus stack as primary intervention; book audiovestibular clinic Dix-Hallpike first. Single Epley resolves ~70-85% of cases at first attempt; this is the highest-leverage intervention in vestibular medicine. Home tinnitus stack appropriate once BPPV is resolved or stable."*

## 3. VESTIBULAR MIGRAINE + TINNITUS COMORBIDITY — FREQUENTLY MISDIAGNOSED AS MÉNIÈRE'S

### 3.1 What the dossier currently covers

Nothing on vestibular migraine specifically. The §11.3 row 18 names "migraine" in the comorbidity-screen context but only in the headache-tinnitus-overlap sense. The migraine sister-dossier (`migraine.md`) presumably covers migraine more broadly but the dossier is silent on **vestibular migraine** (VM) as a distinct condition with its own Bárány Society / International Headache Society 2012 diagnostic criteria (Lempert 2012; refined in ICHD-3 A1.6.6).

### 3.2 Why this matters

- **VM is the second-most-common cause of recurrent spontaneous vertigo** after BPPV (Neuhauser 2006 German population n=4869 → 1-year prevalence ~0.9%; Formeister 2018 US tertiary cohort → ~7-12% lifetime in chronic-headache adults; Bárány-IHS 2012 consensus).
- **~25-30% of VM patients report tinnitus** (Eggers 2014; Volcy 2005). Conversely, ~10-15% of chronic-tinnitus tertiary-clinic patients meet Bárány-IHS criteria for definite/probable VM when systematically screened — yield comparable to or exceeding Ménière's.
- **VM is the most common diagnostic alternative to Ménière's.** Dimension 1's Ménière block is incomplete without the VM differential — Ménière self-screens catch many actual-VM patients, and treatment pathways diverge (VM → migraine prophylaxis + trigger management + CGRP; Ménière → fluid management + betahistine in non-US jurisdictions).

### 3.3 The Bárány-IHS 2012 self-screen for the dossier

§11.5h four-question screen: (1) *"≥5 episodes of vestibular symptoms (spontaneous, positional, visually-induced, or head-motion vertigo with nausea) lasting 5 minutes to 72 hours?"* — episode-count + duration; (2) *"Current or past migraine history per IHS criteria (with or without aura)?"* — migraine-history; (3) *"At least half the vestibular episodes have migraine-quality headache, photophobia + phonophobia, or visual aura?"* — migraine-feature; (4) *"Not better explained by Ménière's, BPPV, brainstem stroke?"* — exclusion. ≥3 yes including criterion 2 → high pre-test probability of VM → defer the §3 vasoactive stack (Ginkgo + Pycnogenol can be migraine triggers — migraine sister-dossier trigger map) and prioritise migraine prophylaxis + trigger management.

### 3.4 Home-stack divergences VM patients need to know

The §8 combos intersect VM in four molecules: **Mg glycinate 400 mg** — migraine-protective (Peikert 1996; Mauskop 2009); aligned. **CoQ10 200 mg** — migraine-protective (Sándor 2005; Hershey 2007 paediatric); aligned. **Ginkgo Tebonin** — mixed trigger-vs-protective signal; honest disclosure to §3.2 qualifier required. **Pycnogenol** — vasoactive mechanism suggests potential trigger; not well-studied in migraine. The §10.7.3 adjunctive layer should add CGRP-receptor-antagonist context for VM patients (erenumab + galcanezumab + fremanezumab + eptinezumab approved EMA/MHRA/TGA/CDSCO/PMDA — non-US-regulator references) as clinic-route option.

- **Edit 3.1.** Add **§11.5h Vestibular migraine — Bárány-IHS 2012 self-screen, differential from Ménière's, home-stack divergences.** ~350 words. Four-question self-screen; differential from Ménière's (the VM patient often has migraine-history + photophobia + visual aura + 5 min - 72 h duration window; the Ménière patient has aural fullness + 20 min - 12 h duration + low-frequency fluctuating SNHL); home-stack divergence on Ginkgo + Pycnogenol; magnesium + CoQ10 as aligned cofactors; trigger-mapping cross-reference to migraine sister-dossier; CGRP-receptor-antagonist landscape (non-US jurisdictions) in §10.7.3.

## 4. POST-STAPES-SURGERY TINNITUS + PERILYMPH FISTULA + SUPERIOR SEMICIRCULAR CANAL DEHISCENCE (PLF + SSCD)

### 4.1 What the dossier currently covers

§0 red-flag pulsatile-tinnitus block mentions superior canal dehiscence (SSCD) once in the imaging ladder ("HRCT temporal bone — second-line if sigmoid sinus dehiscence or superior canal dehiscence suspected") and §12.7 wave-7 PTSD-correction block mentions it once in the blast-injury context ("humming + breath-hold can trigger vertigo in superior-canal-dehiscence patterns common in blast injury"). Perilymph fistula (PLF) is **not mentioned anywhere in the dossier text.** Post-stapedectomy / post-stapedotomy syndromes are **not mentioned anywhere** despite being a named ENT-clinic phenotype with characteristic tinnitus + dizziness presentation patterns.

### 4.2 Why these three matter

All three are **rare but treatable mechanical causes** of tinnitus + vestibular symptoms; the home antioxidant + vasoactive + cognitive stack cannot help, and delayed diagnosis is the load-bearing risk:

- **Perilymph fistula (PLF)** — leak of perilymph via round window, oval window, or stapes footplate (post-surgery). Triggered by head trauma, barotrauma (diving, flying with congestion, scuba ascent, heavy lifting with Valsalva), or as iatrogenic post-stapedectomy complication. Pattern: sudden/fluctuating tinnitus + episodic vertigo + low-frequency SNHL + Hennebert (vertigo on positive ear-canal pressure) + Tullio (vertigo on loud sound). Treatment: bed rest + Valsalva-avoidance + sometimes surgical round-window patching. The dossier's Bhramari + Sensate cervical-vibratory layers are potentially provocative.
- **Superior semicircular canal dehiscence (SSCD — Minor's syndrome)** — congenital/acquired bone thinning creating a "third window." Pattern: autophony (own voice/footsteps/eye-movements/heartbeat unusually loud), Tullio, Hennebert, pulsatile or non-pulsatile low-frequency tinnitus, **paradoxical pseudo-conductive audiogram** (third-window low-frequency air-bone gap with normal middle ear; supranormal bone-conduction at low frequencies). Diagnosis: HRCT temporal bone with Pöschl + Stenvers reformats (axial cuts miss it); cVEMP threshold reduction + amplitude increase. Treatment: middle-fossa or transmastoid SCD repair. Home stack does nothing.
- **Post-stapedectomy / post-stapedotomy syndromes** — ~3-10% of post-stapes patients have post-op tinnitus + dizziness + PLF + dead-ear SNHL (prosthesis migration, reparative granuloma, oval-window fistula, vestibular labyrinthine concussion, floating footplate). §11.6 row 12 covers CI + Vibrant Soundbridge + Esteem but **not stapedectomy/stapedotomy** — a much higher-prevalence surgical population. Missed post-stapes-PLF window forfeits surgical-revision hearing-restoration option.

### 4.3 The Tullio + Hennebert + autophony self-screen for SSCD

§11.5i four-question screen (Minor 2000 + Bárány 2017 third-window criteria): (1) *"Loud sounds (sudden music, alarms, sirens) trigger brief dizziness/vertigo/eye-movement?"* — Tullio; (2) *"Straining (Valsalva, sneezing, weightlifting) or ear-pressure (popping ears, flying) triggers vertigo/eye-movement?"* — Hennebert; (3) *"You hear your own voice, footsteps, eye-movements, chewing, or heartbeat unusually loud in one ear?"* — autophony (load-bearing positive feature); (4) *"Hearing test shows low-frequency conductive loss (air-bone gap at 250-500 Hz) with normal otoscopy on the autophony side?"* — pseudo-conductive audiogram (patient may not know without clinic bone-conduction). ≥2 yes including autophony → high pre-test probability of SSCD → defer the home stack, particularly Bhramari + Sensate + PEMF; book HRCT temporal bone + cVEMP.

### 4.4 The post-trauma / post-barotrauma / post-surgery red flag for PLF

§0 should add a PLF row: *"Tinnitus + vertigo after head trauma, diving, flying with congestion, Valsalva-heavy lifting, or stapedectomy / stapedotomy / cochlear implantation / chronic ear surgery = perilymph fistula pattern. Bed rest + head elevation + Valsalva avoidance + ENT referral. Do NOT start Bhramari + breath-work + taVNS + PEMF until PLF is excluded. Home antioxidant + vasoactive stack remains appropriate as adjunct."*

## 5. VESTIBULAR REHABILITATION — CAWTHORNE-COOKSEY + GAZE STABILISATION + HABITUATION EXERCISES

### 5.1 What the dossier currently covers

§7 Lifestyle / Mind-Body Anchors covers yoga (§7.2 neck-cleared inversions), pranayama (§7.3 Bhramari + Nadi Shodhana), meditation stack (§7.4), sleep + circadian (§7.5), nervous-system reset (§7.6), and exercise periodization (§7.7 — Zone 2 cardio + resistance training). **Nothing on vestibular rehabilitation** — Cawthorne-Cooksey exercises, gaze stabilisation exercises (VOR adaptation), habituation exercises for motion sensitivity, balance retraining, or substitution exercises for unilateral peripheral vestibular loss. This is a structural omission for the tinnitus-with-vestibular-comorbidity sub-cohort (~25-40% of any specialist tinnitus clinic caseload per the introduction to this audit).

### 5.2 Why vestibular rehab belongs in this dossier

- **Highest-evidence non-pharmacological intervention for chronic vestibular dysfunction.** Cochrane McDonnell & Hillier 2015 — moderate-to-strong evidence for unilateral peripheral vestibular dysfunction; Hall 2016 APTA CPG (clinical-pathway context only, not as US-regulator authority) + Bárány 2022 PPPD consensus extend to PPPD + post-concussion + chronic vestibulopathy. Effect sizes for DHI drops exceed §5.5 CBT-T effect sizes for THI/TFI.
- **Mechanistic alignment with §2.3 brain volume-up pathway.** When peripheral sensory input drops, central gain rises — gaze-stabilisation drives cortical re-weighting of vestibular + visual + somatosensory inputs; habituation drives top-down suppression of mismatched signals; substitution drives functional plasticity in cerebellar-brainstem circuits. The §5 MBCT-T + sound enrichment + somatic release layer does the analogous work for the auditory system. Vestibular rehab is the vestibular mirror.

### 5.3 The Cawthorne-Cooksey + gaze stabilisation + habituation home protocol
